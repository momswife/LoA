# **Standardized Mission & Bounty Ranking System (SMB-RS)**

### _Ministerial Classification for All External Contracts, Bounties, Field Missions & Off-Labyrinth Operations_

While the Labyrinths dominate delver culture, the Ministry recognizes that **a significant portion of delver work takes place beyond the stone corridors**—in towns, cities, wildlands, ruined strongholds, and volatile frontier zones.

To regulate these jobs and ensure delver safety, the Ministry codified the **Standardized Mission & Bounty Ranking System (SMB-RS)**:  
a universal system classifying **risk**, **reward**, **political sensitivity**, and **hazard potential** for all non-Labyrinth assignments.

This ensures fair compensation, appropriate delver deployment, and public trust in licensed delvers acting outside the Labyrinths.

---

# **I. Mission Rank Structure**

Each non-labyrinth bounty or mission is placed into one of **six ranks**, determined by risk metrics, magical volatility, monster presence, and geopolitical ramifications.

---

## **Rank E — Minor Field Tasks**

**Risk:** Negligible  
**Threat:** Mundane creatures, petty theft, basic patrols  
**Examples:**

- Escorting caravans
    
- Capturing non-magical beasts
    
- Retrieving lost goods
    
- Simple civic assistance
    

**Delver Requirement:**  
D-Rank and above.

---

## **Rank D — Localized Hazards**

**Risk:** Mild  
**Threat:** Minor spellcasters, weak monsters, small criminal groups  
**Examples:**

- Clearing small bandit camps
    
- Dealing with nuisance magical beasts
    
- Guarding political envoys
    
- Enforcing municipal contracts
    

**Delver Requirement:**  
D-Rank delvers with clean UDMI score.

---

## **Rank C — High-Risk Field Missions**

**Risk:** Moderate  
**Threat:** Skilled foes, magical anomalies, monster packs  
**Examples:**

- Investigating ruins with unstable magic
    
- Hunting rogue delvers or unsanctioned mages
    
- Retrieval of semi-stable relics
    
- Escort missions in frontier zones
    

**Delver Requirement:**  
C-Rank or mixed C/D teams under supervision.

---

## **Rank B — Critical Threat Contracts**

**Risk:** Severe  
**Threat:** Monster elites, dangerous criminals, relic-active foes  
**Examples:**

- Assassination of high-threat entities
    
- Containing curse leaks in villages
    
- Tracking monster lords outside labyrinth zones
    
- Preventing sabotage to Divine Oculi infrastructure
    

**Delver Requirement:**  
B-Rank delvers or above.

---

## **Rank A — National-Level Bounties**

**Risk:** Extreme  
**Threat:** Army-level monsters, major political targets, relic catastrophes  
**Examples:**

- Ending rogue guild uprisings
    
- Neutralizing planar breaches outside Labyrinths
    
- Capturing relic-wielding criminals
    
- Intercepting black-market relic pipelines
    

**Delver Requirement:**  
A-Rank delvers or mixed A/B strike groups.  
Ministerial oversight mandatory.

---

## **Rank S — World-Risk Missions**

**Risk:** Existential  
**Threat:** Convergence-touched foes, catastrophic relic activations, divine anomalies  
**Examples:**

- Stopping Convergence cult uprisings
    
- Securing deific breach-sites
    
- Tracking awakened megafauna
    
- Recovering forbidden relics in the wild
    

**Delver Requirement:**  
S-Rank teams only.  
ABA blackout protocols commonly used.

---

# **II. Mission Classification Metrics**

Each mission is assigned rank based on six criteria:

|**Metric**|**Description**|
|---|---|
|**Threat Density**|Number & strength of hostile entities present|
|**Magical Volatility**|Presence of spells, curses, or relic effects|
|**Environmental Hazard**|Terrain hazards, storms, corruption, planar bleed|
|**Civilian Risk Factor**|Likelihood of public harm|
|**Political Sensitivity**|Impact on guilds, nobility, or international relations|
|**Relic Interference Probability**|Chance of encountering relics or awakening phenomena|
These metrics are weighed by the **Mission Assignment Sphere (MAS)**—a Ministerial predictive algorithm linked to the Arcanet.

---

# **III. Contract Issuers**

Approved issuers include:

- Municipal governments
    
- Recognized guilds
    
- Merchant syndicates
    
- Ministry field offices
    
- Noble houses under charter
    

Unauthorized issuers attempting to hire delvers are guilty of **Contract Laundering (§7-E)**.

---

# **IV. Rewards & Payout Structures**

Payout is based on:

- Risk rank
    
- UDMI influence
    
- Fame Engine relevance
    
- Relic involvement
    
- Political profile
    

Rank S missions may award:

- Land rights
    
- Relic custodianship
    
- Direct Ministerial favor
    

Rank E missions award basic wages unless otherwise incentivized.

---

# **V. Ethical Restrictions**

Delvers must **not**:

- use excessive force if non-lethal resolution is viable
    
- seize relics from mission environments without reporting
    
- accept contradictory contracts from rival issuers
    
- manipulate mission outcomes for Fame Engine exploitation
    
- interfere with local governance structures
    

All ethical violations apply under the broader **Ethical Delving Guidelines**.

---

# **VI. Role of Divine Oculi in External Missions**

Though primarily stationed in Labyrinths, Oculi may deploy to high-ranking missions to:

- monitor delver conduct
    
- record UDMI-affecting actions
    
- document hazard escalation
    
- provide cross-verifiable truth-records
    

Oculi presence instantly upgrades mission monitoring to **Integrity Tier-4**.

---

# **VII. Special Provisions for N-Class Teams**

Due to their Convergence significance, N-Class teams (e.g., **Unit N-7**) follow modified SMB-RS protocols.

### **1. Rank B–S Missions Are Auto-Authorized**

N-Class teams may take these without standard approval delays.

### **2. Convergence Hazard Monitoring**

Mission rank may _escalate_ mid-operation if N-Class resonance alters threat profile.

### **3. Arcanet Core Oversight**

All N-Class external missions are monitored directly by the Core, tracking emergent patterns.

### **4. Fame & UDMI Decoupling**

N-Class teams do not gain Fame Engine bonuses from external missions to prevent destabilizing influence.

### **5. Ministerial Escort Allowed but Not Required**

N-Class teams can operate autonomously except in political missions.

---

# **VIII. Why the SMB-RS Matters**

Without this system:

- Delvers would face wildly inconsistent dangers
    
- Civilian harm would skyrocket
    
- Guild corruption would flourish
    
- Markets would destabilize
    
- Dangerous relics would circulate unchecked
    
- Nations would weaponize delvers illicitly
    

The standardized ranking system ensures that delvers outside the Labyrinths remain **predictable**, **ethical**, and **professionally accountable**.

It preserves order in a world where danger often walks freely.
