## **Overview**

The beasts of the labyrinths are not natural things. Some were born in darkness, others conjured by forgotten rites, and still more evolved to feed upon the brave and foolish alike. Each labyrinth spawns creatures unique to its theme, but certain apex predators and recurring horrors are seen across multiple sites.

This document catalogues the most encountered and deadly entities within the known labyrinths of Aerathon. Each entry includes:

- **Name**
    
- **Origin Labyrinth(s)**
    
- **Threat Tier**
    
- **Known Behaviors**
    
- **Weaknesses (if any)**
    

All S-Rank Delvers are required to submit field observations to maintain the integrity of this registry.

---

## **Threat Tier Ratings**

|Tier|Description|
|---|---|
|Low|Solitary or easily dispatched by low-tier teams|
|Moderate|Requires coordination or mid-tier magic to handle|
|High|Dangerous to entire parties; intelligent or magically empowered|
|Apex|Legendary threat; often guards relics or bosses|

## **Catalogued Beasts**

### _Bloodthorn Strider_

- **Origin:** _Verdant Coil_, _Blooming Crater Fringe_
    
- **Threat Tier:** Moderate
    
- **Behavior:** Ambush predator. Camouflaged as twisted vines. Strikes from above.
    
- **Weakness:** Fire. Searing light spells disrupt its photosensitive nerves.
    
- **Notables:** Leaves a rare sap that enhances druidic casting for 1 hour.
    

---

### _Whisperskull_

- **Origin:** _Hollow Veil_, _Obsidian Crown_
    
- **Threat Tier:** High
    
- **Behavior:** Invisible unless reflected in mirrors or water. Uses voice mimicry to lure individuals into isolation.
    
- **Weakness:** Thunder damage breaks its illusion shell.
    
- **Notables:** Carries fragments of forgotten memories; some speak in voices of fallen allies.
    

---

### _Amberglass Hound_

- **Origin:** _Unbound Maze_, _Shattered Throne_
    
- **Threat Tier:** Moderate
    
- **Behavior:** Hounds made of golden glass that shatter and reform endlessly unless restrained.
    
- **Weakness:** Must be shattered and buried under stone to prevent regeneration.
    
- **Notables:** Used in Arcanet security systems as living patrol glyphs.
    

---

### _Carrion Regent_

- **Origin:** _Salt-Rot Vaults_, _Pit of the Unbodied_
    
- **Threat Tier:** Apex
    
- **Behavior:** Commands swarms of minor undead. Feeds on marrow. Intelligent and speaks in prophecy fragments.
    
- **Weakness:** Immune to radiant, but vulnerable to pure silver and psionic damage.
    
- **Notables:** Slain regents have left behind permanent aura-wounds in local planar structure.
    

---

### _False-Saint Gorgon_

- **Origin:** _Obsidian Crown_, _Dreamcoil Vault_
    
- **Threat Tier:** High
    
- **Behavior:** Appears as a radiant angelic being. Petrifies by offering false hope. Victims smile even as they turn to stone.
    
- **Weakness:** Cannot enter consecrated ground.
    
- **Notables:** Statues left behind sometimes whisper hours before crumbling into dust.
    

---

### _Gravetide Wurm_

- **Origin:** _Drowned Marches_, _Silver Mouth_
    
- **Threat Tier:** Apex
    
- **Behavior:** Tunnels through reality more than soil. Swallows both flesh and memory.
    
- **Weakness:** Temporal disruption effects, such as _Time Stop_, disorient it briefly.
    
- **Notables:** Thought to be responsible for a vanished guild—The Lunar Knives.
    

---

### _Mirror-Touched Stag_

- **Origin:** _Hollow Veil_
    
- **Threat Tier:** Low
    
- **Behavior:** Skittish but lures players into mirrored puzzles. Horns reflect spells back at casters.
    
- **Weakness:** Pure physical strikes, especially bludgeoning.
    
- **Notables:** Some guilds believe capturing it grants a boon of foresight. This remains unproven.

## **Beast Behavioral Patterns**

- Many labyrinth beasts grow stronger with **audience energy**—particularly in livecast-enabled sites.
    
- Some apex beasts **retain memories of past combats**, adjusting tactics between delves.
    
- **Magical resonance** may awaken latent evolutions mid-encounter, especially during celestial events.
    
- Beasts have been known to **mimic player abilities** after consuming corpses of Delvers.
    

---

## **Beast Ecology and Economy**

- Components from slain beasts fuel Aerathon’s magical economy—blood, glands, sinew, and bone are repurposed by guild enchanters.
    
- Apex beasts often nest near **artifact vaults** or sealed chambers.
    
- **Black-market vendors** have been caught selling embryo-eggs of labyrinth beasts for illegal summoning.
