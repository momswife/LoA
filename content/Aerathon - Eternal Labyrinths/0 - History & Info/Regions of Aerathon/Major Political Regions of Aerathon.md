> _“From soaring spires to misted coasts, the cities of Aerathon pulse with magic, commerce, and conflict alike.”_

___

## **Overview**

Following the emergence of the Labyrinths, Aerathon's fractured map reformed under the pressure of new threats, shared survival, and delver-led meritocracy. The former seats of royal and divine power have been reshaped by a delving culture, spontaneous Labyrinth incursions, and the influence of powerful guilds and livecasting networks. The world is now unified across five major continental regions—each a sovereign cultural entity, yet interdependent in an era of delver caravans and magical commerce.

---
# The Main Five

## **Allemance** — _The Heartland of Fellowship and Grain_

**Capital:** Louvain  
**Terrain:** Rolling pastures, vineyard hills, and mountain-fed valleys  
**Culture:** Polychronic, familial, indulgent  
**Known For:** Noble intrigue, delver festivals, wine, and strong community bonds

Allemance is a matrilineal feudal monarchy now centered on Queen Sophia's court in Louvain. Fertile and sprawling, Allemance produces much of Aerathon’s grain and wine. Delver culture is beloved here—danger and glory walk hand in hand through summer feasts, tavern tales, and the Simmer festivals. Cities like **Daltiarna** and **Baritte** balance the old nobility with free communities of thinkers, musicians, and charismatic delvers.

---

## **2. Vinyot** — _The Guild-League of Commerce and Coin_

**Capital:** Pristana  
**Terrain:** Coastal cities, fortified hills, forested trade roads  
**Culture:** Structured, competitive, urban-industrial  
**Known For:** Trade guilds, mercantile politics, delver contracts, magical broadcasting

A mercantile power dominated by powerful trade guilds, Vinyot is Aerathon's economic engine. Cities here compete to sponsor the most successful delvers, forging lucrative sponsorships and manipulating labyrinth access through intricate auctions and bureaucracy. Pristana is a jewel of commerce, and houses the **Vaulted Ledger**, the largest magical stock exchange in Aerathon. Vinyotians trust in law, ledgers, and the certainty of a well-signed contract.

---

## **3. Arneria** — _The Divided Sands of Ideals and Invention_

**Regions:** The Beylik (south), The Bat’yan (east), The Causeway (north)  
**Culture:** Ideological, scholarly, ancestral  
**Known For:** Theocratic debate, magical scholarship, gladiatorial divinity, sandcast cities

Arneria is a vast desert realm unified by debate more than law. Its three primary regions constantly wrestle over interpretation of the gods, governance, and the rights of delvers in society. The **Beylik** centers around divine oaths and desert temples, **The Bat’yan** around survivalism and trade oases, and **The Causeway** holds ancient tomb cities ruled by scribe-cults. Delving is seen as a sacred trial, with relics often traded like scripture.

---

## **4. Al’ar** — _The Isles of Claw and Flame_

**Capital:** Port Tonoro  
**Terrain:** Jungle-draped archipelagos, volcanic islands, cloud-swept mountains  
**Culture:** Expressive, aesthetic, clan-based  
**Known For:** Stick-fighting tournaments, bardic traditions, elemental festivals, fashion

Felines, foxes, and others rule the fragmented but vibrant Feline Isles. Al’ar is home to acrobats, mystics, and warriors who often double as performers. Delvers from Al’ar are flamboyant and well-documented—their pictos as colorful as their silk sashes. Port Tonoro is a port of dreams, where crewmates are made and legends auctioned.

---

## **5. Oria** — _The Shrouded Realm of Storm and Stone_

**Capital:** Jegervalt  
**Terrain:** Nattefrost glaciers, lodge-house fortresses, cold mountain ridges  
**Culture:** Stoic, martial, tradition-bound  
**Known For:** Lodge feuds, mammoth hunting, snowbound delver training camps

Oria is a realm carved by wind and will. The delvers of the north do not chase fame—they endure. The lodge houses of Oria hold ancient bonds, oaths made in fire and storm. Magic here is older, colder, more personal. Jegervalt, the largest of the northern strongholds, hosts brutal trials for new delver crews seeking the famed **Seal of Frostbrand**—a rite of passage only the most hardened survive.

### ⚔️ Beyond the Five

### **The Astral Sea** — _Mirror of the Realms_

**Nature:** Extraplanar, coexistent, liquid-thought realm  
**Access Points:** Celestial rifts, high rituals, astral ascension spells  
**Inhabitants:** Starborn bats, echo-beasts, immortal scholars, Dreamcasters

The Astral Sea is not merely a plane—it is Aerathon’s reflection, dream, and womb. Here, ships sail the nothing-between-stars, and memories take form as solid beings. Delvers who enter may lose years to dream logic or awaken with futures etched into their minds. Some labyrinths spill into this plane, dragging creatures half-formed in concept, not flesh. A growing school of delvers—**the Dreamnavers**—train to navigate and chart it for guild profit and prophecy.

---

### **Loamlink** — _The Rootways Beneath All Worlds_

**Nature:** Transmaterial root-network, ecological subconscious  
**Access Points:** Through elder trees, fungal altars, buried labyrinths  
**Inhabitants:** Ligonines, mold-minds, fungal archons, rootbound gods

The Loamlink lies beneath Aerathon like a mycelial net, connecting roots, spirits, and ancient labyrinths. Its tunnels are natural yet shaped by intention—living corridors grown through pact and sacrifice. Delvers tell stories of labyrinths with soil ceilings that whisper back when touched, of corridors that breathe. Ligonine cities are strung along its spine like pearls, and some whisper the Loamlink existed before the gods themselves.
