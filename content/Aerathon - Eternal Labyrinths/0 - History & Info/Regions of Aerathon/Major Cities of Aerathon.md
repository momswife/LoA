## **1. Louvain** — _Crown of Allemance_

**Region:** Allemance  
**Capital of Allemance**  
**Known For:** Queen Sophia’s court, guild festivals, vineyards, delver culture

Louvain is the radiant heart of Allemance and the seat of Queen Sophia’s matrilineal court. From its marble balconies overlooking **Lake Tranquil**, one can see both the ancient nobility of Allemance and the modern flood of delver caravans who pass through its gates.

As the **capital**, Louvain is the stage where every intrigue of the heartland unfolds. Festivals are constant: summer feasts in honor of harvest gods, autumn celebrations of delver victories, and winter masquerades where nobles and guilds barter secrets as much as coin. Its vineyards, nourished by mountain-fed rivers, produce Aerathon’s finest wines, often traded for relics brought by delvers.

Louvain’s great guildhall doubles as a performance venue, where delver crews recount labyrinthic triumphs for noble patronage. To walk Louvain’s streets is to feel both comfort and conspiracy, where danger wears perfume and history waits in every shadow.

---

## **2. Daltiarna** — _City of Scholars and Strings_

**Region:** Allemance  
**Known For:** Bardic conservatories, scholarly guilds, philosophical debate

Daltiarna is the scholarly soul of Allemance, though not its capital. Nestled in vineyard hills east of Louvain, it thrives as an intellectual counterpoint to the crown. Its bardic conservatories train performers who blend spellwork with song, while the **Forum of Silver Voices** hosts debates that ripple across Aerathon through scrycasting networks.

Though not the throne, Daltiarna holds sway by reputation: guilds come here to consult sages, musicians accompany delver crews to immortalize their exploits, and philosophers argue over the ethics of delving itself. Its festivals are quieter than Louvain’s but no less passionate—nights of strings and voice that blur the line between scholarship and sorcery.

---

## **3. Baritte** — _Caravan Crossroads_

**Region:** Allemance  
**Known For:** Caravan trade, diverse guildhouses, crossroads of culture

Baritte is no capital, but it is the heart of Allemance’s trade roads. Growing where caravan routes converge, it has become the “unofficial council” of merchants, caravaneers, and delvers. Its bazaar is legendary, a place where spices from Arneria, ivory from Oria, silks from Al’ar, and relics from the Labyrinths change hands in a single morning.

The city’s guildhouses are fiercely independent, each backing crews who bear Baritte’s name across Aerathon. The **Hall of Roads**, a domed hub where caravans announce arrivals and guilds bid for cargoes, is often called the city’s throne. Baritte governs itself not with crown or creed, but with coin and courage.

---

## **4. Pristana** — _Ledger of the World_

**Region:** Vinyot  
**Capital of Vinyot**  
**Known For:** Guild contracts, magical stock exchange, bureaucracy, broadcasting

Pristana is the mercantile heart and **capital of the Trade League of Vinyot**, the greatest economic power in Aerathon. Its skyline glitters with marble guildhalls and glass scrycasting towers, and its streets are ordered with merciless efficiency.

At the center stands the **Vaulted Ledger**, a magical stock exchange where relics, Labyrinth rights, and delver contracts are auctioned daily in spectacles of arcane wealth. Delvers here are both heroes and commodities, their names traded like coin. Bureaucracy is its own weapon in Pristana, where guild contracts bind tighter than iron chains.

For all its ruthless commerce, Pristana embodies opportunity. Here, crews rise from obscurity to stardom overnight, their victories broadcast across Aerathon in glowing script and livecast spectacle.

---

## **5. Southwinds** — _Harbor of Whispers_

**Region:** Vinyot  
**Known For:** Sea trade, Tempestrise festivals, stormwatch guilds

Southwinds is not the capital of Vinyot, but it is its storm-tossed harbor jewel. Perched on the southern coast, the city thrives on trade between Arneria and Al’ar, while living at the edge of danger from the **Tempestrise** storm belt.

Its people celebrate risk with lightning festivals, stringing rooftops with rods that channel thunder into dazzling magical displays. The city is home to stormwatch guilds who hire delvers to monitor and harness the elemental chaos nearby.

Whispers cling to its docks: cults who worship the storm, pirates who traffic in relics, and sailors who vanish into lightning-lit waters. Southwinds thrives on the thin line between reverence and ruin.

___
## **6. Porta Strega** — _The Witch City_

**Region:** Vinyot  
**Known For:** Ghost-thin woods, witch covens, spectral commerce

Porta Strega rises at the edge of the **Strega Woods**, where the veil between Aerathon and the Netherworld thins to threads. Its crooked streets are lined with bone-lanterns, rune-marked shutters, and scrying mirrors sold on every corner. Ghosts are as common as merchants here, drifting across bridges or whispering from alley shadows.

The city is famed for its covens, who blend necromancy, herbalism, and labyrinth lore into an economy of charms, potions, and forbidden bargains. For delvers, Porta Strega is both a treasure and a snare: relics and rituals abound, but contracts often bear invisible costs. Festivals here are unsettling affairs, where living and dead dance side by side beneath candlelit branches.

---

## **7. Porta Ventura** — _The Cliffside Beacon_

**Region:** Vinyot  
**Known For:** Lighthouse citadel, storm-diving festivals, sea-delver ports

Porta Ventura perches on jagged cliffs above the Azur Gulf, eternally lashed by wind and spray. Its great lighthouse, the **Beacon of the Eastern Trade**, is said to be carved from storm-forged stone and fueled by a bound elemental flame that never dims.

The city thrives on maritime trade, but its identity is tied to the **Tempestrise** storms that rage just beyond its harbors. Every equinox, festivals are held where storm-divers leap from the cliffs, echoing the daring rites of aarakocra. Delvers often depart from Porta Ventura to brave the gulf’s mysteries—many never return, their names etched into the Beacon’s foundation.

---

## **8. Sourisport** — _The Ash-Tide City_

**Region:** Vinyot  
**Known For:** Verges of Ash incursions, spectral markets, relic trade

Sourisport is both a jewel and a scar of the eastern coast. Its harbors teem with trade caravans and fishing fleets, but its outskirts are perpetually threatened by the **Verges of Ash**. Planar storms roll through its streets without warning, and ghost markets materialize on its piers, where spectral merchants barter goods no mortal forge could make.

The city’s guilds thrive on this danger, contracting delvers to stabilize bleed events or seize planar artifacts before rivals do. For locals, Sourisport is a city of whispers, where a bargain struck at midnight might bind you to a fey noble—or a shade from the Netherworld. Few places embody the peril and profit of the delver age so starkly.

---

## **9. Muraya** — _Court of the Archipelago_

**Region:** Al’ar (Sampura Isle)  
**Capital of Al’ar**  
**Known For:** Clifftop temples, isle councils, resonant choirs

Muraya crowns the cliffs of **Sampura**, its marble spires rising like tuning forks over the sea. As the **capital of the Feline Isles**, it is the seat of the isle courts, where clan leaders gather in heated councils to decide trade, territory, and ritual law. Politics here are theatrical, each speech as much performance as governance.

Muraya’s choirs, trained in the resonance of the **Kasho’ta Caves**, are said to carry voices that stir even the storm. Festivals echo with layered harmonies that seem to bend the air itself. For delvers, Muraya is both a launching point and a proving ground, where crews often perform before the courts to win sponsorship.

---

## **10. Port Tonoro** — _Harbor of Masks and Song_

**Region:** Al’ar (Tonoro Isle)  
**Known For:** Bardic duels, flamboyant festivals, delver-celebrity culture

Port Tonoro is a riot of color, sound, and spectacle. Here, delvers are treated as performers as much as heroes, their victories immortalized in painted murals and retold in mask-plays. Every street thrums with song, every tavern hosts bardic duels where magic and melody intertwine.

Though not the capital, Port Tonoro rivals Muraya in fame. It is the city outsiders think of when they imagine the Isles: flamboyant, theatrical, and fearless. To succeed here is to become a legend; to fail is to be forgotten in the chorus. For delvers, Port Tonoro is both stage and crucible, where survival must always come with flair.

## **11. Tempestat** — _Isle of the Storm’s Eye_

**Region:** Al’ar (Tempestat Isle)  
**Known For:** Volcanic caldera city, elemental festivals, stormforged relics

Tempestat is a city built inside the caldera of an active volcano, its homes clinging to obsidian slopes while steam vents hiss across the streets. Storms roll unnaturally over the isle, drawn to the volcano’s elemental pulse. Locals claim the mountain is alive, its heart beating with the storm’s own rhythm.

Each year, the city hosts the **Festival of Sparks**, where aarakocra and beastfolk challenge one another in lightning duels atop the caldera rim. Delvers come seeking **stormforged relics**—obsidian blades that hum with thunder, crystals that hold bottled lightning—but leaving Tempestat with both relic and life intact is a feat in itself.

## **12. Kuzmek** — _Sun-Seat of the Beylik_

**Region:** Arneria (Beylik)  
**Capital of the Beylik**  
**Known For:** Theocratic courts, Sun Bull rites, sandstone temples

Kuzmek is the **capital of the Beylik**, a golden desert metropolis where sandstone spires catch the dawn and blaze like fire. Every street bears invocations to the **Sun Bull**, whose death beneath the sands is said to have sanctified the region. Priests and scribes debate endlessly in temple courts, their arguments often carrying the weight of law across the desert.

For delvers, Kuzmek is sacred ground. To enter its Labyrinth gates is to prove oneself before both god and mortal alike. Pilgrimages to Kuzmek often end in trials, with the faithful expected to cross the surrounding dunes barefoot, carrying only prayers and resolve. The city’s grandeur is matched only by its gravity—here, faith itself governs.

## **13. Kalabil** — _Bastion of the Bat’yan_

**Region:** Arneria (Bat’yan)  
**Capital of the Bat’yan**  
**Known For:** Paladin orders, Blackwild patrols, defensive walls

Kalabil is the fortress-heart and **capital of the Bat’yan**, built as a bulwark against the cursed rainforest of the **Blackwild**. Its colossal walls are inscribed with radiant wards, glowing faintly at night to keep demonic whispers at bay. The paladin orders rule here, their hierarchies as rigid as the walls themselves.

Within Kalabil’s walls, life is austere but purposeful. Temples double as armories, and every tavern tale eventually bends toward vigilance. Delvers in Kalabil are not adventurers but conscripts in the eternal war to hold the line. To walk its streets is to feel the weight of watchfulness, for the Blackwild grows closer each year.

---

## **14. Mansque** — _Gate of the Mantle_

**Region:** Arneria (Mantle)  
**Capital of the Mantle Causeway**  
**Known For:** Causeway stronghold, symphonic arts, caravan wealth

Mansque sits astride the **Causeway**, the colossal road linking Arneria’s deserts, jungles, and tomb cities. As the **capital of the Mantle’s Causeway region**, it controls trade and travel across the continent’s spine. Its streets are lined with caravanserais large enough to house entire guilds, and its markets never sleep.

Yet Mansque is not only commerce—it is also culture. Inspired by nearby **Symphony Canyon**, the city hosts bardic halls where orchestras of hundreds perform beneath open skies. Caravans often pause here to witness the music, claiming it brings fortune before entering the deserts beyond. Mansque thrives as both gate and guardian, a city where all roads converge.

---

## **15. Kazmak** — _The Desert Forge_

**Region:** Arneria (Beylik)  
**Known For:** Obsidian quarries, smith-guilds, relic-metal trade

Kazmak grew from the ruins of an ancient obsidian quarry, where flames still smolder beneath the sands. The city’s forges run day and night, fed by both mortal smiths and elemental pacts. Weapons and armor forged here are sought across Aerathon, renowned for their resilience against both mortal steel and otherworldly claw.

For delvers, Kazmak is a city of trade and temptation. Relic-metal brought up from Labyrinths often finds its way here, reforged into new creations. Guilds sponsor expeditions specifically to feed Kazmak’s demand. Yet the city is not without peril—its streets echo with the clang of competition, and rival smith-guilds often hire delvers to settle feuds in flame and shadow.

## **16. Jegervalt** — _Stronghold of the North_

**Region:** Oria  
**Capital of Oria**  
**Known For:** Lodge feuds, frostforged warriors, Seal of Frostbrand

Jegervalt is the iron crown of Oria, the **capital of the northern lodges**, carved into cliffs of stone and ice. Its great hall, the **Hall of Storm Oaths**, is built around a fire that has never gone out, said to burn with the first mammoth bones ever hunted.

Here, traditions weigh heavy: lodge houses feud in politics as fierce as their duels, and delvers prove themselves in the brutal trial of the **Seal of Frostbrand**. To earn the mark is to be recognized as frostforged, a warrior who endures not for glory but for survival. Jegervalt embodies Oria’s creed—harsh, proud, and unyielding.

## **17. Riverbed City** — _The Bridge of Beasts_

**Region:** Oria  
**Known For:** River trade, lodge rites, beastfolk heritage

Built where the **Fallensky Pass** cuts through Oria’s mountains, Riverbed City spans a roaring river with colossal bridges of rune-carved stone. Its markets bustle with fur, ivory, and fish from the wilds, while its plazas serve as arenas for lodge rites.

Riverbed is famed as a place of heritage—home to beastfolk clans who trace their bloodlines back to the wild gods. Its festivals often reenact ancestral hunts, and delvers come here to test themselves in ritual combat. Though not a capital, Riverbed is a cultural keystone, where Oria’s pulse beats loud and proud.

---

## **18. Severport** — _Edge of the Ice_

**Region:** Oria  
**Known For:** Frozen trade routes, ivory markets, Nattefrost expeditions

Severport is the last harbor before the endless white of the **Nattefrost**. Its docks are locked in ice most of the year, yet the city thrives on the trade of furs, bone-ivory, and froststeel carried by caravans from the tundra.

Delver guilds stage expeditions into the Nattefrost from Severport, seeking relics entombed in the ice. Taverns here are filled with stories of frozen ruins and beasts with frostfire in their eyes. Though life is perilous, Severport embodies endurance—a city that stands at the very edge of the world and dares the cold to take it.

---

## **19. Tulivuofi** — _The Furnace of Ice_

**Region:** Oria  
**Known For:** Geothermal forges, volcanic rituals, lodge diplomacy

Nestled in a volcanic rift beneath the shadow of the Nattefrost, Tulivuofi thrives where fire and ice collide. Its forges are fueled by geothermal vents, producing weapons prized across Aerathon for their balance of heat and resilience.

Lodges gather here in uneasy diplomacy, as the volcanic heat offers neutral ground against Oria’s frostbitten feuds. Rituals of fire and ash mark its festivals, honoring both the forge and the mountain spirits. For delvers, Tulivuofi is a place of opportunity—contracts here are as much about forging alliances as steel.

---

## **20. Gocaire** — _Stone and Song_

**Region:** Allemance  
**Known For:** Cliff-carved amphitheaters, delver operas, stonecraft guilds

Gocaire rises from cliffs that plunge into the eastern sea, its streets and amphitheaters carved directly from the stone. The city is famed for its delver operas—lavish productions where entire labyrinth expeditions are reenacted in song and spectacle.

Stonecraft guilds also thrive here, their masons blending artistry and endurance to build structures that seem to sing when struck by the sea winds. Gocaire is less political than Louvain or Baritte, but no less influential—its operas and craftworks shape Aerathon’s culture, carrying the tales of delvers far and wide.
