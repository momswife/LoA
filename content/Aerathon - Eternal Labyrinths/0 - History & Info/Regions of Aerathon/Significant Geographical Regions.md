## **Overview**

Beyond the heartlands, guild-cities, and continental powers, Aerathon is shaped by singular regions that defy borders and resist settlement. These are not nations nor empires, but scars left by the Labyrinths, thresholds between planes, and sanctuaries of forgotten divinity. They are remembered in songs, avoided in trade routes, and etched into the annals of delver guilds as both opportunity and doom.

---

## **Monster Meadows** — _The Rift That Will Not Heal_

**Location:** Southern Allemance, near the Betemere Mountains and Quest River  
**Nature:** Aberrant scar & monster sanctuary  
**Known For:** Decadal eruptions, peaceful monster gatherings, Rift Watch towers

Monster Meadows is a paradox of Aerathon. To scholars of Louvain, it is the **Meadow of Monsters**—a strange sanctuary where intelligent, nonviolent creatures from conquered Labyrinths gather. These beings linger peacefully in the grasslands, defying expectation, as though drawn by some instinctual tether. No sage has yet explained why.

To delvers, however, it is a far darker name: the **Monster Meadows Rift**. Beneath the soil lies a Labyrinth scar that was never truly sealed. Once every few decades the Rift stirs, the ground quakes, and abominations spew forth in horrific waves—half-born horrors, stitched flesh-things, and aberrant swarms. Entire caravans are lost when the Rift awakens.

Villages cling stubbornly to the cursed fertility of the soil, their flocks often guarded by guild-trained hounds who bark at things unseen. Watchtowers of the Rift Watch guild ring the Meadows, their horns ready to call retreat when tremors shake the land.

To this day, the place holds two truths: a meadow where monsters seek peace, and a wound where monsters are born from chaos. Perhaps one is the shadow of the other, or perhaps both are the same mystery.

## **Verges of Ash** — _The Border of Three Worlds_

**Location:** Central Vinyot lowlands, between **Ruby Bay**, **Southwinds**, and the inland forests of **Wingsmeet**  
**Nature:** Volcanic planar bleed, confluence of Aerathon, the Feywilds, and the Netherworld  
**Known For:** Ash storms, fissures of ember, ghost markets, phasing villages

The Verges of Ash scar the otherwise fertile plains of Vinyot. To guild maps, they appear as a dark patch of volcanic rock and shifting fissures, bordered by settlements like **Ruby Bay** and **Southwinds** that thrive only by keeping distance. At the heart of the Verges, the soil burns with embers that never cool, and black glass plains stretch where farmland once stood.

The danger of the Verges lies not only in its eruptions, but in its planar bleed. Here, the Feywild and Netherworld press into Aerathon at once, flickering like lanternlight. Entire hamlets vanish overnight into the Fey, returning days later hollow-eyed from the Netherworld. Ghost markets appear unbidden on cracked crossroads, spectral merchants offering dream-forged steel or bones inscribed with impossible scripture.

Delvers risk the Verges for such relics, though guild contracts are heavily insured. Cultists whisper that the Convergence will spark here, where three worlds already kiss in ash and fire.

## **Throne of the Easterlilies** — _The Bloom Untouched_

**Location:** Island east of Vinyot, at the heart of the **Azur Gulf**  
**Nature:** Perpetual storm-clad isle, sacred and uninhabited  
**Known For:** Eternal lilies, hurricane gales, arcane anomalies

The Throne of the Easterlilies (sometimes called the **Throne of the Easterlies**) is the largest unsettled island in the Beast World. Its cliffs rise sheer from the sea, circled by furious trade winds and unending stormfronts that swirl in ceaseless rings about its shores. Even dragons are repelled by the gales, and spells of astral travel collapse when aimed at its coast.

Those few who have breached the storms describe a valley unlike any other: an endless field of lilies taller than mortals, untouched by frost or time. The air hums with silence, voices stolen, dreams pressed upon intruders until they collapse into sleep. It is said this meadow is the resting seat of a forgotten goddess of peace, her body dissolved into bloom.

Arcanists and sailors insist on different truths. To some, the Throne is the point of convergence for trade winds and Easterlies, the storm at the center of the Beast World’s weather. To others, it is a holy sanctuary beyond mortal reach, inviolate even to the Labyrinths. Both tales intertwine in the reverent superstition that surrounds the island.

The Throne remains unclaimed, unexplored, and inviolate. It is Aerathon’s last unbroken sanctuary—and perhaps its most dangerous mystery.

## **Uriah’s Wood** — _The Forest of Broken Hearts_

**Location:** Western Oria, at the base of the **Western Cradle** mountains  
**Nature:** Enchanted woodland, lair of the elder sorcerer Uriah  
**Known For:** Healing transformations, forgotten mansions, soul-cleansing rites

At the edge of Oria’s mountains lies a woodland unlike any other. Known as **Uriah’s Wood**, it is said to be the dwelling place of the elder sorcerer Uriah, though his exact mansion shifts like a mirage among the trees. Travelers speak of being “invited in,” though no path is ever the same twice, and none recall how they reached the sorcerer’s door.

The Wood attracts beasts and mortals alike whose hearts are broken, whose minds are restless, whose souls are weary. Within its borders, the troubled often find themselves transformed—beasts given back their quiet animal nature, mortals granted moments of clarity and purpose. Those who emerge claim to feel lighter, as though a burden has been lifted. And yet, no one remembers precisely what happened once they crossed the threshold of Uriah’s domain.

Some believe Uriah works a magic of renewal, unweaving sorrow and stitching the spirit whole again. Others whisper the Wood is a trap, the sorcerer feeding on fragments of despair offered freely by those who wander in. Whatever the truth, Uriah’s Wood remains one of the most feared and revered groves in Oria.

## **The Blackwild** — _The Forest Where Light Fails_

**Location:** The Bat’yan of Arneria, east of Fort Tawiran  
**Nature:** Demon-tainted rainforest scar  
**Known For:** Ring of False Blessings, whispers of demons, forbidden entry

Deep in the Bat’yan lies the Blackwild, a rainforest consumed by a curse of unknown origin. Here, light itself is strangled—shadows swallow entire groves, and whispers slither in the air, urging travelers to step deeper. No one who enters the true heart of the Blackwild returns unchanged; most never return at all.

Paladins of **Dramphine** guard its borders with absolute vigilance, for the Blackwild is one of the rare places in Aerathon where demons may touch the material world. They have raised a holy barrier to hold the darkness at bay, yet even this ring of protection is warped. Known as the **Ring of False Blessings**, it tempts travelers with impossible fruits, radiant birds that sing with a young woman’s voice, and visions of serenity. But those who accept its beauty vanish, claimed by the forest beyond.

To venture willingly into the Blackwild or its Ring is forbidden, punishable by detainment and interrogation by the paladin order. And yet, the tales of power, of whispered bargains and treasures stolen from demon realms, draw desperate delvers all the same.

The Blackwild grows each year, mile by mile, and the paladins can only hold their lines so long.

## **Symphony Canyon** — _The Song of Falling Waters_

**Location:** Alley Basin, south of Mansque and Fort Kingsfang  
**Nature:** River-carved canyon and bardic sanctuary  
**Known For:** Universal Symphony, Bardic Evermeet, waterfall confluences

At the heart of the Alley Basin lies **Symphony Canyon**, where three mighty rivers meet in a thunderous confluence of waterfalls. To those who row to the canyon’s center, the crash of water becomes something more—a cosmic harmony known as the **Universal Symphony**. This moment of immersion is said to reveal the music that binds the planes themselves, inspiring countless souls to pursue the bardic arts.

For centuries, the **Bardic Evermeet** has stood upon the canyon’s cliffs, a college where students study music, magic, and memory. Its halls echo with songs carried by mist and stone, and its masters teach that to understand the Symphony is to understand Aerathon’s pulse. Delvers who train here often bring instruments into the Labyrinths, hoping to harness the echoes of that greater harmony against chaos itself.

Locals claim the Symphony sometimes shifts—sounding joyous, sorrowful, or martial—foretelling events in distant lands. Whether divine omen or natural marvel, the waters sing still.

## **The Tempestrise** — _The Storm Belt of the Azur Gulf_

**Location:** Eastern Vinyot, along the cliffs south of **Porta Ventura** into **Malduta Bay**  
**Nature:** Storm-thick coastal belt, elemental convergence  
**Known For:** Tenfold storms, storm-diving festivals, stormborn shards

The **Tempestrise** is a stretch of storm-lashed cliffs where the Azur Gulf churns into Malduta Bay. Here, storms gather with a fury ten times greater than anywhere else in Aerathon—hurricanes that rip ships apart, lightning that strikes thrice in the same place, and gales that scream like living things. To sailors it is a graveyard, but to beastfolk it is spectacle and sacred rite.

The **aarakocra** claim the storms as holy, and each season they flock to the cliffs to hold storm-diving festivals. Young thrillseekers leap into the gales, testing their wings against elemental chaos, while crowds of beastfolk line the cliffs to watch, chant, and sing as thunder drowns their voices. Some divers return forever marked, their feathers glowing faintly with stormlight. Others vanish into the tempest, remembered in ballads of courage.

Delvers risk the Tempestrise not only for glory, but for its relics. Lightning sometimes crystallizes into **stormborn shards**, raw fragments of elemental power that hum with the storm’s voice. Chandelle, the storm-diver sorcerer, once claimed such a shard—and her legend has only magnified the Tempestrise’s allure.

For all its danger, the Tempestrise endures as one of Aerathon’s greatest natural theatres: a place where mortals gather not to fear the storm, but to revel in its song.

## **The Kasho’ta Caves** — _Where the Sea Sings Back_

**Location:** Isle of Jarik, northeast of Sampura  
**Nature:** Subterranean crystal caverns beneath tidal cliffs  
**Known For:** Singing stones, inverted reflections, tide-born beasts

Carved into the cliffs of **Jarik**, the Kasho’ta Caves are a labyrinth of crystal-lined caverns that stretch beneath the surf. When the tide crashes through the openings, the entire cave system becomes an instrument—the walls hum, the crystals ring, and the air resounds with unearthly harmonics. Sailors call it the **Sea’s Choir**, and bardic delvers claim the resonance can change a person’s heartbeat to match its rhythm.

Inside, strange phenomena abound:

- **Reflections** in the crystal pools move out of sync with their owners, smiling when the viewer does not.
    
- **Echo-beasts** formed from sound itself stalk the caves, hunting by rhythm rather than sight.
    
- **Inverted voices** sometimes answer spoken words, not in mimicry, but as though replying from another world.
    

The Feline clans of Al’ar treat the Kasho’ta as sacred. Initiates of bardic traditions must survive a night in the caves, listening to the Symphony of Tides. Those who emerge claim their voices carry a sliver of the caves’ song, granting them presence and charisma beyond measure. Some never emerge at all, their names carried forever in the cave’s haunting chorus.

For delvers, the Kasho’ta are both treasure and terror. The crystals resonate with stored sound—songs, screams, even spells—and can be harvested as **Resonant Shards**, dangerous artifacts that replay the moment of their recording with dangerous fidelity.

## **Nattefrost** — _The Crown of Ice_

**Location:** Northernmost Oria, beyond the Elkbrother Islands  
**Nature:** Frozen tundra, windswept permafrost  
**Known For:** Crystal-snow dunes, killing winds, survival rites

At the farthest edge of the world lies the **Nattefrost**, a land of eternal winter where mountains give way to a flat sheet of ice. Here, the soil never thaws, vegetation is nearly absent, and only the hardiest wildlife ekes out survival. Winds lash across the plains in shrieking gales, carrying tendrils of crystalline snow that carve the landscape into dunes of frozen glass.

The people of Oria regard the Nattefrost with both dread and reverence. Lodge traditions speak of it as the land where ancestors walk still, their voices carried on the winds to test the living. To survive a night on the plains without shelter is a rite of adulthood among some clans, marking one’s body with scars of frostbite and one’s spirit with the resolve of survival.

Yet the Nattefrost is not silent. Travelers tell of buried ruins revealed only when the snow dunes shift, their stones engraved with glyphs older than the gods. Some say the Labyrinths once opened here but froze mid-birth, leaving behind skeletal corridors of ice where echoes move without sources. Others whisper that the **Wanehollow** brushes closest in the north, making names themselves fragile in the frost.

## **The Dunes of the Sun Bull** — _Where Sand Bears Witness_

**Location:** Central Beylik desert, along the Causeway  
**Nature:** Shifting sacred dunes, living mirage phenomenon  
**Known For:** Pilgrimages, solar rites, mirage guardians

At the blazing heart of Arneria stretch the **Dunes of the Sun Bull**, a sea of golden sand said to move under its own will. By day, the dunes shimmer in unbearable heat, mirages swelling into shapes of charging beasts. By night, their slopes glow faintly, as if storing sunlight to whisper back in moonlight.

The Arnerian faithful believe the Dunes are where the **Sun Bull**, a divine herald of dawn, died after trampling back a host of demons in ages past. Each grain of sand is said to hold a drop of its blood. Pilgrims walk the Dunes barefoot in blistering heat, testing their endurance, for the faithful claim that to cross without water is to feel the Sun Bull’s breath in your lungs.

Delvers tell darker tales. Within the shifting sands are said to dwell **mirage guardians**—phantasmal bulls of glass and flame that strike at liars, oathbreakers, and cowards. Some believe the Dunes hide a sunken Labyrinth, its entrance sealed beneath miles of burning sand, the mirages merely its leaking guardians.
