## **Overview**

Tenebrines—raccoons and possums—thrive when the rest of the world sleeps. With nimble fingers, keen darkvision, and uncanny humor, these nocturnal folk claim the quiet hours as their own. Though often misunderstood, tenebrines share a rich subculture built on dusk’s edge and shadowed alleys. Cities breathe differently at night, and tenebrines say it’s then that a place reveals its true face.

The raccoons of the Beast World are known for mischief and mirth, often masking their brilliance behind a grin or a gag. Their fingers know locks and ledgers equally well, and their wit could light a torch. Possums, meanwhile, bring bulk and tenacity to the species—silent until suddenly impactful. Possums speak softly, sleep hard, and hit harder.

Together, they make up a proud, if often doubted, people. Others see thieves and tricksters. They see artists, pranksters, and protectors of secrets. Where suspicion casts shadows, tenebrines walk with grace and guile. They adapt, survive, and sometimes—just for fun—thrive.

## **Tenebrine Traits**

**Creature Type.** You are a Humanoid.

**Ability Score Increase.** Your Dexterity score increases by 2.

**Speed.** Your walking speed is 30 feet.

**Darkvision.** You can see in dim light within 60 feet as if it were bright, and in darkness as if it were dim. You can't discern color in darkness.

**Pristine-Blooded.** You are immune to all diseases. Additionally, a creature injected with at least 1 pint of your blood gains advantage on their next Constitution saving throw against disease in the next 24 hours. Donating this blood inflicts 1 level of exhaustion.

**Urbanite’s Fortune.** While in a city of 10,000 or more people, you can reroll one attack roll, ability check, or saving throw and must use the second result. You regain use after a long rest.

---

### **Raccoon**

**Ability Score Increase.** Your Charisma score increases by 1.

**Size.** You are Medium.

**Think with Your Hands.** By handling a key for 1 action, you gain advantage on Dexterity checks to unlock its associated lock for 7 days. You automatically succeed on any Dexterity check to open a lock you've previously unlocked this way.

---

### **Possum**

**Ability Score Increase.** Your Strength score increases by 1.

**Size.** You are Medium.

**Below the Belt.** When hidden and within 5 feet of a creature no more than one size larger, you may use a bonus action to force a Strength save (DC = your Athletics or Acrobatics check) or knock them prone. You deal +1d4 damage with melee weapon attacks against prone targets.

## **Homeland Traits**

### **Allemance**

Alley tenebrines walk a razor's edge between civility and crime. Some flourish as whisper brokers and forgery experts among the gentry's backrooms. Even the honest ones learn to impersonate and adapt, if only to avoid being stepped on by nobles' boots.

**Dittoed Rumors.** You gain proficiency with a forgery kit. When making a Charisma (Deception) check to impersonate someone, you are treated as proficient and double your proficiency bonus.

---

### **Oria**

In Oria's wind-bitten lodge houses, tenebrines survive by becoming invisible. Some are trained by the War Mages to dispatch threats discreetly, their weapons as silent as the falling snow. Others just learn to keep their head down and their tail still.

**Mosquito in the Crowd.** You are treated as proficient and double your proficiency bonus on Dexterity (Stealth) checks made to hide in crowds. Once per long rest, you may delay the effects of a dagger or shortsword strike made from hiding until the start of your next turn. Until then, the target remains unaware.

---

### **Vinyot**

In Vinyot, tenebrines own the night—quite literally. Mask-makers, street performers, and con artists thrive under moonlight and fireworks during the Night of the Veil. Some lean into the charm of illusion, others into its profit.

**Peer Misdirection.** You can throw your voice up to 60 feet. Once per long rest, if you fail a Dexterity (Sleight of Hand) check, you may add a bonus equal to the number of visible onlookers (max +5) to possibly succeed.

---

### **Arneria**

Across the jungle scaffolds and brick courtyards of the Causeway, tenebrines are heroes and heretics alike. Some sniff out spies for the crown; others whisper devilish chants behind locked doors. Arnerian tenebrines live under suspicion and celebration in equal measure.

**Stash Finder.** You gain advantage on Intelligence (Investigation) checks to locate hidden compartments in masonry. You are treated as proficient and double your proficiency bonus when hiding objects on your person using Sleight of Hand.

---

### **Al’Ar**

Among the crowded docks of Tempestat, tenebrines live in comfort or claw for it. Wealthy fishing families hoard secrets and silver alike, and even the poorest possum knows how to fake affluence when it counts. They’ve learned how to walk with swagger, whether they earned it or not.

**The Training of Privilege.** Choose one skill or tool proficiency you gained through early instruction. Once per creature, when convincing someone of your wealth, you may declare a roll result of 15 before rolling. First impressions only.
