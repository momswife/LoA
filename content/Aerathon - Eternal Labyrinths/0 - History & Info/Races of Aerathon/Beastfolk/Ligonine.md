## **Overview**

Most subspecies are grouped by the similarities of the animals they come from. Horses and donkeys call themselves equines. Laetines are comprised of otters and ferrets, both silky-furred, noodle-shaped creatures. Moles, armadillos, and sloths all claim a shared species as well: **ligonine** (LIG-oh-nyne). In this and so many other ways, they are the exception.

Ligonines live in the Beast World’s highest and lowest places. Some are reclusive and insular, others are chatty and curious. What they share are clawed fingers and unflagging weirdness. From the earliest days of the Beast World, their instinct was not to join kingdoms or cities—but to dig, climb, and vanish into the edges of the known world.

Sloths fled skyward, climbing into the Bat’yan rainforest canopy and choosing peace over conflict. Moles tunneled into the hills of Oria, and armadillos struck down through the heat-hardened earth of the Beylik. No shared language, no contact—until they found each other deep below. The joy of that moment remains legendary: two species meeting for the first time, instantly understanding they had been family all along.

The bond between ligonines is mythic. Despite wildly different upbringings and habitats, they declare themselves of the same blood with an unshakable certainty.

## **Ligonine Traits**

**Creature Type.** You are a Humanoid.

**Ability Score Increase.** Your Constitution score increases by 2.

**A Mind Outside the Box.** When you make an Intelligence, Wisdom, or Charisma check that doesn’t allow a specific skill, you are considered proficient.

---

### **Mole**

**Ability Score Increase.** Your Intelligence score increases by 1.

**Size.** You are Medium. Most moles stand between 4'0" and 4'8".

**Speed.** Your walking speed is 25 feet.

**Burrow.** You have a burrowing speed of 10 feet while using both hands.

**Darkvision.** You can see in dim light within 60 ft. as if it were bright light, and in darkness as if it were dim light. You can’t discern color in darkness.

**Soil Sense.** As a bonus action, gain tremorsense 15 ft. for 1 minute. You lose normal and darkvision sight while it lasts. Recharge after a short or long rest.

**Bottlebottoms.** You have disadvantage on sight-based Perception checks unless wearing spectacles crafted for you. While wearing them, you are proficient in such Perception checks. Crafting a pair requires 8 hours, 50 gp, and a DC 15 Dexterity (glassblower’s tools) check.

**Tunnel Sense.** Underground, you know the space’s dimensions and the shortest path to the surface. You know if water lies within 60 feet above you.

**Quick Fix.** You know the _mending_ cantrip. Intelligence is your spellcasting ability.

---

### **Armadillo**

**Ability Score Increase.** Your Strength score increases by 1.

**Size.** You are Medium. Most armadillos stand between 4'4" and 5'0".

**Speed.** Your walking speed is 35 feet.

**Natural Armor.** When not wearing armor, your AC is 14 + your Dexterity modifier. A shield can be used normally.

**Defensive Ball.** When you take the Dash, Disengage, or Dodge action, reduce nonmagical bludgeoning, piercing, and slashing damage by 3 until your next turn. When Dashing in a straight line, your speed is doubled.

---

### **Sloth**

**Ability Score Increase.** Your Wisdom score increases by 1.

**Size.** You are Medium. Most sloths stand between 5'9" and 7'1".

**Speed.** Your walking speed is 25 feet.

**Climb.** You have a climbing speed equal to your walking speed.

**A Mean Right Hook.** Your claws are natural weapons (1d6 + Str slashing damage).

**Long Arms.** Your unarmed strikes have +5 ft. reach.

**Slow and Steady.** If you didn’t move or use a bonus action or reaction since your last turn, your first Strength or Dexterity check or attack on your turn is made with advantage.

## **Homeland Traits**

### **Loamlink**

Most moles and armadillos—and a few adventurous sloths—live deep beneath the earth in the tunnel towns of the Loamlink. A ligonine raised here chooses a scholarly or practical field of focus during puberty, and often dedicates their life to it with fierce devotion. Their work defines them, as much as their name.

These ligonines are proud of their studies but famously private with their research. Even neighbors with near-identical projects often bicker over minuscule details. Outside scholars find them brilliant, but maddeningly unstandardized. The rise of Dungeon threats has begun to push Loamlink thinkers toward collaboration.

**Ligonine Focus.** Gain proficiency in one artisan’s tool and one skill (Arcana, History, Medicine, Nature, or Religion). After 24 hours of uninterrupted study and practice, roll two d20s and save the higher. In the next 7 days, you may replace one check using that tool or skill with that roll (before rolling normally). This feature can be used once per study.

---

### **Surface**

Surface-raised ligonines—especially those living among wolves or deer—have a reputation for intense curiosity and zero boundaries. They pop out of walls, drop from rooftops, and ask wildly inappropriate personal questions, all in the spirit of connection. Their shameless friendliness is matched only by their architectural brilliance.

While laetines engineer clever tools, ligonines build the foundations themselves. From fortified Orian lodge house vaults to the radiant Causeway of Arneria, ligonines leave their mark on the world through stone and survey.

**Stonemovers and Surveyors.** You can cast _floating disk_ once per long rest. The disk is 10 ft. wide, holds up to 1,000 pounds, and can cross gaps up to 20 feet wide (but cannot change elevation except via slope). Intelligence is your spellcasting ability. Additionally, when inside a building, you can sense the presence of any basement or tunnel within 300 feet below you.

---

### **Canopy**

High above the forest floor, ligonines of all kinds share the leaves with birds and bees. Sloths are the most common, but moles and armadillos also dwell here, drawn by serenity and sky. In the Bat’yan and the coastal Al’ari archipelago, ligonines live in tranquil tree-homes, content to watch the world from above.

Their lifestyle fosters introspection and flexibility. Many take up monastic traditions or acrobatic martial arts. Canopy ligonines are known for patience, peace, and sudden bursts of creativity—and the ease with which they can flip their friends onto safe branches.

**Dweller in Leaves.** You can see up to 1 mile with clarity, discerning details as if viewing from 100 ft away. You have proficiency in Acrobatics. If falling at least 10 ft. and there's a handhold, you may roll Dexterity (Acrobatics) vs. DC 12 + 1 per 10 ft fallen. On success, swing to travel that distance horizontally instead of taking fall damage.
