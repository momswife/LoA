## **Overview**

Tieflings in Aerathon are the blooded children of the old bargains. Long ago, when the portals tore through the world and the Labyrinths yawned open, mortals desperate for power made pacts with entities from the lower realms—fiends, archdevils, and things with too many mouths. The descendants of those bargains walk the world today as tieflings: living echoes of promises signed in blood.

Despite the distant origin of their infernal heritage, tieflings are fully mortal and fully people. They bear horns, tails, and other fiendish traits, but none of that defines their soul. In the eyes of many, however, they are walking omens—symbols of old evils and unpredictable powers. Tieflings are often treated with suspicion or reverence, depending on the local culture. Some see them as cursed. Others see them as blessed with insight into the arcane or forbidden.

In the world of delving, tieflings thrive. The Labyrinths do not care for lineage or public opinion—only results. Tieflings often lead their crews into cursed zones, weaving shadow and fire with equal grace. Some lean into their heritage, making masks of menace. Others defy it, their paths ones of healing, kindness, or redemption.

Their connection to infernal power is more than symbolic. Some tieflings feel their ancestors’ whispers echo in their dreams. Others have inherited fragments of magic that bypass study or training altogether. And more than a few have fiendish patrons still keeping watch, eager to steer their bloodline toward greater ambition.

## **Heritage Traits**

### **Allemance**

In Allemagnian high society, tieflings are a scandalous novelty. Nobles whisper of diabolic bloodlines and cursed births, but many secretly invite tieflings to court for their flair and drama. Some serve as magical advisors or entertainers, carefully walking the line between suspicion and prestige.

**Court's Curiosity.** You gain proficiency in Deception. Additionally, when a creature targets you with a divination spell or ability, you can use your reaction to obscure your aura—imposing disadvantage on any Insight or Arcana check related to it. Once you use this feature, you can’t do so again until you finish a long rest.

### **Oria**

Oric tieflings often live in isolation or in close-knit cabals. The mountains remember ancient sins, and many Oric clans view tieflings as cursed. To survive, they’ve developed endurance, resolve, and their own esoteric traditions.

**Mountain Brand.** You gain resistance to cold damage. Additionally, when you take fire or cold damage, you can use your reaction to ignore difficult terrain until the end of your next turn.

### **Vinyot**

In the southern cities of Vinyot, tieflings wear masks—sometimes literal, often social. They are spies, illusionists, and deal-brokers in the arcane black markets. The labyrinths of Vinyot's underworld are filled with fire-eyed smiles and whispered names.

**Masked and Known.** You gain proficiency in Sleight of Hand or Stealth. When you are hidden from a creature and cast a spell, you do not reveal your location until the spell's effects are resolved.

### **Arneria**

The Beylik has a complicated relationship with tieflings. In the Bey’s tales, fiends are villains—but tieflings are often cast as clever heroes who outwit their ancestors. Arnerian tieflings are quick of tongue and rich in story.

**Tongue of the Clever.** You can cast _disguise self_ once per long rest. Additionally, you can add half your proficiency bonus (rounded down) to any Charisma-based skill in which you are not already proficient.

### **Al'Ar**

On the Feline Isles, tieflings are rare but respected. Their appearance is interpreted as a spiritual burden to be carried and overcome. Many join monastic or religious orders that help them 'temper the fire within'—though whether this is metaphor or magic varies.

**Island Flame.** You gain proficiency in Insight. Additionally, when you are reduced to 0 hit points but not killed outright, you can use your reaction to unleash a burst of fire. Each creature within 5 feet of you takes fire damage equal to your level. Once you use this trait, you must finish a long rest before using it again.
