## **Overview**

**Togi the Wagerblade** is an S-Class delver, known across Aerathon not for solemn vows or guild loyalty—but for **gambling with death and winning**. A human samurai hailing from the mist-isles of Al’ar, Togi was once infamous for losing entire fortunes at gaming dens, relic markets, and tavern-side dice circles. After being exiled from three guilds for “financial recklessness,” Togi began placing bets **on his own survival inside Labyrinths**—often publicly, sometimes absurdly.

His most legendary wagers? “I’ll fight every mimic on this floor,” “I’ll disarm a rune-trap blindfolded,” and “I bet my soul I parry the first blow.”  
He won them all.

Some believe Togi fights alongside a spirit—an ancestral duelist who guides his blade from beyond the veil. Others say luck simply kneels for fools bold enough to bet everything.

## **Notable Accomplishments**

**The Mirror Duel at Hollowglass Bastion**  
Togi once bet he could best a mirrored copy of himself summoned by the Labyrinth’s mimic curse. He fought blindfolded, refusing to strike until the illusion made a mistake. It did. Once.

**The Demon’s Dice at Crimson Hollow**  
While exploring a breach held by a dueling fiend, Togi wagered his own **name** for one-on-one combat. After slaying the creature, he walked away with a cursed coin that re-rolls any death save once per delve. He’s never used it.

**The Hundred Hands Bout**  
When 100 summoned duelists emerged in a cursed challenge chamber, Togi bet the labyrinth he could defeat them without breaking a single scabbard strap. It took two hours. He never lost grip.

## **Equipment and Artifacts**

**Kishou no Kaze** _(“Wind of the Last Bet”)_  
A fine-edged katana with a pearl-white blade and obsidian guard. It sings when unsheathed during a wagered duel. Occasionally parries **before Togi moves.** The blade is bound to an unknown ancestral pact.

**The Coin of Crimson Odds**  
A hellmarked copper piece earned from a demon duel. Once per delve, can flip to either reroll fate or **force** an enemy to reroll a critical success.

**Dice of the Forgotten Game**  
Six-sided ivory dice etched with lost runes. When thrown before a battle, they alter the field in a minor but theatrical way—fog, light breeze, ghost applause, etc. Togi uses them for “drama.”
