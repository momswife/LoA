## **Overview**

**Skarn the Emberborn** is an S-Class delver and the first known Beastfolk of ursine lineage to ascend to legendary rank without formal guild backing. Towering and covered in coarse, flame-scarred fur, Skarn emerged during the final year of the Chainscour Revolts, wielding a relic-hammer formed from the bones of a fire elemental. Rumor claims he was once a slave-gladiator kept beneath Varnel’s east arena, surviving thirty-nine matches before his disappearance and the arena’s subsequent burning.

Skarn now roams with no allegiance, no scribe, and no desire for fame. He accepts no livecasts. He speaks only through combat. Yet his name appears etched in scorched stone at the entrances of at least five high-tier Labyrinths—each one since sealed, scorched, or swallowed whole.

## **Notable Accomplishments**

**The Burning of Chainscour Hall**  
When a Delver tribunal ordered his death by imprisonment, Skarn allegedly escaped through burning the entire structure to the ground—freeing over seventy indentured delvers in the process. Survivors say he spoke only one phrase before leaving: “No more prisons.”

**The Trial of Shale-Tooth Crag**  
A rare filmed duel where Skarn faced seven bounty-marked delvers from the Reavers in open challenge. He did not use his hammer once. All seven were rendered unconscious with his bare hands and thrown from the cliffside. No deaths. Only shame.

**The Silence of Brimvault**  
An S-tier Labyrinth known for producing psychic rot and memory traps went completely silent after Skarn entered. Months later, a farming village five miles east reported waking to find **the Labyrinth core placed in their well**, sealed in scorched iron, and whispering the word _“freedom.”_

## **Equipment and Artifacts**

**Ashmaul, the Flame-Twin Hammer**  
Crafted from firebone and smoke-tempered iron. Can channel internal heat into concussive flame bursts. Emits an aura that sears falsehoods and weakens bindings.

**Mantle of the Burnt Howl**  
A cloak of chained ash-wolf hides. Grants resistance to fear and compels nearby slavers or enchanters to involuntarily speak their crimes.

**The Emberbrand**  
A glowing sigil permanently etched across his chest—said to burn brighter when near divine tyranny, cursed relics, or bound souls.
