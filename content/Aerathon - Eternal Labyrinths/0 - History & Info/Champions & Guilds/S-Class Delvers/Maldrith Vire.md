## Overview

**Maldrith Vire** is an S-Class delver and high necromantic strategist responsible for multiple delver massacres, labyrinth desecrations, and known attempts to weaponize soulstuff against both divine and planar targets. Formerly a **Wizard of the School of Necromancy**, Maldrith’s methods have outgrown any arcane discipline, delving now into what scholars call _Oblivioncraft_—the deliberate reshaping of undeath to erase identity, time, and will.

Despite being exiled by every major magical order, and marked for purging by five churches, Maldrith continues to participate in high-level delves—invited not by guilds, but by dark factions, corrupt nobles, and entities who have no stake in the survival of Aerathon’s people.

## Notable Atrocities

**The Zevrin Monastery Collapse**  
Vire entered the 400-year-old spiritual retreat under the guise of sealing a cursed artifact. He instead unbound the crypt-souls beneath it, fusing them into a single shrieking entity. The monastery imploded inward. No survivors.

**The Thirteenth Floor Incident**  
In a multi-party delve within the Labyrinth of Drowned Echoes, Maldrith betrayed three allied teams by summoning a **necrotic time-rift** that aged them into dust. The labyrinth spontaneously generated a new floor, now permanently haunted. His words before disappearing: “I needed silence.”

**The Vivisection of Maestar Brelm**  
When challenged by a high-ranking archivist during a tribunal, Vire resurrected the man mid-sentence and forced his reanimated body to recite a written confession of loyalty. This was broadcast to six cities. The corpse remained animate for twelve days before disintegrating.

## Equipment and Artifacts

**Scripture of Hollow Tongues**  
A necrogrimoire bound in melted crownmetal and stitched faces. Reading from it allows communication with severed souls. Prolonged reading begins to _reverse_ the reader’s memories.

**Soulbrand Circlet**  
A broken halo forged from the ashes of angelic relics. Grants immunity to radiant damage and compels celestials to speak truthfully within 10 feet—if they survive the proximity.

**Wand of the Last Kin**  
A gnarled focus carved from the bones of Vire’s own bloodline. Each charge spent burns a name from his family tree. It is nearly spent.
