## **Overview**

**Kurk of the Loose Rope**, an S-Class delver born in gutters and raised by grave dust, was a wandering monk-thief who became the _unlikely_ hand of Kelemvor, god of death and balance. Wielding humor like a dagger and silence like a prayer, Kurk was best known for his irreverent attitude, uncanny stealth, and deep—if unspoken—faith. He was the type to crack a joke before disarming a death curse, or to sneak into your pack just to rearrange your gear alphabetically “for fun.”

Despite his trickster nature, Kurk followed a strict personal code: **no soul left behind, no death without judgment.** He was often found ferrying the dying out of collapsing Labyrinths, leaving behind only his smudged coin-mark and a single copper on the tongue of the slain.

Delvers still whisper of him slipping through walls, stealing the breath from curses, or tricking necromancers into undoing their own magic with nothing but riddles and smirks.

## **Notable Accomplishments**

**The Silence of Bonebridge**  
An undead lord had cursed a flooded catacomb to trap souls in unending whispers. Kurk dove in alone, reemerging three days later with the lord’s phylactery swapped out for a coin pouch. The whispers stopped. When asked how, he winked and said, “He bet double or nothing.”

**The Wake of Hollow’s Gate**  
In a delve plagued by vengeful spirits, Kurk held vigil over the dead without food, water, or speaking for seven days—escorting their spirits with prayer, jokes, and whispered lullabies. The haunting ceased. A ghost was later recorded saying, _“He made us feel… remembered.”_

**The Pocket Pick of the Pale One**  
While disguised as a jester at a cursed noble gala, Kurk reportedly “borrowed” a soulstone from a death-pact warlock mid-toast. It exploded in moonlight, freeing the bound soul. Kurk left a handwritten receipt: _“One soul. No interest.”_

## **Equipment and Artifacts**

**The Grey Knot Sash**  
A threadbare belt tied with thirteen loops, one for each soul he failed to save. Grants shadowstep abilities, mistmeld dodges, and limited ghostwalk movement when used to “tie off” reality for a moment.

**Monk’s Vow (Coinblade Tonfa)**  
Forged from melted grave coins and funeral bells, this pair of tonfa crackle with necrotic and radiant energy. Can redirect death effects once per long rest by spinning them like prayer wheels.

**The Last Laugh Mask**  
A porcelain half-mask with an eternal smirk. When worn, Kurk’s laughter can disrupt charm and fear effects. It’s said those who die hearing it are ferried to the afterlife smiling.
