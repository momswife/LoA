## **Overview**

Not all treasures of the labyrinths are forged in steel or shaped by gods. Some are whispered. Some are written in tongues dead before the world breathed. Some appear only when the moons align in unrecorded constellations. This page catalogues **arcane knowledge without precedent**—scrolls, pacts, glyphs, and phenomena pulled from the depths of Aerathon’s labyrinths that defy present magical theory.

Though studied by the Arcanet Core, many of these entries remain **incompletely translated**, **unstable in effect**, or **unrepeatable by modern mages**. Use of these materials is restricted by Accord 7:11 of the MDO Doctrine on Pre-Sigilic Arts.

---

## **Classifications**

|Classification|Meaning|
|---|---|
|_Sigil-Broken_|Glyphs present but disconnected from known syntax|
|_Wildform Script_|Scroll form unknown; reacts with living magic|
|_Unclaimed Pact_|Contains pact-form language with no identified entity|
|_Fleshbound Scroll_|Bound to material other than paper or hide|
|_One-Time Ravel_|Activates once; unrepeatable or self-consuming|

## **Known Entries**

### _Scroll of the Drowned Oath_

- **Classification:** Unclaimed Pact
    
- **Description:** Ink smeared and reforms hourly into oceanic runes. Recitation causes the speaker’s voice to drown beneath the sound of waves.
    
- **Effect (Observed):** Summons a spectral shipwreck for 10 minutes. Anything within its bounds resists fire and lightning.
    
- **Origin:** _Labyrinth of the Salt-Rot Vaults_
    
- **Notes:** Pact entity has not answered further callings. Likely a singular bargain.
    

---

### _The Farspeech Fragment_

- **Classification:** Sigil-Broken
    
- **Description:** A triangular shard of obsidian etched with incomplete runes. Emits inaudible tones at irregular intervals.
    
- **Effect (Observed):** When held during spellcasting, allows _Sending_ to a random recipient across planes.
    
- **Origin:** _Labyrinth of the Void Choir_
    
- **Notes:** One incident involved a response from an unknown creature calling itself “The Listener Beneath Iron.”
    

---

### _The Flesh Script of Varn_

- **Classification:** Fleshbound Scroll
    
- **Description:** A living strip of pale flesh branded with moving tattoos. Screams faintly when damaged.
    
- **Effect (Observed):** Grants access to a warlock invocation not known to any patron catalog. “Varn’s Memory-Latch” allows retention of an additional spell after long rest.
    
- **Origin:** _Labyrinth of the Bonehold Sanctum_
    
- **Notes:** Disappeared from containment chamber three years ago. Tracked to the Black Alcove Markets.
    

---

### _Song of the Pale Hearth_

- **Classification:** One-Time Ravel
    
- **Description:** A scroll with no visible ink unless read by firelight.
    
- **Effect (Observed):** Heals all creatures within 60 ft for 20 hit points and permanently instills a shared dream memory of a hearth-fire scene.
    
- **Origin:** _Labyrinth of the Emberdream Nest_
    
- **Notes:** Scroll self-immolated post-casting. Surviving dream-memory bearers refer to each other as “cousin” instinctively.
    

---

### _The Writ of Nine Doors_

- **Classification:** Wildform Script
    
- **Description:** A scroll with no discernible writing that unfolds differently each time opened.
    
- **Effect (Observed):** Creates temporary doorways to previously visited locations. Locations do not require planar proximity.
    
- **Origin:** _Labyrinth of the Unbound Maze_
    
- **Notes:** Current theory suggests the scroll feeds on _intention_ more than knowledge. Scroll requires one week of "rest" between openings.
    

---

## **Observational Concerns**

- Several spells found in these scrolls appear to **replace existing ones in a caster’s memory**, rather than coexisting.
    
- Scrolls and fragments often bear **latent sentience**, occasionally altering shape or form when observed by certain races or classes.
    
- Unclaimed Pacts are strictly forbidden to be entered without Oculi Witnessing Rite—violations have resulted in **possession**, **language loss**, or **full ego erosion**.
