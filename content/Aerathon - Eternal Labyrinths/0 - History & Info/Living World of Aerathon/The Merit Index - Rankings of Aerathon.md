___
In the realm of Aerathon, adventurers are ranked through the **Unified Delving Merit Index (UDMI)**—a magically enhanced scoring system broadcasted in real time to the realm’s populace via scrying orbs, projection crystals, and arcane “echo-mirrors.” These ranks determine prestige, contract opportunities, guild sponsorship, and even national influence.

**The Rank Spread:**
- **S-Rank** – Legendary Elites _(Top 0.1%)_
    
- **A-Rank** – Champions _(Top 5%)_
    
- **B-Rank** – Seasoned Veterans _(Top 30%)_
    
- **C-Rank** – Standard Licensed Delvers _(Majority of active adventurers)_
    
- **D-Rank** – Novices or Probationary Members _(Unproven or penalized)_

___
Each adventurer is assigned a **UDMI Score** (0–10,000) which is updated dynamically. This score is calculated by the **Arcanet Core**, an ancient labyrinth-born sentience bound to a network of magical tracking artifacts.

#### **Factors That Influence UDMI Score**

|Category|Weight|Description|
|---|---|---|
|**Labyrinth Progression**|30%|Floors cleared, bosses defeated, anomalies resolved.|
|**Audience Favor**|20%|Livecast reactions, favor votes, magical donations, and “Boon Throws.”|
|**Team Synergy Score**|15%|Measured by coordination, survival rates, and strategic excellence.|
|**Combat Efficiency**|15%|Kill/death ratio, damage dealt vs. taken, use of resources.|
|**Artifact Recovery**|10%|Quality and rarity of artifacts extracted.|
|**Mission Integrity**|5%|Upholding contracts, completing objectives without betrayal.|
|**Penalties & Infractions**|-5%+|Penalized for killing civilians, sabotage, or breaking pact laws.|

#### **UDMI Point Accrual**

The **Unified Delving Merit Index (UDMI)** is updated passively through **automated magical calculation** _and_ through **official trials** for leaps in rank (A-Rank and higher).
#### Here's the breakdown:

| **Activity**                    | **UDMI Example Gain** |
| ------------------------------- | --------------------- |
| Clearing a Labyrinth Floor      | +250–1000             |
| Defeating a Boss                | +500–1500             |
| Discovering Rare Artifact       | +300–800              |
| Audience Boon Favor (+ votes)   | +10–500 per encounter |
| Solving a Major Puzzle          | +200–600              |
| Saving Civilians or Allies      | +100–500              |
| Creative Problem Solving        | +100–300              |
| Taking Heavy Damage & Surviving | +50–200               |
✦ A wizard who singlehandedly disables a collapsing bridge trap might gain +400 points.  
✦ A bard who turns a crowd’s fear into thunderous support might gain +350.  
✦ A rogue who saves a downed party member and finishes a boss earns heroism bonuses.

___

### **Rank Ascension Trials**

When an adventurer’s UDMI breaches a rank threshold (B ➝ A, A ➝ S), they are summoned by the **Ranking Arbiters** or may choose to issue a **Challenge Writ** to take an **Ascension Trial**.

These trials are similar to **epic level-up events or tier shifts** in D&D.

|**Rank Ascension**|**Trial Type**|**D&D Comparison**|
|---|---|---|
|C ➝ B|Automatic|Level 5 milestone (2nd tier powers)|
|B ➝ A|Trial|Level 11 milestone (3rd tier & subclasses)|
|A ➝ S|Legendary Trial|Level 17+ (epic tier / godlike renown)|
**Trial Examples**:

- Survive a floor solo.
    
- Retrieve a relic under a time limit.
    
- Defeat a ranked enemy under live audience observation.
    
- Solve a planar riddle presented by a Dreaming Oracle.
    
- Endure a "Judgment Duel" versus another A-Ranker for public favor.
