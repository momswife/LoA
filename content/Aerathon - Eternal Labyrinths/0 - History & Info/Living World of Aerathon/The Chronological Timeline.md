## **The First Era — Age of Crowns**

_The Reign of Bloodlines and the Birth of Law_

The Age of Crowns laid the bedrock of Aerathon’s society. Human and dwarven nobles secured dominance through the Twelve Kingdoms Pact, crafting monarchies defined by divine right and magical inheritance. Other races—beastfolk, orcs, goblinoids, tieflings—were declared “lesser” under the law. Elves withdrew to ancient glades, severing diplomatic ties. Magic was regulated and licensed only through noble blood.

- The Twelve Kingdoms Pact creates official borders and titles, favoring humans and dwarves.
    
- Elves seal their realms with magical wards, severing all trade and travel.
    
- Sub-Civilization Laws pass, barring certain races from owning land or joining councils.
    
- Arcane Licensing begins—only those with approved bloodlines may study magic.
    
- Beastfolk are relocated to lowland labor zones under “protection clauses.”
    
- Founding of the High Council of Flame to regulate arcane practices and enforce mage laws.
    
- Anti-mixing policies are introduced to preserve “blood clarity.”

## **The Second Era — Age of Chains**

_Law Refined. Voices Bound._

This era perfected oppression under the guise of order. Legal systems were refined to become more insidious, embedding social hierarchies into education, trade, and labor. The College of Law and Light oversaw a continent-wide push to erase cultural memory, while revolts were systematically crushed or quietly vanished. Order reigned—not through strength, but silence.

- The College of Law and Light publishes the Codex of Harmony, redefining racial roles.
    
- Brandmark sigils enforced for beastfolk, goblinoids, and tieflings across all trade roads.
    
- Mixed-race children denied inheritance, guild access, or temple rites.
    
- Cultural memory suppression campaigns begin in conquered regions.
    
- Goblinoid mine strikes end with arcane flooding and mass disappearances.
    
- The Arcane Clarity Act forbids non-nobles from enrolling in magical colleges.
    
- “Integration Monasteries” open to forcibly re-educate beastfolk youth.
    
- Changelings and tieflings banned from clergy or public speaking roles.

## **The Third Era — The Ember Age**

_Sparks in the Dust. Secrets in Song._

The world began to smolder with quiet resistance. Merchant houses gained wealth independent of noble favor. Pamphlets and songs carried hidden messages. Protests turned violent. In the distance, something ancient stirred: a strange door of silver geometry opened in a ruined city—**the Labyrinth of Echoes**. The ruling elite tried to suppress it. But the world had seen too much to forget.

- Blackmarket printing presses distribute banned writings in city underbellies.
    
- The Crimson Dagger Riots erupt after public executions of mixed-ancestry citizens.
    
- Elven rebels in Moonshade begin smuggling changelings into their hidden schools.
    
- The Gutterfire Rebellion burns an entire noble district to ash.
    
- The Labyrinth of Echoes opens briefly in the ruins of Cael—its geometry impossible to map.
    
- Witnesses to the Echoes event begin vanishing from record.
    
- Rebel enclaves secretly form around hidden leyline nexuses.
    
- The first Delver bands form, though outlawed by every crown.

## **The Fourth Era — Age of Collapse**

_Thrones Shatter. The World Fractures._

This was an age of endings. Governments fell, cities turned on themselves, and the power of bloodlines evaporated in fire and fear. As the Echoes Labyrinth reopened, other anomalies followed. One in the north—**the Violet Hold**—twisted frost and time. Another, in the midlands—**the Black Orchard**—_grew_, consuming land like a spreading garden of mirrors. Old laws burned. New forces took root.

- The War of the Seven Masks decimates five noble dynasties and collapses the House of Ethros.
    
- Moonshade Elves split into two factions: the Isolationist Bloom and the Integrationist Crescent.
    
- The Chainscour Courts rise, replacing monarchs with rotating citizen tribunals.
    
- Guild-backed militias seize three cities after merchant lords declare independence.
    
- The Violet Hold appears in the fjords, freezing magic and memory within.
    
- The Black Orchard consumes entire villages, altering the terrain and echoing back names.
    
- Mass desertion of royal armies occurs after soldiers begin dreaming of the Labyrinths.
    
- Delver-led rebellions sack four arcane universities tied to the High Council of Flame.

## **The Fifth Era — Age of Delving**

_Merit Ascends. Glory Guides._

Out of the rubble, new power emerged—Delvers. They were lawless, raceless, chosen only by courage and ability. The Delver Guilds replaced ambassadors, merchants, even kings in some regions. Where thrones once ruled, rankings did. A relic found was worth more than a treaty signed. The Labyrinths gave power to the bold, and the bold gave rise to a new world order.

- The Free Guild Accords are signed, recognizing Delver companies as neutral sovereign actors.
    
- The Guild-State of Halvask forms, governed by ranked Delvers instead of nobility.
    
- Noble houses dissolve or rebrand as relic sponsors or guild financiers.
    
- Public Eye scrying spells allow the world to watch Delves in real-time.
    
- Racial and class restrictions are lifted within Guild territories.
    
- New Labyrinth fragments appear, though most disappear as quickly as they came.
    
- Relics recovered from the Violet Hold allow limited dreamwalking and short-term time reversal.
    
- Delvers become celebrities, and guilds develop internal laws that rival kingdoms.

## **The Sixth Era — Age of Spectacle**

_The World Watches. The Labyrinths Respond._

This era saw the world transform from survival to performance. Delving became a sport, a profession, a faith. Adventurers competed for fame, for relics, for live audiences. Races fought side by side under unified banners. But as the world celebrated, something changed. The Labyrinths began appearing not in ruins—but in cities. One of them, **The Nameless Spiral**, did not grow like the others. It _replaced_ a district of Halvask overnight.

- Delvers are now ranked globally through merit, kills, survival time, and crowd voting.
    
- Wagers and magical bets become the largest economic engine on the continent.
    
- The Labyrinth of Echoes begins _shifting_, altering based on who enters.
    
- The Nameless Spiral appears inside Halvask, consuming Velgrove Square without warning.
    
- The Guild-State proposes the Spiral Accord to research sentient labyrinth behavior.
    
- Memory interference increases near Labyrinths—some Delvers return altered or rewritten.
    
- The Order of the Last Bell forms, claiming the Labyrinths are testing the soul of the world.
    
- Civil unrest begins to rise in outer territories, believing the Guilds have grown too powerful.
