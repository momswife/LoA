# 🜓 **FIELD NOTES ON THE GILDED HEART**

_Found scrawled on a torn scrap of vellum outside the vault._

---

### **THE FLOOR OF COINS**

If one were to look down, they might notice the gold does not sit still.  
It shifts in slow breaths, as though listening.  
One could stand firm…  
or be swallowed if they do not respect the movement beneath their feet.

Should someone test their balance here, they may find the floor gives way more easily than expected.

---

### **THE FOUR COLD PILLARS**

If one were to run their hand along the pillars, they would feel a chill unlike anything else in this chamber.  
Gold avoids them.  
Even the heat recoils.

A clever delver may wonder why something so cold stands unyielding among such molten wealth.  
Or why the guardian’s gaze lingers there only briefly.

---

### **THE FALSE SKY ABOVE**

If one were to tilt their head upward, they might see that the sky is not a sky at all.  
Panels of painted glass hide bright lights — brighter than torch or sun.  
The construct beneath seems to stir when the light intensifies…  
and slacken when shadow crosses its form.

Those who experiment with light may find surprising results.

---

### **THE KNEELING STATUES**

If one’s eye lingers on the golden statues, they may notice the cracks around their joints — stone peaks through gold, like old bone through gilded armor.  
Their posture is one of reverence, yet tension.  
As if waiting to rise again.

Should someone push their weight against them… they may find the stone within less forgiving than the gold without.

---

### **THE SHATTERED MIRROR**

If one searches carefully, they may come across a relic half-buried in the hoard — a broken mirror that reflects not faces, but light itself.  
The shard feels warm in the hand.  
Some say it hums faintly when held toward the false sky.

If one were to experiment with its angle, they might discover something curious about the chamber’s illumination.


---

### **THE ROOM’S PURPOSE**

If one were to pause and truly look —  
at the pillars,  
at the way the lights fall,  
at the cold veins of stone beneath gold,  
and the broken mirror’s hum —  
they might sense that this chamber was not built for slaughter.  
But for testing.

# 🜓 **BOSS STATS**

**CR 10 (5,900 XP)**  
**Large Construct–Undead (Beastfolk Relic)**  
**Alignment:** Lawful Neutral  
**Role:** Radiant bruiser / arena boss with environmental support and puzzle mechanics

---

## **STAT BLOCK (DM SUMMARY)**

### **Defenses**

- **AC:** 17 (Auric Plating)
    
- **HP:** 190 (20d10+80)
    
- **Speed:** 40 ft.
    
- **Saves:** CON +8, WIS +6
    
- **Resistances:** Nonmagical B/P/S
    
- **Immunities:** Fire, Poison, Charmed, Frightened, Paralyzed
    
- **Senses:** Darkvision 120 ft., Tremorsense 30 ft.
    
- **Languages:** Understands Common & Beast-tongue; speech = echoing telepathic resonance
    
- **Weaknesses:** Radiant or thunder _after dome panels break_; Beastfolk suppress its regen
    

---

## **Passive / Always-On Abilities**

### ⭐ **Hoard-Bound Vitality**

At the start of its turn, the Sentinel heals **10 HP** while standing on treasure worth 500+ gp.  

**Suppressed by:**

- Any Beastfolk attack
    
- Broken or dimmed lights in the dome
    
- Reflected radiant attacks via the Mirror Technique
    


---

## **Actions**

### ⭐ **Multiattack — “Gilded Claws” (x2)**

**To Hit:** +8  
**Damage:** 2d10+4 bludgeoning + 2d6 radiant  
On hit: Coins lash outward for **+3 slashing damage** to adjacent creatures.

---

### ⭐ **Voice of Tharos (Recharge 5–6)**

The Sentinel emits a radiant roar.

- **30-ft radius**, all creatures
    
- **DC 16 WIS save**
    
- Fail:
    
    - **27 (6d8) radiant damage**
        
    - **Blinded** until end of next turn
        
    

This is the move you want players to consider **reflecting** with the mirror shard.

---

### ⭐ **Gilded Slam**

The Sentinel slams the ground, sending molten gold rippling outward.

- **15-ft radius**
    
- **DC 15 DEX save**
    
- **3d8 fire damage** + knocked prone on fail
    
- If used within 10 ft of a pillar → **pillar cracks** or collapses (DM choice)
    

---

## **Legendary Actions (2 per round)**

### ⭐ **Golden Step (Costs 1)**

Teleport 15 ft. to a space of treasure.  
Creatures adjacent to its starting location take **1d10 fire damage**.

### ⭐ **Coinstorm (Costs 1)**

15-ft radius flurry of razor coins.

- DC 15 DEX save
    
- **2d8 slashing damage**
    

### ⭐ **Judgment Stare (Costs 2)**

Targets one creature within 30 ft.

- DC 15 CHA save
    
- On fail: **2d6 psychic damage** and disadvantage on next saving throw.

# **Expected Behavior / Boss Patterns**

This is the “flow” of the fight as intended:

---

## **Opening (Round 1–2)**

- Sentinel rises from hoard → **Gilded Slam** to scatter delvers.
    
- Statues activate and **Bodyguard Charge** to protect boss.
    
- Dome lights brighten → radiant aura intensifies.
    
- Sentinel closes distance to punish frontliners.
    

He tests the party’s **stance**, behavior, and greed.

---

## **Mid-Fight (Round 3–5)**

- Sentinel begins using **Voice of Tharos** aggressively.
    
- Players with the mirror may notice the angle of light reacting — this is their clue.
    
- Golden Step to reposition behind treasure dunes.
    
- Statues attempt to maintain Shielding Stance.
    

During this phase:  
**Players realize brute force is possible but costly.  
Environmental interaction becomes valuable.**

---

## **Turning Point (When Dome Panels Begin Breaking)**

Once players start reflecting radiant attacks or toppling pillars:

- Sentinel’s regeneration stops.
    
- Auras dim.
    
- AC effectively drops as Shielding Stance is disrupted.
    
- Sentinel becomes **increasingly frantic** in movement and attacks.
    

He begins attacking **glass panels or mirror holders**, trying to restore his light.

---

## **Weakened State (Final 25% HP)**

- Sentinel cracks, molten gold leaks from joints.
    
- Golden Step costs him damage (fire bursts on himself and others).
    
- Coinstorm triggers more often.
    
- The hoard beneath him becomes unstable — safe zones form near pillars.
    

This is the cinematic “finish him” moment.

---

## **Behavioral Flavor / Personality in Combat**
    
- He prioritizes:
    
    1. Those who touch treasure disrespectfully
        
    2. Spellcasters who threaten the dome
        
    3. Anyone holding the mirror shard
        
    4. Non-Beastfolk


# **Honor-Statues (Add-Ons)**

Two Large golden statues activate when the Sentinel awakens.

### **Quick Stats**

- AC 16
    
- HP 55
    
- Resist B/P/S
    
- Stone inside the joints (weakness)
    

### **Abilities**

**Shielding Stance:** Within 10 ft of Sentinel → it gains +2 AC.

**Bodyguard Charge:** As reaction, move 20 ft to interpose on incoming spell or range attack aimed at the Sentinel.

**Melting Burst (Recharge 4–6):**  
15-ft cone, 3d6 fire, Dex 13 half.

**Weakness:**  
If shoved into a stone pillar → **Stunned for 1 round** and loses an arm (reducing damage output).

## **1. WHEN THEY FIRST AWAKEN**

As the Golden Sentinel rises, the statues crack and glow.  
Their stone joints grind, shedding gold dust.

They do **not rush in immediately**.  
Instead, they take **defensive positions**:

- One statue moves to the **Sentinel’s left flank**.
    
- The other moves to the **right flank**.
    
- Their goal is to **form a protective crescent** around their master.
    

Their first priority is **defense, not aggression**.

___

## **Bodyguard Charge (Reaction Behavior)**

If a spell, arrow, or ranged attack targets the Sentinel:

- The nearest Honor-Statue **launches itself** 20 ft. in a single bound.
    
- It interposes its body between the Sentinel and the danger.
    
- It takes the blow or forces the attack to hit its plated form instead.
    

They do this **instinctively**, even if it puts them in danger.

This gives players a very clear sense that:

**These guardians were built to die before they allow harm to reach the Sentinel.**

___
## **THEIR OFFENSIVE BEHAVIOR**

The statues attack when:

- Someone threatens the Sentinel directly
    
- Someone holds the mirror shard
    
- Someone approaches a pillar (they do NOT want pillars disturbed)
    
- Someone attempts to break the false sky
    
- Someone attacks from afar
    

They do _not_ hunt lone targets.  
They **react** rather than pursue.
