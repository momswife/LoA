## **Overview**

The **Iron-Mane Forgefiefs** were the last great martial alliance of the Beastfolk Age—part army, part industry, part faith.  
Born in the aftermath of the **Vel-Orann Betrayal** and rising amid the **Age of Collapse**, the Forgefiefs turned survival into defiance.  
To their people, war was not destruction but refinement: a crucible to burn away weakness and forge justice anew.

Their emblem—a roaring lion’s head biting an ingot—symbolized their creed:

> _“To create is to conquer despair.”_

---

## **Formation & The Crucible Oaths**

The Forgefiefs began as smithing coalitions scattered across Vinyot’s border valleys.  
When Brethren warlords began enslaving Beastfolk labor for siegeworks, seven leonine patriarchs united under the **Oath of Embers**, vowing never to craft for tyranny again.

They established the **Crucible Oaths**, tenets that merged blacksmith ritual with military code:

1. **Strike with purpose, not pride.**
    
2. **No blade leaves the forge untested.**
    
3. **Metal remembers the maker.**
    
4. **War without craft is chaos.**
    
5. **Craft without war is vanity.**
    

Each fief swore to provide arms only to free peoples, defending towns and caravans in exchange for materials or protection.  
Their combined forces—armored battalions known as the **Molten Guard**—soon rivaled entire city-states.

---

## **The Ember March**

When labyrinth surges devastated trade lines, the Forgefiefs undertook the **Ember March**—a continental campaign to reclaim and stabilize smelting sites overrun by chaos.  
Hyenid tacticians pioneered **shatter-form warfare**, using detonation hammers and pressure wards to collapse enemy fortifications.  
Leonine captains led **charge-chants**, rhythmic roars synchronized with hammer blows to fortify morale and ignite elemental weapons.

Their victory at **Korr’s Pass**, where molten slag was used to seal a rift, became legendary.  
The Warbell of Korr, cast from the cooled metal, was said to ring only when Aerathon itself was in peril.

---

## **The Forge as Faith**

To the Iron-Manes, the forge was sacred ground.  
Each blow of hammer on steel was believed to echo in the divine realms, a challenge to gods who had abandoned them.  
Shrines were built beside forges; each weapon blessed by heat, not prayer.  
When death came, warriors were entombed standing upright, their armor welded closed—becoming statues called **Silent Sentinels** that still line the passes of northern Allemance.

Their rituals spread through Beastfolk culture, influencing the metallurgic colleges of the later Ember Collegia and even Louvain’s ministry foundries.

---

## **Relations & Legacy**

The Forgefiefs allied with remnants of the **Stone-Song Confederacy**, using echo-carving to strengthen fortress walls.  
They trained early delvers in weapon tuning and armor-sigil craft, laying groundwork for modern relic warfare.

However, their pride carried a cost.  
During the final years of the Collapse, disputes over supply control escalated into the **Fires of Kin**, a civil conflict that consumed half their foundries.  
Hyenid fiefs sided with pragmatism—trade and adaptation—while leonine houses held to strict code and perished with their oaths intact.

By the dawn of the **Age of Delving**, the surviving smiths abandoned monarchic hierarchy for contract law, integrating into the new guild structure.  
Their legacy endures in every delver’s gear tag stamped with the Forgefief emblem: the lion’s bite upon the ingot.
