## **Overview**

Every civilization in Aerathon built its order upon the same foundation: **language as law**.  
From the glowing runes of the Orichalcum Age to the soft growl-tones of the Stone-Song Confederacy, words were never mere description — they were instruments of control.

The Ministry’s current linguistic catalog lists over two hundred “dead” tongues, yet none are truly silent.  
Fragments of ancient grammar still echo in modern spellwork, oathcraft, and labyrinth resonance — the world, it seems, continues to listen for commands written long ago.

---

## **The Auric Line**

**Origin:** Orichalcum Age  
**Nature:** Ideographic inscription that captured _intent_ rather than phonetic sound.  
Each glyph recorded a concept’s emotional resonance; reading one transmitted not information, but sensation.  
Scholars describe Auric Line as “the first spell language,” its syntax based on balance, not order.

Recovered tablets hum faintly when near relics, suggesting residual mnemonic energy.  
Lt. Korrin’s breach analysis indicates the Labyrinth walls’ shifting inscriptions are partial Auric derivatives, rewritten through environmental emotion — living text adapting to thought.

---

## **The Harmonium Tongue**

**Origin:** Convergence Courts  
**Nature:** Hybrid of divine liturgy and arcane notation.  
Its rhythm followed strict metrical patterns; every clause corresponded to a law of equivalence (“As above, so within”).  
Magisters used it to debate theology through mathematical cadence — a practice now revived by ministry rhetoricians.

Certain binding spells still include vestigial Harmonium prefixes (_cor-_, _ven-_, _al-_) which, when mispronounced, destabilize containment wards — a mistake that has closed more than one Ministry lab.

---

## **Knot-Script and Road Cant**

**Origin:** Pride of Seven Roads & Pilgrims of the Three Lanterns  
**Nature:** Physical and linguistic hybrid.  
Knot-Script used braided cords and loops to encode messages; the position of knots mirrored syllables in trade cant.  
Merchants and travelers passed entire contracts through rope, readable by touch even in darkness.

Modern equivalents survive in guild sashes and delver expedition ropes, their knots recording distance or shared kills.  
Ministry interpreters learn basic Knot-Script to read encoded rescue cords left in collapsed labyrinth corridors.

---

## **Stone-Tongue and Echo-Law**

**Origin:** Stone-Song Confederacy  
**Nature:** Harmonic resonance language relying on tone rather than symbol.  
Each vowel carried vibration meaning; each consonant defined its shape.  
A correctly spoken sentence could leave impressions in cave walls measurable centuries later.

The Ministry’s Resonance Bureau has confirmed that certain echo chambers beneath Vinyot still repeat fragments of ancient judicial hymns — law still humming, judgment without judge.

---

## **Common Speech and the Fracture of Babel**

After the **Great Drift**, no single tongue could unite Aerathon.  
Trade necessitated compromise, birthing **Common**, a linguistic alloy of Harmonium syntax, Road Cant brevity, and Beastfolk idiom.  
Its simplicity became its magic: spells written in Common require conscious focus to function, proof that intent now lies with the caster, not the word.

Ironically, this democratization of language weakened spell permanence — modern enchantments fade, while Auric and Harmonium artifacts endure for millennia.  
To the Ministry, this suggests that **the more widely understood a language becomes, the less power it holds**.

---

## **Language and the Labyrinths**

The Labyrinths themselves appear to **speak**.  
Glyphs within their walls shift in recognizable linguistic morphologies, sometimes mirroring known scripts, sometimes forming impossible ones.  
Transcriptions from delver expeditions show recurring motifs:

- Auric intent-glyphs paired with Harmonium clauses.
    
- Echo-Law tonal imprints pulsing in rhythm to delver speech.
    
- New runes that rewrite themselves when read aloud.
    

Lt. Korrin theorizes that the Labyrinths function as **linguistic reactors**, translating emotion and sound into physical alteration — a phenomenon she terms _Lexical Resonance._

If true, then the Tongues of the Old Way were not forgotten — they simply moved underground.
