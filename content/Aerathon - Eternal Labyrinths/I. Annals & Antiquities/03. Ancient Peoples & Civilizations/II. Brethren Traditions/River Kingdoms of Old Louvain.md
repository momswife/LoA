## **Overview**

The **River-Kingdoms of Old Louvain** predate the Free Guild era by nearly a millennium. They began as a network of semi-independent canal-cities along the Seric and Marrow Rivers, united by trade, irrigation, and shared divine patronage of **Oghma**, **Waukeen**, and **Tyr**.  
Their motto—_“Law flows where water flows”_—expressed both prosperity and control.

For centuries, the river itself functioned as a throne: whoever held the lockgates controlled not only commerce, but the spiritual traffic of the Age.

---

## **Formation & Expansion**

In the wake of the Twelve Kingdoms Pact, the southern provinces of Louvain fractured into rival estates.  
A cabal of engineers and water-priests, the **Canal Collegium**, proposed a grand irrigation network linking three basins into a single navigable artery.  
This project, initially civic, soon became political: noble houses established _river thrones_, embedding sigil-locks keyed to their bloodlines.

As the canals expanded, so did their laws. The Collegium’s bylaws evolved into the first **Maritime Codex**, a text still cited in modern MDO charter law.

---

## **Society & Faith**

The River-Kings ruled by **flow** rather than lineage. Taxes, guild charters, and even marriage contracts were measured in **“tides”** — periods of rising or ebbing privilege tied to seasonal floods.  
Dwarves maintained the locks, elves surveyed the currents, and human stewards enforced legal tariffs.

Faith centered on the triune balance of **Knowledge, Commerce, and Judgment**. Temples doubled as counting-houses; judges and priests often shared titles.

The noble cult of **the Red Canal** arose during the second dynasty: a belief that the flow of the river was literal divine blood. Offerings dyed entire canals crimson during festival seasons, a spectacle that drew pilgrims and chroniclers alike.

---

## **Culture & Legacy**

Water dictated all aspects of daily life:

- Births occurred at high tide to ensure prosperity.
    
- Oaths were sealed with cups of river-drawn wine.
    
- The dead were set adrift beneath night-lanterns, believed to carry their memories into the current of Oghma’s thought.
    

When the labyrinth phenomenon began to stir, Louvain’s waterways became the perfect means of containment and transport. Many early delver guilds established along these same routes, converting defunct lockhouses into guild halls and archives.

Even after the river-thrones fell, the **canal logic** of governance endured: bureaucracy as current, law as flow, knowledge as sediment.

---

## **Decline**

Internal rivalries, shifting sediment, and the first **Beastfolk uprisings** fractured the canal thrones.  
By the late **Age of Chains**, Louvain’s central waterways silted with bureaucracy and literal debris.  
The last River-King, **Alestor Varenne**, famously decreed, _“Let the river choose its next ruler.”_  
Within a generation, the entire region flooded, and the dynasty drowned — literally and politically.

From those waters, centuries later, rose the **Free Guild City of Louvain**, inheriting not crowns, but contracts.
