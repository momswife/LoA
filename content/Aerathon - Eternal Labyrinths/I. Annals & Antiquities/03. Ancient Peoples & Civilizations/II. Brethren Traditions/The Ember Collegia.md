## **Overview**

The **Ember Collegia** were a consortium of workshops, foundries, and academies that rose during the moral exhaustion of the **Age of Chains**.  
When faith and crown faltered, the Collegia preached a new doctrine: that creation itself could purify.

They sought not worship, but mastery—blending alchemy, metallurgy, and theology into a single discipline called **Craftwright Theory**. Their motto, carved above every gate:

> _“To shape is to understand.”_

The Collegia’s experiments laid the groundwork for modern magitech, artificery, and the very charter codes of the Ministry itself.

---

## **Founding & Philosophy**

Founded by the dwarf-sage **Arav Dhal-Senn** (yes, the same who later joined the MDO centuries hence as an authority on antiquities), the Collegia began as a small alliance of disenfranchised scholars expelled from the College of Law and Light.

They rejected divine permission as a precondition for invention. Instead, they posited that **the ember**—a flame sustained beyond its source—was the truest symbol of mortal persistence.  
Their laboratories were temples of labor, their prayers the sound of hammer and quill.

This was the first age to formally distinguish **wizardry from clerical magic**, citing the ember as proof that power could remain once faith burned out.

---

## **Organization & Teachings**

The Collegia operated under the **Nine Forged Tenets**, a code equal parts safety manual and creed:

1. Flame remembers its maker.
    
2. Every alloy is an argument.
    
3. Thought may be smelted.
    
4. Iron fears no truth.
    
5. Heat purifies all deceit.
    
6. Sparks obey structure.
    
7. To teach is to temper.
    
8. To temper is to teach.
    
9. Creation is atonement.
    

Each Collegium campus specialized in a “discipline of ember”—alchemy, aether theory, sigil binding, or runic architecture.  
Students were called _temperants_, and graduation required crafting a tool that could survive fire without divine blessing.

---

## **Cultural Influence**

Their crafts fueled an age of enlightenment:

- **Fireglass lenses** revolutionized scrying and optics.
    
- **Aether filaments** became precursors to rune conduits.
    
- **Memory bellows**—living artifacts that inhaled ideas and exhaled new forms—appear in several labyrinths today.
    

The Collegia’s emphasis on merit over birth created social mobility unseen since the Orichalcum Age. Beastfolk apprentices and mixed-blood artificers found acceptance here long before guild equality was codified.

However, the Collegia’s success bred fear. Crowned powers labeled them heretical “flame-mongers,” accusing them of tempting divine wrath by reshaping sacred elements.

---

## **Decline & Dissolution**

The Collegia’s downfall came not through war, but absorption. As delver guilds rose, Collegium facilities were nationalized, their professors “rebranded” as guild instructors.  
By the **Age of Delving**, the Collegia name existed only as a metaphor—a nod to craftsmanship as virtue.

Ironically, their belief that knowledge should circulate like heat directly inspired the **Free Guild Accords**—the same bureaucratic system that replaced them.
