## **Overview**

The **Treaty of Breath & Beast** stands as the first recorded political recognition of the Beastfolk as sentient peoples—and the earliest documentation of their collective betrayal.  
Forged in the dying glow of the **Age of Chains**, when rebellion and theological revision were already spreading, the treaty promised the right to live, trade, and worship in exchange for service to the great crowns and clerical orders.

Though hailed as the “Dawn of Concord” in human chronicles, Beastfolk oral tradition remembers it differently: as a gilded collar wrapped in parchment.

---

## **Formation & Intent**

After years of violent purges and subjugation, an unlikely coalition of reformist nobles, elven pacifists, and sympathetic clerics convened in the valley of **Vel-Orann**. There, before both temple and tribunal, representatives of twelve Beastfolk clans met under banners of silver breath—a symbol of the soul’s right to exist unbranded.

The treaty’s core promise was deceptively simple:

> _“All who draw breath beneath the same sun shall have their place within its warmth.”_

It established a framework of **conditional personhood**, allowing Beastfolk to own property, join select guilds, and receive temple rites—but only under the patronage of recognized Brethren houses.  
The “breath” referred to in the treaty symbolized divine tolerance, not equality.

---

## **Cultural Impact**

Despite its flaws, the treaty sparked an unprecedented exchange of art, language, and labor. Beastfolk crafts and oral poetry infiltrated the low courts of men, while human script and bureaucracy spread through the wild territories.  
For the first time, cross-species families and trade caravans operated openly—though they remained tightly surveilled.

The treaty also standardized the **Mark of Breath**—a tattoo or sigil placed upon Beastfolk forearms signifying lawful identity. For many, it was both passport and shackle.

Songs from this period still survive among Beastfolk elders: laments that mix gratitude and defiance, mourning the price of peace.

---

## **Dissolution**

The accord collapsed during the **Vel-Orann Betrayal**, when human militias, under orders from the College of Law and Light, executed a “purification audit.” Entire Beastfolk settlements were seized under accusations of heretical worship and “unlicensed spellcraft.”  
In response, surviving clans fled to deep forests, mountains, and coasts—the origins of the later **Stone-Song Confederacy**, **Pride of Seven Roads**, and **Sky-Reeve Clans**.

By the dawn of the **Ember Age**, the parchment of the Treaty had become a banner of rebellion.
