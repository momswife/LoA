## **Overview**

The **Convergence Courts** were the first attempt at institutionalizing the mysteries of creation — neither empire nor religion, but something uncomfortably between.  
They began as circles of philosopher-mages who believed magic and divinity were two halves of the same current. Over generations, these circles crystallized into semi-sovereign city-states dedicated to proving, recording, and ultimately **governing** all expressions of power.

Each Court held jurisdiction over a facet of existence — Light, Blood, Thought, Law, Breath, Flesh, Dream, and Flame — and all answered to a shifting council called the **Twelve Harmonists**. From their marble forums came the first codices, oaths, and treaties that defined who could touch the Weave and who could not.

When they fell, the vacuum they left gave rise to the first crowned monarchies of the Age of Crowns.

## **Cultural Snapshot**

### **Society & Belief**

The Courts treated faith as an experiment. Each god was a theory; each spell, evidence.  
Clerics were scholars, and scholars were zealots of logic.  
Every citizen was required to undergo **Attunement Trials** at adulthood — meditative ceremonies testing moral alignment through conjured illusions. Passing granted legal adulthood and the right to bear sigils of study.

Their symbol, the **Interwoven Scales**, represented the pursuit of equilibrium between mortal agency and divine mandate — a balance no civilization since has managed to replicate.

---

### **Architecture & Legacy**

Their cities were architectural paradoxes: symmetrical temples built upon ley fractures. Streets spiraled inward toward central atriums where mirrors, lenses, and magical prisms refracted sunlight into structured color codes — each hue signifying a law, edict, or moral verdict.

The ruins that remain—particularly in **northern Vinyot** and beneath **modern Louvain**—exhibit intricate convergence nodes later mimicked by the **High Council of Flame**. Many early delver guilds adopted these layouts for their own halls, believing the geometry offered both spiritual protection and tactical advantage.

---

### **Languages & Symbols**

The Courts unified dozens of dialects into a proto-lingua known as **The Harmonium Tongue** — a hybrid of spell syntax and ordinary speech.  
Spoken Harmonium carried mathematical rhythm; words were measured in beats, and errors of cadence were considered perjury.

Modern spellcasting terminology still preserves fragments of this tongue — particularly in binding clauses, sigil grammar, and the divine invocation prefix _"Cor-"_ (“by concord”).

---

## **Decline & Disbandment**

Power invites contradiction. The Courts fractured when their own measurements of divinity began to differ.  
Some ruled that the gods were merely ascended mortals; others, that mortal will itself was godhood.  
Debate turned to accusation, then war. The **Thesis Wars**, fought not with armies but with unleashed concepts — time loops, recursive summoning, and thought-plagues — erased entire archives and memories.

In the aftermath, surviving scholars fled or became the first noble arcanists of the **Twelve Kingdoms Pact**, rebranding their authority as “divine right.”  
Thus the Age of Crowns rose directly upon the Courts’ ashes.
