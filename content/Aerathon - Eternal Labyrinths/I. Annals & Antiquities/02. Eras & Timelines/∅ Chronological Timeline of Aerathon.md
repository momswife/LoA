## **I. Mandate of the Unified Timeline**

The **Chronological Timeline of Aerathon** is the Ministry’s controlling framework for the ordering of recorded history.

It establishes:

- the recognized historical eras;
    
- their official boundaries;
    
- the principal transitions between them;
    
- the dates of major civilizational events;
    
- and the degree of confidence assigned to those dates.
    

The timeline does not claim that every kingdom, culture, or surviving archive experienced history in the same manner.

An age may begin in one land generations before its influence reaches another. Empires do not fall everywhere at once. Faith does not vanish from every temple upon the same morning. A Labyrinth event recorded as a single day by one expedition may occupy years within the structure that produced it.

Era boundaries are therefore administrative and historical thresholds—not declarations that the whole world changed at a single Mark.

The purpose of this document is not to erase such differences.

It is to provide a common spine upon which those differences may be responsibly placed.

---

## **II. Principles of Era Classification**

The Ministry recognizes six historical ages following the Dawning, preceded by an unnumbered period of Deep Time and Orichalcum civilization.

An age is formally distinguished from the one before it when a lasting change occurs across three or more of the following measures:

1. **Political Order**  
    The dominant form of government, law, or social authority.
    
2. **Magical Practice**  
    The prevailing means by which magic is understood, taught, restricted, or reproduced.
    
3. **Relationship to Divinity**  
    The degree and manner of verified divine intervention.
    
4. **Labyrinth Activity**  
    The number, behavior, and societal importance of known Labyrinth formations.
    
5. **Cultural Memory**  
    The methods by which history, identity, and law are preserved.
    
6. **Material Organization**  
    The systems through which food, trade, craft, transit, and survival are maintained.
    

The beginning of an age marks the point at which a new order became historically dominant, not necessarily the first appearance of its defining traits.

Observer-responsive Labyrinths, for example, were verified during the late Age of Delving. The **Age of Spectacle** did not begin until observation itself became a central political, economic, and cultural force.

### **Chronological Confidence**

Dates in this record are assigned one of four confidence levels:

|Classification|Meaning|
|---|---|
|**Grade-I Anchor**|Supported by multiple independent records, stable instruments, or surviving legal documents|
|**Grade-II Reconstruction**|Strongly supported, though some details remain disputed|
|**Grade-III Tradition**|Preserved primarily through cultural, oral, religious, or fragmentary sources|
|**Unfixed**|Placement is broadly accepted, but no defensible numbered date survives|

The absence of certainty is not treated as failure.

Within Annals & Antiquities, visible uncertainty is a form of accuracy.

## **III. Master Era Register**

Official ranges use non-overlapping years. The stated beginning of each age is the first year governed by that classification.

|Period|Official Range|Duration|Defining Transformation|
|---|--:|--:|---|
|**Deep Time**|Before fixed B.D. reckoning|Unknown|Divine conflict, primordial formation, and mythic prehistory|
|**The Orichalcum Dawn**|Unfixed B.D.–0 A.D.|Unknown|Resonant civilization and the stabilization of the Three Weaves|
|**I. Age of Crowns**|0–1099 A.D.|1,100 years|Divine monarchy, sacred law, and institutional faith|
|**II. Age of Chains**|1100–2099 A.D.|1,000 years|Theocratic empire, servitude, and resonant rebellion|
|**III. Ember Age**|2100–2599 A.D.|500 years|Secular magic, guild civilization, and uncontrolled invention|
|**IV. Age of Collapse**|2600–2949 A.D.|350 years|Faithfall, chronometric fracture, famine, and Labyrinth proliferation|
|**V. Age of Delving**|2950–3299 A.D.|350 years|Reconstruction through exploration and the rise of the Ministry|
|**VI. Age of Spectacle**|3300–Present|88 years as of 3388|Observation, broadcast culture, and commodified memory|

The present year is **3388 A.D.**

The world currently remains within the **Age of Spectacle**.

No Seventh Era has been formally recognized.

## **IV. Deep Time and the Orichalcum Dawn**

Before the Dawning lies the oldest and least stable portion of Aerathonian history.

Ministry scholars divide this period into two broad categories.

### **Deep Time**

Deep Time includes the prehistoric formation of the world, the oldest divine conflicts, the emergence of primordial peoples, and events for which no continuous mortal chronology survives.

Some theological reconstructions place the great divine conflicts between approximately **30,000 and 20,000 B.D.**, but these numbers remain Grade-III estimates. They are derived from divine genealogies, Labyrinth strata, astronomical decay, and relics that occasionally disagree with their own age.

The Ministry accepts that such conflicts occurred.

It does not certify their traditional sequence as literal fact.

### **The Orichalcum Dawn**

The later pre-Dawning period was dominated by cultures capable of shaping **Orichalcum**, a living alloy responsive to intent, meaning, and harmonic structure.

Surviving sites suggest a civilization—or network of civilizations—in which craft, worship, language, and law were not separate practices. Structures were sung into alignment. Tools retained aspects of their makers’ purpose. Written commands could become physical properties.

The disappearance of the Orichalcum peoples remains unexplained.

Theories include:

- divine war;
    
- voluntary transformation;
    
- catastrophic resonance;
    
- migration beyond the known planes;
    
- and absorption into the early Weaves.
    

Their decline ended the world’s oldest known unity of craft and metaphysics.

The **Dawning**, designated **0 A.D.**, marks the first measurable stabilization following that disappearance.

From that point onward, continuous mortal chronology becomes possible.

## **V. Era I — The Age of Crowns**

### _0–1099 A.D. — The Reign of Blood and Law_

The Age of Crowns began when the civilizations that rose after the Dawning interpreted surviving divine power as proof of hereditary right.

Brethren kingdoms formed around relic sites, sacred bloodlines, and the belief that mortal rule reflected celestial hierarchy. Kings did not merely govern; they claimed to embody a measurable portion of divine order.

Magic became permission.

Law became ritual.

History became the property of those authorized to preserve it.

### **Principal Chronology**

|Date|Event|Confidence|
|---|---|---|
|**0 A.D.**|The Dawning is established as the beginning of continuous historical reckoning.|Grade-I Anchor|
|**c. 140–310 A.D.**|The first High Thrones consolidate around Louvain, Dhalraen, Vaelcour, and neighboring sacred centers.|Grade-II Reconstruction|
|**c. 420 A.D.**|The Crown Concord formalizes the principle that divine resonance grants lawful sovereignty.|Grade-II Reconstruction|
|**c. 600–850 A.D.**|Faith Courts, prayer registries, and miracle-based taxation spread across the Crown kingdoms.|Grade-II Reconstruction|
|**c. 870 A.D.**|The oldest surviving Annals of Petition are compiled beneath the Louvain Crown Chapel.|Grade-I Anchor|
|**c. 1010–1080 A.D.**|Verified divine responses decline; Hollow Masses and failed royal miracles become increasingly common.|Grade-II Reconstruction|
|**1091 A.D.**|The Binding Concord attempts to preserve divine law through mortal bureaucracy.|Grade-II Reconstruction|
|**1100 A.D.**|The Concord’s institutions are absorbed into the emerging Empire of Law and Light. The Age of Chains begins.|Grade-I Boundary|

The Age of Crowns produced lasting administrative systems, but it also excluded entire populations from recognized history.

Beastfolk oral traditions call this period the **Age of the Closed Eye**, when their ancestors lived beyond the concern of royal gods and official archives.

The era ended when kingdoms ceased merely claiming divine favor and began attempting to manufacture, regulate, and enforce it.

The crown became a chain.

---

## **VI. Era II — The Age of Chains**

### _1100–2099 A.D. — The Law Made Flesh_

The Age of Chains was defined by the rise of the **Empire of Law and Light**, the largest theocratic state known to post-Dawning history.

The Empire inherited the administrative machinery of the Crown kingdoms and transformed it into a system of spiritual measurement. Faith became a civic qualification. Magical ability became licensed obedience. Peoples deemed spiritually unstable were branded, relocated, altered, or enslaved.

It is during this age that Beastfolk first appear widely in Brethren records—not as nations or kinships, but as labor classifications and experimental populations.

Beastfolk traditions sharply reject this account of their origin.

The Ministry records both positions and certifies neither as complete.

### **Principal Chronology**

|Date|Event|Confidence|
|---|---|---|
|**1100 A.D.**|The Empire of Law and Light is proclaimed from Sol Oranna.|Grade-II Reconstruction|
|**c. 1180–1320 A.D.**|Faith Density testing and hereditary prayer licenses become standard imperial policy.|Grade-II Reconstruction|
|**c. 1460 A.D.**|The Faith Loom links major imperial temples through synchronized prayer and Orichalcum circuitry.|Grade-II Reconstruction|
|**c. 1700–1900 A.D.**|Beastfolk memory networks, dissident workshops, and unsanctioned resonance traditions spread beneath imperial rule.|Grade-III Tradition|
|**c. 1940 A.D.**|The first coordinated Echo-Wars begin across imperial labor territories.|Grade-II Reconstruction|
|**2073 A.D.**|The Cathedral of Harmonium at Sol Oranna implodes during a combined Beastfolk and Brethren uprising.|Grade-I Anchor|
|**2073 A.D.**|The event later called the **First Withholding** marks the last uncontested public miracle of the imperial order.|Grade-II Reconstruction|
|**2073–2099 A.D.**|Imperial authority fractures; surviving provinces declare independence or collapse into local rule.|Grade-I Reconstruction|
|**2100 A.D.**|Guild-states and liberated confederations become the dominant political order. The Ember Age begins.|Grade-I Boundary|

The destruction of Sol Oranna did not immediately end the Empire.

Imperial institutions survived for another generation in isolated provinces, fortified temples, and legal traditions. Some continued issuing decrees long after the capital had ceased to exist.

The Age of Chains ended when divine law could no longer command obedience and mortal communities began building systems that required no divine answer.

## **VII. Era III — The Ember Age**

### _2100–2599 A.D. — Revolution, Invention, and Reforging_

The Ember Age was the first period in which large civilizations openly treated magic as a reproducible mortal discipline rather than a divine privilege.

Across former imperial lands, craftspeople, scholars, liberated laborers, and Beastfolk memory-keepers formed independent guild-states. Temples became workshops. Liturgical diagrams became engineering notation. Miracles were dismantled, measured, and rebuilt.

The period is remembered as a renaissance.

It was also an age of profound recklessness.

### **Principal Chronology**

|Date|Event|Confidence|
|---|---|---|
|**2100–2140 A.D.**|Secular guild-states emerge throughout the former imperial territories.|Grade-II Reconstruction|
|**c. 2186 A.D.**|Twelve major guild-cities establish the **Concord of Flame**.|Grade-II Reconstruction|
|**c. 2215 A.D.**|The Ember Collegia consolidates from several independent schools of elemental craft.|Grade-II Reconstruction|
|**c. 2215–2245 A.D.**|Cael Emberwright is recognized as the Collegia’s first presiding Master; Arav Dhal-Senn is credited with its later doctrinal and technical codification.|Grade-II Reconstruction|
|**c. 2270 A.D.**|The Tri-Weave Application Model provides the first widely reproducible theory of non-divine spellcraft.|Grade-II Reconstruction|
|**c. 2360–2440 A.D.**|Stone-Song, Shell-Ledger, and allied preservation traditions become central to interregional law and memory.|Grade-II Reconstruction|
|**c. 2410 A.D.**|The earliest documented Reverberant Folds are deliberately entered and studied.|Grade-II Reconstruction|
|**c. 2520–2580 A.D.**|Ignition Catastrophes, sympathetic spell failures, and environmental decay spread across major industrial regions.|Grade-II Reconstruction|
|**2590–2599 A.D.**|The Collegia fractures, trade networks fail, and large-scale magical restraint becomes politically unavoidable.|Grade-I Reconstruction|
|**2600 A.D.**|Systemic failure overtakes invention as the world’s defining condition. The Age of Collapse begins.|Grade-I Boundary|

The Ember Age’s greatest achievement was proving that mortals could shape the Weaves without divine permission.

Its greatest failure was assuming that understanding a force granted the right to use it without limit.

The world did not punish that confidence all at once.

It simply became less capable of carrying it.

## **VIII. Era IV — The Age of Collapse**

### _2600–2949 A.D. — Silence, Fracture, and the Death of Certainty_

The Age of Collapse was not a single apocalypse.

It was three and a half centuries of systems failing faster than they could be replaced.

The gods ceased answering in reliable ways. Guild-states hoarded knowledge. Climate and aetheric patterns destabilized. Trade routes disappeared. Spells persisted beyond their casters, infected language, or calcified into relics.

Labyrinths spread from isolated anomalies into a defining feature of survival.

The period survives unevenly in the record because the institutions responsible for recording it were themselves collapsing.

### **Principal Chronology**

|Date|Event|Confidence|
|---|---|---|
|**2600 A.D.**|The failure of major guild-state infrastructure establishes the beginning of the Collapse.|Grade-I Boundary|
|**c. 2632 A.D.**|Surviving guilds and confederations sign the **Collapse Concord**, restricting dangerous magical practices.|Grade-II Reconstruction|
|**c. 2680–2740 A.D.**|The Quiet Pantheon becomes the dominant theological interpretation of divine silence.|Grade-II Reconstruction|
|**c. 2710 A.D. onward**|Mourn-Howl Circles, mobile Shell-Ledgers, regional Stone-Song choirs, and protected trade roads preserve displaced populations.|Grade-II Reconstruction|
|**2810–2930 A.D.**|The **Great Drift** disrupts time, geography, memory, and regional chronology across much of Aerathon.|Grade-I Anchor|
|**2897 A.D.**|The Withholding Hypothesis is formally published by surviving Brethren theologians.|Grade-I Anchor|
|**c. 2912 A.D.**|The Convergence Catastrophe destroys seven cities and produces the First Collapse Breach.|Grade-II Reconstruction|
|**c. 2920–2949 A.D.**|Permanent settlement around and within stable Labyrinth systems becomes widespread.|Grade-II Reconstruction|
|**2948 A.D.**|Several major surface populations formally relocate into regulated subterranean refuges.|Grade-I Anchor|
|**2950 A.D.**|Guild charters, relic exchange, and organized descent become the foundation of reconstruction. The Age of Delving begins.|Grade-I Boundary|

The **Great Drift** refers specifically to the chronometric and geographic dislocation of 2810–2930 A.D.

Later population movements caused by the Collapse should be described as the **Drift Migrations**, **Collapse Migrations**, or by their regional names. They are consequences of the Great Drift, not the same event.

The Age of Collapse ended when surviving societies ceased regarding the Labyrinths solely as wounds to be sealed and began treating them as places upon which civilization could be rebuilt.

## **IX. Era V — The Age of Delving**

### _2950–3299 A.D. — Order from Below_

The Age of Delving began when exploration became institution.

Labyrinth descent evolved from mourning rite and desperate scavenging into a regulated profession. Relics restored agriculture, transport, communication, and defensive magic. Guild charters replaced broken kingdoms as the most dependable form of political organization.

From these charters arose the **Ministry of Delving Operations**.

The Ministry did not create delving.

It created the laws, classifications, and records by which delving became a civilization.

### **Principal Chronology**

|Date|Event|Confidence|
|---|---|---|
|**2950 A.D.**|The first stable post-Collapse charter networks establish regulated descent and relic exchange.|Grade-I Boundary|
|**2980 A.D.**|The **Free Guild Accords** create shared rules for licensing, salvage, containment, and expedition records.|Grade-I Anchor|
|**c. 3020–3030 A.D.**|The Ministry of Delving Operations is formally constituted from Accord offices and regional guild authorities.|Grade-I Reconstruction|
|**c. 3075 A.D.**|The first standardized Relic Classification System is adopted across Ministry territories.|Grade-II Reconstruction|
|**3147 A.D.**|The **Codex of Breach Law** consolidates expedition licensing, containment protocols, and Labyrinth zoning.|Grade-I Anchor|
|**3197 A.D.**|The first verified observer-responsive Labyrinth formations are recorded.|Grade-I Anchor|
|**c. 3230–3270 A.D.**|Public scry-relays, sponsored expeditions, and spectacle delving become increasingly common.|Grade-II Reconstruction|
|**3292 A.D.**|The **Louvain Concordat** unifies the remaining Free Guild Accords beneath a republican Ministry-charter structure.|Grade-I Anchor|
|**3292–3299 A.D.**|Broadcast rights, public record systems, and guild sponsorship begin shaping policy as strongly as field necessity.|Grade-I Reconstruction|
|**3300 A.D.**|Observation becomes a central economic and political force. The Age of Spectacle begins.|Grade-I Boundary|

The first observer-responsive Labyrinths appeared more than a century before the Age of Spectacle.

Their appearance alone did not create the new age.

The decisive change occurred when the people of Aerathon began organizing society around the act of watching them.

---

## **X. Era VI — The Age of Spectacle**

### _3300 A.D.–Present — Memory as Currency, Truth as Performance_

The Age of Spectacle is the present historical period.

Delving remains essential to survival, but it is no longer understood solely as labor, exploration, or public service. Expeditions are broadcast. Delvers cultivate public identities. Guilds negotiate narrative rights. Recovered memories and emotional impressions are bought, sold, edited, and replayed.

The archive has become both authority and entertainment.

The Labyrinths appear to have noticed.

### **Principal Chronology**

|Date|Event|Confidence|
|---|---|---|
|**3300 A.D.**|Ministry analysts retrospectively identify the beginning of the Age of Spectacle.|Grade-I Boundary|
|**c. 3305–3320 A.D.**|International S-Class broadcasts and formal sponsorship markets become widespread.|Grade-II Reconstruction|
|**c. 3324 A.D.**|Memory Binder certification is standardized for the extraction and sale of expedition recollections.|Grade-II Reconstruction|
|**c. 3330 A.D. onward**|Public Registry Nodes and automated memory transcription expand through major Ministry districts.|Grade-II Reconstruction|
|**c. 3341 A.D.**|Beastfolk unions submit the first coordinated petition for legal Cognitive Autonomy and the right to unrecorded life.|Grade-II Reconstruction|
|**c. 3350–3370 A.D.**|Mirror-Class events, stage-pattern architecture, and self-documenting breaches increase significantly.|Grade-I Reconstruction|
|**c. 3362 A.D.**|The Arcane Breach Division formally adopts the **Mirror-Class** designation.|Grade-II Reconstruction|
|**3388 A.D.**|Present year. The Age of Spectacle remains the governing historical classification.|Grade-I Anchor|

The Age of Spectacle is only eighty-eight years old.

Its institutions are therefore far less settled than those of earlier ages. Many practices treated as ancient tradition are younger than living grandparents. Several laws concerning memory ownership, broadcast consent, and observer-responsive Labyrinths remain under active dispute.

The present age should not be mistaken for the inevitable culmination of history.

It is merely the form that modern Aerathon currently mistakes for permanence.

## **XI. Recognized Era Transitions**

Era transitions are rarely single events. The dates below identify the point at which one historical order became more influential than the one it replaced.

|Boundary|Transition|Primary Historical Cause|
|---|---|---|
|**0 A.D.**|Orichalcum Dawn → Age of Crowns|Stabilization of chronology and rise of post-Orichalcum kingdoms|
|**1100 A.D.**|Crowns → Chains|Sacred monarchy consolidates into imperial theocratic law|
|**2100 A.D.**|Chains → Ember|Imperial collapse permits secular guild-states and self-willed magic|
|**2600 A.D.**|Ember → Collapse|Magical overuse and institutional fracture overwhelm the guild order|
|**2950 A.D.**|Collapse → Delving|Labyrinth-centered charters become the basis of reconstruction|
|**3300 A.D.**|Delving → Spectacle|Observation and public narrative become dominant social forces|

These dates should be used consistently across all Ministry era dossiers.

Individual events may occur before or after their associated boundary without contradiction.

An age begins when its central logic becomes dominant.

It ends when that logic can no longer reliably govern the world it created.

## **XII. Disputed Chronology and Present Assessment**

Several portions of the historical record remain unresolved.

### **The Beginning of the Dawning**

The Ministry can measure Weave stabilization at Sunwound Crater, but cannot prove whether the event occurred simultaneously across all Aerathon.

### **The Origin of the Beastfolk**

Brethren imperial records describe engineered or altered populations. Beastfolk oral traditions describe awakening, inheritance, or the recovery of identities deliberately erased during the Age of Crowns.

Both bodies of evidence contain demonstrable truth.

Neither explains the whole.

### **The First Labyrinth**

Proto-labyrinthal distortions existed during the Age of Chains. Reverberant Folds were entered during the Ember Age. Large self-sustaining Labyrinths became widespread during the Collapse.

The phrase **First Labyrinth** is therefore considered imprecise unless the writer specifies the type of formation being discussed.

### **The Great Drift**

The Great Drift is fixed to **2810–2930 A.D.**

Centuries-long migrations associated with its aftermath must not be presented as a separate event bearing the same name.

### **The Beginning of Spectacle**

Observer-responsive formations were documented in 3197 A.D., but the Age of Spectacle begins in 3300 A.D.

The distinction is intentional.

A phenomenon may exist long before civilization reorganizes itself around that phenomenon.

### **The Proposed Seventh Era**

Several Ministry analysts believe Aerathon approaches another threshold.

Suggested names include:

- **The Age of Reversal**
    
- **The Age of Witness**
    
- **The Age of Unmaking**
    
- **The Second Dawning**
    
- and **The Era of the Living Archive**
    

None possess official standing.

A new age cannot be declared merely because a theory is fashionable, a crisis is frightening, or an archivist wishes to name the future.

The Ministry will recognize a Seventh Era only when the world has changed sufficiently that the old classification can no longer explain it.

Until then, the record remains open.

> _“The past of Aerathon is not buried. It continues to surface—one ruin, one song, one witness at a time.”_
