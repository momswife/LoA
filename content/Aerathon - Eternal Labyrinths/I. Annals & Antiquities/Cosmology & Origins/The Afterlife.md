## **I. Overview**

The Afterlife in Aerathon is not a single place, but a **multi-stage transit system** shaped by the Quiet Edict, the Two Heavens, and the metaphysics of memory itself.  
Every mortal soul undergoes a consistent sequence of events:

1. **Detachment from the Mortal Coil**
    
2. **Passage Through the Veiled Meridian**
    
3. **Judgment or Weighing** (depending on pantheon alignment)
    
4. **Placement into a Dominion of Rest, Service, or Return**
    

The Ministry considers the Afterlife one of the most stable cosmic mechanisms—yet also one of the least understood due to Edict restrictions on divine revelation.

What follows is the Ministry’s best-verified composite from necromantic reports, Echo-Law resonance, recovered funerary relics, and rare labyrinth encounters.

---

# **II. The Soul at the Moment of Death**

When a mortal dies, the soul undergoes **Immediate Detachment**, a universal and measurable phenomenon:

- Body animus collapses.
    
- Faithfield signatures dissolve.
    
- A soul-thread (Echo-Law term: _the Primary Lineage Oscillation_) separates within 2–4 heartbeats.
    

### **Observable Signs (Ministry-confirmed):**

- Sudden pressure drop in nearby aether density.
    
- Brief auditory distortion (“the Last Resonance”).
    
- Weak soul-luminosity detectable for ~10 seconds by trained mages.
    

During this time, resurrective magic may still function—but only before **Meridian Entry** begins.

---

# **III. The Veiled Meridian — Threshold Between Worlds**

All souls, regardless of faith, cross first into the **Veiled Meridian**:  
a liminal plane governed not by gods, but by a metaphysical law older than the divine conflicts.

### **Properties of the Meridian**

- Time behaves inconsistently (perceived minutes may equal hours).
    
- The soul manifests in symbolic form (often wearing familiar memories).
    
- No deity can enter the Meridian directly due to the Quiet Edict.
    
- Souls cannot perceive each other unless bonded in life.
    

### **Ministry Field Reports Indicate:**

Labyrinths sometimes create _false Meridians_, illusions mimicking this passage. Distinguishing real from false requires Echo-Law testing.

---

# **IV. Judgment & Weighing — The Two Paths of Soul Determination**

Souls diverge along one of **two great processes**, depending on their pantheonic resonance in life:

---

## **A. Kelemvor’s Passing (Near Heaven)**

_For souls aligned with the Dawn Pantheon or secular belief systems._

### **Procedure:**

1. **A Kelemvorite Aspect** (never the deity himself) meets the soul.
    
2. Memory, deed, intention, and truth are weighed.
    
3. The soul is directed to its appropriate Near-Heaven dominion.
    

### **Outcomes:**

- **Domains of Rest** (Lathander, Sune, Eldath, Chauntea)
    
- **Domains of Service** (Helm, Torm, Tyr)
    
- **Domains of Craft or Knowledge** (Gond, Azuth, Oghma, Mystra)
    
- **Domains of Renewal or Transition** (Selûne, Deneir)
    

Condemnation is rare; most Near-Heaven dominions simply reflect the soul’s lived values.

**MDO Note:**  
Souls trapped in labyrinths often exhibit Near-Heaven signal corruption—cause unknown.

---

## **B. Anubis’ Weighing (Far Heaven)**

_Primarily for Beastfolk, especially those with ancestral resonance._

### **Procedure:**

1. The soul is brought before **Anubis’ Aspect** in the Hall of True Measures.
    
2. A metaphysical “heart” (totality of essence) is weighed against **Ma’at**—truth, balance, and the cosmic order.
    
3. A soul found “true” enters Far-Heaven dominions.
    

### **Outcomes:**

- **Rebirth in the Verdant Fields** (Osiris)
    
- **Cycle of Ancestral Return** (Hathor, Mielikki)
    
- **Guardianship or Service** (Bast, Ptah)
    
- **Mysteries of Knowledge** (Thoth)
    

A soul deemed false does not face torment—it is **unmade**, returning to cosmic potential.

**MDO Note:**  
Jackalfolk report vivid dreams consistent with Far-Heaven weighing.

---

# **V. The Dominion Realms of the Dead**

Souls do not enter heaven as static spirits. They join **Dominion Layers** appropriate to their beliefs and deeds.

### **Near Heaven Dominions**

- **Lathander**: dawn-lit plains of renewal
    
- **Eldath**: endless green refuges
    
- **Selûne**: silver-lit celestial oceans
    
- **Gond**: infinite workshops
    
- **Mystra**: the Weavefields, where ideas are given shape
    
- **Helm**: the Vigilant Bastion
    
- **Torm & Tyr**: halls of righteous service
    

### **Far Heaven Dominions**

- **Osiris**: riverlands of eternal green
    
- **Thoth**: libraries of infinite memory
    
- **Bast**: hunting-grounds that shift with moon phases
    
- **Hathor**: harmonic plains of joy
    
- **Ptah**: forging-realms of creation and hidden names
    

### **Notable Exceptions**

- **Shar’s Dominion**: The Chamber of Forgotten Light (memory-loss eternal)
    
- **Myrkul’s Dominion**: The Bone Marches (gradual dissolution into entropy)
    
- **Talona’s Dominion**: The Festering Paths (purification through decay)
    

---

# **VI. Lost, Trapped, and Interrupted Souls**

Despite cosmic order, some souls **fail to complete transit**.

### **Reasons:**

- Violent or magical death
    
- Labyrinthic interference
    
- Relic entanglement
    
- Soul-binding curses
    
- Resurrection attempts gone wrong
    
- Interstitial Plane distortion
    

### **Outcomes:**

- **Echo-Specters:** memory loops unable to reach the Meridian
    
- **Hollow Husks:** bodies without souls (dangerous if awakened)
    
- **Labyrinth-Fused Souls:** eternal hauntings tied to specific rooms
    
- **Wandering Lights:** souls stalled in the rifts between realms
    

Ministry procedure requires immediate sealing of areas where soul-interruptions occur.

---

# **VII. Resurrection & the Edict**

Bringing a soul back is possible—but heavily constrained by the Quiet Edict.

### **Confirmed Rules:**

1. **Once a soul passes fully into a Dominion, resurrection becomes extremely difficult.**
    
2. **A soul judged false by Anubis or unbalanced by Kelemvor cannot be retrieved.**
    
3. **Resurrection magic must “call” the soul before Meridian crossing ends.**
    
4. **Gods cannot personally return a soul—they may only allow the spell to succeed.**
    

### **MDO Position:**

Excessive resurrection magic risks attracting Edict enforcement, often manifesting as Meridian flare or Weave resistance.

---

# **VIII. What Happens to Souls with No Faith?**

Souls without religious resonance undergo:

1. **Neutral Passage**
    
2. **Kelemvorite weighing** focusing only on lived intention
    
3. Placement into **Oghma, Gond, Eldath, or Selûne** dominions depending on temperament
    

These souls are often the easiest for clerics to resurrect—before judgment.

---

# **IX. The Final Dissolution — True Death**

Some souls do not enter any dominion. They experience:

- **Entropy decay** (Myrkul)
    
- **Memory annihilation** (Shar)
    
- **Cosmic reabsorption** (Apep, rarely)
    

This is not punishment—it is **finality**.

The Ministry refers to this phenomenon as **Absolute Quiet**, estimated to occur in <5% of souls.
