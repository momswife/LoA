## **I. Overview**

The term **Divine Realms** refers collectively to the distinct, layered territories that compose the celestial architecture above Aerathon. Unlike the mortal plane—bounded by geography, gravity, and chronology—the Divine Realms are structured by **concept**, **immaterial law**, and **pantheonic jurisdiction**.

Classical manuscripts divide these realms into three recognizable orders:

1. **The Near Heaven** — domain of the modern pantheon.
    
2. **The Far Heaven** — dominion of the Old Beastfolk Gods.
    
3. **The Interstitial Planes** — liminal spaces that serve as thresholds, conduits, or prisons of divine influence.
    

This dossier synthesizes the most stable conclusions recognized by the Ministry, though all scholars acknowledge significant gaps. Many realms are reachable only through mythic resonance, labyrinth distortion, or relic interaction—never by deliberate mortal traversal.

---

## **II. The Near Heaven — Seat of the Modern Pantheon**

The **Near Heaven** operates as the structured celestial network from which the gods of current worship extend their influence. It is the most comprehensible of all divine territories, though still far beyond mortal apprehension.

### **Characteristics**

- Composed of multiple **Dominion Strata**, each tied to a deity’s portfolio and doctrine.
    
- Exhibits **harmonic stability**, meaning its “terrain” is shaped by belief, ritual, and doctrinal clarity.
    
- Accessible only through **symbolic resonance**—prayer, oathbinding, clerical rites, and sanctioned miracles.
    

### **Known Dominion Examples**

- **Radiant Aerie** (Pelor): A realm defined by cyclical dawn phenomena.
    
- **The Stone Choir** (Moradin): A resonant plane of endless forges and echoing anvils.
    
- **Court of Bound Scales** (Tyr): A juridical horizon where oaths appear as luminous chains.
    

These dominions do not border each other physically; rather, they coexist as **adjacent truths**, navigable only by beings of divine or semi-divine nature.

---

## **III. The Far Heaven — Seat of the Old Beastfolk Gods**

Older than recorded history, the **Far Heaven** is a realm of mythic memory and ancestral law. It is not a structured pantheon-estate but a shifting, symbolic expanse whose form is dictated by cosmic cycles rather than mortal belief.

### **Characteristics**

- Exhibits **fluid metaphysics**, behaving more like a dreamscape than a spatial territory.
    
- Appears in early Beastfolk scripture as a sequence of **Trials**, **Gates**, and **Weighings**.
    
- Interacts with Aerathon primarily through **bloodline resonance**, labyrinth echoes, and relic activation.
    

### **Recovered Realm-Names**

- **The Hall of True Measures** — site of ancestral judgment.
    
- **The Jackal’s Crossing** — threshold between mortal and cosmic memory.
    
- **The Fourfold Dunes** — a desert-realm whose storms are said to contain unborn histories.
    

Scholarship remains limited: no modern cleric draws power directly from this realm, though its influence persists through artifacts and certain labyrinth floors.

---

## **IV. The Interstitial Planes — Corridors, Boundaries, and Silent Frontiers**

Between the two Heavens lie the **Interstitial Planes**—a collection of liminal realms that do not belong to any pantheon. They are neither divine dominions nor mortal worlds, but **connective tissues** binding creation together.

These planes are considered the most hazardous, as they are vulnerable to distortion, breach, and the unpredictable physics of Labyrinth expansion.

### **Sub-Regions Include:**

#### **1. The Veiled Meridian**

A boundary-layer inhibiting direct contact between Near and Far Heaven. Some scholars believe the Quiet Edict is encoded into this plane’s very geometry.

#### **2. The Astral Fold**

A space of raw ideation—used by gods to perceive mortal thoughtstreams. Small breaches manifest as déjà vu, prophecy fragments, or labyrinth foreshadowing.

#### **3. The Chthonic Null**

An unstable frontier where divine power thins to nonexistence. Relics sealed here often exhibit dangerous mutability or paradoxical behavior.

#### **4. The Echoed Stair**

An ascension-path seen only in near-death experiences or extreme relic resonance. Most reports end abruptly, with no confirmed traversal.

---

## **V. Divine Travel & Limitations**

While mortals cannot physically traverse the Divine Realms, the reverse is equally constrained. The **Quiet Edict** restricts gods from crossing directly into Aerathon or into rival dominions.

### **Known Limitations**

1. **No avatars** may physically enter Aerathon.
    
2. **No pantheon** may breach another’s dominion without triggering cosmic retribution.
    
3. **Relics** may serve as conduits, but only through heavily diluted channels.
    
4. **Labyrinths** may bypass certain boundaries—though whether by design or corruption remains disputed.
    

### **Implication for Clerical Magic**

Clerics draw from **filtered dominion-aspect**, not from raw divine presence.  
This is why clerical miracles vary subtly across cultures and individuals.

---

## **VI. Interactions with the Labyrinths**

The Labyrinths interact with divine realms in irregular ways:

- Some floors mimic **Near Heaven** architecture.
    
- Others pulse with **Far Heaven** iconography.
    
- Certain breach anomalies display **Interstitial** properties.
    
- Deep floors may generate **false dominions**—constructs mistaken for divine realms.
    

The Ministry’s current stance is conservative: no labyrinth should be assumed divine unless corroborated by Echo-Law, relic resonance, and Faithfield density readings.

## **VIII. Research Notes & Outstanding Questions**

Archivist Yelna lists the following inquiries as highest priority:

1. **Do the Divine Realms predate the Labyrinths, or vice-versa?**
    
2. **Why does Far Heaven maintain continuity without mortal worship?**
    
3. **Is the Quiet Edict external, self-imposed, or enforced?**
    
4. **Why do Near Heaven signatures appear disproportionately on lower labyrinth floors?**
    
5. **Are Interstitial Planes expanding, contracting, or reacting to delver presence?**
    

These remain unsolved but central to understanding future cosmological risk.
