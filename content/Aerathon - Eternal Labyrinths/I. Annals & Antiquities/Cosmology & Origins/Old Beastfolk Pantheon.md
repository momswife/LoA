## **I. Overview**

The **Old Beastfolk Pantheon**—known in surviving tablets as **The First Sovereigns** or **Those Who Remember the Shape of the World**—represents the earliest documented religious structure in Aerathon. These deities ruled not only over aspects of nature, death, craft, and magic, but over **ancestral truth itself**, binding Beastfolk lineage to cosmic cycles beyond mortal comprehension.

Their realm, the **Far Heaven**, predates all other known divine structures and remains active despite the near-total collapse of their worship. Modern scholars note that these gods do not weaken from neglect; rather, they operate according to **ancient pacts, buried rites, and immutable roles** forged before mortals learned the written word.

Jackalfolk, in particular, bear mythic connection to this pantheon. Their early priest-kings served as interpreters between Aerathon and the Far Heaven, a bond that dissolved when the Quiet Edict took effect.

The following entries constitute the most complete Ministry record of these deities, compiled from labyrinth inscriptions, relic-resonance studies, and recovered proto-scripts.

# **II. The Old Gods of the Far Heaven**

Each entry below includes:

- **Alignment** (mythic characterization, not moral law)
    
- **Suggested Domains** (for clerical scholars)
    
- **Symbol** (Far Heaven iconography)
    
- **MDO Notes** (relevance to labyrinths, relics, or Beastfolk lineage)
    

---

## **1. Re-Horakhty — The Solar Sovereign**

**Alignment:** LG  
**Domains:** Life, Light  
**Symbol:** Solar disk encircled by a serpent

**Overview:**  
Ruler of the First Sovereigns and the radiant heart of the Far Heaven. Depicted as the sun crowned by an eternal ouroboros-serpent, Re-Horakhty governed cycles of renewal, kingship, judgment, and cosmic order.

**MDO Notes:**

- Labyrinth floors with solar mosaics often correlate to relics exhibiting Far Heaven resonance.
    
- Some archivists argue Re-Horakhty represents the _original_ arbiter of divine balance before the Quiet Edict.
    

---

## **2. Anubis — The Jackal Judge**

**Alignment:** LN  
**Domain:** Death  
**Symbol:** Black jackal

**Overview:**  
Anubis presided over judgment, funerary rites, and the passage of souls. Jackalfolk inscriptions name him **The Path-Maker**, the one who separates truth from illusion.

**MDO Notes:**

- Jackalfolk bloodlines exhibit measurable Faithfield resonance near Anubian relics.
    
- Several high-tier labyrinths show iconography of a single black jackal watching from behind murals—always in shadow.
    

---

## **3. Apep — The Serpent of Ruin**

**Alignment:** NE  
**Domain:** Trickery  
**Symbol:** Flaming snake

**Overview:**  
Apep embodies cosmic destruction, serpentine flame, and the unraveling of divine order. Not worshipped—only feared. Myths claim his existence is necessary to maintain celestial balance.

**MDO Notes:**

- Labyrinth anomalies with cyclical ignition-patterns sometimes match Apep’s serpent-loop fractals.
    
- Breach events involving spontaneous combustion are occasionally attributed to Apep’s influence (unverified).
    

---

## **4. Bast — The Vengeful Huntress**

**Alignment:** CG  
**Domain:** War  
**Symbol:** Cat

**Overview:**  
A protector-goddess of Beastfolk clans, known equally for compassion and sudden, precise violence. Patron of trackers, scouts, and those who avenged wrongs.

**MDO Notes:**

- Claw-shaped relics associated with Bast show high success rates in predictive hazard mapping.
    
- Feral labyrinth guardians often display feline motifs consistent with her cult.
    

---

## **5. Bes — The Laughing Guardian**

**Alignment:** CN  
**Domain:** Trickery  
**Symbol:** Stylized figure of the misshapen deity

**Overview:**  
A god of luck, mirth, and household protection. Though comical in depiction, Bes was fiercely protective of infants, families, and wanderers.

**MDO Notes:**

- Minor relics marked with Bes’ icon often produce random yet statistically favorable outcomes.
    
- Some delvers swear Bes’ laughter has echoed during near-fatal escapes.
    

---

## **6. Hathor — The Hearth-Mother**

**Alignment:** NG  
**Domains:** Life, Light  
**Symbol:** Horned cow’s head with lunar disk

**Overview:**  
Goddess of love, joy, motherhood, and celestial music. She was said to sustain the emotional equilibrium of early Beastfolk tribes.

**MDO Notes:**

- Labyrinth floors with soft luminescence and harmonic resonance often correspond to Hathorian influence.
    
- A rare relic class, the “Milk-Glass Bells,” is attributed to her rites.
    

---

## **7. Imhotep — The First Maker**

**Alignment:** NG  
**Domain:** Knowledge  
**Symbol:** Step pyramid

**Overview:**  
A divine architect and physician, credited with teaching early Beastfolk the first principles of crafting, healing, and geometry.

**MDO Notes:**

- Many labyrinth puzzle-architectures show Imhotepian principles (precision ratios, mirrored chambers, sequential locks).
    
- He is believed—though not confirmed—to have influenced structural aspects of the first Labyrinths.
    

---

## **8. Isis — The Weaver of Hidden Threads**

**Alignment:** NG  
**Domains:** Knowledge, Life  
**Symbol:** Ankh and star

**Overview:**  
Goddess of magic, fertility, and protective spellcraft. The earliest Beastfolk rituals of bloodline awakening credit Isis as the origin-point.

**MDO Notes:**

- Binding spells found in deep labyrinths share glyph-forms nearly identical to Isis’ proto-script.
    
- Relics bearing the ankh motif often accelerate ritual attunement.
    

---

## **9. Nephthys — The Mourning Veil**

**Alignment:** CG  
**Domain:** Death  
**Symbol:** Horns around a lunar disk

**Overview:**  
Patroness of grief, final rites, and nocturnal guidance. Nephthys guarded the lost and forgotten, guiding souls that died far from home.

**MDO Notes:**

- Labyrinth “echo chambers” that preserve last-speech phenomena often show Nephthyan trace-signatures.
    
- Her relics stabilize emotional trauma, especially among delvers exposed to psychic hazards.
    

---

## **10. Osiris — The Verdant Sovereign**

**Alignment:** LG  
**Domains:** Life, Nature  
**Symbol:** Crook and flail

**Overview:**  
God of rebirth, natural cycles, and the underworld. Osiris taught Beastfolk the value of renewal and agricultural stewardship.

**MDO Notes:**

- Regenerative anomalies inside labyrinths strongly correlate to Osirian influence.
    
- Some scholars theorize that certain reborn labyrinth-beasts emulate Osirian myth cycles.
    

---

## **11. Ptah — The Whisper-Smith**

**Alignment:** LN  
**Domain:** Knowledge  
**Symbol:** Bull

**Overview:**  
A silent creator-god of secrets, invention, and craftsmanship. Ptah forged divine names and hidden truths.

**MDO Notes:**

- Ptah’s relics frequently veil themselves from detection until physically handled.
    
- First-era artificers attributed sentient forges and whispering tools to Ptah’s influence.
    

---

## **12. Set — The Storm of the Waste**

**Alignment:** CE  
**Domains:** Death, Tempest, Trickery  
**Symbol:** Coiled cobra

**Overview:**  
God of chaos, the desert, betrayal, and violent storms. The antithesis of Osiris, yet an essential force ensuring the world did not stagnate.

**MDO Notes:**

- Sudden labyrinth weather events—yes, some floors have storms—align with Setian patterning.
    
- Set-linked relics display volatile magic with high risk/high reward capability.
    

---

## **13. Sobek — The Crocodile Lord**

**Alignment:** LE  
**Domains:** Nature, Tempest  
**Symbol:** Crocodile head with horns and plumes

**Overview:**  
A river-god of strength, protection, and predatory dominance. Patron of marshland clans and ancient aquatic hunts.

**MDO Notes:**

- Submerged labyrinth sectors often include Sobekian carvings.
    
- Relics of this deity increase physical capability, but with notable aggression spikes.
    

---

## **14. Thoth — The Scribe of the First Word**

**Alignment:** N  
**Domain:** Knowledge  
**Symbol:** Ibis

**Overview:**  
The divine record-keeper, master of knowledge, language, prophecy, mathematics, and writing. Said to have invented timekeeping and celestial cataloguing.

**MDO Notes:**

- Labyrinths featuring chronological distortions frequently reference Thoth’s glyphs.
    
- “Chrono-Ibis” relics—exceedingly rare—stabilize or slow temporal anomalies.

## **III. Pantheon Structure & Cosmic Roles**

The Old Pantheon functioned through **complementary opposition**: creation against destruction, judgment against chaos, rebirth against decay. Unlike the Near Pantheon, these gods did not seek worship—they **demanded understanding**, obedience, and participation in cosmic cycles.

Their roles exist independently of mortal belief.  
Thus, even now, the Far Heaven remains active—though distant, silent, and bound by the Quiet Edict.

---

## **IV. Current Status Under the Quiet Edict**

- No direct avatars may manifest.
    
- Their influence reaches Aerathon only through symbolism, relics, echoes, and labyrinth distortion.
    
- Beastfolk bloodlines occasionally exhibit dormant resonance, especially Jackalfolk.
    
- Several Ministry-sealed cases document **ancestral dreams** believed to be Far Heaven contact.
