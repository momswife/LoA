## **I. Preface: On the Measurement of Time**

The Ministry defines **time** as both chronology and resonance — a sequence of causality recorded not merely by event, but by its echo upon the Weaves of reality.  
This timeline consolidates seven recognized epochs of Aerathonic history, from the **Orichalcum Dawn** to the present **Age of Spectacle**, incorporating cultural, magical, and cosmological perspectives across both **Brethren** and **Beastfolk** lineages.

As the Ministry notes:

> _“The past of Aerathon is not buried. It continues to surface — one ruin, one song, one labyrinth at a time.”_

All dates are given in **A.D.** (After Dawning), beginning from the moment the first measurable Weave-stabilization occurred at Sunwound Crater — the point where myth and matter first intersected.

## **III. Historical Overview**

### **The Orichalcum Dawn**

_“The metal dreamed before the gods did.”_  
The earliest age known only through resonance and myth. Both Brethren theologians and Beastfolk shamans agree that the world began when **sound became structure** — when the **Three Weaves of Reality** (Matter, Meaning, Motion) intertwined to form consciousness.  
From these vibrations came Orichalcum, a living alloy that sang when shaped.  
This period established the foundations of all later metaphysics: **creation through resonance**.  
The fall of this harmony — the cooling of divine song into silence — marks the start of linear time.

---

### **The Age of Crowns (0–1100 A.D.)**

_The world forgets who sang it into being, and crowns itself instead._  
In this first human-defined age, Brethren civilizations rose around the belief that divine favor conferred rulership.  
Each kingdom claimed descent from the “light-forged bloodlines” of the Orichalcum smiths.  
Monarchies reigned by **Law Divine**, interpreting miracles as justification for conquest.  
Elven and dwarven enclaves became tools of divine industry, while early Beastfolk populations vanished from record — erased, some say, by decree.  
Magic was prayer, and knowledge was treason.  
The era ends with the **Binding Concord**, a theocratic treaty that shackled the world beneath faith’s bureaucracy, sowing the seeds for rebellion.

---

### **The Age of Chains (1100–2100 A.D.)**

_“When order forgot mercy, law became a cage.”_  
This age marks the rise of empire and the weaponization of belief.  
Brethren cities forged the **Law and Light Empire**, where theology dictated physics and hierarchies.  
Through forced labor and magical servitude, the **Beastfolk** were born — shaped from divine experiments or surviving echoes of the primordial kin.  
The gods spoke openly in this era, but only through sanctioned intermediaries.  
Rebellions brewed among scholars and slaves alike, leading to the first **Echo-Wars**, when Beastfolk songs began rewriting law with sound.  
The divine silence that follows — the **First Withholding** — marks the end of the Age of Chains and the beginning of enlightenment by flame.

---

### **The Ember Age (2100–2600 A.D.)**

_The world learns to breathe without gods._  
Revolution sparks from smoldering ruins. The **Ember Collegia** and allied Beastfolk confederations rediscover arcane craft divorced from divinity.  
Magic becomes discipline; invention becomes faith.  
The forging of the first self-taught spellwrights ushers in a cultural renaissance.  
Guilds of artisans, engineers, and philosophers spread the doctrine:

> _“Heat is truth — what survives fire is worth believing.”_  
> In Beastfolk lands, the **Stone-Song Confederacy** and **Shell-Ledger Houses** codify ethics of memory and craft, ensuring survival through record rather than conquest.  
> But each innovation unravels the weave further. Labyrinths begin to form — subtle distortions of reality where too much meaning has been forced into matter.  
> By its end, the gods are silent, the skies dim, and civilization glows with dangerous self-confidence.

---

### **The Age of Collapse (2600–3100 A.D.)**

_The world exhales, and the sound is ruin._  
The great nations fall as the **Quiet Pantheon** takes hold.  
Faith withers into echo; the heavens turn indifferent.  
Famine, political schism, and labyrinth proliferation devastate the continents.  
Brethren and Beastfolk alike splinter into nomadic enclaves and fortress-cities.  
The Beastfolk Pride of Seven Roads reforms trade and diplomacy amid chaos; the **Mourn-Howl Circles** transform grief into sacred ritual.  
Brethren scholars pursue desperate order, birthing the **Convergence Courts**, whose attempt to mechanize miracles causes the largest magical implosion in history — the **First Collapse Breach**.  
The era ends in silence and exhaustion; only wanderers remain to sift through the dust.

---

### **The Age of Delving (3100–3700 A.D.)**

_The brave call it progress; the wise call it penance._  
In the Collapse’s aftermath, the survivors organize around the one reliable constant: the Labyrinths.  
The act of delving — descending into the world’s living memories — becomes both livelihood and liturgy.  
Beastfolk and Brethren collaborate at last, forming the first **Free Guild Accords** to regulate relic retrieval.  
From these accords grows the **Ministry of Delving Operations**, the bureaucratic descendant of all prior empires and priesthoods.  
Magic stabilizes into regulated practice; relics become property.  
The Ministry teaches restraint, yet the populace hungers for spectacle.  
The world reawakens, unaware that it still treads on memory.

---

### **The Age of Spectacle (3700 A.D.–Present)**

_The past performs for the living, and no one dares to look away._  
Modern Aerathon thrives on observation.  
Delving is now not just duty but entertainment; labyrinth expeditions broadcast through crystal relays, relics auctioned like art.  
Faith exists as nostalgia, and politics masquerades as prophecy.  
The labyrinths adapt — reshaping themselves around crowds and stories, learning the language of attention.  
The Ministry maintains order, but beneath every archive hums unease:

> _If the world remembers itself through us, what happens when we stop remembering?_

Prophets whisper of a coming **Seventh Era**, the _Age of Reversal_, when the spiral of time uncoils and the world unweaves its own history.

- **Era Definitions:** Determined by shifts in faith density, linguistic dominance, and labyrinth proliferation curves.
    
- **Cross-Divisional Consensus:** Annals, Breach, and Faith bureaus concur on six stable epochs with a potential seventh pending confirmation.
