### _Standard Reckoning of Wreaths, Cycles, and Turns_

The **Aerathonian calendar** is the unified temporal model adopted by every major kingdom, guild-city, and delver society across the known world.  
It arose in the wake of the **First Labyrinth Incursions**, when time fractures, warped days, and contradictory delver reports made interregional communication nearly impossible.

Where older realms once clung to isolated lunar counts or seasonal omens, the new calendar formalized **celestial observation**, **delver accessibility windows**, and **divine festivals** into one structured system.  
Today it synchronizes everything from **multi-realm sponsorship deals** to **S-Class delve broadcasts**, serving as the quiet spine behind the Age of Spectacle.

---

## **I. Structure of the Aerathonian Year**

- **Year Length:** _360 days_
    
- **Months:** _12_
    
- **Days per Month:** _30_
    
- **Weeks per Month:** _5_
    
- **Days per Week:** _6_
    

This structure—called a **Wreath**—is mirrored magically within several deep Labyrinth strata, a phenomenon that chronoscribes argue is _not_ coincidence.

## **V. Special Observances & Delver Events**

### **• The Day of First Descent — Embermarch 1st**

Marks the first documented Labyrinth breach. Bonfires are lit, and novice delvers perform mock descents.

### **• The Gilded Reckoning — Highsun 15th–20th**

A global sensation: cross-realm tournaments, S-Rank broadcasts, guild auctions, and sponsorship duels.

### **• Twilight Wake — Duskrend 30th**

Delvers leave offerings for those lost in the Labyrinths. Silence is mandatory in many guild halls.

### **• The Veiled Assembly — Final Veilsday of Snowturn**

Clerics of the various pantheons perform miracles, blessings, and omens—some of which rewrite delve permissions for the next year.

---

## **VI. Temporal Magic & Calendrical Rites**

- **Chronoscribes** maintain accurate time during long delves where hours or years may compress or stretch.
    
- **Timebinders** interpret celestial shifts to determine which days are _sanctioned_ or _forbidden_ for delving.
    
- The **Divine Oculi** issue seasonal edicts regulating dangerous delve dates, often aligning with solar eclipses or planar thinnings.
    
- Major guilds enchant their **calendar stones** to update automatically with stellar trajectories and Labyrinth distortions.
