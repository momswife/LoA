## **Overview**

The **Orichalcum Age** is the oldest recorded (and contested) epoch in Aerathon’s remembered history—a radiant era when craft and consciousness were indistinguishable. Few written records survive, but countless ruins, song fragments, and metallurgical anomalies point toward a civilization whose mastery of material and magic surpassed even the later **High Council of Flame**.

Orichalcum, a golden alloy that sang when struck, was said to be the breath of the world made solid. From it, the ancients wove tools, conduits, and entire cities that shimmered with stored light.

When it ended, the world did not burn—it **dimmed**.

---

## **Cultural Snapshot**

### **Society & Belief**

The Orichalcum peoples viewed creation as a reciprocal act: **craft was worship**, and worship was craft. Temples doubled as forges; philosophers were engineers; rulers were “Artisans of Accord.” Hierarchy was defined not by birth but by one’s ability to bind song to matter.

They revered no pantheon—only the principle of **Balance through Making**, a proto-faith that may have inspired later gods of forge, light, and knowledge.

---

### **Architecture & Legacy**

Remnants of their work persist: pillars that refuse to corrode, submerged domes beneath the Shattersea, and spiral lenses embedded in cliffsides that still track the sun. Delvers report faint singing vibrations when near Orichalcum deposits—a harmonic hum mistaken for divine resonance.

Some labyrinths exhibit structural parallels to these ruins, suggesting the Labyrinths themselves may have **absorbed** or **repurposed** this ancient architecture.

---

### **Languages & Symbols**

Fragments of a proto-script called **Auric Line** survive on relics and shard-tablets. The writing glows faintly when touched by spellcasters but cannot be fully translated. Known symbols correspond to concepts of “breath,” “mirror,” “cycle,” and “return.”

Scholars from the Ministry of Delving Operations hypothesize it functioned more as an **intent recorder** than a written language—capturing thought patterns rather than words.

---

## **Collapse & The Sundering Silence**

The fall of the Orichalcum Age is shrouded in contradictory accounts.  
Some claim a solar misalignment—the **Great Eclipse of the Forge Sun**—caused the alloy’s light to die, plunging entire cities into dormancy.  
Others believe the people simply transcended, “forging themselves into song,” leaving only their tools behind.

Whatever the truth, their disappearance created the cultural vacuum later filled by the Twelve Kingdoms and the bloodline empires of the **Age of Crowns**.
