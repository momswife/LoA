## **Overview**

Throughout Aerathon’s history, **clothing has been confession**.  
Every age, empire, and creed has clothed itself not merely for protection but for declaration — of station, of loyalty, of truth.  
Long before written sigils or guild banners, attire marked who belonged, who was owed, and who could be trusted.

From the dye-saturated silks of the Pride Roads to the ink-stained aprons of the Ember Collegia, society learned to read fabric as if it were scripture.  
The Ministry defines these customs collectively as the **Semiotics of Presentation** — “the visible lexicon by which civilization recognizes itself.”

---

## **Status & Station**

During the **Age of Crowns**, hierarchy was literally worn:

- **High Nobility:** layered velvets with embroidered starbursts — symbols of sanctioned magic.
    
- **Middle Trades:** linen tunics bearing stamped dyes indicating guild or parish.
    
- **Servitors & Laborers:** undyed hemp; any ornament beyond function was taxed as luxury.
    

For **Beastfolk**, display differed by clade:

- **Leonine & Hyenid:** braids and manebands denoted triumphs; gold-thread for victory, iron for survival.
    
- **Caninefolk:** scent-imbued leathers marked household allegiance.
    
- **Ursine:** simple girdles with carved bone fasteners engraved with family tone patterns.
    
- **Aarakocra:** plumage ribbons that shifted hue in sunlight, each feather’s tint narrating ancestry.
    

Mixed societies blurred these codes, leading to early color-standard laws — ironically paving the way for modern guild uniforms.

---

## **The Era of Symbols**

With the rise of delver guilds during the **Age of Delving**, fashion became codified messaging.  
Badges, pins, and tabards evolved into **Signwear** — layered elements carrying enchanted thread that glowed under guild light or resonated when near allies.

The **Free Guild City of Louvain** developed the first **Thread-Seals**, woven with minimal illusion enchantments that prevented forgery.  
Each Ministry department still uses these same embroidery wards in formal robes and insignia.

Cultural historians identify three prevailing “languages” of clothing from this age:

1. **Heraldic:** using color and sigil to indicate rank or faction (common among Brethren).
    
2. **Functional:** pockets, belts, and fabrics designed for terrain and trade (common among Beastfolk).
    
3. **Symbolic:** objects or gestures augmenting apparel, such as finger-chains, hair clasps, or claw paints (shared among delvers and hybrid guilds).
    

---

## **Gesture & Motion as Language**

Beyond fabric, Aerathon’s cultures employ **micro-gestures** that act as punctuation to the visible word:

- The **Open Paw** (Pride origin): peace, fair trade, or mutual recognition.
    
- The **Hand Over Heartstone** (Allemance origin): gratitude and surrender, used in formal apology.
    
- The **Lantern Dip** (Pilgrim tradition): lowering one’s lantern to waist height to indicate safe passage.
    
- The **Folded Wing** (Sky-Reeve origin): acknowledgment of authority or surrender in aerial combat.
    

These signs remain vital to delvers traversing multilingual regions.  
Ministry field manuals include a glossary of fifty such gestures under the section _“When Words Fail.”_

---

## **Taboos and Transgressions**

The misuse of signwear carries grave insult.  
To wear another’s braid, clan clasp, or insignia without permission is **theft of self**, punishable by ritual humiliation or exile.  
In Beastfolk clans, the wearing of a rival’s cloth over one’s shoulder symbolized conquest — a custom gradually outlawed after the **Treaty of Breath & Beast**.

Among delver guilds, **unauthorized sigil display** constitutes “visual fraud.”  
The MDO maintains legal authority over all recognized insignia, and forging one carries the same penalty as forging a royal seal.

---

## **Modern Continuations**

Even in the **Age of Spectacle**, the language of dress thrives:

- Guild uniforms employ subtle color harmonics derived from old clan tones.
    
- Academy robes of the **Arcane Breach Division** shimmer between blue and gold — a nod to Orichalcum alloy.
    
- Beastfolk artisans incorporate old oaths into thread patterns, visible only under moonlight.
    
- Civilian fashion trends borrow insignia shapes, though stripped of meaning — a quiet irony the Ministry tolerates.
    

Despite changes, every delver still checks a companion’s clothing before a delve — for color, for emblem, for the telltale seams that say _friend_ or _fraud_.
