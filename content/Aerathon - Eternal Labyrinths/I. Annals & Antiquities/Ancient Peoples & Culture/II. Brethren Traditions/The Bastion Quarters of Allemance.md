## **Overview**

The **Bastion-Quarters of Allemance** were a network of fortified urban districts that arose during the waning days of the Ember Age, when civil unrest and labyrinth emergence forced cities to turn inward.  
Each Bastion was a self-contained micro-city — a walled district with its own food stores, forges, aquifers, and temples.

They represented the first synthesis of **architectural defense and civic autonomy**, where masonry itself was political language.  
To build a Bastion was to declare independence.

---

## **Origins & Purpose**

The movement began after the **Fireglass Riots**, when collapsing Collegia experiments ignited half of Allemance’s riverfront.  
The survivors — masons, veterans, and craftsmen — swore to never again trust a single central ruler or deity.  
Instead, they constructed **Quarters**: districts governed by compact, guarded by stone, and sanctified by communal labor.

Each Quarter built its walls differently to reflect its founders’ values:

- **North Quarter:** dwarven stone-ribs and echo-wards.
    
- **East Quarter:** elven terraces and wind channels for spell dispersal.
    
- **South Quarter:** human redbrick labyrinthine streets for misdirection.
    
- **West Quarter:** ursine echo-law vaults that could store entire chants within the stone.
    

At their heart lay the **Bastion Heart** — a crystalline node engraved with the oaths of every citizen, capable of resonating protective barriers when chanted in unison.

---

## **Society & Defense Culture**

Life within the Bastions revolved around maintenance and ritualized vigilance.  
Every citizen learned at least one craft related to fortification: carving, mortar-mixing, resonance humming, or ward painting.  
Children were trained to recognize wall vibrations the way sailors know the sea.

Allemance’s walls **sang**, literally — harmonics woven into the masonry created low droning tones at night, meant to warn of approaching tremors or arcane distortion. These “wall-carols” later evolved into ceremonial songs still performed by masons’ guilds across Aerathon.

Culturally, Bastion dwellers equated **safety with duty**. Every door was a checkpoint, every courtyard a refuge, every silence a held breath.  
In time, paranoia and pride became indistinguishable.

---

## **The Siege Codex**

Compiled by the **Engineer-Judge Maern Vosk** (later commemorated as an MDO legacy archivist), the _Siege Codex_ collected centuries of siege records, architectural experiments, and “psychological stress diagrams.”  
It included detailed instructions on how to re-channel emotional fear into stone resonance, an early theory that birthed **empathic warding** — the magic discipline now used to stabilize labyrinth barriers.

---

## **Decline**

When the labyrinth phenomenon intensified, Bastion walls proved both salvation and trap.  
Those who sealed themselves in endured longer, but at great cost: starvation, civic madness, and resonance collapse.  
The western Bastion of Allemance fell after its walls began echoing the screams of the dying — a feedback loop of fear that shattered stone itself.

By the dawn of the **Age of Collapse**, the surviving Quarters were united into a single confederated city, adopting the Bastion-Carver’s motto:

> _“We do not fall — we close.”_

Modern Allemance still bears these walls, now studded with delver watch-towers and Ministry sigil stations.  
Their “singing stone” is studied by the MDO for its ability to store emotional signatures — an echo of the first empathic architecture.
