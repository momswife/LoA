### Overview

This document codifies the Ministry of Delving Operations’ **current survivable model** of divinity.

It is not intended to define what gods _should be_, nor what they _claim_ to be.  
It defines what gods **are observed to be**, how they persist, and why their unregulated activity has repeatedly resulted in existential-scale failures.

This doctrine exists because previous models were insufficient.  
Those insufficiencies no longer exist.

---

## I. Operational Definition of Divinity

For the purposes of Ministry classification and enforcement, a **Divine Entity** is defined as:

> A self-coherent concentration of agency capable of exerting influence across multiple layers of recorded reality without continuous physical presence, sustained through external witness, structured belief, and/or systemic inscription.

This definition is functional, not philosophical.

Divinity is treated as a **state of influence**, not a moral condition, birthright, or immutable rank.

---

## II. Core Properties Shared by All Verified Divine Entities

All confirmed divine entities—regardless of pantheon, origin myth, or cultural framing—exhibit the following properties:

### Supra-Local Agency

Divine entities are capable of acting beyond their immediate location, often simultaneously across distant sites. This capacity degrades sharply near thresholds, null zones, and unrecorded regions.

### Witness Dependence

No god persists without **external stabilization**. This may take the form of:

- Widespread belief
    
- Ritualized invocation
    
- Institutional record
    
- Persistent symbolic infrastructure
    

Private belief alone is insufficient to maintain divine coherence.

### Conceptual Anchoring

Each divine entity is bound to one or more **conceptual domains** (e.g., judgment, decay, harvest, silence). Actions taken outside these anchors generate severe Echo backlash and structural instability.

### Echo Accumulation

Divine action produces Echo at magnitudes exponentially greater than mortal acts. Pre-Edict history confirms that unmitigated Echo accumulation leads to cascading reality failure.

---

## III. What Divinity Is Not

For clarity and risk mitigation, the following statements are affirmed:

- Gods are not absolute creators of reality
    
- Gods are not exempt from causality
    
- Gods are not immune to contradiction
    
- Gods are not sustained by truth alone
    

Multiple divine deaths are historically verified.  
Several divine remains continue to pose active containment risks.

---

## IV. Competing Origin Models 

The Ministry recognizes three dominant explanatory models. None are considered complete.

### Model A — Emergent Aggregation

Divinity arises when belief, narrative, and ritual surpass a critical density, forming stable agency.

_Strength:_ Explains regional pantheons and god-death via cultural collapse  
_Limitation:_ Cannot account for pre-civilizational entities

### Model B — Primordial Survivorship

Gods are remnants of earlier reality states, adapted to persist under current constraints.

_Strength:_ Aligns with prehistoric conflict records and non-human pantheons  
_Limitation:_ Fails to explain verifiable modern-origin deities

### Model C — External Ingress

Divinity represents intrusion from outside recorded reality, stabilized by mortal systems.

**Supporting material restricted under Level-IV clearance.**

---

## V. Maintenance Costs and Limitations

Divinity is not self-sustaining.

Observed maintenance costs include:

- Continuous Echo expenditure
    
- Narrative consistency enforcement
    
- Suppression of internal contradiction
    
- Restriction of direct intervention
    

Entities that exceed sustainable influence thresholds exhibit:

- Avatar instability
    
- Realm collapse
    
- Name erosion
    
- Eventual dormancy or fragmentation
    

---

## VI. Divine Failure States

Divine entities may degrade into one or more of the following conditions:

### Dormancy

Agency collapses inward. The entity persists but does not act.

### Fragmentation

Conceptual anchors split into lesser aspects, frequently hostile or contradictory.

### Degradation

Loss of identity coherence. Entity becomes an environmental hazard, curse-field, or labyrinth-adjacent anomaly.

### Annihilation

Rare. Requires total removal of recording vectors. Residual Echo contamination is guaranteed.

---

## VII. The Quiet Edict (Contextual Summary)

The Quiet Edict is not a moral law.  
It is a **containment protocol**.

Historical review demonstrates that unrestricted divine action:

- Destabilizes large-scale reality
    
- Produces non-linear collateral damage
    
- Treats mortal extinction as acceptable loss
    

The Edict exists because continued existence required it.

---

## VIII. Mortal Interaction Summary

Mortals interact with divine entities through:

- Structured worship (stabilization)
    
- Oaths and vows (direct Echo routing)
    
- Relics and sites (anchoring nodes)
    
- Mass witness events (amplification with volatility risk)
    

Unauthorized experimentation has resulted in:

- Localized reality collapse
    
- Spontaneous god-spawn
    
- Audience-driven anomaly escalation
    

---

## Concluding Assessment

Divine entities are not custodians of reality.  
They are participants within it—operating at scale, under constraint.

They can be bargained with.  
They can be wounded.  
They can fail.

> _Divinity is dangerous not because it is evil, but because it is powerful, limited, and poorly suited to restraint._
