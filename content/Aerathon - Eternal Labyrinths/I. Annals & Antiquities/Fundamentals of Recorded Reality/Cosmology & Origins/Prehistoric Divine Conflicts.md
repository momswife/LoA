## **I. Overview**

The **Prehistoric Divine Conflicts** are a series of catastrophic and poorly understood confrontations between pantheonic forces that occurred long before written history. These events shaped the very structure of the cosmos, defined the boundaries between the Near and Far Heavens, and set the stage for the Quiet Edict—a metaphysical ban that still governs divine behavior.

No single account survives intact.  
Everything known derives from fragmentary stelae, labyrinth inscriptions, relic resonance, and proto-myth cycles that survived only through Jackal Progenitor oral tradition.

The Ministry classifies these events under **Pantheon Conflict Category: Redline**, due to their potential implications for modern divine phenomena.

---

## **II. The Dawn Schism — The First Sundering of the Heavens**

The earliest and most widely attested conflict is the **Dawn Schism**, a cosmic dispute among the First Sovereigns of the Far Heaven.

### **Key Features**

- Stemmed from differing philosophies of **order, destruction, renewal, and truth**.
    
- Involved catastrophic clashes between deities resembling **Re-Horakhty**, **Apep**, **Osiris**, and **Set**.
    
- Resulted in the first fracture of the celestial layers, producing proto-dimensions that would later become the Interstitial Planes.
    

### **Consequences**

- **The Veiled Meridian** formed — the divider between realms.
    
- The First Sovereigns began assuming fixed **cosmic roles** rather than freely contested dominions.
    
- The Far Heaven adopted its fluid dream-like state, shaped by myth rather than hierarchy.
    

**MDO Note:**  
Labyrinth zones exhibiting solar-serpentine imagery frequently reference this schism.

---

## **III. The War of Names — Conflict of Knowledge and Becoming**

This conflict centered on **Thoth**, **Ptah**, and various now-lost deities associated with secrets and creation. This era saw combat not with weapons but with **concepts**.

### **Key Features**

- Battles fought through **alterations of divine language** and **rewriting of cosmic laws**.
    
- Creation tools—glyphs, sigils, and true names—were weaponized.
    
- The mortality of certain proto-deities is attributed to their names being erased.
    

### **Consequences**

- The concept of a **“true name”** became the foundation of both magic and identity.
    
- Several Interstitial Planes formed from discarded or broken words.
    
- Relics such as the **Star-Trellis Maps** appear to contain written scars from this conflict.
    

**MDO Note:**  
Arcane cartographers note that some labyrinth glyphs act as “name traps,” echoing hazards from this war.

---

## **IV. The Verdant Revolt — The Struggle Over Life and Death**

Centered on deities of rebirth, wilderness, and the underworld—primarily **Osiris**, **Hathor**, and oppositional forces linked to **Set** and **Nephthys**—this revolt defined the rules of mortal life.

### **Key Features**

- Contests over the **ownership of souls** and the **cycle of reincarnation**.
    
- Beastfolk tribes believed they were shaped directly by this conflict.
    
- Myths suggest Osiris was once “killed” and resurrected, stabilizing the cycle.
    

### **Consequences**

- Mortality became **linear** rather than cyclical for most species.
    
- The underworld boundaries formalized.
    
- Some scholars argue this conflict created the earliest **proto-Labyrinth spaces**, where life and death overlap unnaturally.
    

**MDO Note:**  
Far Heaven relics from this era can destabilize biological processes—regenerating or decaying tissue unpredictably.

---

## **V. The Jackal Tribunate — The Age of Divine Mediation**

In the late stages of the conflicts, Beastfolk—guided by the Jackal Progenitors—became **mediators** between warring gods.

### **Key Features**

- Jackalfolk served as **judges, interpreters, and envoys**.
    
- Anubis was believed to empower mortal agents directly during this period.
    
- Records describe a tribunal of divine and mortal beings called the **Thrice-Weighted Court**, though its location remains unknown.
    

### **Consequences**

- Several divine confrontations ceased abruptly after Jackalfolk intervention.
    
- Mortal spiritual authority increased dramatically.
    
- Modern scholars believe the **Quiet Edict** was born from this period’s negotiations.
    

**MDO Note:**  
Labyrinth rooms with black-jackal carvings often contain judicial or moral puzzles; scholars consider these echoes of the Tribunate.

---

## **VI. The Serpent’s Eclipse — The Final Celestial Catastrophe**

This event, tied to Apep and Set, is believed to be the last divine conflict before the Edict silenced all further war.

### **Key Features**

- Involved a breach between the Far Heaven and the mortal plane.
    
- Myth fragments describe a world-consuming serpent of fire and shadow.
    
- Near-total collapse of Beastfolk civilizations of the era.
    

### **Consequences**

- The heavens were **rebuilt** or **re-aligned**, forming the Two Heavens structure.
    
- The Interstitial Planes became unstable frontier zones.
    
- Divine intervention ceased abruptly across all myth cycles.
    

**MDO Note:**  
Only one relic—**The Ashen Chain**—is confirmed to originate from this era. Its activation protocols are sealed under §12-F.

---

## **VII. The Quiet Edict — Birth of the Silence**

Following the Serpent’s Eclipse, all surviving sources agree that a binding force—origin uncertain—was enacted:

### **Terms of the Edict**

1. No pantheon may manifest in full on Aerathon.
    
2. No pantheon may wage war on another.
    
3. Divine influence must be **indirect**, filtered, symbolic, or mediated.
    

### **Unresolved Scholarship**

- Was the Edict imposed _by gods_, _against gods_, or _by a third metaphysical authority_?
    
- Why do both Near and Far Heavens obey the Edict with perfect consistency?
    
- Did the rise of the Near Heaven correlate directly with the Edict’s enforcement?
    

**MDO Note:**  
The Edict’s signatures appear faintly in the Veiled Meridian, suggesting it may be metaphysical law rather than pact.

---

## **VIII. Implications for Modern Aerathon**

These prehistoric conflicts established the entire cosmological framework under which Aerathon now exists:

- Divine power is constrained.
    
- Pantheons cannot unify or war.
    
- Magic governing identity and death derives from ancient compromises.
    
- Labyrinths may be remnants or scars of these old confrontations.
    
- Beastfolk ancestries still resonate with Far Heaven during labyrinthic pressure.
    

The Ministry considers studying these conflicts essential for predicting future celestial anomalies—and potential Edict destabilization.
