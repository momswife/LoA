# **Echoes, Ghosts and Interrupted Passage**

### _The Remnants Left by Death, the Souls That Fail to Cross, and the Unnatural Continuance of the Dead_

**Filed Division:** Ministry of Delving Operations — Annals & Antiquities  
**Responsible Office:** Mortuary and Post-Existence Bureau  
**Primary Compiler:** Inspector Jao “Greyglass” Vell  
**Contributing Scholars:** Priest-Analyst Thera Daan, Archivist Yelna Stonevoice, Magistrate Talar Shellmark, Lt. Salvia Korrin  
**Current Revision:** 3388 A.D., Age of Spectacle  
**Document Class:** _Foundational Post-Mortal Classification / Grade-I Verified Phenomena with Grade-II Causal Interpretation_  
**Primary Evidence:** Labyrinth death investigations, spirit-contact records, necromantic seizures, resurrection examinations, Echo containment reports, psychopomp testimony

---

## **I. Purpose of This Record**

Death does not always proceed cleanly.

A soul may refuse passage, become trapped, be forcibly bound, or return through an unfinished purpose. A corpse may be animated without its soul. A Labyrinth may preserve the memory of a death long after the deceased has passed beyond it.

These phenomena are often mistaken for one another.

That confusion has caused:

- failed exorcisms;
    
- unlawful destruction of trapped souls;
    
- false resurrection claims;
    
- unnecessary fear of harmless Echoes;
    
- and attempts to communicate with memories incapable of answering.
    

This record distinguishes the principal phenomena associated with interrupted or imitated death.

Its most important conclusion is simple:

> **A Ghost is a soul. An Echo is a memory. Undeath is a condition.**

The three may overlap.

They are not the same.

---

## **II. Ordinary Passage and Its Failure**

Under ordinary conditions, death proceeds through:

1. bodily cessation;
    
2. soul loosening;
    
3. Final Recognition;
    
4. crossing of the Veiled Meridian;
    
5. reception within the Netherworld or another lawful road;
    
6. judgment and Final Rest.
    

An **Interrupted Passage** occurs when the soul cannot complete this sequence.

Possible causes include:

- sudden magical death;
    
- necromantic binding;
    
- possession;
    
- curses;
    
- damaged celestial roads;
    
- unresolved covenants;
    
- extreme attachment;
    
- relic entanglement;
    
- divine interference;
    
- or Labyrinth assimilation.
    

Not every unusual event surrounding death indicates interruption.

A body may produce an Echo while the soul crosses normally.

A corpse may be animated after the soul has departed.

A voice may be preserved without any continuing awareness behind it.

Investigation must determine what, if anything, remains.

---

# **PART ONE: ECHOES**

## **III. What an Echo Is**

An **Echo** is a corrupted memory unintentionally preserved by a Labyrinth when a living being dies within it.

The deceased may be:

- mortal;
    
- animal;
    
- monster;
    
- fey;
    
- celestial;
    
- or another sufficiently coherent living entity.
    

The Labyrinth records the event because death produces a sudden collapse in the relationship between:

- Matter;
    
- Meaning;
    
- Motion;
    
- and soul-presence.
    

For one moment, the Labyrinth witnesses a living identity become a body from which that identity has departed.

It attempts to retain what occurred.

It does so imperfectly.

The resulting memory becomes embedded within the Labyrinth and partially expressed as reality.

This is an Echo.

---

## **IV. What an Echo Is Not**

An Echo is not:

- the deceased’s soul;
    
- a ghost;
    
- an undead spirit;
    
- a deliberate illusion;
    
- a normal memory;
    
- or proof that the deceased remains trapped.
    

The soul may complete ordinary passage while an Echo remains indefinitely.

A delver may:

- die inside a Labyrinth;
    
- cross the Veiled Meridian;
    
- undergo judgment;
    
- enter Final Rest;
    
- and later be resurrected
    

while the Echo of their death continues occupying the chamber where it occurred.

The returned person and the Echo may therefore exist at the same time.

One is the continuing soul.

The other is the Labyrinth’s damaged record.

---

## **V. Appearance of an Echo**

Echoes often resemble still-life scenes that reality has failed to render correctly.

Common manifestations include:

- a figure suspended in the act of falling;
    
- a body repeating the same final movement;
    
- blood hanging motionless in the air;
    
- a weapon shifting between several nearby positions;
    
- a creature frozen halfway through a roar;
    
- voices playing without visible speakers;
    
- a room briefly appearing as it was at the instant of death;
    
- or a person turning their head through the same broken sequence.
    

Echoes frequently display **recording faults**, including:

- missing facial features;
    
- limbs occupying contradictory positions;
    
- repeated fragments of speech;
    
- abrupt changes in lighting;
    
- objects passing through one another;
    
- silence where sound should exist;
    
- sound occurring before movement;
    
- and portions of the scene vanishing when viewed directly.
    

Some Echoes remain entirely still until a living creature crosses a particular point.

Others replay continuously.

Many alternate unpredictably between stillness and motion.

---

## **VI. Echo Boundaries**

Most Echoes are restricted to the place where the death occurred.

Their boundaries may be defined by:

- the chamber;
    
- the visible scene;
    
- a specific object;
    
- the path followed during the final moments;
    
- or an area of strong Labyrinth influence.
    

An Echo ordinarily cannot travel beyond the memory that defines it.

A figure that died while reaching for a door may repeat that action but cannot choose to walk through another exit.

A dying creature may turn toward a sound if that reaction was part of the original event.

It cannot decide to pursue someone it never encountered.

The Labyrinth may later enlarge, distort, or combine the memory.

When this occurs, the Echo may appear to develop new behavior.

This is not necessarily true awareness.

It may be the Labyrinth repeatedly rewriting the recording.

---

## **VII. Stored Memories That Are Not Echoes**

Labyrinths can unintentionally preserve memories even when no death occurs.

Such phenomena are classified as **Stored Memories**, **Residual Scenes**, or **Labyrinth Impressions**, depending upon their stability.

They may preserve:

- a conversation;
    
- a ritual;
    
- repeated labor;
    
- a strong emotional event;
    
- a battle in which no one died;
    
- or the ordinary activity of people who once occupied the location.
    

These memories may resemble Echoes.

The Ministry reserves the term **Echo** for memories formed through death within a Labyrinth.

The distinction matters because death creates a deeper and more unstable imprint than ordinary experience.

A Stored Memory may show what occurred.

An Echo often preserves the exact moment when reality lost a living soul.

---

## **VIII. Can an Echo Think?**

Ordinary Echoes do not possess continuing will.

They may display:

- apparent fear;
    
- apparent recognition;
    
- fragmented speech;
    
- defensive motion;
    
- or limited response to repeated stimuli.
    

These behaviors usually belong to the recorded event.

An Echo may answer a question if the original person answered a similar question shortly before death. It may look toward a newcomer who stands where the killer once stood. It may plead for help because the deceased pleaded during the original event.

Such behavior can be emotionally convincing.

It does not prove awareness.

A true person can:

- form new intentions;
    
- reconsider;
    
- learn;
    
- recognize unfamiliar circumstances;
    
- and act beyond prior patterns.
    

An Echo ordinarily cannot.

---

## **IX. Echo Corruption**

Echoes often change over time.

Corruption may result from:

- alteration of the surrounding floor;
    
- repeated exposure to delvers;
    
- nearby magic;
    
- damage to the Labyrinth;
    
- overlapping deaths;
    
- relic resonance;
    
- or the Labyrinth attempting to integrate the memory into a new purpose.
    

A corrupted Echo may:

- combine several deaths into one scene;
    
- place words in the wrong mouth;
    
- substitute one person’s face for another;
    
- change the apparent cause of death;
    
- repeat an event that never occurred;
    
- or begin incorporating living observers into the recording.
    

The last condition is particularly dangerous.

A delver may enter an Echo and find their movements becoming constrained by the remembered event.

They may be forced into the position of:

- victim;
    
- attacker;
    
- witness;
    
- or rescuer.
    

This is called **Echo Envelopment**.

The person is not transported into the past.

The Labyrinth is attempting to complete or restage the stored memory using present material.

---

## **X. Multiple Echoes**

Where several deaths occur in the same location, their Echoes may overlap.

Possible results include:

### **Layered Echoes**

Several distinct scenes occupy the same space and appear at different intervals.

### **Composite Echoes**

Features from multiple deaths merge into one impossible event.

### **Recursive Echoes**

One death repeatedly triggers another stored death, creating a continuing sequence.

### **Contradictory Echoes**

The same event appears in incompatible forms.

Contradiction does not necessarily mean one Echo is false.

A Labyrinth may record:

- several perspectives;
    
- several possible outcomes;
    
- or an event after its local reality has already begun to fail.
    

Echoes are valuable evidence.

They are not perfect records.

---

## **XI. Echoes as Evidence**

An Echo may preserve details unavailable elsewhere, including:

- the appearance of a killer;
    
- a hidden route;
    
- the last words of the deceased;
    
- a trap activating;
    
- a relic changing state;
    
- or the earlier form of a chamber.
    

However, investigators must account for:

- corruption;
    
- missing information;
    
- repeated reconstruction;
    
- symbolic substitution;
    
- and interference from later observers.
    

An Echo should be treated as a damaged witness recording.

It must not be treated as unquestionable truth.

No person may be convicted solely upon an unauthenticated Echo.

---

## **XII. Destroying an Echo**

Destroying an Echo does not ordinarily harm the soul of the deceased.

The Echo can often be disrupted by:

- altering the chamber;
    
- removing its anchor;
    
- applying counter-resonance;
    
- collapsing the stored pattern;
    
- or severing the Labyrinth structure sustaining it.
    

Destruction may still carry consequences.

It may erase:

- evidence;
    
- historical information;
    
- warnings of a recurring hazard;
    
- or the final known record of a missing person.
    

Some families request preservation.

Others consider the continued replay of a loved one’s death intolerable.

The Ministry weighs investigative value against the rights and welfare of the living.

No family possesses automatic ownership of an Echo, but their wishes should be considered before public study or exhibition.

---

# **PART TWO: GHOSTS AND SOULS THAT REMAIN**

## **XIII. What a Ghost Is**

A **Ghost** is the surviving soul, or a substantial portion of it, prevented from completing lawful passage.

Unlike an Echo, a Ghost possesses genuine continuity.

A Ghost may:

- recognize new people;
    
- learn;
    
- remember;
    
- regret;
    
- change its mind;
    
- form plans;
    
- and respond to circumstances that occurred after death.
    

Ghosts remain because something binds them to mortal reality.

Common bindings include:

- fear;
    
- unresolved duty;
    
- violent death;
    
- a curse;
    
- a person;
    
- an object;
    
- a place;
    
- an oath;
    
- or deliberate necromancy.
    

Some Ghosts understand that they are dead.

Others do not.

---

## **XIV. Ghosts Are Not Always Hostile**

A Ghost may be:

- frightened;
    
- confused;
    
- grieving;
    
- protective;
    
- curious;
    
- angry;
    
- or completely lucid.
    

Hostility often develops through:

- prolonged isolation;
    
- repeated reenactment of trauma;
    
- inability to communicate;
    
- spiritual hunger;
    
- or the gradual loss of identity.
    

A Ghost should not be destroyed merely because it is present.

The preferred response is to determine:

1. whether the soul remains complete;
    
2. what prevents passage;
    
3. whether the binding can be removed;
    
4. and whether the Ghost wishes to cross.
    

Some souls knowingly choose temporary delay.

This may be lawful when the choice is informed and does not endanger others.

Permanent refusal carries increasing risk of spiritual deterioration.

---

## **XV. Revenants**

A **Revenant** is a soul returned to mortal influence through one dominant unresolved purpose.

That purpose may be:

- vengeance;
    
- protection;
    
- completion of an oath;
    
- delivery of a warning;
    
- recovery of a stolen body;
    
- or defense of another person.
    

Revenants differ from ordinary Ghosts because the purpose itself sustains their return.

They may inhabit:

- their original corpse;
    
- a temporary body;
    
- a constructed form;
    
- or a visible spiritual presence.
    

A Revenant may remain highly intelligent.

Its judgment is often narrowed by the purpose sustaining it.

When the purpose is completed, the soul usually resumes passage.

If the purpose becomes impossible, the Revenant may deteriorate or seek increasingly distorted interpretations of completion.

---

## **XVI. Bound Spirits**

A **Bound Spirit** is a soul held by external force.

Bindings may attach the soul to:

- a relic;
    
- a weapon;
    
- a building;
    
- a bloodline;
    
- a living host;
    
- a magical contract;
    
- or an undead body.
    

The soul may remain conscious, dormant, divided, or unable to communicate.

Binding a soul without informed consent is a grave spiritual crime.

Even consensual binding requires review because consent given during life may not account for:

- endless duration;
    
- pain;
    
- identity loss;
    
- or changes in purpose after death.
    

A protective ancestor bound voluntarily to a family relic remains a person.

They are not merely an enchantment.

---

## **XVII. Severed Souls**

A soul may be divided into several portions.

This can occur through:

- failed resurrection;
    
- powerful curses;
    
- conflicting bindings;
    
- Divine or Archaic Magic;
    
- necromantic experimentation;
    
- or catastrophic Labyrinth interference.
    

Each portion may preserve different aspects of the original person, such as:

- memory;
    
- anger;
    
- affection;
    
- duty;
    
- fear;
    
- or will.
    

A fragment may claim to be the whole soul.

It may sincerely believe this.

Soul fragments should not be dismissed as unreal, but neither should one fragment automatically receive full legal identity without investigation.

Reunification may be possible.

It may also be resisted by fragments that have developed independently.

---

# **PART THREE: UNDEATH**

## **XVIII. What Undeath Is**

**Undeath** is the unnatural continuation or reactivation of a dead body, soul, or spiritual pattern.

It is a condition rather than one specific creature type.

Undeath may involve:

- a corpse moving without a soul;
    
- a soul trapped inside its corpse;
    
- a foreign spirit inhabiting dead flesh;
    
- a magical hunger sustaining identity;
    
- or a necromantic pattern imitating life.
    

Not all undead contain the original person.

Not all undead are mindless.

Not all undead are inherently malicious.

All undead exist outside the ordinary process of death.

---

## **XIX. Soulless Undead**

Some undead bodies contain no soul.

Examples include corpses animated through simple necromantic force.

Such beings may:

- follow commands;
    
- repeat physical habits;
    
- respond to magical direction;
    
- or display residual bodily behavior.
    

They are not automatically the deceased person.

A body remembering how to walk is not the same as a soul choosing where to go.

The lawful animation of soulless remains is permitted in some jurisdictions under strict conditions involving:

- prior consent;
    
- funerary law;
    
- public safety;
    
- and assurance that no soul remains attached.
    

The body’s former identity still grants it legal and cultural protection.

---

## **XX. Ensouled Undead**

Some undead contain the deceased’s original soul.

This may occur through:

- deliberate transformation;
    
- failed passage;
    
- curses;
    
- necromantic binding;
    
- or chosen continuation.
    

An ensouled undead being remains a person.

They retain moral responsibility and legal rights unless their condition has destroyed the capacity for independent will.

Their existence may still involve harmful needs, such as:

- life-force consumption;
    
- blood;
    
- memory;
    
- emotion;
    
- or bodily replacement.
    

Personhood does not make every behavior lawful.

Danger does not erase personhood.

The condition must be judged according to conduct, consent, and the possibility of safe continued existence.

---

## **XXI. False Personality in Undead Bodies**

A corpse may preserve habits, memories, or fragments of Meaning without containing the original soul.

Such an undead being may:

- speak familiar phrases;
    
- recognize locations;
    
- repeat routines;
    
- or react to loved ones.
    

This may result from:

- bodily memory;
    
- implanted recollection;
    
- residual magic;
    
- an Echo pattern;
    
- or a foreign intelligence using the deceased’s identity.
    

The presence of memory does not establish authentic return.

Soul verification is required before the being is legally recognized as the deceased.

---

# **PART FOUR: CLASSIFICATION AND RESPONSE**

## **XXII. Principal Distinctions**

|Phenomenon|Soul Present?|Continuing Will?|Origin|
|---|--:|--:|---|
|**Echo**|No|Ordinarily no|Labyrinth records a death|
|**Stored Memory**|No|No|Labyrinth preserves a nonfatal event|
|**Ghost**|Yes|Yes|Soul fails or refuses to complete passage|
|**Revenant**|Yes|Yes, narrowed by purpose|Soul returns through unresolved purpose|
|**Bound Spirit**|Yes|Variable|Soul held by external force|
|**Severed Soul**|Partial|Variable|Soul divided into fragments|
|**Soulless Undead**|No|Limited or artificial|Corpse animated after soul departure|
|**Ensouled Undead**|Yes|Yes|Soul remains within unnatural continuation|
|**False Return**|Not the original soul|Possibly|Copy, spirit, or imitation occupies the role|

This classification determines the appropriate response.

An exorcism suitable for a Ghost may do nothing to an Echo.

Destroying a soulless corpse may be physically necessary but does not release a trapped soul.

A spirit-binding ritual performed on an Echo may create a new hazard by forcing the memory into artificial coherence.

Correct identification must precede intervention.

---

## **XXIII. Echo or Ghost?**

The Ministry uses several tests.

### **Novel Response**

Can the phenomenon answer a question unrelated to the recorded death?

### **Learning**

Does it remember new interactions across repeated contact?

### **Boundary Resistance**

Can it move beyond the place, object, or sequence defining its manifestation?

### **Soul Resonance**

Does it produce a continuing soul-signature?

### **Psychopomp Recognition**

Does an authenticated psychopomp identify a soul requiring passage?

### **Contradictory Choice**

Can the entity choose to act against the emotional or behavioral pattern it repeatedly displays?

A Ghost may fail some tests if badly damaged.

An Echo may appear to pass one if the Labyrinth has incorporated observers into its pattern.

No single test is conclusive.

---

## **XXIV. Echo and Ghost Overlap**

A death inside a Labyrinth may produce both an Echo and a Ghost.

In such cases:

- the Echo records the death;
    
- the Ghost contains the soul that failed to pass.
    

The two may appear identical.

They may occupy the same scene.

The Ghost may also become confused by its own Echo, repeatedly watching or entering the memory of its death.

This can trap the soul more deeply.

A Ghost may mistake the Echo for:

- its body;
    
- another version of itself;
    
- proof that it is still alive;
    
- or an event it must change before it can leave.
    

Mortuary teams must separate the soul from the recording before attempting passage.

Destroying the Echo first may help.

It may also destabilize a Ghost that has used the memory as its primary anchor.

---

## **XXV. Labyrinth Copies of the Dead**

A Labyrinth may eventually construct an active imitation from an Echo or Stored Memory.

Such a copy may possess:

- the deceased’s appearance;
    
- fragments of memory;
    
- repeated speech;
    
- combat habits;
    
- emotional responses;
    
- and limited adaptability.
    

Most remain Labyrinth-generated entities rather than the original person.

Some may develop sufficient independent identity to become new persons.

The crucial distinction remains:

> **A copy may become someone. It does not thereby become the person copied.**

A newly aware Labyrinth copy bears responsibility for its own choices.

It does not inherit the guilt, debts, titles, relationships, or legal identity of the dead individual whose memory shaped it.

---

## **XXVI. Ministry Response Protocol**

When an apparent deceased person is encountered, investigators must determine:

1. Was the original death confirmed?
    
2. Did the death occur inside a Labyrinth?
    
3. Is a soul-signature present?
    
4. Does the phenomenon display genuine novel thought?
    
5. Is a corpse being animated?
    
6. Is the entity bound to a location, object, or purpose?
    
7. Does an Echo of the death exist separately?
    
8. Has the original soul undergone resurrection?
    
9. Is another spirit using the deceased’s identity?
    
10. Does intervention risk damaging a real soul?
    

Recommended responses include:

- **Echo:** observe, document, contain, or safely disperse;
    
- **Ghost:** identify the binding and restore passage;
    
- **Revenant:** determine the sustaining purpose and lawful means of completion;
    
- **Bound Spirit:** release or renegotiate the binding;
    
- **Severed Soul:** stabilize and assess possible reunification;
    
- **Soulless Undead:** contain according to physical threat and funerary law;
    
- **Ensouled Undead:** treat as a person while addressing dangerous needs or conduct;
    
- **False Return:** protect any emergent personhood while preventing fraudulent identity claims.
    

---

## **XXVII. Legal Personhood after Death**

A continuing soul remains a person.

A Ghost, Revenant, Bound Spirit, or ensouled undead being therefore retains moral and legal consideration.

An Echo does not ordinarily possess personhood because it lacks continuing will.

A Labyrinth copy, construct, or false return may acquire personhood if it develops:

- self-recognition;
    
- independent choice;
    
- continuity across time;
    
- and the capacity to form new relationships.
    

Personhood is determined by what exists now.

Identity with the dead is determined by continuity with what existed before.

These are separate questions.

---

## **XXVIII. Ministry Position**

The Ministry recognizes the following conclusions as established or strongly supported:

1. Death may leave behind souls, bodies, memories, or magical patterns that must not be confused with one another.
    
2. An Echo is a corrupted memory unintentionally preserved when a being dies inside a Labyrinth.
    
3. The original soul may complete ordinary passage while its Echo remains.
    
4. Echoes may display speech, movement, fear, and limited reaction without possessing true will.
    
5. The term Echo is reserved for death-linked Labyrinth memories.
    
6. Labyrinths may also preserve nonfatal events as Stored Memories or Residual Scenes.
    
7. Echoes may become corrupted, layered, recursive, contradictory, or capable of enveloping living observers.
    
8. Echoes are useful but imperfect historical evidence.
    
9. Destroying an Echo does not ordinarily harm the departed soul.
    
10. A Ghost is a continuing soul prevented from completing passage.
    
11. Ghosts may learn, change, and respond to new circumstances.
    
12. Revenants are souls sustained by a dominant unresolved purpose.
    
13. Bound Spirits and Severed Souls remain persons or portions of persons and require lawful treatment.
    
14. Undeath is a condition that may or may not involve the original soul.
    
15. Memory and familiar behavior do not prove that an undead body contains the deceased.
    
16. Echoes and Ghosts may coexist following the same death.
    
17. A Labyrinth copy may develop new personhood without becoming the original person.
    
18. Intervention must distinguish memory, soul, body, and imitation before destruction, exorcism, resurrection, or release is attempted.
    

Death can leave many things behind.

A body may remain.

A soul may remain.

A memory may remain.

A Labyrinth may hold the shape of a final moment so tightly that the moment refuses to finish.

Only one of these is necessarily the person who died.

> _“When the dead appear before you, do not begin by asking what they want. Begin by determining whether anyone is truly there to answer.”_
