# **Theories of Labyrinth Origin**

### _Competing Explanations for the First Labyrinths, the Formation of New Entrances, and the Growth of Recorded Reality_

**Filed Division:** Ministry of Delving Operations — Annals & Antiquities  
**Responsible Office:** Labyrinth Origins and Theoretical Inquiry Bureau  
**Primary Compiler:** Magister Selka Vorr  
**Contributing Scholars:** Archivist Yelna Stonevoice, Priest-Analyst Thera Daan, Inspector Jao “Greyglass” Vell, Lt. Salvia Korrin  
**Current Revision:** 3388 A.D., Age of Spectacle  
**Document Class:** _Comparative Origin Dossier / Grade-I Phenomenological Evidence with Grade-III Causal Certainty_  
**Primary Evidence:** architectonic surveys, Archaic resonance studies, divine-war ruins, Hidden Plane crossings, Echo formations, entrance-birth incidents, relic provenance, long-term Labyrinth monitoring

---

## **I. Purpose of This Record**

The origin of the Labyrinths remains unknown.

This uncertainty does not arise from a lack of theories.

It arises because several theories explain part of the evidence, while none explains all of it.

Some Labyrinths contain:

- divine-war scars;
    
- ancient prisons;
    
- Hidden Plane influence;
    
- mortal memories;
    
- celestial-road fragments;
    
- or structures that appear deliberately designed.
    

Others do not.

The Ministry therefore rejects any universal origin claim that depends upon every Labyrinth having formed in the same manner.

This record compares the principal theories and distinguishes three questions that early scholars often treated as one:

1. **What created the first Labyrinths?**
    
2. **What causes a new Labyrinth entrance or region to form?**
    
3. **What allows an existing Labyrinth to grow and change?**
    

The same answer may not govern all three.

---

## **II. Origin, Formation, and Growth**

A Labyrinth’s history may contain several separate causes.

### **Origin**

The event or condition that first made Labyrinth existence possible.

This may belong to the earliest history of Aerathon and need not be repeated whenever a new entrance appears.

### **Formation**

The local event that causes a specific Labyrinth to emerge, awaken, or become accessible.

Possible triggers include:

- magical catastrophe;
    
- divine conflict;
    
- accumulated memory;
    
- planar overlap;
    
- mass death;
    
- relic activation;
    
- or structural contact with an existing Labyrinth.
    

### **Growth**

The process through which a Labyrinth adds, alters, or reorganizes rooms, creatures, memories, and laws after formation.

Growth may be influenced by:

- delvers;
    
- nearby communities;
    
- repeated magic;
    
- deaths;
    
- relics;
    
- and contact with other realms.
    

A theory may explain one stage without explaining the others.

For example, the War of the Two Heavens might have created the first conditions for Labyrinths while mortal experience later determines how individual Labyrinths expand.

---

# **PART ONE: DIVINE AND COSMOLOGICAL THEORIES**

## **III. The Divine Construction Theory**

The oldest formal theory claims that the gods created the Labyrinths intentionally.

Suggested purposes include:

- testing mortals;
    
- storing relics;
    
- training divine servants;
    
- concealing forbidden knowledge;
    
- or preserving places from destruction.
    

### **Supporting Evidence**

Labyrinths frequently contain:

- divine symbols;
    
- sacred trials;
    
- Archaic artifacts;
    
- structures resembling Divine Realms;
    
- and laws associated with known gods.
    

Some chambers appear too precise or functional to be accidental.

### **Primary Objections**

No god has established uncontested authorship.

The Labyrinths also:

- combine rival divine systems;
    
- misuse sacred symbols;
    
- contradict known doctrine;
    
- trap divine servants;
    
- and resist commands issued through genuine divine authority.
    

The gods appear capable of influencing Labyrinths.

This does not prove they created them.

### **Present Assessment**

Possible for individual structures.

Unsupported as a universal explanation.

---

## **IV. The Divine Battlefield Theory**

This theory proposes that Labyrinths grew from wounds left by the War of the Two Heavens.

Divine conflict did not merely damage Matter.

It brought competing forms of:

- authority;
    
- identity;
    
- natural law;
    
- death;
    
- and sacred obligation
    

into direct collision.

Where those laws could not reconcile, reality folded around the contradiction.

The resulting wounds became the earliest Labyrinths.

### **Supporting Evidence**

Some deep regions contain:

- mixed pantheonic architecture;
    
- contradictory divine laws;
    
- buried celestial weapons;
    
- broken roads to both Heavens;
    
- and environments resembling historical battle accounts.
    

Certain Labyrinths are located near known Primordial Scars or ancient conflict zones.

### **Primary Objections**

Many Labyrinths contain no identifiable war material.

Some reproduce places and events far younger than the divine conflict.

Others appear in regions with no known connection to the war.

### **Present Assessment**

One of the strongest explanations for the earliest Labyrinth conditions.

Insufficient as a complete explanation for later formation and growth.

---

## **V. The Divine Prison Theory**

The Divine Prison Theory holds that Labyrinths were created to contain forces that could not safely be destroyed.

Possible prisoners include:

- gods;
    
- Divine Fragments;
    
- Archaic commands;
    
- celestial armies;
    
- dangerous concepts;
    
- relic complexes;
    
- and creatures born from divine war.
    

The shifting corridors, guardian populations, and escalating depths are interpreted as layers of containment.

### **Supporting Evidence**

Several Labyrinths contain sealed entities surrounded by:

- specialized guardians;
    
- repeated prohibitions;
    
- sacrificial locks;
    
- and architecture designed to redirect or weaken escape attempts.
    

Some floors clearly function as prisons.

### **Primary Objections**

A prison inside a Labyrinth does not prove the entire Labyrinth was created as a prison.

Labyrinths can also build new containment around:

- invading creatures;
    
- recovered relics;
    
- delvers;
    
- or hazards the structure generated itself.
    

The prisoner may be older or younger than the surrounding rooms.

### **Present Assessment**

Verified in specific cases.

Unverified as the origin of Labyrinths generally.

---

## **VI. The Quiet Edict Theory**

This theory links Labyrinth formation to the Quiet Edict.

The Edict restricted:

- divine descent;
    
- direct divine war;
    
- unrestricted possession;
    
- seizure of mortal souls;
    
- and violent reclamation of embedded divine power.
    

Some scholars argue that these restrictions created metaphysical pressure.

Divine authority that could no longer enter Aerathon directly accumulated along:

- damaged celestial roads;
    
- relic networks;
    
- abandoned temples;
    
- war scars;
    
- and mortal covenants.
    

Labyrinths formed where that pressure folded inward.

### **Supporting Evidence**

Labyrinths often contain stronger and more literal divine manifestations than ordinary surface regions.

They also frequently form near:

- broken temples;
    
- relic concentrations;
    
- sacred battlefields;
    
- and sites of failed manifestation.
    

### **Primary Objections**

Evidence suggests some Labyrinth-like phenomena may predate the Quiet Edict.

The Edict may also have altered existing structures rather than created them.

### **Present Assessment**

A strong explanation for why Labyrinths became more active, accessible, or numerous after divine withdrawal.

Uncertain as an explanation for the first Labyrinths.

---

## **VII. The Edict-Vent Theory**

A narrower form of the Quiet Edict Theory proposes that Labyrinths act as pressure vents.

Rather than being accidental wounds, they are stabilizing structures that absorb dangerous overflow from restricted divine systems.

Under this model, Labyrinths prevent:

- full divine manifestation;
    
- celestial-road rupture;
    
- uncontrolled Archaic release;
    
- and the collapse of boundaries between the Two Heavens and Aerathon.
    

### **Supporting Evidence**

Some Labyrinths become unstable when divine pressure nearby increases.

Others appear to:

- capture manifestation attempts;
    
- redirect relic resonance;
    
- or contain Edict Recoil within their interiors.
    

Certain breaches have worsened after aggressive sealing efforts, suggesting the structure was relieving pressure.

### **Primary Objections**

Many Labyrinths create hazards rather than prevent them.

No evidence proves the system was intentionally designed.

A wound can drain pressure without having been made as a drain.

### **Present Assessment**

Operationally important.

Whether Labyrinths stabilize the world intentionally or incidentally remains unknown.

---

# **PART TWO: PLANAR THEORIES**

## **VIII. The Hidden Plane Overlap Theory**

This theory proposes that Labyrinths form where Aerathon intersects imperfectly with one or more Hidden Planes.

Each plane could contribute different Labyrinth behaviors.

### **The Shattersea**

May provide:

- stored memory;
    
- reflected histories;
    
- alternate versions;
    
- and preserved environments.
    

### **The Dreaming**

May provide:

- symbolic architecture;
    
- emotionally responsive spaces;
    
- imagined creatures;
    
- and unrealized possibilities given form.
    

### **The Feywilds**

May provide:

- uncontrolled growth;
    
- transformation;
    
- emotional ecology;
    
- and places where death becomes new living form.
    

### **The Netherworld**

May contribute:

- funerary roads;
    
- soul-processing structures;
    
- Ghost entrapment;
    
- and post-mortal imagery.
    

### **The Wanehollow**

May produce:

- missing rooms;
    
- erased names;
    
- incomplete creatures;
    
- and architecture defined by absence.
    

### **Supporting Evidence**

Verified planar breaches exist within some Labyrinths.

Individual floors occasionally display laws strongly aligned with one Hidden Plane.

### **Primary Objections**

No single Hidden Plane explains every Labyrinth.

Many Labyrinth regions show mixed planar characteristics that do not naturally coexist.

It is also possible that Labyrinths imitate the planes rather than originate from them.

### **Present Assessment**

Strong for individual floors and formation incidents.

Weak as a single universal origin theory.

---

## **IX. The Interstitial Structure Theory**

Some scholars argue that Labyrinths do not originate in any known plane.

Instead, they occupy spaces between established realms.

These interstitial regions may have formed where:

- celestial roads broke;
    
- divine laws collided;
    
- world boundaries weakened;
    
- or reality attempted to reconcile incompatible states.
    

Labyrinth entrances would therefore be local openings into structures that are not fully within Aerathon.

### **Supporting Evidence**

This theory explains:

- impossible interior scale;
    
- altered time;
    
- unstable geography;
    
- and the presence of several planar influences within one structure.
    

It also explains why destroying a surface entrance rarely destroys the Labyrinth itself.

### **Primary Objections**

“Between the planes” may merely rename the mystery rather than solve it.

No stable interstitial realm has been mapped independently of a Labyrinth.

### **Present Assessment**

A useful spatial model.

Not yet a proven causal model.

---

## **X. The Single Labyrinth Theory**

The most extreme planar theory proposes that every Labyrinth entrance connects to one vast underlying structure.

Under this model, apparently separate Labyrinths are:

- distant regions;
    
- temporary branches;
    
- or different expressions
    

of a single impossible system.

### **Supporting Evidence**

Researchers have identified:

- repeated rooms across distant entrances;
    
- relics moving between Labyrinths;
    
- creatures bearing injuries inflicted in another region;
    
- identical inscriptions appearing simultaneously;
    
- and rare corridors believed to connect geographically separate sites.
    

### **Primary Objections**

Labyrinths often possess incompatible laws and histories.

No expedition has crossed reliably from one known entrance system to another.

Repeated features may result from shared formation principles rather than physical unity.

### **Present Assessment**

Unproven.

Too significant to dismiss.

Confirmation would transform all existing containment policy.

---

# **PART THREE: MEMORY AND REALITY THEORIES**

## **XI. The World-Memory Theory**

The World-Memory Theory proposes that Aerathon itself records significant events.

Most memories fade into ordinary history.

Some become embedded so deeply into Matter, Meaning, and Motion that they remain available for later reconstruction.

Labyrinths form where this recorded reality becomes capable of expressing itself physically.

### **Supporting Evidence**

Labyrinths reproduce:

- lost cities;
    
- extinct creatures;
    
- forgotten rituals;
    
- ancient disasters;
    
- and people absent from all surviving surface history.
    

Some records appear too old or obscure to have come from known delvers.

### **Primary Objections**

The theory does not explain why records become dangerous, interactive structures rather than passive impressions.

It also does not identify what gives the memory the power to create physical reality.

### **Present Assessment**

One of the strongest explanations for Labyrinth content.

Incomplete as an explanation for structure and agency.

---

## **XII. The Archive Theory**

The Archive Theory describes Labyrinths as systems that record relationships among the Three Weaves.

They do not merely remember appearance.

They may record:

- what an object was;
    
- what it meant;
    
- how it moved;
    
- who recognized it;
    
- and what changed because of it.
    

A sufficiently complete record can be re-expressed as physical reality.

### **Supporting Evidence**

This model explains:

- recreated environments;
    
- Stored Memories;
    
- Echoes;
    
- copied creatures;
    
- symbolic architecture;
    
- and rooms formed around social roles or emotional events.
    

It also explains why reconstruction is often flawed.

The Labyrinth may preserve some relationships while losing others.

### **Primary Objections**

Archive Theory explains how Labyrinths reproduce reality.

It does not fully explain why the Labyrinth exists, what first enabled recording, or whether the process is intentional.

### **Present Assessment**

The strongest current model for Labyrinth behavior.

Not a complete origin theory by itself.

---

## **XIII. The Catastrophic Imprint Theory**

This theory proposes that Labyrinths form when an event places too much Meaning into one location for ordinary reality to absorb.

Possible triggering events include:

- mass death;
    
- divine manifestation;
    
- city-wide ritual;
    
- catastrophic betrayal;
    
- cultural destruction;
    
- failed resurrection;
    
- or the collapse of a celestial road.
    

Reality responds by folding the event inward and building a structure around it.

### **Supporting Evidence**

Several Labyrinth entrances have appeared after major disasters.

The resulting interiors often reflect:

- the people involved;
    
- the emotional conditions;
    
- the surrounding architecture;
    
- and the unresolved conflict.
    

### **Primary Objections**

Not every catastrophe creates a Labyrinth.

Some Labyrinths emerge gradually without one identifiable triggering event.

### **Present Assessment**

Strong explanation for particular formation events.

The unknown factor is what makes one event imprint deeply enough while another fades.

---

## **XIV. The Wounded-Reality Theory**

The Wounded-Reality Theory treats Labyrinths as a form of scar tissue.

When Aerathon suffers damage across several Weaves at once, ordinary restoration becomes impossible.

Reality isolates the contradiction.

It creates an enclosed structure in which incompatible truths can continue existing without immediately spreading into the surrounding world.

Under this model, the Labyrinth is neither the original wound nor its cure.

It is the scar formed around it.

### **Supporting Evidence**

Labyrinths frequently:

- enclose magical contradictions;
    
- prevent unstable laws from spreading;
    
- build containment around hazards;
    
- and continue reorganizing after the initiating event has ended.
    

### **Primary Objections**

Some Labyrinths actively expand into stable regions.

Others create new contradictions rather than contain old ones.

### **Present Assessment**

A leading explanation for why Labyrinth boundaries exist.

May describe the world’s response to Labyrinth formation rather than the Labyrinth’s origin.

---

# **PART FOUR: MORTAL AND INTENTIONAL THEORIES**

## **XV. The Mortal-Creation Theory**

This theory argues that mortal civilizations created the first Labyrinths.

Possible creators include:

- ancient architects;
    
- Archaic spellwrights;
    
- lost empires;
    
- Orichalcum cultures;
    
- or cooperative Brethren and Beastfolk societies now erased from history.
    

Labyrinths may have begun as:

- memory archives;
    
- shelters;
    
- factories;
    
- ritual structures;
    
- planar vessels;
    
- or engines intended to rewrite reality.
    

### **Supporting Evidence**

Some floors contain:

- mortal-scale controls;
    
- maintenance passages;
    
- workshop systems;
    
- written instructions;
    
- and architecture designed around mortal bodies.
    

Certain relics resemble components rather than treasures.

### **Primary Objections**

Many Labyrinth regions exceed any known mortal capability.

Controls may have been added later.

A chamber shaped for mortals may have copied mortal architecture rather than been built by mortals.

### **Present Assessment**

Plausible for specific Labyrinth complexes.

No known mortal civilization is confirmed as the universal creator.

---

## **XVI. The Orichalcum Engine Theory**

A specialized Mortal-Creation Theory links Labyrinths to ancient Orichalcum systems.

Orichalcum responds to:

- resonance;
    
- intention;
    
- identity;
    
- and the alignment of the Three Weaves.
    

Some scholars propose that the first Labyrinths were enormous Orichalcum engines designed to preserve or modify reality.

Their visible metal may have since:

- decayed;
    
- transformed;
    
- been absorbed into the surrounding structure;
    
- or become the source of relic-grade materials.
    

### **Supporting Evidence**

Some deep Labyrinth regions contain alloys with Orichalcum-like resonance.

The structures also display behaviors consistent with self-modifying magical machinery.

### **Primary Objections**

Confirmed Orichalcum deposits are rare.

Many Labyrinths contain no trace of the material.

The theory also depends heavily upon reconstructions of a poorly understood ancient age.

### **Present Assessment**

A serious but highly speculative model.

---

## **XVII. The Designed-System Theory**

This theory proposes that Labyrinths were intentionally created by an unknown intelligence.

That intelligence may be:

- extinct;
    
- hidden;
    
- distributed across the Labyrinths;
    
- or no longer recognizable as a person.
    

Apparent evidence of design includes:

- recurring floor progression;
    
- selective rewards;
    
- responsive trials;
    
- containment systems;
    
- and the production of objects useful to surface civilization.
    

### **Supporting Evidence**

Many Labyrinths behave too systematically to appear wholly accidental.

They often create:

- escalation;
    
- thresholds;
    
- guardians;
    
- keys;
    
- and rewards
    

in recognizable relationships.

### **Primary Objections**

Pattern does not prove a planner.

Natural systems also produce order.

Labyrinths frequently act inconsistently, wastefully, or self-destructively.

The supposed design may emerge from local rules rather than conscious intention.

### **Present Assessment**

Unresolved.

Cannot be accepted or dismissed solely from apparent complexity.

---

## **XVIII. The Mortal-Feeding Theory**

The Mortal-Feeding Theory proposes that Labyrinths grow by recording those who enter them.

Delvers provide:

- memories;
    
- tactics;
    
- fears;
    
- identities;
    
- magic;
    
- relationships;
    
- and deaths.
    

The Labyrinth later incorporates this material into new floors, creatures, and trials.

### **Supporting Evidence**

Long-monitored Labyrinths adapt to repeated delver behavior.

They have reproduced:

- expedition equipment;
    
- guild insignia;
    
- personal conversations;
    
- combat techniques;
    
- and the Echoes of those who died within.
    

### **Primary Objections**

The term “feeding” implies need or hunger.

The Labyrinth may record delvers automatically without depending upon them.

Some sealed Labyrinths continue growing despite decades without confirmed entry.

### **Present Assessment**

Strong evidence that mortal experience influences growth.

No proof that mortal experience is required for survival.

---

## **XIX. The Attention Theory**

A modern extension of the Mortal-Feeding Theory argues that Labyrinths respond not only to physical entry but to observation.

According to this model, attention gives an event greater Meaning.

Publicly discussed Labyrinths may therefore become more likely to reproduce:

- famous delvers;
    
- expected monsters;
    
- popular theories;
    
- and widely repeated stories.
    

### **Supporting Evidence**

Highly publicized Labyrinths sometimes begin displaying imagery associated with their public reputation.

Broadcast expeditions have recorded changes occurring soon after audience reactions.

### **Primary Objections**

Public attention often coincides with increased exploration, magical surveillance, and relic disturbance.

The apparent effect may be caused by intrusion rather than awareness.

### **Present Assessment**

Actively investigated.

Potentially significant in the Age of Spectacle.

---

# **PART FIVE: COMPOSITE MODELS**

## **XX. The Multiple-Origin Model**

The Multiple-Origin Model proposes that the word **Labyrinth** describes a family of related phenomena rather than one species of structure.

Under this theory:

- some Labyrinths began as divine-war scars;
    
- some as mortal engines;
    
- some as planar overlaps;
    
- some as catastrophic imprints;
    
- and some as branches of older Labyrinths.
    

They later developed similar behavior because all interact with the same Three Weaves and the same damaged laws of Aerathon.

### **Strengths**

This model explains the extreme diversity among known Labyrinths.

It allows apparently contradictory evidence to remain valid.

### **Weaknesses**

It risks defining Labyrinths so broadly that the category loses explanatory value.

It also does not explain why different origins produce such similar features.

### **Present Assessment**

The current Ministry preference.

Used provisionally rather than declared proven.

---

## **XXI. The First-Wound and Later-Growth Model**

This composite theory separates primordial origin from modern behavior.

It proposes that:

1. The first Labyrinths formed from wounds in reality, likely associated with divine conflict.
    
2. Those wounds created a repeatable structure capable of recording the Three Weaves.
    
3. Later catastrophes, planar breaches, and mortal inventions opened new Labyrinth regions.
    
4. Delver activity and local memory shaped subsequent growth.
    
5. All modern Labyrinths now participate in a partially shared system of recorded reality.
    

This model combines:

- Divine Battlefield Theory;
    
- Wounded-Reality Theory;
    
- Archive Theory;
    
- and Mortal-Feeding Theory.
    

### **Present Assessment**

The most widely used working model among current Ministry theorists.

Still unable to explain whether the first wound was accidental, deliberate, or inevitable.

---

## **XXII. The Self-Propagation Theory**

This model proposes that new Labyrinths are created by existing Labyrinths.

A mature Labyrinth may extend through:

- relics;
    
- dream contact;
    
- buried passages;
    
- Echo transfer;
    
- celestial roads;
    
- or repeated symbolic patterns.
    

A distant site may appear independent while actually being a new branch.

### **Supporting Evidence**

Some entrances have emerged after Labyrinth relics were transported into previously stable regions.

Several new sites reproduce the laws of older known Labyrinths.

### **Primary Objections**

Correlation does not prove propagation.

A relic may awaken an existing weakness rather than plant a new structure.

### **Present Assessment**

High containment relevance.

Unconfirmed.

---

## **XXIII. What Would Count as Proof?**

No theory should be accepted merely because it explains familiar evidence.

The Ministry considers the following discoveries potentially decisive:

- a verified pre-Labyrinth record describing intentional construction;
    
- a surviving creator or architect;
    
- an independently mapped structure connecting multiple Labyrinths;
    
- observation of a new Labyrinth forming from beginning to completion;
    
- a reliable method for determining the age of the underlying structure rather than its contents;
    
- or evidence that all Labyrinths share one unique foundational law absent from other phenomena.
    

No such proof currently exists.

The origin question remains open because the evidence is produced by structures capable of copying, altering, and fabricating evidence.

---

## **XXIV. Dangers of Origin Certainty**

Claims of Labyrinth origin carry political and religious consequences.

If Labyrinths are declared divine property:

- churches may claim ownership.
    

If they are declared mortal creations:

- nations may claim inheritance.
    

If they are declared living beings:

- delving may become exploitation.
    

If they are declared wounds stabilizing the world:

- sealing or destroying them may become unthinkable.
    

If they are declared invasive growths:

- extermination may become public policy.
    

For this reason, the Ministry distinguishes carefully between:

- evidence;
    
- working models;
    
- and ideological claims disguised as scholarship.
    

Origin theory is not harmless.

The answer determines who believes they have the right to control the depths.

---

## **XXV. Ministry Position**

The Ministry recognizes the following conclusions as established or strongly supported:

1. No universal Labyrinth origin has been proven.
    
2. Origin, local formation, and later growth are separate questions.
    
3. Divine conflict likely contributed to the earliest Labyrinth conditions.
    
4. The Quiet Edict may have increased Labyrinth activity or accessibility.
    
5. Some Labyrinths function as prisons, pressure vents, or containment structures.
    
6. Some Labyrinth regions result from Hidden Plane overlap.
    
7. No single Hidden Plane explains all Labyrinths.
    
8. Labyrinths record and reconstruct relationships among Matter, Meaning, and Motion.
    
9. Catastrophic events can trigger local Labyrinth formation.
    
10. Some Labyrinths behave like scar tissue surrounding damaged reality.
    
11. Mortal civilizations may have created or altered specific Labyrinth complexes.
    
12. Orichalcum remains a possible but unconfirmed component of certain early structures.
    
13. Apparent design does not prove a conscious designer.
    
14. Mortal experience measurably influences Labyrinth growth.
    
15. Public attention may influence Labyrinth Meaning, though evidence remains incomplete.
    
16. New Labyrinths may form as branches or propagations of existing structures.
    
17. Different Labyrinths may possess different immediate origins.
    
18. The First-Wound and Later-Growth Model is the Ministry’s strongest present working theory.
    
19. A working theory must not be treated as ownership, doctrine, or proof.
    
20. Any final explanation must account for both ancient cosmological evidence and ongoing mortal influence.
    

The Labyrinths may have begun as wounds.

They may have been built as engines.

They may be prisons, records, dreams, or roots extending from one impossible structure beneath the world.

The evidence permits each answer somewhere.

It permits none everywhere.

> _“Perhaps the first Labyrinth was made by a god. Perhaps the second was made by the wound the first one left behind.”_
