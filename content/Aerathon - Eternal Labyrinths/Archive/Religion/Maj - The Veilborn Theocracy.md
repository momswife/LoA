## **"All things fade. All things pass. Only the Veil remains."**

—Inscribed over the gates of **Nocthollow, capital of the Veilborn Theocracy**

The Veilborn Theocracy is a nation unlike any other in Aerathon—an empire of shadows, quiet faith, and inevitable endings. Where other civilizations worship gods of light, war, or conquest, the Veilborn place their faith in what comes after—the passage beyond life, the cycle of fate, and the mysteries of the Veil itself.

Their rule is not one of tyranny, conquest, or ambition, but one of necessity. They do not expand their borders by force, nor do they seek to convert the unwilling. The Veil will take all things in its own time, and to rush fate is to deny the very nature of existence itself.

Though their lands are cold, quiet, and shrouded in eternal mist, the Theocracy is not cruel—but it is unyielding, emotionless, and absolute. They do not fear death, nor do they fight to prevent it—they merely ensure that it happens as it must.

To the rest of the world, they are a mystery. To some, they are guardians of the natural order. To others, they are a cold, soulless empire, untouched by grief, joy, or ambition.

But one thing is certain: when the end comes, the Veilborn will be waiting.

---

## **The Faith of the Veilborn Theocracy**

The Veilborn do not believe in salvation or damnation—only passage. To them, the afterlife is not a reward for the righteous or a punishment for the wicked, but simply the next step.

They follow the will of three deities, each tied to the mysteries of death, memory, and fate.

### **The Raven Queen – The Keeper of the Veil**

**Domains:** Death, fate, winter, memory  
**Symbol:** A black raven in flight  
**Holy Orders:** The Fadewalkers, The Silent Choir

The Raven Queen is the ultimate guardian of souls and destiny, ensuring that no spirit lingers beyond its time. She is neither merciful nor cruel, acting only as the hand that guides all things to their rightful end.

Her followers act as:

- **Psychopomps**, ensuring the dead are **properly sent beyond the Veil**.
- **Keepers of memory**, preserving the **names of those who pass**, for once forgotten, a soul is truly lost.
- **Destroyers of the undead**, viewing necromancy as **an abomination that defies the cycle.**

### **Wee Jas – The Mistress of Death and Magic**

**Domains:** Death, arcana, judgment  
**Symbol:** A red skull with glowing eyes  
**Holy Orders:** The Crimson Arbiters, The Arcane Mortuary

Wee Jas is the goddess of forbidden magic, divine law, and the secrets of mortality. Where the Raven Queen guides souls, Wee Jas judges them, determining where they will dwell in eternity. She is also the patron of those who study death itself, from scholars of necromancy to morticians who prepare bodies for their final rest.

Her followers act as:

- **Mages and judges**, ensuring that **death magic is wielded with purpose, not recklessness**.
- **Executioners and inquisitors**, tasked with ensuring that **souls do not escape their fate**.
- **Guardians of sacred burial sites**, ensuring that the dead **remain undisturbed**.

### **Kelemvor – The Judge of the Dead**

**Domains:** Law, judgment, the afterlife  
**Symbol:** A skeletal hand holding scales  
**Holy Orders:** The Gray Sentinels, The Keepers of the Silent Gate

Kelemvor is the final arbiter of all souls, ensuring that none escape their rightful place. He is a god of impartiality, order, and balance, neither cruel nor merciful—only just. Unlike the Raven Queen, who merely guides souls to their end, Kelemvor decides their fate within the afterlife.

His followers act as:

- **Judges and lawmakers**, ensuring that mortal justice **mirrors divine law**.
- **Wardens of lost souls**, hunting down **ghosts, revenants, and other beings who refuse to pass on**.
- **Advisors to rulers**, offering counsel on matters of **law, legacy, and the weight of power.**

## **The Structure of the Veilborn Theocracy**

Unlike most nations, the Veilborn do not have a single ruler—their government is a council of death-priests, prophets, and the highest judges of the faith.

### **The Pale Council**

The highest governing body of the Theocracy, composed of:

- **The Fadelord** – The highest priest of the Raven Queen, responsible for guiding the faithful.
- **The Crimson Arbiter** – The supreme judge of Wee Jas, overseeing **law, magic, and trials of heresy**.
- **The Veilkeeper** – Kelemvor’s chosen, who acts as the final **interpreter of divine justice**.

### **The Fadewalkers**

A secretive order of agents, assassins, and soul shepherds who ensure that death happens as it must. If a soul refuses its fate, the Fadewalkers ensure that it is corrected.

### **The Keepers of the Silent Gate**

A militant order dedicated to hunting down necromancers, undead, and heretics who defy the natural cycle of death.

## **The Influence of the Theocracy**

While not expansionist, the Veilborn hold vast influence, particularly in matters of law, mortality, and judgment.

### **In the Umbral Dominion**

- Their capital, Nocthollow, is a city of fog, silence, and sacred burial sites, where the dead rest undisturbed in vast catacombs.
- Their people dress in muted colors, believing that to live in excess is to deny the inevitable end.

### **In Halvask**

- The Veilborn serve as **advisors and judges**, ensuring that **executions and legal trials follow proper divine law**.
- They are **respected but feared**, for their presence often means **that something is about to end.**

---

## **Controversies and Conflicts**

Despite their wisdom, the Veilborn Theocracy is **often seen as cold, unfeeling, and absolute**.

- **The Ban on Resurrection** – They believe that to **bring someone back from death is a crime against fate**, and they actively **hunt down rogue resurrectionists**.
- **Their View on Undead** – Unlike other faiths that see undeath as purely evil, the Veilborn **acknowledge exceptions**—if undeath serves a purpose (such as guarding a tomb), it **may be tolerated**.
- **Their Isolationism** – They do not interfere in **worldly conflicts**, refusing to **take sides in war**, believing that **all nations will fall in time.**

---

## **Final Observations: The Veil and the Labyrinths**

The Veilborn do not seek to **conquer the Labyrinths**, nor do they believe they should be **explored freely**. Instead, they see them as **places where something was meant to be forgotten**.

Some believe that the **Nameless Labyrinth in Halvask is an aberration**, something **that should never have been revealed**.

And if that is true…

**Then perhaps the Veil is beginning to tear.**
