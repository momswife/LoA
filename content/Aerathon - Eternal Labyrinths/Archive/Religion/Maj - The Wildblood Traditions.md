## **"The land does not ask for your loyalty, nor does it care for your suffering. But those who listen, those who learn—those who truly understand it—will never be alone."**

— **High Shaman Arka Two-Trees, Keeper of the Wandering Song, 5E 3029**

The Wildblood Traditions are not a singular faith, nor are they dictated by holy books, grand temples, or divine law. They are a way of life, a collection of oral histories, ancient rites, and sacred pacts passed down through generations of those who live in harmony with the untamed world.

Unlike the structured kingdoms of Halvask, the ruthless hierarchies of the Sunfire Expanse, or the cold, inevitable order of the Veilborn Theocracy, the Wildbloods see themselves as part of something greater, something that cannot be conquered, chained, or controlled—the land itself.

Some call them savages, nomads, or relics of a forgotten time, but the Wildbloods do not seek to change the world, nor do they desire conquest or riches. They listen to the wind, speak with the rivers, and let the mountains whisper their wisdom. They believe that life is not about bending the world to one's will—but about finding one's place within it.

To those who live under steel and stone, they are mystics, druids, and warriors of the old ways. To the Wildbloods themselves, they are simply those who remember.

---

## **The Gods of the Wildblood Traditions**

The Wildbloods do not see their gods as distant figures seated upon golden thrones, nor do they believe in holy wars or grand cosmic battles. To them, the gods are woven into the land itself, their presence felt in every tree, every storm, every shifting tide.

Unlike other religions, the Wildblood faiths have no singular pantheon—different tribes, clans, and circles honor different deities, often blending myths together or interpreting the gods in ways unique to their lands.

### **Obad-Hai, the Shaper of the Wilds – The Ever-Growing One**

**Domains:** Nature, balance, renewal  
**Symbol:** An oak tree with shifting seasons  
**Sacred Sites:** The Whispering Wilds, the Titan Highlands

Obad-Hai is the heart of the untamed, the force that ensures the cycle of life and death continues unbroken. He is neither gentle nor cruel, but simply inevitable—forests will grow, rivers will carve stone, and mountains will rise and fall.

His followers include:

- **Druids, shamans, and seers**, who listen to **the voice of the land itself**.
- **Wanderers and hermits**, who reject **civilized chains** in favor of freedom.
- **Protectors of the wild**, who see destruction for greed as **the greatest sin.**

---

### **Ehlonna, the Keeper of Beasts – The Mother of the Wilds**

**Domains:** Life, fertility, animal kinship  
**Symbol:** A silver stag under a crescent moon  
**Sacred Sites:** The Whispering Wilds, the Frostlands

Ehlonna is the guardian of all things untamed and sacred, the goddess of hunters, beasts, and those who walk alongside nature rather than against it. Unlike Obad-Hai, who represents nature’s uncaring balance, Ehlonna is a protector, a nurturer, a guide for those who live by the hunt.

Her followers include:

- **Beast-tamers and rangers**, who **fight not alongside armies, but with the spirits of the wild.**
- **Midwives and healers**, who know that **nature can take, but it can also give.**
- **Those who reject cruelty**, believing that **a true hunter kills only what they need.**

---

### **Melora, the Lady of the Tides – The Wandering Wave**

**Domains:** The sea, storms, change  
**Symbol:** A spiraling wave breaking against stone  
**Sacred Sites:** The Tempest Sea, Stormcall Isles

Melora is the ever-moving, the unchained, the goddess of freedom, travel, and the eternal tides. She is worshipped by coastal Wildblood clans and wandering tribes, those who see the ocean not as a barrier, but as a path leading to new stories, new lands, and new destinies.

Her followers include:

- **Sailors and nomads**, who **never stay in one place for long.**
- **Free spirits and outcasts**, who refuse to be **tied to one destiny.**
- **Stormwalkers**, who hear **her voice in thunder and rain.**

## **The Rites, Rituals, and Traditions of the Wildbloods**

Unlike the structured ceremonies of Halvask or the rigid laws of the Veilborn, the Wildbloods do not have temples or holy texts. Their faith lives in their stories, their rituals, and their way of life.

### **The Rite of the First Hunt**

When a child comes of age, they must track, hunt, and kill their first beast alone. This is not a test of violence, but of understanding—they must know the land, read the wind, and respect their prey.

### **The Burning Walk**

To become a shaman, one must wander alone into the heart of the wilds, carrying only a single flame. If they return, they are forever bound to the spirits of the land.

### **The Night of Echoes**

Once a year, the Wildbloods speak with their dead, calling upon the spirits of their ancestors beneath the full moon.

---

## **The Wildblood Clans and Circles**

While all Wildbloods share a connection to the land, they are diverse in belief, culture, and way of life.

### **The Moonfang Clans** _(Wanderers, Nomads, Storytellers)_

The Moonfang never settle, believing that to remain in one place is to invite stagnation. They travel by star and song, telling stories that stretch back to the dawn of time.

### **The Skyborn Circle** _(Stormcallers of the Wilds)_

Believers in the power of wind and storm, they claim to have mastered the language of the skies, using storm magic to shape their fates.

### **The Rootbound Elders** _(Keepers of the Old Ways)_

The most ancient and reclusive of the Wildbloods, they live deep in the Whispering Wilds, ensuring that civilization does not encroach upon the sacred places of the earth.

---

## **The Wildbloods and Other Faiths**

- They despise the Gilded Pantheon, believing that greed and dominion poison the land.
- They are wary of the Radiant Covenant, seeing its laws as chains that bind the spirit.
- They respect the Stormcallers, for they both understand that nature cannot be tamed—only ridden.

---

## **Final Observations: The Wildbloods and the Labyrinths**

To the Wildbloods, the Labyrinths are not ruins to be plundered or curses to be feared—they are wounds.

They believe the Labyrinths are places where the world was broken, where something ancient and powerful was buried, and where the land itself has not yet healed.

And they whisper that if too many are opened—

The land will never heal at all.
