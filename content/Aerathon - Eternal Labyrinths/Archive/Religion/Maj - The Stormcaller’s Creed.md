## **"The storm does not ask if you are ready. The tide does not care if you beg. You stand or you fall—nothing more."**

— **Eirik Halvarsson, War-Speaker of the Stormcall Isles, 6E 772**

The Stormcaller’s Creed is not merely a religion—it is a way of life, a battle philosophy, and a pact with the forces of the sea and sky. It is a belief forged in tempest and steel, held by those who sail through chaos, battle through adversity, and emerge unbroken.

The Stormcallers do not pray for salvation. They do not kneel before their gods. They do not beg for mercy, for mercy is for the weak, and the storm has no care for weakness. Instead, they stand, they fight, they earn their place in the storm’s embrace.

To them, life is a series of trials, and only those who rise after every fall, who grip the mast even as the ship is torn asunder, who fight even when they cannot win—only these souls are truly worthy of the Storm.

And when they die, if they have lived as warriors, they will not rot in the earth like cowards. The storm will take them home.

---

## **The Gods of the Stormcaller’s Creed**

Unlike other faiths that emphasize order, civilization, or divine hierarchy, the Stormcallers see their gods not as masters, but as forces to be honored and proven worthy of.

They do not fear their gods, nor do they seek their favor in idle prayer. Instead, they challenge them, pushing themselves to greater heights, proving their strength not with words, but with action.

### **Kord, the Thunderer – Strength Without Limits**

**Domains:** Strength, battle, endurance, personal glory  
**Symbol:** A clenched fist wrapped in lightning  
**Holy Orders:** The Stormborn Champions, The Fists of Kord

Kord is the god of warriors, brawlers, and those who seek strength above all else. His creed is simple: only those who test their might, who push beyond their limits, are truly alive.

His followers include:

- **Stormborn warriors**, who **train endlessly, fight fiercely, and die well.**
- **Brawlers and pit fighters**, who believe that **every battle is a lesson, every scar a trophy.**
- **Those who stand defiant**, refusing to bow to kings, gods, or fate.

To the Stormcallers, Kord is not a god to be worshiped—he is a rival to be honored.

---

### **Procan, the Storm Lord – The Unyielding Sea**

**Domains:** The ocean, chaos, travel, the unpredictable tides  
**Symbol:** A crashing wave over a blackened sun  
**Holy Orders:** The Tidewalkers, The Sworn to the Deep

Procan is the living force of the ocean—uncaring, wild, and relentless. He does not love or hate his followers, nor does he demand tribute or worship. He simply is.

His followers include:

- **Sailors and navigators**, who respect the **will of the sea and know it cannot be tamed.**
- **Deep-divers and wreck-scavengers**, who believe that **the ocean buries truth beneath its waves.**
- **Skyship pilots**, who see the **storm and the sea as two halves of the same force.**

To the Stormcallers, Procan is not a benevolent protector, but an ever-present trial—one they must overcome every time they set sail.

---

### **Valkur, the Captain’s Hand – The Guardian of the Worthy**

**Domains:** Protection, leadership, courage, safe passage  
**Symbol:** A ship’s helm with a golden star  
**Holy Orders:** The Windborne Brotherhood, The Stormwardens

Valkur is the only god among the Stormcallers who is seen as a protector, but even he does not offer safety freely. He is the god of captains, warriors, and those who lead from the front, and he only shields those who have proven themselves worthy of survival.

His followers include:

- **Captains and war-leaders**, who inspire **loyalty through courage, not fear.**
- **Shipwrights and navigators**, who believe that **a well-made ship is as sacred as a temple.**
- **Those who defy impossible odds**, surviving battles, shipwrecks, and storms against all logic.

To the Stormcallers, Valkur is not a savior—he is the voice that tells a warrior to stand when all others have fallen.

## **The Laws of the Storm**

Though they reject rigid structure, the Stormcallers live by a code—one that is not written, but spoken, carried in the wind, taught through battle and hardship.

### **"Stand Tall, Strike Hard"**

Cowardice is unforgivable. The Storm does not respect those who kneel to tyrants, flee from battle, or beg for mercy. Stand your ground. Win or lose, face your fate with steel in your hand.

### **"Earn Your Place"**

Strength is not inherited, nor is leadership given. To claim a ship, a crew, or a warrior’s title, one must fight for it, bleed for it, prove they deserve it.

### **"The Storm Does Not Wait"**

Hesitation is as deadly as weakness. Whether in battle, at sea, or in life, one must act boldly, move forward, and seize what is theirs.

These tenets shape every aspect of Stormcaller life—from how they fight, to how they govern, to how they die.

## **The Stormcaller Tribes and Brotherhoods**

Though united in belief, the Stormcallers are not one people, but many clans, crews, and warrior bands, each with their own interpretation of the Creed.

### **The Tideborn Clans** _(Masters of the Sea)_

The oldest and most traditional of the Stormcallers, the Tideborn reject the skyships of Halvask and the Dominion, believing that a true warrior commands the sea, not the sky.

- **Famous for their longships**, they raid and explore the Tempest Sea, seeking **glory in battle and treasure in shipwrecks.**
- **They tattoo their victories upon their skin**, believing that **only the marked will be welcomed in the afterlife.**

### **The Skyborne Marauders** _(Lords of the Storm)_

A breakaway faction that turned away from the sea and took to the skies, believing that lightning and wind are just as sacred as the waves.

- **They pilot massive storm-rigged skyships**, using the wind as their **greatest weapon.**
- **They believe that Procan has abandoned the sea**, and that Kord’s true domain is **above, not below.**

### **The Red Sails** _(Blood and Coin)_

A ruthless warrior cult that believes the only true law is battle. They follow Kord above all others, believing that glory is earned through bloodshed.

- **They serve as mercenaries**, selling their blades **to those who offer the greatest challenge.**
- **They do not care for politics or honor**—only the thrill of the fight.

---

## **The Stormcaller’s Conflict with Other Faiths**

Though respected, the Stormcallers often clash with more structured faiths.

- They reject the Radiant Covenant, believing its laws are chains placed on the strong.
- They have no patience for the Veilborn, seeing them as cold cowards who accept death too easily.
- They do not trust the Gilded Pantheon, believing that wealth and bloodlines do not determine worth—only strength does.

However, they hold great respect for the Titanborn Clans, as both believe that one’s strength must be earned, not inherited.

## **Final Observations: The Storm and the Labyrinths**

To the Stormcallers, the Labyrinths are the ultimate trial. They are not sacred, nor cursed—only a test.

Those who enter prove their strength, or they fall.

And if they fall, the sea will take them.

The wind will carry their name.

And the storm will rage on.
