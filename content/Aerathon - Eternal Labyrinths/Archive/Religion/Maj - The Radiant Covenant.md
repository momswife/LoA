## **Through light, we build. Through law, we endure.**

—**Excerpt from The Book of the Covenant, High Theurgist Marcellis Vex, 6E 399**

The Radiant Covenant is one of the most powerful and structured faiths in all of Aerathon, shaping empires, laws, and the very foundation of civilization. Devoted to Pelor, Heironeous, and Moradin, the Covenant preaches that order, justice, and creation are the pillars upon which the world stands. To defy these tenets is to invite ruin, chaos, and darkness.

Though its temples and churches stretch across many lands, the Covenant is most deeply entrenched in Halvask, where it has become both a religion and a political force, guiding the city's laws and moral foundations. Some see it as a beacon of hope, ensuring prosperity and order, while others believe it to be an instrument of control, maintaining power through doctrine and divine law.

---

## **The Divine Triad of the Covenant**

The Radiant Covenant is a trifold faith, with each god representing a fundamental pillar of civilization. Though followers often worship all three, individuals may devote themselves more fully to one over the others.

### **Pelor, the Dawnfather – The Pillar of Light**

**Domains:** Sun, healing, agriculture, leadership  
**Symbol:** A golden sunburst  
**Holy Orders:** The Dawncloaks, The Luminaries of the Covenant

Pelor represents the guiding light of civilization, the warmth of the sun that nurtures life and prosperity. His priests believe that to lead is to illuminate, bringing knowledge and moral clarity to those who dwell in darkness. He is seen as a fatherly protector, but also as an unyielding judge, for no shadows can escape his gaze.

His followers include:

- **Rulers and lawmakers**, who claim divine right under his wisdom.
- **Healers and farmers**, who rely on his blessings for health and harvest.
- **Paladins and knights**, who see themselves as **his sword against the wicked.**

### **Heironeous, the Archpaladin – The Pillar of Law**

**Domains:** War, justice, honor, righteous conquest  
**Symbol:** A silver lightning bolt  
**Holy Orders:** The Adamant Shield, The Knights of the Covenant, [[Minor Religions & Cults| The Order of Iron Ascendants]]

Heironeous represents law, chivalry, and righteous warfare. He is the patron of warriors, kings, and those who fight for a just cause. While Pelor guides through wisdom and leadership, Heironeous guides through action and discipline, ensuring that civilization remains strong against all threats.

His followers include:

- **Generals and soldiers**, who fight under banners of divine justice.
- **Judges and inquisitors**, who see law as the **foundation of society**.
- **Conquerors**, who believe that **law must be imposed to bring order.**

### **Moradin, the Forgefather – The Pillar of Creation**

**Domains:** Craftsmanship, civilization, perseverance  
**Symbol:** A hammer striking an anvil  
**Holy Orders:** The Iron Hands, The Forgewrights of Moradin

Moradin represents craft, industry, and the endurance of civilization. He is worshiped not only as a god of smiths, but as a builder of empires, ensuring that what is made must last. His followers believe that creating something permanent—whether a kingdom, a temple, or a mighty blade—is the greatest calling.

His followers include:

- Artificers, masons, and blacksmiths, who see their work as sacred.
- Kings and rulers, who forge not steel, but legacies.
- Hardworking commoners, who believe that perseverance is its own reward.

## **The Structure of the Radiant Covenant**

The Radiant Covenant is not just a religion—it is a structured institution, with political, military, and scholarly branches that influence nearly every aspect of society.

### **The Holy See of the Covenant**

At the head of the faith sits the Holy See, a council of High Theurgists who interpret divine law and scripture. Their rulings shape the moral compass of entire nations, and their decrees hold weight even among kings and rulers.

### **The Inquisitorial Order**

The Inquisition serves as the Covenant’s law enforcement, hunting down heresy, necromancy, and forbidden magic. They are both feared and revered, for while they claim to protect civilization, their methods are often seen as merciless and authoritarian.

### **The Knightly Orders of Heironeous**

The Covenant maintains several knightly orders, each devoted to upholding the will of the gods. Some serve as holy warriors, while others act as enforcers of law.

Notable orders include:

- **The Adamant Shield** – Defenders of the faith, sworn to protect sacred sites.
- **The Knights of the Covenant** – An elite force that fights in the name of righteous conquest.

### **The Forgewright Guilds**

Moradin’s followers maintain vast guild networks, ensuring that artifice, industry, and progress continue under divine guidance. They are highly influential in Halvask, where they oversee architecture, city planning, and magical infrastructure.

## **The Influence of the Radiant Covenant**

The Covenant holds tremendous power, acting as both a religious and political force across multiple regions. Some see it as a benevolent force, ensuring that law and justice prevail, while others see it as a mechanism of control, using divine authority to suppress dissent.

### **In Halvask**

- The Covenant influences law and governance, shaping moral doctrine and social order.
- The Grand Cathedral of Pelor is one of the most important religious sites in the world.
- Many Delvers seek blessings from the Covenant before entering Labyrinths.

### **In the Stormcall Isles**

- The Covenant’s influence is weaker, as many Stormcallers view Heironeous as rigid and overbearing.
- Some knights of the Covenant have clashed with Stormcaller warriors, believing their ways to be too chaotic.

### **In the Sunfire Expanse**

- The Magocrats respect the power of Pelor but reject the Covenant’s rigid structure.
- Some Magocrats view Moradin’s teachings as useful, particularly in the creation of magical artifacts.

---

## **Controversies and Criticisms**

Though widely respected, the Radiant Covenant is not without its critics. Many accuse it of hypocrisy, tyranny, and an obsession with control.

- The Inquisition’s ruthlessness – Some believe the Inquisition is more about enforcing obedience than protecting faith.
- Moral rigidity – The Covenant’s strict laws clash with cultures that value personal freedom.
- The conquest of "heathens" – Certain factions within the Covenant believe that nations without divine law must be brought under its rule—by force if necessary.

Despite this, the Covenant remains unshaken, believing that order, justice, and progress must prevail over all else.

---

## **Final Observations: The Covenant and the Labyrinths**

Though the Radiant Covenant does not claim to rule the Labyrinths, its influence on Delvers is undeniable. Many Delvers pray for Pelor’s light, Heironeous’s strength, or Moradin’s craftsmanship before venturing into the unknown.

But some whisper that the Labyrinths predate the gods themselves.

And if that is true…

Then perhaps the Covenant’s greatest test has yet to come.
