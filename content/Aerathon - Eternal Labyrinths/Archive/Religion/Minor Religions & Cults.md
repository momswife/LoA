_Transcribed from the Grand Archives of Halvask, 7th Era, Year 943_

Not all faiths seek order, civilization, or salvation. Some exist in the margins of history, whispered in darkened halls, forgotten tombs, and among the desperate and the damned. Some are dangerous philosophies, others are ancient remnants of beliefs buried long ago—and a few should never have been remembered at all.

The following are seven of the most enigmatic, heretical, or feared faiths in Aerathon, each existing on the fringes of power, knowledge, and reality itself.

---

## **The Hollow Creed – Keepers of the Forbidden Word**

**Deity:** Vecna, the Whispered One  
**Belief:** Knowledge is power. Secrets belong only to those willing to take them.  
**Regions:** Hidden sects, Umbral Dominion

The Hollow Creed is not a faith in the traditional sense—it is a philosophy of control, secrecy, and ambition. Its followers believe that truth is the greatest weapon, and only those who hoard it deserve to rule.

They do not build temples, nor do they openly worship—they operate in the shadows, infiltrating courts, libraries, and arcane academies, ensuring that the most valuable knowledge is kept from the unworthy.

- They deal in forbidden texts, lost histories, and arcane secrets that have been erased from the public record.
- They believe that ignorance is a tool, and only those willing to take knowledge—by force if necessary—deserve to wield it.
- They have no loyalty to kingdoms, rulers, or laws. They serve only the accumulation of knowledge and the mastery of those who hold it.

---

## **The Crimson Garden – Devotees of the Blooming Rot**

**Deity:** Zuggtmoy, the Demon Queen of Fungi  
**Belief:** Life and death are meaningless concepts—only decay matters.  
**Regions:** Whispering Wilds, the Crimson Labyrinth

The Crimson Garden is not a cult—it is an infection. Its followers do not worship in temples, nor do they seek converts through words. Instead, they spread, letting the spores of the Garden take root where they may.

To them, life is an illusion, death is a lie, and all things must return to the Bloom.

- They do not fear the end—they embrace it, seeing decay as transformation rather than loss.
- They cultivate hidden groves of crimson fungi, where the air is thick with spores and the dead rise again—not as mindless undead, but as something… different.
- They believe that the world is already dead, and it simply does not know it yet.


---

## **The Blood-Tithe – Worshipers of Carnage**

**Deity:** Erythnul, the Many-Faced God  
**Belief:** Bloodshed is the purest truth. Only in violence is true strength revealed.  
**Regions:** The Howling Maw, warbands of the Wilds

The Blood-Tithe is not a structured religion—it is a frenzy, a philosophy of endless conflict. Its followers do not build shrines or pray—they offer blood to the battlefield, screaming their devotion with every drop spilled.

- They believe that peace is weakness, and that those who refuse to fight deserve only death.
- They see war as a sacred act, a battle where only the worthy survive.
- They do not seek to rule, only to kill, to revel in slaughter, to drown the world in carnage.


---

## **The Nameless Choir – Voices of the Forgotten Ones**

**Deity:** Unknown (suspected Tharizdun)  
**Belief:** Forgotten gods must be remembered, for they are not dead—only waiting.  
**Regions:** Umbral Dominion, unknown ruins

The Nameless Choir speaks in whispers, in dreams, in places where history has been deliberately erased. They believe that there were gods before the gods, and that their absence is not by their own choice, but by force.

- They seek lost temples, ruined altars, places where something older was buried beneath time.
- They chant in languages that have no written record, reciting names that should never be spoken.
- They believe that the gods of today fear what came before them—and that the old ones are stirring once more.


---

## **The Sunless Scribes – Preservers of Forbidden Knowledge**

**Deity:** Ioun (worshiped in secret)  
**Belief:** Knowledge must be preserved, even when forbidden.  
**Regions:** Sunfire Expanse, underground archives

The Sunless Scribes are a secret order of scholars, historians, and record-keepers who defy the will of those who erase inconvenient truths. Unlike the Hollow Creed, who hoard knowledge for power, the Scribes believe that no truth should be lost, no history forgotten.

- They operate in hidden archives, preserving texts that rulers and religious orders have tried to erase.
- They smuggle knowledge across kingdoms, ensuring that even the darkest truths survive censorship.
- They are hunted by those who believe that some knowledge—particularly about the Labyrinths—should remain lost.


---

## **The Hollowed Masks – Keepers of Stolen Identities**

**Deity:** Mask, the Lord of Shadows  
**Belief:** Identity is meaningless—wear a better one.  
**Regions:** Veilshroud Expanse, criminal networks

To the Hollowed Masks, life is a game of deception, and the self is nothing more than a role to be discarded when necessary. They do not worship Mask as a god of simple thievery, but as the patron of fluid identity, the master of becoming whomever the moment demands.

- They possess enchanted masks that allow them to steal voices, faces, and memories.
- They believe that a true master of identity should never be known by their real face.
- Some say that the highest members of the cult have no faces of their own anymore—only the ones they wear.


---

## **The Drowned Prophets – The Sea’s Forgotten Voices**

**Deity:** Dagon, the Deep Father  
**Belief:** The sea does not take—it beckons.  
**Regions:** Tempest Sea, shipwreck survivors

The Drowned Prophets are those who have survived what no one should survive—shipwrecks, drowning, and the embrace of the abyss. When they return, they are not the same. They claim that they heard voices beneath the waves, whispering secrets the land was never meant to know.

- They do not fear drowning—they welcome it, believing that the deep holds revelations.
- They see themselves as messengers, speaking of things no surface-dweller could understand.
- Some say that when they speak, the tide listens—and follows.
