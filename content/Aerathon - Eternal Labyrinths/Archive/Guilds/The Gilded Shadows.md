## **Overview: The Gilded Shadows**

**Motto:** _"What’s in the Labyrinth is ours—gods and kings be damned."_  
**Headquarters:** **Unknown (Rumored to Operate from the Halvask Undercity, Veilshroud Expanse, and Freeports in the Tempest Sea)**  
**Guild Type:** Treasure Hunters, Black-Market Artifact Dealers, Smugglers, and Rogue Delvers  
**Notable Members:**

- **Velka the Unseen** (Guildmaster, Master Thief, and Alleged Founder)
- **Kael Riven** (Ex-Noble turned Smuggler Lord)
- **The Black Whisper** (Unknown Operator, Allegedly an Information Broker with Hidden Ties to the Arcane Broadcast Authority)

The Gilded Shadows are the most infamous and elusive of all Delver guilds, operating entirely outside of Halvaskian law and the authority of the HDA.

Unlike the Argent Banner, which seeks glory, or the Hollow Pact, which seeks knowledge, the Gilded Shadows seek wealth—pure and simple.

- **Master Thieves & Treasure Hunters** – They specialize in retrieving high-value relics from Labyrinths before official expeditions can claim them.
- **Black Market Dealers** – The Shadows operate a vast underground trade network, selling forbidden artifacts, relics, and stolen goods to the highest bidder.
- **Ghosts in the System** – No one truly knows their full numbers, and even the most well-planned security measures fail against their resourcefulness.

To the HDA, they are criminals and scavengers.

To the wealthy elite, they are a dangerous but necessary evil.

To those who operate outside the law, they are the only way to obtain power otherwise deemed “forbidden.”

## **Structure & Leadership**

Unlike most guilds, the Gilded Shadows do not operate with a traditional hierarchy. Instead, they function as a network of independent cells, each run by its own leader but loosely connected by shared resources and information.

### **Known Operators (Suspected Leadership Figures)**

|**Title**|**Name**|**Role in the Guild**|
|---|---|---|
|**The Unseen Mistress**|**Velka the Unseen**|Alleged Guildmaster—**if she exists at all**.|
|**The Silver Fox**|**Kael Riven**|Oversees **smuggling operations and black-market sales.**|
|**The Black Whisper**|**Unknown (Alias)**|Information broker **suspected to have ties to the ABA.**|
|**The Shadow Fang**|**Vaylen Drae**|Enforces guild law, eliminating **those who betray the organization.**|

Because of their fluid structure, the Gilded Shadows can never be fully eradicated—as long as someone is willing to deal in stolen relics, they will always exist.

## **What the Gilded Shadows Offer**

Unlike guilds bound by honor or contracts, the Gilded Shadows promise one thing and one thing only—gold.

### **Guild Benefits**

- **First Pick of High-Value Relics** – Members steal artifacts before the HDA can confiscate them, ensuring maximum profit from black-market sales.
- **Access to a Vast Criminal Network** – The Shadows have contacts within noble houses, merchant guilds, and underground factions, allowing stolen goods to be “laundered” into legitimate trade.
- **Complete Anonymity** – Members operate under false identities, untraceable by the law.
- **The Freedom to Walk Between Worlds** – Unlike rogue mercenary guilds, the Gilded Shadows can blend into high society, corrupt officials, and manipulate Livecast narratives.
- **No Masters, No Rules** – Unlike the Argent Banner or the HDA-controlled Delver guilds, the Shadows serve no one but themselves.

For those who wish to escape the control of governments, noble families, or religious institutions, the Gilded Shadows offer a life of danger, wealth, and absolute freedom.

---

## **Who Joins the Gilded Shadows?**

The Gilded Shadows recruit only the most resourceful, cunning, and fearless individuals.

- **Thieves, Smugglers, and Relic Poachers** – Those who have spent their lives working outside the law.
- **Disgraced Nobles & Exiled Aristocrats** – Some of their best operatives are former aristocrats who fell from grace but still know how to work the system.
- **Rogue Mages & Forbidden Scholars** – The Shadows possess knowledge of relics and artifacts that are considered too dangerous for public access.
- **Fugitive Delvers & Wanted Criminals** – Anyone who has burned their bridges with the HDA or elite guilds will find refuge among the Gilded Shadows.
- **Spies, Informants, and Silver-Tongued Diplomats** – Some of their most valuable members are those who never lift a blade—but know how to move gold in silence.

To the outside world, they are a faceless menace.

To those within their ranks, they are a family bound by coin, cunning, and survival.

---

## **Relationship with Other Governing Bodies**

### **Declared Enemies of the Halvaskian Delving Authority (HDA)**

- The HDA considers the Gilded Shadows to be one of the greatest threats to regulated Delving, as they undermine artifact control and bypass taxation laws.
- The Delver Marshals have standing kill orders on high-ranking members.
- Despite this, the Shadows continue to thrive, with corrupt officials turning a blind eye in exchange for a share of their profits.

### **A Love-Hate Relationship with the Grand Scrying Network (GSN)**

- The GSN profits from sensationalized stories about the Shadows, painting them as “the mysterious rogue guild that steals relics under the noses of kings.”
- Some suspect the ABA secretly brokers deals with the Shadows, ensuring that certain artifacts disappear before they can be exposed to the public.

### **Hostility with The Argent Banner & The Hollow Pact**

- The Argent Banner considers the Shadows to be nothing more than “vultures”, stealing relics that should belong to “true heroes.”
- The Hollow Pact condemns the Shadows for selling priceless knowledge to the highest bidder instead of preserving it for study.

Despite these rivalries, no one has ever successfully destroyed the Gilded Shadows—because their greatest strength is simple:

If you don’t know who they are, you can’t stop them.

---

## **The Guild’s Darkest Secret: The Hidden Vaults**

While the Gilded Shadows claim to sell everything they steal, rumors persist that they have hidden certain artifacts away for their own use.

- **The Veilshroud Cache** – A legendary vault of stolen relics, containing objects deemed “too powerful” to be sold.
- **The Nameless Archive** – Documents that supposedly prove the Labyrinths are connected in ways the HDA refuses to acknowledge.
- **The Argent Theft** – The Shadows once stole an artifact so dangerous that the Argent Banner erased all records of it. No one knows what it was—only that it was worth killing for.
