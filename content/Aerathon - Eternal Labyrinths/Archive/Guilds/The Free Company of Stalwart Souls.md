## **Overview: The Free Company of Stalwart Souls**

**Motto:** _"We go together, or we don’t go at all."_  
**Headquarters:** **Halvask, The Rusted Lodge (West District, Delver’s Row)**  
**Guild Type:** Open-Entry, Mixed-Rank, Unaffiliated Delver Collective  
**Notable Members:** **No single leader—operates as a loosely structured fraternity of Delvers.**

The Free Company of Stalwart Souls (often simply called “The Stalwart Souls”) is a last refuge for Delvers with nowhere else to go. Unlike the prestigious, sponsor-backed Delver guilds, or the shadowy and selective black-market groups, the Stalwart Souls are the guild of the unwanted, the unchosen, and the forgotten.

- No sponsor? No problem.
- No reputation? You’ll earn one.
- No team? You’ll find one.

Anyone willing to sign the charter and take an expedition contract is welcome—regardless of skill, background, past failures, or personal grudges.

---

## **Structure & Leadership**

Unlike other highly hierarchical guilds, the Free Company is run by its members, with no true leader beyond a small council of senior Delvers who oversee logistics.

### **The "Council of Elders" (Loose Administration)**

A rotating group of seasoned Delvers (often veterans too injured or burned out to actively explore anymore) serves as the guild’s guiding hand.

|**Title**|**Name**|**Role in the Guild**|
|---|---|---|
|**Guild Quartermaster**|**Keldrin "Half-Dead" Rusk**|Manages gear storage & loaning out basic supplies to new Delvers.|
|**Expedition Coordinator**|**Darla "Iron-Fangs" Velmire**|Assigns jobs, matches solo Delvers to teams.|
|**Vault-Keeper**|**Hargun "Coin-Cutter" Fisk**|Ensures fair profit distribution, settles internal disputes.|
|**Scribe of the Lost**|**Jessa the Silent**|Keeps records of members who never returned.|

Despite this loose structure, the Free Company enforces a single ironclad rule:

*"You do not betray your team. Ever."*

Anyone caught abandoning or betraying fellow Delvers in the Labyrinths is permanently exiled—or worse.

---

## **What the Free Company Offers**

The Free Company is not wealthy, powerful, or well-connected, but it provides one thing no other guild does: a chance.

### **Guild Benefits**

- **No Membership Fees or Sponsorship Requirements** – Anyone willing to sign their name into the charter can join.
- **Matchmaking for Solo Delvers** – If you don’t have a party, the Free Company will assign you to one.
- **A Guildhall & Safe Haven** – The Rusted Lodge in Halvask serves as a refuge for unaffiliated Delvers, offering cheap bunks, warm food, and survival tips from veterans.
- **Basic Equipment Lending** – Guild Quartermasters loan out salvaged weapons and armor—they may be rusted, dented, or barely holding together, but they’re free.
- **Fair Profit Sharing** – The guild takes only a small cut of any treasure recovered, ensuring Delvers keep most of what they earn.
- **Burial Rites & Remembrance** – Every Delver who dies in the Labyrinths is given a recorded name in the Guild Ledger, ensuring they are not forgotten.

---

## **Who Joins the Free Company?**

The Stalwart Souls are not heroes, champions, or chosen ones. They are:

- **Friendless Delvers** – Those abandoned by former teams or who never had one to begin with.
- **Failed Adventurers** – Those who survived a doomed expedition but lost their reputations in the process.
- **Criminals Seeking Redemption** – Ex-thieves, smugglers, and war deserters looking for a fresh start.
- **Exiled or Fallen Nobles** – Disgraced aristocrats who can no longer return home.
- **Labyrinth Survivors Haunted by the Past** – Those who should have died but didn’t—and are still searching for answers.

While other guilds boast discipline, elite training, and prestigious reputations, the Free Company survives purely through grit, camaraderie, and sheer stubbornness.

---

## **Relationship with Other Governing Bodies**

### **Halvaskian Delving Authority (HDA) – Cautiously Tolerated**

- The HDA recognizes the Free Company but does not respect it, viewing it as a collective of “low-tier” Delvers with little influence.
- While the Free Company operates legally, they receive no government backing or financial support.
- The HDA secretly believes that the Free Company exists to absorb disposable Delvers, keeping them out of the way of major guild-backed expeditions.

### **Grand Scrying Network (GSN) – A Constant Source of Entertainment**

- Livecasters love the Free Company—viewers enjoy watching underdogs struggle and sometimes triumph against impossible odds.
- The ABA often lets Free Company expeditions air unfiltered, as their failures make for great drama, and their victories make for inspiring stories.
- Some Free Company Delvers gain accidental celebrity status, though most die unknown and unseen.

### **Rivalry with Elite Guilds (Especially the Argent Banner)**

- The Free Company resent guilds like the Argent Banner, seeing them as privileged and soft, protected by noble funding.
- Certain sponsored Delvers look down on Free Company members, dismissing them as expendable and unworthy of serious recognition.
- The Free Company doesn’t care—they know that, at the end of the day, all Delvers bleed the same in the Labyrinths.

---

## **The Downside of the Free Company**

While the Free Company welcomes all, it also suffers for it.

- **Mortality Rate is High** – More Free Company members die in the Labyrinths than any other guild.
- **No Guaranteed Protection** – Unlike sponsored guilds, members must rely purely on their own skills and their teammates to survive.
- **Constant Infighting** – With no strong leadership, internal disputes often escalate into fights, backstabbing, and betrayals.
- **Some Members Are... Unstable** – Some join the Free Company because no one else would take them. And sometimes, there’s a good reason for that.

Despite these flaws, the Free Company endures—because as long as there are Delvers with nowhere else to go, the Free Company will always have new recruits.
