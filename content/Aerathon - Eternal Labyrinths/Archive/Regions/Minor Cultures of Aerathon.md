_Transcribed from the Grand Archives of Halvask, 7th Era, Year 943_

While the great nations and ruling factions define much of Aerathon, the lands are home to countless minor cultures, sub-societies, and divergent traditions that break from their regional norms. Some have splintered from larger civilizations, rejecting their ways, while others have evolved alongside them, embracing unusual customs, hidden knowledge, or forgotten traditions.

Though smaller in number, these cultures are no less significant. Some thrive in isolation, unwilling to bend to the world’s changes, while others act as hidden forces pulling the strings from the shadows.

---

## **The Minor Cultures of Each Region**

### **Halvask and the Dominion – The Spectacle and Its Shadows**

While Halvask thrives on Delving, celebrity, and the pursuit of influence, not all of its people embrace its culture of excess. Within the great city and beyond its borders, there exist those who see themselves as keepers of truth, architects of fate, or unseen hands guiding the future.

#### **The Archivists of the Veiled Codex**

Among the noble houses and Delver magnates, a secretive order known as the Veiled Codex works to alter and control historical records, believing that the truth is a weapon best wielded by those who understand its consequences. While Halvask presents itself as the heart of knowledge and progress, the Codex ensures that some stories remain untold, and some truths never reach the light.

- **Core Belief:** "History is not recorded—it is written."
- **Conflict with Halvask**: They manipulate public records to maintain control over knowledge, directly opposing the idea of freedom of information.

#### **The Gutter Lords of the Low Spire**

Beneath the opulent districts of Halvask lies the Low Spire, a city beneath a city, where failed Delvers, exiles, and outcasts form their own shadow society. These are the Gutter Lords, crime bosses and information brokers who thrive in the ruins beneath the ruins, where forgotten relics and discarded riches are bartered for blood.

- **Core Belief**: "We take what the highborn waste."
- **Conflict with Halvask**: They oppose the structured hierarchy of fame and power, believing true survival lies in the cracks of civilization.

---

## **The Stormcall Isles – Honor, Blood, and the Ones Who Turned Away**

The warriors of the Stormcall Isles are bound by tradition and strength, but not all of their people follow the same code. Some reject the sea entirely, while others see the old ways as chains that must be broken.

#### **The Skyborn Marauders**

A rogue faction of skyship raiders, the Skyborn are Stormcallers who turned away from the sea and took to the skies instead. They believe that true warriors should not be bound to the tides, but should ride the wind and storm as free spirits.

- **Core Belief**: "The sea has limits. The sky does not."
- **Conflict with the Stormcallers**: Seen as dishonorable traitors for abandoning naval combat in favor of skyfaring raids.

#### **The Tidemarked**

Marked by ritualistic drowning and rebirth, the Tidemarked are a cult that believes death by the sea grants enlightenment. Those who survive their trials are said to hear the voices of the depths, guiding them toward hidden truths beneath the waves.

- **Core Belief**: "Drown, awaken, and listen."
- **Conflict with the Stormcallers**: They are seen as fanatics, dangerously obsessed with sacrificing themselves to the ocean’s will.

---

### **The Sunfire Expanse – The Gilded Tyrants and Those Who Defy Them**

The Magocrats of the Sunfire Expanse rule with gold, power, and strict hierarchy, believing that greatness is inherited, not earned. But beneath their rule, others seek freedom, chaos, or lost truths buried beneath the dunes.

#### **The Sunless Scribes**

A secret order of scholars and fugitives, the Sunless Scribes work to preserve forbidden knowledge that the magocratic elite have attempted to erase. Their hidden libraries hold books that no longer exist in the public record, and their agents work in secrecy, passing down truths that could undermine the entire ruling class.

- **Core Belief**: "What is erased is not forgotten."
- **Conflict with the Theocrats**: They oppose the controlled flow of knowledge, seeking to uncover truths hidden by the ruling elite.

#### **The Glassborn Aristocrats**

A faction of decadent elites who have taken the obsession with luxury and magic to extremes, the Glassborn believe that wealth alone determines a person’s worth. They surround themselves with enchanted servants, living statues, and artificial life, ensuring that they never need to sully themselves with labor.

- **Core Belief**: "Perfection is purchased."
- **Conflict with the Theocrats:** Even among the tyrants of the Sunfire Expanse, the Glassborn’s detachment from reality is seen as dangerous.

---

### **The Umbral Dominion – The Silent Watchers and Those Who Dare to Speak**

The people of the **Umbral Dominion** believe in **secrecy, patience, and restraint**, but not all accept this fate. Some whisper **forbidden names**, while others wish to **dismantle the silence entirely.**

#### **The Nameless Choir**

A group of heretics within the Dominion, the Nameless Choir is said to recite prayers to gods who were never meant to be remembered. Their songs are forbidden, their rituals unrecorded, and those who hear them speak of memories that are not their own.

- **Core Belief**: "Some names must be spoken."
- **Conflict with the Dominion:** The Choir threatens the Dominion’s entire purpose—keeping dangerous knowledge hidden.

#### **The Hollowed Masks**

Rather than erase themselves from history, the Hollowed Masks seek to steal the identities of others, wearing enchanted masks that allow them to assume the lives, voices, and memories of those they replace.

- **Core Belief**: "A mask is a second chance."
- **Conflict with the Dominion**: While the Dominion believes in controlling knowledge, the Masks believe in rewriting it entirely.

---

## **Final Observations: The Fractured Threads of Civilization**

The dominant nations and ruling factions of Aerathon are not as united as they appear. Beneath their banners, rogue orders, hidden sects, and diverging cultures thrive, shaping the world in unseen ways.

Some fight to preserve the past, while others seek to erase it entirely.

Some wish to reclaim power, while others want to dismantle it forever.

But the rise of the Nameless Labyrinth in Halvask has shown one undeniable truth—

The world’s foundations are not as solid as we once believed.

And history may not be written by the victors—

It may be rewritten by those who know how to bend it.
