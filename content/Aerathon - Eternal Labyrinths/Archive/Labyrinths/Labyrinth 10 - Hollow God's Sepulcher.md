### **"The Buried Deity"**

_"The god beneath the roots is not dead. It is waiting."_  
— **Final words of Seeress Valna Greenbloom**

Deep within the **Whispering Wilds**, buried beneath **ancient, gnarled trees and tangled roots**, lies a **tomb that was never meant to be found**. The **Hollow God’s Sepulcher** is **not a ruin, not a temple**, but a **prison**, its entrance hidden for millennia beneath layers of **earth, moss, and sacred wards placed by hands long since turned to dust**.

It was meant to remain **sealed forever**.

Until the land itself **shifted**, tearing open the forest floor to reveal an **impossible descent**—**an obsidian staircase spiraling downward**, leading to a place where **no birds sing, no wind stirs, and the ground itself seems to hold its breath.**

---

### **⛓️ The Sepulcher That Was Never Meant to Be Opened**

The **Hollow God’s Sepulcher** is a **massive subterranean labyrinth**, carved from a single, seamless stone, its walls **lined with murals of a forgotten faith**. These murals tell **a story with no ending**, depicting **a being crowned in golden roots, rising above the world in adoration—only to be cast down, shackled, and entombed.**

Inside, explorers have encountered:

- **Endless stone halls**, where the further one walks, the heavier the air becomes—as if something is pressing down, unseen.
- **Vast, root-choked chambers**, where the vines pulse like **slow, beating veins**.
- **Alters without names**, their surfaces still **slick with crimson**, though no one has performed a ritual here in recorded history.
- **A deep, echoing hum**, emanating from somewhere **far below**, like the exhale of a sleeping colossus.

The further one descends, the **louder the hum grows**.

The closer one gets, the more the murals begin to **change**.

Their figures shift, their faces **turn to watch those who enter**. And in some, the god **is no longer bound.**

---

### **🔻 The Vanishing of the Verdant Priests**

The **first recorded expedition** into the Sepulcher was led by **Seeress Valna Greenbloom** of the **Verdant Priests**, a druidic order who once safeguarded the **deepest reaches of the Whispering Wilds**.

Her order had **long warned against disturbing the tomb**, believing it to be **a binding place, not a burial site**. But when the Sepulcher revealed itself, she and **twelve of her most trusted disciples** entered, determined to uncover **the truth of what lay beneath.**

Only one returned.

A young initiate, found **wandering the outskirts of the Wilds days later**, her robes **torn, her hands covered in sigils carved deep into her skin**, her voice **barely above a whisper**.

She spoke only one sentence before falling into a coma.

_"We were wrong. It is not dead."_

The **Verdant Priests vanished soon after**, their sacred groves **abandoned**, their names **forgotten in less than a decade**.

As if they had never existed at all.

---

### **🌿 The Throne Below**

Legends say that **deep within the Sepulcher**, beyond the endless tunnels, beyond the choking roots and shifting murals, lies **a throne of petrified wood**, ancient and cracked, upon which **something once sat… and still might**.

No one has reached it.

Or if they have, **they were never seen again.**

Some claim that the god entombed here was **never a god at all, but something far older**—something that had no followers, but still **demanded worship**.

Something that **was never meant to be given a name.**

And yet, the Sepulcher **waits**.

Its doors **open, its halls whispering**, as if inviting someone to descend.

To kneel.

To listen.

And to understand **why it was buried so deep in the first place.**

---

### **📜 Final Observations: The Sepulcher Breathes**

Unlike other Labyrinths, the **Hollow God’s Sepulcher does not test strength, wit, or willpower**.

It does not **set traps, nor challenge those who enter**.

It simply **waits**.

And each year, the roots grow **a little further outward**.

As if whatever sleeps within is **beginning to stir.**
