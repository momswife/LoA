### **"The Labyrinth That Bleeds"**

_"It is not stone. It is not metal. The walls are warm beneath your hands, and when you cut them—oh gods, when you cut them—it screams."_  
— **Delver Mareth Voss, last survivor of the Bloodmarked Expedition**

The **Crimson Labyrinth** is one of the most **feared and least understood** of the Eternal Labyrinths.

It is **not a ruin**, nor **a tomb**, nor **a forgotten fortress**. It is **a living thing**, a structure of **flesh and sinew**, its walls **pulsing with unnatural life**, its veins threading through tunnels that **shift, contract, and breathe**.

It is **not built of stone**, but of **bone and blood**.

It is **not ancient**.

It is **growing.**

---

### **🩸 The Crimson Labyrinth: A Living Nightmare**

The **Crimson Labyrinth** does not stay in one place. Unlike the **other Labyrinths**, which remain **fixed** in their locations, the Crimson Labyrinth **emerges**—growing from the ground **like a cyst**, its entrance appearing **wherever the land itself seems weakest**.

It has appeared:

- **In the heart of the Whispering Wilds**, rising overnight, its flesh-like walls entwined with ancient trees.
- **Beneath the Titan Highlands**, bursting from a mountainside, spilling veins and tendrils across the stone.
- **In the middle of the Frostlands**, where nothing should grow, where the ice itself turned red with something warm and wet.

Each time, it lingers for **a single year**, before **collapsing in on itself**, leaving behind **only scarred, blackened land**, and the echoes of something still **breathing beneath the soil.**

It does not call for **explorers**.

It does not call for **warriors**.

It calls for **those who hunger.**

And those who answer **never return the same.**

---

### **🗡️ The Bloodmarked Expedition and the Labyrinth’s Horrors**

In **6E 801**, the **Delver Guild of Halvask** launched the first **fully funded expedition** into the Crimson Labyrinth.

Known as the **Bloodmarked Expedition**, it consisted of:

- **High Inquisitor Mareth Voss**, a warrior-priest tasked with **unraveling unnatural anomalies**.
- **Archmagus Elira Xelthorne**, a mage specializing in **organic arcana**—magic tied to **life, blood, and flesh.**
- **Delver-Lord Thrayn Ironholt**, a titanborn berserker whose **strength was said to rival the storms of the Sunfire Expanse.**
- **Scholar-Physician Dren Valos**, a medical theorist seeking **to understand the nature of life and death.**
- **Seeker Cassia Rhyne**, a relic hunter who had survived **three Labyrinth expeditions before this one.**

They entered the **Crimson Labyrinth** at its latest emergence, in the **ashen valley of Duskreach**, where its entrance had split the earth like a **gaping wound**.

They were inside for **three weeks**.

Only **Mareth Voss** returned.

His body was **intact, but changed**—his skin **marked with red veins that pulsed beneath the surface**, his eyes **black as the void, rimmed with crimson**, his voice **hoarse from screams he refused to explain.**

He **never spoke of what happened inside**.

Only that the others were **not dead**.

They had **become something else.**

And the Labyrinth had **let him go for a reason.**

### **🩸 Theories and Terrors: What the Crimson Labyrinth Wants**

Unlike other Labyrinths, which hold **treasures, secrets, and trials**, the **Crimson Labyrinth does not reward those who enter.**

It **changes them.**

- Some scholars believe it is **a growing entity, a living organism**—not a dungeon, but a **creature**.
- Others believe it is **not of this world**, that it **gestates beneath the land**, emerging **only when it must feed**.
- The **Umbral Dominion** theorizes that the Labyrinth is **a failed god**, a divine being that was **never fully born**, seeking **to complete itself by consuming those who enter it.**

Whatever the truth, the **few Delvers who have entered and returned are never the same.**

They suffer from:

- **Unnatural healing**, their wounds closing **too quickly, too perfectly**, as if the Labyrinth itself still lingers inside them.
- **A constant hunger**, an ache in their bones, a need to consume **more than food, more than drink—something deeper, something missing.**
- **Whispers in their sleep**, voices calling them back, calling them **home.**

And always, always, their **blood does not clot the way it should.**

It moves.

It **responds.**

It **remembers.**

---

### **⚖️ The Fate of the Bloodmarked Expedition**

While Mareth Voss **never spoke of what he saw**, others who have brushed against the Labyrinth’s influence **have glimpsed visions**—fragmented, broken images of what **became of his party.**

- **Elira Xelthorne, the Archmagus**, was seen **woven into the walls**, her body half-flesh, half-arcane sigil, whispering spells that **made the walls pulse in time with her breath.**
- **Thrayn Ironholt, the titanborn berserker**, was spotted **leading something**, a **warband of figures with red-veined skin**, their weapons **glistening with fresh blood.**
- **Dren Valos, the scholar-physician**, was **nowhere to be found.** Only his voice remained, echoing through the tunnels, **repeating surgical notes in a language that should not exist.**
- **Cassia Rhyne, the relic hunter**, was last seen **staring into the largest chamber of the Labyrinth, where something vast, something writhing, something incomplete, waited in the dark.**

**The Labyrinth does not kill.**

It **repurposes.**

---

### **📜 Final Observations: The Labyrinth is Growing**

The **Crimson Labyrinth** does not stay **where it is born.**

It is not **a ruin to be conquered, not a kingdom to be claimed, not a dungeon to be mapped.**

It is **a wound in the world**, an infection that **emerges, consumes, and vanishes—only to surface again elsewhere.**

And each time it returns, it is **bigger.**

One day, the Crimson Labyrinth will **not disappear.**

One day, it will **not be confined to a single place.**

One day, it will **become something whole.**

And when that day comes, **all of Aerathon will bleed.**
