### **"The Trial of The Ascended"**

_"The monks who walked these halls sought ascension. They found something else."_  
— **Scholar Fendris Vaelor**

High atop the Wraithspire Peaks, where the air grows too thin to breathe and the sky darkens long before twilight, lies the Shattered Monastery—a Labyrinth not of stone corridors or twisting caves, but of floating ruins, suspended in defiance of gravity itself.

Once, it was a place of great enlightenment, a sanctuary where monks of the Celestial Path devoted themselves to the pursuit of true transcendence.

Now, it is a fractured realm, a ruin of shattered platforms and broken bridges, where the voices of the long-departed still echo.

They sought to become more than mortal.

Instead, they became something else entirely.

---

### **🌫️ A Labyrinth Above the Clouds**

The Shattered Monastery is unlike any other Eternal Labyrinth—it is a realm in pieces, where gravity is not absolute, where stone walkways float in midair, and where the only way forward is to leap across the void and trust that the path will find you.

Delvers who enter have reported:

- Bridges that appear only when walked upon, then vanish behind them.
- Statues of monks, arms outstretched in meditation—until they move, their heads turning to watch trespassers pass.
- Mantras carved into the floating stones, their meanings shifting with each glance, revealing truths one may not wish to learn.
- Chambers where gravity bends, sending those within walking along walls or drifting weightless.

The monks believed that, by shedding the burdens of the mortal world, they could ascend beyond the cycle of life and death.

They succeeded.

But not in the way they intended.

---

### **🕉️ The Trial of the Ascended**

Unlike other Labyrinths, the Shattered Monastery does not test strength, wit, or endurance.

It tests the soul.

To reach its heart, one must pass the Trial of the Ascended—a test that has no written rules, no clear answers, and no second chances.

Some Delvers have spoken of their trials:

- A bridge of light that only appears when one walks without fear.
- A bell that must be rung, but only by one who accepts their own mortality.
- A door that only opens to those who have let go of everything they once were.

Some fail and fall into the abyss.

Some succeed and step through.

And some remain, joining the monks in a form beyond flesh, trapped in the endless repetition of their meditation—forever hovering above the world they left behind.

---

### **🌟 The Twin Stars and the First Conquerors**

For centuries, no one had ever reached the inner sanctum of the Monastery. Many had attempted, but all had fallen short—until the arrival of two Delvers, known now as the Twin Stars of Aerathon.

#### **Sarina Lumehart** _(The Wandering Star)_

_A mystic of unparalleled grace, Sarina moves like the wind itself. Known for wielding a staff of celestial glass, she sees glimpses of the future in the stars—though never her own fate._

#### **Kiel Vaelor** _(The Falling Star)_

_A warrior-philosopher, Kiel is famed for wielding the Blade of the Empty Moon, a sword said to cut not flesh, but the attachments of the soul. He seeks enlightenment, but fears what he may become if he finds it._

Together, they ventured into the Monastery, facing its trials not as rivals, but as reflections of one another.

And against all odds—they reached the summit.

At the highest peak, where the last monks had once meditated beneath an open sky, they found something no one had expected:

A single, unbroken pathway of light, stretching beyond the clouds, leading somewhere else entirely.

Sarina stepped forward without hesitation.

Kiel hesitated, then followed.

And in an instant—they were gone.

The Labyrinth remains conquered, but the Twin Stars have never returned.

Some say they ascended beyond mortal form, fulfilling the monks’ original goal.

Others say they found something no mortal should have witnessed—and could not return even if they wished.

All that is known is that, since their disappearance, the floating ruins have begun to shift once more.

As if the Labyrinth is still waiting for someone else to follow.

---

### **📜 Final Observations: The Labyrinth That Leads Elsewhere**

The Shattered Monastery is not simply a ruin.

It is a threshold.

A door to something beyond understanding, waiting for those who dare step forward without looking back.

It was conquered once.

But that does not mean its story is over.

Because the monks believed that ascension was not the end of the journey—only the beginning.

And now, someone must take the next step.
