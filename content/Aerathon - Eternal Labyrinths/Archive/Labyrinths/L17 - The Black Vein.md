## **The Heart of the Dead Mountain**

> _"It should not be there. The mountain is solid stone. And yet it beats."_  
**— Last recorded words of Geomancer Aldros Veyne**

Deep beneath the Titan Highlands, hidden within the roots of Mount Karadris, lies a Labyrinth that should not exist. Unlike the others, the Black Vein was not discovered by explorers, Delvers, or kings. It was dug up, unearthed by dwarven miners working the deepest tunnels beneath the mountain.

The first sign that something was wrong came when their pickaxes struck something that bled. Not stone. Not metal. Something darker. Something alive.

That was the first warning. The miners did not heed it.

And so, the mountain began to eat them.

---

## **The Collapse of Karadris**

For centuries, the Karadris Mines had been among the most productive in the Titan Highlands, their tunnels running deeper than any other dwarven excavation. The Stonetouched Priests had long warned that the mountain had limits, that there were places beneath the rock that should never be disturbed.

But greed is louder than wisdom, and the Delving Guild of Grom-Khaal ignored the warnings. When their tunnels reached veins of an unknown black mineral, richer and denser than any ore they had ever seen, they dug deeper still.

Then, on the third day, the tunnels collapsed. Entire chambers caved inward, sealing off dozens of workers.

The surviving miners reported hearing something beneath the stone—a pulse, like a heartbeat, slow and deep, reverberating through the rock itself.

They dug desperately to recover their lost brethren. When they finally broke through the rubble…

The tunnels were empty.

There were no bodies. No tools. No sign that anyone had ever been there.

Only the stone remained. And the beating grew louder.

---

## **The First Delvers and Their Failure**

Within weeks, the news of the "Bleeding Mines" of Karadris had spread across Aerathon. The Delvers of Halvask—eager for new discoveries—petitioned the Titan Highlands to open the ruins for exploration.

By **7E 922**, the **Black Vein Expedition** was formed:

- **Delver-Lord Vathren Aelkor**, a warlock-scholar known for his mastery of void magic.
- **High Mason Kargun Stonefist**, a dwarven earthshaper able to speak with the bones of the world.
- **Warden Aelira Lys**, a paladin of the Celestial Arbiters, tasked with purging the darkness from forgotten places.
- **A contingent of 40 elite warriors**, backed by the Magocratic Court of Halvask and the Warlord Tribes of Grom-Khaal.

Their mission: enter the Labyrinth beneath the mountain, map its depths, and return with its secrets.

Their fate: obliteration.

---

## **The Labyrinth of Flesh and Stone**

The expedition entered the Black Vein with arcane torches and runestones, expecting tunnels of stone and dust.

Instead, they found hallways that breathed.

The deeper they ventured, the less like a mountain it became. The walls—once rough-hewn stone—became slick, pulsing with a rhythmic beat, as if the mountain itself were alive.

Their torches dimmed. Their words echoed back at them, whispered in voices that did not belong to their own.

And then, the first delver vanished.

One moment he walked among them. The next, his place was empty, as if he had never existed. His name? No one could recall it.

One by one, more disappeared, until only three remained—Aelkor, Stonefist, and Lys.

They turned to flee—only to realize the tunnel had changed. Where the entrance had once been, now stood a vast, gaping wound in the earth, a chasm lined with jagged ribs of blackened stone, leading downward into an infinite abyss.

Something moved within it. Something vast. Something aware.

They ran.

---

## **The Aftermath and the Curse of the Vein**

Of the **43 Delvers**, only **three escaped**—and even they were not untouched.

- **Delver-Lord Aelkor** spoke of the thing beneath the mountain, but as the years passed, he forgot its name, then its shape, then the words to describe it. By his final days, he could only scream when asked about the Black Vein, clawing at his eyes, unable to recall why.
- **High Mason Stonefist** returned to the dwarven halls of Skallvik, where he took a vow of silence. He never spoke again, save for his dying words: _"It’s still listening."_
- **Warden Lys**, once a beacon of faith, cast down her holy sigil and fled the clergy. She disappeared into the Veilshroud Expanse, where she was later found writing in a language no one could understand, her body covered in blackened veins that pulsed as if something inside her still lived.

Since then, the Black Vein has remained sealed, the tunnels collapsed, the mountain warded by Stonetouched Priests and Highland shamans.

But the heartbeat remains.

And some say, on quiet nights, you can hear it calling.

---

# **Final Observations: The Mountain Watches**

Unlike other Labyrinths, the Black Vein does not move, does not shift, does not trick.

It simply waits.

There is no treasure inside, no monsters, no traps in the conventional sense.

But those who enter do not return.

And those who listen for too long find themselves drawn back to it, even in their dreams.

Something beneath Mount Karadris is alive.

And if it wakes, the mountain itself may no longer be enough to contain it.
