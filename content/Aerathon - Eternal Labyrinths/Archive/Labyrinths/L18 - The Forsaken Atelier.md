## **The Workshop of The Forgotten**

> _"They built the perfect spell. And then it devoured them."_  
— **Headmaster Oryn Velsh, 7E 880**

The Forsaken Atelier was once a sanctuary of genius, a workshop where the greatest arcanists, artificers, and spellcrafters of an age long past gathered to push the limits of magic itself. It was a place of pure, unshackled creation, where knowledge was pursued with no thought of consequence.

But in their pursuit of perfection, they went too far.

Something they crafted turned against them.

And in a single night, the Atelier fell silent—sealed away beneath Halvask, its halls abandoned, its name struck from the records of the Magocratic Court.

For centuries, it remained untouched, forbidden, until the day a Delver walked inside and emerged victorious.

The Forsaken Atelier has been conquered.

But the cost of its reclamation is still being calculated.

---

## **The Labyrinth of Living Magic**

Unlike the ruins and tombs of other Labyrinths, the Atelier was not built to test or contain—it was a living archive, a laboratory of spellcraft, a factory of impossible creations.

Even after its abandonment, the experiments continued without their masters. The spells within became self-sustaining, shaping the Atelier into something alive, shifting, and ever-changing.

Among the most dangerous discoveries were:

- Sentient Arcane Glyphs, symbols that crawl across the walls, rewriting spells even as they are spoken.
- The Wandering Loom, a massive, golden weaving machine that, once activated, begins spinning silk out of raw magic—and does not stop until its creator has been woven into the tapestry.
- The Hall of Unfinished Works, where artifacts abandoned mid-construction still attempt to complete themselves, fusing with anything—or anyone—that enters.
- The Echo of the First Spell, an ancient incantation that no one dares to recite, for fear that its first speaker still waits to answer.

To reclaim the Atelier was to tame its chaos.

But even now, it is unclear whether anything within was truly destroyed… or simply waiting for its next master.

---

## **The Conquering of the Atelier**

The Forsaken Atelier was conquered in 7E 904 by House Arvendis, a powerful family of Halvaskan artificers, led by Master Delver Kaelis Arvendis and his team of mage-engineers.

For three months, they mapped its shifting corridors, outwitted its living traps, and sealed away its most unstable creations.

Their greatest challenge was the Heart of the Atelier, a vast chamber where the minds of its original creators still lingered—trapped within their own spells, whispering incantations that should never have been spoken.

Kaelis Arvendis sealed the chamber, entombing the restless sorcerers within, and bound the Labyrinth to his family’s name.

For the first time in history, the Forsaken Atelier had a master once more.

But as with all things in the Atelier, the victory came with a price.

---

## **The Atelier Today: The Arvendis Vault**

With the Atelier now under House Arvendis’ control, its treasures, secrets, and experiments have been reclaimed. The Magocratic Court of Halvask has declared it a restricted site, allowing only sanctioned scholars and artificers to study its wonders.

However, strange anomalies persist.

- The lights never dim—even in sealed chambers, illuminations flicker as if responding to something unseen.
- Blueprints continue to write themselves, drawing designs for machines no one understands.
- Kaelis Arvendis himself has not spoken since his victory, his mind seemingly lost in deep contemplation—or in something else entirely.

Some whisper that the Atelier never needed a conqueror.

It needed a new caretaker.

---

## **Final Observations: A Victory Without End**

The Forsaken Atelier has been reclaimed.

Its halls no longer belong to the forgotten spellcrafters of the past.

But a Labyrinth is never truly conquered—only claimed.

And those who claim it inherit everything within.

Even the things that should have been left behind.
