## **The Labyrinth Where War Never Ends**

> _"You do not leave it. You survive it. And when you die, you are born into it again."_  
> **— Last words of Delver Ralvas Thorne**

The Labyrinth of Rust and Ruin is not a place of puzzles, nor a dungeon of traps, nor a prison of shifting illusions. It is a war without end—a battlefield frozen in time, locked in an eternal cycle of conflict, where the fallen never truly die and victory does not exist.

First discovered in 2E 1039, deep within the Titan Highlands, the Labyrinth of Rust and Ruin has remained one of the deadliest and least understood of the Eternal Labyrinths. Those who enter do not simply fight its trials—they become part of the war itself.

And those who fall are not allowed to rest.

---

## **The Endless War of the Rusted Dead**

The first Delvers who entered the Labyrinth of Rust and Ruin believed it to be a forgotten battlefield, its gates carved into the ruins of an ancient, abandoned fortress, half-buried in the crags of the Highlands.

But when they crossed the threshold, they found themselves somewhere else entirely.

The land within the Labyrinth is not dead ruins, but a battlefield frozen in an eternal twilight, stretching far beyond what should be possible, the sky choked with ashen clouds, the ground littered with broken weapons, rusted armor, and the skeletal remains of those who have fought before.

And the warriors—the ones who should be dead—stand again.

- Legions of rusted soldiers, bearing banners of forgotten empires, march endlessly through the wastelands.
- Ghostly war horns sound in the distance, calling the lost to battle.
- Steel-clad knights, their armor fusing to their very bones, fight duels that have no end.
- Archers loose arrows, only for them to fall endlessly from the sky, never striking the ground.

Time does not move forward in the Labyrinth of Rust and Ruin.

It simply repeats.

## **The First Expeditions and the Fate of the Fallen**

The first warband to enter the Labyrinth, led by General Targan of the Highland Clans, vanished without a trace. No bodies were recovered. No messages returned.

A second attempt was made by a team of Halvaskan scholars, escorted by mercenaries and Delvers. They lasted six days inside, until only one returned—a mage named **Kelys Thorne**, who emerged aged decades beyond his years, his hands stained red with rust that could not be washed away.

When asked what had happened, he said only this:

_"Ralvas is still in there. They all are. They are still fighting. And I think… I think I was too."_

He took his own life within the year.

His brother, **Ralvas Thorne**, was among the missing. A decade later, in 4E 19, a third expedition entered the Labyrinth. Among their ranks was a young warrior-scholar, **Lysara Vael**, who had heard the stories of Kelys Thorne’s failure and sought to uncover the truth.

She returned alone, after three weeks inside.

And when she did, she carried the helmet of Ralvas Thorne.

It was not rusted.

It was freshly bloodied.

And the blood was still warm.

## **The Curse of the War Without End**

Unlike other Labyrinths, which hold treasures, traps, or trials, the Labyrinth of Rust and Ruin offers nothing but war.

And yet, some still seek it out, drawn by rumors that to conquer it would mean an end to an ancient cycle of suffering. Some believe that the warriors trapped within are the spirits of a forgotten army, bound to fight until someone, or something, breaks the cycle. Others believe that the Labyrinth is not a place of the past, but a vision of the future—an omen of a war yet to come.

No one has ever reached the heart of the battlefield.

Some say that in its center stands a throne of rusted blades, and that whoever sits upon it may claim dominion over all warriors who have fallen before them.

But to reach it, one must first fight through eternity.

And no one has ever been strong enough to reach the end.
