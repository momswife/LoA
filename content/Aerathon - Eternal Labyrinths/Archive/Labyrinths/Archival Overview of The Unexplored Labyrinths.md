_Transcribed from the Grand Archives of Halvask, 7th Era, Year 943_

Though twenty of the **Eternal Labyrinths** have been at least **partially explored**, there are **twelve whose nature remains a mystery**. Some have been **discovered but never entered**, others have **appeared only once before vanishing**, and a few are **spoken of only in prophecy and madness**.

No Delver has survived them.

And some believe they exist **not to be conquered, but to warn.**

---

### **21. The Bleeding Door** _(Unknown – The Labyrinth That Walks)_

_"We camped in an empty field. At dusk, the door was there. At dawn, it was gone."_ – **Delver’s Account, 6E 812**

The **Bleeding Door** is not **a structure**, but **an event**. Appearing only during **a Blood Moon**, it manifests as **a massive red door**, freestanding with **no walls to support it**, oozing **black ichor from its hinges**.

Those who enter are **never seen again**.

But sometimes, the door **reappears with new scratches on the inside**.

---

### **22. The Obsidian Mire** _(Tempest Sea – The Black Marsh Below)_

_"The ocean should not have a bottom. And yet, something waits beneath the waves."_ – **Captain Veyra Maldris, last transmission**

Deep beneath the **Tempest Sea**, where the water **blackens**, there is a place where ships **sink without a sound**, their remains **never found**.

Those who sail too close hear **bubbles rising from the deep**—not from air, but from **something breathing below**.

No diver has ever returned.

And yet, sometimes, **ships lost centuries ago wash ashore, their hulls intact, their decks empty—except for footprints leading back into the surf.**

---

### **23. The Hollow God’s Chamber** _(Whispering Wilds – The Unfinished Shrine)_

_"The idols bear no names. The prayers have no voices. The god has no face."_ – **High Priestess Elyva Soln**

Hidden beneath the **roots of the oldest tree in the Whispering Wilds**, this **sunken temple** predates all known civilization. The inscriptions upon its walls **do not match any recorded language**, and yet, when read, **all who gaze upon them weep.**

At its heart, there is **a throne**, carved from petrified wood, **too large for any mortal to sit upon**.

The floor before it is stained **with the handprints of those who knelt before it in reverence—or in fear.**

---

### **24. The Glass Labyrinth** _(Halvask – The Reflection That Watches)_

_"I looked into the mirror. It blinked first."_ – **Scribe Dannis Erel, recovered journal**

Beneath the city of **Halvask**, sealed behind **runes that have not faded for centuries**, lies a **Labyrinth made entirely of glass**.

No explorer has stepped inside.

Yet those who stand before its entrance **swear they see movement** within—**reflections that should not be there, figures that do not belong to them.**

Some nights, those reflections **press their hands against the glass**.

As if waiting for someone to **open the way.**

### **26. The Dreaming Sepulcher** _(Veilshroud Expanse – The Tomb That Breathes)_

_"It should not have lungs. And yet, I heard it sigh."_ – **Oracle Thyssen Mal**

A **mausoleum that predates recorded time**, carved from a single piece of **black stone**, its surface inscribed with **names that belong to no one.**

Though it has never been opened, **those who sleep near it dream the same dream**—a vision of themselves **lying upon its altar**, their own names **etched into the walls**, as if they had been buried there centuries ago.

---

### **27. The Veil Between** _(Unknown – The Labyrinth That Is Not There)_

_"No doors. No walls. No halls. Just the feeling that if I take one step further, I will never return."_ – **Delver Teyrin Vol**

Unlike other Labyrinths, **this one has never been seen**, only **felt**—a presence at the edge of reality, a **threshold that does not exist**.

Some Delvers have **reported walking into it by accident**, stepping through a doorway, a cave, a shadow—only to find themselves **somewhere else**, surrounded by **a vast, infinite white void**.

They always return **an instant later**, but with **days or even years missing from their memories**.

And **sometimes, they return knowing things they should not.**

---

### **28. The Wraithspire** _(Titan Highlands – The Tower of Forgotten Names)_

_"No one built it. No one remembers it. And yet, it waits."_ – **Dwarven Cartographer’s Notes, 5E 114**

A **black tower**, perched atop a **mountain with no recorded name**, appearing and disappearing **from maps without explanation**.

Those who try to approach **never reach it**—they climb the mountain, only to find themselves **somewhere else entirely**, their footprints erased from the snow.

But each year, on **the longest night**, lights flicker from its highest chamber.

As if someone inside **still remembers.**

---

### **29. The Silent Chapel** _(Umbral Dominion – The Church Without Prayer)_

_"No gods ever listened here. Only something else."_ – **Inquisitor Jeral Voln**

A **church that has no records of ever being built**, its pews untouched, its altar **unmarked by any holy symbol**.

No voices can be heard inside. **No matter how loud one speaks, no sound leaves their lips.**

Yet those who stand in the silence **feel something stir behind them**—a **presence unseen**, waiting, listening.

If it has a god, it is **one that does not ask for worship**.

Only **obedience**.

---

### **30. The Rotting Spire** _(Whispering Wilds – The Tower That Should Have Fallen)_

_"Wood decays. Stone crumbles. This place… persists."_ – **Scholar Evren Yalis**

A **wooden tower**, standing in the heart of the **Whispering Wilds**, its beams **warped and blackened with rot**, its floors so thin that **no structure should be able to hold weight.**

And yet, it does not collapse.

It is **older than the trees**, older than the Wilds themselves. The door is always **unlocked**, yet no one has dared to step inside.

Because something **walks within it**.

And it is **not alone**.

---

### **31. The Devouring Garden** _(Sunfire Expanse – The Bloom That Hungers)_

_"Flowers should not have teeth."_ – **Gardener’s Remains, recovered near the entrance**

A **field of flowers**, blooming in the middle of the **Sunfire Expanse**, where no life should thrive. Their colors shift with the seasons, their scent is said to be **irresistible**, drawing wanderers deeper within.

But the roots run **too deep**. The soil is **too red**.

And some nights, the flowers **breathe.**
