### **"The Sunken Necropolis"**

_"The dead do not stay dead in Ysaros."_  
— **Sailor’s Superstition**

Beneath the **Tempest Sea**, where the waves crash upon the remains of the long-ruined **Isle of Ysaros**, there is a place where the ocean refuses to reclaim its own. A city of **tomb-lined streets, crumbling spires, and half-sunken corridors**, swallowed by the tide but never truly lost.

This is the **Drowned Catacombs**—the **Sunken Necropolis**, where the dead were once laid to rest but have since **forgotten how to stay buried**.

Here, the water is **always still**. The air is **thick with whispers**, even though **there is no air to breathe**. And the deeper one descends, the stronger the feeling that **something ancient is still walking these halls**, waiting for **the living to join them.**

---

### **🌊 A City That Will Not Sink**

Unlike other ruins lost to the sea, the **Drowned Catacombs** have not been **reclaimed by time or tide**. The **stonework remains eerily intact**, its carvings **preserved as though untouched by water**, and the **tombs remain sealed—though not always from the outside.**

Those who have ventured inside report:

- **Chambers lined with stone coffins**, yet some rest **half-open**, waterlogged hands reaching from within.
- **Corridors that loop endlessly**, always leading back to where one started—until the walls begin to **change**.
- **Figures walking the flooded streets**, their faces obscured beneath veils of drifting seaweed, moving **as if performing rituals from long-dead faiths**.
- **Candlelight flickering beneath the water**, where no flame should burn.

There is **no current, no tide, no motion** in the depths of the Catacombs.

Yet sometimes, the water **moves on its own**.

---

### **⚰️ The Curse of Ysaros**

The **Drowned Catacombs** were once part of **the grand capital of Ysaros**, an empire that fell **overnight**, its people vanishing into legend.

Some say they were **punished by the gods**, their city **cast into the sea** for a sin long forgotten. Others believe they **chose to go below**, seeking **a different kind of eternity**, one where **death itself could not claim them**.

What is certain is that **no records of Ysaros remain**, save for the ruins themselves—**and the drowned who still walk within them.**

There is a tale among sailors, one whispered only in the **darkest of nights**:

_"The people of Ysaros did not drown."_  
_"They are still breathing."_  
_"And they are still waiting."_

---

### **🕯️ The Deepest Vault**

At the lowest point of the Catacombs, past the flooded tunnels and shifting corridors, there is a **vault sealed in gold**, its doors covered in inscriptions from **a language that does not exist anywhere else in the world**.

Each line hums with **a quiet resonance**, a song that **only the drowned can hear**.

No Delver has ever **breached the vault**.

No Delver has ever **returned from trying**.

But sometimes, when a ship drifts too close to the ruins, its crew will hear a sound rising from the depths—

A chorus of voices, humming in **perfect unison**, calling for someone to **open the door.**

---

### **📜 Final Observations: The Dead Remember**

The **Drowned Catacombs** do not threaten with **monsters or traps**.

They do not **consume** or **deceive**.

They simply **offer a choice**—

To step into the water.

To follow the voices.

To **become part of what waits below.**
