### **"The Maze That Remembers"**

_"The trees know your name. The flowers remember your sorrow. And the vines… the vines never forget."_  
— Inscription at the entrance

The Hollow Garden is not a labyrinth of stone, nor a fortress lost to time, but a living maze—an eternal grove, where nature does not follow the rules of the world outside.

Its paths are lined with twisted trees, their branches arching overhead, forming a canopy that blocks out the sky, letting through only a pale, ghostly light. The deeper one walks, the more the vines tighten, the paths shift, and the very air becomes thick with the scent of flowers that bloom only in memory.

The Hollow Garden does not trap the body.

It holds the soul.

---

### **🌿 The Garden That Knows You**

The Hollow Garden is said to be sentient, responding to those who walk its paths, shaping itself to mirror their past, their regrets, their buried thoughts.

Delvers have reported:

- Trees that whisper their own names, in voices that sound eerily like their own.
- Petals that turn to ash in their hands, only to regrow the moment they look away.
- Statues of people they once knew, half-covered in moss, their eyes turned toward the entrance as if waiting to be spoken to.
- Paths that lead back to where they started, but with subtle changes—footprints missing, scars erased, small details of the past rewritten as if the Garden is trying to correct something.

And in the deepest parts of the Hollow Garden, where the flowers bloom brightest, Delvers have seen phantoms walking the paths—figures they recognize, but who should be long dead.

Some claim they are illusions.

Others fear they are echoes of those who entered… and never left.

---

### **❀ The Blooming Cycle**

Unlike other Labyrinths, the Hollow Garden **changes with the seasons**, though no time passes for those inside. Each cycle brings **a different danger**, a different challenge:

- **The Season of Blossoms** – The safest time to enter. The air is filled with the scent of unfamiliar flowers, and memories manifest as harmless phantoms.
- **The Season of Thorns** – The vines tighten. The statues begin to move when unobserved. Footsteps echo that do not belong to you.
- **The Season of Wilt** – The flowers begin to whisper forgotten things. Delvers report feeling weaker, as if something in the air is drawing them deeper against their will.
- **The Season of Rot** – No Delver has ever entered during this time and returned. The statues are gone. The paths no longer lead back. The Garden is awake.

---

### **🗝️ The Gate of Names**

At the heart of the Hollow Garden lies a single, massive gate, entwined with roots and thorns, its surface covered in hundreds of names. Some are worn with age, others fresh as if carved only yesterday.

Delvers who reach the gate feel compelled to find their own name among them.

And those who do…

Are never seen again.

Some say the gate leads to the true heart of the Labyrinth, a place where memories can be rewritten, pasts reshaped, lives undone and remade in bloom and decay.

Others believe it is not a door at all.

But a tombstone.

---

### **📜 Final Observations: The Garden Does Not Forget**

The Hollow Garden does not threaten with traps or monsters.

It does not need to.

It remembers.

Every footstep. Every thought. Every regret.

And when you leave, it may not let you take everything with you.

Some return with memories altered, their pasts changed in ways too subtle to notice at first. A face missing from a childhood recollection. A wound that was never earned. A dream that lingers too long, whispering of paths they never walked—but should have.

And some return to find their own names missing from the world outside.

As if the Hollow Garden had claimed them after all.
