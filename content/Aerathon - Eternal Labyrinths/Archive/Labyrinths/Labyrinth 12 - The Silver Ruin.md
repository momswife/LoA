### **"The Frozen Metropolis"**

_"It’s older than the mountains. Older than the world we know. And yet, the lights are still on."_  
— **Dwarven Excavation Report, 5E 913**

Deep within the **Frostlands**, where the cold has held dominion for **millennia**, lies a city **that should not exist**. The **Silver Ruin** is **not a dungeon, nor a temple, nor a forgotten crypt**—it is **a metropolis of impossible scale**, buried beneath centuries of ice, its towers frozen mid-collapse, its streets still paved with metal that does not tarnish.

No one built it.

No records speak of its existence.

And yet, it has been waiting, untouched, as if the world simply **forgot** it was ever there.

---

### **🏙️ A City Without Time**

Unlike the shattered ruins of lost civilizations, the **Silver Ruin** has not **crumbled, decayed, or been consumed by time**. Instead, it is **perfectly preserved**, as though it was frozen **in an instant**, locked in an unchanging state while the rest of the world moved on.

Explorers who enter have reported:

- **Buildings of flawless silver**, their doors **sealed without visible mechanisms**, as if meant to remain closed **forever**.
- **Mirrors lining every corridor**, but the reflections within **move a fraction of a second too late**.
- **Statues of figures in long robes**, positioned **as if in mid-sentence**, their faces **uncannily human, yet unfamiliar**.
- **A soft, ceaseless hum beneath the ice**, like the slow, steady breathing of something that **has not yet awakened**.

The city **is not dead**.

It is **waiting**.

---

### **⚠️ The Frozen Anomaly**

The first recorded discovery of the **Silver Ruin** occurred in **5E 913**, when a dwarven mining expedition struck a **barrier of unknown metal** beneath the Frostlands. The moment they breached the ice, every **torch, lantern, and spell of fire dimmed**, and the temperature in their camp **plunged to impossible lows**.

Within hours, **three members of the team vanished**.

They were found the next morning—**standing perfectly still in the city’s streets, covered in frost, their eyes open but sightless.**

They had no pulse.

Yet their breath still fogged in the air.

The surviving members abandoned the site, but even as they fled, they swore they could see **figures moving in the ice**, walking beneath the surface, watching.

---

### **❄️ The Lights That Should Not Be**

Unlike other Labyrinths, which are **dark, crumbling places of ruin**, the **Silver Ruin is still illuminated**.

At **precisely midnight**, lights flicker on in the highest towers, casting a glow that **should be impossible in a city abandoned for untold millennia**. Those who have gazed upon it from a distance report seeing **shadows moving through the windows**, figures walking in the frozen streets.

No one knows who—or what—they are.

But each time an explorer disappears, **a new shadow appears in the city**.

---

### **🛑 The Silent Quarter and the Whispering Hall**

At the heart of the ruin, there is a **district known as the Silent Quarter**, where even the wind **does not stir**. It is **always colder here**, and those who pass through hear **no footsteps but their own.**

At its center lies **the Whispering Hall**, a great domed structure, its ceiling a perfect sphere of mirrored glass.

Those who step inside hear **voices speaking in a language no one understands**, yet **feels familiar, as if spoken just beyond the edge of memory**.

Some say it is **a council chamber**, a place where something **once ruled this city**.

Others believe it is **a mausoleum**, a tomb where **the rulers of a forgotten age still linger, frozen but not gone**.

No one has dared remain long enough to find out.

---

### **📜 Final Observations: A City That Remembers**

The **Silver Ruin** is not simply **a lost metropolis**.

It is **preserved memory**—a place frozen in the moment of its vanishing, untouched by time, locked in **a waiting state**.

And something within it still stirs.

The lights still turn on.

The statues still listen.

The city **has not forgotten itself.**

And it has **not forgotten us.**
