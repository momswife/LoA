### **"The Labyrinth That Hungers"**

_"The tide was high, the sky was clear, and then the ocean opened its mouth."_  
— **Captain Verin Oathwave, sole survivor of the Maiden’s Descent**

The **Sunken Maw** is not just a Labyrinth. It is a **force of nature**, a **living hunger beneath the waves**, an abyss where the sea itself **collapses into something older, something deeper, something that should not exist.**

Unlike the **fortresses, dungeons, and ruins** that define the other Eternal Labyrinths, the **Maw has no walls, no doors, no visible entrance at all.** It **drags its victims to it**—not through choice, not through discovery, but by **pulling entire ships, entire fleets, entire civilizations into its depths.**

No Delver has ever **walked through its gates.**

Because the **only way to enter is to drown.**

---

### **🌊 The Sunken Maw: An Ocean Without End**

First recorded in **4E 189**, the Sunken Maw is located somewhere **within the Tempest Sea**, its position **constantly shifting, never fully charted.**

- **Ships that sail too close vanish.**
- **Sailors who drown near its waters are never seen again.**
- **Storms appear and disappear without warning, their winds whispering in languages long forgotten.**

To the untrained eye, the Maw is **invisible**—just another patch of ocean, vast and empty. But for those who **know what to look for**, the signs are clear:

- The water above it is **too still, too dark, too quiet.**
- The horizon is **too far, stretching beyond what the eye should see.**
- The sky directly above it **lacks stars, as if something beneath is swallowing their light.**

And then, without warning, the **sea falls away.**

A whirlpool, **miles wide**, spirals into the abyss. The waves collapse. The sky twists. The stars vanish.

And whatever is caught within it **never surfaces again.**

---

### **🚢 The Great Vanishings and the Cities Lost to the Maw**

While many Labyrinths claim **countless Delver lives**, the Sunken Maw **has devoured entire civilizations.**

- In **4E 201**, the **Stormcall Empire**—a nation of seafarers, renowned for their mastery of the ocean—**vanished overnight**. Over **twelve coastal cities**, hundreds of warships, and an entire ruling dynasty were **dragged beneath the waves**, leaving behind only **ruins along the shores**.
- In **5E 512**, the Halvaskan navy sent **a fleet of 50 ships to chart the Maw**. Only **one man returned**—**Captain Verin Oathwave**, who washed ashore two months later, **blind and half-mad, whispering of the "sunken halls of the drowned."**
- **Even now**, sailors speak of **phantom vessels**, ancient warships that **rise from the waves**, their decks lined with **crewmen who do not breathe, their eyes black with the void of the deep.**

It is believed that the Maw is **not simply an abyss**, but a **gateway to something beyond the sea itself**—a place where the drowned **do not die**, but instead become **part of the hunger.**

---

### **🛑 The First (and Last) Delving Expedition**

In **6E 732**, a team of **Delvers, scholars, and deep-sea explorers** attempted to **descend into the Maw** using enchanted diving vessels, reinforced with arcane wards. The expedition, funded by the **Magocratic Court of Halvask**, was led by:

- **Admiral Elias Corvan**, a **legendary naval commander**, known for surviving the **Leviathan Wars.**
- **High Thaumaturge Velyss Eiren,** an **oceanic mage**, capable of **manipulating water and bending currents.**
- **Arix Dawncaster,** an artificer who designed the **"Stormcaller Submersibles,"** vessels specifically built to **withstand the Maw’s pull.**
- **Seeker Ralyn Vaelor,** a priestess of **Veyna, the Pale Watcher**, sent to study the **spiritual significance of the Maw.**

Their mission: **To enter the Maw, to explore its depths, to return with proof of what lay beneath.**

They never returned.

But **their voices did.**

For weeks after their descent, sailors near the Maw reported hearing **distorted echoes through the water**, voices **screaming, singing, whispering in tongues that no longer belonged to the living.**

One ship, the **Starward Horizon**, recovered **a fragment of a message**—a desperate transmission from the depths, transmitted through an arcane mirror before the connection **was lost forever.**

It contained only seven words:

_"We are below. The city is waking."_

### **🕳️ Theories, Fears, and the Drowned City**

Most scholars believe that the **Maw is not merely a void, but a sunken kingdom—a city swallowed by the sea long before history began.** Some say it was the **capital of the Stormcall Empire**, while others believe it is **older, belonging to an age when the gods still walked the world.**

But all agree on one thing:

Whatever lies at the bottom is **still alive.**

The few relics recovered from shipwrecks near the Maw **bear markings unknown to any recorded civilization.** Some resemble **prayers to a nameless god**, others **etched warnings of something sleeping beneath the waves.**

A recent discovery has **unnerved even the greatest scholars of Halvask**—a **monolithic structure**, deep beneath the ocean floor, **a tower of obsidian and coral**, rising from the abyss.

At its peak is **a single unbroken window, blacker than the night sky.**

And some sailors claim that, when the moon is full, **something watches from inside.**

---

### **📜 Final Observations: The Maw Still Calls**

The **Sunken Maw** cannot be explored. It **cannot be fought, cannot be conquered, cannot be reasoned with.** It is **not a place of trials**, nor **a dungeon of puzzles**.

It is **a hunger. A wound in the ocean. A mouth that has never closed.**

Even now, its whispers **linger in the tides**.

And one day, **something will rise from its depths.**

When that day comes, **the sea itself may no longer be enough to contain it.**
