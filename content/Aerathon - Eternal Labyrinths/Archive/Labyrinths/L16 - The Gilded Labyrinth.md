### **"The Temple of Endless Gold"**

_"The wealth of a thousand empires lies within. But tell me, Delver—how much of yourself are you willing to spend?"_  
— **High Vizier Zorahn al-Rahim**

Buried beneath the scorching sands of the Sunfire Expanse, hidden within dunes that shift like the tides, lies the Gilded Labyrinth—a temple to greed, temptation, and ruin.

It is said that no treasure hoard in all of Aerathon compares to the riches stored within its halls. Gold flows like water, gemstones line the very walls, and relics of fallen kings and lost dynasties sit untouched, waiting to be claimed.

And yet, few who enter ever leave with more than ashes in their hands.

For the Gilded Labyrinth does not simply hoard wealth.

It demands a price in return.

---

### **💰 A Labyrinth of Wealth and Ruin**

Unlike other Labyrinths, which are fortresses of the past or prisons of the forgotten, the Gilded Labyrinth is still active—a place of opulence without end, where the walls shimmer with arcane gold, and the very air is thick with the scent of rare perfumes and incense that never fades.

Yet the deeper one walks, the more the weight of gold presses down—not upon their body, but upon their very soul.

Delvers have reported:

- Gold appearing in their packs without explanation, yet growing heavier with every step.
- Jewels clinging to their skin, their facets shifting, reflecting their desires rather than their face.
- Gilded statues in reverent poses, yet when touched, the gold crumbles to reveal desiccated bodies beneath—their fingers still curled around treasures they refused to let go of.
- Doors that open only to those who offer something of equal value—but never reveal the cost until the bargain is struck.

Wealth flows freely here.

But nothing is ever truly free.

---

### **⚖️ The Curse of the Coinborn**

The Gilded Labyrinth does not simply tempt. It changes those who take from it.

Among the few who have escaped, there exists a legend of the Coinborn—Delvers who took too much, only to find themselves forever bound to what they claimed.

- Their blood runs thick like molten gold.
- Their skin hardens, becoming metallic, yet brittle at the joints.
- They become obsessed with wealth, hoarding it even as they waste away, unable to spend a single coin without screaming in agony.

Some say that if a Coinborn dies, their body does not rot.

It is simply melted down and returned to the Labyrinth.

---

### **🔑 The Chamber of Kings**

At the heart of the Gilded Labyrinth, beneath archways lined with crown-shaped glyphs, lies the Chamber of Kings—a vast golden vault said to contain every treasure ever lost to time.

Weapons of legendary heroes, the crowns of fallen rulers, scrolls of forbidden magic—all untouched, pristine, waiting.

And upon the throne at the center of it all sits a figure of solid gold, its features worn smooth by time, its empty hands resting upon the armrests as if awaiting an heir.

No Delver has ever reached this chamber.

Or if they have, they never left.

Some believe that to claim its riches is to become its next guardian, forever bound to the throne, watching over the wealth of a world that can never spend it.

Others say that the figure on the throne was the first to take from the Labyrinth—and paid the price in full.

---

### **📜 Final Observations: The Labyrinth That Takes as Much as It Gives**

The Gilded Labyrinth is not a place of battle, nor of puzzles, nor of trials.

It is a test of will, restraint, and greed.

And it offers one simple choice:

Take what you desire.

And see if you can bear the cost.
