### **7. The Labyrinth of the Faceless** _(Umbral Dominion – The Masked Nightmare)_

_"I cannot recall their faces. I cannot recall my own."_  
— Delver’s journal, recovered outside Nocthollow

The Labyrinth of the Faceless is a place of shifting identity, where those who enter slowly lose themselves—not to death, but to forgetting.

It is a maze of mirrored corridors, endless halls of featureless statues, and doors that lead back to where one started, but never as the same person.

No one has ever mapped its halls. No one has ever walked out unchanged.

Some who enter return maskless, voiceless, with faces that seem unfamiliar even to themselves.

Some return with nothing at all—no name, no past, no shadow to cast upon the ground.

And some never return at all, their names scratched from history as if they had never existed in the first place.

---

### **🪞 A Maze Without Identity**

Unlike other Labyrinths, the Labyrinth of the Faceless does not present monsters, traps, or guardians.

Its only threat is doubt, confusion, and the slow erosion of self.

Those who step inside report:

- Walls of shifting mirrors, where reflections change—showing faces that do not belong to them, or expressions they have never made.
- Statues of faceless figures, arranged as if caught mid-conversation, their heads tilting just slightly when unobserved.
- Voices whispering in their own tone, offering names that sound almost right, but not quite.
- Masks appearing on the floor, the walls, the ceiling—each marked with a name, none of which they recognize… until they put one on.

And once they wear the mask, they cannot remove it.

Because it is not on their face.

It has become their face.

---

### **⏳ The Vanishing of the Nocthollow Expedition**

The most infamous attempt to explore the Labyrinth occurred in 5E 909, when the Nocthollow Expedition, a team of twelve elite Delvers, entered the Labyrinth under the guidance of **Master Delver Alric Thorne.**

A week later, seven emerged, stumbling from the entrance, their expressions blank, their voices monotone, their movements eerily synchronized.

When asked what happened inside, they spoke in unison:

_"We are home."_

Then, without hesitation, they turned and walked back inside.

Their names were erased from records within the year.

And the remaining five?

No one knows.

But some nights, if you stand at the entrance, you can still hear footsteps just beyond the threshold.

Waiting.

---

### **🎭 The Masks That Remain**

The Labyrinth of the Faceless does not allow all who enter to leave.

But those who escape often return changed, carrying with them a mask—smooth, featureless, and impossibly cold to the touch.

Some say these masks are what remains of those who were lost, a piece of them still trapped within the Labyrinth, waiting to be worn again.

And on rare occasions, those who find one cannot resist putting it on.

They claim it feels right.

That it belongs.

And when they finally remove it…

Their own face is gone.

---

### **📜 Final Observations: The Price of a Name**

The Labyrinth of the Faceless does not kill.

It does not wound.

It simply takes.

A face. A voice. A name.

Until nothing remains at all.
