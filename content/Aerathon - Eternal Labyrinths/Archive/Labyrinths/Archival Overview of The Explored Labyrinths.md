### **The Doors of the Unknown: Collected Reports on the Eternal Labyrinths**

For centuries, the Eternal Labyrinths have stood as monuments of mystery, their blackened gates and shifting corridors defying all natural law. To some, they are divine trials, forged by gods long forgotten. To others, they are remnants of an ancient civilization, their purpose long lost to time.**

What is known is this: no two Labyrinths are alike. Each one bears its own laws, its own dangers, and its own rewards. Some have been fully explored, mapped, and conquered, their secrets laid bare for scholars and kings to study. But others remain an enigma, their depths uncharted, their trials still unwritten.

Here follows the known records of the Labyrinths of Aerathon, both those that have been survived—and those that have not.

---

## **The 20 Known Labyrinths**

(_The following excerpts are collected from Delver memoirs, royal decrees, and recovered transcripts._)

### **[[Labyrinth 1 - The Argent Spire | The Argent Spire]]** _(Halvask – First Discovered)_

_"It was not built. It was revealed."_ – Archivist Adrastia, 3E 15

The first of the Eternal Labyrinths, the Argent Spire lies deep beneath Halvask, its silver halls seemingly untouched by time. Unlike later Labyrinths, it has no monsters, no deadly traps—only winding corridors of mirrors, each reflecting something that should not be there. Those who enter find themselves face to face with their own past, their own failures, their own unspoken truths.

Even today, scholars remain unsure if it was truly conquered—or if those who emerged were the same as the ones who entered.

---

### **[[Labyrinth 2 - Of Rust and Ruin | Of Rust and Ruin]]**  _(Titan Highlands – Known for its Endless War)_

_"You do not leave it. You survive it. And when you die, you are born into it again."_ – Last words of Delver Ralvas Thorne

The Labyrinth of Rust and Ruin is a battlefield without end. Those who enter find themselves trapped within an eternal war, forced to take up arms for warring factions who do not remember why they fight. Death is not an escape—the fallen simply wake anew, their past lives nothing more than fading echoes.

To this day, none have reached the end.

---

### **[[Labyrinth 3 - The Sunken Maw | The Sunken Maw]]** _(Stormcall Isles – Beneath the Tempest Sea)_

_"The tide was high, the sky was clear, and then the ocean opened its mouth."_ – Captain Verin Oathwave, sole survivor

The Sunken Maw does not have walls or halls—it is an abyss, a chasm beneath the Tempest Sea, where ships do not sink, but descend. Those who have dared the depths speak of sunken vessels still crewed by the drowned, of merfolk with hollow eyes, of voices that call to sailors in their own voices.

Its depths have never been fully explored, and its floor has never been found.

---

### **[[Labyrinth 4 - The Clockwork Tomb | The Clockwork Tomb]]** _(Halvask – A City of Metal and Time)_

_"Time does not move forward here. It moves inward."_ – Master Chronomancer Ilythia

A labyrinth of ticking gears and endless halls, where the laws of time are broken and rewoven. Some who enter return decades older, others return before they have even left. Those who delve too deeply are said to become one with the mechanism itself, their bodies replaced with metal and mind replaced with purpose.

---

### **[[Labyrinth 5 - The Twilight Bastion | The Twilight Bastion]]** _(Veilshroud Expanse – The Throne of Shadows)_

_"We went in as five. We came out as legends."_ – Dain Veldros, Founder of the Twilight Vow

The Twilight Bastion is a fortress lost to time, a place where the sky never brightens, where the halls of kings stand empty, and where a throne waits for a ruler who will never come.

Its gates have always been open, yet none who entered had ever claimed it—until the Twilight Vow, the first Delver party to prove that the Labyrinths were not meant for lone warriors, but for those who stood together.

Inside, they fought sentinels of shadow, deciphered forgotten glyphs, and uncovered the last words of a kingdom that erased itself from history.

When they reached the throne room, they did not fight for it.

They took it together.

---

### **[[Labyrinth 6 - The Ashen Labyrinth | The Ashen Labyrinth]]**  _(Sunfire Expanse – The Smoldering Trial)_

_"The fire does not burn flesh. It burns truth. And those who have none are reduced to cinders."_ – High Priest Rashan Sunblade

A twisting inferno, where flames do not consume the body, but the soul. Those who pass through its halls must face not monsters, but themselves, stripped of illusion, deception, and falsehoods.

Some emerge enlightened. Others emerge hollow, their very identities burned away.

---

### **[[Labyrinth 7 - Labyrinth of The Faceless | Labyrinth of The Faceless]]**  _(Umbral Dominion – The Masked Nightmare)_

_"I cannot recall their faces. I cannot recall my own."_ – Delver’s journal, recovered outside Nocthollow

A place of shifting identities, where those who enter slowly forget their own names, their own voices, their own faces. Many return maskless, voiceless, their reflections nothing more than blank slates.

Some never return at all, their names scratched from history as if they had never existed.

---
### **[[Labyrinth 8 - The Black Star Descent | The Black Star Descent]]**  _(Stormcall Isles – The Abyss That Watches)_

_"We fell into the darkness together. We climbed back out as legends."_ – Seraphine Vex, Captain of the Black Star Company

Unlike the twisting corridors and shifting halls of other Labyrinths, the Black Star Descent is a void, a wound in the world itself, where the sea collapses into endless shadow.

Sailors who stray too close never return.

Arrows fired into its depths never land.

Ships have been swallowed whole, their crews seen walking upon the waves in spectral silence long after they were lost.

Only one group has ever entered willingly—the Black Star Company—and returned alive.

---

### **[[Labyrinth 9 - The Radiant Crucible | The Radiant Crucible]]** _(Sunfire Expanse – The Trial of Ascension)_

_"The fire does not burn flesh. It burns weakness. And those who have none emerge reborn."_ – Vaelrik Dawnpiercer, the First Labyrinth King

The Radiant Crucible is not a ruin to explore, nor a dungeon to conquer—it is a trial, a test of the soul, a path that only the worthy may walk.

It is a bridge of molten gold, suspended over an ocean of fire, where every step forward strips away illusion, doubt, and fear. Those who falter are consumed, their ashes becoming part of the Crucible itself.

Only one has ever reached the end—Vaelrik Dawnpiercer, the First Labyrinth King, who stepped through the flames and emerged remade, his body wreathed in golden scars, his eyes burning with the fire of ascension.

---

### **[[Labyrinth 10 - Hollow God's Sepulcher | Hollow God's Sephulcher]]** _(Whispering Wilds – The Buried Deity)_

_"The god beneath the roots is not dead. It is waiting."_ – Final words of Seeress Valna Greenbloom

A vast underground tomb, sealed for thousands of years. When disturbed, the forest above withered overnight, and those who entered returned half-mad, their veins pulsing with something black and alive.

At its heart, a chamber of stone and bone, containing a massive, unmoving figure, wrapped in vines that pulse like arteries.

Some believe the Hollow God was never meant to be found.

Others believe it is not sleeping—but listening.

---

### **[[Labyrinth 11 - The Vault of Eternity | The Vault of Eternity]]** _(Titan Highlands – The Forge of Gods)_

_"The world does not craft legends. Legends craft the world. And all things of power must come from somewhere."_ – Master Artificer Kellis Vaelor

The Vault of Eternity is not a ruin, nor a dungeon, nor a tomb—it is a forge, a crucible where artifacts of legend are shaped and reforged.

Every century, during the Silver Eclipse, its gates open once more, revealing a labyrinth of floating anvils, halls of golden metal, and runes that whisper of weapons not yet wielded.

Many of the greatest relics in history were born here—weapons that could cut through time, armors that could withstand the divine, tomes that could rewrite the world itself.

But all power has a cost.

And those who take from the Vault do not die normal deaths.

One day, the First Forge will be opened.

And when it does, the world will tremble.

---

### **[[Labyrinth 12 - The Silver Ruin | The Silver Ruin]]** _(Frostlands – The Frozen Metropolis)_

_"It’s older than the mountains. Older than the world we know."_ – Dwarven Excavation Report, 5E 913

A city of metal and ice, buried beneath the Everfrost Expanse for millennia. Its streets are empty, its towers silent, and its halls lined with statues that do not decay.

No bodies have ever been found.

Only footprints that start in the snow and lead into the dark.

---

### **[[Labyrinth 13 - The Howling Maw | The Howling Maw]]** _(Titan Highlands – The Battlefield of the Damned)_

_"You do not leave it. You survive it. And when you die, you are born into it again."_ – Last words of Delver Ralvas Thorne

The Labyrinth of Rust and Ruin is not a place of puzzles, nor a dungeon of traps, nor a prison of shifting illusions. It is a war without end—a battlefield frozen in time, locked in an eternal cycle of conflict, where the fallen never truly die and victory does not exist.

- Legions of rusted soldiers, bearing banners of forgotten empires, march endlessly through the wastelands.
- Ghostly war horns sound in the distance, calling the lost to battle.
- Steel-clad knights, their armor fusing to their very bones, fight duels that have no end.

Time does not move forward in the Howling Maw.

It simply repeats.

And those who enter do not leave.

They become part of the war.

---

### **[[Labyrinth 14 - The Hollow Garden | The Hollow Garden]]** _(Veilshroud Expanse – The Maze That Remembers)_

_"The trees know your name. The flowers remember your sorrow. And the vines… the vines never forget."_ – Inscription at the entrance

The Hollow Garden is a living maze, a place where the paths shift and grow, guided not by stone, but by roots, petals, and thorns. It is said that the deeper one travels, the more the Garden begins to mirror their own thoughts, memories, and regrets.

The vines whisper secrets only you should know.

The statues take the shape of those you have lost.

The deeper you go, the less certain you become that you were ever meant to leave.

Some return, speaking of lovers long dead, of choices they never made, of faces they longed to forget but the Garden refused to release.

---

### **[[Labyrinth 15 - The Drowned Catacombs | The Drowned Catacombs]]** _(Tempest Sea – The Sunken Necropolis)_

_"The dead do not stay dead in Ysaros."_ – Sailor’s Superstition

A labyrinth of half-submerged tunnels, hidden beneath the ruined Isle of Ysaros. The walls are lined with endless tombs, yet the bodies inside still move.

Some ships report seeing figures in sodden robes, standing atop the waves, whispering in languages long forgotten.

They are waiting for something.

Or someone.

---

### **[[Labyrinth 16 - The Gilded Labyrinth | The Gilded Labyrinth]]** _(Sunfire Expanse – The Temple of Endless Gold)_

_"The wealth of a thousand empires lies within. But tell me, Delver—how much of yourself are you willing to spend?"_ – High Vizier Zorahn al-Rahim

Buried beneath the golden dunes of the Sunfire Expanse, the Gilded Labyrinth is a temple of wealth, temptation, and ruin. Its halls glisten with untouched treasure, its walls are carved with the names of those who succumbed to greed, and its doors open only to those who believe they deserve fortune.

But nothing within is freely given.

The deeper one ventures, the heavier the weight of their own riches becomes. Those who take too much find themselves slowed, shackled, drowning in their own gold, their reflections in the polished floors showing something hollow behind their eyes.

At the heart of the Labyrinth stands the Eternal Scales, a balance upon which all must place their wealth, their past, or their soul.

---

### **[[Labyrinth 17 - The Black Vein | The Black Vein]]** _(Titan Highlands – The Mountain That Breathes)_

_"Time does not move forward here. It moves inward."_ – Master Chronomancer Ilythia

Discovered by dwarven miners, the Black Vein is a labyrinth of living stone, a structure that should not exist, buried beneath Mount Karadris.

When struck by a pickaxe, the rock did not crumble—it bled.

As miners descended deeper, they heard a pulse, rhythmic and slow, beating from beneath the mountain itself.

Those who have entered speak of hallways that breathe, of whispers in the stone, of figures emerging from the walls—shaped like the missing, speaking with their voices, but not truly them.

It is believed that something beneath the mountain is alive.

And if it wakes, the mountain itself may no longer be enough to contain it.

---

### **[[Labyrinth 18 - The Forsaken Atelier | The Forsaken Atelier]]**  _(Halvask – The Workshop of the Forgotten)_

_"They built the perfect spell. And then it devoured them."_ – Headmaster Oryn Velsh, 4E 729

Once a great workshop of magical innovation, the Forsaken Atelier was abandoned after an experiment went horribly wrong.

Those who enter see floating pages, writing themselves in real-time, diagrams of spells too complex for mortal minds, and, in the deepest chamber, a single locked vault.

The key was lost when its creators were erased from history.

Or worse—they are still inside, but no longer human.

---

### **[[Labyrinth 19 - The Shattered Monastery | The Shattered Monastery]]** _(Titan Highlands – The Trial of the Ascended)_

_"The monks who walked these halls sought ascension. They found something else."_ – Scholar Fendris Vaelor

A floating monastery, split into hundreds of suspended stone platforms, connected by bridges that shift on their own.

Inside, the walls are lined with murals, depicting monks ascending into light—only to return as something hollow.

The final chamber holds a pedestal, upon which rests a single bowl of water.

Those who drink are never seen again.

---

### **[[Labyrinth 20 - The Crimson Labyrinth | The Crimson Labyrinth]]**  _(Unstable – The Labyrinth That Grows)_

_"It is not stone. It is not metal. The walls are warm beneath your hands, and when you cut them—oh gods, when you cut them—it screams."_ – Delver Mareth Voss, last survivor of the Bloodmarked Expedition

The Crimson Labyrinth is not a ruin, nor a temple, nor a dungeon. It is a living thing, a structure of flesh and sinew, its walls pulsing with unnatural life, its veins threading through tunnels that shift, contract, and breathe.

It is growing.

Those who enter do not die—they become something else. Some return with veins pulsing red beneath their skin, their eyes blackened, their voices hoarse from screams they refuse to explain.

But most do not return at all.

The Crimson Labyrinth does not stay in one place.

It emerges wherever the land is weakest, gorging itself, and then vanishing again.

And each time it returns, it is larger than before.

---

Thus concludes the **20 known Eternal Labyrinths** of Aerathon.

*🔖 Archivist’s Note: Though these records are the most complete known accounts of the Eternal Labyrinths, we must acknowledge that they are, at best, fragments of a larger truth. The Labyrinths are not fixed structures—they shift, they change, and some say they respond. Even the conquered Labyrinths are not truly silent; some scholars believe they are merely dormant, waiting for a reason to wake once more.*

*Of particular concern are the 12 unknown Labyrinths, especially those that have appeared within the last century. Their presence suggests that the Labyrinths are not relics of the past, but an evolving force—one that is still in motion.*

*And now, we face something unprecedented. The newest Labyrinth, the one that moves, is unlike any before it. It does not stay in one place. It does not obey the known patterns. It is the first to be described as having a voice.*

*Is it calling to us? Warning us? Or are we simply hearing it for the first time?*

*We do not yet know. But we must be prepared for the possibility that, after all these centuries, the Labyrinths have not been waiting for us to understand them—only for the right moment to speak.*

*We must listen. And we must hope we are ready for what they have to say.***
