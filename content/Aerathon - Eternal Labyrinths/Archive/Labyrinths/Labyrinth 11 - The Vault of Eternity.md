### **"The Labyrinth That Forges the Impossible"**

_"The world does not craft legends. Legends craft the world. And all things of power must come from somewhere."_  
— **Master Artificer Kellis Vaelor, on the Vault of Eternity**

Among the **Eternal Labyrinths**, there is one whose name is whispered not in **fear**, but in **reverence**. A place where **weapons of legend, tomes of untamed magic, and relics of unimaginable power** were born.

The **Vault of Eternity.**

Unlike other Labyrinths, the **Vault does not seek to consume, nor deceive, nor slaughter.** It offers something far more dangerous: **potential.**

It is a forge of **creation**. A crucible of **possibilities**. A sanctum where **artifacts are not merely found—but shaped, tempered, and made eternal.**

Some say it is **the workshop of forgotten gods**. Others believe it is **a vault of the first magi**, a place where **the laws of reality bend to the will of the one strong enough to claim them.**

What is certain is this: **No Delver who has entered has ever left empty-handed.**

But the cost of such power?

That is a different question entirely.

---

### **🔷 The Vault of Eternity: A Workshop of the Divine**

Unlike other Labyrinths, which are **ruins, fortresses, or realms of horror**, the **Vault of Eternity is a place of impossible wonder**.

Located **beneath the shattered peak of Mount Y’Velar**, its entrance appears only **once every century**, revealed when the **Silver Eclipse** casts its light upon the mountain. When the eclipse fades, so too does the Vault’s entrance, disappearing from the world for another hundred years.

Those who have glimpsed inside describe:

- **Great halls of golden metal**, stretching into infinity, their surfaces engraved with **blueprints of weapons and artifacts never before seen.**
- **Floating anvils**, each carrying the shattered remnants of items that **once shaped the fate of empires.**
- **Ethereal smiths**, figures of light and shadow, moving like ghosts, reforging **weapons that have yet to be wielded, armor that has yet to be worn, spells that have yet to be cast.**
- **A massive vault door**, locked by **seals of celestial and abyssal script**, behind which it is said lies **the First Forge—the place where all magic began.**

No one has ever **opened the door.**

No one knows what lies beyond.

---

### **🛠️ The Artifacts of the Vault: Power Beyond Mortal Hands**

The **greatest artifacts in history** have come from the Vault. Some were **claimed**, some were **forged anew**, and some were **taken at a price no one dares to name.**

Among them:

- **Starforged Aegis** – A **shield crafted from a fallen star**, able to **reflect any spell cast against its wielder.**
- **Veyna’s Grimoire** – A tome said to contain **spells that have never been spoken aloud**, capable of rewriting the nature of reality itself.
- **The Crown of Ten Thousand Echoes** – A helm said to allow its wearer to **see all possible futures—but at the cost of never knowing which one is real.**
- **Sundawn, the Piercing Flame** – The **legendary glaive of Vaelrik Dawnpiercer**, reforged from the Vault’s heart, capable of **cutting through illusions, lies, and fate itself.**

And then, there are the **weapons that were never recovered.**

- **The Wraithblade of Silence** – A dagger forged from **pure shadow**, said to be able to **kill an immortal.**
- **The First Key** – A relic theorized to **unlock the sealed door within the Vault.** If it exists, its location is **unknown.**

Each artifact is **more than just a weapon, more than just an object**—it is a **piece of the Vault itself, carrying with it whispers of its origin, a sliver of the endless creation that lies within.**

But **every item taken from the Vault comes with a cost.**

Some call it **a curse**.

Others call it **a promise.**

Whatever it is, none who wield these relics **die a quiet death.**

---

### **⚖️ The Teams Who Have Entered**

Unlike most Labyrinths, which claim **countless lives**, the Vault does not **slaughter Delvers outright**.

It does not **trap them**.

It does not **deceive them**.

It simply **gives them a choice.**

Among the greatest Delver parties to enter and return:

#### **🔥 The Argent Blades (4E 712)**

- Led by **Highlord Veyric Starfall**, the Blades entered seeking **a weapon to end the Twilight Wars**.
- They returned **with the Silverbrand**, a blade said to have been **forged before the first king was crowned**.
- **Two members of their party never spoke again**, their eyes burned with silver light, their hands forever trembling.

#### **⚔️ The Exalted Seven (5E 214)**

- **A legendary band of warrior-mages**, seeking **knowledge lost to time.**
- They emerged with **The Crown of Ten Thousand Echoes**, but their leader, **Archmage Valeria Moraine**, **refused to wear it, claiming it had already shown her too much.**
- Three members disappeared within the year, vanishing into **golden mist**, never to be seen again.

#### **🌑 The Silent Choir (6E 522)**

- Entered with **twelve members**.
- Returned with **six.**
- Brought back **no weapons, no armor, no tomes**—only a **single key, black as night, pulsing like a heartbeat.**
- **No one knows what door it opens.**

### **📜 Final Observations: The Vault Awaits**

The **Vault of Eternity** does not call for **the strong**.

It does not call for **the wise**.

It calls for **those who seek to shape the world.**

It is **a forge, a promise, a temptation.**

It does not **punish greed**, nor **reward virtue**—it simply **offers power**, and allows the **wielder to decide what to do with it.**

But every blade must be **sharpened.**

Every shield must be **tested.**

And **nothing** that comes from the Vault is **without consequence.**

One day, the **First Forge** will be opened.

One day, the **greatest weapon ever made** will be taken from its depths.

And when that day comes, **the world will tremble.**
