### **"The Labyrinth That Swallowed the Sky"**

_"We fell into the darkness together. We climbed back out as legends."_  
— **Seraphine Vex, Captain of the Black Star Company**

Among the Eternal Labyrinths, few are spoken of in reverence and terror alike as the Black Star Descent—a bottomless abyss, an unlit chasm that should not exist, a place where the sky itself collapses into shadow.

For centuries, it was thought unconquerable. Those who entered were never seen again. Those who studied it claimed it had no bottom, no end, only eternal, suffocating darkness. Some whispered that it was not a place, but a wound, a gaping scar where the sky itself had been torn open.

Then, in 5E 447, a group of Delvers leaped willingly into the abyss.

And against all odds, they crawled back out.

They were the Black Star Company, the first and only Delvers to conquer the Descent.

And their victory changed the way the world viewed the Labyrinths forever.

---

### **🕳️ The Black Star Descent: The Abyss That Hungers**

The Black Star Descent is unlike any other Labyrinth. It is not a fortress, nor a maze, nor a buried ruin. It is a hole in the sky, a massive, yawning void in the Stormcall Isles, hovering over the open sea, its edges lined with broken fragments of floating stone, as if the world itself had been shattered around it.

- The wind does not touch it.
- Light bends around it.
- Magic warps upon entering it.

Anything that falls in does not return. Birds that stray too close disappear without a sound. Arrows fired into its depths never land. Ships have been swallowed whole, dragged into its maw by storms that seem to form from nowhere.

And yet, on certain nights, if one stands close enough, they can see stars in its depths—not reflections, not illusions, but constellations that do not exist in the sky above.

Some believe it is a portal to another world. Others claim it is a hole in the fabric of reality itself.

No one knew for certain.

Until the Black Star Company threw themselves inside.

---

### **⚔️ The Company That Conquered the Abyss**

Before they were legends, they were just five reckless fools.

A band of skyfaring mercenaries, smugglers, and outcasts, unwanted by the great nations, unwanted by the guilds, driven not by duty or fame—but by the thrill of the unknown.

They were:

#### **⚔️ Seraphine Vex, The Unyielding Star**

- Human, former pirate-captain, leader of the Black Star Company.
- Wielded twin runed cutlasses, said to glow like the stars when drawn in darkness.
- Known for her reckless confidence, sharp tongue, and willingness to gamble everything on a single moment.

#### **🔮 Orren Sable, The Abyss-Touched Mage**

- Tiefling arcanist, void-walker, rumored sorcerer of the Veilshroud Expanse.
- Survived a brush with the Labyrinth years before, claiming he had already seen the bottom once in a dream.
- His staff, "Nightpiercer," was made of an unknown metal recovered from the Descent.

#### **🎭 Darius Black, The Ghost of the Isles**

- Changeling duelist, master of deception and infiltration.
- Could walk unseen in the Descent, becoming one with the shadows when the abyss called to him.
- Wrote the infamous "Black Star Gambit", a tactical guide for Delvers.

#### **🛡️ Karu Ironjaw, The Skyborn Titan**

- Goliath war-priest of the Stormcallers, bearer of the Shield of the Drowned.
- Survived a direct fall into the Abyss, landing in an unknown ruin deep within the Descent.
- Once fought a being made of starlight and shadow—and won.

#### **🕳️ Isolde Veyne, The Shadow Between Stars**

- Elven dream-weaver, illusionist, and seer.
- Claimed to have heard the Abyss whisper before ever setting foot inside.
- Became one of the first recorded Delvers to "walk the void" and return.

Together, they did the impossible—they threw themselves into a hole with no bottom, into a Labyrinth that had never returned a single soul.

And three months later, they climbed back out.

---

### **🔻 The Impossible Victory**

What they found inside the Black Star Descent has never been fully revealed.

What is known is that they returned with:

- Weapons and relics crafted from metals that do not exist anywhere else in Aerathon.
- Maps of a labyrinthine city made of obsidian and shattered constellations.
- Marks upon their bodies—veins like silver starlight, eyes that burned with the glow of falling embers.
- A single, bound book, filled with symbols that have never been translated.

They did not speak of what they saw, only that they had found the bottom, and that the bottom was looking back at them.

They refused to return.

They refused to let anyone else go.

And then, they disbanded.

The Black Star Company, the greatest Delvers of their age, the only ones to conquer the Descent, split apart. Some say they had seen too much. Others believe they had learned something that should never be spoken aloud.

Whatever the truth, the Descent remains.

And it is still waiting.

---

### **📜 Final Observations: The Sky’s Open Wound**

The Black Star Descent has never been closed. It has never shifted, never vanished, never altered its form. It is simply there, swallowing light, swallowing time, swallowing anything foolish enough to fall inside.

The Black Star Company forbade all further exploration, but their warnings have only tempted new generations of Delvers to seek its depths.

And yet, of all those who have entered since, none have returned.

Only the five who claimed it ever walked away.

And perhaps, if the abyss still remembers them, it is only waiting for them to return.
