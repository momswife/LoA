## **Background & Reputation**

### **The Rising Star of the Argent Banner**

Before he was one of the most feared names in Delving history, Rhys Valden was a celebrated warrior, an elite strategist, and a prodigy of the Argent Banner.

- Recruited at the age of 19, he quickly rose through the ranks, becoming one of the youngest Gold-Ranked Delvers in history.
- Charismatic, skilled, and fiercely intelligent, he was seen as the ideal Delver—a symbol of heroism and perfection.
- Favored by noble sponsors, he was set to become one of the most famous figures in the world of Livecast Delving.

His early successes included:

- Leading a flawless expedition into the Black Star Descent, recovering an ancient artifact now held in the HDA vaults.
- Breaking a siege against an awakened golem construct in the Radiant Crucible, proving his skill in tactical combat.
- Becoming a fan-favorite Livecast personality, due to his sharp wit, leadership, and reputation as an honorable warrior.

But everything changed in Year 919, during an expedition into Labyrinth #4 – The Clockwork Tomb.

By the time he stepped back out of the Labyrinth, his legacy was gone.

And so were the other four members of his team.

## **The Clockwork Tomb Expedition – Year 919**

### **The Mission**

In 919, the Argent Banner sanctioned an expedition into Labyrinth #4 – The Clockwork Tomb, a long-dormant structure that had only recently reactivated.

- The Labyrinth, believed to be one of the oldest mechanical constructs in existence, had been deemed low-risk, but valuable due to the potential for ancient automaton relics.
- Rhys Valden was chosen to lead the mission, accompanied by four other elite Delvers.

### **The Incident** _(Based on recovered Livecast footage and expedition logs.)_

The expedition began without issue. The team progressed through the early sections of the Labyrinth, navigating intricate mechanical puzzles and automaton guardians.

- The footage shows Rhys commanding with precision, issuing perfectly calculated orders to his squad.
- Their progress was smooth—too smooth. The team seemed to be avoiding major encounters, as if Rhys already knew where the dangers were.

Then the footage cuts out.

- For exactly 17 minutes, all visual and audio feeds were lost.
- When the Livecast resumed, Rhys was the only one left.
- His armor was stained with blood—not just on his blade, but on his hands.

He walked alone through the final chamber, activating the central mechanism of the Tomb.

- Clockwork constructs deactivated.
- A hidden vault unlocked.
- Rhys retrieved an artifact known only as "The Black Cog."

Then, without a word, a single glance back, or any attempt to explain what had happened, he walked out of the Labyrinth.

When questioned by the Argent Banner and the HDA, his only response was:

_"There were supposed to be five of us."_

And then, he disappeared.

## **Aftermath & Cover-Up**

### **Official HDA Report (Year 919, Restricted Archives)**

- Rhys Valden was declared "officially deceased" despite his body never being found.
- The Clockwork Tomb was sealed indefinitely, with no further expeditions permitted.
- The "Black Cog" was taken into HDA custody—it has never been seen or mentioned since.
- All footage of the missing 17 minutes was erased from Livecast archives.

### **Theories & Unanswered Questions**

- Did Rhys kill his team? Some believe he betrayed them, sacrificing them to access the Black Cog.
- Was the Black Cog controlling him? Certain artifacts have been known to affect Delver behavior—could it have twisted his mind?
- What happened in those 17 missing minutes? The ABA sealed the footage permanently, labeling it a "national security risk."

### **Sightings & Unconfirmed Reports**

Though officially dead, Rhys Valden has allegedly been seen multiple times over the past two decades.

- A hooded figure matching his description was spotted in the Veilshroud Expanse, entering an underground ruin.
- A masked warrior with his exact combat style was seen dueling Delver Marshals near an abandoned Labyrinth.
- A trader in the Titan Highlands claimed to have sold a mechanical gauntlet to a man with a "strangely empty gaze."

Every time, before confirmation could be made, the figure vanished.
