## **Background & Reputation**

### **The Shield of the Argent Banner**

Dain Veldros was the embodiment of endurance, resilience, and unyielding willpower. Unlike the sponsored aristocrats and tacticians of the Argent Banner, he was a soldier first and a legend second.

- Born into a soldier’s life, he fought in border skirmishes before becoming a Delver.
- Handpicked by the Argent Banner’s elite for his raw toughness and unwavering loyalty.
- Fought in over 47 recorded Delving expeditions—a number that still stands as one of the highest on record.

He was not the fastest.  
He was not the most brilliant strategist.  
But he could not be stopped.

- Survived wounds that would have felled lesser warriors.
- Defended his team against overwhelming odds time and time again.
- Refused to retreat, even when ordered.

Many believed he was unkillable.

They were wrong.

## **The Last Stand at the Twilight Bastion

### **The Mission**

In Year 882, the Argent Banner sent a six-man team into Labyrinth #5 – The Twilight Bastion, a fortress-like Labyrinth filled with spectral knights, endless corridors, and shifting battlefields.

- Unlike other Labyrinths, the Twilight Bastion was a place of war—a battlefield that never ended.
- Every chamber contained illusory generals, ghostly armies, and magical sieges in a cycle of eternal conflict.
- The mission was to reach the Labyrinth’s central keep and retrieve the "Everflame Standard", an artifact believed to be a relic of an ancient kingdom lost to time.

Dain was the team’s frontline defender, chosen for his endurance.

By the end, he was the only one left.

---

### **The Fall of the Bastion – (Based on fragmented Livecast footage & recovered expedition logs)**

The initial expedition was smooth—the team cut through waves of spectral soldiers, moving from siege towers to war-torn hallways, deeper into the Bastion’s heart.

But as they approached the central keep, the Labyrinth adapted.

- Illusory soldiers became solid.
- The battlefield refused to stay the same—walls shifted, doorways vanished, terrain collapsed.
- The dead Delvers of past expeditions rose to fight again.

## **Aftermath & Legacy**

### **The Last Sentinel**

- Dain was declared KIA (Killed in Action) after no retrieval team could find his remains.
- The Everflame Standard was never recovered.
- The Twilight Bastion remains active to this day, its battles still raging.

But his legend did not die.

- The Argent Banner posthumously knighted him, calling him "The Last Sentinel of the Twilight Bastion."
- His name became synonymous with endurance and sacrifice.
- Livecasters still replay his final moments as an example of unshakable resolve.

---

### **Sightings & Unanswered Questions**

Though he is officially dead, rumors persist that Dain Veldros still stands somewhere in the Twilight Bastion, fighting a battle that never ends.

- Some Delvers claim to have seen him, his body fully armored, his stance unbroken.
- The spectral knights of the Bastion whisper his name—but never attack him.
- Some believe that he did not die, but became part of the Labyrinth itself.

Is Dain still holding the line, even in death?

Or is he waiting for someone strong enough to relieve him of his eternal duty?
