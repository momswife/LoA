## **Overview: Active Delvers Today**

For centuries, Delvers have braved the deadly depths of the Labyrinths, seeking fortune, glory, knowledge, and power. Some have carved their names into legend, while others vanished into the darkness, their stories half-told, their legacies unfinished.

Below are the most famous, infamous, and enigmatic Delvers—figures who shaped history, changed the way Delving is seen, or left behind mysteries that remain unsolved.

___

### **Seraphis the Unshackled** – _The Redeemer of the Crimson Labyrinth_

**Race:** Aasimar | **Class:** Redemption Paladin | **Guild:** Unaffiliated  
_"I walked through fire and emerged whole. I can show you the way—if you are willing to burn."_

Once a prisoner of the Crimson Labyrinth, Seraphis was believed dead for seven years before emerging from the depths unscathed. Now, he seeks to cleanse the corruption within the Labyrinths, healing the minds and souls of Delvers who return… broken.

- His past is a mystery—no records exist of him before his disappearance.
- Rumors persist that he struck a bargain with something inside the Labyrinth.
- Some believe he is immortal—never aging, never weakening.

---

### **Vasha the Stormwolf** – _Champion of the Maw, Crimson Reaver Warlord_

**Race:** Human | **Class:** Berserker Barbarian | **Guild:** **Crimson Reavers**  
_"I don’t need maps. I don’t need plans. I need a blade in my hands and a beast in my path."_

One of the most violent, reckless, and ferocious warriors of the age, Vasha became infamous for surviving a solo expedition into the Howling Maw, emerging covered in blood, carrying the severed head of an ancient, abyssal horror.

- Loyal to nothing but the thrill of the fight.
- Has fought and won more duels than any other active Delver.
- Refuses to take sponsorships—"I am not for sale."

Some believe she cannot be killed—others believe she is merely waiting for an opponent worthy of ending her.

---

### **Edris Valcayne** – _The Master Tactician of the Argent Banner_

**Race:** Human | **Class:** Battle Master Fighter | **Guild:** **Argent Banner**  
_"Victory is won before the battle begins."_

A strategic genius, Edris is responsible for some of the most efficient, high-profile Delving operations in history. He is a master of preparation, positioning, and controlling the battlefield.

- Never rushes into battle—plans every move before stepping into the Labyrinth.
- Has never lost a single expedition under his command.
- Some believe he manipulates the Grand Scrying Network to ensure favorable outcomes.

Despite his flawless record, whispers suggest that his victories are not always as clean as they seem.

---

### **Velka the Unseen** – _The Ghost of the Labyrinths, Master Thief of the Gilded Shadows_

**Race:** Elf | **Class:** Arcane Trickster Rogue | **Guild:** **Gilded Shadows**  
_"If it exists, I have stolen it. If you think you own it, check again."_

A legendary thief and master infiltrator, Velka has stolen priceless artifacts from Labyrinths that were supposedly impenetrable. Her true identity is unknown—some believe she wears different faces, changing with each heist.

- Has never been caught. Not once.
- Allegedly infiltrated the Argent Banner’s vault and stole a relic even they won’t name.
- Rumors persist that she has ties to the Hollow Masks and may not be a single person, but multiple operatives using the same name.

If Velka wants something, no lock, wall, or spell will stop her.

---

### **The Painted Man** – _The Hollow Mask That Never Speaks_

**Race:** Unknown | **Class:** Unknown | **Guild:** **Hollow Masks**  
_"A name is a weight. A face is a weakness."_

No one knows who The Painted Man truly is. He appears in Livecast footage, battles, and Labyrinth dives, but never speaks, never reveals his face, and never leaves evidence of his past.

- Has killed dozens of high-profile Delvers in duels, yet remains unaffiliated with any guild.
- Some believe he is a former noble who erased his own existence.
- Others claim he is no longer human, but something born from the Labyrinths themselves.

Whatever he is, those who cross him do not live long enough to learn more.

---

### **Luthar Kane** – _The Delver Who Knew Too Much_

**Race:** Human | **Class:** Warlock | **Guild:** **Hollow Pact (Formerly)**  
_"There is a pattern. There is a purpose. And it is watching us."_

Luthar Kane was a brilliant but reckless researcher, one of the first to publicly claim that the Labyrinths were not just dungeons, but something far more sinister.

- Vanished after entering the Radiant Crucible, leaving behind only his notes.
- Before disappearing, he claimed the Labyrinths were "growing" and that they "hungered for memory."
- His writings were confiscated by the HDA and sealed within restricted archives.

Some whisper that his theories were right… and that’s why he was erased.

---

### **Cassandra Vale** – _The Oracle Who Walked Into the Dark_

**Race:** Tiefling | **Class:** Divination Wizard | **Guild:** **None**  
_"I have seen the end. I must meet it myself."_

Cassandra Vale was the most powerful Seer of her generation, capable of foretelling deaths with perfect accuracy.

- One day, she simply walked into the Black Star Descent and was never seen again.
- Left behind a prophecy stating, "When I am gone, the next Labyrinth will open in the heart of a city."
- The Nameless Labyrinth appeared in Velgrove Square exactly seven years later.

Did she see her own fate? Or did she become something else?

---

### **Aric Hollowborn** – _The Man Who Never Died_

**Race:** Unknown | **Class:** Unknown | **Guild:** **Unknown**  
_"Every story has an ending. Except mine."_

Aric Hollowborn is a myth, a legend whispered in Delver taverns—a Delver who supposedly died in multiple Labyrinths, yet keeps appearing in new Livecasts.

- Confirmed to have perished in the Vault of Eternity, the Hollow God’s Sepulcher, and the Drowned Catacombs.
- Each time, a man with his face appears again years later, with no memory of his past lives.
- Some claim he is trapped in an endless cycle, cursed to die and return forever.

If this is true, then who—if anyone—remembers the real Aric?

---

### **Zaelith "The Hollow Blade"** – _The Sword Saint Who Cast Off His Name_

**Race:** Elf | **Class:** Echo Knight Fighter | **Guild:** **None (Formerly Argent Banner)**  
_"A blade does not question. A blade only cuts."_

Once considered the most promising warrior of the Argent Banner, Zaelith walked away from noble sponsorship, wealth, and fame, choosing a life of solitude and wandering.

- Wields an ancient Labyrinth-forged sword that seems to move on its own.
- Refuses all sponsorships, saying, "A sword with an owner is a sword without a purpose."
- Has fought in over a dozen Delver duels and has never been seen to lose.

Some say he is searching for something deep within the Labyrinths. Others believe he is running from something he cannot face.

---

### **"Madcap" Mirella Thorn** – _The Queen of Chaos_

**Race:** Halfling | **Class:** Wild Magic Sorcerer | **Guild:** **None (Previously Crimson Reavers, Kicked Out)**  
_"Oh, you didn’t want an explosion? Whoops. No refunds."_

A walking catastrophe, Mirella is infamous for surviving against all odds through sheer dumb luck and magical insanity.

- Expelled from the Crimson Reavers for "excessive destruction"—which is saying something.
- Once survived a collapsing Labyrinth by surfing down a landslide of debris.
- The Grand Scrying Network loves her Livecasts because "something always explodes."

Despite her recklessness, she is shockingly competent when it matters most—but the question remains:

Is she lucky, or is something watching over her?

---

### **Rythan "The Undying Scholar"** – _The Seeker of Forgotten Gods_

**Race:** Human | **Class:** Knowledge Cleric | **Guild:** **The Hollow Pact**  
_"The Labyrinths are older than our gods. But something else came first."_

A former priest who abandoned his faith, Rythan now seeks answers that even the gods fear.

- Has recovered relics and texts that predate known history.
- Believes the Labyrinths are not "dungeons," but "prisons" meant to contain something ancient.
- The HDA has attempted to silence him multiple times, but he always resurfaces.

Some say his discoveries could shatter reality itself. Others believe he is already doomed to be consumed by what he seeks.

---

### **Captain Rook Blacktide** – _The Pirate Delver_

**Race:** Human | **Class:** Swashbuckler Rogue | **Guild:** **None (Formerly of the Gilded Shadows)**  
_"Gold is useless if you're dead. But it sure makes dying more comfortable."_

A former pirate-turned-Delver, Rook Blacktide is infamous for treating the Labyrinths as his personal treasure vault.

- Has stolen priceless artifacts before major expeditions could claim them.
- Wanted in six different cities for smuggling forbidden relics.
- Frequents the Tempest Sea, selling stolen goods to the highest bidder.

The HDA wants him dead. The Gilded Shadows want him back. The Delver Marshals want him captured.

Rook just wants another drink.

---

### **Lysara the Dreaming Blade** – _The Woman Who Walks Between Worlds_

**Race:** Elf | **Class:** Psi Warrior Fighter | **Guild:** **Unknown**  
_"I remember futures that have not yet come to pass. I have walked through Labyrinths that have not yet been built."_

Lysara claims to have memories of events that have never happened, claiming that time does not work the way people think it does within the Labyrinths.

- Has been seen in multiple places at once.
- Knows things she should not—secrets, prophecies, and names of people she has never met.
- Has entered certain Labyrinths and emerged before they were even discovered.

Is she a prophet, a liar, or something far stranger?

---

### **Baelgor Ironhand** – _The One Who Fought the Labyrinth and Won_

**Race:** Dwarf | **Class:** Rune Knight Fighter | **Guild:** **Argent Banner (Retired)**  
_"I have seen the Labyrinths bleed. That means they can die."_

One of the few Delvers to ever "break" a Labyrinth, Baelgor triggered a collapse in the Vault of Eternity, causing part of it to cease existing entirely.

- No one knows how he did it.
- Some say he carved runes into the very walls, forcing the structure to weaken.
- The HDA refuses to acknowledge that a Labyrinth was ever "damaged"—but something was lost forever.

Baelgor has since retired, refusing to speak of what he saw—because, as he puts it:

"If I tell you what I learned, you’ll try to do it too. And you won’t come back."

### **The Drowned Priestess** – _The Whisper in the Tides_

**Race:** Unknown | **Class:** Warlock (Great Old One) | **Guild:** **None**  
_"The sea does not take. The sea does not give. The sea only watches."_

No one knows who she was before she entered the Drowned Catacombs—only that she returned changed.

- Her voice is never heard—only felt, like an echo in the back of the mind.
- Her eyes are the color of deep abyssal waters, and she does not blink.
- Some claim she does not breathe.

She does not seek treasure, fame, or knowledge. She simply walks, waits, and watches.

And sometimes, when she touches a Delver’s forehead, they drown in their sleep—lungs filling with water that is not there.

### **Callista "The Silver Phoenix"** – _The Delver Who Would Not Die_

**Race:** Human | **Class:** Devotion Paladin | **Guild:** **Argent Banner (Formerly), Free Company of Stalwart Souls (Currently)**  
_"I burned. I broke. And then I stood back up."_

Once the shining prodigy of the Argent Banner, Callista was believed dead after a failed expedition into the Radiant Crucible, where her entire team perished. Yet three years later, she returned—scarred, changed, but alive.

- Refuses to speak of how she survived.
- Turned her back on the Argent Banner, calling them "liars wrapped in silver."
- Joined the Free Company of Stalwart Souls, saying, "The real heroes don’t have sponsors."

The Argent Banner denies ever sending an expedition to the Radiant Crucible that day. So if Callista was never there…

Where was she?

### **Draykus Thorn** – _The Black Hand of the HDA_

**Race:** Human | **Class:** Assassin Rogue | **Guild:** **Halvaskian Delving Authority (Special Operative Division, Unofficial)**  
_"You shouldn’t know my name. Which means you won’t remember it for long."_

A ghost in the system, Draykus is the HDA’s answer to rogue Delvers, black-market relic dealers, and anyone who steps too far out of line.

- If a Delver suddenly vanishes, rumors say Draykus Thorn was involved.
- Carries no known relics or enchanted gear—just a single, perfectly maintained dagger.
- No known Livecast footage of him exists. Any that do are mysteriously erased.

Even among the Delver Marshals, his existence is whispered of, but never confirmed.

Because to confirm him is to invite his attention.

### **Kassir Velmire** – _The Man Who Sees Too Much_

**Race:** Elf | **Class:** Divination Wizard | **Guild:** **Hollow Pact (Formerly), Declared Rogue Researcher**  
_"The Labyrinths are not what they seem. And neither are we."_

A once-respected scholar of the Hollow Pact, Kassir became obsessed with the idea that the Labyrinths are more than dungeons—they are "recording" something.

- Developed the "Velmire Theory," which suggests the Labyrinths are conscious observers of history.
- Banned from the Hollow Pact after refusing to turn over his research.
- His last known Livecast showed him standing before a shifting wall of stone, whispering, "They remember us."

A week later, he vanished. No trace of him or his research has ever been found.

### **Rojan Greaves** – _The Man Who Sold His Own Death_

**Race:** Human | **Class:** Swashbuckler Rogue | **Guild:** **The Gilded Shadows (Formerly), Unknown**  
_"Death is only a problem if you pay full price for it."_

Once a brilliant artifact smuggler, Rojan faked his own death to escape a debt to the Crimson Reavers. The problem?

- His own body was recovered, examined, and burned.
- A year later, he was spotted alive—wearing the same scars, the same memories, the same damn smirk.
- When asked how he was still breathing, he simply laughed and said, "I made a trade."

Whatever deal he made, it wasn’t free.

And one day, whoever owns his soul will come to collect.

---

### **Wrenna "The Hollow Song"** – _The Singer of the Forgotten_

**Race:** Half-Elf | **Class:** College of Spirits Bard | **Guild:** **None**  
_"There are names the Labyrinths try to erase. I sing them back into the world."_

Wrenna is no warrior, no thief, no mage. She is simply a singer who remembers the names of those who never returned.

- Knows the names of over a thousand lost Delvers by heart.
- Claims to hear their voices in her dreams, whispering their final moments.
- Some say she once stepped into a Labyrinth, walked its halls untouched, and left with names no one else could remember.

She does not fight the Labyrinths.

She does not fear them.

She only listens.

And one day, she will sing your name too.

---

### **Ferris Kaelthorn** – _The Only Man to Cheat a Labyrinth_

**Race:** Tiefling | **Class:** Trickery Cleric | **Guild:** **None (Banned from All Major Guilds)**  
_"I didn’t break the rules. I just bent them until they snapped."_

Ferris Kaelthorn is the only Delver to ever "win" a Labyrinth without actually entering it.

- Used a complex illusion ritual to trick an entire expedition into thinking he went inside.
- Remotely manipulated their findings, having them unknowingly retrieve artifacts for him.
- By the time they realized he never stepped inside, he was already gone—with the relics in hand.

The HDA declared him an outlaw.

The Argent Banner declared him a disgrace.

Ferris, however, declared himself the smartest Delver in history.

And honestly?

He’s probably right.

### **Isa "The Silent Bell"** – _The One Who Survived When No One Else Did_

**Race:** Human | **Class:** Mercy Monk | **Guild:** **Unknown**  
_"They told me to run. I didn’t listen. Now they are all gone, and I am still here."_

Isa was part of a five-person expedition into the Silver Ruin.

- Livecast footage showed her team moving through frost-covered halls, searching for an ancient relic.
- The footage cut out for exactly three minutes.
- When it returned, she was the only one left, kneeling in the snow. The others were gone.

She will not speak of what happened in those three minutes.

She will not enter another Labyrinth.

She only rings a small silver bell, softly, at every Delver’s funeral she attends.
