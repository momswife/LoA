## **Overview: The Arcane Broadcast Authority (ABA)**

The Arcane Broadcast Authority (ABA) is the governing body responsible for regulating, controlling, and censoring all magical transmission networks across Aerathon, with a primary focus on Livecasting Delver expeditions, state broadcasts, and regulated scrying transmissions.

While publicly known as a neutral organization that ensures ethical broadcasting, the ABA is a highly secretive and politically entangled agency, wielding unprecedented power over what the public sees and what is erased from history.

With the rise of Livecasting and the Grand Scrying Network (GSN) as the most dominant entertainment and information source in Aerathon, the ABA has evolved into a shadow government of its own, influencing politics, commerce, and even the Delving industry itself.

---

Unlike most bureaucratic organizations, the ABA operates with near-military precision, ensuring absolute control over scrying networks, Livecast transmissions, and arcane surveillance.

## **The Role of the ABA**

While the **Grand Scrying Network (GSN) physically operates the magical transmission system**, the **ABA dictates what can and cannot be seen**.

### **Key Responsibilities**

- **Regulating Livecasted Delver Expeditions** – Ensuring footage remains suitable for public viewing while censoring "destabilizing" content.
- **Monitoring & Censoring Magical Transmissions** – Intercepting unauthorized broadcasts, suppressing forbidden knowledge, and altering historical narratives.
- **Managing Public Perception** – Using Livecasting, commentary teams, and scripted narratives to shape public opinion about Delvers, Labyrinths, and government policies.
- **Controlling Commercial Sponsorships** – Ensuring only ABA-approved noble houses, merchant guilds, and organizations can fund Delvers.
- **Enforcing Surveillance & Anti-Piracy Laws** – Cracking down on rogue mages, unauthorized scrying, and black-market broadcasting.

## **The ABA's Control Over Livecasting & Public Access**

Livecasting has become one of the most powerful tools in Aerathon, transforming Delvers into celebrities, cautionary tales, or political symbols. However, not everything that happens in the Labyrinths makes it to public view.

### **Censorship & Restricted Footage**

The ABA has strict protocols on what can and cannot be shown:

| **Content**                          | **Allowed?**           | **Censorship Notes**                                                                                   |
| ------------------------------------ | ---------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| Standard Delving Battles             | **Allowed**            | Minor editing to enhance tension.                                                                      |
| Fatalities & Injuries                | **Allowed (Modified)** | Slow, painful deaths may be edited for "dramatic pacing".                                              |
| Delvers Losing Sanity                | **Censored**           | Any footage of mental breakdowns, incoherent rambling, or possession is erased.                        |
| Encounters with "Anomalies"          | **Classified**         | If a Delver sees something that "should not be seen," the footage is locked away.                      |
| Reality Distortions & Time Anomalies | **Highly Restricted**  | Confirmed Labyrinth events that warp time, space, or physics are erased from the public record.        |
| Forbidden Relics & Dark Magic        | **Banned**             | Any discovery of artifacts that challenge religious, political, or historical narratives is concealed. |

Most citizens believe they are watching "uncut reality." In truth, they are watching a carefully curated version of the truth.

## **Surveillance & Information Control**

The ABA does not just regulate entertainment—it serves as an intelligence organization, monitoring magical communications, Delvers, and secret knowledge that could threaten stability.

### **Covert Operations & Scrying Surveillance**

- "Echo Wardens" patrol Delver hubs, disguised as merchants, scholars, or fellow adventurers, reporting suspicious conversations or forbidden knowledge.
- All Livecasted Delvers wear enchanted sigils, which can remotely terminate their transmission if they begin speaking of censored topics.
- Scrying hubs track unauthorized magical communication, ensuring that forbidden discoveries do not spread.

### **The Restricted Archives – The Footage That No One Can See**

Deep within the Vaults of the ABA, thousands of hours of erased footage, classified transmissions, and redacted scrying events are stored under maximum security.

Some whisper that the archives contain:

- Live footage of Delvers encountering creatures that defy comprehension.
- Records of vanished expeditions that never should have been seen.
- Evidence that the Labyrinths are more than mere dungeons—but something far worse.

Only the highest-ranking members of the ABA have access to these archives, and any unauthorized attempt to retrieve lost footage is punishable by death.

## **Relationship with Other Governing Bodies**

### **Alliance with the Halvaskian Delving Authority (HDA)**

- The ABA ensures that the HDA maintains control over Delving permits, preventing independent Delvers from revealing unsanctioned discoveries.
- The HDA, in turn, provides armed support to enforce the ABA’s censorship laws.

### **Manipulation of the Grand Scrying Network (GSN)**

- While the GSN physically controls the broadcasting towers, it operates under ABA restrictions.
- The ABA controls which Delvers are "pushed" as heroes and which are erased from the public record.

### **Conflict with the Arcane Guild of Labyrinth Studies (AGLS)**

- The AGLS seeks to publish unrestricted knowledge, while the ABA believes that some knowledge is too dangerous to share.
- There are ongoing disputes over classified discoveries, with some AGLS scholars mysteriously disappearing after uncovering censored Labyrinth secrets.
