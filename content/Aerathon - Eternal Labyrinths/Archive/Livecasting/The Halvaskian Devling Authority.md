## **Overview: The Halvaskian Delving Authority (HDA)**

The Halvaskian Delving Authority (HDA) is the primary governing body responsible for overseeing all official Delving operations across Aerathon. It was established to regulate access to the Labyrinths, enforce safety protocols, and control the economic and arcane implications of Delving.

With the rise of Livecasting, corporate-backed Delvers, and independent expeditions, the HDA has become one of the most powerful and controversial institutions in the world, balancing the interests of governments, noble houses, arcane scholars, and Delver guilds.

While it presents itself as a force for order and responsible exploration, many believe that the HDA is deeply compromised, serving as a tool for the elite rather than protecting the interests of common Delvers.

## **HDA Regulations & Laws on Delving**

All Labyrinth exploration falls under strict HDA oversight. Unauthorized Delving is a federal offense, punishable by fines, imprisonment, or execution in extreme cases.

### **Core Delving Laws**

#### **1. The Delver Licensing Act**

- Only licensed Delvers may legally enter a Labyrinth.
- Licenses are ranked (Copper, Silver, Gold, and Platinum), determining which Labyrinths a Delver may explore.
- High-risk Labyrinths require Guild or Noble sponsorship.

#### **2. Artifact Recovery & Arcane Containment Law**

- All artifacts retrieved from Labyrinths must be inspected, taxed, and cataloged by the HDA.
- Certain restricted relics (high-tier magical weapons, lost historical records, or reality-altering objects) are confiscated immediately.
- Unauthorized possession or smuggling of artifacts is punishable by exile or death.

#### **3. The Livecasting & Public Viewing Regulations**

- Livecasting Delving expeditions requires HDA approval to prevent "public destabilization."
- Any disturbing, anomalous, or politically sensitive footage is immediately classified and stored in the Grand Vault.
- Only approved commentary teams may broadcast Delver experiences, ensuring that the public sees what is "safe" to be seen.

#### **4. The Labyrinth Containment & Security Act**

- The HDA controls all active Labyrinth entrances, deploying guards, arcane seals, or containment squads when necessary.
- Delvers may not open new passages without approval.
- If a Labyrinth exhibits spatial instability, monster emergence, or reality shifts, it may be immediately sealed by government decree.

## **HDA Military & Law Enforcement**

Given the dangers of Labyrinth incursions, rogue Delvers, and black-market relic dealers, the HDA maintains a formidable military and policing force.

### **The Delver Marshals (Law Enforcement Division)**

A specialized force responsible for tracking and apprehending illegal Delvers, smugglers, and unsanctioned expeditions.

- Highly trained in anti-Delver tactics, capable of countering rogue adventurers.
- Operate undercover within Delver communities, monitoring suspicious activity.
- Employ enchanted tracking sigils to mark and locate fugitives.

### **The Labyrinth Guard (Military Division)**

The HDA’s direct combat force, tasked with guarding unstable Labyrinths and enforcing security at known entrances.

- Deploys containment teams if creatures or anomalies escape a Labyrinth.
- Uses elite Paladin and Wizard battalions for high-threat engagements.
- Equipped with some of the most advanced anti-magic and containment weaponry in Aerathon.

### **The Scryer Intelligence Bureau (Surveillance & Espionage Division)**

A secretive branch working alongside the Grand Scrying Network, responsible for:

- Monitoring Livecast footage for anomalies.
- Intercepting and suppressing leaked Delver information.
- Tracking and classifying individuals who may be "changed" by Labyrinth exposure.

## **Economic Control & Financial Influence**

The HDA ensures that Delving remains a profitable enterprise under its control, taxing every step of the process.

### **Delver Licensing & Taxation**

- All relics retrieved from Labyrinths are taxed up to 40%.
- Licensed Delvers must renew their permits annually, with higher-ranking Delvers paying increased fees.
- Guild-affiliated Delvers receive preferential treatment, while independent Delvers are heavily restricted.

### **Guild Sponsorship & Noble Investments**

- Many noble houses own partial rights to certain Labyrinths, demanding a portion of all treasure recovered.
- The HDA sells Delving permits to wealthy investors, who then control which adventurers are allowed inside.
- Some suspect HDA officials receive bribes to ensure certain Delvers rise to prominence while others are suppressed.

---

## **Controversies & Public Criticism**

While the HDA presents itself as the protector of civilization, many question its true motives.

### **Major Criticisms**

- **Corruption & Bribery**: It is widely believed that noble families and merchant elites control HDA policies, ensuring only the rich benefit from Delving.
- **Artifact Hoarding**: Many scholars argue that the HDA withholds powerful relics for their own use, rather than allowing their knowledge to be shared.
- **Livecast Manipulation:** Some claim that certain Delver deaths are staged or "allowed to happen" to increase entertainment value.
- **Harsh Punishments:** Unauthorized Delvers are often executed or sent into "exile"—a convenient way of disposing of unwanted figures.

The rise of black-market Delving guilds and illegal expeditions suggests that many adventurers do not trust the HDA’s oversight, preferring to risk criminal charges rather than submit to the Authority’s restrictions.
