## **Overview: The Grand Scrying Network (GSN)**

The **Grand Scrying Network (GSN)** is the largest and most advanced magical broadcasting system in Aerathon, responsible for Livecasting Delver expeditions, major political events, gladiatorial combat, and entertainment across the world. Controlled by a conglomerate of noble investors, arcane institutions, and government-appointed officials, the GSN is both a technological marvel and a powerful tool of influence, shaping how the world sees history unfold in real time.

Though initially established as a means of mass communication, the Network has grown into a global spectacle-driven industry, with the Labyrinth Delver Livecasts becoming its most profitable and controversial venture.

With the Nameless Labyrinth appearing in Velgrove Square, Halvask, the demand for Delver broadcasts has reached unprecedented levels, bringing fortune, glory, and danger to those involved.

---

## **The Purpose & Function of the Grand Scrying Network**

### **Mass Communication & Global Influence**

Originally designed as a **mage-to-mage relay system**, the GSN has evolved into **a worldwide arcane broadcasting network**, linking **kingdoms, cities, and noble estates** through a system of **magical transmission hubs**.

- **Delver Expeditions:** The most popular broadcasts, featuring **Livecasted Labyrinth explorations with real-time commentary, betting markets, and sponsorships**.
- **Political Announcements:** Used by rulers and governments for **mass declarations, war updates, and policy shifts**.
- **Gladiatorial Combat & Arena Games:** The second-largest entertainment sector, rivaling Delver broadcasts in sheer spectacle and bloodlust.
- **Exclusive Scrying Contracts:** Nobles, merchants, and arcane scholars **pay vast sums** for private viewings of restricted events.

### **How the Grand Scrying Network Works**

At its core, the GSN relies on a series of interconnected magical relays, called Arcane Transmission Towers, which process and amplify visual and auditory feeds from Divine Oculi, distributing them across the continent.

1. **Divine Oculi** – Floating arcane constructs that capture and transmit live footage.
2. **Arcane Relays** – Large scrying towers that amplify and distribute the transmission.
3. **Receiving Devices** – Personal enchanted mirrors, public projection screens, and scrying orbs that allow citizens to view Livecasts.

Due to the highly secretive nature of its infrastructure, only elite artificers and divination experts fully understand the inner workings of the Grand Scrying Network—a knowledge kept under strict governmental control.

## **Governance & Control**

The GSN is not an independent entity—it is tightly regulated by the Halvaskian government, noble investors, and arcane institutions that ensure its profitable operation and political utility.

### **The Arcane Broadcast Authority (ABA)**

A governing body of high-ranking officials, tasked with overseeing what is permitted to be broadcasted, ensuring compliance with political and arcane law.

- **High Minister of Communications – Lord Adrivane Elsith** _(Oversees all live transmissions and maintains censorship protocols.)_
- **Archmagus of Transmission – Thellian Mir** _(Head of arcane engineering & security for the network.)_
- **Scryer-General of Public Viewings – Luthas Dain** _(Manages regulated public Livecast events & noble sponsorships.)_

### **Security & Censorship Protocols**

Despite its reputation as a source of raw, real-time entertainment, the GSN is heavily controlled, with strict censorship laws and classified footage archives.

- Live Footage May Not Be Recorded for Public Access _(See: Decree on Livecasting Regulations.)_
- All transmissions pass through a controlled delay system, allowing emergency censorship of extreme or destabilizing content.
- Restricted Archives exist within the Grand Vault of Halvask, containing footage deemed too dangerous, politically sensitive, or reality-breaking to be viewed again.

The Arcane Broadcast Authority has complete jurisdiction over what remains public knowledge and what disappears into secrecy.

## **Economics & Sponsorships – The Business of Livecasting**

With the rise of Delver Livecasting, the GSN has transformed into a billion-gold industry, allowing corporate investors, noble patrons, and arcane guilds to influence which Delvers become stars, and which fade into obscurity.

### **Major Investors & Financial Backers**

- The Halvaskian Crown _(Uses the GSN for political announcements and social influence.)_
- The Arcane Merchant Consortium _(Provides funding in exchange for exclusive broadcast rights.)_
- The Gilded Pantheon’s Noble Houses _(View Delvers as living advertisements for wealth & power.)_
- Underground Betting Syndicates _(Manipulate outcomes & influence Livecast sponsorships.)_

### **The Role of Sponsors & Endorsements**

The wealthiest Delvers receive personalized sponsorships, granting them:

- Customized enchanted weapons & armor, displaying sponsor sigils.
- High-end living quarters, tailored gear, and post-Delve benefits.
- Exclusive financial backing, guaranteeing their prominence in future broadcasts.

Meanwhile, independent Delvers struggle—unless they prove themselves in the Labyrinths, they may never be noticed by powerful backers.

## **Controversies & Ethical Concerns**

Though widely **celebrated**, the GSN has come under **heavy scrutiny** for its **treatment of Delvers, censorship practices, and moral ambiguity in capitalizing on human suffering**.

### **Major Ethical Concerns:**

- **The Disposable Delver Issue** – Lower-ranked Delvers often enter knowing they will die, yet their deaths are glorified for entertainment.
- **Corruption & Rigged Sponsorships** – Certain Delvers are quietly assisted, receiving hidden advantages to shape their narratives.
- **Oculi That See Too Much** – Rumors persist that some Divine Oculi have glimpsed things within the Labyrinths that should never have been recorded.

The Veilborn Theocracy, in particular, has called for tighter restrictions, claiming that the Labyrinths should not be exploited for public amusement. Others, including the Order of Iron Ascendants, demand that the GSN be dismantled entirely—believing that Livecasting fuels greed, recklessness, and the continued existence of the Labyrinths themselves.
