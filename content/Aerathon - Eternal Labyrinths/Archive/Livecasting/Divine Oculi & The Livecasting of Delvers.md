## **"We watched as the fire consumed them—saw their last breaths, heard their final prayers. And yet, we could do nothing. But what of it? The people cheered. The crowd roared. They love the ones who survive, and they love the ones who die. All that matters is that they saw it."**

— **Luthas Veyne, Halvaskian Livecaster, 7E 215**

The adventurers who brave the Labyrinths are no longer just warriors, scholars, or seekers of lost power. They are spectacles—gods of entertainment, heroes of the common folk, or tragic victims whose deaths play out before an audience of millions.

At the heart of this phenomenon lies the Divine Oculi—arcane constructs of divination and illusion magic, created to livecast the dangers, glories, and horrors of the Labyrinths to the rest of the world.

These enchanted artifacts allow nobles, merchants, commoners, and rulers alike to witness the triumphs and failures of Delvers in real-time, turning the once-hidden dangers of the Labyrinths into the most popular spectacle in history.

---

## **The Divine Oculi – The Eyes of the World**

### **What Are Divine Oculi?**

A Divine Oculus (pl. Oculi) is a floating arcane eye, constructed from a combination of crystallized divination magic, eldritch engineering, and celestial essence. They serve as recording devices, livecasters, and arcane transmitters, sending real-time visual and auditory feeds from the depths of the Labyrinths to Viewhalls, private scrying chambers, and enchanted mirrors across the world.

- They float autonomously, following assigned Delvers at a safe distance.
- They are impervious to most conventional damage, though powerful anti-magic fields can disrupt them.
- They broadcast sights, sounds, and even emotions, immersing audiences in the experience.

### **How Are They Created?**

The creation of a Divine Oculus is an intricate process requiring expertise in both arcane engineering and divine magic. The Artificer-Priests of Halvask and certain arcane guilds control their production, ensuring that only approved Oculi are distributed.

- Each Oculus contains a shard of celestial crystal, capable of capturing and projecting real-time scrying images.
- They are enchanted with divination wards, allowing them to track, observe, and record events without direct interference.
- Some theories suggest that the most powerful Oculi are linked to the Astral Plane, making them capable of perceiving things beyond normal sight.

---

## **Livecasting: The Spectacle of the Labyrinths**

While the Labyrinths were once feared as deadly enigmas, the introduction of livecasting has transformed them into a worldwide obsession. Delvers, once regarded as foolhardy risk-takers or desperate scavengers, are now global celebrities, their every move watched, analyzed, and discussed.

### **How Livecasting Works**

The Oculi transmit their footage through powerful arcane relays, which are then distributed to major cities, scrying halls, noble estates, and magical broadcast centers. The most prominent Livecasting Halls exist in Halvask, where thousands gather to watch high-risk Delves in real time.

- Enchanted mirrors and crystal scrying devices allow commoners and nobles alike to tune in from anywhere.
- Magic-infused "Echo Chambers" project audio and emotion, allowing viewers to feel the intensity of the experience.
- Wealthy patrons often host private watchings, gambling on the outcomes of Delves and sponsoring favored adventurers.

---

## **The Livecasters – Architects of the Story**

### **Who Controls the Narrative?**

The raw footage from Divine Oculi is not always shown unaltered. Livecasters, individuals trained in storytelling, arcane media, and audience manipulation, oversee what is broadcast to the world.

- They weave narratives, highlighting heroes, villains, rivalries, and tragedies.
- They edit, enhance, and emphasize key moments, ensuring maximum emotional impact.
- They market Delvers as characters, turning some into beloved icons and others into cautionary tales.

### **The Role of Sponsorship**

Delvers who gain notoriety and popularity are often sponsored by noble houses, wealthy patrons, or corporate entities, receiving enchanted gear, funding, and influence in exchange for wearing sigils, delivering messages, or performing in a way that benefits their backers.

- Elite Delvers sign lucrative contracts, ensuring their survival and future wealth—if they live.
- Noble families invest in Delvers, hoping to associate their name with heroic conquests.
- Merchants and artisans sponsor fighters, ensuring that their weapons, potions, and enchantments are seen by millions.

The wealthiest Delvers become icons, with merchandise, statues, and even plays written in their honor—while the unfortunate ones are remembered only for the manner of their deaths.

---

## **The Cultural Impact of Livecasting**

With the rise of Livecasting, the world has changed.

### **A World Obsessed**

- Entire economies revolve around Labyrinth viewings. Taverns, gambling halls, and high-end scrying theaters attract millions of spectators for major Delves.
- Merchants sell replica weapons and clothing modeled after famous Delvers.
- Street performers reenact legendary Delves, turning real deaths into theater for entertainment.

### **Betting, Wagering, and Audience Influence**

- Gambling rings take bets on Delvers, with odds based on past performance, Labyrinth difficulty, and rival teams.
- Some nobles and high-tier patrons have begun influencing Delves, providing hidden assistance or sabotage to create more compelling spectacles.
- Spectator-driven challenges have emerged, with some Delvers accepting audience-voted trials or handicaps for greater rewards.

### **Ethical Controversies**

- Should a Delver’s suffering be entertainment? Some question whether watching people die for spectacle is moral—but the industry remains stronger than ever.
- What happens when Delvers refuse to perform? Certain wealthy patrons pressure Delvers into more dangerous missions, risking their lives for the sake of higher ratings.
- Do the Oculi see everything? Some suspect that the most powerful Divine Oculi can see beyond what is meant to be seen, glimpsing into hidden layers of reality, past and future alike.
