## **Recap**

The Grand Scrying Network broadcasted the spectacle of a lifetime as Velgrove Square erupted into a storm of fanfare, betting pools, and corporate sponsorships. The 21st Labyrinth—the Nameless Labyrinth—had appeared, and the world was watching.

The Delvers gathered in the square, surrounded by roaring crowds, flickering Divine Oculi, and the ever-watchful eyes of Declan Vael, the Grand Scrying Network’s most infamous personality. Interviews were given, odds were adjusted, and backroom deals were made.

Meanwhile, tension simmered between rival teams:

- **The Argent Banner**, backed by the Halvaskian military, stood tall and prepared.
- **The Crimson Reavers**, bloodthirsty mercenaries, sized up their competition.
- **The Veilborne Coven**, scholars and mystics, whispered amongst themselves.
- **The Lost Company**, a desperate band of underdogs, barely held their nerves.

Even our party wasn't able to avoid some brief rivalry, as *Vaelka "The Grin"* and the rest of the party slowly notched holes in each other's confidence. Or perhaps, garnered some respect for the Crimson Reaver in the process? 

While on stage, as the party was introduced, Kurk took it upon himself to investigate the pockets of *Declan Vael* as he took the attention of the crowd. Inside, Kurk found a strange note and an illegal *scrying eye*. The party pondered what he had these for, and on top of that, who was looking through the *scrying eye* to begin with? 

Shortly after the party was interviewed by *Milo Thorne*, a not well-known interviewer (or so it seemed) who took brief interest in their reasoning behind delving into the Labyrinth in the first place. Each with their own values, and their own respective answers, the party addressed the press and continued on their journey.

As the moment of entry arrived, the Halvaskian Delving Authority (HDA) issued their final warnings—once the doors closed, there would be no turning back. Yet, something felt off. The Divine Oculi glitched briefly, displaying a flickering silhouette that no one could explain.

Finally, the gates opened. The Delvers stepped inside, and with a slow, ominous finality… the entrance sealed behind them.

Inside, the party found themselves in an impossibly smooth stone corridor, bathed in the eerie glow of cold blue torches. The air was silent. Too silent. Three paths lay ahead:

- A long, straight hallway that stretched into darkness.
- A spiraling stone staircase, descending deeper into the unknown.
- A narrow passage, its walls subtly shifting when no one was looking.

When prayers were made for divine guidance, Kelemvor’s judgment fell swift and absolute, guiding the way forward, while Heironeous’ presence crackled with the promise of righteous battle.

Then, just as the party began to move, something shifted in the shadows.

Something else was here.
