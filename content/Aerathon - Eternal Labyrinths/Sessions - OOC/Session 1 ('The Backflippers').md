## **Recap**

The Grand Scrying Network broadcasted the spectacle of a lifetime as Velgrove Square erupted into a storm of fanfare, betting pools, and corporate sponsorships. The 21st Labyrinth—the Nameless Labyrinth—had appeared, and the world was watching.

The Delvers gathered in the square, surrounded by roaring crowds, flickering Divine Oculi, and the ever-watchful eyes of Declan Vael, the Grand Scrying Network’s most infamous personality. Interviews were given, odds were adjusted, and backroom deals were made.

While on stage, as the party was introduced, the three party members showed off their skills with stoicism, backflips, and flexing - successfully wooing the audience and perhaps some sponsorships for later. Even Declan appreciated the charismatic approach on the main stage. 

Perhaps from pure enthusiasm - or perhaps impatience - the group immediately set their eyes on getting to the Labyrinth's gate first. As they approached, they noticed that a number of Divine Oculi began to short-circuit and almost freeze - which is peculiar for such enhanced equipment. 

Togi and the other crew also found themselves neck and neck with *Captain Lirian Voss,* the HDA officer in charge of preparing delvers to be launcher into the Labyrinth, after Togi attempted to try and get behind the line of picketed soldiers. Thanks to Cap & Zahel's calming words, and the pure intention of the group to simply get inside, Lirian let them off the hook - expecting to never have to see them again. 

As the party approached the gate, they found themselves so overwhelmed with adventure and excitement, that they all needed to partake in a spontaneous backflipping entrance. In one fell launch, only Cap & Zahel were able to make it through. Yet this didn't stop Togi. With a brave yell and another double backflip, he found himself inside - but not in the right place. 

Zahel & Cap arrived to a an impossibly smooth stone corridor, bathed in the eerie glow of cold blue torches. The air was silent. Too silent. 

Togi was left behind. The entrance was gone. No crack in the stone, no seam where the doors had been. No sounds from the outside world. Just silence. And then—a new door. One that wasn’t there before. With no choice but to enter, Togi found himself in a narrow, spiraling passage, its walls rougher than the others—like something clawed through the rock to carve a path. Among another walkway, the sounds of his party members, an eerie darkness, and a seemingly important choice, Togi braved his way through this unknown place.

After taking the path forward, Togi reached a final barrier—one that pulsed like living flesh. He pushed through. And he reappeared before the party. The others didn’t see a door. From their perspective, he simply stepped out of a blank wall. Togi looked fine. But something was wrong. His fingernails were stained with something dark. It wouldn’t come off.

After a quick re-union, Three paths lay ahead:

- A long, straight hallway that stretched into darkness.
- A spiraling stone staircase, descending deeper into the unknown.
- A narrow passage, its walls subtly shifting when no one was looking.

Rather quickly, the group rushed down the Whispering Staircase. Then, just as the party began to make progress, something shifted in the shadows. Something else was here. As they cautiously maneuvered around a traps and what seemed to be something hidden - the group has now found them face to face with a rough outline of.... something....
