## **Overview**

Whether by nature or nurture, _Equines_ run. Their minds drift toward the horizon, stirred by a restlessness shared across horses and donkeys alike. Where some species build castles, equines are content to witness wonders in passing. Horses chase the wind with grace; donkeys carry burdens with stubborn will. Together, they are the long-road hearts of the Beast World.

Their powerful legs and sturdy builds make them ideal for long treks, swift charges, and holding lines that must not break. Though generally taller than most beastfolk, equines vary in size—horses especially, ranging up to 7’5”, while donkeys are more compact but no less enduring.

Equines are common sights in delver caravans, serving as scouts, muscle, or messengers. And when the Dungeon calls, they answer—not to conquer, but to keep moving forward.

## **Equine Traits**

**Creature Type.** You are a Humanoid.

**Ability Score Increase.** Your Wisdom score increases by 2.

**Speed.** Your walking speed is 35 feet.

**March On Hooves.** You have advantage on saving throws to avoid exhaustion from a forced march.

**Powerful Build.** You count as one size larger when determining your carrying capacity and the weight you can push, drag, or lift.

**Eyes on the Horizon.** You add your Wisdom modifier to initiative rolls.

---

### **Horse**

**Ability Score Increase.** Your Strength score increases by 1.

**Size.** You are Medium, ranging between 5’0” and 7’5”.

**Out of the Gate.** Your speed is doubled during the first round of combat.

**Sure-Footed Stomp.** You can move through a creature's space without it counting as difficult terrain if it's smaller than you or prone. When doing so, you may make an unarmed attack (1d4 + Strength bludgeoning). If it hits, the creature can’t make opportunity attacks against you this turn. You can't use this ability against the same target until your next turn.

---

### **Donkey**

**Ability Score Increase.** Your Constitution score increases by 1.

**Size.** You are Medium, usually between 5’3” and 5’10”.

**Unbroken Focus.** You have advantage on Constitution saving throws made to maintain concentration on spells.

**Sleepwalker.** You require only 4 hours of sleep for a long rest. While asleep, you may move up to your speed on your turn in a half-conscious state. While doing so, you have disadvantage on Perception checks and your speed becomes 0 if carrying more than half your capacity.

## **Homeland Traits**

### **Allemance**

Alley equines work the fields in spring and walk the land the rest of the year. Their memory for paths and their quiet vigilance form the backbone of the homeland’s unseen defenders.

**Hunter Legacy.** You have advantage on saving throws against being charmed.

---

### **Oria**

Oric equines are trailblazers between distant lodges, trained in harsh northern routes. Their instincts make them efficient hunters and essential lifelines in remote regions.

**Horseshoes in the Drifts.** You have advantage on Survival checks in arctic terrain. Snow and ice aren’t difficult terrain for you. You are proficient with javelins and have advantage on attacks made with them against creatures smaller than you.

---

### **Vinyot**

Religious and rebellious in equal measure, Vinyotian equines share lessons on Pirhoua’s mercy and ferry secret messages under holy guise. Their code is as sacred as their stories.

**Carriers of the Good Word.** You gain proficiency in Religion. You may hide coded messages inside a book's normal text. Teaching an ally to decode takes 1 hour. Discovering hidden text requires 1 hour and a DC 15 Investigation check. Decoding without a key requires DC 25.

---

### **Arneria**

The Donkey Post is legend in Arneria, and even noble colts grow up walking deliveries. Arnerian equines are defined by balance, endurance, and a relentless sense of purpose.

**Mail Runner.** When making a Dexterity saving throw or Acrobatics check to keep balance or avoid being knocked prone, treat a d20 roll of 9 or lower as 10. You have advantage on Wisdom checks made for navigation.

---

### **Al’Ar**

The open roads are absent on Al’ar’s islands, so equines there channel their restless energy into martial training. The Hammerhead Horses of Al’ar are known for breaking weapons and walls alike.

**Hammer Masters.** You gain proficiency with warhammers. When attacking objects with one, you deal double damage.
