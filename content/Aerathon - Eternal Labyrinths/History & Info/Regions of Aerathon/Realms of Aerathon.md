> _“Beyond Aerathon’s soil and sea lie other realities—fractured mirrors, dream pressures, inverted shadows. Delvers who cross these thresholds seldom return unchanged.”_

---

## **Overview**

The **Realms of Aerathon** are not continents nor kingdoms, but overlapping planes that brush against the waking world. Some are echoes of creation, some are prisons of subtraction, others are verdant mirrors of life and death. Delvers enter them through Labyrinth incursions, ritual doors, or accidents of fate. Each realm leaves its mark, and none can be crossed without cost.

---

## **The Shattersea** — _The Broken World That Came Before_

**Nature:** Ashen ruin, birthplace of humanity  
**Access Points:** Faulted portals, broken relics, Labyrinthic echo-bleeds  
**Inhabitants:** Ashborn humans, scarred dragons, cracked-machina, storm ghosts

The **Shattersea** is humanity’s origin—a world long broken, now a realm of drifting landmasses and poisoned sky. Its oceans are clotted with ash, its cities cracked into void-borne ruins. Long ago, humanity fled this place through the Pilgrimage, arriving in Aerathon. But the Shattersea is not dead. Its echoes bleed into Labyrinths, dragging back fragments of its history.

Delvers return with relics of impossible alloys, texts in shattered tongues, or scars that burn like coal. Ashborn refugees sometimes stumble through portals, hollow-eyed and desperate. Dragons once chained to humanity’s wars still drift the skies, blind and bitter. The Shattersea is not a realm to conquer—it is a reminder of what was lost, and of what might come again.

---

## **The Dreaming** — _Where Labyrinths Are Born_

**Nature:** Lucid chaos, proto-reality  
**Access Points:** Prolonged unconsciousness, magical trances, Astral sinkings  
**Inhabitants:** Seelie echoes, beast spirits, half-dreamed gods

The **Dreaming** is less a place than a pressure, a canvas of possibility where reality has not yet hardened. Artists, mad prophets, and sleeping delvers brush against it unbidden. Here, landscapes bend to whim, and names shift like rivers. It is said the Labyrinths gestate within the Dreaming before tearing through into Aerathon.

Those who linger too long forget their own histories, replacing them with dreams that feel just as true. Others awaken with fragments of impossible art or prophecies etched into their minds. Cults in Louvain and Pristana now whisper that the Dreaming is Aerathon’s true divine source, unshaped by gods, unbound by law.

## **The Wanehollow** — _Where Light Fears to Go_

**Nature:** Inverted void realm, source of the Voidblight  
**Access Points:** Failed rituals, forbidden Labyrinths, backfolds of reality  
**Inhabitants:** Hollow beasts, null-seraphs, inverted angels

The **Wanehollow** is the antithesis of creation, a place of subtraction and silence. Here, names unravel, memories decay, and even light rots into shadow. Labyrinths sometimes collapse inward into the Wanehollow, trapping entire delver crews in endless twilight.

Those who return speak of inverted cities made of bone-glass, seas of black dust, and angels who have fallen inside-out, their wings shedding silence instead of feathers. The Divine Oculi forbid expeditions here without anchoring tokens, for the Wanehollow does not merely consume flesh—it devours meaning itself.

And yet, the relics recovered from its depths are among the most sought-after in Aerathon: blades that erase memory, tomes that rewrite names, lanterns that burn with subtraction.

## **The Feywilds** — _The Verdant Mirror_

**Nature:** Overgrown reflection, untamed creation  
**Access Points:** Crossroads, ancient groves, Fey bargains, Verges of Ash  
**Inhabitants:** Archfey courts, beast spirits, living landscapes

The **Feywilds** are Aerathon’s echo of abundance. Forests grow thicker, rivers run brighter, and beasts stride as lords. The Feywilds are not stable geography—mountains sing, meadows wander, and castles move like hunters. The Archfey rule in courts of whim and wonder, their laws as fleeting as laughter.

Delvers who stray into the Feywilds may find themselves guests at a feast for a century, only to return to Aerathon a day later—or the reverse. The Feywilds bleed into Aerathon at places like the **Verges of Ash**, where its beauty mixes with Netherworld shadow. For some, it is paradise; for others, a gilded snare.

---

## **The Netherworld** — _The Shadow Below_

**Nature:** Realm of death, echo of the departed  
**Access Points:** Grave-sites, labyrinth ruptures, Nether rifts  
**Inhabitants:** Ghosts, revenants, ferrymen, shade-beasts

The **Netherworld** lies beneath the skin of Aerathon, a place where the dead linger. Unlike the Wanehollow, it is not absence, but echo—a half-life where memory and spirit coil together. Souls wander here when unclaimed by gods, or when pulled back by grief, vengeance, or magic.

Its geography mirrors Aerathon in strange ways—cities of ghosts where once-thriving ports stood, rivers of shadow flowing alongside Aerathon’s true waters. Ferrymen drift its banks, offering passage at impossible costs. Delvers who trespass find relics heavy with sorrow, and sometimes bring back shades that do not belong.

The Netherworld is feared, yes—but also relied upon. Countless rituals, bargains, and spells of Aerathon are drawn from its twilight. To walk its paths is dangerous, but to ignore it is to deny half of life itself.
