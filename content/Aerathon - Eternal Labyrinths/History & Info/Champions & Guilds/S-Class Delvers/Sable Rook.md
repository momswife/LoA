## **Overview**

**Sable Rook**, the **Warden of Borrowed Tomorrows**, is an S-Class delver whose art turns fate into finance. A former Louvain court-scrivener gone labyrinth-banker, Sable practices **temporal forensics** and **harm-deferment magic**—he “escrows” injuries, disasters, and curses into binding contracts payable later, then collects those debts from monsters, warlocks, and (when necessary) the overly adventurous. To the Ministry, he’s a miracle accountant of risk; to criminals and cults, he’s the repo man of destiny.

Rook is famously polite, relentlessly punctual, and **painfully literal**. He will not step into a room without first naming it (“_This threshold is hereby entered_”), and he refuses to kill when a lien will do. Between the Arcane Courts and the Labyrinths’ own hungry mathematics, Sable Rook has saved countless lives by **buying everyone time**—and then making sure time gets paid back.

## **Notable Accomplishments**

**The Stay of Execution at Portico Gate**  
When a collapsing stair-helix threatened to grind a refugee column into gravel, Rook **escrowed the kinetic force**—binding it into a signed iron chit he nailed to the wall. The stairs held. He later **collected the debt** by luring a marrow-wyrm to crash into the marked masonry during a sanctioned hunt. Two problems solved, ledger balanced.

**The Betemere Annuity**  
A cult had ringed a village with a **slow-burn blight** set to bloom at first snow. Rook amortized the curse across a hundred harmless events—dropped plates, stubbed toes, snapped broomstraw—**bleeding the doom to zero**. He then found the cult’s treasurer and made them sign an admission of fault in their own blood… diluted to nonlethal levels, of course.

**The Masque of Mirrors in Louvain**  
During a contested delve, rival S-Rank operatives used illusory duplicates to mask lethal intent. Rook **audited the illusions**: each false self was forced to pay a tithe in breath and heartbeat. The mirror-gang wheezed, their glamours popped, and arrests proceeded **without a death**.

## **Equipment and Artifacts**

**Ledger of Atonement**  
A black-bound codex that **records injuries, debts, and bargains** as luminous script. Any entry can be invoked as a **binding ritual**: damage can be **deferred**, **refinanced**, or **reassigned** to the truly liable party (the monster, the trapmaker, the curse). Attempts to burn pages result in a fine cough and a fine—both collectible.

**Gavel-Hourglass**  
A double-ended timepiece set inside a magistrate’s mallet. **Inverts** to grant a 6-second **stay of effect** for anything just triggered (poison needle, falling portcullis, fireburst). Slamming the gavel **finalizes** the stay—either nullifying it by payment (spell slots, hit dice, coin, favor) or letting the danger **fall on a marked sigil** elsewhere.

**Basilisk-Skin Retainer’s Cloak**  
Grey-green scales stitched into courtly cut. **Advantage on concentration** against forced contracts and glamour. Once per short rest, can **“stone-liquidate”** one nonliving object into escrow, storing it as a weighty IOU that reconstitutes on command.
