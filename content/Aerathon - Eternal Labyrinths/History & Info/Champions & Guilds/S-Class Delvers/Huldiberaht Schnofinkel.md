## **Overview**

**Huldiberaht Schnofinkel**, an infamous S-Class delver of terrible renown, was a human druid whose command of nature was matched only by his **gleeful cruelty**. He is most remembered for his signature spell—_**"Earthen Meteorform"**_—a twisted blend of wildshape, gravitational magic, and elemental enhancement that allowed him to **launch himself skyward and come crashing down with devastating force**, obliterating monsters, delvers, and structures alike.

Once a fringe scholar of druidic convergence theory, Huldiberaht quickly turned to **delving not for balance, but domination**. He believed the Labyrinths were tests meant to separate “the bloom from the blight,” and took it upon himself to be nature’s bludgeon. Many rival delvers who insulted or challenged him ended up as mulch beneath cratered stones. Yet to the Arcane Courts’ frustration, he never once violated the **laws of delving protocol.**

That’s because Huldiberaht didn't break rules. He simply **flattened them.**

## **Notable Accomplishments**

**The Pummeling of Cragspire Bastion**  
During a heavily contested delve between three guilds, Huldiberaht soared nearly 200 feet into the air using enhanced Druidic momentum, then **transformed mid-flight into a stone-plated badger**, crushing an entire chamber of rival delvers in one impact. He then demanded they “name the spell after him.” They did.

**The Root-Flood Trap at Oathgrove**  
Huldiberaht lured a corrupted guardian beast into a naturally unstable cave system, then collapsed the terrain via a series of earth-rending hops, shattering stalactites and pinning the creature. When a party of young delvers tried to “rescue” the beast for study, he crushed them, declaring: _“Sentiment is rot in bloom.”_

**The Court of Flattened Blades**  
Once challenged to a tribunal combat by a noble paladin. Huldiberaht stood still for the entire first round. Then leapt into the clouds and came down so hard that the arena had to be rebuilt. The paladin survived. His pride did not.

## **Equipment and Artifacts**

**Stonegut Totem**  
A gnarled oaken icon embedded in his stomach, fused with druidic ironwood. Channels gravity-based elemental magic. Enhances falling-based spells and wildshapes. Emits seismic pulses with each impact.

**Mantle of Mossbone**  
A cloak of living ivy and petrified wolf-bark. Regrows over time. Grants resistance to kinetic force damage and reduces vertical impact trauma—to **him**, not to others.

**Sigil of Crushing Bloom**  
An etched tattoo across his back. Glows whenever he performs “Earthen Meteorform.” The sigil cracks further with each use—believed to be linked to a deeper force that slowly consumes his soul.
