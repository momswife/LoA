## **Overview**

**Jon Average**—registered on every form as **“Jonathan A. Average”**—is an S-Class delver from Louvain whose single defining traits are **punctuality, compliance, and unremarkableness**. He brings his lunch. He files his reports. He wears a hi-vis tabard when appropriate. And somehow, impossibly, **Labyrinths rearrange themselves into service corridors** when he steps inside.

Jon does not banter, posture, or grandstand. He **follows the posted procedure**, keeps an eye on the clock, and **escorts civilians to the nearest marked exit** with the calm of a librarian shelving books. Monsters surrender, traps present warning placards, and cursed machinery develops comprehensible manuals. No one knows why. The Ministry calls it **Baseline Conformance Field**; Jon calls it **“doing what we’re supposed to do.”**

He is, by every account, the most **boring** S-Class on record—and one of the **most effective**.

## **Notable Accomplishments**

**The Eight-Hour Clear of the Nameless Annex**  
A shifting sub-labyrinth notorious for time dilation collapsed into a tidy loop under Jon’s **baseline stride**. He **clocked in at 07:55**, labeled hazards with chalk, walked civilians out in single file, and **clocked out at 16:01**. Post-incident report: “_Nothing unusual to note._”

**The Compliance of the Basilisk Foundry**  
A basilisk-haunted forge kept petrifying crews. Jon posted a **laminated “Eye Protection Required” sign**, issued smoked goggles, and read the introduction of the manual **aloud**. The basilisks proceeded to **queue**, accepted tags, and turned their gaze to approved angles during operation. Output met quota by Thursday.

**The Quieting of the Wanehollow Stair**  
A haunted switchback that ate sound and hope re-painted its risers when Jon opened a tin of **approved yellow**. With each stripe, moans softened to **OSHA-level hum**, the handrail installed itself, and the ghost foreman signed off the site. Jon swept the landings.

## **Equipment and Artifacts**

**Hi-Vis Tabard of Ordinary Authority**  
A reflective vest with stitched badge **“Operations.”** While worn, hostile entities must make a **Wisdom save** to ignore a **lawful, safe instruction** (e.g., _“Step back behind the line,” “Single file, please”_). Failure compels **orderly compliance** for one round.

**Punchcard of Adherence**  
A brass-edged timecard that **stamps reality**. When Jon “punches in,” environmental effects within 30 feet **seek posted procedure**: fires look for extinguishers, doors look for hinges, puzzles surface **plain-language instructions**.

**Thermos of Lukewarm**  
A dented steel thermos filled with perpetually **lukewarm tea**. Drinking grants **immunity to panic, charm, and fear** for 10 minutes and **advantage on concentration** to maintain mundane tasks under duress. Refills at first break.

**Tape of the True Line**  
A chalk-line reel and tape measure. Snapping a line creates a **30-foot safe lane** that suppresses **randomization, illusion drift, and slippery surfaces**. Monsters find it **awkward** to cross; supervisors find it **gratifying**.
