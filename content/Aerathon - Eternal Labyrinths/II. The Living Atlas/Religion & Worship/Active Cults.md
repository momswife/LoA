## **I. Overview**

As mainstream worship becomes increasingly cultural and delvers grow more secular, fringe religious movements have grown in number, brazenness, and ideological intensity.  
These groups—commonly referred to as **cults** by the Ministry—take advantage of:

- weakened guild–temple alliances,
    
- declining clerical presence in delver life,
    
- a public shift toward symbolic rather than doctrinal faith,
    
- labyrinth-induced trauma,
    
- desperation for meaning,
    
- and gaps in community oversight.
    

Most cults remain small, but several have caused disruptions ranging from ritual vandalism to unauthorized labyrinth incursions.

This dossier catalogs the Ministry’s current list of **active, monitored, or suspected cults operating within Aerathon**.

---

## **II. Classification of Cult Activity**

The Ministry recognizes five primary categories of cult behavior:

1. **Devotional Extremists** – fanatical worship of a single deity
    
2. **Apocalyptic or Eschatonic Orders** – groups claiming knowledge of impending cataclysm
    
3. **Labyrinth Mystics** – groups claiming the labyrinths themselves are divine
    
4. **Forbidden Arcanists** – those blending divine belief with illegal arcane practices
    
5. **Heritage Revivals** – groups resurrecting religions considered extinct or socially destabilizing
    

The following sections document notable examples.

# **III. Notable Active Cults**

## **1. The Bleeding Garden — Loviatar Extremists**

**Category:** Devotional Extremists  
**Status:** Active, scattered cells  
**Practices:**

- ritual pain bonding
    
- recruitment of emotionally vulnerable delvers
    
- underground trial chambers
    

**Notes:**  
Attracts delvers coping with trauma, offering pain as “clarity.”  
Extremely dangerous; Ministry recommends immediate dispersal of gatherings.

---

## **2. The Children of True Night — Sharic Nadaists**

**Category:** Eschatonic Order  
**Status:** Controlled but growing  
**Practices:**

- grief cult centered on Shar
    
- rejection of the sun (“the false light”)
    
- obsession with emotional collapse as enlightenment
    

**Notes:**  
Popular among delvers suffering labyrinth-induced PTSD.  
Their philosophy encourages emotional isolation and disconnection from guilds.

---

## **3. House of Seven Whispers — Leiran Illusionists**

**Category:** Forbidden Arcanists  
**Status:** Actively monitored  
**Practices:**

- blending minor illusioncraft with false-prophecy
    
- identity-dissociation rituals
    
- recruitment through anonymity promises (“Become no one”)
    

**Notes:**  
Mostly harmless but occasionally implicated in identity fraud rings.

---

## **4. The Tide-Taken — Umberlee Devotional Order**

**Category:** Devotional Extremists  
**Status:** Regionally active (coastal)  
**Practices:**

- drowning trials
    
- ocean fealty rites
    
- targeted vandalism against ships
    

**Notes:**  
Not widely supported; sailors often petition the Ministry for greater crackdowns.

---

## **5. The Fanged Dawn — Malarite Blood Sect**

**Category:** Heritage Revival  
**Status:** Rising in frontier regions  
**Practices:**

- violent hunt rituals
    
- territorialism
    
- transformation fetishism (“the beast within”)
    

**Notes:**  
Their presence has grown as delvers abandon traditional rites.  
Some cells have attempted unauthorized labyrinth “beast hunts.”

---

## **6. The Perfect Line — Talos Performance Cult**

**Category:** Eschatonic / Labyrinth Mystic Hybrid  
**Status:** Expanding  
**Practices:**

- storm-chasing
    
- ritualized “death dances” at cliff edges
    
- attempts to provoke “the Storm Father's attention”
    

**Notes:**  
A cult of thrill-seekers; many delvers drift into it after burnout.

---

## **7. The Order of the Obsidian Eye — Old Pantheon Revivalists**

**Category:** Heritage Revival  
**Status:** Unconfirmed, long-term monitoring  
**Practices:**

- resurrection of Old Beastfolk rites
    
- obsession with jackalfolk lineages
    
- rumors of funerary judgment reenactments
    

**Notes:**  
Ministry considers this cult low-risk but high-interest.  
Their existence is debated, but sightings recur every decade.

---

## **8. The Lotus-Eclipse Covenant — Bhaalic Death Seekers**

**Category:** Devotional Extremists  
**Status:** Highly dangerous  
**Practices:**

- ritual assassination
    
- “purity killings”
    
- worship of cyclical rebirth through violence
    

**Notes:**  
Thought extinct.  
Recently resurfaced in eastern Vinyot.

---

## **9. The Tide of Silver — Waukeen Paramilitary Mercantile Sect**

**Category:** Economic Cult  
**Status:** Monitored quietly (sensitive)  
**Practices:**

- belief that profit is divine will
    
- economic domination strategies
    
- ritualized auctions treated as worship
    

**Notes:**  
Not overtly violent but destabilizing in trade hubs.  
Politically protected in some cities.

---

## **10. The Order of Amber Silence — Mystra Fragmentarians**

**Category:** Arcane Extremists  
**Status:** Fragmented  
**Practices:**

- belief that magic fragments are alive
    
- whisper-listening rituals
    
- attempts to “feed” arcanotech devices divine energy
    

**Notes:**  
Increasing incidents reported near labyrinth spillovers.

---

## **11. The Vein-Singers — Labyrinth Mystics**

**Category:** Labyrinth Mystics  
**Status:** Active in ruins and spillover zones  
**Practices:**

- treating breach-lines as holy texts
    
- chanting to harmonic frequencies
    
- painting murals of “the sleeping god beneath”
    

**Notes:**  
Not violent but often trespass into restricted MDO zones.

---

## **12. The Golden Maw — Tiamatic Smuggling Ring**

**Category:** Forbidden Arcanists  
**Status:** Active  
**Practices:**

- worship of a draconic prosperity myth
    
- hoarding relics
    
- running black-market treasure vaults
    

**Notes:**  
Religious devotion is largely performative; profit motivates them.

---

## **13. The Dawn Without a Sun — Anti-Lathander Movement**

**Category:** Eschatonic Philosophical Cult  
**Status:** Emerging  
**Practices:**

- claiming the “sun has abandoned Aerathon”
    
- advocating spiritual nihilism
    
- hosting “lightless rites”
    

**Notes:**  
Appeals to disillusioned delvers and young urbanites.

---

## **14. The Choir of the Last Breath — Kelemvor Deviants**

**Category:** Devotional Extremists  
**Status:** Troubling  
**Practices:**

- ritual near-death experiences
    
- mock funerals
    
- fascination with “meeting the Judge early”
    

**Notes:**  
Often overlaps with delver burnout and trauma.

---

## **15. The Gilded Thorn — Ilmatari Pain-Hedonism Splinter**

**Category:** Forbidden Sect  
**Status:** Banned  
**Practices:**

- distortion of Ilmater’s doctrine
    
- exaltation of suffering, not endurance
    
- scarification rites
    

**Notes:**  
Clerical councils reject them fully.

# **IV. Why Cults Flourish in the Age of Secular Delvers**

The Ministry identifies five systemic conditions enabling cult growth:

1. **Loss of traditional structure in delver life**
    
2. **Decreased clerical presence in guild halls**
    
3. **Rising loneliness and stress among delvers**
    
4. **Labyrinth-induced trauma**
    
5. **Vacuum left by fading belief in conventional worship**
    

Where faith loses structure, fringe ideologies fill the gaps.
