### _A Fallen Faith of Desert Crowns and Jackal Kings_

## **I. Overview**

Long before the rise of guilds, the spread of the Dawn Pantheon, or the first Ministry charters, the Beastfolk of Aerathon followed a powerful and disciplined religion centered around the **Old Gods**—the pantheon now recognized as belonging to the **Jackal Dynasties**, **River Kingdoms**, and the **Sun-Barrow Cultures**.

Today, that religion is effectively **extinct**.

Not outlawed.  
Not erased.  
Simply… forgotten.

Outside of scholarly study and a handful of rumored hidden sects, the Old Beastfolk Religion survives only in:

- architectural remnants,
    
- funerary motifs,
    
- idioms passed down unconsciously,
    
- and the haunted prestige of the Jackal race.
    

This dossier examines how a once-mighty faith vanished, and why some believe a few still keep its embers alive.

---

## **II. The Ancient Faith in Summary**

The Old Beastfolk Religion honored a pantheon now associated with:

- **Re-Horakhty** — the Solar Crown, ruler of the divine
    
- **Anubis** — judge of souls
    
- **Osiris** — lord of the eternal kingdom
    
- **Bast, Sobek, Set, Ptah, Isis, Thoth** and others who shaped nature, magic, death, craft, and fate
    

In ancient cultures, worship was highly structured, emphasizing:

- cosmic order
    
- ritual purity
    
- ancestral loyalty
    
- sacred rulership
    
- the journey of the soul
    

The Jackalfolk in particular held unique religious authority, serving as judges, mortuary priesthoods, and keepers of divine law.

To many ancient Beastfolk peoples, **the Dawn Pantheon did not yet exist.**  
The Old Gods were the only gods.

---

## **III. Why the Religion Declined**

Historians identify four primary pressures that extinguished the ancient faith.

---

### **1. The Quiet Edict**

When the Edict severed divine intervention, the Old Gods—whose rites depended on visible signs and precise metaphysical response—grew silent.

Their worship structures had **no adaptive form** for a mute cosmos.

Whereas the Dawn Pantheon evolved through metaphor, art, and culture, the Old Faith withered without divine acknowledgment.

---

### **2. The Dispersal of Beastfolk Civilizations**

Major Beastfolk nations fractured during:

- internal migrations,
    
- environmental changes,
    
- predatory expansion by early human kingdoms,
    
- and labyrinth accidents that severed trade routes.
    

Without centralized temples and hereditary priesthoods, the religion shattered region by region.

---

### **3. The Rise of the Modern Pantheon**

The Dawn Pantheon spread rapidly due to:

- flexible worship practices
    
- clerical orders adapting to urban life
    
- multi-species applicability
    
- universal symbolism
    
- MDO-era integration into civic infrastructure
    

The Old Religion, rooted heavily in Beastfolk cosmology, simply lost its cultural foothold.

---

### **4. Historical Rebranding**

Over centuries, symbols of the Old Gods became:

- aesthetic motifs
    
- architectural curiosities
    
- children's tales
    
- delver superstitions
    
- marketplace trinkets
    
- university lecture topics
    

Its sacred meaning dissolved into decoration.

Forgotten gods fade fastest.

---

## **IV. How the Old Faith Appears Today**

Though the religion is gone, traces survive across Aerathon.

### **1. Burial Customs**

Many Beastfolk still wrap the dead in linen or bury them with carved feather-shapes—a faint echo of Osiris and Anubis.

### **2. Idioms & Sayings**

Phrases like “May the Scales Favor You” or “Sun Above, Earth Below” trace back to ancient worship.

### **3. Artifacts in Circulation**

Delvers occasionally uncover relic-imitations shaped after the Old Gods, though these are rarely actual relics.

### **4. Architecture**

Sun-barrel temples, jackal statues, lotus-carved columns—once sacred, now historic curiosities.

### **5. Ritual Gestures**

Some Beastfolk instinctively touch two fingers to the brow when speaking of death—a remnant of the Anubite Invocation.

### **6. Academic Reconstruction**

Universities maintain Old Pantheon research departments (usually small and underfunded).

---

## **V. Rumors of Surviving Followers**

Though the Old Faith is considered extinct, the Ministry has logged recurring reports of:

---

### **1. The Dust-Cloaked Courts**

Claims of desert-dwelling jackalfolk who still weigh the hearts of criminals in symbolic judgment rites.

### **2. The River Mothers**

Small Beastfolk enclaves who leave offerings of milk and lotus at dawn—an Isis ritual.

### **3. The Crocodile Lodges**

Fishermen who swear by Sobek and tattoo their arms with horned crocodile heads, reportedly as protection charms.

### **4. The Order of the Obsidian Eye**

Whispered to guard tombs, ruins, and ancient labyrinth entrances.  
No confirmed membership.

### **5. The Solar Choir**

A wandering group of singers who chant in a language nobody has fully translated; some lyrics match archived hymns of Re-Horakhty.

### **6. Jackalfolk Solitaries**

A few reclusive jackalfolk—rare, long-lived, and deeply private—who refuse comment on their ancestry or beliefs.

The Ministry notes these claims but lists them as **Low Confidence** unless supported by Echo-Law analysis.

---

## **VI. Why the Religion Never Fully Died (Culturally)**

Despite extinction, the Old Faith remains compelling because:

- its imagery is powerful,
    
- its rites evoke mystery,
    
- its gods feel mythic and ancient,
    
- and Beastfolk cultures still feel the void of a once-unified identity.
    

The Jackalfolk’s lingering mystique keeps interest alive—especially among delvers, occult researchers, and pseudo-historical enthusiasts.

Some scholars speculate that if the Old Gods responded again—even faintly—the religion could resurge overnight.

---

## **VII. Ministry Assessment**

|Category|Status|
|---|---|
|**Active Worship**|Near-zero (public)|
|**Cultural Residue**|Moderate, unconscious|
|**Rumored Sects**|Low-to-moderate confidence|
|**Political Influence**|Negligible|
|**Archaeological Value**|High|
|**Delver Interest**|Growing (caution advised)|

The Ministry advises caution when encountering modern claimants to Old Pantheon rites; while most are harmless enthusiasts, some have used lost-era symbolism to justify criminal behavior or relic hoarding.

The Old Beastfolk Religion does not guide Aerathon today—  
but neither is it gone.  
It sleeps, quiet as desert stone, waiting for the right memory to awaken it.
