# **Choir-Bone Angels**

### _Undead Remnants of Forgotten Hymns — “Voices That Rise From Empty Ribs.”_

**Filed Division:** Ministry of Delving Operations — _The Living Atlas_ (Undead, Echoed & Forgotten Entity Archive)  
**Primary Compiler:** Priest-Analyst Thera Daan (Death-Liturgical Phenomena & Echo-Afterlife Studies)  
**Advisory Editors:** Archivist Yelna Stonevoice (Echo-Law Historian), Lt. Salvia Korrin (Arcane Breach Liaison), Inspector Jao “Greyglass” Vell (Forensic Thanatomancy)  
**Date of Filing:** 3734 A.D.  
**Document Class:** _Echo-Necrotic Entity Record / Grade-IV Hazard_

---

## **I. Overview**

**Choir-Bone Angels** are undead constructs formed from the intermingling of **forgotten hymns**, **abandoned divine resonance**, and **skeletal remains** of long-dead clerics, choristers, or healed martyrs.  
They are not true angels, nor typical undead — but **echoes of holy songs trapped in bone**, seeking completion of a hymn no mortal remembers.

They emerge in ruins of old temples, beneath collapsed sanctuaries, or in labyrinth chambers once used for prayer or healing. Their creation is spontaneous: a divine resonance too powerful to die, clinging to bones that cannot sing.

The Ministry classifies them as **Type-UNF / Echo-Necrotic Sacral Aberrations**, among the most dangerous “holy undead” currently documented.

> _“A Choir-Bone Angel does not serve a god.  
> It serves a memory of worship, half-rotted and half-divine.”_  
> — Stonevoice

---

## **II. Physical Description**

|**Attribute**|**Detail**|
|---|---|
|**Height**|6–9 ft|
|**Body**|Tall skeletal form woven with luminous tendon-like glyph strings|
|**Skull**|Elongated, halo-shaped cracks; jaw fused partially open in a perpetual “singing” posture|
|**Wings**|Not flesh — bone spines wrapped in spectral hymn-light|
|**Aura**|Somber harmony of layered voices echoing from nowhere|
|**Movement**|Slow in stillness, impossibly fast when reacting to sound|
Choir-Bone Angels glow faintly with **pale gold or blue radiance**, but the light pulses off-tempo, flickering like a heart struggling to beat.

The gaps between their ribs emit soft whisper-songs in dead languages.

---

## **III. Behavior & Phenomena**

Choir-Bone Angels respond to **sound**, especially singing, humming, sobbing, or whispered prayer. They interpret sound not as communication, but as **unfinished hymns**.

**Documented Behaviors:**

- **Harmonic Pursuit:** Move toward anyone making sound, believing them to be part of the hymn.
    
- **Voice-Collection:** Absorb vocal essence from the living; victims become mute for hours or days.
    
- **Resonance Overload:** Emit sonic blasts that crack stone, shatter glass, and rupture eardrums.
    
- **Soul-Recall:** Briefly revive memories of the dead associated with the location.
    
- **False Benediction:** Press skeletal hands over a target’s heart, inducing calm… and then cardiac arrest.
    

> _“They kill by comforting you.  
> Your body relaxes so deeply it forgets to live.”_  
> — Daan

Choir-Bone Angels are not cruel — they are hopelessly confused, trying to reconstruct a divine harmony long since lost.

---

## **IV. Habitat & Occurrence**

They manifest where **faith died violently** or **prayers went unanswered**:

- Sunken chapels in **Namira’s storm-flood plains**
    
- Deserted healing houses overtaken by Blightborn Seraph rot
    
- Abandoned labyrinth sanctuaries used during ancient pilgrimages
    
- Ruined temples in the Blackwild smothered by cursed vines
    
- Collapsed cathedrals of Louvain built atop echo-active leylines
    

Most appear only at **dusk**, **midnight**, or **moonrise**, when divine resonance wanes.

---

## **V. Ministry Observations**

- Echo-Law mapping reveals Choir-Bone Angels contain **fractured hymn-patterns**, fragments of once-powerful divine spells.
    
- Their presence increases local **emotional sensitivity**, especially grief, guilt, and nostalgia.
    
- Lt. Korrin reports that Choir-Bone Angels will halt aggression to listen to a perfectly pitched note — as if seeking recognition.
    
- When two Angels meet, they attempt to harmonize; the resulting dissonance often triggers area-wide sonic quakes.
    
- Bone dust around their emergence sites retains faint blessings twisted into harmful side-effects.


> _“They are the aftertaste of faith — bitter, longing, and far from holy.”_  
> — Stonevoice

## **VI. Weakness & Delving Considerations**

|**Vulnerability**|**Explanation / Practical Note**|
|---|---|
|**Discordant Noise**|Off-key singing or sudden dissonant chords repel or stun them.|
|**Silent Wards**|They cannot perceive beings inside zones of magical silence.|
|**Salt & Iron**|Break hymn-light bindings; disrupt skeletal integrity.|
|**True Prayer**|Genuine, heartfelt prayer (any deity) calms the entity.|
**Delver Advisory:**  
Do **not** attempt to harmonize with them.  
Matching their pitch binds your voice to their hymn, causing vocal bleed or “choir assimilation.”

## **IX. Cultural & Mythic Notes**

- **Clerics of Lathander:** Fear them as “the sunrise that never came.”
    
- **Namiran Sailors:** Leave chimes at ship prows to distract them during fog crossings.
    
- **Oria Rangers:** Claim they “sing for the moon’s dead children.”
    
- **Delvers:** Believe that an Angel appearing before a delve is a warning that someone in the party will lose their voice — metaphorically or literally.
    

> _“They mean no harm.  
> But they have forgotten what harm is.”_  
> — Daan

---

## **X. Summary**

**Choir-Bone Angels** are haunting, tragic remnants of worship — skeletons animated by the echoes of divine hymns long erased from Aerathon’s memory.  
They seek completion, harmony, and meaning, but their broken divine resonance twists these desires into lethal behaviors.

They are the undead chorus of forgotten prayers:  
beautiful, sorrowful, and deadly.

Their song once healed the world.  
Now it breaks it.
