# **Snow-Echo Phantoms**

### _Cold-Born Echoes of the Lost — “Where Footprints Fade, But Voices Do Not.”_

**Filed Division:** Ministry of Delving Operations — _The Living Atlas_ (Undead, Echoed & Forgotten Entity Archive)  
**Primary Compiler:** Archivist Yelna Stonevoice (Echo-Law Historian & Emotional Residue Specialist)

**Date of Filing:** 3734 A.D.  
**Document Class:** _Cryo-Echo Undead Record / Grade-III Hazard_

---

## **I. Overview**

**Snow-Echo Phantoms** are spectral manifestations that form where **death, memory, and deep winter converge**.  
They are not ghosts, nor are they illusions — but fragments of emotion, regret, and unfinished purpose captured in snow-laden air and given the faintest semblance of form.

Whenever a person dies in **whiteout conditions**, especially in isolation, their final emotional signature can imprint upon the storm. Over days or years, the blizzard replays that feeling — grief, fear, longing, resolve — until an echo forms.  
And then the echo learns to walk.

They are classified as **Type-UNF / Cryo-Echo Aberrants**, subtle but dangerous due to emotional influence and unpredictable manifestation.

> _“They are winter rehearsing tragedy.”_ — Daan

---

## **II. Physical Description**

|**Attribute**|**Detail**|
|---|---|
|**Shape**|Rough humanoid silhouette of snow-dust and drifting frost|
|**Eyes**|Two cold flickers of pale blue or white light; not true eyes|
|**Body**|Vaguely defined; limbs trail off into swirling particulate|
|**Movement**|Silent gliding; often appears as if carried by wind|
|**Voice**|Thin, breathless echoes of the last words spoken near their deathsite|
|**Aura**|Sharp cold, increased melancholy, auditory distortion|
Snow-Echo Phantoms often flicker between forms — sometimes looking vaguely like the deceased, sometimes like snow-sculptures imitating life.

---

## **III. Behavior & Phenomena**

Snow-Echo Phantoms are not malevolent by intention. Their danger lies in **emotional resonance** and **storm-bound compulsion**.

**Observed Behaviors:**

- **Memory Repetition:** They reenact gestures, phrases, or movements from the moment of death.
    
- **Cold Lure:** Emit soft cries or familiar voices to lure travelers off safe paths.
    
- **Frost Echo:** Cause auditory illusions — footsteps behind the listener, distant calls for help.
    
- **Storm Shepherding:** Intensify blizzard conditions around themselves, extending visibility loss.
    
- **Clinging Grief:** Touch induces overwhelming sadness, guilt, or memories not one’s own.
    

> _“They’re not hunting you.  
> They’re asking you to finish their sentence.”_  
> — Stonevoice

Most Phantoms dissipate at dawn, but some persist through entire winters if their emotional imprint was strong enough.

---

## **IV. Habitat & Occurrence**

Snow-Echo Phantoms appear in regions with sustained winter or heavy storm activity, especially where isolation is common:

- **Nattefrost** tundra fields
    
- **Frost-Mane Elk migration routes**
    
- **High mountain passes in the Betemeres**
    
- **Tempestrise altitude freeze pockets**
    
- **Collapsed Wayfarer shrines buried in drifts**
    
- **Louvain’s northern expedition ruins**
    

The probability increases exponentially during:  
• blizzards  
• moonless nights  
• emotionally charged events (mass deaths, disappearances)

---

## **V. Ministry Observations**

- Echo-lens analysis confirms Phantoms hold **fragments of memory**, not souls.
    
- Their presence slightly alters snowflake geometry around them, forming repeating sigils or patterns linked to their origin.
    
- Lt. Korrin encountered a Phantom that mimicked her own voice from a past mission; she confirmed this was **not** a haunting, but mirror-echo projection.
    
- Vell measured their temperature field: a Phantom decreases ambient warmth as if absorbing emotional heat, not physical.
    
- Phantoms can inadvertently “collect” additional memories over time, forming complex emotional mosaics.
    

> _“A Phantom that has seen too many winters becomes a storm wearing a face.”_  
> — Vell

## **VI. Weakness & Delving Considerations**

|**Vulnerability**|**Explanation / Practical Note**|
|---|---|
|**Direct Heat**|Fire spells, torches, or body warmth destabilize their cohesion.|
|**Sunlight**|Nearly all dissipate instantly at sunrise.|
|**Emotional Grounding**|Strong communal emotions counteract their grief-field.|
|**Name Calling**|Speaking the name of the deceased can calm and disperse them.|
**Delver Advisory:**  
Avoid traveling alone in snow-bound regions; Phantoms mimic voices of companions to isolate targets.

Touching a Phantom’s core can induce **memory frostbite** — a psychic cold that erases short-term recollection until warmed magically.

## **IX. Cultural & Mythic Notes**

- **Nattefrost Nomads:** Leave lanterns in snowbanks to “guide the lost to rest.”
    
- **Blackwild Shamans:** Call them “Winter’s Ghost-Breath,” claiming they show the forest what it has forgotten.
    
- **Delver Folklore:** If a Phantom echoes your voice, it means the storm fears you will die there too.
    
- **Oria Storytellers:** Teach children never to answer voices in the snow unless the speaker’s shadow is visible.
    

> _“They aren’t warnings.  
> They’re reminders — that someone out there never made it home.”_  
> — Daan

---

## **X. Summary**

**Snow-Echo Phantoms** are haunting embodiments of winter’s memory — fragile, sorrow-filled echoes of those lost to the cold.  
They replay final moments, mimic familiar voices, and follow invisible patterns of grief woven into the storm.

Tragic rather than wicked, they are dangerous only because they **confuse longing for contact with guidance into death.**

They seek warmth, closure, and remembrance:

**Ghosts not of people — but of their last thoughts.**
