# **Glass-Eyed Pilgrims**

### _Wanderers Claimed by Forgotten Reflections — “When a Journey Outlives the Traveler.”_

**Filed Division:** Ministry of Delving Operations — _The Living Atlas_ (Undead, Echoed & Forgotten Entity Archive)  
**Primary Compiler:** Archivist Yelna Stonevoice (Echo-Law Historian & Dissonant Memory Research)

## **I. Overview**

**Glass-Eyed Pilgrims** are undead wanderers whose souls have been partially absorbed by **reflective memory phenomena** — cursed mirrors, echo-lakes, broken cathedral glass, or remnants of the Shatterfall dust left by the Glassfolk.

They resemble travelers on an endless pilgrimage: cloaked, weary, and dragging packs full of relics no one recognizes. But their eyes are unmistakable:

Two perfect, unmoving discs of **glass**, each containing shifting reflections of moments that are _not their own._

They roam Aerathon following invisible paths — likely the remnants of old faith routes, necrotic waylines, or unfinished personal quests. They do not respond to speech, but will halt to study any reflective surface… or any person who resembles one.

The Ministry classifies them as **Type-UNF / Reflective Echo-Undead**, often harmless unless provoked or displaced from their “route.”

> _“They do not seek the living.  
> They seek the version of themselves they once saw in glass.”_  
> — Stonevoice

|**Body**|Gaunt humanoid wrapped in tattered traveler’s garb|
|**Skin**|Pale, cracked, with faint iridescent sheen under moonlight|

|**Movement**|Slow, steady, unerring; never fatigued|
|**Aura**|Cold, metallic; induces déjà vu and dreamlike familiarity|
When close, their eye-reflections behave independently, sometimes showing:

- People who have been dead for centuries
    
- Landscapes that no longer exist
    
- Scenes from labyrinth floors long collapsed
    
- Unfinished rituals of forgotten faiths
    

---

## **III. Behavior & Phenomena**

Glass-Eyed Pilgrims exhibit unique echo-behavior tied to reflection-memory.

**Observed Phenomena:**

- **Route Fixation:** They follow invisible pilgrimage paths; interference causes agitation or violence.
    
- **Echo-Gaze:** Their eyes reflect a target’s forgotten memories, often ones the target never personally experienced.
    
- **Glassbound Touch:** Contact induces vivid visions of past tragedies or uncompleted journeys.
    
- **Wanderer’s Lament:** Low humming heard when they pass; matches tomb-chant patterns of pre-Spectacle faiths.
    
- **Mirror-Lure:** Pilgrims gather inadvertently around reflective objects, sometimes circling them for hours.
    

> _“They are moths to memory, masquerading as men.”_  
> — Vell

They rarely initiate aggression unless:

- Their path is blocked,
    
- Their reflective “anchor” is destroyed,
    
- Someone attempts to remove items from their pack,
    
- Or they mistake a delver for a missing individual from their reflected memories.
    

---

## **IV. Habitat & Occurrence**

They are most often found in:

- **Old pilgrimage roads leading to ruined temples**
    
- **Blackwild clearings where moonlight pools perfectly still**
    
- **Mirrored caverns of Porta Strega**
    
- **Forgotten shrines in Namira’s island labyrinths**
    
- **Shatterfall dust plains where reflections behave unpredictably**
    
- **Cathedral ruins of Louvain’s earliest districts**
    

Emergence correlates strongly with **collective memory events**, such as anniversaries of tragedies or relic-worship festivals.

---

## **V. Ministry Observations**

- Echo-Law analysis shows that Pilgrims’ glass-eyes store **imprinted memory shards**, not belonging to the corpse beneath.
    
- When two Pilgrims meet, their eyes flicker rapidly; they may “exchange” routes and continue in opposite directions.
    
- Lt. Korrin observed a Pilgrim kneeling before a broken mirror and whispering a phrase in a dead dialect before vanishing.
    
- Their packs contain relics with no consistent origin: cracked lenses, empty scroll tubes, prayer beads fused by heat, sketches of strangers, and glass fragments that resonate with emotional fields.
    
- Removing a pack item at random sometimes induces **route collapse**, causing the Pilgrim to dissolve into reflective dust.
    

> _“Their journey continues even after their death.  
> The body simply follows.”_  
> — Stonevoice

## **VI. Weakness & Delving Considerations**

|**Vulnerability**|**Explanation / Practical Note**|
|---|---|
|**Shattering Sound**|Loud, discordant noise disrupts reflective memory and immobilizes them.|
|**Matte Surfaces**|Covering their eyes with non-reflective cloth pacifies them.|
|**Radiant Spells**|Breaks the echo-anchoring effect; weakens mobility.|
|**Removing Their Route Anchor**|Destroying or moving the object/place tied to their reflection causes dissipation.|
**Delver Advisory:**  
Never look directly into a Pilgrim’s eyes.  
Doing so risks a **memory echo-inversion**, where you temporarily recall events from the Pilgrim’s life (or death) instead of your own.

## **VIII. Containment & Research Status**

- **Classification:** Level III — Wandering Echo-Undead / Memory Hazard
    
- **Containment:** Only limited; they dissolve if confined too long. Best strategy is rerouting via reflective decoys.
    
- **Monitoring:** Route-mapping and Echo-Law resonance tracking.
    
- **Active Projects:**  
     • _Project Pilgrimage Map_ — reconstructing the ancient path-network Pilgrims follow.  
     • _Operation Calm Gaze_ — safe methods for studying their eye-reflection phenomena.
    

---

## **IX. Cultural & Mythic Notes**

- **Louvain Pilgrims:** Consider them “failures of faith who still seek home.”
    
- **Namiran Sailors:** Tell stories of Pilgrims walking on the ocean at dawn, following reflections of the sun.
    
- **Blackwild Druids:** Believe they are “memory-lost kin” of Moonstalkers, forever seeking lost shadow-roads.
    
- **Delver Superstition:** If a Pilgrim stares at you, you must leave an offering of water or glass — or risk becoming part of its next reflection.
    

> _“They aren't chasing the living.  
> They’re chasing a memory strong enough to move the dead.”_  
> — Daan

---

## **X. Summary**

**Glass-Eyed Pilgrims** are haunting remnants of forgotten journeys — undead walkers driven not by hunger or malice, but by echoes of memories that do not belong to them.  
They follow old routes long erased from maps, searching endlessly for completion to a pilgrimage that died centuries before they did.

Tragic, eerie, and strangely reverent, they remind Aerathon that:

**Some paths never end —  
even when those who walked them are long gone.**
