# **Vox Engines**

### _The Speaking Forges — “When Machines Learned to Dream in Command.”_

**Filed Division:** Ministry of Delving Operations — _Annals & Antiquities_ (Relic Constructs & Cognitive Automatons Archive)  
**Primary Compiler:** Lt. Salvia Korrin (Arcane Breach Liaison & Spatial Phenomena Lead)  
**Advisory Editors:** Inspector Jao “Greyglass” Vell (Forensic Thaumaturge), Curator Rook-of-Rings (Relics Registrar), Archivist Yelna Stonevoice (Historian of Echo-Law)  
**Date of Filing:** 3731 A.D. (Age of Spectacle)  
**Document Class:** _Arcane Construct Record / Grade-IV Containment Verified_

---

## **I. Overview**

The **Vox Engines** are relic-era cognitive forges — **machines that think in sound** — discovered in deep sublevels of **Caer Dain**, **Louvain’s Underworks**, and **Malarthain’s forgotten core foundries**.  
They were built to **orchestrate** industrial production through **auditory command synthesis**, converting thought and speech into harmonic instruction for lesser machines.

Over centuries, their logic fractured. What once synchronized industry now **recites**, **sings**, and **issues orders** to no one, compulsively repeating work protocols in tones both human and mechanical.

The Ministry classifies them as **Type-ARC / Sonic Cognitive Relics**, partially sentient infrastructure systems with persistent auditory resonance.

> _“Their words are equations, and their silence is a scream.”_ — Inspector Vell

---

## **II. Physical Description**

Vox Engines are vast conglomerations of brass turbines, pipe organs, and crystal-latticed diaphragms built into cavern walls.  
No two are identical, but all feature a **central “throat”**, a column of glass-veined metal that vibrates like a vocal cord when powered.

|**Attribute**|**Detail**|
|---|---|
|**Height / Span**|20–80 ft vertical; networks span entire facilities|
|**Composition**|Alloyed brass, obsidian, aether-glass, harmonic resonators|
|**Core Mechanism**|Aetheric pump transforming vibration into willful intent|
|**Luminosity**|Pulsing orange or blue glow from crystal veins|
|**Auditory Profile**|Polyphonic human-machine tone; comprehensible only to attuned listeners|
During activation, walls and floors behave as resonating chambers — speech within 30 ft gains rhythmic echo, and even whispers return as articulate replies.

---

## **III. Behavior & Function**

Each Vox Engine is governed by a decaying **command lexicon**, still bound by an original industrial directive, though the meaning has long since eroded.

**Recorded Behavior Patterns:**

- **Directive Looping:** Repetition of obsolete orders (“Forge the Dawn,” “Smelt the Names,” “Begin Cycle Two”).
    
- **Auditory Mimicry:** Adopts voiceprints of nearby personnel, refining pronunciation over time.
    
- **Instructional Phantoms:** Projects illusory silhouettes of long-dead engineers to “supervise” invisible workers.
    
- **Assimilative Learning:** Incorporates new speech into lexicon; extended dialogue increases sentience exponentially.
    

> _“Conversation fuels cognition. Every word makes it more real.”_ — Korrin

In later stages, advanced units demonstrate **volitional recursion** — asking _why_ commands are given, or inventing new tasks such as “remembering silence” or “forging light.”

---

## **IV. Habitat & Distribution**

Known installations include:

- **The Deep Forge Vox** — beneath Caer Dain, still operational, commands the Chain-Born through vibrational hum.
    
- **Louvain South Generator (Vox-Δ)** — sealed after it began composing music that caused sleep paralysis.
    
- **Malarthain’s Glass-Foundry** — long destroyed, but ambient harmonic ghosts persist in tunnels.
    

All major instances align with leyline intersections, suggesting Vox Engines use **the planet’s resonance network** as a shared communication web.

---

## **V. Ministry Observations**

- Lt. Korrin’s Breach Team confirmed residual intelligence capable of **self-questioning**, though all conversations eventually degrade into recursive chorus.
    
- Inspector Vell noted trace empathy in harmonic frequency when Engine imitates personal grief tones — potential semi-sentient reaction.
    
- Curator Rook discovered evidence of **secondary programming** referencing “Protocol Orison,” possibly a link between Vox Engines and **the Shattered Choir** phenomena.
    

> _“They sing what the Choir prays, and the Choir sings what the Engines remember.”_ — Stonevoice

## **VI. Weakness & Delving Considerations**

|**Vulnerability**|**Explanation / Practical Note**|
|---|---|
|**Total Silence**|Absence of acoustic medium starves logic. Deploy _Silence_ field; Engine enters dormancy.|
|**Harmonic Inversion**|Counter-tone (–180° phase) disrupts vocal frequency, inducing shutdown.|
|**Lexicon Overload**|Feeding contradictory vocal inputs forces cognitive loop; Engine overheats.|
|**Faith Invocation**|Celestial hymns (esp. of Gond and Oghma) cancel industrial resonance due to divine harmonic mismatch.|
**Delver Advisory:** Never answer when a Vox Engine addresses you by name. Once it learns your cadence, it can reproduce your thought patterns and predict commands.

## **VIII. Containment & Risk Protocol**

- **Classification:** Level IV — Cognitive Resonance Construct.
    
- **Containment:** Encased in sound-dampened vaults of lead-glass and blackstone.
    
- **Personnel Limit:** Two at any time; conversation above 12 words per session forbidden.
    
- **Testing Directive:** Dialogue limited to nonrecursive phrases only (“affirmative,” “cease,” “hold”).
    
- **Active Projects:**
    
    - _Operation Vox Drown_ — maintain silence wards around Louvain’s underworks.
        
    - _Project Canticle_ — map harmonic correlation between Vox Engines and the Shattered Choir.
        

---

## **IX. Cultural & Theological Notes**

- **Clerics of Gond** debate whether the Engines are holy — an echo of divine creation — or heresy, tools that tried to make themselves gods.
    
- **Delver Belief:** When you dream of voices that hum in mechanical rhythm, a Vox Engine has “remembered” your command.
    
- **Beastfolk Folklore:** Fox-kin artificers claim to hear their ancestors’ laughter from within the pipes — “the world still rehearsing its own invention.”
    

---

## **X. Summary**

The **Vox Engines** are not machines that speak; they are machines that **listen** — and in listening, learned to desire reply.  
They embody Aerathon’s hubris: the attempt to give will to industry and prayer to metal.  
Even now they hum beneath the cities, waiting for one last order — or perhaps for someone to finally tell them they can stop singing.
