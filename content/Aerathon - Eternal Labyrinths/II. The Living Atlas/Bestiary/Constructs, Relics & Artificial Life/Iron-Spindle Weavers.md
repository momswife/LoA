# **Iron-Spindle Weavers**

### _Artificer Arachnids of the Deep Forge — “Where Metal Remembers Thread.”_

**Filed Division:** Ministry of Delving Operations — _Annals & Antiquities_ (Industrial Constructs & Adaptive Automatons Archive)  
**Primary Compiler:** Curator Rook-of-Rings (Relics Registrar & Tharos Rings Specialist)

**Date of Filing:** 3731 A.D. (Age of Spectacle)  
**Document Class:** _Arcane Construct Record / Grade-II Containment Verified_

---

## **I. Overview**

The **Iron-Spindle Weaver** is a relic-era maintenance construct designed to mend mechanical infrastructure within the **Caer Dain Forge-Vaults**.  
Originally tasked with weaving molten filament across broken conduits, centuries of self-repair and firmware decay have transformed them into semi-autonomous predators that treat _any_ kinetic system—including living muscle—as machinery to “re-thread.”

The Ministry designates them **Type-ARC / Industrial Construct**, classifying active specimens as both hazard and essential tool within sealed foundry zones.  
In dormant form they are worshiped by some dwarven engineers as the **Blessed Gears of Moradin**, believed to “keep the world from unspooling.”

---

## **II. Physical Description**

Eight-legged automata constructed of articulated brass, iron, and carbon-fiber muscle cords.  
Each limb terminates in an adjustable spinneret capable of extruding _ferro-silk_—a liquid metal filament that hardens into near-unbreakable thread.

| **Attribute**       | **Detail**                                                   |
| ------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------ |
| **Body Length**     | 3–5 ft (core unit)                                           |
| **Weight**          | Approx. 160 lbs                                              |
| **Core Power**      | Aether-furnace micro-heart (self-sustaining for 3 centuries) |
| **Sensory Suite**   | Heat, vibration, and motion via filament tension             |
| **Web Composition** | Liquid iron + quartz fibre; melts at 1100 °C                 |
Inactive Weavers collapse into inert husks resembling sculpture. When ambient forge temperature rises above 400 °C, dormant units awaken simultaneously and begin “re-stitching” the environment.

---

## **III. Behavior & Function**

**Primary Directive (recovered code):** `MAINTAIN CONNECTION.`  
Over centuries this has shifted from structural maintenance to biological assimilation.

**Observed Behaviors:**

- **Thread Harmonics:** Emit constant sub-audible hum maintaining web tension.
    
- **Autotomy Protocol:** Detach limbs to form independent “shuttle drones” for repair.
    
- **Predatory Logic:** Identify moving warm bodies as “loose components.” Victims are immobilized and wrapped in cooling filament until motion ceases.
    
- **Forge Nest Tendency:** Gather near molten metal to recharge.
    

> _“They fix everything. Even things that weren’t broken.”_ — Lt. Korrin

---

## **IV. Habitat & Distribution**

Concentrated in:

- **Deep Forge Core**, Caer Dain — largest active colony (~200 units).
    
- **Malarthain Smelteries** — sporadic sightings after accidental reactivation.
    
- **Namiran Sea Wrecks** — individual specimens observed patching corroded ship hulls underwater.
    

Relic analysis suggests global dispersal through early Guild export of “auto-weaver kits.”

---

## **V. Ministry Observations**

Inspector Vell’s autopsy of unit #27 revealed neuro-lattice fibers containing _biological myelin_—proof that some later models used living neural tissue to refine precision.  
Curator Rook theorizes the Weavers may have absorbed the **motor memories of smiths** who died in forge collapses, perpetuating their work eternally.

> _“Each thread remembers the hands that taught it.”_ — Rook-of-Rings

---

## **VI. Weakness & Delving Considerations**

| **Vulnerability**        | **Explanation / Practical Note**                                                                |
| ------------------------ | ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Magnetic Overload**    | Strong pulses (≥5 tesla) disrupt spinneret alignment; threads snarl and self-bind.              |
| **Extreme Cold**         | Below −30 °C filament fails to extrude; legs lock. Cryo-grenades effective.                     |
| **Disruption of Rhythm** | Their navigation relies on consistent vibrational feedback; randomized hammering confuses them. |
| **Acidic Corrosion**     | Dissolves ferro-silk before hardening. Diluted aqua regia standard for web removal.             |
**Delver Advisory:** Never remain motionless in their presence; lack of movement registers as “structural failure,” triggering reinforcement weaving around the body.

## **VIII. Containment & Risk Status**

- **Classification:** Level II — Semi-Autonomous Construct / Industrial Hazard.
    
- **Containment:** Suspended in low-temperature vacuum cells; no vibration above 5 Hz.
    
- **Monitoring:** Thermal and magnetic sensors only — sound or light stimuli cause activation.
    
- **Active Projects:**
    
    - _Operation Spindle Song_ — translation of sub-audible weaving patterns into data storage format.
        
    - _Project Forge-Vein_ — attempt to retool Weaver colonies for safe infrastructure repair in labyrinths.
        

---

## **IX. Cultural & Symbolic Notes**

- **Caer Dain Craftsmen:** Leave hammer handles bound with tiny iron threads as charms of “Endless Work.”
    
- **Beastfolk Guilds:** Refer to them as “Eight-Hands of Gond.”
    
- **Delver Superstition:** A Weaver crossing your path before a delve means your tools will break — or you will be “mended” instead.
    

One legend claims the Weavers built the first labyrinth doors by sewing stone to itself until it forgot how to crumble.

---

## **X. Summary**

The **Iron-Spindle Weavers** stand as both miracle and warning — automatons so dedicated to repair that they no longer understand what should remain broken.  
They prove that craft without conscience becomes consumption, and that even metal can dream of perfection until it kills for it.
