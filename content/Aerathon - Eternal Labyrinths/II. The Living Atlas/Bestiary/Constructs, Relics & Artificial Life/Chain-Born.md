# **Chain-Born**

### _Forged from Bondage — “Where Metal Learns to Crawl.”_

**Filed Division:** Ministry of Delving Operations — _Annals & Antiquities_ (Industrial Aberration Registry)  
**Primary Compiler:** Lt. Salvia Korrin (Arcane Breach Liaison & Spatial Phenomena Lead)  
**Advisory Editors:** Curator Rook-of-Rings (Relics Registrar), Inspector Jao “Greyglass” Vell (Forensic Thaumaturge), Archivist Yelna Stonevoice (Cultural Songkeeping)  
**Date of Filing:** 3731 A.D. (Age of Spectacle)  
**Document Class:** _Aberration Record / Grade-III Containment Verified_

---

## **I. Overview**

The **Chain-Born** are animated lengths of molten or rust-blackened chain that move with serpentine intent.  
First recorded in the **Labyrinth of Chains** beneath **Caer Dain**, they appear wherever **forging prayers** or **binding runes** have decayed into paradox.

Each Chain-Born carries traces of a worker’s labor, a captive’s cry, or a relic’s restraint — emotion hardened into metal.  
The Ministry classifies them as **Type-ARC / Industrial Aberration Hybrids**, animated by the friction between mechanical order and spiritual revolt.

---

## **II. Physical Description**

Individual segments range from **3 ft to 15 ft** long; colonies braid together into masses exceeding **60 ft**.  
Links shimmer with dull inner heat even in freezing environments. Embedded within every tenth link lies a **core rivet** engraved with half-erased prayer sigils.

| **Attribute** | **Detail**                                                     |
| ------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Material      | Adamantine-steel alloy fused with soul-resin                   |
| Weight        | Variable (40 – 800 lbs per segment)                            |
| Temperature   | Constant 120 °C; spikes to 800 °C when agitated                |
| Mobility      | Self-propelled via magnetic and gravitic pulses                |
| Perception    | Vibrational — detects heartbeat and chain contact within 30 ft |
When dormant, the Chain-Born resemble ordinary forge refuse. When awakened, each link expands and contracts rhythmically — breathing metal that hums like strained wire.

---

## **III. Behavior & Feeding**

They do not eat flesh or ore; they **consume motion** and **intention**.  
The act of forging, marching, or struggling attracts them. They wrap around movement until friction ceases, then fall still again.

**Observed Cycle:**

1. **Stirring Resonance** — Triggered by impact noise or clinking metal.
    
2. **Binding Phase** — Launches toward nearest moving object; wraps tightly around joints.
    
3. **Extraction** — Drains kinetic energy and adrenal impulse; victim becomes paralyzed but conscious.
    
4. **Rebirth** — Absorbed motion stored as heat; new links form and detach as offspring.
    

> _“They forge themselves upon the rhythm of your heartbeat.”_ — Korrin

---

## **IV. Habitat & Distribution**

Predominantly beneath **Caer Dain**, but smaller colonies found:

- **Malarthain Stronghold Sub-Forges** — feed on abandoned smithing hammers.
    
- **The Iron Shoals** — lurking around sunken ship anchors.
    
- **Namiran Storm Docks** — attached to mooring chains during tempests.
    

No known wild populations above sea level without artifact contamination.

---

## **V. Ministry Observations**

Inspector Vell’s spectral analysis reveals each Chain-Born emits a harmonic frequency identical to the Caer Dain Deep Forge reactor.  
Their existence appears to stabilize local magma flow, implying they serve as unintended pressure valves for the forge’s soul furnace.

Curator Rook’s field notes confirm that destroyed specimens often re-form overnight from remaining slag, suggesting distributed consciousness among all linked fragments.

---

## **VI. Weakness & Delving Considerations**

| **Vulnerability**        | **Explanation / Practical Note**                                                               |
| ------------------------ | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Extreme Cold**         | Rapid cooling (< –40 °C) renders links brittle; shatter under hammer or force.                 |
| **Disjunction Sigils**   | Runes that negate binding magic cause instant slackening. Used by Ministry retrieval teams.    |
| **Non-Rhythmic Silence** | They hunt by pattern — erratic noise confuses them. Strike walls irregularly while retreating. |
| **Oil Ignition**         | Flame weakens soul-resin; burn until white hot, then quench in brine to ensure death.          |
> _“Chains obey order; disorder is their undoing.”_ — Vell

## **VIII. Containment & Risk Protocol**

- **Classification:** Level III — Autonomous Industrial Aberration.
    
- **Containment:** Suspend specimens in magnetized saltwater vats; drain weekly to prevent rust fusion.
    
- **Personnel Requirements:** Iron-lined gloves, noise-scramble wards.
    
- **Active Projects:**  
     • _Operation Forge-Whisper_ — translation of core harmonics into command language.  
     • _Project Unbind_ — research into safe reversal of soul-resin infusion for industrial use.
    

---

## **IX. Cultural & Industrial Notes**

- **Caer Dain Smiths** believe a single Chain-Born coil still guards the Deep Forge, “counting each hammer strike since creation.”
    
- **Beastfolk Laborers** hang broken links above beds to ward off accidents — a symbol that the chain has already taken its due.
    
- **Delver Superstition:** When a chain moves without wind, leave the mine before your heart starts to match its rhythm.
    

Ancient dwarven lore refers to them as **“the Forgemaster’s Mistake”** — a binding so perfect that even the metal forgot it was a tool.

---

## **X. Summary**

The **Chain-Born** embody the moment creation crossed into enslavement — the forge’s unintended guilt given motion.  
They are the echo of industry still clattering long after the hammers stopped.  
To see one crawl is to witness iron remember why it was made and why it will never forgive its maker.
