# **Lantern Fawns**

### _Glow-Touched Creatures of the Feydream Fringe — “Little Lights That Wander Where Wishes Sleep.”_

**Filed Division:** Ministry of Delving Operations — _The Living Atlas_ (Fey, Dreamborn & Illusionary Entities Archive)  
**Primary Compiler:** Researcher Falyn Onarra (Beastfolk Sociology & Dream-Ecology)  
**Advisory Editors:** Archivist Yelna Stonevoice (Echo-Law Historian), Lt. Salvia Korrin (Arcane Breach Liaison), Inspector Jao “Greyglass” Vell (Thaumaturgic Forensics)  
**Date of Filing:** 3733 A.D.  
**Document Class:** _Dream-Fey Entity Record / Grade-I Observation Verified_

---

## **I. Overview**

**Lantern Fawns** are gentle feydream creatures resembling small, luminous deer calves with soft-glowing horns and lantern-like hearts.  
They are born where **innocent hope** saturates the Glittering Veil — places like abandoned children’s rooms, untouched forest clearings, broken labyrinth chambers where someone once wished desperately for rescue.

Unlike Hollow Dancers or Lachyrmoths, Lantern Fawns are **benign by nature**, though their presence warps local dreamflow in subtle ways.  
They embody **protective longing** — the instinct to guide, shepherd, and warm those who feel lost.

The Ministry classifies them as **Type-FDI / Dream-Fey Guardian Entities**, considered low-risk but emotionally potent.

> _“They are what loneliness turns into when it refuses to become sorrow.”_ — Stonevoice

---

## **II. Physical Description**

|**Attribute**|**Detail**|
|---|---|
|**Size**|Comparable to a small dog; 2–3 ft at shoulder|
|**Coloration**|Pale cream or soft grey with faint shifting opalescence|
|**Horns**|Translucent and lantern-like; internal glow brightens with emotion|
|**Eyes**|Wide, pearlescent, and reflective like shallow moonlit pools|
|**Movement**|Soft, drifting steps leaving trails of warm motes|
|**Scent**|Warm rain, honey, and old paper lanterns|
Their hooves never leave prints; each step replaces what should be a track with a tiny patch of glowing moss.

The “lantern-heart” — a soft chest glow — pulses rhythmically with nearby emotional fields.

---

## **III. Behavior & Phenomena**

Lantern Fawns are **empathetic pseudo-guides**, drawn to those in emotional distress: isolation, fear, or gentle longing.

**Documented Behaviors:**

- **Trail of Warmth:** Fawns illuminate safe paths through dangerous terrain, often guiding delvers away from traps or unstable dreamfolds.
    
- **Hearth Aura:** Within 10 ft, emotional regulation improves; panic fades, nightmares weaken.
    
- **Echo Affection:** Mimics comforting gestures seen in Beastfolk or delvers (nuzzles, gentle leaning).
    
- **Dream Shepherding:** Can enter light sleep-state visions and guide individuals through symbolic challenges.
    
- **Memory Reflection:** Horns display faint illusions of happy memories — never harmful, but sometimes disarming.
    

> _“If it stands beside you, something inside you is trying to heal.”_ — Daan

Lantern Fawns rarely flee from danger unless threatened with cold iron or aggressive magic.

---

## **IV. Habitat & Occurrence**

Lantern Fawns appear where **kindness lingers** despite ruin:

- Forgotten corners of the Blackwild where children once played
    
- Hollow Dancer groves after the illusions disperse
    
- Abandoned labyrinth safe rooms touched by sacrifice or mercy
    
- Grave markers of companions who died protecting others
    
- Small shrines left by Beastfolk families in Oria
    
- Dream-saturated forest edges touched by Lunar or Aurora phenomena
    

They are more common during solstices, when dreamflow is unbound.

---

## **V. Ministry Observations**

- Echo-lens readings reveal each Fawn contains a **thread of dreamstuff connected to a living creature**—origin unknown.
    
- Their glow resonates inversely with fear — brighter in courage, dimmer near panic.
    
- Lt. Korrin recorded one instance of a Fawn shielding a delver from an illusion attack by dispersing its lantern-glow.
    
- Priest-Analyst Daan notes that Fawns avoid places heavy with guilt, implying they feed on unresolved hope rather than sorrow.
    

> _“They are hope’s answer to fear’s question.”_ — Stonevoice

---

## **VI. Weakness & Delving Considerations**

|**Vulnerability**|**Explanation / Practical Note**|
|---|---|
|**Cold Iron**|Instantly disrupts their dreamstuff cohesion; they vanish in fear.|
|**Loud Aggression**|Yelling, violence, or psychic malice causes them to flicker erratically.|
|**Despair Fields**|Areas thick with hopelessness extinguish their heart-lantern.|
|**False Affection**|Attempts to deceive or manipulate them cause withdrawal and emotional backlash.|
**Delver Advisory:**  
Treat Lantern Fawns gently. They mirror emotional fields — cruelty produces a phenomena known as a **“Dimheart,”** a corrupted, sorrow-sick variant.

## **VIII. Containment & Research Status**

- **Classification:** Level I — Benevolent Dreamborn / Low Hazard
    
- **Containment:** Not recommended; Fawns wither in captivity. Observation only.
    
- **Monitoring:** Dreamflow tracers and emotion-field sensors.
    
- **Active Projects:**  
     • _Project Kindling_ — use of Fawn aura in trauma therapy for delvers.  
     • _Operation Gentle Step_ — mapping Lantern Fawn waypaths through labyrinths for safe-route prediction.
    

---

## **IX. Cultural & Mythic Notes**

- **Beastfolk of Oria:** Consider them “Lantern-Children,” protectors of lost cubs.
    
- **Blackwild Folklore:** A Fawn appearing before dawn means the forest forgives you.
    
- **Delver Superstition:** If a Lantern Fawn follows you through a delve, you will return alive — but changed.
    
- **Louvain Nursery Tales:** Children place tiny lanterns by their beds “so the little deer knows where to find them in dreams.”
    

> _“Where they walk, even nightmares step aside.”_ — Onarra

---

## **X. Summary**

The **Lantern Fawns** are flickering guardians of dream and emotion — shy, luminous creatures born of hope’s refusal to die.  
They guide the lost, soothe the frightened, and warm the forgotten corners of Aerathon’s shadowed places.

They are not powerful.  
They are not dangerous.  
And yet, in the quiet spaces between delves, they may be the most precious beings in all the Veil:

**Proof that even in a world of labyrinths and ruin, something small still chooses to glow.**
