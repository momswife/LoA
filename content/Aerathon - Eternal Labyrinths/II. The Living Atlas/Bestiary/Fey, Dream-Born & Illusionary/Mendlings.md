# **Mendlings**

### _Tiny Repairers of Dreamstuff — “Little Hands That Gather the Fallen Pieces.”_

**Filed Division:** Ministry of Delving Operations — _The Living Atlas_ (Fey, Dreamborn & Illusionary Entities Archive)  
**Primary Compiler:** Archivist Yelna Stonevoice (Echo-Law Historian & Dream-Ecology Specialist)  
**Advisory Editors:** Researcher Falyn Onarra (Beastfolk Sociology), Lt. Salvia Korrin (Arcane Breach Liaison), Inspector Jao “Greyglass” Vell (Thaumaturgic Forensics)  
**Date of Filing:** 3733 A.D.  
**Document Class:** _Dream-Fey Entity Record / Grade-I Hazard Verified_

---

## **I. Overview**

**Mendlings** are minuscule dream-fey that appear wherever **broken things want to be whole again** — splintered toys, cracked mirrors, torn journals, frayed bonds, or fractured emotions.  
They embody the tender instinct of mending: the desire to restore, to soothe, to bring back what once was.

Their bodies are woven from **threads of the Glittering Veil**, giving them the appearance of tiny ragdoll-like figures stitched together from scraps of light, memory, and imagination.

The Ministry classifies them as **Type-FDI / Restorative Dreamborn Entities**, generally harmless but capable of unintended psychological effects.

> _“They cannot tell the difference between a broken cup and a broken heart.”_ — Onarra

---

## **II. Physical Description**

|**Attribute**|**Detail**|
|---|---|
|**Size**|4–8 inches tall|
|**Body Structure**|Humanoid ragdoll appearance; seams of glowing dream-thread|
|**Eyes**|Two bright stitch-knots resembling firefly lights|
|**Hands**|Needle-like fingers perfect for tying or stitching dreamstuff|
|**Movement**|Soft pattering steps; often climb walls or float briefly|
|**Coloration**|Soft blues, quilt-like greys, faint pastel hues|
|**Sound**|Gentle rustling, like fabric being folded|
Their bodies unravel into loose threads if injured, later re-forming once the “damage” feels symbolically resolved.

---

## **III. Behavior & Phenomena**

Mendlings are **compulsive fixers**, correcting disarray in both literal and emotional forms.

**Observed Behaviors:**

- **Patchwork Touch:** They attempt to stitch torn fabric, mend broken hinges, or reassemble shattered objects (often incorrectly).
    
- **Emotional Stitching:** They nudge individuals to reconcile, forgive, or express unspoken feelings — sometimes too forcefully.
    
- **Threadweave Aura:** A faint field that calms arguments within 10 ft, slowing aggressive impulses.
    
- **Memory Knotting:** They tie emotional fragments into dream-symbols that appear in future dreams.
    
- **Gathering Ritual:** Mendlings travel in clusters to sites of collective loss, stitching the air as if “repairing” the sorrow itself.
    

> _“They fix the shape of things, not the function — a chair with four mismatched legs, or a heart stitched together but still hurting.”_ — Stonevoice

They avoid loud conflict, sharp iron, and sudden change.

---

## **IV. Habitat & Occurrence**

Mendlings manifest in quiet, gentle dream-threshold spaces:

- Old workshops filled with half-finished crafts
    
- Abandoned nurseries and toy rooms
    
- Labyrinth safe rooms with lingering emotional impressions
    
- Blackwild groves with childlike illusions
    
- Bedrooms where someone cried softly but never called for help
    
- Shelves of forgotten books or broken heirlooms
    

They are particularly active during the equinoxes, when dreams and reality briefly overlap.

---

## **V. Ministry Observations**

- Echo-lens scanning reveals each Mendling carries a **thread-core**, a tiny knot of dreamstuff representing the last thing it tried to fix.
    
- When near irreparable damage (emotional or physical), Mendlings panic — their seams flicker, and they tear themselves apart into stray thread-lights that drift away.
    
- Some Mendlings attempt to “repair magic,” resulting in wild, unintended spell interactions.
    
- Exposure to despair collapses them instantly into piles of thread-dust.
    

> _“They cannot survive true hopelessness. Their world is made of second chances.”_ — Daan

---

## **VI. Weakness & Delving Considerations**

|**Vulnerability**|**Explanation / Practical Note**|
|---|---|
|**Cold Iron**|Disrupts dream-thread; causes immediate unraveling.|
|**Sudden Violence**|They flee or unravel when startled by aggression.|
|**Despair Fields**|Emotional voids extinguish their internal glow.|
|**Complex Machinery**|They compulsively attempt “repairs” and may break mechanisms further.|
**Delver Advisory:**  
Do not allow Mendlings near magical devices — they will _try_ to fix them, often disastrously.

## **VIII. Containment & Research Status**

- **Classification:** Level I — Benevolent Dreamborn / Minor Hazard
    
- **Containment:** Gentle; silk-lined terrariums for short intervals only.
    
- **Monitoring:** Dreamstuff resonance probes; emotional field sensors.
    
- **Active Projects:**  
     • _Project Thread-Bridge_ — studying Mendling ability to repair dream fractures in labyrinths.  
     • _Operation Little Hands_ — deploying Mendlings in controlled emotional support roles for delvers.
    

---

## **IX. Cultural & Mythic Notes**

- **Fey Courts:** Call them _“Needle Children,”_ believing they were made from discarded scraps of unfinished dreams.
    
- **Beastfolk Craftsmen:** Leave broken toys outside workshops so Mendlings “have something safe to fix.”
    
- **Delver Superstition:** If a Mendling tugs your sleeve, a relationship in your life needs mending.
    
- **Louvain Families:** Keep tiny baskets of buttons and thread as offerings to “invite gentle repair.”
    

> _“They are not healers.  
> They are reminders that healing is possible.”_ — Stonevoice

---

## **X. Summary**

The **Mendlings** are tender, innocent dream-born beings who gather the broken fragments of the world — objects, memories, and hearts alike — and try to stitch them back together, even when they do not fully understand what they’re fixing.

Harmless, hopeful, and heartbreakingly earnest, they embody a truth Aerathon often forgets:

**Not everything can be made whole again —  
but even the smallest hands can try.**
