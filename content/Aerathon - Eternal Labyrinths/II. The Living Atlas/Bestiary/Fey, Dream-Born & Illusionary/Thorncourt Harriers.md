# **Thorncourt Harriers**

### _Feyborn Pursuers of the Briar Thrones — “Those Who Hunt for the Will of the Thorn.”_

**Filed Division:** Ministry of Delving Operations — _The Living Atlas_ (Fey, Dreamborn & Illusionary Entities Archive)  
**Primary Compiler:** Lt. Salvia Korrin (Arcane Breach Liaison & Feyline Conflict Specialist)  
**Advisory Editors:** Archivist Yelna Stonevoice (Echo-Law Historian), Inspector Jao “Greyglass” Vell (Thaumaturgic Forensics), Researcher Falyn Onarra (Beastfolk Sociology)  
**Date of Filing:** 3733 A.D.  
**Document Class:** _Fey Entity Record / Grade-III Combat Hazard_

---

## **I. Overview**

**Thorncourt Harriers** are elite feyborn trackers and enforcers serving the **Briar Throne**, one of the oldest and most volatile Courts of the Glittering Veil.  
They embody **pursuit as artistry**, hunting intruders, oathbreakers, and rival fey across both the Veil and physical Aerathon.

Unlike many dreamborn entities, Harriers possess **solid, lethal bodies**, though their appearance is unmistakably fey: humanoid forms clad in braided vine-armor, limbs elongated like coiled thorns, and facial features obscured by blossoming masks.

They are classified as **Type-FEI / Martial Fey Entities**, considered extremely dangerous and notoriously difficult to outrun.

> _“You don’t hear them approach.  
> You hear the forest deciding you’ve gone far enough.”_  
> — Stonevoice

---

## **III. Behavior & Phenomena**

Harriers embody **relentless pursuit** — they do not harm without cause, but once given a quarry, they **never abandon the chase** unless their Court is appeased.

**Documented Behaviors:**

- **Bramble Step:** They vanish into briars, reappearing miles ahead or behind their target.
    
- **Oath Scenting:** Harriers can detect broken promises like a scent on the wind.
    
- **Pack Synchrony:** Teams of 3–7 Harriers move with perfect shared awareness, striking in shifting patterns.
    
- **Thorn Judgment:** Once they corner a quarry, the mask opens and the Harrier issues its “Verdict,” a single word determining whether to bind, mark, or kill the target.
    
- **Veil-Harrow:** Their presence temporarily warps plantlife, making vines act with predatory intent.
    

> _“They do not chase you for punishment. They chase you to restore symmetry.”_  
> — Daan

They avoid fire and unnatural metal, preferring to hunt in forests, ruins, and labyrinth segments containing flora.

---

## **IV. Habitat & Occurrence**

Thorncourt Harriers are summoned or dispatched from the **Briar Thrones**, appearing most frequently:

- At boundaries where the **Glittering Veil** touches overgrown forest
    
- In the **Blackwild**, especially in old Moonstalker paths
    
- Around relic sites contaminated with plant-blood magic
    
- Within labyrinth sectors containing living walls or briar illusions
    
- Near fey oaths broken intentionally
    

Harriers rarely appear alone unless on ceremonial hunts or delivering formal declarations from their Court.

---

## **V. Ministry Observations**

- Harrier vine-armor is alive and reacts independently to threats, sometimes shielding them or lashing out.
    
- Lt. Korrin recorded one Harrier stepping through a briar patch that acted as a planar doorway, implying they navigate a **network of plant-based fey gates**.
    
- When slain, Harriers disintegrate into black rose petals that rot into iron-rich soil within minutes.
    
- Their voices contain layered tones — forensic analysis indicates **voices of past quarry** mixed into their own.
    
- Harriers have been documented “sparring” with Iron-Bough Boars, apparently testing new chase paths.
    

> _“Every hunt is a lesson. Every kill is a pruning.”_  
> — Stonevoice

|**Vulnerability**|**Explanation / Practical Note**|
|---|---|

|**Broken Rhythm**|Irregular footwork or chaotic motion confuses their predictive chase patterns.|

Never break an oath within earshot of forest walls.  
If a Harrier hears it, the hunt begins immediately — regardless of intent.

## **IX. Cultural & Mythic Notes**

- **Blackwild Rangers:** Claim Harriers protect the deeper groves from “unnatural wanderers.”
    
- **Briar Court Folk:** Regard Harriers as holy agents of balance — neither cruel nor kind.
    
- **Delver Belief:** If a Harrier spares you after cornering you, the forest itself has forgiven you.
    
- **Children’s Tale (Oria):** “Don’t break a promise among thorns, or the thorns will come walking.”
    

> _“No one escapes the Thorncourt.  
> Only some are invited to walk away.”_  
> — Onarra

---

## **X. Summary**

The **Thorncourt Harriers** are relentless fey hunters — enforcers of symmetry, balance, and oathbound consequence.  
They are neither villains nor heroes, but instruments of the Briar Throne’s will, pruning chaos where it threatens the quiet weave of the Veil.

Swift, silent, merciless in pursuit, yet strangely honorable, they remind Aerathon that:

**The forest always hears —  
and the forest always answers.**
