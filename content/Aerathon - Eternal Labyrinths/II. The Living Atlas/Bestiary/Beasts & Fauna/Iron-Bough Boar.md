# **Iron-Bough Boar**

### _Forged in Root and Blood — “Where the Trees Remember the Anvil.”_

## **I. Overview**

Native to the temperate forests between **Louvain** and the **Malarthain strongholds**, the **Iron-Bough Boar** is an omnivorous ungulate species whose bones and tusks incorporate ferromagical ore drawn directly from the soil.  
Centuries of habitation near refinery run-off, relic dust, and ley-tainted mulch have turned the once-common forest boar into a living forge animal.

The Ministry classifies it as a **Type-NAT / Arc-Ferric Hybrid**, occupying the ecological niche between predator deterrent and forest engineer.  
Each rooting herd aerates and metal-salts the soil — a process vital to Louvain’s timber growth but catastrophic when left unchecked.

---

## **II. Physical Description**

Iron-Bough Boars are massive, muscular quadrupeds cloaked in coarse charcoal fur threaded with metallic fibers.  
The tusks, often mistaken for raw iron, are **ferrite-bone composites** capable of gouging stone.

| **Attribute**        | **Detail**                                 |
| -------------------- | ------------------------------------------ |
| Average Height       | 5 ft at shoulder (boars), 4 ft (sows)      |
| Average Weight       | 1 000–1 400 lbs                            |
| Tusks                | Up to 3 ft long, magnetically polarized    |
| Diet                 | Roots, fungi, carrion, and trace ore       |
| Internal Temperature | 47 °C (maintained via metabolic oxidation) |
| Disposition          | Territorial, intelligent herd behavior     |
During breeding season, males clash with enough force to ignite sparks visible at dusk — an event locals call the _“Boar’s Welding.”_

---

## **III. Habitat & Behavior**

Iron-Bough herds roam mixed forest and old slag fields, preferring mineral-rich loam.  
They dig complex burrows called **Root Forges**, where heat from friction and oxidized mud maintains constant warmth for piglets.

- **Dietary Magnetism:** Their stomach lining filters metallic dust, later excreted as **iron-soil**—a resource prized by Louvain blacksmith-farmers.
    
- **Social Structure:** Led by a sow known as the **Matron Anvil**, whose tusks are permanently dull from grooming juveniles.
    
- **Defensive Habit:** When threatened, boars lock tusks and release low-frequency grunts that resonate through trees, stunning predators and collapsing loose soil.
    

---

## **IV. Ecological Role**

Despite property damage to sawmills and caravans, Iron-Bough Boars are essential to maintaining Louvain’s ore-infused ecosystems.  
Their rooting redistributes ferric minerals and purges rot by heating soil pockets.  
Trees grown in their wake develop dark, iron-veined bark prized for relic housing.

> _“Without them, Louvain’s forest would lose its temper — and its steel.”_ — Curator Rook-of-Rings

## **VII. Cultural Significance**

- **Beastfolk of Louvain:** View the Matron Anvil as symbol of endurance; her effigy stands at foundry gates.
    
- **Delver Guilds:** Employ tamed boars for ore detection; guild lore says a herd’s trail points to buried relic veins.
    
- **Faith Rites:** Priests of Gond bless newborn hammers with a single boar hair to “temper intent.”
    

In rural legend, the first Iron-Bough rose from a smith’s apprentice buried alive beneath his failed forge — the forest took pity and lent him hooves.

---

## **VIII. Containment & Observation Status**

- **Classification:** Level II — _Moderate Threat / High Ecological Value._
    
- **Containment:** Electrified perimeter fences set to harmonic frequency to discourage tusk strikes; never lethal.
    
- **Active Projects:**
    
    - _Operation Root Forge_ — mapping soil enrichment radius from herd migration.
        
    - _Thermal Conductivity Study_ — analyzing boar heat output for industrial heating designs.
        

---

## **IX. Summary**

The **Iron-Bough Boar** is both beast and blacksmith — a creature that tempers the forest with every step.  
It roots in slag and seed alike, shaping Louvain’s wilderness into something stronger than either alone.  
To hunt one is folly; to follow its trail is to walk where the world reforges itself each dawn.
