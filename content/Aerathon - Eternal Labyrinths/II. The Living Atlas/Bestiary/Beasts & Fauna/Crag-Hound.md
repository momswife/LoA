## **I. Overview**

The **Crag-Hound** is a hardy predator-scavenger populating the cliff trails and broken roads between **Louvain**, **Malarthain**, and the southern ridges of **Allemance**.  
Descended from ancient guild-bred watch-beasts, its body fused over centuries with trace mineral deposits from quarry runoff and relic dust.  
Ministry zoologists designate it a **Type-NAT / Arc-Adapta**, a natural species whose physiology now includes semi-crystalline growths functioning as armor and resonance organs.

Commonly seen shadowing delver caravans, the Crag-Hound straddles the line between guardian and hazard. Packs trail travelers for days, deciding only at dusk whether the scent means protection or prey.

---

## **II. Physical Description**

Stocky and broad-shouldered, the Crag-Hound stands **4 ft at the shoulder** with a length near **8 ft**.  
Hide composed of thick stone-gray fur threaded with mica shards; along spine and forelegs grow **calcite scales** that shimmer when struck by lightning.

|**Attribute**|**Detail**|
|---|---|
|Average Weight|650–900 lbs|
|Hide Density|Comparable to granite (5 Hardness rating)|
|Diet|Carrion / ore-lichens / mineralized marrow|
|Senses|Echolocation via jaw resonance; smell enhanced by dust content|
|Average Lifespan|40 years (half in feral state)|
|Temperament|Cautious, territorial, hierarchical pack structure|
When threatened, a Crag-Hound releases a **throat-rattle** that echoes through stone as seismic vibration — a warning tremor detectable half a mile away.

---

## **III. Habitat & Behavior**

Crag-Hounds dwell in **quarry networks**, **cavern mouths**, and along **abandoned delver roads** lined with metallic debris.  
Their dens are marked by concentric claw-scrapes in stone, forming sigils that ward off lesser predators.

Behaviorally, they display near-military discipline:

- **Pack Alpha:** Always female, known as the Gravel-Queen; organizes patrol patterns around moon cycles.
    
- **Howl Chorus:** Used to communicate seismic threats; some guilds employ recorded howls to predict rockfalls.
    
- **Feeding Order:** Young eat first; elders last — social inversion believed to strengthen pack empathy.
    

Despite their aggression, domesticated strains still serve caravan guilds as **“Stone Wardens.”** These wear rune-collars regulating aggression and enhancing loyalty.

---

## **IV. Ecological Role**

The Crag-Hound maintains the balance between scavenger and sentinel. Packs clear carrion that would otherwise attract oozes or necro-insects, and their mineral waste deposits enrich soil for cliff moss and silverleaf ferns.

Guild records show a correlation between declining Hound populations and increased landslide frequency — implying their seismic howls aid natural stabilization.

---

## **V. Ministry Observations**

Lt. Korrin’s seismographs at **Fort Kingsfang** detected low-frequency hums matching Crag-Hound howls during tremor avoidance incidents.  
Inspector Vell confirmed that each pack maintains a shared harmonic tone unique to its bloodline — a natural ley stabilizer accidentally cultivated by centuries of living near relic sites.

Tame individuals trained by the **Louvain Road Watch** respond to whistle patterns derived from their own resonance signatures.

---

## **VI. Cultural Significance**

- **Beastfolk Masons:** Depict Crag-Hounds in carvings beside miners as “stone-kin.”
    
- **Delver Superstition:** A Crag-Hound pacing ahead of a caravan signals safe footing; one following too closely portends collapse.
    
- **Local Cuisine / Taboo:** Consuming Hound flesh is forbidden — bones ring like bells when boiled, believed to call avalanches.

## **IX. Containment & Field Guidelines**

- **Classification:** Level II – Moderate Threat / Domestication Possible.
    
- **Taming Directive:** Younglings must be bonded via tone imprinting within first week post-weaning.
    
- **Containment Protocol:** Keep within dry, resonance-proofed kennels; avoid metal enclosures that amplify howl waves.
    
- **Active Projects:** _Operation Stone Ward_ — study of Crag-Hound seismic communication for infrastructure monitoring.

## **X. Summary**

The **Crag-Hound** is the mountain’s memory given muscle — a relic guardian still pacing the roads it once protected.  
To the reckless, it is a beast of jaws and shale.  
To those who listen, it is the hum of the world reminding mortals that stone hears everything.
