# **Frost-Mane Elk**

### _Crownbeasts of the Nattefrost — “The Winter That Walks.”_

## **I. Overview**

The **Frost-Mane Elk** are colossal tundra grazers native to the permafrost plains of northern Oria and the **Nattefrost** Exclusion Zone.  
They are considered both miracle and menace: creatures so well adapted to the eternal cold that their presence lowers temperature within a hundred paces.

Local lodge-folk call them _“Hjortvinter”_ — _the Winter that Walks_ — believing each herd embodies the lingering breath of the first northern wind.  
The Ministry recognizes them as a keystone cryofaunal species whose migrations maintain the permafrost’s mana balance.

---

## **II. Physical Description**

Standing nearly **15 feet at the shoulder**, bulls bear antlers of **translucent frost-bone** that refract sunlight into spectral halos.  
A mane of crystalline filaments sprouts along the spine, constantly shedding hoarfrost that drifts like feathers.  
Their eyes scatter light rather than emit it — a natural defense that blinds predators and renders traditional tracking ineffective.

|**Attribute**|**Detail**|
|---|---|
|Height|12–15 ft (bulls), 10 ft (cows)|
|Weight|3–5 tons|
|Internal Temperature|−12 °C|
|Diet|Frost moss, lichen, glacial salts|
|Lifespan|≈ 200 years|
|Temperament|Passive unless provoked; violently territorial during rut|
___
## **III. Habitat & Migration**

Frost-Manes trace counter-sunward spirals through the **Nattefrost Plateau**, preserving equilibrium between ley-frost saturation and geothermal vents.  
Each equinox, herds vanish into the **White Maw** for hibernal communion — theorized to be a mass stasis ritual anchoring the region’s time field.  
Southward migrations leave mirrored hoof-ice paths visible from orbiting scry-eyes, termed _“ice-writing.”_

---

## **IV. Behavior & Ecology**

- **Thermoregulation:** Body aura converts ambient heat into structured snow crystals, forming moving blizzards.
    
- **Social Hierarchy:** Led by a matriarch marked by six-pointed antler spread; males defend perimeter via sonic bellows.
    
- **Predators:** None confirmed; even frost wyrms avoid their presence.
    
- **Corpse Reaction:** Dead Frost-Manes sublimate within hours, leaving perfect skeletal outlines burned into ice.
    

> _“When they run, the world around them slows, as if the cold rewrote time to match their stride.”_ — Lt. Korrin

---

## **V. Ministry Observations**

Drones from Station Epsilon recorded infrasonic pulses harmonizing with Nattefrost’s geomantic hum.  
Inspector Vell concludes that Frost-Manes distribute temporal stasis through resonance, preventing catastrophic thaw events.  
Field teams attempting live tagging experienced spontaneous frostbite through insulated suits. **All captures suspended under Directive §7-Polar-Fauna-Moratorium.**

---

## **VI. Cultural & Symbolic Role**

- **Oria Lodges:** Symbol of perseverance; carved antlers bless newborns against despair.
    
- **Beastfolk Artisans:** Reproduce mane fibers in song-woven cloaks to mimic “patience in motion.”
    
- **Delver Guilds:** Regard sightings as divine approval to traverse glacial labyrinths.
    

Legends foretell that when the final Frost-Mane perishes, the world’s last dawn will freeze in the sky.

## **VIII. Weakness & Delving Considerations**

Despite their monumental endurance, Frost-Manes possess vulnerabilities exploitable under controlled conditions:

|**Vulnerability**|**Explanation / Practical Note**|
|---|---|
|**Heat Shock Instability**|Sudden exposure to sustained radiant heat (> 80 °C) disrupts antler resonance, stunning the creature. Delvers using sun-lamps or fire glyphs can disperse a charging bull for ~12 seconds.|
|**Resonance Overload**|Strong bass-frequency vibration (≈ 22 Hz) causes mana interference; creature withdraws or collapses from equilibrium loss. Field horns tuned by the Rift Watch mimic this effect.|
|**Emotional Empathy Loop**|Empathic auras echo nearby fear; calm minds reduce aggression. Paladins trained in serene breathing recorded 0 hostile incidents within 10 m.|
|**Mirrored Ice Hazard**|Their reflected images contain trace temporal bleed; striking reflection rather than flesh negates retaliation but erases five minutes of attacker’s memory. _Use at own risk._|

## **X. Summary**

The **Frost-Mane Elk** embodies the covenant between life and the cold that sustains it.  
To witness a herd is to see the north breathe; to harm one is to make the world hold its breath.  
They are not beasts of winter — they are winter itself choosing to move.
