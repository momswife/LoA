# **Dewflame Moth**

### _Lanterns of the Wet Season — “The Candle That Drinks the Dark.”_

**Filed Division:** Ministry of Delving Operations — _The Living Atlas_ (Beastfolk & Natural Phenomena Studies)  
**Primary Compiler:** Archivist Yelna Stonevoice (Historian of Echo-Law & Cultural Songkeeping)

**Document Class:** _Natural Fauna Record / Verified Authenticity_

---

## **I. Overview**

The **Dewflame Moth** is a bioluminescent nocturnal insect found throughout **Vinyot’s rainforests** and the **southern Arnerian wetlands**.  
Renowned for its glimmering flight trails, it appears most frequently after rainstorms, drifting through mist like embers lost in fog.

The Ministry classifies the Dewflame as a **Type-NAT / Arc-Lumina Hybrid**, a naturally evolved species partially attuned to moisture-based aether.  
Its unique glow results not from heat, but from **condensed mana condensation** — each droplet of dew refracting stored light as the moth exhales mana vapor.

Locals regard it as both messenger and omen: to see one land upon your hand before dawn is said to mean a wish will soon find its answer.

---

## **II. Physical Description**

The Dewflame Moth’s wings span nearly **nine inches**, thin as rice paper yet patterned with veins of gold and faint cerulean.  
Each is dusted with microscopic **aether scales** that store moisture and release it as faint luminescence.

|**Attribute**|**Detail**|
|---|---|
|**Average Wingspan**|8–10 inches|
|**Weight**|< 1 oz|
|**Coloration**|Pale ivory with gradient blue tips; thorax glows when feeding|
|**Diet**|Nectar, mana-dew, fermented sap|
|**Lifespan**|12–14 days (wild), up to 60 days (containment)|
|**Temperament**|Non-aggressive; attracted to soft singing or rainfall vibrations|
When viewed in darkness, their wings appear as **candleflames without smoke**, trembling and reforming with every wingbeat.

---

## **III. Habitat & Behavior**

- **Habitat Preference:** Moist groves, flooded ruins, or labyrinth thresholds where condensation accumulates on stone.
    
- **Nesting:** Egg clusters laid on surfaces infused with relic residue — Ministry containment officers must check abandoned delver camps regularly.
    
- **Feeding Cycle:** Attracted to residual spell discharge; known to absorb small fragments of cantrip energy to sustain bioluminescence.
    
- **Nocturnal Swarming:** Every midsummer, thousands gather over marsh pools in the **Dewwake Migration**, producing light pillars visible for miles.
    

> _“The moth’s glow is neither borrowed nor stolen — it’s the world’s sigh turned visible.”_ — Yelna Stonevoice

---

## **IV. Ecological Role**

The Dewflame contributes to Aerathon’s humid ecosystems by pollinating **lumisap trees** and **mirage orchids**, plants dependent on mana-diffused pollinators.  
Their shed scales, known as _glowdust_, enrich soil conductivity, promoting growth of flora linked to small planar rifts.

In the wild, **frogfolk** and **reedsingers** follow Dewflame trails to locate leywater springs — a practice so reliable that Ministry cartographers use recorded flight patterns for seasonal mapping.

---

## **V. Magical Properties & Applications**

|**Material**|**Description**|**Usage**|
|---|---|---|
|**Glowdust**|Powder of discarded scales; faintly radiant when moist.|Alchemical reagent for non-heat lanterns and visibility potions.|
|**Mana-Dew Extract**|Fluid gathered from feeding sites.|Ingredient for restoration draughts; volatile when boiled.|
|**Aether Silk**|Wing membrane harvested humanely.|Used by scribes for “living ink” that glows when read aloud.|
Harvesting is legally limited to postmortem collection or shed material under **Living Atlas Provision 22-B**.

---

## **VI. Weakness & Delving Considerations**

|**Vulnerability**|**Explanation / Practical Note**|
|---|---|
|**Dry Heat Exposure**|Dehydrates the aether sacs, extinguishing light and causing death within minutes. Avoid open flame near swarms.|
|**Sonic Disruption**|Frequencies above 18 kHz distort wing rhythm, dispersing groups; useful for redirecting infestations from camps.|
|**Reflective Surfaces**|They mistake reflections for rival moths; mirror spells can redirect their migration harmlessly.|
|**Predatory Attraction**|Glow attracts nocturnal predators such as cloud vipers or ash-bats; delvers using bottled moth-light risk ambush.|
> _“Their beauty is their burden — every spark draws something that remembers hunger.”_ — Lt. Korrin

---

## **VII. Cultural & Symbolic Role**

- **Vinyot Poets:** Call them _“letters from the rain.”_ Poems etched in glowdust are burned at dusk so the message “flies home.”
    
- **Clerics of Selûne:** Use their wings as devotional bookmarks — believed to hold lunar blessings.
    
- **Delvers’ Superstition:** If a Dewflame lands on your pack before a delve, you will return — though not necessarily as yourself.
    

During the **Festival of Reignition**, citizens release glass orbs containing moth colonies skyward, each lamp dedicated to a lost friend or mentor.

---

## **VIII. Containment & Ministry Notes**

- **Classification:** Level I – Benign / Ecological Asset.
    
- **Containment:** Humidity-controlled aviaries lined with lumisap bark; no direct light spells within 20 ft.
    
- **Research Projects:**
    
    - _Operation Mothlight_ — study of bioluminescent reaction to residual spell auras.
        
    - _Dewwake Synchrony Map_ — tracking migration to predict monsoon cycles.
        

Unauthorized use of Glowdust in alchemy or weaponry constitutes **Class-1 Bioluminescent Harvest Violation** under Ministry Law 428.

---

## **IX. Summary**

The **Dewflame Moth** is the gentlest echo of Aerathon’s living magic — a creature of rainfall and memory that paints the night with quiet defiance.  
To see their light drift over still water is to glimpse the moment before a dream remembers it must fade.
