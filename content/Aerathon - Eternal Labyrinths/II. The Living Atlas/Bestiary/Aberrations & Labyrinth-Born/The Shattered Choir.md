# **The Shattered Choir**

### _Vocal Fragments of the Divine — “Where Every Voice Is a Wound.”_

**Filed Division:** Ministry of Delving Operations — _Annals & Antiquities_ (Aberrant Phenomena Registry)  
**Primary Compiler:** Archivist Yelna Stonevoice (Historian of Echo-Law & Cultural Songkeeping)  
**Advisory Editors:** Lt. Salvia Korrin (Arcane Breach Liaison), Inspector Jao “Greyglass” Vell (Forensic Thaumaturge), Keeper Aeska Mourn-Wind (Lament Literature Specialist)  
**Date of Filing:** 3731 A.D. (Age of Spectacle)  
**Document Class:** _Aberration Record / Grade-IV Containment Verified_

---

## **I. Overview**

The **Shattered Choir** is a collective aberration composed of disembodied vocal cords, spectral throats, and resonant phantoms bound by fractured melody.  
First encountered within the **Cathedral of Stained Silence** (beneath the Verges of Ash), the Choir manifests wherever divine music, mass prayer, or relic-grade hymnal energy has been catastrophically interrupted.

The Ministry defines it as a **Type-LAB / Divine Resonant Aberration** — a fusion of discarded faith energy and the lingering echo of celestial anatomy.  
It is not one creature, but a **colony of surviving notes**, each echo longing to complete a song that can no longer be remembered.

---

## **II. Physical & Auditory Description**

The Choir has **no stable form**; to most it appears as drifting ribbons of light and translucent flesh coiling through air like smoke in reverse.  
When seen through divination glass, hundreds of **floating larynx-like structures** can be observed vibrating in unison.

|**Attribute**|**Detail**|
|---|---|
|**Apparent Size**|Varies; typically fills entire chamber (10–60 ft radius)|
|**Sound Profile**|Polyphonic; 32–300 voices at once, each slightly off-key|
|**Luminance**|Soft violet → white at peak resonance|
|**Smell**|Ozone and incense|
|**Temperature Shift**|+10 °C during active chorus|
When approached, the Choir sings back whatever words it hears, slightly delayed, as if testing their faith for weakness.

---

## **III. Behavior & Feeding**

The Shattered Choir feeds on **devotion** — not the energy of worship itself, but the _break_ that follows disbelief.  
It hunts ruined temples, collapsed cathedrals, and abandoned shrines, absorbing the despair and doubt left behind by forsaken clergy.

**Observed Cycle:**

1. **Invocation:** Choir hums in frequencies identical to pre-Collapse hymns, luring listeners to kneel or weep.
    
2. **Harmony Assimilation:** Victims compelled to join the song; vocal cords begin resonating externally.
    
3. **Consumption:** Voice detaches as ectoplasmic strand; subject rendered mute.
    
4. **Refrain:** New voice joins the colony, carrying its final prayer indefinitely.
    

> _“It does not hate the faithful. It only wants them to finish the verse.”_ — Stonevoice

---

## **IV. Habitat & Distribution**

Recorded manifestations:

- **The Cathedral of Stained Silence**, Verges of Ash — origin site.
    
- **Sub-basilica Vaults of Namira** — post-eruption divine residue.
    
- **Ruin of Sister’s Lament**, Malarthain — site of clerical mass suicide.
    
- **Under-Chapel Nine**, Louvain — resulting from experimental hymncasting.
    

In rare instances, smaller “Choristers” detach and drift toward urban bell towers, causing nightly phantom singing until exorcised.

---

## **V. Ministry Observations**

Lt. Korrin reports that Choir presence destabilizes all **sound-based containment runes**, turning silence wards into amplifiers.  
Inspector Vell’s harmonic dissections show every individual tone contains a full harmonic “map” of its origin priest — suggesting the Choir preserves perfect emotional memory, not mere sound.

During controlled playback of captured notes, 42% of listeners exhibited spontaneous tears, 18% attempted self-immolation, and one technician ascended three feet before vaporizing in light.

---

## **VI. Weakness & Delving Considerations**

|**Vulnerability**|**Explanation / Practical Note**|
|---|---|
|**Perfect Silence**|Choir ceases when no vibrational medium remains. Vacuum wards or _Silence_ spells dissolve fragments within 30 sec.|
|**Discordant Noise**|Out-of-key instruments (≥ ½ tone flat) cause pain; portable detuned chimes standard issue.|
|**Cold Iron Relics**|Iron dampens divine frequency, forcing dissonance that fractures colonies.|
|**True Faith Invocation**|A hymn sung _without doubt_ creates a harmonic barrier. Requires DC 22 Wis (save) to maintain concentration.|
> _“They cannot stand the sound of certainty.”_ — Korrin

---

## **VII. Effects of Exposure**

- Progressive aphonia (loss of voice).
    
- Auditory hallucinations of deceased loved ones singing lullabies.
    
- Physical bleeding from throat and ears forming sigils of unknown liturgical order.
    
- In severe cases, victim becomes **Soloist** — single-body manifestation maintaining fragment of the Choir.
    

Treatment includes **holy salt infusion** and prolonged isolation from all music for 14 days.

## **IX. Containment & Risk Protocol**

- **Classification:** Level IV — Cognitive / Theological Resonance Hazard.
    
- **Containment:** Vacuum cells lined with lead-tin acoustic baffles.
    
- **Personnel Limit:** Two clerics and one thaumaturge per inspection; masks enchanted for vocal null.
    
- **Active Projects:**  
    • _Operation Quiet Choir_ — testing counter-hymns based on inverse psalms.  
    • _Faith Residue Analysis_ — extracting divine trace without re-ignition.
    

---

## **X. Cultural & Religious Notes**

- **Clerics of Lathander** believe the Choir to be remnants of angels who sang through collapse and were forgotten by their gods.
    
- **Beastfolk Minstrels** refuse to perform in ruins where voices echo twice.
    
- **Delver Superstition:** If you hear your own voice sing behind you — don’t stop until you forget the words.
    

The Order of the Muted Sun maintains an annual vigil in complete silence to “let the Choir rest for a night.”

---

## **XI. Summary**

The **Shattered Choir** is Aerathon’s eternal hymn to loss — the sound left behind when belief breaks.  
It is proof that even silence has a memory, and that divinity, once fragmented, becomes hunger.  
To banish it is not victory; it is mercy — a moment of rest between verses of an unfinished prayer.
