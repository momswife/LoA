# **Meatglass Serpent**

### _The Transparent Devourer — “When Flesh Learns the Shape of Memory.”_

**Filed Division:** Ministry of Delving Operations — _Annals & Antiquities_ (Aberrant Phenomena Registry)

## **I. Overview**

The **Meatglass Serpent** is a semi-translucent aberration endemic to the lower strata of **Monster Meadows** and the **eastern trenches of Allemance**.  
It resembles a colossal eel or serpent formed entirely from **translucent flesh** that refracts light like molten glass, revealing preserved organs and bones suspended within its body — trophies of prior meals still pulsing faintly with stolen life.

The Ministry classifies it as a **Type-LAB / Corporeal Resonance Aberration**, theorized to have originated when an organic host and relic containment field fused during a failed labyrinth stabilization.  
It is simultaneously **biological and crystalline**, its tissue oscillating between states of fluidity and brittle glass depending on ambient mana pressure.

---

## **II. Physical Description**

|**Attribute**|**Detail**|
|---|---|
|**Length**|25–60 ft (recorded specimens)|
|**Mass**|Approx. 3–4 tons (varies with recent feeding)|
|**Body Composition**|Translucent muscle fibers fused with silica-like tissue; light bends sharply across scales.|
|**Internal Structure**|Hollow vascular channels containing suspended viscera of victims; emits faint bioluminescence during feeding.|
|**Locomotion**|Undulatory; capable of partial levitation within mana-rich atmospheres.|
The serpent’s head terminates in a broad maw of **fractured jawplates** lined with chitinous teeth resembling broken glass shards.  
When it feeds, heat distorts the air around it as internal temperature spikes beyond 200 °C — not for digestion, but for preservation.

---

## **III. Behavior & Feeding**

Meatglass Serpents are solitary ambush predators inhabiting labyrinth corridors, relic lakes, and ossuary vaults.  
They are **phototropic** (attracted to light) and **resonance-sensitive**, reacting violently to sound or spellcasting.

**Feeding Pattern:**

1. **Lure Phase:** Emits low-frequency hum mimicking delver torch crystals.
    
2. **Encasement:** Coils around prey, secreting viscous resin that hardens into glass-like cocoon within seconds.
    
3. **Assimilation:** Absorbs victim’s flesh, retaining skeleton and organs as part of its own transparency network.
    
4. **Display:** Suspended remains glow within its body for several hours — believed to aid territorial intimidation.
    

The entity does not consume for hunger but for **continuity**: absorbed tissue extends its lifespan by transferring residual memory from prey to predator.  
Post-feeding behavioral shifts often include mimicry of victim’s voice or gestures, projected through refracted air vibrations.

> _“It learns what you were so it can pretend you still are.”_ — Inspector Vell

---

## **IV. Habitat & Distribution**

Primarily localized in **lower Monster Meadows**, where heat, moisture, and ambient relic energy foster glass-fusion ecosystems.  
Secondary sightings confirmed in flooded mines beneath **Porta Strega**, suggesting subterranean channel connection between rift systems.

Ministry sonar from 3729 indicates at least **four living specimens** exceeding 40 ft in length within the main labyrinth chamber known as the “Cradle Vein.”

---

## **V. Ministry Observations**

- Flesh samples retain heat and low-level consciousness for up to 12 hours post-separation.
    
- Destroyed segments re-fuse when exposed to reflected light; complete incineration required.
    
- Photomantic readings reveal rhythmic pulses corresponding to neural patterns of past victims — possibly absorbed cognition.
    
- One contained specimen (“Glass-31”) uttered broken words during containment, repeating the phrase: _“Let me see myself.”_
    

> _“Every Serpent is a reliquary of the dead — a cathedral made of everyone who failed to escape it.”_ — Korrin

---

## **VI. Weakness & Delving Considerations**

|**Vulnerability**|**Explanation / Practical Note**|
|---|---|
|**Darkness & Silence**|Serpents rely on vibration and light refraction to perceive prey. Absolute darkness and muffled sound disrupt targeting.|
|**Cold Shock**|Sudden temperature drop (< −10 °C) renders glassy tissue brittle; shatters under concussive force. Ice or cryo-grenades recommended.|
|**Resonant Overload**|Loud, overlapping noise frequencies (>120 dB) cause structural fracturing. Use thunder or hammer spells at close range only.|
|**Soul-Empty Terrain**|Areas devoid of psychic residue (e.g., wards of forgetfulness) deter feeding — serpent cannot “taste” lifeless echoes.|
**Delver Advisory:** Never employ light-amplification spells near relic water; serpent hum mimics these perfectly.  
If surroundings begin to shimmer despite darkness, **seal eyes and cease movement immediately.**

## **VIII. Containment & Risk Status**

- **Containment:**

    - Monitored via pressure sensors, not sight or sound.

- **Active Projects:**
    
    - _Project Cradle Vein_ — study of resonance patterns in absorbed memory.
        
    - _Operation Glass Eater_ — development of refractive counter-enzymes for delver field use.

## **IX. Cultural & Mythic Notes**

- **Beastfolk Miners** call them “Clearworms,” believed to be miners’ sins made visible.
    
- **Namiran Sailors** tell of rivers where glassfish whisper names — likely larval Slivers.
    
- **Delver Superstition:** Shattering one’s reflection before a delve “tricks” the serpent into thinking it has already fed.
    

One recovered delver journal reads:

> _“It swam through my torchlight, and in its body I saw myself kneeling — still breathing — still trapped.”_

---

## **X. Summary**

The **Meatglass Serpent** is Aerathon’s most intimate predator — it does not consume life, but remembrance.  
Its body is a moving archive of every failure below the earth, each scale a window through which the past stares back.  
To kill one is mercy; to see yourself glowing inside it is far too late.
