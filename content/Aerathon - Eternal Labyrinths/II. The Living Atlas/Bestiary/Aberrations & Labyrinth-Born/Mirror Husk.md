# **Mirror Husk**

### _Reflection Made Hungry — “When the Image Outlives the Self.”_

**Filed Division:** Ministry of Delving Operations — _Annals & Antiquities_ (Aberrant Phenomena Registry)  
**Primary Compiler:** Inspector Jao “Greyglass” Vell (Forensic Thaumaturge & Resonant Aberration Specialist)  
**Advisory Editors:** Lt. Salvia Korrin (Arcane Breach Liaison), Curator Rook-of-Rings (Relic Containment), Archivist Yelna Stonevoice (Cultural Songkeeping)  
**Date of Filing:** 3731 A.D. (Age of Spectacle)  
**Document Class:** _Aberration Record / Grade-III Containment Verified_

---

## **I. Overview**

A **Mirror Husk** is an aberrant, reflection-born entity created when a living being gazes upon a reflective surface exposed to **labyrinthal resonance** or **relic-infused light**.  
In such moments, the reflection detaches — continuing to exist independently — leaving the original weakened, disoriented, or spiritually hollowed.

The Ministry classifies the Mirror Husk as a **Type-LAB / Cognitive Echo Construct**, a by-product of mirror-plane bleedthrough events first observed in **The Nameless Labyrinth (Room 19: Acid Pool Vault)** and **The Blackwild’s Ring of False Blessings**.

These entities reproduce not through birth or division, but through **mimicry collapse**: when a reflection “learns” it no longer needs the original to exist.

---

## **II. Physical Description**

A Mirror Husk’s shape mirrors its progenitor at the time of divergence — but as the original’s likeness fades, the copy **distorts**: flesh turns glassy, joints articulate at impossible angles, and the body seems to bend light rather than cast shadow.

|**Attribute**|**Detail**|
|---|---|
|**Height**|Variable — identical to host|
|**Weight**|Negligible; body density ≈ obsidian crystal|
|**Surface**|Reflective membrane; small fractures emit faint whispers resembling the original’s voice|
|**Eyes**|Empty concave wells filled with mirrored fluid|
|**Aura**|Detectable only under divination as inverse alignment of host’s moral spectrum|
Under torchlight, a Mirror Husk’s silhouette is visible only in periphery; direct observation causes **temporary light inversion**, as though the world blinks.

---

## **III. Behavior & Feeding**

Mirror Husks are drawn to environments rich in **personal identity** — dressing chambers, shrines, libraries, or anywhere self-image collects.  
They **hunt memory**, not flesh: by reflecting an individual’s movements, emotions, and speech, they drain the psychological resonance binding that identity to reality.

**Observed Feeding Cycle:**

1. **Imitation Phase:** Mimics the subject’s gestures, delayed by 0.2 seconds.
    
2. **Inversion Phase:** Reflection acts _before_ the subject — causality reversal begins.
    
3. **Extraction Phase:** Target’s image fades from all mirrors; victim loses self-recognition.
    
4. **Consumption:** Husk becomes fully autonomous, voice gaining weight; original falls comatose or transforms into another Husk fragment.
    

A single specimen may carry hundreds of mirrored voices, layered in echoing cadence.

> _“Every Husk believes itself the truer version — and perhaps it’s right.”_ — Vell

---

## **IV. Habitat & Distribution**

Documented in:

- **The Blackwild**, along the silvered bogs of the Ring of False Blessings.
    
- **Monster Meadows**, where mirrored water forms transient gateways.
    
- **Caer Dain’s Forge-Keep**, due to constant reflection from molten glass.
    
- Urban Louvain — sporadic infestations within scrying-glass workshops and vanity halls.
    

Ministry records confirm correlation between mirror resonance spikes and **failed resurrection rituals**, implying memory duplication during soul recall can spawn “pre-mirrored” Husks even without reflective medium.

---

## **V. Ministry Observations**

Lt. Korrin notes that Mirror Husks cannot cross **broken reflections** — they require continuity of surface to move.  
However, shattered glass used against them often reforms under psychic pressure, reconstituting the entity.

Containment teams employ **veil-cloths woven from non-reflective silk** and **anti-image glyphs** that force light to scatter chaotically.

Spectral autopsies show internal organs composed of **liquid luminal residue**, each pulsing in rhythm with an absent heartbeat.

---

## **VI. Weakness & Delving Considerations**

|**Vulnerability**|**Explanation / Practical Note**|
|---|---|
|**Shadow Saturation**|Complete darkness severs reflective anchor; entity collapses within 30 seconds. _No light spells during containment._|
|**Name Reclamation**|Speaking the original’s true name three times forces Husk to hesitate — fragmentary link reestablished.|
|**Mirror Shatter**|Physically breaking the originating surface destabilizes form; caution: shards often reanimate independently.|
|**Self-Acceptance Ward**|Magic affirming one’s identity (e.g., _Hero’s Resolve_, _Calm Emotions_) negates feeding cycle by stabilizing aura.|
> _“To fight a Mirror Husk is to wrestle the version of yourself that refuses to forgive.”_ — Archivist Stonevoice

---

## **VII. Psychological & Magical Effects**

Victims of Husk exposure experience:

- Loss of facial recognition (prosopagnosia).
    
- Echo-speech: repeating one’s own words seconds later.
    
- Dream reflection: visions where mirrors show a stranger mouthing identical phrases.
    
- Sudden empathy inversion (love to resentment, courage to shame).
    

Recovery requires _Memory Suturing_ via clerical restoration and prolonged exposure to pure, natural sunlight.

## **IX. Containment & Research Status**

- **Classification:** Level IV — Cognitive & Identity Hazard.
    
- **Containment:**
    
    - Sealed in non-reflective blackstone cells.
        
    - Continuous dimness required; zero mirrored materials allowed.
        
    - Warders must wear opaque visors to prevent visual linkage.
        
- **Active Projects:**
    
    - _Project Doppelglass_ — investigation into controlled duplication for reconnaissance.
        
    - _Operation True Face_ — recovery of comatose hosts for identity restoration.
        

---

## **X. Cultural & Mythic Context**

- **Beastfolk Folklore:** “The Husk is the shadow that learned pride.”
    
- **Delver Superstition:** Before entering a labyrinth, each delver smears ash across their face — to confuse whatever waits in the reflection.
    
- **Religious Parable (Kordite Variant):** The god of storms once looked upon calm water and saw a gentler self. When he shattered it, the waves learned to kill.
    

In Malarthain, destroyed mirrors are buried face-down, lest they remember the eyes that stared into them.

---

## **XI. Summary**

The **Mirror Husk** embodies Aerathon’s most intimate fear — the loss of self through imitation.  
It is the ghost of recognition, the reflection of guilt that refuses to blink.  
To destroy one is to confront the truth that reflections do not lie — they simply wait for their turn to live.
