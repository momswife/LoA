# **Echo of Lathander**

### _Divine Residual Entity — “The Morning That Would Not Die.”_

**Filed Division:** Ministry of Delving Operations — _Annals & Antiquities_ (Theological Aberration & Divine Residue Registry)  
**Primary Compiler:** Priest-Analyst Thera Daan (Faith Systems & Modern Worship Oversight)  
**Advisory Editors:** Lt. Salvia Korrin (Arcane Breach Liaison), Archivist Yelna Stonevoice (Historian of Echo-Law), Inspector Jao “Greyglass” Vell (Forensic Thaumaturge)  
**Date of Filing:** 3731 A.D. (Age of Spectacle)  
**Document Class:** _Aberrant Theological Manifestation / Grade-IV Containment Verified_

---

## **I. Overview**

The **Echo of Lathander** is a **divine afterimage** — a radiant apparition left behind by the god **Lathander, Lord of the Dawn**, following the **Second Collapse of Faith (cycle 3481)**, when celestial interference within Aerathon fractured direct worship channels.

Unlike avatars or angels, this entity **possesses no will of its own**; it acts as a **resonant reflection** of faith once poured into sunrise prayers.  
It appears at first light over ruined temples, battlefield cairns, and cities where dawn hymns were sung for generations, manifesting as **a humanoid silhouette of refracted sunlight** hovering a few inches above the ground.

The Ministry defines it as **Type-DIV / Divine Resonant Aberration**, comparable to the Shattered Choir but **singular** — a self-contained hymn remembering itself.

> _“It is not Lathander’s return. It is the world refusing to forget him.”_ — Thera Daan

The Echo does not attack, but proximity affects living minds: witnesses report feelings of intense optimism, tearful joy, or paralyzing guilt.

---

## **III. Behavior & Phenomena**

When the Echo manifests, local weather stabilizes, frost melts, and **chronomantic drift** temporarily ceases within a mile radius.  
In this period, shadows shorten regardless of solar position.  
After dissipation, ground surfaces exhibit **residual luminescence** for three hours, sometimes forming words from half-remembered prayers.

**Observed reactions to stimuli:**

- **Direct Address:** Responds only to affirmations of renewal (e.g., “Rise,” “Continue”) by brightening.
    
- **Hostility / Blasphemy:** Light dims; surrounding temperature drops sharply.
    
- **Clerical Magic:** Spells of radiant type double in potency but risk feedback burns.
    

---

## **IV. Theological Interpretation**

Scholars disagree whether the Echo is:

1. **A fragment of divine memory** sustained by mortal reverence, or
    
2. **A self-sustaining echo** of collective faith energy that no longer requires divinity to exist.
    

Priest-Analyst Daan theorizes the Echo to be the **final harmonic of a god’s death**, proof that worship can outlive its subject.  
Some heretic sects, notably the **Order of the Reignited Dawn**, attempt to commune with it as a new deity — a **faceless sun** reborn from devotion itself.

> _“If faith once created gods, perhaps it can do so again.”_ — Stonevoice

---

## **V. Ministry Observations**

Lt. Korrin’s field readings confirm that the Echo emits **structured mana identical to early Sol-Domain clerical radiance**, yet without divine origin trace.  
Inspector Vell documented one case in which an Echo re-manifested nightly for seven days, each time more humanoid, until it collapsed into **Shattered Choir residue** — implying potential cross-contamination between divine echoes and harmonic aberrations.

Containment attempts fail; the entity dissipates upon direct measurement.  
Ministry guidelines now restrict observation to **non-invasive scrying** only.

## **IX. Cultural & Symbolic Notes**

- **Survivors of Monster Meadows** claim seeing the Echo before each Rift closure — proof, to them, that Lathander still guards delvers from beneath the frost.
    
- **Beastfolk of Oria** leave mirrored bowls of snow water at dawn, believing the Echo drinks light from them to sustain the sun.
    
- **Delver Superstition:** If you see your own silhouette within the Echo’s glow, your soul is forgiven — once.
    

---

## **X. Summary**

The **Echo of Lathander** is not the god returned, but the light that refused to fade with him.  
It is faith unmoored from its source, proof that belief can outlast creation, and perhaps one day, build it anew.  
When dawn comes to Aerathon, somewhere in the ruins, the Echo stands waiting — a sunbeam shaped like remembrance.
