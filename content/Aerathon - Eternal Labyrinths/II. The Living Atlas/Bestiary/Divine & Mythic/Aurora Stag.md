# **Aurora Stag**

### _Crownlights of the Nattefrost Expanse — “The Sky’s Memory Given Hooves.”_

**Filed Division:** Ministry of Delving Operations — _The Living Atlas_ (Beastfolk & Natural Phenomena Studies)  
**Primary Compiler:** Researcher Falyn Onarra (Beastfolk Sociology & Adaptation Studies)

**Date of Filing:** 3731 A.D. (Age of Spectacle)  
**Document Class:** _Natural Fauna Record / Verified Authenticity_

## **I. Overview**

The **Aurora Stag** is a legendary tundra species that inhabits the **Nattefrost Expanse**, sharing migratory corridors with the **Frost-Mane Elk** but existing in smaller, more elusive herds.  
Where the Frost-Mane tempers the cold, the Aurora Stag **creates light from it** — bending ambient mana through crystalline antlers that refract auroral ribbons into the night sky.

The Ministry classifies it as a **Type-NAT / Mana-Photonic Herbivore**, a rare example of organic lumomancy.  
Local trappers call them _“Skyborne Kings,”_ believing their gallop sustains the northern lights themselves.

> _“They are not chased by dawn — they are its couriers.”_ — Stonevoice

---

## **II. Physical Description**

Aurora Stags are sleek, tall cervids with pale silver coats that shimmer like mirrored frost under moonlight.  
Their **antlers** are the defining trait — a prismatic lattice of translucent crystal that constantly hums with refracted mana.  
During high aurora activity, these antlers glow in gradients of turquoise, violet, and rose, often visible from miles away.

|**Attribute**|**Detail**|
|---|---|
|**Height**|8–9 ft (shoulder), 12 ft including antlers|
|**Weight**|900–1,200 lbs|
|**Antler Composition**|Cryo-silicate crystal threaded with leyline energy|
|**Eye Color**|Pale opal, shifting hue with local aurora|
|**Diet**|Frost lichen, mana-infused moss, glacial salt|
|**Temperament**|Cautious, migratory, fiercely protective of calves|
## **III. Habitat & Behavior**

Aurora Stags dwell in **high-altitude plateaus**, **ley-charged permafrost fields**, and **crystalline valleys** where sunlight refracts strongly through ice.  
They move seasonally between **the Nattefrost**, **northern Oria**, and the **Isle of Cries** when leyflow surges northward.

**Behavioral Notes:**

- Migrate in herds of three to five; larger groups indicate seasonal convergence.
    
- Communicate through light pulses emitted from antlers — readable as rhythmic Morse-like flashes.
    
- Their presence stabilizes weather, lowering storm volatility and aligning aurora patterns overhead.
    
- During solstice, bulls duel by clashing antlers, producing radiant shockwaves that momentarily silence all sound.
    

> _“When they fight, even thunder holds its breath.”_ — Korrin

---

## **IV. Magical Properties**

The Aurora Stag channels **photomantic resonance** — absorbing ambient mana and releasing it as structured light.  
When startled, it emits a **“Radiant Bloom”** — a blinding halo that disrupts sight and suppresses evocation magic within 60 ft for up to 10 seconds.  
Their hooves leave behind faintly glowing prints that linger for hours, often mistaken as waypoints by delvers lost in whiteouts.

### **Aurora Trails**

Each step along migration paths infuses the snow with prismatic frost. Scholars believe these trails form **temporary ley conduits** that equalize mana discharge across the tundra, preventing geomantic collapse during auroral storms.

---

## **V. Ecological Role**

Aurora Stags serve as **environmental regulators** in polar ecosystems:

- **Mana Dispersal:** Prevents leyline surges from destabilizing tundra flora.
    
- **Cryo-Photosynthesis Catalysts:** Stimulate growth of light-dependent moss species beneath ice layers.
    
- **Predator Deterrence:** Emit radiant pulses that repel frost-wyrms and cold elementals.
    

The Ministry categorizes them as _Tier-I Preservation Priority_; poaching or artifact harvesting constitutes a **Class-1 Ecological Breach** under Law 421.

---

## **VI. Weakness & Delving Considerations**

|**Vulnerability**|**Explanation / Practical Note**|
|---|---|
|**Darkfield Environments**|Lack of ambient light prevents photomantic refraction; stag becomes sluggish and partially blind.|
|**Sound Shock**|Sonic bursts (>120 dB) destabilize antler resonance, disorienting balance.|
|**Mirror Traps**|Excessive reflection confuses light-navigation instincts, inducing panic.|
|**Corrupted Leyflow**|Exposure to chaotic mana causes aurora inversion, turning light black — lethal within minutes.|
**Delver Advisory:** Never attempt to mount or ride one; its reflexive light surge burns exposed skin and overloads enchantments.

> _“To cage an aurora is to silence the sky.”_ — Vell

## **VIII. Cultural & Mythic Notes**

- **Beastfolk of Oria:** Call them _“Dawn-Walkers.”_ Tradition says the gods painted the first sky upon their antlers.
    
- **Delver Superstition:** If a stag’s reflection appears in your weapon, you are destined to survive your next delve — but your blade will never kill cleanly again.
    
- **Nattefrost Tribes:** Hold solstice vigils in silence; if the sky flares, it means an Aurora Stag has accepted their prayer.
    

> _“Their antlers are the firmament’s handwriting.”_ — Stonevoice

---

## **IX. Containment & Research Status**

- **Classification:** Level I — Protected Natural Fauna / Low Threat, High Significance.
    
- **Containment:** None; open-range observation only.
    
- **Active Projects:**
    
    - _Project Dawnweave_ — study of antler refractive mana for sustainable light grids.
        
    - _Operation North Halo_ — track migration through astral triangulation satellites.
        

No specimens currently held in Ministry custody; to capture one is considered ecological desecration.

---

## **X. Summary**

The **Aurora Stag** is the breath between storm and silence — living light treading frozen earth.  
It is neither prey nor spirit, but the north’s living covenant with the sky: a reminder that beauty in Aerathon can still be born of balance, not violence.  
When its antlers blaze, even the labyrinths pause to watch.
