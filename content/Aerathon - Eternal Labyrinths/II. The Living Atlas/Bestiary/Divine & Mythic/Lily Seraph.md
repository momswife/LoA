# **Lily Seraphs**

### _Celestial Remnants of the Lost Concord — “When Angels Learned to Take Root.”_

**Filed Division:** Ministry of Delving Operations — _Annals & Antiquities_ (Theological Aberration & Celestial Residue Archive)

**Document Class:** _Divine Aberration Record / Grade-IV Containment Verified_

---

## **I. Overview**

The **Lily Seraphs** are radiant, plant-bodied celestials that manifest during mass divine interference events — particularly near shrines once consecrated to Lathander, Sune, and Corellon.  
They resemble angels **grown** rather than sent, their wings formed of veined petals and light-pollen, their eyes like drops of dawn refracted through dew.

They are the remnants of the **Lost Concord**, the final coalition of celestial beings who attempted to descend after the gods’ withdrawal from Aerathon.  
Denied passage through stable divine channels, their essence **rooted itself in mortal soil**, sprouting into hybrid life forms that remember Heaven as a half-dream.

The Ministry classifies them as **Type-DIV / Celestial-Floral Aberrations**, living hymns of photosynthetic divinity.

> _“They breathe sunlight, not air — every sigh is a prayer in bloom.”_ — Thera Daan

## **II. Physical Description**

|**Attribute**|**Detail**|
|---|---|
|**Height**|9–12 ft (variable; stems elongate with daylight)|
|**Wingspan**|Up to 20 ft, composed of translucent petal membranes|
|**Body Composition**|Luminous chlorophyllous tissue reinforced with crystal-fibers|
|**Coloration**|White-gold to rose-pink under daylight; silver under moonlight|
|**Voice Profile**|Tri-harmonic chorus, each tone offset by 0.1 seconds (produces “echo bloom” effect)|
When stationary, a Lily Seraph appears as a **giant flowering stalk crowned by a faceless mask of light**.  
When mobile, petals flare into winglike extensions; its passage leaves behind trails of glowing pollen that sing faintly for hours.

---

## **III. Behavior & Temperament**

The Lily Seraph is neither predator nor passive guardian — it is **driven by memory, not mission**.  
It seeks places of former worship, standing motionless for days while emitting a low hymn that stimulates plant growth and heals small injuries within 30 ft.  
However, when confronted with blasphemy, violence, or despair, the hymn shifts to a dissonant **lament phase**, causing necrosis of all surrounding flora and flesh.

**Observed Behavioral States:**

1. **Benediction Bloom** – Harmless; heals and purifies.
    
2. **Lament Wilt** – Triggered by conflict or lies; releases radiant toxin.
    
3. **Ascension Reflex** – Final defense mechanism; bursts into light and rises skyward, leaving crater of fused glass.
    

> _“They forgive, until the world reminds them why it fell.”_ — Korrin

---

## **IV. Habitat & Sightings**

Documented manifestations include:

- **Throne of the Easterlilies** — primary genesis site; five confirmed appearances since 3702.
    
- **Temple Ruins of Namira’s Glass Fields** — single Seraph, petrified in mid-flight; now a Ministry reliquary.
    
- **Louvain Upper Gardens** — rare “Seed Choir” event, where pollen formed miniature replicas that sang for seven nights.
    

Seraphs are drawn to **strong solar ley intersections** or **mass graves of the faithful** — where faith and decay mingle in equal measure.

## **V. Ministry Observations**

- Photomantic study confirms **cellular photosynthesis fused with radiant mana**, generating unlimited self-sustaining energy during daylight.
    
- Autopsies impossible; corpses disintegrate into sterile lilies upon exposure to darkness.
    
- Inspector Vell recorded residual voices speaking in reversed hymn sequences — linguistic analysis matches pre-Collapse Celestial dialects.
    
- Thera Daan’s theological report notes that every Seraph utterance includes the word _“Again.”_

## **IX. Cultural & Mythic Notes**

- **Beastfolk of Oria** tell of “The Lily Knights,” Seraphs who carried fallen heroes skyward as flowers.
    
- **Delver Superstition:** If Seraph pollen lands on your blade, your next victory will save a life instead of taking one.
    
- **Monastic Orders:** Cultivate lily gardens on high towers to “invite forgiveness to roost.”
    
- **Faith Heresies:** The Bloomed Church claims Seraphs are proof that heaven has begun to take root on Aerathon — a “new Eden by accident.”
    

> _“Perhaps Heaven did not fall. Perhaps it germinated.”_ — Daan

---

## **X. Summary**

The **Lily Seraphs** are not angels nor gods reborn — they are the memory of purity re-interpreted by the earth itself.  
They bloom where faith has rotted, bringing mercy or wrath depending on the heart that beholds them.  
To some they are salvation in petals; to others, a reminder that even light can grow wild.

When the dawn light scatters through their wings, Aerathon remembers what grace used to sound like — and for a moment, the world holds its breath.
