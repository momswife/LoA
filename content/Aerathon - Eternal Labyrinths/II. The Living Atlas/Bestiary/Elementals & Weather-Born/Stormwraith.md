# **Stormwraith**

### _Echoes of the Tempestrise — “When the Wind Learns Your Name.”_

## **I. Overview**

The **Stormwraith** is an electrical revenant formed from the union of thunder magic and lost souls struck down amid violent tempests.  
They manifest within the cloud-belts of **Tempestrise**, above the **Betemere Peaks**, and across open seas during leyline electrical surges.  
Unlike typical air elementals, Stormwraiths retain **memory and intent**, haunting the winds where they died.

They are considered **Type-ELEM / Electrogenic Phantasm Hybrids**, conscious weather phenomena sustained by residual spirit charge.  
Pilots and delvers call them _“Thunderborn,”_ though sailors prefer _“The Grieving Wind.”_

> _“Each one carries a storm’s unfinished sentence.”_ — Stonevoice

---

## **II. Physical Description**

|**Attribute**|**Detail**|
|---|---|
|**Height (manifest)**|8–12 ft when condensed|
|**Composition**|Ionized vapor, plasmic lightning threads, spectral residue|
|**Luminance**|Pulses in rhythm with thunder — blue-white to violet|
|**Temperature**|Variable; drops ambient air by 10–15 °C before strike|
|**Sound Profile**|Layered whispers beneath thunderclaps, often in names|
To mortal eyes they appear as **humanoid outlines woven from storm-light**, drifting with electrical trails that mimic torn banners.  
Within the core flicker faint skeletal shapes — not bone, but flash-burn silhouettes preserved from their deaths.

---

## **III. Behavior & Phenomena**

Stormwraiths are **neither malicious nor benign** — they are drawn to strong emotion, repeating gestures from their final moments.  
Common displays include arms outstretched toward the sky, kneeling mid-air, or circling flocks of birds like escorts.

**Documented Abilities**

- **Chain Lightning Possession:** Can inhabit active electrical currents, redirecting bolts with intent.
    
- **Voice of the Gale:** Speaks through wind pressure differentials; words audible only in lulls between thunder.
    
- **Atmospheric Memory:** Briefly restores clarity to skies they haunt — a calm “eye” surrounding their form.
    
- **Soul Induction:** Contact with living beings induces vivid recollections of drowning, falling, or ascent.
    

> _“They remember falling forever, and envy everything that lands.”_ — Korrin

---

## **IV. Habitat & Occurrence**

Most frequent during:

- **Tempestrise’s Spiral Season** — electrical storms exceeding 60 mana kiloteslas.
    
- **Namiran Sea Fronts** — where lightning rods and ship masts form artificial “spires.”
    
- **The Aether Spine** — high mountain ridges with metallic ore veins acting as spirit conductors.
    

During calm years, residual Stormwraiths linger as faint ball lightning, often mistaken for _faelight orbs._

---

## **V. Ministry Observations**

Inspector Vell’s resonance logs show each Wraith carries a distinct harmonic pattern — matching the heartbeat of its former body at death.  
This rhythm, called the **Thunder Pulse**, can be mirrored to pacify or communicate with them.  
Lt. Korrin’s experiments with grounding sigils demonstrate temporary containment achievable by **harmonic grounding forks** tuned to 432 Hz.

Rogue occurrences have ignited three Ministry airships since 3726.  
Containment priority upgraded to _Grade-III Live Hazard._

## **IX. Cultural & Mythic Notes**

- **Beastfolk Seafarers:** Leave bronze bells unbound on masts so the dead can “ring their way home.”
    
- **Storm-Chaser Guilds:** Believe a captured Wraith grants immunity to fear — until its first scream.
    
- **Delver Superstition:** If lightning strikes in silence, a Wraith has taken the thunder for itself.
    
- **Faith Orders of Kord:** Interpret them as fragments of divine laughter — proof the storm still remembers joy.
    

> _“Each flash of lightning is the sky rehearsing their resurrection.”_ — Daan

---

## **X. Summary**

The **Stormwraith** is the will of the tempest given echo — a ghost made of weather, mourning its own thunder.  
They are the elegies of fallen sky-walkers, the breath between bolt and silence.  
To see one is to glimpse the moment when nature’s rage becomes remembrance, and realize that even the storm grieves what it strikes.
