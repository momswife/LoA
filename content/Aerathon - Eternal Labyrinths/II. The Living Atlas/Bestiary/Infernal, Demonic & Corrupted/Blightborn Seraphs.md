# **Blightborn Seraphs**

### _Fallen Healers Twisted by Rot — “When Mercy Is Taught to Decay.”_

**Filed Division:** Ministry of Delving Operations — _The Living Atlas_ (Infernal, Demonic & Corrupted Entity Archive)  
**Primary Compiler:** Priest-Analyst Thera Daan (Faith Oversight & Corruption Phenomena)  
**Advisory Editors:** Lt. Salvia Korrin (Arcane Breach Liaison), Inspector Jao “Greyglass” Vell (Forensic Thaumaturge), Archivist Yelna Stonevoice (Echo-Law Historian)  
**Date of Filing:** 3733 A.D.  
**Document Class:** _Corrupted Celestial Record / Grade-IV Combat Hazard_

---

## **I. Overview**

**Blightborn Seraphs** are corrupted remnants of celestial servitors once tasked with safeguarding growth, healing, and renewal in Aerathon.  
Once radiant, they were twisted by **The Briar Plague**, **Infernal psalm-poison**, or **Labyrinthic cursefields**, depending on region — but the result is always the same:

An angel whose grace has turned septic.  
A healer who now unravels flesh.  
A winged being dripping rot instead of light.

They are classified by the Ministry as **Type-INF / Corrupted Celestial Aberrants** — a category reserved for former divine beings whose original function persists in perverse, destructive form.

> _“Their blessings curdle into boils.  
> Their hymns ferment into disease.”_ — Daan

---

## **II. Physical Description**

|**Attribute**|**Detail**|
|---|---|
|**Height**|7–10 ft|
|**Wings**|Once radiant; now tattered membranes veined with black ichor|
|**Skin**|Sickly pale or grey-green, cracked like dying bark|
|**Eyes**|Burning pits of yellow infection-light; tears drip as corrosive ichor|
|**Halo**|Corrupted into a rusted thorn-ring floating unevenly|
|**Hands**|Elongated, bone-thin, ending in needlelike talons|
|**Aura**|Radius of wilting plantlife, mildew scent, and faint sobbing choir-tones|
Their bodies perpetually leak **blight ichor**, a substance that behaves half like acid, half like fungal contagion.

When wounded, instead of bleeding, they shed pale mothlike spores.

---

## **III. Behavior & Phenomena**

Blightborn Seraphs behave with tragic, inverted purpose.  
They seek out sickness, injury, and sorrow — not to heal, but to **“complete the cycle”** by hastening decay.

**Observed Behaviors:**

- **Mercy-Seeking:** Approach those in pain and “comfort” them with lethal touch.
    
- **Rotting Benediction:** Their prayers cause crops, limbs, or stone to crumble.
    
- **Choir of Flies:** Summon swarms of bile-touched insects that replicate their afflicted hymns.
    
- **Penance Pursuit:** Fixate on individuals hiding guilt or shame, often whispering their sins aloud.
    
- **Lightless Resurrection:** Attempt to “raise” dead matter as fungal puppets — grotesque but short-lived.
    

> _“They believe they are helping.”_ — Stonevoice

Their minds retain the logic of angels, but every instinct is reversed into harm.

---

## **IV. Habitat & Occurrence**

Blightborn Seraphs emerge at corruption hotspots:

- **Briar Throne war-zones** where Feyline curses go septic
    
- **Depth chambers of corrupted labyrinths**
    
- **Battlefields soaked with infernal residue**
    
- **Ancient shrines where divine wards have collapsed**
    
- **The ash forests of Veyra following plague storms**
    
- **Temple ruins where healing magic curdled after mass death**
    

They are most active following moons of **collective despair or disease outbreaks**, drawn to areas where many pleaded for salvation that never came.

---

## **V. Ministry Observations**

- Celestial sigils within their flesh still glow faintly, suggesting their divine code struggles against corruption.
    
- Their ichor reacts violently to holy symbols, sometimes screaming in multiple voices.
    
- Lt. Korrin recorded a Seraph pausing mid-attack to cradle a dying boar — before liquefying it in sorrow.
    
- Choir residue analysis indicates they still chant **aborted healing psalms**, but the words have been warped by entropy.
    
- Some Seraphs have attempted to “repair” destroyed landscapes, inadvertently spreading fungal overgrowth that collapses entire ecosystems.
    

> _“They remember mercy.  
> That is what makes them so dangerous.”_ — Vell

## **VI. Weakness & Delving Considerations**

|**Vulnerability**|**Explanation / Practical Note**|
|---|---|
|**True Radiance**|Genuine holy magic burns corruption, weakening them dramatically.|
|**Silvered Weapons**|Prevent ichor regeneration; sever corrupted sigils.|
|**Purity Soundfields**|Singing healing hymns or playing clear tones disrupts their psalm-poison.|
|**Dry Heat**|Evaporates blight ichor; slows their fungal abilities.|
**Delver Advisory:**  
Never accept a “blessing” from a Blightborn Seraph.  
Their touch induces rapid necrosis disguised as warm relief.

## **IX. Cultural & Mythic Notes**

- **Namiran Sailors:** Describe them as “angels drowned in sickness.”
    
- **Veyran Priests:** Burn thorn effigies to ward against their passage.
    
- **Delver Folklore:** A Seraph’s shadow falling across you means someone close will fall ill.
    
- **Briar Throne Rumor:** Some claim the Seraphs were born when a Fey Queen attempted to “heal the gods” and failed catastrophically.
    

> _“They grieve for what they used to be.  
> And grief, corrupted, becomes plague.”_ — Daan

---

## **X. Summary**

**Blightborn Seraphs** are living tragedies — once radiant healers, now agents of decay and inverted mercy.  
Driven by their former purpose but twisted by rot, they wander Aerathon seeking to “finish” the cycle of life by accelerating its end.

They are not inherently evil.  
They are catastrophically misguided.

And perhaps that is what makes them the most terrifying of all corrupted beings:

**They still believe they are saving us.**
