# **Cinder Imps**

### _Ashspawn Tricksters of Flame — “What Mischief Looks Like When It Learns to Burn.”_

**Filed Division:** Ministry of Delving Operations — _The Living Atlas_ (Infernal, Demonic & Corrupted Entity Archive)  
**Primary Compiler:** Curator Rook-of-Rings (Relics Registrar & Thermal Anomaly Specialist)  
**Advisory Editors:** Lt. Salvia Korrin (Arcane Breach Liaison), Inspector Jao “Greyglass” Vell (Thaumaturgic Forensics), Priest-Analyst Thera Daan (Faith Oversight)  
**Date of Filing:** 3733 A.D.  
**Document Class:** _Lesser Infernal Entity Record / Grade-II Hazard_

---

## **I. Overview**

**Cinder Imps** are volatile, ash-born infernal creatures that skitter out of **overheated forges, volcanic vents, firepits, and cursed furnaces**.  
They form when **malice, impatience, or petty rage** is exhaled into flame — emotional pollution that ignites into a spark of living mischief.

While significantly weaker than true demons, Cinder Imps are dangerous due to their unpredictability, pyromantic nature, and tendency to multiply in chaotic environments.

The Ministry classifies them as **Type-INF / Minor Infernal Pyromorphs**, closely related to soot devils and ember sprites but far more malicious.

> _“They’re not born evil. They’re born annoyed… and it gets worse from there.”_  
> — Korrin

---

## **II. Physical Description**

|**Attribute**|**Detail**|
|---|---|
|**Size**|1–2 ft tall|
|**Body**|Humanoid silhouette made of charcoal, ember-veins, and constantly shedding ash|
|**Eyes**|Bright pinpoints of molten gold|
|**Mouth**|Cracked grin that drips sparks when they laugh|
|**Limbs**|Long, clawed fingers like burnt twigs|
|**Tail**|Ember whip capable of delivering light burns|
|**Voice**|Squeaky cackle mixed with soft crackles|
Their bodies crumble like charcoal when struck — but each crumble releases flammable dust.

---

## **III. Behavior & Phenomena**

Cinder Imps are defined by **pyromantic mischief** and love to spread chaos:

**Observed Behaviors:**

- **Spark-Jump:** Teleport through flames or hot coals up to 30 ft.
    
- **Ash Mischief:** Steal small items, especially metal, tossing them into fires.
    
- **Coal Mimicry:** Curl into a dormant ember-state and “wake” when someone stokes the fire.
    
- **Forge Sabotage:** Warp temperature flow, crack crucibles, or ignite unintended materials.
    
- **Ember Swarm:** When frightened, split into 3–5 smaller imps, each weaker but faster.
    

> _“If something’s burning that shouldn’t be, odds are a Cinder Imp thought it was funny.”_  
> — Rook-of-Rings

They rarely attack directly unless cornered, instead preferring to provoke missteps, start small fires, or taunt delvers.

---

## **IV. Habitat & Occurrence**

Cinder Imps emerge wherever **heat, emotion, and infernal resonance** intersect:

- **Caer Dain’s industrial furnaces**
    
- **Malarthain volcanic tunnels**
    
- **Porta Strega’s glass kilns during surge storms**
    
- **Labyrinth chambers powered by fire cores**
    
- **Battlefields where fire magic was cast recklessly**
    
- **Smithies where an apprentice cursed the forge too hard**
    

The more emotionally chaotic the environment, the more likely imps appear.

---

## **V. Ministry Observations**

- Cinder Imps appear to “feed” on frustration, gaining heat when exposed to arguments or tantrums.
    
- They can be temporarily pacified with **gentle praise**, which confuses them enough to freeze in place.
    
- Lt. Korrin documented a case where imps attempted to “adopt” a delver’s torch as their “big sibling.”
    
- If destroyed, imps burst into a **5-ft cloud of burning ash**, which can ignite cloth or dry foliage.
    
- Rook-of-Rings notes that Cinder Imps sometimes gather shiny metal scraps to build monument-like “ash idols,” purpose unknown.
    

> _“They imitate creation by destroying things in the shape of something better.”_  
> — Stonevoice

---

## **VI. Weakness & Delving Considerations**

|**Vulnerability**|**Explanation / Practical Note**|
|---|---|
|**Dousing Water**|Rapidly cools their ember-core; dissolves them into harmless ash.|
|**Cold Iron Tools**|Their claws cannot break iron; repelled by smithing hammers.|
|**Lack of Fuel**|Die out in cold environments; extinguish if unable to reach heat.|
|**Kindness & Calm**|Emotional serenity weakens them; avoid anger during encounters.|
**Delver Advisory:**  
Never engage them in enclosed wooden structures — their frantic sparks can ignite an entire room.

## **VIII. Containment & Research Status**

- **Classification:** Level II — Minor Infernal / Environmental Hazard
    
- **Containment:** Fireproof containment urns; soot-suppression wards.
    
- **Monitoring:** Heat spikes, forge emotional-resonance scans, and volatile mana soot detectors.
    
- **Active Projects:**  
     • _Operation Cool-Down_ — calming rites to prevent mass imp formation during industrial surges.  
     • _Project Emberkin_ — examining potential for controlled use of imps in flame maintenance (denied).
    

---

## **IX. Cultural & Mythic Notes**

- **Caer Dain Workers:** Blame “Cinder Brats” for every cracked crucible.
    
- **Beastfolk Artisans:** Leave sweets by furnaces to appease them (rarely works).
    
- **Delver Superstition:** If a spark lands on your hair, an imp wants your attention.
    
- **Infernal Scholars:** Claim Cinder Imps are “first-stage devils,” immature forms of more dangerous ash demons.
    

> _“They are the tantrums of fire, not yet old enough to be wrath.”_  
> — Daan

---

## **X. Summary**

The **Cinder Imps** are mischievous, fiery nuisances — dangerous in groups, volatile near fuel, and prone to chaotic antics.  
They are the smallest sparks of infernal corruption, born from temper, frustration, and the hungry nature of flame itself.

They do not seek dominance or destruction.  
They seek **attention**, and they cause destruction by accident.

In the grand hierarchy of infernal threats, they are low — but in a cramped forge with dry timber floors?

**They might as well be a wildfire with feet.**
