# **Sin-Roots**

### _Corrupted Vines of Moral Decay — “Where Regret Becomes a Garden.”_

**Filed Division:** Ministry of Delving Operations — _The Living Atlas_ (Infernal, Demonic & Corrupted Entity Archive)  
**Primary Compiler:** Priest-Analyst Thera Daan (Faith Oversight & Corruption Phenomena)  
**Advisory Editors:** Lt. Salvia Korrin (Arcane Breach Liaison), Archivist Yelna Stonevoice (Echo-Law Historian), Inspector Jao “Greyglass” Vell (Thaumaturgic Forensics)  
**Date of Filing:** 3733 A.D.  
**Document Class:** _Corrupted Flora Record / Grade-III Hazard_

---

## **I. Overview**

**Sin-Roots** are infernally warped vegetation that grow in areas steeped in **moral failing**, **repressed guilt**, **intentional cruelty**, or **acts of betrayal**.  
They resemble thick, blood-dark vines that pulse faintly beneath their bark like veins full of regret.

Unlike normal corrupted flora, Sin-Roots do not feed on organic nutrients — they devour **negative intention**, absorbing malice and sin the way other plants drink water. As they grow, they take on the spiritual imprint of the area, manifesting physical signs of the sins beneath.

The Ministry classifies them as **Type-INF / Corrupted Moral Parasites**, capable of affecting the mind, altering terrain, and spawning sentient offshoots.

> _“You can lie to yourself.  
> The roots can tell when you do.”_  
> — Stonevoice

---

## **II. Physical Description**

|**Attribute**|**Detail**|
|---|---|
|**Structure**|Thick vines and knotted roots, often rising into humanoid shapes|
|**Coloration**|Deep crimson, sickly violet, or bruise-black|
|**Texture**|Pulsing, warm, and slightly yielding — like flesh under bark|
|**Thorns**|Glassy dark spikes that drip emotional venom|
|**Growth Pattern**|Spirals and knots resembling twisted limbs or clasped hands|
|**Scent**|Copper, burnt sugar, and damp earth|
When close to a Sin-Root, the bark seems to ripple with faint whispers — usually echoing the hidden guilt or shame of nearby creatures.

---

## **III. Behavior & Phenomena**

Sin-Roots do not move quickly, but they are **reactive** to emotion and intent.

**Observed Phenomena:**

- **Guilt Response:** The roots surge or twist toward individuals carrying secret wrongdoing.
    
- **Sin Blooming:** Flowers manifest that correspond to specific sins (envy-green orchids, wrath-thorns, lust-roses, etc.).
    
- **Emotional Drain:** Proximity induces intense self-criticism, anxiety, or shame.
    
- **Confessional Mirage:** Illusory scenes of past regrets appear between the vines.
    
- **Rootspawn:** Occasionally form humanoid figures of knotted vine that act on the dominant sin they absorbed.
    

> _“Wrath roots burn. Envy roots strangle. Greed roots hoard.”_  
> — Daan

They are not intelligent, but they act with unnervingly appropriate malice.

---

## **IV. Habitat & Occurrence**

Sin-Roots grow where spiritual or emotional rot saturates the land:

- **Ruined shrines desecrated by betrayal**
    
- **Fields where soldiers slaughtered their own**
    
- **Old labyrinth rooms where companions turned on each other**
    
- **Porta Strega mansions where families hated in silence**
    
- **Abandoned prisons soaked in guilt and cruelty**
    
- **The Blackwild, near places where Moonstalkers died with unfinished regrets**
    

The stronger the guilt or betrayal, the faster they spread.

---

## **V. Ministry Observations**

- Root bark contains sigil-shaped **moral residue**, readable through Echo-Law sequencing.
    
- Priest-Analyst Daan confirmed Sin-Roots “prefer” unresolved guilt over confessed wrongdoing; honesty weakens them.
    
- Lt. Korrin’s containment attempts show they recoil from radiant truth-magic as if burned.
    
- Inspector Vell noted the roots “listen” to spoken confessions — twisting subtly as if adjusting their “nutrition.”
    
- Large clusters generate **ambient sinfields**, affecting delvers’ judgment and cooperation.
    

> _“They are the soil’s memory of our worst impulses.”_  
> — Stonevoice

---

## **VI. Weakness & Delving Considerations**

|**Vulnerability**|**Explanation / Practical Note**|
|---|---|
|**Radiant or Holy Magic**|Purifies sin residue; roots shrivel rapidly.|
|**Honest Confession**|Weakens their grip on individuals; branches retract.|
|**Cold Iron Tools**|Cuts through bark-clean; roots cannot regenerate iron wounds.|
|**Emotional Unity**|Group cooperation creates resonance they cannot feed on.|
**Delver Advisory:**  
Avoid lying, concealing panic, or sowing discord near Sin-Roots — they exalt in fractured teams and desperate minds.

## **VIII. Containment & Research Status**

- **Classification:** Level III — Environmental Moral Corruption
    
- **Containment:** Radiant grids, confession wards, and iron-lined trenches.
    
- **Monitoring:** Sinfield sensors and emotional telemetry.
    
- **Active Projects:**  
     • _Project Purity Root_ — attempting to revert Sin-Roots to pre-corruption flora.  
     • _Operation Mirror-Soil_ — studying whether Mirrorfiend residue accelerates Sin-Root growth.
    

---

## **IX. Cultural & Mythic Notes**

- **Blackwild Druids:** Call them “The Shame Vines” and warn children not to lie near old oaks.
    
- **Briar Throne Fey:** Treat Sin-Roots as “failed cousins,” plant life corrupted beyond beauty.
    
- **Delver Superstition:** “If the roots whisper your name, confess something while you still can.”
    
- **Clerics of Lathander:** Burn them as part of sunrise rituals symbolizing renewal and truth.
    

> _“They crave the sins we bury — but they starve on the truth.”_  
> — Daan

---

## **X. Summary**

**Sin-Roots** are the botanical embodiment of buried wrongdoing — vines that drink guilt, bloom regret, and twist into shapes that reflect the worst in mortals.

They are not just corruption.  
They are **memory**, shaped into thorns.  
They thrive where truth dies and wither when honesty prevails.

In Aerathon’s darkest places, the roots crawl not toward the living…  
but toward the secrets they haven’t admitted yet.
