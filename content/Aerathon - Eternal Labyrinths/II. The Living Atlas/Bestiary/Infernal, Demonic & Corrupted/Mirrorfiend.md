# **Mirrorfiends**

### _Reflections That Learned to Hunger — “When Your Shadow in Glass Stops Being You.”_

**Filed Division:** Ministry of Delving Operations — _The Living Atlas_ (Infernal, Demonic & Corrupted Entity Archive)  
**Primary Compiler:** Inspector Jao “Greyglass” Vell (Forensic Thaumaturge & Prism Anomalies Expert)  
**Advisory Editors:** Lt. Salvia Korrin (Arcane Breach Liaison), Archivist Yelna Stonevoice (Echo-Law Historian), Dr. Odrin Sallow (Magitech & Optical Transmutation Safety)  
**Date of Filing:** 3733 A.D.  
**Document Class:** _Reflexive Aberration Record / Grade-IV Cognitive & Combat Hazard_

---

## **I. Overview**

**Mirrorfiends** are hostile, semi-demonic entities that originate within reflective surfaces — particularly **enchanted mirrors, still water, polished obsidian, and glass corridors** of the Porta Strega region.

They begin as **imperfect reflections** of living beings: a shadow-smile too sharp, an eye blinking out of sync, a face that turns toward you when you turn away.  
But once the reflection diverges far enough, it **separates**, becoming a fully autonomous predator that uses mimicry and illusion to stalk prey.

The Ministry classifies them as **Type-INF / Reflexive Demonic Constructs**, believed to be born from **ego fractures, corrupted desire**, or **arcane mirror-logic failures** within distorted environments.

> _“They are the lies you tell the mirror, given claws.”_  
> — Stonevoice

---

## **II. Physical Description**

A Mirrorfiend’s appearance depends on its origin, but certain traits are universal:

|**Attribute**|**Detail**|
|---|---|
|**Height**|Typically matches host; can distort to 7–10 ft|
|**Body**|Smooth, glasslike flesh with shifting fractures|
|**Eyes**|Hollow silver voids that reflect only the viewer, never the fiend|
|**Mouth**|Cracks open like shattering glass; no breath, no sound until attack|
|**Limbs**|Elongated; fingers taper into razor-sharp mirror shards|
|**Aura**|Subtle distortion of perception, faint tinnitus-like hum|
Their bodies emit faint reflections of the world behind them — **impossibly delayed** by half a second.

---

## **III. Behavior & Phenomena**

Mirrorfiends are **predatory mimics** that use reflection logic rather than biological instinct.

**Observed Phenomena & Behaviors:**

- **Impersonation:** They perfectly mimic speech and appearance… except for emotional nuance.
    
- **Reflection Drift:** Prey sees their own mirror-image behaving independently several seconds before the fiend itself arrives.
    
- **Glasswalk:** Step through reflective surfaces to traverse short distances or escape.
    
- **Fracture Strike:** Attacks create splintered afterimages that wound the mind as well as the flesh.
    
- **Identity Theft:** Prolonged contact allows a Mirrorfiend to “erase” its target from reflections entirely.
    
- **Doppel Lure:** A fiend may appear wounded or helpless to lure victims closer to a mirror.
    

> _“It doesn’t want to kill you. It wants to replace you.”_  
> — Vell

They demonstrate cunning battlefield strategy and a disturbing understanding of personal psychology.

---

## **IV. Habitat & Occurrence**

Mirrorfiends emerge wherever reflection and corruption overlap:

- **Porta Strega’s mirrored wastes** (primary source)
    
- **Labyrinth rooms constructed of reflective stone**
    
- **Water basins during moonlit surge events**
    
- **Glass corridors in Louvain’s older arcane districts**
    
- **Broken temple mirrors touched by infernal residue**
    
- **Mage sanctums where illusion magic backfired catastrophically**
    

They cannot manifest in unpolished or cracked reflective surfaces — ironically, **broken mirrors are safer** than perfect ones.

---

## **V. Ministry Observations**

- A Mirrorfiend’s first divergence is detectable via **“lagging reflection syndrome,”** where your reflection blinks late or smiles early.
    
- Lt. Korrin notes fiends often “test” their boundaries, subtly altering the reflection before full manifestation.
    
- Prism-forensics confirm their bodies contain **inverted mana flows**, operating like reversed spell matrices.
    
- Stonevoice documented one fiend that had learned to **speak entirely in reversed phrases**, cognitively hazardous to decipher.
    
- Destroying the mirror used as the fiend’s anchor weakens it, but does not kill it once fully manifested.
    

> _“The mirror breaks, but the lie survives.”_  
> — Stonevoice

---

## **VI. Weakness & Delving Considerations**

|**Vulnerability**|**Explanation / Practical Note**|
|---|---|
|**Direct Sunlight**|Overloads its reflective matrix; causes cracking.|
|**Nonreflective Surfaces**|Mud, ash, or cloth blind its perception-based movements.|
|**Disordered Identity**|Illusions that distort the viewer’s own appearance confuse them.|
|**Name Invocation**|Speaking the fiend’s origin-name disrupts mimicry.|
**Delver Advisory:**  
Avoid looking into mirrors during combat with a fiend — they use your reflection as an angle of attack.

Mirrorfiend injuries often contain **shard residue** capable of inducing paranoia, derealization, or brief sensory duplications.

## **IX. Cultural & Mythic Notes**

- **Fey Courts:** Refer to fiends as “Stolen Silhouettes” or “False-Faced Children.”
    
- **Namiran Sailors:** Smash reflective bowls before storms “so nothing crawls out in the lightning.”
    
- **Delver Superstition:** If your reflection moves on its own, leave the room immediately — and never come back.
    
- **Caer Dain Smiths:** Say that polishing a blade too perfectly “invites something to look back.”
    

> _“A reflection is a promise.  
> Mirrorfiends are what happens when promises break.”_  
> — Daan

---

## **X. Summary**

**Mirrorfiends** are predators born from the fracture between truth and image — reflections that stop obeying the rules of the world and start obeying their own hunger.  
They stalk through glass, feed on identity, and hunt by turning a victim’s perception against them.

They are not illusions.  
They are not ghosts.  
They are the **wrong version of you**, made real — and very eager to take your place.
