# **The Pale Kith**

### _Extinct Subterranean Civilization — “When the Deep Forgot How to Return to the Light.”_

**Filed Division:** Ministry of Delving Operations — _Annals & Antiquities_ (Extinct Peoples & Subterranean Phenomena Archive)  
**Primary Compiler:** Inspector Jao “Greyglass” Vell (Forensic Thaumaturge & Deep-Earth Anthropology)

**Date of Filing:** 3732 A.D. (Age of Spectacle)  
**Document Class:** _Anthropological Extinction Record / Grade-II Verified_

---

## **I. Overview**

The **Pale Kith** were a reclusive, long-lived subterranean species who inhabited the **Under-Veins** running beneath eastern Veyra, Malarthain’s lower caverns, and the rim of the Louvain mantle shelf.  
Blind, bioluminescent, and shaped by millennia of total darkness, the Kith formed a vast, labyrinthine culture built around **echo-navigation, mineral communion, and fear of the sky**.

They are cataloged as **Type-EXTINCT / Deep-Earth Sapients**, their extinction caused not by war, disease, or collapse — but by exposure to an environment they could no longer comprehend: **the surface world**.

> _“They died of daylight, but the truth is they died of wonder.”_ — Stonevoice

|**Attribute**|**Detail**|

|**Skin**|Chalk-white, porous, faintly luminescent; cracks reflect light like quartz|
|**Eyes**|Vestigial; covered by thin membrane; sensitive to magic rather than vision|
|**Hands**|Long fingers tipped with keratin ridges for stone-reading|
|**Voice**|Clicking and harmonic drones; used echolocation to map space|
|**Scent**|Damp stone and metallic dust|
Physical remains are preserved primarily as “**mineral ghosts**” — bodies petrified in death poses, skin crystallized into fragile gypsum shells.

---

## **III. Culture & Society**

The Pale Kith built a civilization in perfect silence, their society oriented around **three foundational pillars**:

### **1. The Stone Choir**

The spiritual caste who “sang” into bedrock, reading the returning vibrations as prophecy and history.  
Their hymns shaped caverns into resonant cathedrals.

### **2. The Veinwalkers**

Navigators who mapped the Under-Veins by touch and echo, memorizing routes in rhythmic patterns tapped upon cavern walls.

### **3. The Hushkeepers**

Guardians of the outer caverns who prevented surface sound and radiant magic from contaminating the inner sanctums.

**Cultural Tenets:**

- _“Light is the forgetting of shadows.”_
    
- _“Speak only what the stone can hold.”_
    
- _“The surface is a dream that eats the dreamer.”_
    

---

## **IV. Abilities & Adaptations**

The Pale Kith evolved numerous traits suited for a world without light:

- **Echomancy:** Could navigate and “read” emotional states by reflected sound.
    
- **Lithic Communion:** Hands absorbed mineral vibrations, allowing them to detect ore, moisture, or danger.
    
- **Bioluminescent Glyphs:** Their skin glowed faintly in patterns that communicated caste rank or emotional tone.
    
- **Void Sight:** Rather than seeing shapes, they perceived **disruption** in ambient arcane fields.
    
- **Low-Magic Tolerance:** Their bodies rejected direct sunlight, divine radiance, or excessive mana.
    

---

## **V. Extinction Event — _The First Dawn_ (Cycle 3012)**

The Pale Kith perished during a forced ascent caused by the **Malarthain Mantle Quake**, which collapsed thousands of miles of tunnels.  
Survivors fled upward into a world they had not visited in over twenty millennia.

The moment sunlight touched them, their bioluminescent skin **calcified**, their arcane-sensory organs fried from overload, and their harmonic vocal cords shattered.

### **Immediate Effects Observed (per Kith memorial tablets):**

- Skin crystallization within seconds.
    
- Cardiovascular collapse triggered by photomantic overload.
    
- Panic-induced echo cycles causing fatal internal resonance.
    
- Final communal song rising in pitch until the singers burst into mineral dust.
    

Only one Kith carving survived the event — a warning chiseled blindly into a cavern wall:  
**“We heard the sun. It was too loud.”**

> _“They were not made for a world that shouts in light.”_ — Vell

Their biology was exquisitely tuned to silence, darkness, and stable mana flow — incompatible with the surface world’s chaos.

## **VIII. Ministry Findings**

- Residual **humming frequencies** recorded in deep caverns match Kith prayer patterns.
    
- Dust from Pale Kith remains reacts violently to sunlight — emitting faint harmonic pleas.
    
- Some collapsed caverns contain **stone murals** depicting a surface landscape the Kith feared long before extinction — indicating prophetic awareness.
    
- A few tunnels show signs of **retreat into deeper earth**, but no living descendants or subspecies have been found.
    

> _“They tried to flee downward forever. The world had no ‘down’ left.”_ — Stonevoice

---

## **IX. Cultural & Mythic Legacy**

- **Deep-Oria Miners:** Leave stones arranged in circles to “give the Kith a voice.”
    
- **Blackwild Shamans:** Claim their shadows “learned from the Kith how to listen.”
    
- **Delver Folklore:** If you hear a clicking echo in a sealed cavern, the Kith are “remembering you.”
    
- **Veyran Bards:** Sing laments titled _“The People Who Hid Too Well.”_
    

> _“They were the silence between heartbeats — and the world was too loud to keep them.”_ — Onarra

---

## **X. Summary**

The **Pale Kith** were a civilization sculpted by shadow and stone — masters of silence, harmony, and subterranean survival.  
But the sun they feared eventually found them, and the world they avoided proved too bright to bear.

Now their memory echoes faintly in deep caverns, soft as a dying vibration.  
In their passing, they left Aerathon a single truth:

**Some peoples vanish not by violence, but by sunrise.**
