# **Moonstalkers**

### _Extinct Lunar Hunters of the Blackwild — “When Night Trusted Its Own Ghosts.”_

## **I. Overview**

The **Moonstalkers** were a nocturnal humanoid people native to the **Blackwild**, the deep forest region where moonlight rarely touches the ground.  
They evolved — or were shaped — for a world of perpetual dusk, becoming **shadow-adaptive hunters** with bodies that half-existed within lunar illumination and half within living darkness.

Folklore calls them “**Night’s Favorite Children**,” beings who walked the boundary between substance and silhouette.  
The Ministry attests they were **Type-EXTINCT / Liminal Sapients**, a culture defined by its supernatural connection to the moon’s phases.

Their extinction, known as **The Long Waning**, occurred around **Cycle 2950**, when the lunar mana flow collapsed during a celestial convergence — and the Moonstalkers evaporated with it.

> _“They did not die. They un-happened.”_ — Vell

|---|---|
|**Height**|5’10”–6’7”|
|**Skin**|Silvery-black, matte under direct light, luminous in darkness|
|**Eyes**|Large, reflective pupils; crescent-shaped iris|
|**Voice**|Soft, layered echo; tone changed with lunar phase|
|**Scent**|Cold moss, moonlit rain|
|**Body-State**|Semi-corporeal; limbs occasionally blurred into shadow arcs during movement|
Recovered artifacts preserve **lunar glyphs** describing them as “**Those Who Step Between**,” suggesting they could partially phase into dim-light spaces.

No intact skeleton has ever been discovered — Moonstalker remains dissolve into fine black dust within minutes of lunar exposure.

---

## **III. Culture & Society**

Moonstalker civilization was deeply tied to cycles of light and shadow, forming a structured society around **three sacred lunar roles**:

### **1. Newmoon Seekers**

Silent scouts who traveled when the moon was dark. Their bodies were nearly invisible even to magical detection.

### **2. Halfmoon Weavers**

Mystics who inscribed **moon-thread sigils** into tree bark, producing spells of concealment, memory, and fearlessness.

### **3. Fullmoon Hunters**

Elite warriors who fought using **crescent-blades** — weapons of pale iron forged only during lunar zenith.  
Fullmoon Hunters guided the tribes’ migrations through the Blackwild.

**Cultural Tenets:**

- _“Take no fire into the forest.”_
    
- _“Speak softly where shadows listen.”_
    
- _“Honor moonlight; distrust dawn.”_
    

They believed sunlight “burned the truth from the world,” while moonlight revealed it.

---

## **IV. Abilities & Adaptations**

The Moonstalkers’ physiology made them legendary among early Blackwild delvers:

- **Shadow-Step:** Short-range phase movement between overlapping shadows.
    
- **Moonskin:** Light from the moon strengthened their bodies, granting enhanced speed and perception.
    
- **Nocturne Sight:** Able to see emotional auras as pale halos around living beings.
    
- **Silence Weaving:** Could dampen sound within a 20–30 ft radius.
    
- **Blood of Night:** Their wounds shed luminous ink rather than blood — a fluid used in rituals.
    

These adaptations collapsed with the lunar mana flow during the Long Waning.

---

## **V. The Long Waning (Extinction Event)**

Around **Cycle 2950**, a rare celestial alignment known as the **Four-Shadow Eclipse** severed a major lunar leyline that crossed the Blackwild.  
Moonstalkers lost the metaphysical anchor that allowed their semi-liminal biology to persist.

**Documented Effects (via glyph and oral tradition):**

- Skin dulled into full shadow then dispersed into vapor.
    
- Bodies lost cohesion within hours of the eclipse.
    
- Their villages, tools, and weapons “forgot their owners,” fading into mist or collapsing into dust.
    

The few that survived longer walked into the deepest groves and left no trace.

> _“The moon turned its face, and its people vanished.”_ — Stonevoice

Moonstalkers avoided all artificial illumination, believing it “muddied the night.”

## **VIII. Ministry Findings**

- Echo-Law analysis indicates lingering **Moonstalker voices** trapped in late-night wind patterns.
    
- Shadows in Old Blackwild ruins shift independently during full moons — likely residual mana imprint rather than true survival.
    
- One glyph tablet depicts a Moonstalker figure stepping into a **vertical moonbeam doorway** — theorized to be an extradimensional refuge.
    
- No bodies, villages, or detailed remains survive; extinction considered total.
    

> _“They were a civilization made of half-light. When the light went out, their half of the world went with them.”_ — Vell

---

## **IX. Cultural & Mythic Legacy**

- **Blackwild Beastfolk:** Tell stories of “Moon-kin” who hunt only evil spirits.
    
- **Delver Folklore:** Seeing your shadow move independently on a full moon means a Moonstalker walks beside you.
    
- **Lunar Priests:** Claim their departure weakened the moon’s protective aura — causing modern curse-bleed.
    
- **Rangers of Oria:** Leave strips of black cloth on branches so “the lost can find something to hold.”
    

> _“They were the forest’s whisper, and now the forest is too quiet.”_ — Onarra

---

## **X. Summary**

The **Moonstalkers** were a haunting, beautiful people who thrived in twilight and perished when their sky abandoned them.  
They lived as boundary-walkers — part shadow, part light — and vanished in a moment when both betrayed them.

No monument marks their graves.  
No ruins bear their names.  
Only the shifting Blackwild shadows remember that once, the night had children who walked in its trust.
