## **I. Overview**

The **Glassfolk** were an ancient humanoid culture that thrived in what is now **Porta Strega** — the obsidian-and-mirror wasteland famous for volatile lightstorms, Glassfire Djinn manifestations, and echo-heat anomalies.

They did not simply _use_ glass.  
They **became** it.

A mixture of organic evolution and intentional arcanotech alteration, the Glassfolk transformed their bodies into refractive crystal over centuries, believing that **to be closer to light was to be closer to divinity.**

Their extinction occurred approximately **Cycle 2910**, during the event now called **The Shatterfall** — a continent-wide cascade of photomantic overload that fractured every remaining Glassfolk into dust-fine shards of memory.

The Ministry designates them **Type-EXTINCT / Photomantic Sapients**, a once-living people whose biology blended seamlessly with arcane optics.

> _“Light remembers them more clearly than we do.”_ — Stonevoice

## **III. Culture & Society**

The Glassfolk believed the mortal body was an _unfinished vessel_, and that perfection came from transparency — stripping away lies, imperfections, and shadows until they resembled their gods.

### **Key Cultural Features**

- **The Doctrine of Illumined Flesh:** Body-alteration rituals involving heat, sand refinement, and photomancy.
    
- **Light Canticles:** Language composed partly of spoken words and partly of reflected signals from their skin.
    
- **Mirror Pilgrimage:** Symbolic journeys through mirror corridors meant to confront one’s “many selves.”
    
- **Soul Refractors:** Holy devices said to store ancestral memories as light patterns within crystal cores.
    

Children were said to be born organic and “transformed through adolescence,” suggesting a gradual crystalline metamorphosis.

---

## **IV. Abilities & Adaptations**

While alive, Glassfolk possessed unique physiological features:

- **Refractive Body:** Could absorb, redirect, or split magical light.
    
- **Photomantic Memory:** Skin reflections stored emotional impressions; elders acted as living archives.
    
- **Heat Resilience:** Bodies tolerated temperatures up to 900°C.
    
- **Illusion Immunity:** Their multifaceted vision prevented deception by glamour or mirage.
    
- **Vocal Harmonics:** Speech could disrupt enchantments or stabilize raw magic if sung through resonance chambers.
    

These adaptations made them prized allies — and feared adversaries — of early desert guilds.

---

## **V. The Shatterfall (Extinction Event)**

The Shatterfall was not a battle.  
It was a **catastrophic resonance failure**.

When the Glassfolk attempted to ascend as a culture-wide ritual of illumination, they synchronized their body-light in a single massive hymn.  
Eyewitness stone carvings depict a blinding white corona rising from Porta Strega… followed by silence and drifting shards.

### **Contributing Causes**

- Overloaded photomantic latticework.
    
- Incomplete Soul Refractor alignment.
    
- Interference from a Glassfire Djinn “wish cascade.”
    
- Sudden ley spike beneath the Strega desert.
    

The result was total fragmentation.  
Every Glassfolk body turned to razor-fine dust, carried by winds across the dunes.

Even now, the sands sing faintly during dawn — some say it is the last whisper of their final hymn.

> _“They reached for the sun. The sun reached back.”_ — Vell

While powerful, their bodies were fragile to extremes — and their culture dangerously reliant on metaphysical stability.

## **VIII. Ministry Findings**

- Light-residue patterns correspond to extinct dialects, partially translatable via Vox Engine harmonics.
    
- Some Djinn bear **facial echoes** similar to Glassfolk, hinting at shared ancestry or memetic inheritance.
    
- Subterranean surveys reveal a **sealed cavern of mirrors**, believed to be their final ritual site.
    
- Null-creed wards distort Shatterfall dust, suggesting lingering psionic imprint.
    

The Ministry considers the Glassfolk the **most complete case of sapient self-extinction** in recorded history.

---

## **IX. Cultural & Mythic Legacy**

- **Beastfolk of the Dunes** scatter mirrored beads at dusk to “appease the Shining Dead.”
    
- **Delvers** swear that cracking a mirror in Porta Strega releases a Glassfolk whisper.
    
- **Sun-Clerics** regard them as tragic martyrs — “a people who mistook radiance for resurrection.”
    
- **Arcanists** study their extinction as a warning against society-wide metamancy.
    

> _“They wanted to become light.  
> Light made them memory instead.”_ — Stonevoice

---

## **X. Summary**

The **Glassfolk** were a civilization that traded permanence for brilliance, flesh for radiance, hope for transcendence.  
Their beauty was unmatched.  
Their ambition was fatal.

Now the desert carries their voices as dust, refracting dawn across Aerathon in colors no living eye can fully name.

And when the sun hits the dunes just right, observers swear they see silhouettes of crystal figures walking the horizon — as if still searching for the light they could never hold.
