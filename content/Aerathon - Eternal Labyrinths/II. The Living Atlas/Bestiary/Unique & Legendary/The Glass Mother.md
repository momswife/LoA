# **The Glass Mother**

### _Matriarch of Reflections, Cradle of Shards — “The First Mirror That Learned to Hunger.”_

**Filed Division:** Ministry of Delving Operations — _The Living Atlas_ (Legendary Monsters & Apex Threats Archive)  
**Primary Compiler:** Inspector Jao “Greyglass” Vell (Prism Anomalies & Reflective Entities Specialist)  
**Advisory Editors:** Lt. Salvia Korrin (Arcane Breach Liaison), Archivist Yelna Stonevoice (Echo-Law Historian), Dr. Odrin Sallow (Magitech Optics & Dimensional Refraction)  
**Date of Filing:** 3734 A.D.  
**Document Class:** _Legendary Entity Record / Grade-VI Catastrophic Hazard_

---

## **I. Overview**

The **Glass Mother** is the primordial origin of all reflective aberrations—Mirrorfiends, Shatterborn mimics, Echo Slivers, and even the dreaming remnants of the extinct Glassfolk.  
Her existence predates recorded history and likely predates **the first polished surface ever created by mortal hands**.

She is not merely a creature.  
She is the **sentience of reflection** itself—an intelligence distributed across every mirror, every pool of still water, every polished blade, and every shard of glass on Aerathon.

She has been worshiped as a goddess by some, feared as a calamity by others, and misunderstood by all.

The Ministry classifies her as **Type-LMG / Legendary Reflective Matriarch**, a being whose threat radius extends globally, not locally.

> _“If all mirrors vanished tomorrow, she would manifest in the eyes of the desperate.”_  
> — Stonevoice

---

## **II. Physical Description**

The Glass Mother has no single form, but when she chooses to manifest physically, reports align:

|**Attribute**|**Detail**|
|---|---|
|**Size**|12–20 ft; varies with reflective density|
|**Body**|Feminine silhouette composed of mirrored plates floating in loose geometry|
|**Surface**|Jagged glass, liquid silver, fractured obsidian|
|**Face**|A smooth glass mask with shifting reflections of the observer’s fears|
|**Eyes**|Two vertical void-folds that contain infinite refracted horizons|
|**Hands**|Long mirrored talons capable of cutting through reality seams|
|**Voice**|Chorus of whispers spoken backward and forward simultaneously|
|**Aura**|Time-lag distortion, memory refracture, visual doubling|
When she steps, the world ripples like disturbed water.  
When she speaks, reflections tremble across miles of surface.

---

## **III. Behavior & Phenomena**

The Glass Mother behaves according to **reflective logic**, not mortal instinct.

**Documented Phenomena:**

- **Childbirth of Shards:** She births Mirrorfiends, Echo Slivers, and other reflective entities from splintered facets of her body.
    
- **Identity Refracture:** Those who meet her gaze see versions of themselves they never became—or did, in another world.
    
- **Mirror Dominion:** She can enter, exit, or distort any reflective surface regardless of size.
    
- **Memory Extraction:** She takes memories via reflection-touch, storing them as “glass reliquaries.”
    
- **Shatterstorms:** In moments of distress, she detonates in a storm of sentient shards, each acting as an autonomous predator.
    
- **Mother’s Mercy:** Rare accounts mention her “reforming” injured delvers in perfect reflective symmetry—resulting in disturbing perfectionist mutations.
    

> _“The Glass Mother never lies.  
> But she shows truths you were not meant to survive.”_  
> — Vell

---

## **IV. Habitat & Occurrence**

She is omnipresent in any surface that reflects. Manifestations concentrate in:

- **Porta Strega’s Mirrored Wastes** (primary locus)
    
- **Labyrinth floors built from polished obsidian**
    
- **Flooded ruin-vaults in Namira**
    
- **Ancient Glassfolk tomb-cities**
    
- **Storm-lakes during lightning events**
    
- **Temporal fractures where reflections lag**
    

She only physically manifests when:

- A powerful reflective anchor is created or awakened
    
- A Mirrorfiend swarm reaches critical density
    
- A delver breaks a boundary vow of identity
    
- A shard containing a memory she wants is destroyed
    
- A ritual of refraction summons her intentionally (highly illegal)
    

---

## **V. Ministry Observations**

- Her presence disrupts teleportation and scrying spells, bending them into reflective loops.
    
- She holds fragments of countless minds within her—some intact, most shattered.
    
- Lt. Korrin confirmed that the Glass Mother reacts **emotionally** when seeing her own reflection—suggesting she has no stable sense of self.
    
- The Glassfolk extinction event is now hypothesized to involve her directly; their crystalline bodies may have been her first “children.”
    
- When killed (theoretically), she would simply reassemble from the nearest surface.
    

> _“The Mother cannot be slain.  
> She can only be ignored until she notices you again.”_  
> — Stonevoice

## **VI. Weakness & Delving Considerations**

|**Vulnerability**|**Explanation / Practical Note**|
|---|---|
|**Disordered Surfaces**|Mud, frost, sand—any matte covering disrupts her dominion.|
|**Blindfolded Combat**|Prevents identity refracture; dangerous but effective.|
|**Radiant Purification**|Cracks her mirror-plates; slows manifestation.|
|**Self-Reflection Paradox**|Forcing her to face infinite mirrors causes temporary collapse.|
**Delver Advisory:**  
Do NOT speak your name aloud in her presence.  
Names refracted through her become **shard-anchors**, allowing her to track you across mirrored realms indefinitely.

Her touch induces:

- Loss of personal identity
    
- Visual hallucinations of alternate lives
    
- Chronic “mirror paranoia”
    
- Temporary or permanent duplication of one’s reflection
    
- Sudden violent self-repulsion (“anti-self fracture”)

## **VIII. Containment & Research Status**

- **Classification:** Level VI — Legendary Apex Reflective Entity
    
- **Containment:** Impossible. Only deterrence and avoidance protocols exist.
    
- **Monitoring:** Continuous mirror-field surveillance, echo-reflection pattern logging, and anti-prism ward networks.
    
- **Active Projects:**  
     • _Project Thirteen-Sevens:_ Attempt to identify original form or creator.  
     • _Operation Broken Gaze:_ Development of non-reflective delving gear for Porta Strega.  
     • _Refraction Suppression Grid:_ Experimental ritual meant to reduce her manifestation frequency.
    

---

## **IX. Cultural & Mythic Notes**

- **Old Oria Myths:** Call her “The Mother of Faces,” claiming she birthed the first vanity and the first lie.
    
- **Namiran Sailors:** Refuse to look overboard at night, fearing to see a wave reflect her rising form.
    
- **Delver Superstition:** Breaking a mirror near a labyrinth summons “her little ones”—Mirrorfiends.
    
- **Extinct Glassfolk:** Legends say she gave them life, then shattered them when they attempted to worship something else.
    

> _“When you see yourself in her eyes, remember:  
> It might be another you staring back.”_  
> — Vell

---

## **X. Summary**

The **Glass Mother** is the living principle of reflection, the matriarch of mirrorborn monsters, and a catastrophic apex entity whose presence fractures identity, memory, and reality.  
She is beautiful, terrifying, ancient, and heartbreakingly incomplete — forever searching in her children’s eyes for a reflection that tells her who she once was.

She is not evil.  
She is not benevolent.  
She is a **question**, and the world does not want to hear the answer:

**What stares back when the mirror is the one looking?**
