# **The Hundred-Tongued Leviathan**

### _Abyssal Devourer of Voices — “Where the Sea Learns to Speak, and Then Regrets It.”_

**Filed Division:** Ministry of Delving Operations — _The Living Atlas_ (Legendary Monsters & Apex Threats Archive)  
**Primary Compiler:** Warden Cyra Hesh (Environmental Hazards & Oceanic Megafauna Division)

**Date of Filing:** 3734 A.D.  
**Document Class:** _Legendary Entity Record / Grade-VI Cataclysmic Hazard_

---

## **I. Overview**

The **Hundred-Tongued Leviathan** is one of Aerathon’s most feared oceanic apex entities — a titanic, ancient creature dwelling in the deepest trenches of the Namiran waters and the abyssal regions beneath Tempest’s Wake.

It earned its name from the **hundreds of independent, prehensile tongues** housed within its cavernous maw, each capable of emitting a different voice:

- whale-song,
    
- human screams,
    
- delver shouts,
    
- infant cries,
    
- distorted prayers,
    
- incomprehensible abyssal speech,
    
- and sometimes… your own voice speaking back at you.
    

It is an ancient intelligence, though fractured — a being that **collects voices, memories, and sonic echoes**, weaving them into a shifting identity so vast it cannot hold itself still.

The Ministry designates it as **Type-LMG / Abyssal Voice Aberrant**, a near-mythic leviathan capable of capsizing entire fleets and breaking minds from leagues away.

> _“It doesn’t want to eat you.  
> It wants to know what you sound like when you break.”_  
> — Vell

|**Mouth**|Gaping maw that opens into a seemingly bottomless vortex|

|**Eyes**|Multiple abyssal lantern-eyes that flicker with deep-sea bioluminescence|
|**Fins**|Massive, ragged fins that generate storms when breached|
|**Aura**|Low-frequency hum audible through water, hull, and bone|
Its underside is lined with glowing sigils — not magical, but **bio-luminous resonance organs** that store harvested voices.

---

## **III. Behavior & Phenomena**

The Leviathan is an apex predator, but its methods are **psychic, sonic, and emotional**, not purely physical.

**Documented Phenomena:**

- **Voice Harvesting:** Uses its tongues to steal the voices of sailors, delvers, and entire crews. Victims become mute or speak only in echoes.
    
- **Sirenfold Mimicry:** Mimics loved ones, dead companions, or shipmates to lure prey.
    
- **Depthsong Pressure:** Emits infrasonic pulses that rupture organs or induce mass hallucinations.
    
- **Stormbreach:** Its surfacing releases shockwaves that create instant supercells, pulling ships into whirlpools.
    
- **Tongue-Spawn:** Detached tongues become independent horrors (“Voice Larvae”) that infest wrecks.
    
- **Abyssal Beacon:** Emits lost radio-like signals from long-sunken ships, confusing navigators.
    

> _“Every tongue is a life it swallowed.  
> Every life becomes a voice it can wield.”_  
> — Stonevoice

It hunts rarely, but when active, its path becomes a graveyard.

---

## **IV. Habitat & Occurrence**

The Leviathan’s territory is vast and almost unmappable:

- **The Mouth of Namira** (a trench deeper than recorded in any world atlas)
    
- **Tempest’s Wake’s spiral currents**
    
- **Submerged labyrinth gates with acoustic properties**
    
- **Blackwater pit-zones where no sunlight has ever reached**
    
- **Storm convergences where the sea “goes quiet”**
    

Encounters spike during:

- eclipses,
    
- new moons,
    
- island-quaking storms,
    
- or unexplained mass disappearances of delvers.
    

---

## **V. Ministry Observations**

- Its tongues “speak” in frequencies outside normal perception — some affect emotions, others time sense, others dreams.
    
- Korrin witnessed an entire crew jump overboard after hearing their own dead relatives call their names.
    
- Echo-Law analysis indicates the Leviathan accumulates **memories**, not just voices.
    
- Vell hypothesizes each tongue is a “profile,” a semi-autonomous echo derived from those it devoured.
    
- When a tongue cluster mimicked a Ministry hymn perfectly, all observers became violently nauseous.
    

> _“It is not one creature.  
> It is all the voices the deep sea never returned.”_  
> — Hesh

## **VI. Weakness & Delving Considerations**

|**Vulnerability**|**Explanation / Practical Note**|
|---|---|
|**Overlapping Resonance**|Multiple singers or soundcasters can “jam” its Voicefield briefly.|
|**Harsh Light**|Sudden radiant bursts disorient its abyssal eyes.|
|**Silence Magic**|Cancels its mimicry — but only for a few heartbeats.|
|**Shadowed Depths**|Oddly, it avoids areas where no reflection exists in water.|
**Delver Advisory:**  
Never respond to a voice at sea.  
Never shout your name across the water.  
Never listen to anything speaking in your own voice.

The Leviathan’s psychic echo-touch can cause:

- auditory hallucinations,
    
- dream bleed,
    
- mutism,
    
- compulsive fixation on lost loved ones,
    
- or the inability to speak except in languages you never learned.

## **IX. Cultural & Mythic Notes**

- **Namiran Sailors:** Call it “The Speaker in the Deep.” No one whistles at sea because “the Leviathan whistles back.”
    
- **Oria’s River Priests:** Believe it was born when a god’s tongue fell into the sea after being cut out.
    
- **Blackwild Shamans:** Say it collects the stories of the drowned.
    
- **Delver Superstition:** If you hear your own voice calling from the fog, you must bind your mouth shut until sunrise.
    

> _“Its hunger is not for flesh.  
> It devours what makes a person recognizable in the dark.”_  
> — Stonevoice

---

## **X. Summary**

The **Hundred-Tongued Leviathan** is a planetary-scale threat, a monstrously ancient voice-collector whose tongues speak with the drowned, the lost, and the forgotten.  
Its mimicry breaks minds, its presence moves storms, and its hunger is not for bodies — but for **identity**.

It is the ocean’s echo of all who vanished beneath its waves:  
a titan wearing the voices of a thousand tragedies.

And when it surfaces, the sea itself seems to scream.
