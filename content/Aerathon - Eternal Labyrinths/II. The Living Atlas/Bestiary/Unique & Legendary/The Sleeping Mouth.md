# **The Sleeping Mouth**

### _Dormant World-Eater of the Deep Labyrinth — “The Labyrinth Dreams of Hunger, and This Is Its Dream Given Flesh.”_

**Filed Division:** Ministry of Delving Operations — _The Living Atlas_ (Legendary Monsters & Apex Threats Archive)  
**Primary Compiler:** Lt. Salvia Korrin (Arcane Breach Liaison & Spatial Abyss Specialist)  
**Advisory Editors:** Inspector Jao “Greyglass” Vell (Forensic Thaumaturgy), Archivist Yelna Stonevoice (Echo-Law Historian), Warden Cyra Hesh (Environmental Cataclysms)  
**Date of Filing:** 3734 A.D.  
**Document Class:** _Legendary Entity Record / Grade-VI Cataclysmic Dimensional Threat_

---

## **I. Overview**

The **Sleeping Mouth** is an ancient, dreambound apex entity believed to rest at the **lowest convergent tier of the Labyrinths** — a place theorized to be older than Aerathon, older than gods, older than memory itself.

It is not truly asleep, nor truly awake.  
It exists in a state of **perpetual near-rising**, stirred by dimensional pressure, emotional resonance, or the cumulative noise of mortal civilization.

When delvers report hearing distant grinding, breathing from behind stone, or soft murmuring beneath the floor — the Ministry presumes the Sleeping Mouth has rolled in its slumber.

If it ever fully awakens, the Arcane Breach Division projects:

- **continent-scale labyrinth collapse**,
    
- **dimensional implosion**, and
    
- **possible consumption of the foundational ley-grid** beneath Aerathon.
    

Therefore, the entity is classified as **Type-LMG / Pre-Existential Cataclysmic Predator** — a “proto-leviathan” of unknown origin.

> _“It dreams the world.  
> If it wakes, it will remember hunger first.”_  
> — Stonevoice

---

## **II. Physical Description**

The Sleeping Mouth has no confirmed fully-visible form. Only partial awakenings, sensed through cracks in reality, have been documented.

**Observed Anatomy (Fragmentary):**

|**Feature**|**Description**|
|---|---|
|**Teeth**|Rows of titanic stone-fused fangs miles long; grind like tectonic plates|
|**Throat**|A void-tunnel lined with moving glyphs that erase memory on sight|
|**Lips**|Mountain-range scale, appearing as curved cliffs|
|**Tongue**|Never fully seen; reportedly stirs like a shifting continent|
|**Voice**|A deep, oceanic inhale that alters the gravity of nearby rooms|
|**Drool**|Viscous reality-melt; dissolves stone into fractal spirals|
Its presence is best described not by sight, but by **experience**:

- Time dilates.
    
- Walls sweat.
    
- Light bends inward.
    
- Everyone feels suddenly, painfully mortal.
    

---

## **III. Behavior & Phenomena**

Though dormant, the Sleeping Mouth exhibits **dream-behaviors** that impact entire regions of the Labyrinths.

**Documented Phenomena:**

- **Dream Spill:** Portions of its nightmares manifest as minor monsters or illusions.
    
- **Gravity Drag:** Objects slide slowly toward unseen depths.
    
- **Hunger Echo:** Delvers feel compelled to walk downward, no matter where “down” is.
    
- **Breath of the Deep:** Sudden gusts of cold, ancient air extinguish torches miles above.
    
- **Teeth-Grind Tremors:** Quakes that collapse older labyrinth floors.
    
- **Gutterspawn:** Servitor creatures formed from its saliva-matter.
    

> _“It does not hunt.  
> Even asleep, it simply consumes whatever falls inside.”_  
> — Korrin

It does not awaken in a traditional sense — instead, it **turns over**, and the world suffers for it.

---

## **IV. Habitat & Occurrence**

The Sleeping Mouth rests at the **lowest uncharted level** of the Labyrinths, believed to be:

- Beneath the Rift-roots of Monster Meadows
    
- Beneath the molten corridors of Caer Dain’s Chain-Labyrinth
    
- Beneath Porta Strega’s mirrored catacombs
    
- Beneath Namira’s drowned temple floors
    
- Beneath the Briar Thrones’ subterranean crowns
    

This implies the Labyrinths are not structures built downward…  
but **layers built upward**  
over something ancient and unfathomably hungry.

## **V. Ministry Observations**

- Arcane triangulation suggests its body spans **hundreds of miles**, or exists partly outside physical space.
    
- Vell’s analysis shows its breath carries **pre-lexicon glyphs**, older than written magic.
    
- Stonevoice notes its dreams occasionally reference extinct species — possibly those it devoured ages ago.
    
- Korrin recorded a unique event: every reflective surface in Porta Strega showed a giant, closed eye behind the observer for exactly six heartbeats.
    
- Hesh logged deep-sea tremors synced with its breathing cycles.
    

> _“The world rests on its ribcage.”_  
> — Stonevoice

## **VI. Weakness & Delving Considerations**

Though unkillable, the Sleeping Mouth has exploitable **stabilizing factors**.

|**Factor**|**Effect / Advisory**|
|---|---|
|**Silence & Stillness**|Minimizes dream agitation; avoid combat near deep floors.|
|**Anti-Fear Wards**|Reduces psychic resonance that might stir it.|
|**Dimensional Anchors**|Prevents minor reality shifts caused by its tossing.|
|**Faith Presence**|Active worship of any deity creates stabilizing counter-resonance.|
**Delver Advisory:**  
Never descend into a floor where:

- the walls pulse,
    
- the air tastes like stone-blood,
    
- or you hear **distant chewing**.
    

Those signs mean you are walking on its palate.

If you hear breathing _behind your heartbeat_, turn back.

## **X. Summary**

The **Sleeping Mouth** is an ancient, dormant world-eater dreaming beneath the Labyrinths, older than gods, older than worlds, older than hunger itself.  
It stirs in its sleep, shaking the deep floors, spawning nightmare-echoes, and reminding civilization that Aerathon rests atop an unfathomable maw waiting for its next breath.

If it wakes fully:  
**The world ends not in fire, nor cold, but in teeth.**
