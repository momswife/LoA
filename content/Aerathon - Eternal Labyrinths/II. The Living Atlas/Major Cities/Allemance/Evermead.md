# **Evermead**

### _The City of Harvest and Hearth — “To Feed Is to Remember.”_

**Filed Division:** Ministry of Delving Operations — Annals & Antiquities (Cultural Geography Archive)**  
**Primary Compiler:** Archivist Yelna Stonevoice (Echo-Law)  
**Advisory Editors:** Keeper Aeska Mourn-Wind (Ecological Archives), Lt. Salvia Korrin (Arc. Scriv.), Director Halin Drauve (Economic Oversight)  
**Date of Filing:** 3174 A.D. (Age of Spectacle)**  
**Document Class:** _Urban Record / Grade-I Authenticity_

---

## **I. Overview**

Nestled in the golden plains south of Louvain, **Evermead** is the agricultural soul of Allemance — a city of rolling vineyards, enchanted fields, and orchards that bloom in rhythm with song.  
It is often called **“The City That Feeds the Flame”**, for its harvests sustain not only Louvain’s people but its faith.

Where Louvain writes laws, Evermead writes recipes.  
Where Gocaire hammers stone, Evermead tills joy.

**Population:** ~830,000  
**Dominant Races:** Humans, Halflings, Beastfolk (Vulpine and Bovine), Dwarves  
**Regional Symbol:** A sheaf of wheat encircling a radiant lantern  
**Cultural Motto:** _“We grow what the world forgets.”_

---

## **II. Geography & Architecture**

Evermead stretches along the **Songbrook River**, surrounded by terraces of fruiting vines and wheatfields that glimmer faintly under moonlight — a magical side effect of druidic cultivation.  
Buildings are built from **sunbaked brick and whitewashed timber**, with roofs of living ivy that serve as natural cooling wards.

Notable districts:

- **The Feastward:** Central market and plaza for trade and public festivals.
    
- **Lanternfields:** Expansive farmland surrounding the city, where every row is lined with glowing lights to deter pests and guide workers by night.
    
- **The Grainspire:** A monumental silo-temple dedicated to Chauntea; also functions as the Ministry’s agricultural registry.
    
- **The Cask Quarter:** Brewery district supplying enchanted spirits and ceremonial wines to every major region.
    

The city has no walls — the fields themselves are its defense, sustained by a network of ritual wards that bloom with protective magic during danger.

---

# **III. History**

Evermead began as a **harvest camp** during the earliest days of reconstruction after the Age of Collapse, founded by refugees who tilled the land around the first fertile relic bloom.  
According to legend, when the first seed sprouted in ruined soil, a divine voice whispered:  
_“Feed the flame, and it will feed you.”_

**Historical Milestones:**

- **443 A.D.** — The **First Feastkeeper’s Compact** signed, officially establishing Evermead as the agricultural ward of Allemance.
    
- **736 A.D.** — Creation of the **Harvest Chorus**, a druidic order responsible for maintaining fertility rites and relic-nurtured soil.
    
- **1312 A.D.** — Founding of the **Lantern Festival**, which grows into Aerathon’s most widely celebrated holiday of light, food, and remembrance.
    
- **2591 A.D.** — The **Grainspire** rebuilt after a planar storm; the miracle of **unending wheat** recorded for the first time.
    

Evermead has since remained the heart of Allemance’s nourishment — a city where soil remembers kindness and harvests answer prayers.

---

## **IV. Governance**

Evermead is ruled by the **Compact of Hands**, a council of farmers, druids, and clerics devoted to Chauntea, Pelor, and Mielikki.  
Their decisions are reached over shared meals — a tradition known as the **Table of Accord**.  
The Ministry’s agricultural inspectors are permanently stationed here, led by the **Grainwarden**, currently **Elyra Darn**, a halfling bureaucrat whose patience rivals her appetite.

Unlike most cities, Evermead’s governance operates entirely through **oral contracts**, witnessed by magic-infused lanterns that change color if promises are broken.

---

## **V. Economy**

Evermead feeds the continent, exporting grain, fruit, wine, and alchemical produce refined from relic-enriched soil.  
Every transaction is recorded not in coin, but in **measure** — a custom known as **Tithe-Trade**, ensuring barter is weighted by sustenance, not profit.

**Primary Exports:** Wheat, fruit, blessed oils, druidic reagents, enchanted liquor.  
**Imports:** Machinery, relic containers, artisans, and parchment from Louvain.

**Key Institutions:**

- **The Grainspire Temple:** Houses both faith and agriculture registry.
    
- **The Harvest Chorus:** Druids who maintain seasonal equilibrium; known for “singing seeds awake.”
    
- **The Cask Guild:** Produces Evermead’s famed glowing spirits used in holy rites.
    
- **Lantern Market:** Where each booth illuminates with different hues based on food freshness.
    

---

## **VI. Faith & Culture**

Faith here is tangible — to farm is to worship.  
Every field is a shrine, every harvest an offering.  
Clerics and druids coexist as equals, blending divine and natural magic in daily life.

Major celebrations:

- **The Lantern Festival:** Held each autumn; thousands of floating lights drift down the Songbrook River, symbolizing gratitude for the year’s bounty.
    
- **The Feast of Renewal:** Occurs after the first spring rain; citizens share meals with strangers by oath.
    
- **The Quiet Bloom:** A solemn ceremony where seeds are planted for the dead — believed to reincarnate their memories as fruit.
    

Evermead’s people live by the **Rule of the Table:** no one eats alone if another is hungry.

---

## **VII. Law & Defense**

Crime in Evermead is rare; hunger is rarer still.  
Most disputes are settled by the **Circle of Harvest**, a panel of mediators who determine restitution through service, not punishment.

In times of threat, Evermead’s druids awaken the **Lanternfields**, transforming the farmland into a shimmering maze of living crops and defensive thorns.  
The city’s militia — the **Threshers** — wield enchanted scythes and pitchforks that channel radiant energy through the soil itself.

> _“A blade that cuts wheat can cut wickedness too.”_

---

## **VIII. Relationship to the Labyrinths**

Though lacking a labyrinth of its own, Evermead maintains one of the largest **provision networks** for delvers — ensuring that every expedition departing from Louvain or Baritte carries blessed rations grown here.  
The **Harvest Chorus** also studies relic-induced crop mutations, cultivating safe strains for potioncraft and druidic use.

Rumors persist of an ancient labyrinth buried beneath the Lanternfields, sealed after a “green flood” centuries ago — an event the Ministry refers to only as **The Rooted Silence**.

## **X. Cultural Identity & Legacy**

Evermead is not simply a city — it is a covenant between land and labor.  
Its citizens believe that memory, when planted with kindness, grows into sustenance.  
They live simply, laugh loudly, and measure wealth in shared tables rather than coffers.

> _“Feed one, feed all — the earth is listening.”_ — Feastkeeper’s Oath

The Ministry sees Evermead as the **moral center of Allemance**, proof that faith and fertility are the same flame burning at different temperatures.

### **Urban Feature: Feastkeeper’s Blessing**

_Any creature raised in or blessed within Evermead gains the following boon:_

> **Feastkeeper’s Blessing:** Once per long rest, when you use an ability, spell, or item that restores hit points, one additional creature within 10 ft also regains hit points equal to your proficiency bonus. When this occurs, faint motes of golden pollen drift in the air and the scent of fresh bread lingers for a minute.
