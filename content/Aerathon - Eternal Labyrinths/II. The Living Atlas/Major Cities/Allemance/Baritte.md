# **Baritte**

### _The Crossroads City — “Where Every Journey Begins, and None Truly End.”_

**Filed Division:** Ministry of Delving Operations — Annals & Antiquities (Cultural Geography Archive)**  
**Primary Compiler:** Inspector Jao “Greyglass” Vell  
**Advisory Editors:** Archivist Yelna Stonevoice (Echo-Law), Director Halin Drauve (Economic Oversight), Lt. Salvia Korrin (Arc. Scriv.)  
**Date of Filing:** 3176 A.D. (Age of Spectacle)**  
**Document Class:** _Urban Record / Grade-I Authenticity_

---

## **I. Overview**

Baritte is the **trade and transport capital of Allemance**, and the largest crossroads city in all Aerathon.  
It lies at the intersection of four major highways — the **Lantern Road (to Louvain)**, the **Iron Trail (to Oria)**, the **Spice Road (to Arneria)**, and the **Glassway (to Vinyot)** — making it the logistical heart of the known world.

Baritte is not beautiful like Louvain, nor tranquil like Daltiarna; it is alive in motion, a constant storm of wagons, voices, and banners.  
To live here is to never stand still.

**Population:** ~1.1 million  
**Dominant Races:** Humans, Halflings, Dwarves, Leonine and Canine Beastfolk  
**Regional Symbol:** A wheel encircled by four crossed roads  
**Cultural Motto:** _“All roads burn the same underfoot.”_

---

## **II. Geography & Architecture**

Baritte is a **circular city**, divided into four vast quarters and one central core.  
Each quarter mirrors the terrain of the region it leads toward — a living map made of stone, sand, and soil.

|**Quarter**|**Orientation**|**Description**|
|---|---|---|
|**The Brass Quarter (South)**|Faces Louvain|The administrative and Ministry sector, built of polished bronze; home to the Delver Registry.|
|**The Dust Quarter (East)**|Faces Arneria|Desert architecture; markets specializing in spices, relic shards, and ritual artifacts.|
|**The Iron Quarter (North)**|Faces Oria|Industrial zone of forges, mechanical workhouses, and thundersteel smithies.|
|**The Glass Quarter (West)**|Faces Vinyot|Economic hub and auction center for magical items and guild contracts.|
At the center lies **The Wayheart**, a circular plaza said to mark the precise center of Aerathon.  
A relic brazier burns there eternally — the **Traveler’s Flame** — believed to guide those lost in labyrinth or storm.

---

# **III. History**

Baritte’s first incarnation began as a **pilgrim rest stop** during the Reconstruction after the Age of Collapse.  
Nomadic merchants and Beastfolk traders established the **First Waystone**, a marker promising safety to travelers who offered bread and coin.

Over centuries, the city expanded in great concentric rings until its original heart became a myth whispered by caravans.  
By the **Second Concord**, Baritte had become the official midpoint for Ministry convoys — **Aerathon’s circulatory center** for trade, goods, and information.

**Key Historical Events:**

- **387 A.D.** — Establishment of the **Lantern Road** between Louvain and Baritte, creating the first protected trade artery.
    
- **642 A.D.** — Construction of the original **Wayheart Monument**, marking Baritte as sacred ground for travelers.
    
- **1481 A.D.** — Official recognition as the **Neutral City** — no guild, kingdom, or noble house may lay claim to it.
    
- **2319 A.D.** — Ministry’s **Transit Charter** ratified; Baritte becomes the centralized hub for archives, convoy scheduling, and continental logistics.
    

Baritte remains the crossroads of Aerathon — a city built not for walls or war, but for movement, memory, and safe passage.

---

## **IV. Governance**

Baritte is led by the **Council of Roads**, an elected council of five — each representing one major route, and one for the city itself.  
They are chosen from among the guildmasters, caravan leaders, and navigators.  
No one may hold the same chair twice in a lifetime, preserving constant circulation of power.

The Ministry maintains **a permanent sub-office**, the **Transit Bureau**, overseen by the **Marshal of Roads** — currently **Halra Venn**, a leonine Beastfolk veteran renowned for enforcing neutrality with claws and charm alike.

---

## **V. Economy**

Everything that moves passes through Baritte.  
It is the beating heart of Aerathon’s supply lines, where caravans unload spices beside relics, and farmers barter with scholars for parchment.

**Primary Exports:** Logistics contracts, relic documentation, trade licensing, and caravan security.  
**Primary Imports:** Everything. Literally.

**Major Institutions:**

- **The Wayheart Exchange:** Global trade floor for relics and transport goods.
    
- **The Guild of Tides and Wheels:** Oversees transportation guild charters.
    
- **The Concord Ledger:** Ministry-supervised vault tracking cross-regional economic data.
    
- **The Thousand Tents:** A temporary market that rebuilds itself differently each dawn.
    

The city’s economy is so large that its market shifts hourly — the **Hourbell**, a massive time-keeping relic, rings to synchronize all transactions.

---

## **VI. Faith & Culture**

Baritte’s faith is practical. Travelers pray not for salvation, but safe passage.  
Temples to **Fharlanghn**, **Avandra**, and **Pelor** dominate the city’s skyline, joined by roadside shrines maintained by guilds.

Festivals are constant — every arrival and departure celebrated equally. The **Festival of First Steps** (Cycle Opening Day) is marked by the lighting of thousands of handheld lamps carried from the Wayheart outward across Aerathon’s roads.

Cultural proverbs include:

- _“Hospitality is the first trade.”_
    
- _“A liar can’t cross Baritte twice.”_ (referring to the city’s magical oaths for travelers)
    

The city’s multicultural nature ensures every accent, species, and creed finds a voice here.

---

## **VII. Law & Defense**

Baritte’s greatest weapon is neutrality.  
Its open gates are guaranteed by the **Transit Charter**, enforced by both the Ministry and the five major trade guilds.  
Criminals are branded with **Traveler’s Ink**, a glowing mark preventing them from crossing the city’s borders without permission.

The **Roadguard**, a multi-racial militia, keeps peace within the quarters, commanded jointly by the Marshal and a rotating council of guild leaders.

In crisis, the city’s streets can **reconfigure** themselves through a lattice of embedded relic sigils — forming circular barricades that seal each quarter.

---

## **VIII. Relationship to the Labyrinths**

Many labyrinth entrances lie beneath the trade routes radiating from Baritte, making it an ideal launch point for **mercenary delvers** and **supply convoys**.  
The **Wayheart Flame** is believed to react faintly when labyrinth energy shifts — the Ministry uses it as a **surface resonance monitor**, detecting disturbances across the continent.

Several delver guilds maintain chapterhouses here, including the **Cinderpaw Pact**, **Argent Banner**, and **Iron-Bough Syndicate**, all drawn to its neutral ground.

## **X. Cultural Identity & Legacy**

Baritte stands as the one city no one truly owns.  
Its neutrality is sacred, its pace unending, its hospitality unmatched.  
Every delver begins here, every merchant returns here, and every pilgrim’s ashes eventually pass through its roads.

> _“Baritte doesn’t remember names — only footsteps.”_

The Ministry views it as **Aerathon’s pulse**, the measure by which all other cities are timed.

---

### **Urban Feature: Traveler’s Flame**

_Any creature who has spent time in or trained at Baritte gains the following boon:_

> **Traveler’s Flame:** When you complete a short or long rest, choose one direction within sight or hearing (north, east, south, west). Until your next rest, you cannot become lost by non-magical means when traveling that direction. Once per long rest, you may also produce a faint magical flame in your palm, shedding light and warmth for 1 hour — extinguishable only by your will.
