# **Veyra**

### _The Shard Frontier — “The Desert Bleeds Glass.”_

**Filed Division:** Ministry of Delving Operations — Arcane Breach Division & Annals & Antiquities (Field Containment Archive)**

## **I. Overview**

**Veyra** is Arneria’s **westernmost settlement**, constructed within the **Obsidian Expanse** — a vast desert of fused black glass stretching to the horizon.  
The city gleams like a wound at sunset, every surface fractured and aflame with light.

Originally a delver outpost, Veyra grew into a mercantile fortress and research hub for relic anomalies.  
Today, it is equal parts **market, frontier, and containment site**, drawing scavengers, heretics, artificers, and the desperate in equal measure.

**Population:** ~880,000  
**Dominant Races:** Humans, Tieflings, Beastfolk (Hyena, Jackal, and Vulture), Half-Orcs  
**Regional Symbol:** A broken ring encircling a burning shard  
**Cultural Motto:** _“Glory is found in the cut.”_

---

## **II. Geography & Architecture**

Veyra sprawls across a landscape of sharp ridges and molten plateaus, divided into zones of habitation, excavation, and containment.

|**District**|**Description**|
|---|---|
|**The Shard Market**|Bazaar built into the glass hollows; sells relic fragments and containment charms.|
|**The Ember Warrens**|Worker barracks and delver quarters; network of cooling tunnels beneath the dunes.|
|**The Black Spire**|Central tower housing the Ministry’s Field Containment Office.|
|**The Gilded Cut**|Noble enclave and mercantile palaces protected by obsidian domes.|
|**The Below**|Sealed labyrinth threshold beneath the old crater — restricted zone.|
Architecture here is paradoxical: half sculpture, half ruin. Homes are carved into cooled glass, their interiors glowing with trapped embers from the desert’s creation.  
Every step echoes; every window reflects too much.

# **III. History**

Veyra’s creation is tied to the **Glassfall**, an event during the late Age of Collapse when an uncontained relic detonation turned the surrounding desert into a sea of molten glass.  
The survivors — miners, delvers, and pilgrims — built shelters within the cooled ruins and declared it sacred ground for those **“who refuse to yield to heat or gods.”**

**Historical Milestones:**

- **418 A.D.** — The **Glassfall** devastates the region; estimated death toll unknown as entire caravans vanish beneath molten dunes.
    
- **703 A.D.** — Founding of the first **delver enclaves**, carved into fractured obsidian caverns for shelter and ritual work.
    
- **1196 A.D.** — Establishment of an official **Ministry containment presence**, tasked with monitoring residual relic heat and glassstorms.
    
- **1583 A.D.** — Recognition as an **official frontier settlement**, granting rights to trade, defend, and excavate the Glasslands.
    
- **2768 A.D.** — Partial reactivation of the labyrinth beneath **“The Below”**; contained successfully after a three-day resonance event.
    

Even today, glass storms scour the outskirts — razor shards carried by winds that **sing like bells**.

---

## **IV. Governance**

Veyra is governed by a **Triarchate**, composed of:

- **The Flame Warden** — Oversees relic safety and containment.
    
- **The Ashlord** — Controls trade, guild affairs, and surface law.
    
- **The Mirror Speaker** — Mediates disputes through ritual reflection magic.
    

Each is appointed by the **Frontier Compact**, a council of guildmasters and Ministry observers.  
Unlike other Arnerian cities, divine rule holds little sway here — **pragmatism trumps piety.**

> _“Every oath melts in the sun; every truth hardens in the dark.”_ — Mirror Speaker Raal

---

## **V. Economy**

Veyra’s wealth lies in **relic salvage, glasswork, and alchemical refinement.**  
Its forges produce obsidian blades sharp enough to cut steel and containment glass resistant to spell energy.

**Primary Exports:** Refined obsidian, relic shards, spellglass, alchemical sand, beastbone.  
**Primary Imports:** Water (Evermead), ore (Malarthain), divine wards (Kuzmek).

**Institutions of Note:**

- **The Black Spire:** Ministry stronghold and primary containment facility.
    
- **The Glassblood Guild:** Craftsmen who shape molten glass into armor and weapons.
    
- **The Ember Ledger:** Registry for delvers, mercenaries, and expeditions.
    
- **The Forge of Broken Suns:** Relic reactor used for experimental containment.
    
- **The Shard Shrine:** Temple to Kossuth and Sarenrae; dedicated to endurance and rebirth.
    

---

## **VI. Faith & Culture**

Veyra’s people see **pain as proof of purpose.**  
Worship is pragmatic, focused on gods of endurance, fire, and redemption: **Kossuth**, **Sarenrae**, and **Ilmater**.  
Offerings are burned in glass crucibles, creating stained flame patterns called “veyras,” for which the city is named.

The populace embraces scars as marks of faith — visible reminders of having survived where others would have shattered.

**Festivals include:**

- **The Glassfall Vigil:** Commemoration of the city’s birth in destruction; citizens burn offerings that melt into sculptures.
    
- **The Rite of Cuts:** A coming-of-age trial where youths inscribe runes into molten glass by hand.
    
- **The Black Dawn:** Nightly market festival when obsidian lamps are lit with molten gold, illuminating the dunes.
    

---

## **VII. Law & Defense**

The **Ashguard** enforces order through strict but practical law — violence is tolerated if compensated, theft punished by labor, and lying to officials results in exile to the dunes.  
Prisons are nearly nonexistent; the desert itself serves that function.

Defenses include relic-tuned towers that **reflect heat into focused beams**, and militia groups of delvers armed with **glassforged polearms**.  
In times of emergency, the entire city can seal its entrances with slabs of fused sand, creating a fortress impossible to breach without melting through.

---

## **VIII. Relationship to the Labyrinths**

The labyrinth beneath Veyra, called **The Furnace Heart**, is one of the oldest and most unstable in the desert.  
Its shifting tunnels exude heat and light, and its walls contain veins of semi-living glass that pulse like molten arteries.

Delvers have recovered relics such as **the Mirror-Soul**, **the Blade of Breath**, and **Veyran Core Crystals** — all possessing residual energy from the Glassfall.  
The Ministry maintains permanent containment seals under the Black Spire, though incidents of “heat ghosts” — apparitions of long-dead delvers — remain common.

## **X. Cultural Identity & Legacy**

Veyra is **the scar that healed wrong — and made art of the wound.**  
Its people are cynical but creative, forging beauty out of ruin. They value resilience over purity, cunning over faith, and survival over peace.

> _“The desert makes us cruel to stay kind.”_ — Ashlord Mehren Taal

The Ministry views Veyra as **both miracle and warning** — proof that even the ruins of catastrophe can house civilization, so long as someone is stubborn enough to rebuild.

### **Urban Feature: Glassblood Tenacity**

_Any creature native to or trained within Veyra gains the following boon:_

> **Glassblood Tenacity:** Once per long rest, when you take fire or radiant damage, you can channel the Furnace Heart’s resilience. You gain resistance to that damage type until the end of your next turn, and your melee attacks deal +1d4 fire damage while your skin glows faintly like molten glass.
