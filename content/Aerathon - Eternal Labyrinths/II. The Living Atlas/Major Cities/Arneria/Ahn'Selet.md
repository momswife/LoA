# **Ahn’Selet**

### _The City of the Last Light — “Memory Burns Brighter Than Flesh.”_

## **I. Overview**

Hidden between the canyons of the **Sundered Reach**, **Ahn’Selet** stands as the **necropolis of Arneria**, a city carved into twilight itself.  
Here, the sun’s light refracts eternally along the canyon walls, creating an endless dusk — neither night nor day.

It is a place of quiet radiance and final transactions: the dying come to surrender memories, names, or faces in exchange for luminous preservation.  
Within Ahn’Selet’s glass catacombs, the dead remain visible, glowing faintly like stars beneath the earth.

**Population:** ~540,000 (living); ~2.1 million (interred)  
**Dominant Races:** Humans, Dwarves, Tieflings, Beastfolk (Jackal, Vulture), and Undying Constructs  
**Regional Symbol:** A descending sun encased within crystal  
**Cultural Motto:** _“We end as light.”_

---

## **II. Geography & Architecture**

Ahn’Selet is carved into a winding canyon that descends in spirals, each level older — and dimmer — than the last.  
Every wall is inscribed with glowing funerary runes that softly illuminate the path downward.

|**District**|**Description**|
|---|---|
|**The Dawn Gate**|Upper city where pilgrims and mourners arrive.|
|**The Mirror Courts**|Residential quarter of priests, scribes, and memory merchants.|
|**The Ember Halls**|Mortuary for body preparation and soul-lit glasswork.|
|**The Luminarium**|Cathedral-library where preserved souls are displayed and studied.|
|**The Depths of Silence**|Forbidden vaults housing relics and the city’s oldest immortal remains.|
The deeper one travels, the colder and quieter it becomes — until the air no longer carries sound, only the hum of trapped souls vibrating faintly through crystal walls.

---

# **III. History**

Founded in the aftermath of the **Glassfall of Veyra**, Ahn’Selet began as a resting site for delvers whose remains could not be burned.  
Its priests discovered that molten glass could hold **imprints of consciousness**, birthing the first **Lumin Crystals** — relics of memory made visible.

**Historical Milestones:**

- **562 A.D.** — First successful **preservation of a soul** within crystalline form, inspiring the earliest rites of luminous remembrance.
    
- **909 A.D.** — Construction of the **Luminarium**, a sanctuary where stored memories could be viewed, studied, or communed with.
    
- **1371 A.D.** — Establishment of the **Death-Tithe Law**, allowing voluntary surrender of memories in exchange for funded pilgrimages and final rites.
    
- **1886 A.D.** — Ministry recognition as a **sanctioned funerary district**, granting Ahn’Selet authority over relic-grade memorial artifacts.
    
- **2704 A.D.** — Opening of the **Depths of Silence** for containment research; site designated **Restricted Access** under Ministry law.
    

Ahn’Selet remains a city where echoes are tended like gardens —  
where the dead shine, and the living listen.

---

## **IV. Governance**

Ahn’Selet is ruled by the **Council of Luminaries**, an order of **Glass Priests** — mortal custodians who oversee the preservation process.  
Their leader, the **First Lightkeeper**, is chosen through **Inheritance of Flame**, a ritual where the souls of past leaders pass visions into a chosen successor’s dreams.

Ministry operations here are handled by the **Post-Mortem Phenomena Bureau**, a specialized division tasked with monitoring spectral anomalies and preventing relic corruption.

> _“To govern Ahn’Selet is to speak gently with ghosts.”_ — First Lightkeeper Serah Oul

---

## **V. Economy**

The city’s economy revolves around **funerary craft, relic preservation, and memory exchange.**  
Those near death come to barter what they can no longer keep — memories, skills, or secrets — for a chance to be immortalized in light.

**Primary Exports:** Lumin crystals, memorial glasswork, soul-ink, spectral reagents.  
**Primary Imports:** Food (Evermead), cloth (Daltiarna), relic wards (Kuzmek).

**Institutions of Note:**

- **The Luminarium:** Serves as both temple and archive; houses thousands of preserved souls.
    
- **The Ember Halls:** Mortuary district and center for light-forging techniques.
    
- **The Memory Bank:** Records memories transferred into crystal form, often leased by scholars or nobles.
    
- **The Death-Tithe Office:** Regulates voluntary donations of consciousness and personal history.
    
- **The Depths of Silence:** Restricted facility where aberrant relics and failed preservations are contained.
    

---

## **VI. Faith & Culture**

Faith in Ahn’Selet is **quiet devotion** to **Kelemvor**, **Sarenrae**, and **Mystra**, united under the belief that death is transformation, not negation.  
The city’s priests refer to the soul as “heat within the glass” — a spark to be cooled, shaped, and remembered.

Music is banned near the Luminarium; words are replaced with hums that vibrate the crystal walls in harmonic prayer.  
Pilgrims are expected to leave one keepsake behind — a symbolic “first death.”

**Festivals include:**

- **The Procession of Lights:** When the city’s upper terraces glow in cascading color as newly preserved souls are installed.
    
- **The Vigil of Remembrance:** A silent night where citizens light candles for every name etched in glass.
    
- **The Refracting Dawn:** Annual sunrise ritual that renews the canyon’s luminescent wards.
    

---

## **VII. Law & Defense**

The city’s legal code, **The Covenant of Still Flame**, mandates absolute respect for the dead — desecration is punishable by exile and memory erasure.  
Criminals may be sentenced to **The Dimming**, a process that severs their reflection, rendering them unseen by light.

Defense falls to the **Order of the Last Watch**, a solemn guard of mortals and spectral allies who patrol the canyon at dusk.  
Should corruption rise from the Depths of Silence, the priests activate the **Glass Choir** — thousands of soul crystals resonating in unison to repel undead or planar breaches.

---

## **VIII. Relationship to the Labyrinths**

Beneath the Depths of Silence lies a **subterranean labyrinth** known as **The Hollow Mirror**, believed to be the original resting place of the souls now contained in Ahn’Selet’s walls.  
The labyrinth emits faint spectral light and is rumored to connect to the **Furnace Heart** beneath Veyra.

The Ministry forbids delving, though whispers persist that the First Lightkeeper occasionally sends chosen priests to “listen for new names.”

Recovered relics include **Lumin Hearts**, crystals that record the final sensations of the dying, and **Echo Glass**, which can project stored memories as spectral apparitions.

## **X. Cultural Identity & Legacy**

Ahn’Selet is **the afterthought of every Arnerian prayer** — the hush that follows the forge, the battle, and the storm.  
Its people are serene, meticulous, and haunted by purpose. They do not fear death; they prepare for it like a ceremony.

> _“The dead are not gone. They are simply quieter now.”_ — Common saying among Seletans

To the Ministry, Ahn’Selet is **a vital containment site** and a philosophical question:  
how long can light remember a soul before it begins to believe it is alive again?

### **Urban Feature: Last Light’s Boon**

_Any creature consecrated, interred, or trained in Ahn’Selet gains the following boon:_

> **Last Light’s Boon:** Once per long rest, when you would be reduced to 0 hit points, your body erupts in faint golden light. You instead remain conscious with 1 HP, and emit dim light in a 10-ft radius. Allies who can see you gain advantage on death saving throws until the start of your next turn.
