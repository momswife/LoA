### _The Glass City — “What You See Is What You’ve Paid For.”_

## **I. Overview**

**Porta Strega** is Vinyot’s **arcane capital**, a cliffside city built around a natural prism canyon where sunlight fractures into perpetual auroras.  
It is the home of **witches, illusionists, and scrywrights**, whose enchantments turn entire streets into living artworks.

Every window in Porta Strega sees something different. Every shadow hides something worth studying.

Here, beauty is a weapon, and secrecy a form of currency.

**Population:** ~910,000  
**Dominant Races:** Elves, Humans, Aasimar, and Beastfolk (Ravens, Foxes, Serpents)  
**Regional Symbol:** An open eye surrounded by fractured crystal shards  
**Cultural Motto:** _“Truth refracts.”_

---

## **II. Geography & Architecture**

Perched upon the **Stregan Bluffs**, the city cascades down toward the sea in shimmering terraces of **enchanted glass, silverstone, and living light.**  
When sunlight strikes the cliffs, the entire skyline becomes a cascade of chromatic reflection — disorienting, beautiful, and entirely deliberate.

| **District**           | **Description**                                                                              |
| ---------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **The Prism Quarter**  | Central plaza of illusion academies and scrying towers.                                      |
| **The Liminal Market** | Shifting bazaar that changes location daily; no map has ever stayed accurate.                |
| **The Shardsteps**     | Residential tier cut into glass canyons, accessible only by illusionary bridges.             |
| **The Veilhouse**      | Ministry-sanctioned illusion guild that regulates dream and memory enchantments.             |
| **The Mirror Vaults**  | Underground labyrinth of mirrored chambers housing the city’s archives and forbidden relics. |
At night, the city’s glass emits soft light from within — a magical phenomenon known as **The Stregan Glow**, created by residual scrying energy trapped in the walls themselves.

---

# **III. History**

Porta Strega began as a **witch enclave** during the early Reconstruction.  
Refugees from Louvain and Al’ar brought their traditions of glassblowing, spellcraft, and augury to the cliffs, seeking a place where light and magic could mingle freely.

**Historical Milestones:**

- **487 A.D.** — Founding of the first coven, the **Order of the Prism Veil**, whose glass sigils still mark the oldest cliff halls.
    
- **921 A.D.** — **The Glassblight** — an uncontrolled illusion storm fractures reality along the cliffline; the city is rebuilt using stabilized arcane glass.
    
- **1386 A.D.** — Ministry recognition as an **independent academic district**, granting autonomy for sanctioned illusion research.
    
- **1724 A.D.** — Establishment of the **Veilhouse**, codifying legal illusion craft and regulating mirrorwork, glamours, and reflective wards.
    
- **3012 A.D.** — **The Reflection Schism** — half the city’s population vanishes during a failed mirror experiment; Ministry classification remains _unresolved_.
    

Porta Strega endures as a sanctuary of mirrors, mages, and mysteries — a place where light bends, glass breathes, and reflections sometimes whisper back.

---

## **IV. Governance**

Porta Strega is ruled by the **Council of Nine Mirrors**, composed of the nine eldest members of the Veilhouse covens.  
Each holds authority over one “School of Reflection” — illusion, enchantment, divination, dreamcraft, glamour, perception, misdirection, scrying, and glasswork.

The Ministry’s **Arcane Breach Division** operates a permanent outpost beneath the **Mirror Vaults**, ensuring magical containment and compliance with relic law.

Council proceedings are observed through _memory echoes_ rather than public presence — each decision replayed afterward to the citizens as a projected illusion in the Prism Quarter.

> _“We trust what we saw, not who showed it to us.”_ — Veilhouse Axiom

---

## **V. Economy**

The city’s wealth flows from **illusion licensing, divination contracts, and enchanted architecture**.  
Every noble house in Aerathon employs Stregan enchanters, and every Ministry archive owes some of its preservation to their memory-glass.

**Primary Exports:** Enchanted glass, scrying lenses, illusion charms, glamour reagents, dream distillates.  
**Primary Imports:** Food (Evermead), metals (Malarthain), magitech conduits (Pristana).

**Institutions of Note:**

- **The Veilhouse:** Central authority for illusion craft and magical licensing.
    
- **The Academy of Reflected Thought:** Prestigious university for mages and delvers alike.
    
- **The Mirror Vaults:** Secure relic repository and arcane research site.
    
- **The Glass Cathedral:** Temple to Mystra and Savras, its ceiling refracting constellations from another sky.
    
- **The House of False Faces:** Theater guild known for its living masks that change expression independently.
    

---

## **VI. Faith & Culture**

The Stregans’ faith is built upon paradox. They worship **Mystra** (Goddess of Magic) and **Savras** (God of Divination), but believe both exist only in reflection — as images of what mortals think magic to be.

To them, illusion is not deceit; it is interpretation.  
Every lie holds a truth about the liar.

Festivals include:

- **The Festival of Refraction:** Annual event where the entire city’s glass facades project visions of the year’s memories — true or not.
    
- **The Night of Unmasking:** When all citizens wear mirrors instead of faces, reflecting each other’s image until dawn.
    
- **The Glassblight Vigil:** Commemoration of the vanished; every house lights a candle for the reflections that never returned.
    

---

## **VII. Law & Defense**

Porta Strega enforces the **Veil Codex**, a set of laws governing illusion ethics and magical consent.  
Deceit without consent is a capital crime; paradoxically, _deceit with consent_ is an accepted art form.

The **Mirrorguard** patrols the city equipped with enchanted visors that reveal falsehoods and suppress glamours.  
In crisis, the city activates the **Ninefold Shroud** — a defensive illusion that multiplies the entire skyline, confusing invaders and deflecting spells.

Criminals are not executed, but _unmade_ — stripped of reflection until they cast no shadow, a sentence known as **Erasure**.

---

## **VIII. Relationship to the Labyrinths**

Beneath Porta Strega lies **The Inverted Vault**, a labyrinthine mirror realm formed by unstable scrying rituals centuries ago.  
The Ministry forbids entry, though rumors persist that reflected versions of the living still wander its halls.

The city’s enchanters supply relic containment lenses and memory vaults to Ministry divisions worldwide, though several “unverified constructs” have been linked to breaches of perception law.

Delvers who train here learn the art of **Veilcraft** — magic that manipulates reality’s edges without breaking it.

## **X. Cultural Identity & Legacy**

Porta Strega exists in contradiction: honest deception, structured chaos, immortal impermanence.  
Its citizens live by the creed that **truth is what endures when beauty fades.**

To outsiders, it is a city of vanity. To insiders, it is a temple to perception — proof that reality itself is an act of creation.

> _“If Louvain writes what is real, Porta Strega dreams what could be.”_ — Lt. Salvia Korrin

The Ministry regards Porta Strega as both **danger and necessity** — a reminder that understanding magic is not the same as controlling it.

### **Urban Feature: Veilcraft Insight**

_Any creature initiated or trained within Porta Strega gains the following boon:_

> **Veilcraft Insight:** Once per long rest, when you are targeted by an illusion or enchantment spell, you may choose to perceive its structure instead of resisting it. You gain advantage on Intelligence (Arcana) or Wisdom (Insight) checks for one minute, and can detect hidden illusions as faint ripples in the air.
