# **Sourisport**

### _The Ash-Tide Haven — “Trade Drowns Truth.”_

**Filed Division:** Ministry of Delving Operations — The Living Atlas (Economic Oversight & Field Intelligence Division)**

## **I. Overview**

Built at the delta convergence between the **Ashwater River** and the **Gilded Sea**, **Sourisport** is Vinyot’s _unofficial_ southern port — a trade junction for contraband, relic salvage, and backchannel diplomacy.  
The air smells of oil, ozone, and ambition.

Unlike Southwinds’ bright harbors, Sourisport hides beneath its own smoke.  
Half its population lives on anchored barges, and its riverfront warehouses form a semi-submerged maze known locally as **The Drownmarket**.

The city thrives on contradictions: lawful in charter, criminal in practice.

**Population:** ~1.4 million  
**Dominant Races:** Humans, Ratfolk (Beastfolk), Goblinoids, and amphibious species  
**Regional Symbol:** A black coin beneath a silver wave  
**Cultural Motto:** _“What the river forgets, we remember.”_

---

## **II. Geography & Architecture**

Sourisport sprawls across **three riverfront levels**, gradually sinking into the Ashwater’s tidal basin.  
Its infrastructure is a patchwork of stone, barge, and rope — designed less to last than to _float just long enough._

| **District**        | **Description**                                                           |
| ------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **The Drownmarket** | Floating bazaar for relics, stolen goods, and mercenary contracts.        |
| **The Riverforge**  | Industrial district producing smog, tools, and counterfeit seals.         |
| **The Ashbanks**    | Shantytown of raft homes and temporary guild camps.                       |
| **The Fogspire**    | Ministry customs tower overseeing official trade (and unofficial bribes). |
| **The Silt Vaults** | Subterranean warehouse network doubling as black auction venue.           |
The city’s shifting tides often submerge entire streets, forcing locals to build upward in narrow, stacked layers.  
At night, torchlight reflects on the oily water like a thousand eyes watching.

---

# **III. History**

Sourisport was never meant to exist.  
It began as a **refugee dock**, later absorbed into Vinyot’s merchant network when smuggling became too profitable to ignore.  
The city’s very name — _Souris_, meaning **shadow-river** in old Brethren dialect — reflects its murky legality and ever-shifting loyalties.

**Historical Milestones:**

- **532 A.D.** — First mention of Sourisport as a “**free port**” in Ministry trade logs; unofficial tariffs noted.
    
- **889 A.D.** — Outlaw harbor seized by League mercenaries, then quietly returned to operation after unexplained political “adjustments.”
    
- **1276 A.D.** — Ministry establishes the **Fogspire** for monitoring illicit activity — documented corruption appears within _two weeks_.
    
- **1683 A.D.** — Emergence of the **Dockshadow Syndicates**; inter-guild conflicts erupt into open warfare across the south piers.
    
- **2958 A.D.** — Signing of the **Ashwater Accord**, formally recognizing Sourisport as **“autonomous under oversight”** — the most begrudging category in Ministry law.
    

Sourisport remains a place where coin changes hands faster than the tides —  
a haven for smugglers, traders, exiles, and anyone who thrives in the space between legality and opportunity.

---

## **IV. Governance**

The city is governed — unofficially — by the **Dockshadow Syndicates**, a coalition of crime families, merchant guilds, and Beastfolk trade unions.  
Each controls one tide of the city (High, Mid, or Low) and meets monthly under the **Bridge of Knives** to negotiate “tides of peace.”

Officially, Sourisport answers to a **Harbormaster**, currently **Vellan Kreel**, whose authority extends only as far as her bribe money allows.  
The Ministry’s **Customs Envoy**, Lt. Salvia Korrin, acts as liaison to ensure relic transport remains on record — or at least, _appears_ to be.

> _“Every law has a loophole. Sourisport is what happens when a city becomes one.”_ — Halin Drauve

---

## **V. Economy**

Sourisport’s economy is a study in gray: half legal, half lucrative.  
It functions as the **nexus for off-ledger trade** — goods that cannot or should not exist elsewhere.

**Primary Exports:** Contraband, relic fragments, forged documents, mercenary contracts, spell-ink, counterfeit charms.  
**Primary Imports:** Grain (Evermead), alcohol (Bellhaven), and smuggled relics from Malarthain.

**Institutions of Note:**

- **The Drownmarket:** The beating heart of Sourisport; floating platforms where delvers sell raw relics before the Ministry can inspect them.
    
- **The Fogspire:** The one honest building in the city; houses the Ministry’s Customs Office and Enforcement Contingent.
    
- **The Gutterlight Guild:** Thieves’ guild that regulates “unofficial commerce” to prevent anarchy.
    
- **The Rusted Cup:** Tavern-bourse for mercenaries; its patrons are rumored to include Ministry agents.
    
- **The Silverwake Consortium:** Moneylenders specializing in “resurrection credit” — loans to delvers payable only if they return alive.
    

---

## **VI. Faith & Culture**

Sourisport’s culture worships practicality. Its gods are whispered, not named.  
Shrines to **Mask**, **Olidammara**, and **Beshaba** stand beside makeshift altars to **Avandra**, patron of luck and freedom.

Festivals are rare but lively — the most famous being:

- **The Floodwake Revel:** When the Ashwater overruns the docks, locals decorate rafts with lanterns and float through the flooded streets, drinking and trading by moonlight.
    
- **The Feast of the Forgotten:** Names of lost delvers are carved into driftwood and set adrift to sea.
    

Superstition rules the people more than faith: every Sourisport merchant keeps a coin with a hole through it, a charm said to “let truth leak out slowly.”

---

## **VII. Law & Defense**

The **Tideguard** enforces order when paid to.  
Otherwise, protection comes from the Syndicates or Ministry oversight — whichever is faster.  
Crimes are settled through the **Trial of Rope**, where offenders duel while suspended over the river on swaying bridges.

The city’s greatest defense lies in confusion: no map stays accurate for long, and no patrol crosses the same street twice in one night.

Should a true crisis arise, the Syndicates activate the **Ashfire Pact** — a coalition militia of dockworkers, delvers, and smugglers united under the threat of losing profit.

---

## **VIII. Relationship to the Labyrinths**

The labyrinth-adjacent caverns beneath the Ashwater Delta are among the most unstable in Vinyot.  
Known collectively as **The Black Channels**, these tunnels serve as both smuggling routes and relic dumping grounds.

Several documented relic surges have created **“phantom delves”**, temporary labyrinth echoes that fade after weeks.  
Delvers often race to claim their spoils before the Ministry even arrives.

The **Fogspire Office** maintains an ongoing investigation (case #C-1194) into missing delvers presumed “swallowed by the river.”

## **X. Cultural Identity & Legacy**

Sourisport exists in the moral twilight — a city that proves civilization can survive without purity, only persistence.  
Its people prize adaptability above all else; they are survivors, negotiators, and liars who still know the meaning of loyalty.

> _“In Sourisport, even betrayal comes with a receipt.”_ — Dock proverb

The Ministry regards Sourisport as a **controlled infection** — necessary to keep the arteries of trade from clotting, so long as it remembers who its host is.

### **Urban Feature: Coin of Shadows**

_Any creature native to or employed in Sourisport gains the following boon:_

> **Coin of Shadows:** Once per long rest, you may call upon the city’s code of convenience. When rolling Deception, Sleight of Hand, or Persuasion, you may flip a coin; on heads, treat your roll as if you rolled a 19. On tails, you automatically fail and the next hour’s attempts of that skill are made at disadvantage. The coin vanishes in smoke afterward.
