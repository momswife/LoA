# **Tempest’s Wake**

### _The Storm-Fleet Citadel — “Where the Sky Comes to Die.”_

---

## **I. Overview**

**Tempest’s Wake** is not a city in the traditional sense but a **massive flotilla** of interlocked ships, floating citadels, and airborne platforms that eternally circle the eye of the **Maelwind Crown** — a storm so vast it appears as a luminous spiral from the stars.

It is the last frontier of Al’ar — the final port before the edge of known seas and the first home of those who consider the storm itself a god.

**Population:** ~1.3 million (variable; losses and new ships are constant)  
**Dominant Races:** Humans, Aarakocra, Genasi (Air/Storm), Dragonborn, and Beastfolk (Eagle, Shark, Crocodile)  
**Regional Symbol:** A broken mast crowned with lightning  
**Cultural Motto:** _“Ride the edge, or be forgotten.”_

---

## **II. Geography & Structure**

The Wake consists of **six major fortress-vessels** and hundreds of minor crafts lashed together into storm-formations that shift according to the prevailing winds.  
These are collectively called the **Fleet-Cities**, each with its own culture and function.

|**Fleet-City**|**Description**|
|---|---|
|**The Crownspire**|Central floating citadel; headquarters of the Stormwardens.|
|**The Wavereach**|Naval foundry and port; ships anchored by chain-harpoons into the lightning fields.|
|**The Thundersway**|Market district; home to delvers, smugglers, and relic traders.|
|**The Eyefall**|Religious fleet dedicated to the worship of the storm as divine will.|
|**The Howling Span**|Gladiatorial decks and dueling platforms suspended between ships.|
|**The Driftbones**|Graveyard fleet; hollow hulls filled with salt and wind charms for the dead.|
Above all looms the **Crownspire**, a relic of impossible scale — a rotating tower anchored in midair by chained lightning. It is visible from miles away, glowing in the clouds like a second sun.

---

# **III. History**

The first records of Tempest’s Wake date back to the **Storm Exodus**, when survivors of shattered fleets chose to live _within_ the Maelwind rather than flee it.  
They learned to ride its spiraling currents, guiding their ships through conductive relic chains and lightning-forged sails.

**Historical Milestones:**

- **411 A.D.** — The First Fleet unites under Captain **Vaela Tern**, later called _The Tempest-Born._
    
- **739 A.D.** — Founding of the **Stormwardens** and construction of the first Crownspire Core, stabilizing the inner winds.
    
- **1182 A.D.** — Ministry designation as an **Autonomous Maritime Zone**, granting self-governing rights.
    
- **1694 A.D.** — **Lightning Harvest** discovered; the Wake becomes energy self-sufficient through captured skyfire.
    
- **2603 A.D.** — **The Great Drowning** — one-third of the fleet lost to a sudden stormshift event.
    

Even after near-total ruin, the Wake rebuilt — its people saying simply,  
_“The wind still remembers us.”_

---

## **IV. Governance**

Tempest’s Wake is ruled by the **Council of Chains**, composed of captains representing each fleet-city and the **High Stormwarden**, chosen every nine years through ritual combat and prophecy.

The Ministry holds nominal jurisdiction under the **Aerothaumic Charter**, granting autonomy so long as relic weaponry remains contained.  
In practice, the Wake governs itself, answering only to the storm — and to those strong enough to command it.

> _“Authority here must shout louder than thunder.”_ — Lt. Salvia Korrin

---

## **V. Economy**

The Wake’s economy thrives on **lightning harvest, relic salvage, and mercenary labor.**  
Every storm is a forge — sailors use conductor spires to trap lightning, channeling it into capacitors called **stormhearts** that power ships, forges, and weapons.

**Primary Exports:** Stormhearts, skyship components, relic alloys, bottled lightning, tempest-glass.  
**Primary Imports:** Food (Evermead), sacred oils (Kuzmek), relic fuel (Caer Dain).

**Institutions of Note:**

- **The Stormforge:** Industrial ship where lightning is refined into weapon energy.
    
- **The Crownspire Core:** Heart of the city; functions as both power source and temple.
    
- **The Gale Market:** Nomadic bazaar suspended between fleets.
    
- **The Vault of Chains:** Ministry-regulated containment ship for relics of unknown classification.
    
- **The Wind Temple:** Floating shrine dedicated to Akadi, Kord, and Talos.
    

---

## **VI. Faith & Culture**

The people of Tempest’s Wake believe the storm is **alive** — not as a god of destruction, but of **motion, trial, and rebirth.**  
They worship the triad of **Akadi, Talos, and Kord**, but refer to them collectively as _The Breath Eternal._

To die in the storm is considered sacred; to survive it, divine favor.  
Children are baptized by being lashed to the mast during their first thunder squall — their screams joining the choir of wind.

**Festivals include:**

- **The Lightning Feast:** Annual celebration of the first caught bolt; ships race through the outer winds to collect it.
    
- **The Day of Sails:** When the storm calms, sails are unfurled in complete silence.
    
- **The Return of the Chain:** A mourning procession for ships lost to the Maelwind — their relic anchors cast into the eye to “feed the storm.”
    

---

## **VII. Law & Defense**

Law aboard the Wake follows the **Code of Storms**: _“If you can hold the deck, you can hold the right.”_  
Justice is public and immediate — duels fought atop storm masts settle most disputes, while executions are rare but dramatic: offenders are bound to lightning rods during harvest.

Defense is constant motion.

- **The Stormwardens:** Elite aerial knights commanding relic-gliders and skyblades.
    
- **The Skyforge Marines:** Amphibious troops maintaining hull integrity and outer barricades.
    
- **The Crownspire Array:** A network of rods and sails channeling lightning into destructive arcs — enough to incinerate entire fleets.
    

In the event of catastrophe, the Wake activates **the Eye Retreat** — a coordinated inward spiral into the Maelwind’s calm center, where the fleet can survive indefinitely.

---

## **VIII. Relationship to the Labyrinths**

Deep within the Maelwind’s eye drifts the **Labyrinth of Chains and Storms**, believed to be the atmospheric twin of Caer Dain’s forge-labyrinth.  
Its corridors manifest as floating rings of condensed cloud-metal, forming and dispersing with each thunderclap.

Delvers who enter report losing all sense of up or down, claiming to hear whispers of gods “forging lightning with their hands.”  
Recovered relics include **Stormhearts**, **Skyshards**, and **Tempest Blades**, each carrying sentient sparks that must be contained in dampening casings.

The Ministry classifies the labyrinth as **Mobile-Elemental (Containment Grade Beta)** — dangerous but invaluable.

## **X. Cultural Identity & Legacy**

Tempest’s Wake is **a civilization that refuses to anchor.**  
Its citizens live on the knife’s edge of annihilation and call it freedom.  
They revere risk, despise stillness, and define home as “anywhere the thunder follows.”

> _“We are lightning pretending to be people.”_ — Stormborn saying

The Ministry calls Tempest’s Wake _the impossible city_ — proof that even chaos can be built upon, if one accepts that foundations need not be solid.

### **Urban Feature: Wrath of the Maelwind**

_Any creature initiated or born within Tempest’s Wake gains the following boon:_

> **Wrath of the Maelwind:** Once per long rest, when you roll a critical hit or a natural 20 on a saving throw, lightning dances across your body. For one minute, your movement speed increases by 10 ft, your weapon attacks deal +1d4 lightning damage, and ranged attacks against you have disadvantage due to violent gusts.
