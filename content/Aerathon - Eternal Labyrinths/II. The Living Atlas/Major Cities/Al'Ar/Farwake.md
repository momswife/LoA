# **Farwake**

### _The Sunken Metropolis — “The Deep Remembers What the Sky Forgets.”_

**Filed Division:** Ministry of Delving Operations — Arcane Breach Division & Annals & Antiquities (Subaquatic Relic Oversight Bureau)**

## **I. Overview**

**Farwake** is a submerged city built within the **Farwake Trench**, a continental fissure descending over six miles beneath the Shattersea.  
It is the **lowest inhabited settlement in Aerathon**, its domes lit by bio-luminescent coral and the eerie blue glow of the **Abyssal Vault** — a labyrinthic relic structure slumbering on the trench floor.

The city’s inhabitants claim that time passes differently below; hours stretch, voices carry for miles, and dreams echo longer than words.

**Population:** ~830,000 (variable; fluctuates with current shifts)  
**Dominant Races:** Tritons, Merfolk, Humans, Genasi (Water), and Abyss-touched Beastfolk (Seal, Shark, Lanternfish)  
**Regional Symbol:** A single glowing ring descending into darkness  
**Cultural Motto:** _“We live in the pause between heartbeats.”_

---

## **II. Geography & Architecture**

Farwake is arranged in concentric **pressure-domes** anchored to the trench wall by massive coral chains. The domes are connected by transparent tunnels that glow faintly from interior plankton light.

|**District**|**Description**|
|---|---|
|**The Upper Veil**|Arrival docks; Ministry consulate and trade quarters.|
|**The Glass Quarter**|Main residential area; domes constructed of relic glass that pulses with current flow.|
|**The Luminous Market**|Bazaar suspended by chains above a light well; sells relic pearls and bottled biolight.|
|**The Dreaming Hall**|Temple-library dedicated to the study of deep resonance.|
|**The Verge of Black**|Outermost observation platforms near the Abyssal Vault; restricted zone.|
From above, the city appears as a **halo of blue-white lights**, drifting like a constellation fallen into the sea.

---

# **III. History**

The founding of Farwake traces back to the aftermath of the **Storm of 993**, when relic divers from Namira discovered breathable pockets deep within the trench.  
They uncovered remnants of a civilization predating the Collapse — crystalline corridors still functioning after millennia.

**Historical Milestones:**

- **304 A.D.** — Discovery of the trench’s air pockets following the Storm of 993.
    
- **587 A.D.** — Construction of the first habitation domes carved into the upper trench walls.
    
- **1129 A.D.** — Activation of the Abyssal Ward Network, stabilizing pressure and preventing trench-collapse.
    
- **1764 A.D.** — Ministry designation as **Deepwater Outpost Alpha**, granting Farwake research sovereignty.
    
- **2418 A.D.** — **Breach Event 3-Ω**: partial awakening of the Abyssal Vault; containment successful, though the deep hum has never fully ceased.
    

Since then, Farwake has remained a paradox — a thriving settlement built beside a sleeping god of machinery.

---

## **IV. Governance**

Farwake is ruled jointly by the **Council of Depths**, a triarchic system consisting of:

- **The Abysswarden** — Oversees containment of the Vault and relic security.
    
- **The Tide Consul** — Manages civil infrastructure and trade with Namira.
    
- **The Echo Seer** — Interprets signals and dreams received from the trench below.
    

The Ministry’s **Subaquatic Oversight Bureau** serves as the fourth, silent authority — vetoing any decision deemed a containment risk.

> _“The deeper we go, the less we command. We negotiate.”_ — Abysswarden Seru Mal

---

## **V. Economy**

Farwake’s economy revolves around **salvage, alchemy, and relic resonance.**  
The city exports **bio-luminescent crystals, deep pearls, and compressed pressure-glass**, all harvested from living coral grown around ancient machinery.

**Primary Exports:** Biolight reagents, pressure alloys, sound-glass, Abyssal ink, and dream elixirs.  
**Primary Imports:** Food (Evermead), machinery (Balthaer), relic wards (Caer Dain).

**Institutions of Note:**

- **The Dreaming Hall:** Temple-library where resonance data is translated into melody.
    
- **The Coral Bank:** Currency storage — values measured in light intensity, not coin.
    
- **The Vault of Stillness:** Ministry-supervised containment sphere surrounding the Abyssal Vault.
    
- **The Pressure Yards:** Foundries crafting deep-tolerant armor and automata.
    
- **The Lantern Gardens:** Residential domes cultivating symbiotic bio-light flora.
    

---

## **VI. Faith & Culture**

Farwake’s faith is **a theology of depth.**  
The people worship **Thassa**, **Deep Sashelas**, and **Umberlee**, but not as gods of chaos — rather as reflections of patience, pressure, and silence.  
They believe enlightenment lies in surrender, not struggle — to sink is to understand.

Songs are replaced with humming rituals that resonate through water, forming visible rings of light.  
Children learn to breathe rhythmically — **the “Three Tides”** — to calm the heart during pressure shifts.

**Festivals include:**

- **The Descent:** Annual ceremony where divers swim to the edge of the Verge, tethered by light cords, and sing until echoes return.
    
- **The Lightless Vigil:** Night when the domes extinguish illumination, honoring those lost to the trench.
    
- **The Renewal of Breath:** Celebration of surface visitors bringing new air to the domes.
    

---

## **VII. Law & Defense**

Farwake follows the **Edict of Weight**, which values equilibrium above all else: _“Every action in the deep has a cost.”_  
Justice is based on restitution; major crimes result in **service to the Verge**, maintaining containment seals in perpetual dark.

Defense is technological and aquatic:

- **The Abyss Wardens:** Elite divers operating relic-armor that channels hydrokinetic force.
    
- **The Pressure Gates:** Energy fields regulating current flow and repelling intruders.
    
- **The Luminous Guard:** Sentinels wielding light-based weaponry to blind and burn deep-born entities.
    

In the event of total breach, the entire city can ascend via buoyant engines, severing itself from the trench — a process referred to as the **Rising Protocol.**

---

## **VIII. Relationship to the Labyrinths**

The **Abyssal Vault** beneath Farwake is one of the oldest known labyrinths in Aerathon.  
Its corridors pulse like veins, and the air within tastes metallic and ancient.  
Delvers report hearing “songs of whales that never existed,” suggesting the structure itself dreams.

The Ministry classifies it as **Dormant-Sentient (Containment Grade Omega).**  
Relic fragments from within show faint self-repair and emotion response to human presence.

> _“It dreams of the surface. We ensure it never wakes.”_ — Lt. Korrin

## **X. Cultural Identity & Legacy**

Farwake is **the silence beneath creation.**  
Its citizens are measured, deliberate, and eerily serene — they laugh softly, speak sparingly, and claim to dream more vividly than they live.

> _“We are not beneath the sea. The world above is simply louder.”_ — Abyssal proverb

The Ministry regards Farwake as **a masterpiece of coexistence** — a fragile balance between civilization and the sleeping power of the labyrinth below.

### **Urban Feature: Breath of the Abyss**

_Any creature trained or initiated in Farwake gains the following boon:_

> **Breath of the Abyss:** Once per long rest, you can invoke the rhythm of the deep. For one minute, you can breathe underwater, gain darkvision to 60 ft if you do not already have it, and your movement is unhindered by water pressure. While this boon lasts, your voice carries twice its normal range underwater.
