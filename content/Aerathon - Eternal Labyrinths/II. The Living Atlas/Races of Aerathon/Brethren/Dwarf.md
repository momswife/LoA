## **Overview**

Dwarves in Aerathon are the anchors in the storm. Forged in hardship and tempered by legacy, they are a people of stone, oaths, and memory. Where humans stride boldly into the unknown and elves chart patterns in the stars, dwarves carve their path one strike at a time, each blow deliberate, each step earned.

Though not blessed by beastly transformation nor descended from divine blood, dwarves claim a sacred bond with the world’s raw foundations. The oldest dwarven records speak of a pact made not with gods, but with the mountain itself—a compact of patience, strength, and endurance. Whether myth or truth, it has shaped their culture since before the Convergence.

Dwarves are most common in Oria's cliffside holds and deep labyrinthine vaults, though many have found homes in Arnerian strongholds, Vinyotian foundries, and Allemagnian artisan guilds. While not every delver crew includes a dwarf, those who do tend to survive longer.

Dwarves are famed delvers not only for their physical toughness, but for their capacity to read structural stress like a second language. Where others see ruin, dwarves see potential. Where others see danger, dwarves see design.

They are private, proud, and deeply communal—a paradox only understood by living alongside them. They carry grudges like others carry heirlooms, but loyalty forged in the heat of delving never cools.

## **Stronghold Traits**

### **Allemance**

Allemagnian dwarves are guildsmen and guildswomen, respected for their craftwork, legal acumen, and ceremonial forgemoots. They wear brocade and chain both, and consider diplomacy a form of smithing: loud, hot, and capable of reshaping steel and stubborn minds alike.

**Guildhammer Wit.** You gain proficiency in Persuasion. When using artisan’s tools you are proficient with, you can add your Charisma modifier to the result of the check.

---

### **Oria**

Oric dwarves are reclusive, stoic, and bound to ancient traditions of quarry-right and ancestor-honor. They build their cities beneath glaciers and fire-veined mountains, their work driven by principles older than kingdoms. Many have turned their holds into fortress-monasteries that guard against the deep things that crawl from the Labyrinth.

**Mountain Vow.** You have advantage on saving throws against being frightened. Additionally, if you are underground or touching stone, you can reroll one failed saving throw per long rest.

---

### **Vinyot**

Dwarves in Vinyot thrive as inventors and metallurgists, combining ancient traditions with industrial drive. Steamworks, arcane elevators, and gear-forged limbs are just a few of the wonders that emerged from Vinyotian dwarven halls. They treat contracts like iron and debts like molten lead—a matter to be reshaped only with great heat.

**Arcforge Savvy.** You gain proficiency with tinker’s tools. When crafting a magical or mechanical item, you reduce the required time by half.

---

### **Arneria**

In the sandstone reaches of the Beylik and the catacombs of the Causeway, dwarves have carved out a legacy among the scribes and stone-layers of the desert. They are prized for their ability to preserve structure against erosion, both in architecture and in society.

**Dust-Marked.** You have resistance to damage from effects caused by sand, dust, or wind. You also have advantage on Intelligence (Investigation) checks to locate hidden mechanisms, switches, or pressure plates.

---

### **Al’Ar**

Island dwarves of Al’Ar are rare but revered. Here, their smithing skills are turned toward coral-laced alloys, ship-keels, and water-channel architecture. Many serve as underdeck engineers or shrine-forgers in service of sea-gods.

**Salt-Hardened.** You gain a swim speed of 25 feet. You can hold your breath for up to 15 minutes and ignore the effects of natural rust or corrosion on metal equipment you wear or carry.
