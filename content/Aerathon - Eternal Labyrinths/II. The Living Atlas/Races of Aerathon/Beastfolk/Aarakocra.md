> _"The wind is not my enemy. It is my heartbeat. It carries my soul forward, and my strike downward."_

___

## **Overview**

Aarakocra in Aerathon are the skyborne spirits of the high reaches, descendants of windbound pacts and elemental bloodlines that once tethered the clouds to the peaks of the world. Born with wings and hearts full of motion, they hail from jagged cliff-cities and floating sanctuaries anchored to storms. Most call the updrafts of Oria or the cloud-shadowed ranges of Arneria home, though scattered flocks can be found in every homeland.

While other species dig, build, or march, aarakocra _soar_. Their culture values speed, clarity, and high vantage—both literal and philosophical. They speak in short phrases, rarely wasting breath, and their judgment of others is often delivered as briskly as a dive from the sky.

Aarakocra delvers are natural scouts and messengers. In the Labyrinths, they use their aerial agility to bypass traps, cross chasms, and relay tactics from above. Their affinity for the elemental winds allows some to manipulate air itself, whether as monks, storm sorcerers, or primal wardens.

Unlike their grounded kin, aarakocra often view the Labyrinths not as trials or places of conquest, but as _earthbound wounds_ — a disruption of the natural currents. Delving, to them, is a form of healing, and each descent is followed by a flight to cleanse the spirit.

## **Heritage Traits**

### **Allemance**

In the gilded cities of Allemance, aarakocra serve as sky-scribes, tacticians, and ornamental sentries. Their crisp precision and aerial maneuvering make them ideal couriers for high-priority messages. Allemagnian towers often have roost balconies, where these windfolk perch between duties.

**Courier Precision.** You gain proficiency in the Insight skill. When you Dash or Disengage on your turn, you can also make a Wisdom (Perception) check as a bonus action.

---

### **Oria**

This is the true ancestral home of Aerathon’s aarakocra. Oric flocks build aerie-settlements into the very marrow of the mountains. There, flight is taught with lullabies, and high-altitude duels are rites of passage. To fall in battle is tragic; to fall in _flight_ is unforgivable.

**Aerie Pride.** When flying, you have advantage on saving throws against being charmed or frightened. Additionally, when you are reduced to half your hit points or fewer, you can use your reaction to fly up to half your flying speed without provoking opportunity attacks. You can use this reaction once per long rest.

---

### **Vinyot**

The winding ports of Vinyot have opened new skies for aarakocra flocks. Here, they trade their lofty solitude for urban spectacle. Aarakocra performers and racers dominate the sky arenas, while wind-channeling delvers are sometimes hired by guilds to drop into Labyrinths from great heights.

**Spiral Descent.** While falling, you can reduce the fall speed of yourself or a willing ally within 5 feet by 60 feet per round, negating fall damage. You can also make Acrobatics checks with advantage while airborne.

---

### **Arneria**

The winds of Arneria are strange—intertwined with prophecy, fate, and war. Aarakocra from this region are considered omens. Some train as storm heralds or diviners, reading wind patterns for signs of the Labyrinths’ intentions. Their songs are mournful, full of thunder.

**Wind-Seer.** You know the _gust_ cantrip. At 3rd level, you can cast _feather fall_ once per long rest. At 5th level, you can cast _augury_ as a ritual, but only using flight patterns, cloud formations, or wind currents as your divine tools.

---

### **Al'Ar**

Tropical storm aarakocra ride the monsoons between the Al'ari isles. Their wings are broader, their feathers rain-dark, and their culture values laughter in the face of danger. Aarakocra in Al’Ar often live side by side with feline sky-priests, racing the winds to deliver prayers to the gods.

**Storm Laugh.** When you use your Skyward Pulse trait, you can also emit a sharp, defiant screech. Each creature of your choice within 10 feet must succeed on a Constitution saving throw (DC 8 + your proficiency bonus + your Charisma modifier) or be deafened until the start of your next turn. You can use this effect once per long rest.
