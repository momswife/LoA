## **I. Political Identity of Oria**

Authority in Oria is neither inherited automatically nor seized by force alone. It is earned, challenged, reaffirmed, and—once settled—respected without hesitation.

The backbone of Oric politics is the **House system**: immense lodge-cities, each a self-contained polity, competing endlessly for honor, influence, and leadership—yet bound by shared survival.

In Oria, disagreement is constant.  
Disobedience is rare.

---

## **II. The House System**

Each **House of Oria** is a political, cultural, and military entity.

A House governs:

- Its lodge-city and surrounding lands
    
- Its people’s labor, defense, and welfare
    
- Its traditions, rites, and internal law
    

Houses are led by **berendey** (chieftains), supported by councils of elders, champions, and skilled specialists.

No House is subordinate by default.  
Hierarchy emerges only through recognition.

---

## **III. Competition as Governance**

Rivalry is not a flaw—it is a mechanism.

Houses compete through:

- Martial prowess
    
- Engineering and craftsmanship
    
- Scholarship and innovation
    
- Endurance in hardship
    

Prestige determines influence. Influence determines leadership.

The question of “who leads Oria” is never permanently answered—only **held**.

> _“The strongest House today may not be the strongest tomorrow.”_

---

## **IV. The Housemeet & Shared Authority**

Inter-House matters are settled at the **Housemeet**.

- Attendance is mandatory
    
- Decisions require consensus or overwhelming recognition
    
- Violence within the Housemeet is taboo
    

Once leadership or policy is agreed upon, dissent ends. The losing Houses do not sabotage—they prepare to compete again later.

This is Oria’s greatest strength.

---

## **V. Respect for Authority**

While Oria contests authority relentlessly, it honors it absolutely once established.

- Orders are followed without complaint
    
- Defense obligations are met fully
    
- Tribute is paid as promised
    

To defy a recognized leader is to defy the entire cultural fabric of Oria.

Authority is sacred because it is **earned**.

---

## **VI. Military Unity**

In war, Oria moves as one.

- Houses contribute forces proportionally
    
- Command is unified under the recognized war-leader
    
- Rivalries pause without resentment
    

Even Houses locked in bitter competition will die for one another when the snow falls and the enemy advances.

---

## **VII. Faith, Tradition & Legitimacy**

Faith in Oria reinforces governance rather than replacing it.

- Rituals sanctify leadership transitions
    
- Ancestor reverence emphasizes continuity
    
- Spiritual legitimacy follows communal recognition
    

Under the Quiet Edict, gods do not rule—but tradition still judges.

---

## **VIII. Delvers, the Labyrinths, and Prestige**

Delving has introduced a new arena for competition.

- Houses sponsor delvers
    
- Labyrinth success elevates House standing
    
- Failures are endured, not hidden
    

Delvers are admired as risk-bearers but never elevated above House loyalty.

---

## **IX. External Relations**

Oria’s unity surprises outsiders.

### **Allemance**

Respected, but seen as politically soft.

### **Vinyot**

Viewed as efficient yet impersonal.

### **Arneria**

Admired for discipline, distrusted for rigidity.

### **Al’Ar**

Seen as culturally rich but dangerously loose.

---

## **X. Political Character of Oria**

Oria thrives on pressure.

Conflict sharpens competence.  
Consensus prevents collapse.

The Houses argue endlessly—until survival demands silence.

> _“We fight among ourselves.”_  
> _“We stand together against the cold.”_
