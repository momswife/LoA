## **I. Political Identity of Al’Ar**

Al’Ar is not a unified state, despite frequent assumptions made by outsiders.

It possesses:

- No singular crown
    
- No standing continental army
    
- No codified national law
    

Instead, Al’Ar exists as a **maritime cultural sphere**—a constellation of island cities, dock-states, and seasonal fleets bound together by shared tradition, ocean travel, and negotiated obligation.

If Al’Ar were ever to become a nation, **Tempestat** would serve as its capital by default rather than decree. This reality is widely acknowledged and equally resisted.

Al’Ar’s politics are fluid, decentralized, and deeply personal.

---

## **II. City-States & Dock Polities**

Each major Al’Ari city functions as a **sovereign dock-state**, governing its own:

- Harbor law
    
- Trade tariffs
    
- Civic codes
    
- Religious observances
    

Cities such as **Tempestat**, **Muraya**, **Mijnbou**, and **Trebes** cooperate out of necessity, not hierarchy.

Agreements are made ship-to-ship, season-to-season.

> _“You don’t rule the tide. You agree when to sail.”_

---

## **III. Seasonal Authority & the Storm Voyage**

Political power in Al’Ar is **seasonal**.

During the dry season, many Al’Ari cities disperse:

- Fleets split
    
- Citizens travel or live abroad
    
- Authority decentralizes further
    

During the Storm Voyages, authority reconsolidates—only briefly.

This rhythm prevents long-term accumulation of centralized power and makes permanent tyranny nearly impossible.

---

## **IV. Religious Influence & Civic Faith**

Religion in Al’Ar shapes politics indirectly.

Faiths are:

- Publicly acknowledged
    
- Culturally respected
    
- Politically restrained
    

No deity claims temporal authority under the Quiet Edict, but religious symbolism still guides civic behavior—especially in cities like **Springbok’s Flight**, where pilgrimage routes influence policy decisions.

Religious disputes rarely escalate to conflict; they are resolved through **refusal to sail together**, a potent sanction in Al’Ar culture.

---

## **V. The Role of Tempestat**

Tempestat is Al’Ar’s **largest city and most influential port**, but it holds no legal supremacy.

Its influence comes from:

- Dock capacity
    
- Inland travel alternatives
    
- Cultural export (music, festivals, craft)
    

Tempestat’s leadership walks a careful line—acting as coordinator without appearing to command.

Too much authority would provoke withdrawal by smaller cities.

---

## **VI. Delvers, the Labyrinths, and Political Friction**

Al’Ar cities vary widely in their stance toward the Labyrinths.

- Some see delvers as economic accelerants
    
- Others regard them as destabilizing outsiders
    
- Most allow access conditionally
    

There is **no unified Al’Ari delver policy**. Each dock sets its own rules, leading to political friction when delvers move freely between jurisdictions.

The MDO maintains a strong presence in Al’Ar ports to prevent disputes from escalating.

---

## **VII. External Relations**

### **Vinyot**

Trade partner and cultural rival. Al’Ar values Vinyot’s stability but resists its corporate consolidation.

### **Allemance**

Respected for structure and land-based logistics. Al’Ar sailors often find Allemagnian law stifling.

### **Arneria**

Viewed with curiosity and caution. Causeway politics are alien to Al’Ari sensibilities.

### **Oria**

Mutual respect built on endurance and craft, though cultural exchange is limited.

---

## **VIII. Conflict Resolution & Maritime Law**

Al’Ar resolves political conflict through:

- Dock bans
    
- Refusal of harbor access
    
- Seasonal non-cooperation
    

Violence between cities is exceedingly rare. A city that draws blood at sea risks becoming isolated—a fate worse than defeat.

---

## **IX. The MDO & External Oversight**

The **Ministry of Delving Operations** serves as:

- Neutral mediator
    
- Record-keeper
    
- Crisis stabilizer
    

While Al’Ar bristles at outside authority, the MDO is tolerated because it **respects local autonomy** and understands maritime necessity.

---

## **X. Political Character of Al’Ar**

Al’Ar’s politics are defined by:

- Movement over territory
    
- Agreement over command
    
- Memory over law
    

It is a region that cannot be conquered without being emptied—and cannot be unified without losing what makes it Al’Ar.

> _“We meet where the water allows.”_
