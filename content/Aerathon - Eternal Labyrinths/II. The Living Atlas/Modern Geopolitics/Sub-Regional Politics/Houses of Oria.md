## **I. The Oric House System**

In Oria, **a House is not a family**.

A **House** is:

- A lodge-city
    
- A governing institution
    
- A military and economic unit
    
- A cultural obligation passed across generations
    

Families may **hold stewardship** of a House, but the House itself is eternal. Stewardship is conditional, revocable, and measured by performance.

Oric politics are defined by **constant competition**—but once authority is recognized, it is obeyed without hesitation. Rivalry ends where survival begins.

> _“We argue so that we know who to follow.”_

## **II. House Edelstann**

### _The Diamond Gale Lodge_

**Seat:** Edelstann  
**Steward Family:** **Vanlig**  
**Renown:** Naval defense, maritime trade, Mantle War legacy

House Edelstann governs Oria’s most strategically significant southern port. The Vanlig family holds stewardship by virtue of unifying the region during the Mantle War and defending the coast against Allemagnian invasion.

Authority is exercised through:

- The **High Lodge-Bearer** of House Edelstann
    
- A council of batko representatives
    
- Direct control of port and naval operations
    

Loyalty to the Vanlig line is strong—but never assumed permanent.

---

## **III. House Frostguarde**

### _The Northern Watch_

**Seat:** Frostguarde City  
**Steward Family:** **Stonewarden**  
**Renown:** Extreme-environment defense, existential threat containment

House Frostguarde exists by mandate, not ambition. It is the only Oric House operating under **formal dual authority** with the **Ministry of Delving Operations**.

- The **Stonewarden family** appoints a **Warden-Regent** for fixed ten-year terms
    
- The MDO holds embedded authority over labyrinth surveillance and evacuation doctrine
    
- All growth, prestige, and dynastic accumulation are forbidden
    

Frostguarde does not compete for leadership.  
It holds the line.

---

## **IV. House Heltenlave**

### _The Aurora Lodge_

**Seat:** Heltenlave  
**Steward Family:** **Skjoldhrafn**  
**Renown:** Martial excellence, champions, ritualized leadership challenge

House Heltenlave embodies Oria’s belief that **authority must be visible and tested**.

- Governed by a **Lodge-Bearer**
    
- Advised and challenged by a **Speaker Circle**
    
- Subject to **Formal Challenges of Competence** by any sworn member
    

Leadership is public, precarious, and respected only while deserved.

---

## **V. House Jegervalt**

### _The Gatehouse of Oria_

**Seat:** Jegervalt  
**Steward Family:** **Roetvald**  
**Renown:** Tunnel wardenship, logistics, continental transit

House Jegervalt controls the single most critical passage through the Grensa mountains. Governance here is **operational**, not political.

- Led by the **High Gatewarden**
    
- Managed internally by **Layer Councils**
    
- Emergency authority centralizes instantly during tunnel threats
    

Failure is unthinkable. The Roetvald family is entrusted because it has never failed.

---

## **VI. House Podgorod**

### _The Deep Forge_

**Seat:** Podgorod  
**Steward Family:** **Bronzemolev**  
**Renown:** Engineering, steamcraft, subterranean systems

House Podgorod is governed by **technical merit**.

- Led by the **Prime Mechanarch**
    
- Districts governed by **Layer-Engineers**
    
- Authority transfers upon system failure—not vote or challenge
    

The Bronzemolev family produces stewards, not heirs.

---

## **VII. House Priozhevsky**

### _The Learned Lodge_

**Seat:** Priozhevsky  
**Steward Family:** **Medvednaya**  
**Renown:** Scholarship, medicine, historical preservation

House Priozhevsky governs through stewardship and contribution.

- Led by the **High Curator**
    
- Guided by **Scholastic Councils**
    
- External partnerships strictly regulated
    

The current High Curator, **Oleg the Bold Doctor**, exemplifies Oria’s belief that service—not dominance—earns authority.

---

## **VIII. House Severport**

### _The Ice Fleet_

**Seat:** Severport  
**Steward Family:** **Severinhald**  
**Renown:** Naval supremacy, winter patrols, northern defense

House Severport mirrors naval command structure.

- Ruled by the **High Admiral-Batko**
    
- Fleet captains assume civic authority during winter
    
- Civil councils defer immediately in emergencies
    

In Severport, authority is absolute when survival demands it.

---

## **IX. Inter-House Competition & Unity**

The Houses of Oria are in **perpetual contest**:

- Prestige
    
- Capability
    
- Readiness to lead
    

Yet when a House is recognized as leader—during war, crisis, or shared undertaking—**unity is immediate and complete**.

Sabotage is unthinkable. Defiance is dishonor.

---

## **X. Political Character of the Houses**

Oria survives because its Houses are never comfortable.

No House rules forever.  
No steward is beyond challenge.  
No authority exists without proof.

> _“The House stands.”_  
> _“The steward must earn tomorrow.”_
