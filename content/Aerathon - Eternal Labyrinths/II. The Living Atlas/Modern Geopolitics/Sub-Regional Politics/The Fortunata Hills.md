## **I. Political Identity of the Fortunata Hills**

The **Fortunata Hills** are not governed as a province in the conventional sense.

They are instead a **cultural stronghold zone**—a region where political authority is derived from **tradition, labor, and continuity**, rather than chartered nobility or centralized rule. Power here is heavy, slow, and resistant to reform.

The hills answer to **Malarthain Stronghold** not by law, but by **gravity**.

---

## **II. Malarthain as Regional Anchor**

Malarthain Stronghold functions as the **de facto capital** of the Fortunata Hills, though no such title is formally claimed.

Its influence is exerted through:

- Control of high-grade metallurgy and forge expertise
    
- Stewardship of orthodox Beastfolk faith
    
- Cultural legitimacy rooted in lineage and ritual
    

No other settlement in the hills rivals Malarthain’s authority, and none seriously challenge it. Even independent villages align their customs, trade practices, and rites to Malarthain precedent

.

---

## **III. Dual Authority as a Regional Model**

The **dual-authority system** practiced within Malarthain—Inner Stronghold tradition balanced against Outer City industry—has become the **implicit political template** for the Fortunata Hills.

Across the region:

- Elders and faith-keepers govern internal matters
    
- Guilds and trade councils manage outward commerce
    
- Neither sphere is permitted absolute dominance
    

This separation is viewed not as inefficiency, but as **necessary restraint**.

---

## **IV. Relationship to Vinyot**

Though geographically within **northwestern Vinyot**, the Fortunata Hills remain politically distinct.

- Vinyotian corporate governance has little traction here
    
- External investment is allowed only through Outer City guild channels
    
- Cultural interference in faith or lineage is rejected outright
    

Vinyot benefits from Fortunata steel—but does not command it.

---

## **V. Faith as Political Boundary**

The Fortunata Hills are the **last unified bastion of orthodox Beastfolk worship**, centered on Malar as resilience, hunt, and survival rather than savagery.

This faith functions as a **political boundary**:

- It defines who may lead
    
- It limits acceptable reform
    
- It frames the Labyrinths as trials, not opportunities
    

Settlements that abandon these rites often find themselves culturally isolated—even if they prosper materially.

---

## **VI. Military & Defensive Posture**

There is no standing regional army.

Defense is handled through:

- Forge militias sworn to Malarthain
    
- Local watches trained in siege doctrine
    
- Shared emergency protocols between settlements
    

Any large-scale threat to the Fortunata Hills would inevitably consolidate authority under Malarthain’s banners, whether invited or not.

---

## **VII. Delvers & the Labyrinths**

The region maintains a **restrictive stance** toward delvers.

- Access to the Under-Crucible is tightly regulated
    
- Delving is treated as a **rite of testing**, not a profession
    
- Outsiders are rarely trusted with regional secrets
    

The Fortunata Hills accept the Labyrinths as part of existence—but refuse to let them redefine identity.

---

## **VIII. Internal Tensions**

While outwardly unified, the region faces quiet strain:

- Younger artisans push for modernization
    
- Outer City guilds chafe at Inner Stronghold restraint
    
- Trade pressure from Vinyot increases yearly
    

Thus far, tradition holds—but it must continue to prove itself.

---

## **IX. The MDO’s Role**

The **Ministry of Delving Operations** maintains a limited but respected presence.

- Oversees labyrinth interfaces
    
- Certifies relic metallurgy
    
- Avoids interference in faith or governance
    

The MDO is tolerated because it recognizes what not to touch.

---

## **X. Political Character of the Fortunata Hills**

The Fortunata Hills do not seek relevance.  
They **outlast** it.

Their power is not loud, fast, or adaptable—but it is trusted, proven, and difficult to uproot.

> _“Stone breaks slowly.”_  
> _“So do we.”_
