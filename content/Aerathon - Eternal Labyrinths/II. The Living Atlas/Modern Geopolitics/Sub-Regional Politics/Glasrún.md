## **I. Political Identity of Glasrún**

Glasrún is not ruled.

It is **allowed**.

The vast forest of northeastern Allemance exists under a centuries-old accord known colloquially as the **Glasrún Pact**—an agreement in which noble authority was formally withdrawn in exchange for peace, tax exemption, and silence.

Since that moment, Glasrún has remained **legally Allemagnian**, **culturally independent**, and **politically uninterested**.

Power here does not flow downward.  
It circulates—slowly, deliberately, and without ceremony.

---

## **II. The Glasrún Pact**

The Pact is the cornerstone of Glasrún governance.

Under its terms:

- No noble may directly govern the forest
    
- No standing baronial guard may be stationed within
    
- Glasrún owes no regular tax or tithe
    
- In return, Glasrún does not petition the crown
    

The agreement persists not because it is loved—but because **breaking it would cost more than honoring it**.

---

## **III. Decentralized Authority**

There is no capital of Glasrún.

Cities like **Daltiarna**, **Domeall**, **Barraille**, and countless smaller settlements govern themselves through:

- District councils
    
- Trade speakers
    
- Rotating civic representatives
    

Authority is local, conditional, and often temporary. Leadership is accepted only as long as it serves the community.

> _“If someone speaks too long, we stop listening.”_

---

## **IV. Relationship with Allemance**

Officially, Glasrún belongs to Allemance.

Practically, the relationship is distant.

- The crown recognizes Glasrún’s autonomy
    
- Old noble houses still **claim** the land symbolically
    
- Enforcement of royal law is minimal to nonexistent
    

Allemance benefits from Glasrún’s resources—ore, timber, naval access—without bearing the cost of governance.

This suits both sides.

---

## **V. Border Tension with Oria**

Glasrún sits opposite **Oria**, divided by the Mantle Mountains.

Despite the distance, cultural respect runs deep:

- Oric traders are welcomed
    
- Neutrality during historic conflicts is remembered
    
- Mutual recognition of endurance and restraint
    

Glasrún’s refusal to take sides has earned it trust—if not affection—on both borders.

---

## **VI. Economy as Political Shield**

Glasrún’s economy is its greatest defense.

- Mining routes into the Mantle
    
- Naval trade through **Domeall**
    
- Timber and crafted goods
    
- Skilled labor unbound by guild monopolies
    

Any attempt to subjugate Glasrún would disrupt supply chains across Allemance and beyond.

Independence is protected by inconvenience.

---

## **VII. Law, Justice & Social Order**

There is no unified legal code.

Justice is handled by:

- Community arbitration
    
- Trade consequences
    
- Social exclusion
    

Violence between settlements is rare. The forest remembers offenders longer than any court.

---

## **VIII. Delvers & the Labyrinths**

Glasrún is cautious but pragmatic.

- Delvers are tolerated, sometimes welcomed
    
- Labyrinth access is negotiated locally
    
- No external authority controls delving rights
    

Settlements like **Gocaire** actively court delvers, while others bar them outright. This inconsistency is intentional.

---

## **IX. The MDO’s Role**

The **Ministry of Delving Operations** is one of the few institutions allowed broad access.

- Acts as mediator between settlements
    
- Provides neutral record-keeping
    
- Avoids imposing centralized rules
    

The MDO understands that Glasrún cannot be managed—only documented.

---

## **X. Political Character of Glasrún**

Glasrún survives by refusing definition.

It is not rebellious enough to conquer.  
Not submissive enough to rule.  
Not unified enough to coerce.

> _“The forest does not answer summons.”_
