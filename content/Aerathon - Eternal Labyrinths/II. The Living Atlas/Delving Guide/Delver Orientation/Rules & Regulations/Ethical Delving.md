# **Ethical Delving**

**Filed Division:** _Ministry of Delving Operations — Living Atlas Division (Charter Counsel Office)_  
**Primary Compiler:** Magistrate Talar Shellmark (Counselor General & Keeper of Canon Articles)  
**Advisory Editors:** Archivist Yelna Stonevoice (Historian of Echo-Law), Lt. Salvia Korrin (Arcane Breach Liaison)  
**Date of Filing:** **11th Cycle, 3100 A.D.**  
**Document Class:** _Institutional Code of Conduct / Grade-I Ethical Mandate_

### _Standards of Conduct for Licensed Delvers in the Labyrinth Network_

The Ministry of Delving Operations recognizes that every descent is not only an act of courage, but an act of **impact**—upon the Labyrinth, upon the public sphere, and upon the fragile balance of realms touched by the stone.  
Accordingly, all licensed delvers are bound by the principles of **Ethical Delving**, the codified standards that ensure delving remains a regulated, humane, and societally constructive profession.

These mandates apply to all delvers—novice to S-Rank—and infractions carry penalties ranging from UDMI demerits to full license revocation.

Ethical Delving is not a suggestion.  
It is law.

---

# **I. Foundational Principles**

## **1. Preservation of Life**

Delvers must prioritize the safety of:

- Fellow delvers
    
- Bystanders
    
- Civilian populations near breach-zones
    
- Recognized non-hostile labyrinth lifeforms
    

Lethal force is justified only when necessary to neutralize direct threat.

---

## **2. Integrity of Purpose**

Delvers may not:

- Sabotage active parties
    
- Interfere with sanctioned missions
    
- Manipulate relic outcomes
    
- Engage in fraud to boost UDMI or Fame scores
    

Delving is a public trust; betrayal corrodes its foundation.

---

## **3. Respect for the Labyrinth**

The Labyrinth is not a resource to be plundered without restraint.  
Ethical conduct includes:

- Avoiding wanton destruction of stable pathways
    
- Not defacing sacred shrines or divine constructs
    
- Reporting anomalous findings to the MDO
    
- Keeping relic extractions within legal quota
    

---

## **4. Honesty in Representation**

Delvers must present themselves truthfully in:

- Sponsorship agreements
    
- Guild contracts
    
- Broadcast appearances
    
- Ascendancy Trials
    
- Statements to Oculi or Core Auditors
    

Falsified claims or staged narrative arcs constitute Ministerial fraud.

---

# **II. Prohibited Actions**

The following are considered **High Infractions** under MDO Charter Law:

### **1. Civilian Endangerment**

Direct or negligent harm caused to non-combatants during delving operations.

### **2. Black-Route Descent**

Entering an unsanctioned labyrinth without Ministry approval.

### **3. Oculi Interference**

Damaging, obscuring, or magically deceiving Divine Oculi.

### **4. Relic Smuggling & Tampering**

Removing artifacts outside of registered procedures, or altering relics to falsify value.

### **5. Sabotage of Parties or Guilds**

Including trap displacement, false signaling, and forced hazard escalation.

### **6. Psychological Exploitation**

Inflicting intentional emotional trauma for Fame Engine manipulation or spectacle effects.

---

# **III. Ethical Combat Mandates**

All delvers must adhere to the following during combat:

### **1. Proportionate Response**

Use force equivalent to the threat. Wanton cruelty results in immediate UDMI penalty.

### **2. Controlled Spell Usage**

Area-of-effect spells cannot be deployed:

- Near collapse-prone structures
    
- In shared delving corridors
    
- Within marked safe-zones
    
- Without warning to allied parties
    

### **3. Mercy Under Surrender**

Sentient foes who yield—whether monstrous, humanoid, or arcane constructs—must not be executed except in cases of:

- Possession
    
- Incurable corruption
    
- Breach-protocol override
    

---

# **IV. Ethics in Audience-Interactive Delves**

In the Spectacle Age, delvers must balance public influence with responsible conduct.

### **Delvers may NOT:**

- Harm themselves for dramatic effect
    
- Stage injuries or betrayals for audience sympathy
    
- Manipulate viewer votes via forbidden charm magic
    
- Engage in “trauma-loops” for boosted metrics
    

### **Delvers MUST:**

- Maintain clear ethical signaling
    
- Avoid inflaming mob behavior
    
- Report malicious audience interference
    

The Ministry maintains that a delver’s dignity is not a commodity.

---

# **V. Ethical Relic Use**

Relics are not to be used:

- To forcibly alter another delver’s will
    
- As bargaining tools for illegal guild leverage
    
- As personal enhancement outside sanctioned windows
    
- To provoke anomalous distortion for livecast spectacle
    

Relics belong first to the world—not to the individual who carries them.

---

# **VI. Penalties & Enforcement**

Violations of Ethical Delving standards may result in:

- **UDMI Penalty Adjustments** (mild to extreme)
    
- **Temporary License Suspension**
    
- **Forced Sponsorship Review**
    
- **Probationary Delves Under Supervision**
    
- **Mandatory Ethics Retraining**
    
- **Removal from Guildstream Privileges**
    
- **Full License Revocation**
    
- **Ministerial Detainment** (in extreme cases)
    

**N-Series delvers**—such as the members of **Unit N-7**—are held to heightened scrutiny due to their Core-designated significance.

---

# **VII. Why Ethical Delving Matters**

Delvers walk the line between order and catastrophe.  
Without ethical grounding, a delver becomes a hazard—  
to their team, to the realm, and to the fragile cosmology the Labyrinths disturb.

Ethics are not bureaucratic chains.  
They are the **shield that keeps the world from breaking**.
