### _Ministerial Protocols for Surviving & Stabilizing the Labyrinth Network_

Labyrinths are shifting, sentient, and often hostile environments that warp reality, distort emotion, and obey no natural logic.  
Because of their unpredictable structure and catastrophic potential, the Ministry of Delving Operations enforces a strict and evolving body of **Labyrinth Safety Protocols** to mitigate risk to delvers, civilians, infrastructure, and planar stability itself.

These protocols are binding on **all licensed delvers**, guilds, expedition leaders, Divine Oculi operators, and MDO personnel.

Failure to follow these standards is considered a breach of Ministerial law.

---

# **I. Pre-Descent Safety Requirements**

Before any sanctioned descent, delvers must complete:

## **1. Hazard Classification Briefing**

All labyrinths are categorized by Tier (0–6). Briefings include:

- Known anomalies
    
- Unstable corridors
    
- Curse density
    
- Environmental hazards (acid fog, void pockets, time-drift)
    
- Divine or infernal resonance markers
    

## **2. Equipment Verification**

Mandatory items include:

- Crystal flare (lost-team locator)
    
- Oculi-recognition sigil
    
- Emergency barrier charm
    
- Rationed spellstones
    
- Basic antitoxin or anti-miasma kit
    

Failure to carry minimum gear results in denied entry.

## **3. Team Safety Roles**

Each party must designate:

- **Hazard Scout** (frontline trap observer)
    
- **Stability Anchor** (resists distortion magic)
    
- **Warden** (responsible for civilian extraction)
    
- **Recorder** (maintains event logs for Arcanet verification)
    

Solo delvers must sign a **High-Risk Waiver**, countersigned by an MDO official.

---

# **II. Core Safety Principles**

## **1. The Rule of Controlled Advancement**

Parties may not sprint, teleport blindly, or charge into unscouted chambers.  
Labyrinths change shape—doors become walls, floors become voids, and corridors loop into themselves.

Steady engagement is not cowardice; it is survival.

## **2. The Rule of Resonance Stability**

Sudden emotional outbursts, elemental surges, or divine channeling can provoke:

- Spatial collapse
    
- Etheric lashback
    
- Monster spawning events
    
- Sympathy reactions from relics
    

Delvers must maintain emotional and magical moderation.

## **3. The Rule of Non-Disruption**

Do not disturb:

- Ancient shrines
    
- Sealing glyphs
    
- Bound spirits
    
- Old-world inscriptions
    
- Biological nodes
    

Many labyrinth deaths result from needless tampering.

## **4. The Rule of Retreat**

If structural instability exceeds 47% (as detected by Oculi vibration), all delvers must evacuate.  
Retreat is not dishonor—it is Ministerially mandated.

---

# **III. Hazard Categories & Safety Responses**

## **1. Mechanical Hazards**

Includes: crushing halls, rotating blades, collapsing platforms.  
**Protocol:**

- Slow movement
    
- Shared verbal signals
    
- Scout checks every 15 ft
    
- Disable, do not destroy (destruction triggers secondary mechanisms)
    

## **2. Environmental Hazards**

Includes: arcane fire zones, miasma pools, void winds, time distortion bubbles.  
**Protocol:**

- Barrier charms active
    
- Avoid prolonged exposure
    
- Immediate evacuation upon vertigo, memory drift, or bleeding shadows
    

## **3. Bestial Hazards**

Includes: labyrinth-native beasts, corrupted fauna, echo-monsters.  
**Protocol:**

- Maintain formation
    
- Do not overextend
    
- Identify signs of sentience (sentient beings fall under Ethical Delving statutes)
    

## **4. Cursed Zones**

Includes: whisper chambers, guilt-hollows, memory-eaters, bleeding altars.  
**Protocol:**

- No lone travel
    
- No naming aloud of loved ones
    
- Emergency blindfold protocol for visual-cognitive hazards
    
- Report any personality drift immediately
    

---

# **IV. Oculi-Based Safety Observations**

Divine Oculi are equipped to detect:

- Structural weakness
    
- Arcane resonance spikes
    
- Emotional instability
    
- Predatory intent from monsters or delvers
    

If an Oculi issues a **Silver Warning**, delvers must halt.  
A **Violet Warning** means immediate extraction.

Interference with Oculi signals is illegal and can result in arrest.

---

# **V. Civilian & Guild Safety Impact**

Delver safety is inseparable from public safety.  
Protocols are designed to:

- Prevent labyrinth collapses into city foundations
    
- Avoid release of cursed or planar-threatening entities
    
- Reduce ecological contamination from relic discharges
    
- Maintain psychological well-being of viewers (in livecasted delves)
    

Delvers are reminded:  
**“Your safety is the realm’s safety.”**

---

# **VI. Emergency Response Procedures**

## **1. Collapse Protocol (CP-7)**

Triggered when two or more structural supports fail in under 30 seconds.

- Deploy barrier charms
    
- Follow the lead of the Stability Anchor
    
- Retreat along mapped route
    
- Signal Oculi for spatial correction
    

## **2. Breach Contamination Protocol (BC-5)**

For planar tears, monster overflow, or void rupture:

- Do not investigate alone
    
- Activate flare
    
- Erect containment wards if trained
    
- Await MDO Breach Division arrival
    

## **3. Missing Delver Protocol**

Triggered after 90 seconds of no Arcanet heartbeat signal.

- Stop party advance
    
- Oculi triangulation
    
- Resume movement only after Auditor approval
    

---

# **VII. Safety Compliance & Penalties**

Failure to follow Labyrinth Safety Protocols results in:

- UDMI reduction
    
- Temporary suspension of license
    
- Team penalties for negligence
    
- Mandatory retraining under MDO supervision
    
- Fines up to 4,000 gold
    
- In severe cases: **revocation of license**
    

**N-Series delvers**, including N-7, are held to highest safety scrutiny due to their predicted convergence roles.

---

# **VIII. Why Labyrinth Safety Matters**

Labyrinths are not dungeons to be conquered—they are living, shifting systems that respond to delver behavior.  
Safety is the discipline that maintains:

- Structural order
    
- Magical balance
    
- Ethical integrity
    
- Public trust
    
- Life itself
    

To enter the Labyrinth without respect is to enter its jaws willingly.
