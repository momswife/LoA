# **The Canon Charter of the Ministry of Delving Operations**

### _Ten Articles Upholding the Order of Descent_

**Filed Division:** Annals & Antiquities — Charter Counsel Office

---

## **Overview**

When the Age of Collapse ended and the world’s ruins began to breathe again, explorers and scavengers flooded the labyrinths without rule or restraint.  
Accidents, thefts, and miracles gone feral threatened to unravel what little civilization remained.

To restore order, the surviving guilds and scholar-priests convened in Louvain’s half-burned cathedral to codify the first unified law of exploration: the **Canon Charter of the Ministry of Delving Operations**.

The Charter’s ten Articles form the bedrock of all modern law in Aerathon.  
They are brief by design—each a binding statement of survival, morality, and record.  
Every license, seal, duel, and discovery issued since the Ministry’s founding cites at least one of these Articles.

---

## **The Ten Articles of the Canon Charter**

**§1-A Chronometric Integrity**  
Time belongs to the Ministry. Any falsified or unsanctioned timeline is Historical Fraud (Class-1).

**§2-B Reliquary Safety**  
No relic may be awakened, attuned, or transported without a licensed Handler and containment ledger.

**§3-C Labyrinth Entry & Claim**  
Provisional claim belongs to the first licensed entrant; ownership matures only upon safe return and proof of record.

**§4-D Hospitality & Bell-Peace**  
Guest-Right and Bell-Peace suspend violence within lantern wards and sanctuaries; violations convert to duel-arbitration at dawn.

**§5-E Oath & Record**  
Any vow spoken before stone, flame, or mirror gains legal force once read into the Archive; breaking it demands restitution in kind.

**§6-F Provenance & Authenticity**  
All official seals must cite invoked Articles; forged sigils and counterfeit manifests constitute Identity Fraud (Class-2).

**§7-G Duel Arbitration (Coin-Law)**  
Sanctioned duels may resolve insult or debt; lethal terms require a Magistrate’s mark and an authenticated Sandglass Coin.

**§8-H Faithfield Conduct**  
Attempts to provoke divine response without Priest-Analyst oversight are prohibited; sanctioned miracles remain property of the state.

**§9-I Breach Discipline (Emotive Hazard)**  
Uncontrolled emotion within unstable labyrinths endangers all; repeated offenses result in license suspension and ejection.

**§10-J Custody of Memory (Cognitive Autonomy)**  
Citizens may request masking of private records; unauthorized deletion or trade of live memories is Historical Contamination (Class-1).

---

## **Cultural Role**

The Charter functions as both scripture and statute.  
To Brethren jurists it represents the triumph of order over collapse; to Beastfolk historians, the first peace achieved by consent rather than conquest.  
Its language is taught verbatim in every guildhall; each article is carved upon the pillars of the Ministry’s Hall of Charters, polished daily by apprentices.

Though centuries have passed, the Ministry maintains that **these ten clauses are immutable**—the bones of civilization itself.  
All later circulars, regional addenda, and field directives merely interpret, never replace, the Canon Charter.
