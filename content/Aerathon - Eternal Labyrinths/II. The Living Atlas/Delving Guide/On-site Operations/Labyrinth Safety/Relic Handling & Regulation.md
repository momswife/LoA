**Filed Division:** _Ministry of Delving Operations — Annals & Antiquities (Relic Adjudication Office)_  
**Primary Compiler:** Curator Rook-of-Rings (Relics Registrar & Tharos Rings Specialist)  
**Advisory Editors:** Inspector Jao “Greyglass” Vell (Forensic Thaumaturgy), Professor Arav Dhal-Senn (Antiquities Restoration Technician)  
**Date of Filing:** **14th Cycle, 3104 A.D.**  
**Document Class:** _Operational Archive / Grade-I Artifact Governance_

### _Ministerial Protocols for Identification, Containment, Transportation & Ethical Use of Labyrinth-Born Artifacts_

Relics—objects forged within, mutated by, or awakened through the Labyrinths—are among the most powerful and dangerous items in Aerathon.  
They can enhance reality, break it, rewrite it, or—if improperly handled—consume those who wield them.

The Ministry of Delving Operations upholds strict procedures to ensure:

- **Safe retrieval**
    
- **Clear identification**
    
- **Ethical utilization**
    
- **Preventing public catastrophe**
    
- **Criminal deterrence**
    
- **Controlled market circulation**
    

Relic Handling is not a courtesy.  
It is a **public safety mandate**.

---

# **I. Relic Categories**

Relics are classified based on **stability**, **origin**, and **hazard potential**.

---

## **Category A — Stable Relics**

- Minor enchantments
    
- Utility charms
    
- Non-volatile materials
    
- Safe for public distribution after registration
    

Typical examples:  
glowstones, whisper-notes, portable runebooks.

---

## **Category B — Semi-Stable Relics**

- Mildly volatile
    
- React to emotional states or magical auras
    
- Require Delver license to handle
    

Examples:  
sentient lanterns, echo daggers, gravity coins.

---

## **Category C — Volatile Relics**

- High magical output
    
- Curse adjacency
    
- Unstable resonance or planar bleed
    
- Restricted to licensed guilds and MDO contractors
    

Examples:  
curse-break rods, distortion flasks, blood-tempered relics.

---

## **Category D — Ultra-Hazard Relics**

- Exhibits active consciousness
    
- Alters physical laws
    
- Responds to bloodline or fate-thread
    
- Requires Ministerial escort
    

Examples:  
artifact hearts, shifting armor, cognitohazards.

---

## **Category X — Forbidden Relics**

- Capable of existential harm
    
- Linked to Convergence events
    
- Bound to great Labyrinth entities
    
- Interaction requires Divine oversight
    

Examples:  
Void-calling stones, paradox seals, Tharos-lineage relics.

---

# **II. Retrieval Procedures**

---

## **1. Initial Assessment**

Delvers must determine:

- Stability
    
- Activity state
    
- Emotional resonance
    
- Danger class
    
- Entity signature
    

The nearest Divine Oculi logs this moment automatically.

---

## **2. Containment Protocol**

Depending on category:

### **A-Relics:** simple ward wraps

### **B-Relics:** runic dampener boxes

### **C-Relics:** stabilized containment coffin

### **D-Relics:** multi-layer sigil crate with curse-weave isolator

### **X-Relics:** quarantined in null-magic vault & sealed by ministerial order

---

## **3. Party Safety Requirements**

Delvers must:

- Wear anti-resonance gloves
    
- Maintain mental warding
    
- Avoid blood contact
    
- Refrain from emotional volatility
    
- Keep relics away from children, animals, and raw spellcasters
    

---

## **4. Transportation to a Registered Archive**

All relics must be delivered to:

- Nearest Relic Adjudication Office
    
- Authenticated Guild Archive
    
- Ministerial containment facility
    

Failure to report a relic is punishable under **Charter §8-F (Artifact Concealment)**.

---

# **III. Identification & Analysis**

Upon arrival, relics undergo:

- **Resonance scan**
    
- **Curse detection**
    
- **Origin trace**
    
- **Memory echo review**
    
- **Sentience audit**
    
- **Oculi cross-referencing**
    
- **UDMI relevance tagging**
    

A relic receives its **Official Ministerial Sigil (OMS)** before it enters circulation.

---

# **IV. Legal Regulations & Ownership Rights**

---

## **1. Delver Claim Rights**

Delvers may claim relics **only** if:

- It is Category A or B
    
- The delve contract permits ownership
    
- No criminal ties exist
    
- The relic is not bound to Convergence anomalies
    

---

## **2. Guild Ownership**

Guilds may hold:

- Category B–D relics
    
- Only under annual compliance audits
    
- With registered armory facilities
    

---

## **3. Forbidden Items**

Category X relics:

- Cannot be owned
    
- Cannot be sold
    
- Cannot be wielded without MDO supervision
    
- Are archived permanently
    

---

## **4. Relic Commerce Laws**

Unauthorized relic trade constitutes:

- Smuggling
    
- Spectacle Fraud
    
- Black-Market Arcana Dealing
    

Penalty: **up to 80,000 gold**, guild suspension, or imprisonment.

---

# **V. Ethical Use Requirements**

Delvers must:

- **Avoid deliberate overuse**
    
- **Refrain from cruelty amplified through relics**
    
- **Not employ relics for political coercion**
    
- **Not leverage relic consciousness without consent**
    
- **Report signs of corruption immediately**
    

Use of relics for:

- mass harm
    
- emotional manipulation
    
- UDMI exploitation  
    is a Class-1 ethical violation.
    

---

# **VI. Relic Hazards**

### **1. Cognitohazard Drift**

Relic begins influencing thoughts.

### **2. Curse Bloom Leakage**

Aura expands beyond container.

### **3. Blood Resonance Activation**

Relic awakens near lineage matches.

### **4. Parasocial Bonding**

Sapient relic believes itself bonded to delver.

### **5. Reality Distortion Faults**

Gravity shifts, memory anomalies, temporal flickers.

All must be reported within **24 hours**.

---

# **VII. N-Class Exception Protocols**

Due to their Convergence significance, N-Class teams (e.g., **Unit N-7**) follow modified relic protocols.

### **1. Expanded Handling Rights**

N-Class teams may handle:

- Category C relics without escort
    
- Category D relics with minimal oversight
    
- Category X relics only with Oculi supervision
    

### **2. Automatic Core Observation**

Any relic handled by an N-Class team is:

- Logged by the Arcanet Core
    
- Analyzed for fate-thread significance
    

### **3. Enhanced Containment Measures**

The Ministry deploys:

- Three-tier resonance seals
    
- Dedicated hazard vanguards
    
- Live Divine Oculi monitoring
    

### **4. Convergence Risk Mitigation**

If relic + N-Class presence creates excessive resonance:

- GSN triggers blackout
    
- ABA restricts feeds
    
- Core issues stabilization pulses
    

### **5. Prime Archival Status**

All relic interactions involving N-Class teams are stored permanently under **Tier-Prime Access Only**.

---

# **VIII. Why Relic Handling Matters**

Without Relic Handling:

- Relics corrupt bearers
    
- Guilds start wars
    
- Monsters evolve uncontrollably
    
- UDMI destabilizes
    
- Divine Oculi lose calibration
    
- The Labyrinth expands influence
    
- Convergence accelerates
    

Relics are not trophies.  
They are **agreements**—between the Labyrinth and the living.

The Ministry ensures those agreements are honored safely.
