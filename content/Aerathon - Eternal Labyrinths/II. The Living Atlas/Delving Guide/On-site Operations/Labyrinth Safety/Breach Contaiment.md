**Filed Division:** _Ministry of Delving Operations — Arcane Breach Division (Hazard Response Command)_  
**Primary Compiler:** Lt. Salvia Korrin (Arcane Breach Liaison & Field Commander)  
**Advisory Editors:** Inspector Jao “Greyglass” Vell (Forensic Thaumaturgy), Warden Cyra Hesh (Environmental Hazard Auditor)

**Document Class:** _Operational Directive / Grade-I Emergency Protocol_

### _Ministerial Protocols for Stabilizing, Sealing, and Neutralizing Labyrinthic Breach Events_

A **Breach** occurs when a Labyrinth extends its influence beyond its sanctioned boundaries—through portals, rupture seams, curse leaks, monster overflow, or planar contamination.  
These events are among the most dangerous phenomena in Aerathon, capable of collapsing cities, poisoning ecosystems, and destabilizing the Arcanet itself.

The **Arcane Breach Division (ABD)** of the Ministry of Delving Operations is tasked with immediate intervention, containment, field triage, and stabilization of all breach types.

Breach Containment Protocols are absolute.  
Failure to follow them can escalate a breach from a localized threat into a **Convergence-tier catastrophe**.

---

# **I. Classification of Breach Events**

Breaches are sorted into five recognized categories:

---

## **1. Type I — Spatial Rupture**

Symptoms:

- Tears in physical reality
    
- Warped corridors spilling into public space
    
- Architectural inversion
    

Threat Level: **Moderate–High**  
Requires stabilizing anchors and reality-reset glyphs.

---

## **2. Type II — Monster Overflow**

Symptoms:

- Labyrinth-native fauna or hostile entities breaching into populated zones
    
- Spawn cycles outside expected patterns
    

Threat Level: **Low–Extreme**  
Often triggered by disturbance of monster lords.

---

## **3. Type III — Curse Bloom**

Symptoms:

- Emotional inversion
    
- Psychic bleed
    
- Memory fog
    
- Echo-entity manifestations
    

Threat Level: **High**  
Containment requires anti-curse obelisks and mental ward deployment.

---

## **4. Type IV — Planar Contamination**

Symptoms:

- Elemental storms
    
- Gravity distortions
    
- Time-shear zones
    
- Divine/infernal residue
    

Threat Level: **Severe**  
Extremely dangerous; controlled by specialized task forces.

---

## **5. Type V — Convergence Resonance Breach**

Symptoms:

- Presence of fate-thread anomalies
    
- Core-directed resonance spikes
    
- Reality predictive flaring
    

Threat Level: **Existential**  
Only recorded in proximity to N-Class teams or convergence-linked relics.

---

# **II. Core Principles of Breach Containment**

## **1. Establish the Perimeter**

First responders must:

- Erect five-layer ward boundaries
    
- Evacuate civilians
    
- Mark hazard radius using beacon stakes
    
- Summon Divine Oculi for live mapping
    

## **2. Assess Breach Type Immediately**

Use:

- Scrytech resonance scans
    
- Ambient curse-density metrics
    
- Monster frequency analysis
    
- Divine Oculi hazard pulses
    

## **3. Prevent Escalation**

Delvers must not:

- Attempt solo sealing
    
- Disturb active relic nodes
    
- Engage altered terrain without anchors
    
- Cast destabilizing magic (wild surges, fire storms, temporal spells)
    

## **4. Seal, Not Destroy**

Destruction of breach edges may:

- Expand rupture seams
    
- Multiply curse fields
    
- Trigger echo duplication  
    Ministerial doctrine prioritizes **containment over eradication**.
    

---

# **III. Standard Operating Procedure (SOP)**

---

## **Step 1 — Breach Lockdown (Code: BL-1)**

- Deploy containment barriers
    
- Activate Oculi hazard mapping
    
- Initiate public hazard alerts via GSN
    

---

## **Step 2 — Stabilization (Code: ST-3)**

Scrywrights and ABD agents anchor the rupture via:

- Stabilizer spikes
    
- Rune-circuits
    
- Ether dampeners
    
- Planar tether lines
    

---

## **Step 3 — Threat Neutralization (Code: TN-7)**

Field squads resolve:

- Loose monsters
    
- Cursed constructs
    
- Environmental threats
    

Delvers are authorized to use lethal force when necessary.

---

## **Step 4 — Seal the Breach (Code: SB-5)**

Seal via:

- Crystallized sigils
    
- Divine Oculi resonance
    
- Arcanet feedback loops
    
- Specialized relics if applicable
    

---

## **Step 5 — Post-Containment Audit (Code: PC-2)**

Mandatory procedures:

- Oculi resonance review
    
- Spell residue analysis
    
- Monster corpse cataloging
    
- Environmental cleansing
    
- Memory stone record filing
    

All breach data enters the Ministerial Archive.

---

# **IV. Forbidden Actions Inside Breach Zones**

- Do **not** attempt to use Blessing Tokens
    
- Do **not** teleport within rupture fields
    
- Do **not** touch active curse nodes without ward gloves
    
- Do **not** sever planar tethers prematurely
    
- Do **not** remove relics from breach sites
    
- Do **not** tamper with Divine Oculi readings
    

Violations are high infractions under Ministerial law.

---

# **V. Breach Containment Specialists**

Key roles include:

### **Arcane Breach Engineer**

Stabilizes spatial tears.

### **Warden-Arcanist**

Handles curse blooms and emotional contamination.

### **Hazard Vanguard**

Clears hostile fauna and holds the perimeter.

### **Scrywright Technician**

Deploys anti-distortion runes and supports Oculi mapping.

### **Oculi Liaison (Lt. Rank+)**

Interprets divine hazard pulses and anomaly readings.

---

# **VI. Role of the Divine Oculi in Breach Events**

Oculi provide:

- Real-time mapping
    
- Risk prediction
    
- Monster aggression indicators
    
- Emotional hazard detection
    
- UDMI hazard modifiers
    

**Crimson Pulses** indicate imminent collapse.  
**Violet Rings** signal Convergence-triggered breach escalation.

---

# **VII. Special Protocols for N-Class Teams**

N-Class teams (such as **Unit N-7**) are uniquely tied to Convergence phenomena and may be deployed in breach scenarios that exceed standard response capacity.

### **N-Class Privileges**

- **Automatic authorization** to enter Tier 4–5 breach events
    
- **Exemption** from standard team-size rules
    
- **Priority support** from Oculi and GSN
    
- **Direct routing** to Core-linked hazard data
    

### **N-Class Responsibilities**

- Must report resonance anomalies
    
- Must avoid deliberately provoking breach evolution
    
- Must refrain from relic removal unless commanded by the Core or the Ministry
    

### **N-Class Risks**

Their presence may:

- Attract further anomalies
    
- Intensify reality overlap
    
- Trigger fate-resonance cascades
    

Thus, deployment is considered high-stakes and strategic.

---

# **VIII. Why Breach Containment Matters**

If breaches go uncontained:

- Cities collapse
    
- Ecosystems warp
    
- Monsters spill into the overworld
    
- Curses infect entire regions
    
- Planar boundaries weaken
    
- Convergence accelerates uncontrolled
    

Breach Containment is not simply an emergency practice—  
it is a **battle against the Labyrinth itself**.

The Ministry does not fear the Labyrinth.  
But it respects what happens when the Labyrinth stops playing by its own rules.
