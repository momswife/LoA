# **The Founding & Maintenance of Guild Charters**

### _A Procedural Guide for Aspiring Orders of Exploration_

**Filed Division:** _Ministry of Delving Operations — Living Atlas Division (Guild Relations Bureau)_  
**Primary Compiler:** **Chief Registrar Pyra Koll** (Guild Certification & Charter Custodianship)  
**Advisory Editors:** **Magistrate Talar Shellmark** (Charter Counsel & Canon Oversight), **Lt. Salvia Korrin** (Arc. Scriv., Breach Verification), **Archivist Yelna Stonevoice** (Echo-Law & Oral Recordkeeping)  
**Date of Filing:** **12th Cycle, 2999 A.D.**  
**Document Class:** _Institutional Directive / Grade-II Authenticity_

---

## **I. Overview**

The establishment of a new delving guild is an act of both ambition and law.  
Every labyrinth in Aerathon is Ministry-regulated territory; to claim, explore, or profit from one requires Guild Charter approval under the **Canon Charter §3-C (Labyrinth Entry & Claim)** and **§6-F (Provenance & Authenticity)**.

A Guild Charter is not merely a license—it is a _contract with civilization._  
Those who fail to honor it are erased from the record as if they never descended at all.

---

## **II. The Seven Steps of Founding**

### **1. Petition for Recognition**

A written request must be submitted to the **Guild Relations Bureau** including:

- A proposed **Guild Name**, emblem, and motto.
    
- A founding roster of **no fewer than three delvers**, one of whom holds an active Ministry license.
    
- A **statement of creed or operational intent** (250 words minimum).
    
- A **sponsorship letter** from either an active guild, a registered noble house, or an MDO officer of Archivist rank or higher.  
    Petitions without sponsorship are discarded unread.
    

---

### **2. Charter Interview & Background Audit**

Applicants undergo interrogation and record review to ensure no outstanding violations under Charter §1-A, §6-F, or §9-I.  
This process includes:

- Magical truth appraisal (Echo-Law harmonic testing).
    
- Background audit of all listed delvers.
    
- Personality resonance assessment (Faithfield density & breach discipline).  
    Failure of honesty or harmony nullifies petition for three years.
    

---

### **3. Submission of the Founding Fund**

Each applicant must deposit a **Founding Fund** proportional to projected Guild Tier:

|**Tier Sought**|**Initial Fee (gold standard)**|**Required Staff**|**Typical Approval Period**|
|---|---|---|---|
|**C-Class**|10,000|3+|2 months|
|**B-Class**|35,000|6+|4 months|
|**A-Class**|100,000|12+|6–8 months|
|**S-Class (Private)**|500,000+|25+|Ministerial review; years|
Funds are held in trust by the Ministry for one cycle. Should the guild dissolve before full approval, half is forfeited to the Crown Archive.

---

### **4. Oath of Charterhood**

Upon provisional approval, founders must gather within a licensed sanctuary and swear the **Oath of Charterhood** before a registered flame and Ministry witness:

> _“We descend together, we return together, and we are recorded together.  
> The labyrinth remembers us not as heroes, but as one name.”_

Breaking the Oath results in dissolution and the loss of all relic rights.

---

### **5. Approval & Insignia Registration**

Once the Charter Counsel signs off, the new Guild receives:

- A **Guild Seal** (silver tier for private, bronze for MDO-Sponsored).
    
- A **License Ledger** for expedition reports.
    
- Access to **Public Registry Nodes** for name and sigil protection.  
    Guild insignia are magically trademarked; duplication is punishable under §6-F as forgery.
    

---

### **6. First Expedition & Proof of Purpose**

Within six months, the new Guild must complete a sanctioned delve and return at least one:

- Verified relic,
    
- Labyrinthic map segment, or
    
- Ministry-approved field report.
    

Failure to produce proof of activity within this period results in **Charter Suspension**.  
A second failure constitutes **Guild Nullification**, with all relics and funds seized for redistribution.

---

### **7. Public Induction & Registry Placement**

Successful Guilds are announced at the **Annual Convocation of Banners** in Louvain.  
A ceremonial bell is rung once for each founding member, and the Guild’s emblem is projected into the Hall of Charters, where it remains until the Guild dissolves, merges, or falls to ruin.

---

## **III. Maintaining a Functional Guild**

The Ministry defines relevance not by size, but by **sustainability of purpose.**  
Guilds that lose discipline, direction, or public trust fade swiftly.

To remain active, a Guild must:

1. Submit expedition reports quarterly.
    
2. Pay annual renewal dues (scaled by tier).
    
3. Maintain casualty rates below 60% per annum.
    
4. Host at least one public education or civic service event.
    
5. Avoid three consecutive relic misreports (§2-B, §6-F).
    

Neglect of any two clauses triggers an **Automatic Review** by the Guild Relations Bureau.  
Neglect of three or more invokes **Provisional Seizure**—the Ministry’s polite term for nationalization.

---

## **IV. Criteria for Success**

The most stable guilds share three qualities:

- **Transparency:** Even private guilds that publish findings build public trust.
    
- **Mythmaking:** A guild without story is soon forgotten. The most famous understand spectacle as survival.
    
- **Purpose:** The labyrinth rewards obsession—but civilization demands meaning.
    

Ministry analysts summarize:

> _“Those who delve for glory burn out; those who delve for reason endure.”_

---

## **V. Causes of Dissolution**

A Guild may be dissolved by:

1. **Voluntary Merger** (with another Guild).
    
2. **Prolonged Inactivity** (two years without sanctioned delve).
    
3. **Catastrophic Casualty** (loss of 80% membership in one expedition).
    
4. **Charter Revocation** (Ministry order due to law breach).
    
5. **Faithfield Collapse** (guild-wide psychological or magical instability).
    

Once dissolved, all claims revert to Ministry custodianship under **Article §3-C.5 (Abandonment Rule).**

---

## **VI. Notes on Sponsorship & Legacy**

Most successful guilds begin under the mentorship of an existing organization.  
The **Free Company of Louvain** remains the largest sponsor of new delvers, offering apprenticeships and co-signed petitions.  
Private guilds, such as the **Argent Banner**, are notoriously selective—preferring to adopt prodigies rather than build novices.  
The **Crimson Reavers**, meanwhile, view sponsorship as weakness; their initiates simply survive an expedition or die in its attempt.

---

## **VII. Final Admonition**

The Ministry reminds all aspirants:

> _“A Guild is not a banner. It is a promise to be accountable to more than oneself.”_

Every labyrinth that opens demands a corresponding guild to face it.  
But those who pursue power without record soon learn the oldest truth of Aerathon:  
**unregistered deeds are forgotten faster than they are forgiven.**
