## **I. Overview**

From the ashes of the Collapse came the Guilds—born not from faith, but from necessity.  
The **Free Guild Accords (2980 A.D.)** formalized delving as sanctioned enterprise, transforming chaos into vocation.  
Today, guilds define Aerathon’s economy and identity: explorers, merchants, scholars, and performers unified under a single obsession—the labyrinths.

Every delver, whether saint or scoundrel, operates beneath a guild crest.  
Some answer to the Ministry’s call for order; others answer only to ambition.

---

## **II. Branches of Guild Authority**

### **MDO-Sponsored Guilds (Concordant Orders)**

The Ministry sanctions only one **S-Class Concordant Guild**—the **Free Company of Louvain**.  
All others serve under its mentorship as **Apprentice or Affiliate Houses**.  
MDO-sponsored guilds receive state funding, access to restricted labyrinths, and priority relic contracts in exchange for strict transparency.

They must:

- Submit expedition ledgers within 72 hours (§3-C).
    
- Train at least one cohort of cadets per cycle.
    
- Maintain public access to delver education.
    
- Uphold all ten Articles of the Canon Charter without amendment.
    

Their purpose is civic: to keep exploration open to every race and creed, and to ensure delving remains a service, not a spectacle.

---

### **Private Charter Guilds**

Private guilds operate autonomously under the Charter but beyond Ministry sponsorship.  
They finance their own expeditions, choose their members, and answer only to the labyrinths and their ledgers.  
While bound by law, they are **not bound by ethics**—a freedom both envied and feared.

Private guilds may:

- Deny membership without cause.
    
- Conceal or delay expedition reports.
    
- Trade relics through private markets (subject to §6-F audit).
    
- Broadcast or embargo delving footage at discretion.
    

In the Age of Spectacle, these guilds have become Aerathon’s aristocracy—ruthless, efficient, and adored.

## **IV. The S-Class Guilds of Record**

### **The Free Company of Louvain** _(MDO-Sponsored / Concordant Order)_

The Free Company stands as the Ministry’s hand, heart, and hallway light.  
Founded during the first decade of the Age of Delving, it trains every novice delver in Charter Law and survival craft.  
Its headquarters, the **Hall of Orientation**, doubles as academy and pilgrimage site—walls carved with every oath ever sworn into the Archive.  

_“To descend for all, not for oneself.”_ — Guild Motto

---

### **The Argent Banner** _(Private Charter Guild)_

The Argent Banner prizes **purity of record over allegiance**.  
Its delvers wear mirrored cloaks and vow to recover truth—be it relic or crime—no matter who owns it.  
They act as impartial mercenaries of discovery, selling verified truths to the highest bidder or the most righteous cause.  
They are lawful, precise, and politically uncontrollable.  

> _“We serve the fact, not the faith.”_ — Captain Rellis Orin, Argent Banner Commander

---

### **The Crimson Reavers** _(Private Charter Guild)_

Where the Banner records, the Reavers reap.  
A militarized consortium of ex-delvers, mercenaries, and Beastfolk veterans, they specialize in **high-risk extraction and violent containment.**  
Officially censured thrice, unofficially employed twice as often, the Reavers thrive where diplomacy fails.  
Their ranks operate by coin oath: a share of the take or a blood tithe.  

> _“Mercy ends at the threshold.”_ — Guild Warden Vask Marrow-Hide

---

## **V. Guilds and the Ministry**

The Ministry governs, but the Guilds **animate**.  
Each licensed guild answers to the **Guild Relations Bureau** under Chief Registrar Pyra Koll, which:

- Issues and renews licenses;
    
- Arbitrates conflicts and sponsorship petitions;
    
- Enforces §6-F (Provenance & Authenticity) on relic trade;
    
- Publishes the **Annual Performance Index** ranking all guilds by yield, casualty, and public favor.
    

Only the **Free Company** holds the title _Ministerial Ally_, granted perpetual sanction to act in Ministry stead during crises.

---

## **VI. Guild Culture in the Age of Spectacle**

In modern Aerathon, delving is **public theatre**.  
Scry-feeds broadcast live expeditions; guilds trade fame as currency.  
The Free Company’s humanitarian delves are civic holidays.  
The Argent Banner’s legal exposés topple corrupt nobles.  
The Crimson Reavers’ blood-matches sell out amphitheatres.  
Each defines virtue differently—service, truth, or power—but all sustain the labyrinthic economy that keeps Aerathon alive.

## **VIII. Outlook**

The Ministry anticipates growing friction between the Free Company’s egalitarian mission and the private guilds’ monopoly on relic markets.  
Should open conflict arise, Article §4-D (Bell-Peace) may extend to inter-guild diplomacy for the first time since the Accords.  
Until then, Aerathon endures its paradox: a world united by the very competition that could undo it.
