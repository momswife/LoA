## **I. Overview**

The **Crimson Reavers** are Aerathon’s most infamous private guild — a violent, profit-driven order whose creed rejects hierarchy, law, and mercy.  
Headquartered in **The Reaver’s Hold** in the Titan Highlands (Free Frontier Region), they are less a guild than a _cult of survivalists_.

Born from the ashes of deserter companies and banned delving houses, the Reavers revere **violence as sanctity**, **freedom as faith**, and **bloodshed as spectacle**.  
Where other guilds swear oaths, the Reavers make wagers.  
Where others seek legacy, they seek _the next fight_.

The Ministry recognizes their existence under duress; their profits and broadcast appeal outweigh the chaos they cause.

## **III. Creed & Ethos**

To the Crimson Reavers, **freedom is not granted — it is taken, and kept through violence**.  
They believe the Labyrinth rewards only those who surrender to its rhythm of brutality and chance.

### **Reaver Maxims**

- **Violence as Creed** — Combat is worship. Each kill a hymn.
    
- **Glory through Chaos** — Predictability is cowardice.
    
- **Freedom in Blood** — Coin proves value, not law.
    
- **Death as Merit** — To die spectacularly is to live forever.
    

The Reavers mock the Ministry’s bureaucracy, calling it “paper armor.” Yet they gladly sell relics to the very inspectors sent to arrest them.

---

## **IV. The Chain of Strength**

The Reavers reject formal ranks; leadership is decided through ritual combat known as the **Chain of Strength**.  
Each victory grants authority until defeated. No duels are refused.

|**Title**|**Name**|**Notable Deed**|
|---|---|---|
|**Warlord of the Reavers**|**Orik “The Bloodmarked”**|Claimed leadership after the Trial of Blades; wears the skull of his predecessor as a belt trophy.|
|**Champion of the Maw**|**Vasha the Stormwolf**|Returned alone from the Howling Maw, Shard of Ulkhar embedded in her chest.|
|**The Red Fox** (Strategist)|**Riven Dain**|Escaped three extermination attempts; outdueled Argent Banner elites on live scry.|

Any Reaver may challenge a superior; duels are broadcast live via the Grand Scrying Network (GSN), often doubling as public entertainment.

## **V. Recruitment & Membership**

The Crimson Reavers recruit only those who have _already lost something irretrievable._  
Membership begins with a ritual called **The Offering**, where candidates cast a personal belonging—often symbolic of restraint—into a burning pit before entering their first labyrinth unarmed.

**Typical Recruits:**

- Exiled soldiers and condemned delvers
    
- Pit fighters and blood-arena champions
    
- Outlaws fleeing Ministry prosecution
    
- Beastfolk berserkers and thrill-addicts
    
- Fallen nobles seeking violent absolution
    

No contracts, no dues, no allegiance—only the next battle and its winnings.

---

## **VI. Operations & Practices**

### **Delving Protocol**

The Reavers ignore nearly all Ministry expedition procedures.  
They claim rights to any labyrinth they enter, recording only what benefits them.  
While technically unlawful, their success rate (and GSN ratings) ensures minimal enforcement.

Their delves are _chaotic, unsanctioned carnivals of combat_:

- Multiple warbands enter the same labyrinth simultaneously, competing to retrieve relics.
    
- Corpses of failed Reavers are left as “warnings to cowards.”
    
- Survivors drink from the **Bowl of Return**, a relic basin that stains their lips crimson for one week — symbol of renewed membership.
    

### **Relic Trade**

Reavers sell relics through black-market brokers or directly to collectors.  
The Ministry estimates annual income exceeding 12 million G, though none of it is taxed.  
Transactions often include blood-oath clauses enforceable only through duel.

---

## **VII. Cultural Role & Media Presence**

The Reavers are both feared and worshipped.  
Their exploits dominate public scry-feeds, where the **Grand Scrying Network** glamorizes their carnage with illusion filters, converting massacres into performance art.  
Many cities ban live broadcasts, yet taverns play them anyway.

Fan cults known as **Red Circles** reenact famous battles, sometimes lethally.  
Merchants sell “authentic” Reaver dust, weapons, and fake scars.  
The Ministry calls it _moral corrosion._  
The people call it _the best show in Aerathon._

---

## **VIII. Relations with Other Powers**

### **Ministry of Delving Operations**

Official stance: _“Rogue, but statistically valuable.”_  
The MDO avoids direct confrontation due to the Reavers’ popularity and combat capability.  
Lt. Korrin once remarked:

> _“They do our dirtiest work before we decide if it was necessary.”_

### **The Free Company of Louvain**

Ideological opposites.  
Free Company delvers see the Reavers as the embodiment of chaos—the proof that delving without doctrine corrupts the soul.  
The Reavers see them as “domesticated dogs.”

### **The Argent Banner**

Professional rivals turned informal partners.  
The Banner purchases raw combat footage from Reaver delves to splice into its dramatized broadcasts, lending “authentic grit” to their edited performances.  
In return, the Reavers gain sponsorships and fame.  
Both deny the arrangement.

## **IX. Symbolism & Uniform**

- **Emblem:** Typically **two bloodied axes paired with a daunting blackened steel helmet** 
    
- **Colors:** Deep crimson and blackened steel.
    
- **Dress:** Minimal armor, trophies of slain foes, crimson warpaint in geometric streaks (the “Marks of Memory”).
    
- **Speech Rite:** Reavers greet each other with the phrase _“Still breathing?”_ — to which the answer is always _“For now.”_
    

---

## **X. The Reaver’s Hold**

Located deep within the **Titan Highlands**, the Reaver’s Hold is a fortress-colosseum carved from the bones of a fallen titan.  
Its walls echo with constant combat, its arenas open to any who dare challenge for place or pride.  
The Hold functions as:

- Headquarters and barracks
    
- Auction site for relic trades
    
- Public fighting pit (the **Drowned Colosseum**)
    
- Shrine to lost members — a literal river of blood sealed in glass beneath the floor
    

---

## **XI. Political & Economic Influence**

Despite—or because of—their anarchy, the Reavers hold staggering economic power.  
They control unregistered labyrinths across three regions, selling access rights to lesser guilds at predatory rates.  
Ministry economists estimate that 1 in 8 relics on the market passed through Reaver hands at least once.

Rumors persist that the Reavers fund small-scale rebellions in Arneria and Vinyot using labyrinth profits, though no proof has ever survived long enough to be verified.
