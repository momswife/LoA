## **I. Overview**

The **Dawnwright Circe** is the Ministry’s most enduring **training guild**, an officially sanctioned C-Class organization operating across Aerathon’s major cities as the **first step in every delver’s career**.  
It exists not to amass wealth or renown, but to prepare souls — teaching basic survival, ethics, and teamwork before recruits earn their official guild sponsorship.

Every major guild in modern Aerathon — from the **Free Company** to the **Argent Banner** — counts Dawnwright graduates among its ranks.

To the Ministry, the Circe is a civic necessity.  
To delvers, it’s the closest thing they’ll ever have to a childhood.

## **III. Founding & Purpose**

The Dawnwright Circe was established by the early **Free Company of Louvain** as a solution to a tragic problem: new delvers were dying before their first sunrise underground.  
Marshal Ysella Brant and Instructor Maraen Vale co-authored the **First Light Curriculum**, emphasizing mental preparation over martial prowess.

Its guiding philosophy is simple:

> _“Better one living novice than ten dead prodigies.”_

Since its inception, the Circe has reduced labyrinth entry fatalities among new delvers by nearly 60%.

---

## **IV. Curriculum & Instruction**

The Circe teaches a holistic method known as the **Three Lights Doctrine**, representing the essentials of a delver’s foundation:

1. **The Light of Knowledge** — Practical delving theory, labyrinth geography, and relic safety (taught by Ministry archivists).
    
2. **The Light of Skill** — Physical training, survival, combat discipline, and spellcraft basics.
    
3. **The Light of Spirit** — Emotional regulation, Faithfield stability, and ethical conduct within the labyrinths.
    

Every student must successfully complete a **Proving Run**—a mock delve using controlled illusions crafted by Emberlight engineers.

Graduation culminates with the **Torchwalk Ceremony**, where initiates carry a single enchanted flame from the Hall’s brazier to the outer streets—symbolizing their passage from student to delver.

## **VI. Relationship with the Ministry**

The Dawnwright Circe operates under **direct Ministry oversight**, functioning as a hybrid of academy and welfare institution.  
Its funding comes primarily from the **Ministry Education Reserve**, supplemented by guild tithes from those employing its graduates.

It is also the **primary pipeline for relic safety certification**, required under Charter §2-B for all delvers entering sanctioned labyrinths.

Lt. Korrin describes it as:

> _“The first flame every torch owes its spark to.”_

## **VIII. Facilities**

- **The Hearthhall:** Central academy and dormitory; includes classrooms, training pits, and the Proving Halls.
    
- **The Mirror Garden:** A reflective courtyard designed for meditation and emotional regulation exercises.
    
- **The Lantern Library:** A living archive of student journals — thousands of entries preserved in enchanted flame, recited annually at the Torchwalk Festival.
    

Each facility is built for comfort, not spectacle; walls lined with old torches of every color from graduates across generations.

---

## **IX. Symbolism & Rituals**

- **Emblem:** A radiant sunburst encircling a small torch — “The first light of many.”
    
- **Colors:** Pale gold and dusk orange.
    
- **Oath of the Circe:**
    
    > _“We walk toward the dark with a light that is shared,  
    > not to conquer, but to understand.”_
    
- **Festival:** _The Dawnmarch_, held yearly when the city’s torches are ceremonially extinguished and relit by new graduates.

## **XI. Outlook**

The Circe remains stable and vital in the Age of Spectacle, though its humble image is increasingly overshadowed by the flashier Argent Banner and Emberlight Union.  
Still, as Marshal Brant once said:

> _“When all the lights burn out, the dawnwrights will still be teaching someone how to hold a flame.”_
