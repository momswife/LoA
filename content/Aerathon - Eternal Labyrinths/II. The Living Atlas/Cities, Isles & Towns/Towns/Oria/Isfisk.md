### _River Settlement of the Viskaflod_

**Region:** **Oria**  
**Nearest Authority:** **House Heltenlave** and **House Edelstann** (equidistant, neither dominant)  
**Settlement Type:** River town  
**Population:** Unknown (estimated small)

---

## **I. Overview**

**Isfisk** is a small settlement situated along the **Viskaflod River**, roughly between **Heltenlave** and **Edelstann**. Beyond its location, little is recorded with certainty.

It is mentioned in transit logs, seasonal route notes, and river charts—never in political records. No House claims it outright, and none deny its existence.

Isfisk persists quietly, apparently content to remain unremarkable.

---

## **II. Geography & Layout**

Isfisk sits low along the riverbank.

- Built close to the waterline
    
- Likely subject to seasonal flooding and ice drift
    
- Positioned where river traffic naturally slows
    

No reliable map of the settlement’s internal layout exists. What structures are present are assumed to be temporary or easily rebuilt.

---

## **III. Economy & Work**

Isfisk appears to exist in service to the river.

Speculated activities include:

- River fishing and netting
    
- Barge tending and ferrying
    
- Icebreak assistance during freeze seasons
    

No exports are associated with Isfisk by name. Goods pass through it rather than originate there.

---

## **IV. Governance & Authority**

No formal governance structure is recorded.

Given Oric custom, Isfisk likely operates through:

- Senior riverfolk
    
- Seasonal consensus
    
- Practical authority rather than inherited rule
    

Its position between two powerful Houses suggests deliberate neutrality.

---

## **V. Labyrinth Pressure (Unspoken)**

No Labyrinth activity has been recorded in or near Isfisk.

Whether this reflects absence, avoidance, or lack of reporting is unclear. Oric records do not speculate.

---

## **VI. People & Tone**

Those who pass through describe Isfisk as quiet and watchful.

Common impressions include:

- Few questions asked
    
- Minimal hospitality, but no hostility
    
- A sense that people here listen more than they speak
    

Travelers rarely stay longer than necessary.

---

## **VII. Rumors & Narrative Use**

Isfisk is referenced only indirectly:

- As a safe place to tie off for the night
    
- As a town that never grows
    
- As a place no one governs because no one needs to
    

**Isfisk is best used as:**

- A neutral waypoint between Oric powers
    
- A quiet stop on a river journey
    
- A reminder that not every settlement seeks importance
