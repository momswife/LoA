### _Druidic Hold at the Fallensky Pass_

**Region:** **Oria**  
**Nearest Authority:** **House Edelstann** (ritual respect only)  
**Settlement Type:** Sacred outpost & pass-ward  
**Population:** ~600 (druids, wardens, seasonal allies)

---

## **I. Overview**

**Fallensky’s Rest** lies at the headwaters of the **Teplo River**, where its waters trickle down from the **Mantle**. Centuries ago, around the year 350, records describe a fragment of the sky falling into the mountains from the heavens. The impact reshaped the land in an instant, carving the **Fallensky Pass** and violently reversing the flow of the Teplo itself.

When the burning fragment struck, it tore open a cavern of impossible scale within the Mantle. Around this wound in the world, a settlement formed—not to exploit it, but to **guard it**.

Fallensky’s Rest exists to watch what should not be disturbed.

---

## **II. Geography & Layout**

The settlement is arranged around the pass and the cavern mouth.

- **The Pass Road:** a carefully maintained mountain route
    
- **Cavern Gate:** a wide-mouthed opening descending into darkness
    
- **Stone-and-wood lodges:** built low against wind and avalanche
    
- **Sky Shrines:** open platforms dedicated to clear air and flight
    

The surrounding peaks funnel wind and weather sharply. Clear days are treated as blessings.

---

## **III. Economy & Work**

Fallensky’s Rest does not trade in volume.

Primary work includes:

- Pass maintenance and escorting
    
- Weather observation and recording
    
- Ritual stewardship of the Mantle cavern
    

Material needs are modest and met through limited trade and seasonal aid from nearby regions. Wealth here is measured in **favor**, not coin.

---

## **IV. Governance & Authority**

The settlement is governed by an **Oric druid circle**, devoted to sky, wind, and avian life.

- Authority is ritual and custodial
    
- Decisions are made by consensus
    
- Outsiders are permitted only under supervision
    

No House rules Fallensky’s Rest, though nearby Houses respect its function and avoid interference.

---

## **V. Labyrinth Pressure (Contained)**

The cavern beneath Fallensky’s Rest is known to connect to deeper **Labyrinth strata** within the Mantle.

Access is sealed and monitored. Only wardens and selected delvers are permitted to descend, and only when necessary. Interloping Labyrinth creatures occasionally emerge, threatening the pass and settlement.

These incidents are handled quietly and decisively.

---

## **VI. People & Tone**

Those who live at Fallensky’s Rest are calm, observant, and deeply attentive to the sky.

Common traits include:

- Reverence for birds and wind
    
- Measured speech
    
- Long memory for disturbances
    

A large flock of **talking snowbirds** often accompanies sanctioned delvers traveling toward the **Vanlig region**. These birds act as watchers and messengers, reporting anything the druids require.

---

## **VII. Rumors & Narrative Use**

Whispers associated with Fallensky’s Rest include:

- A fragment of the sky buried deep below
    
- Druids granted subtle power by what lies beneath
    
- Sailors rewarded with favorable winds after aiding the wardens
    

**Fallensky’s Rest is best used as:**

- A sacred chokepoint and guardian settlement
    
- A gateway between surface travel and Mantle depths
    
- A place where nature bargains, rather than commands
