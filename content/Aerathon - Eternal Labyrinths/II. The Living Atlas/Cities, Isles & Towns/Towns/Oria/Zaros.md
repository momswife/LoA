### _Lodge-Town of the Zaros Mountains_

**Region:** **Oria**  
**Nearest Authority:** **Housemeet** (envoys only)  
**Settlement Type:** Isolated lodge-town  
**Population:** ~1,500

---

## **I. Overview**

**Zaros** is hidden deep within the mountain range that shares its name, accessible only by a single road no wider than a wagon. Even within Oria, the lodge is frequently forgotten—so much so that university students studying Oric geography often omit it entirely.

Isolation has shaped Zaros completely. Its envoys appear reliably only during the **Housemeet**, and otherwise the lodge remains content to exist beyond notice.

Paradoxically, Zaros delights in visitors.

Those who find their way in are welcomed enthusiastically, fed generously, and subjected to long, rambling monologues about the incomparable beauty of Zarosi sunsets, stone, and air. Visitors are strongly encouraged to return someday.

---

## **II. Geography & Layout**

Zaros is built into a sheltered mountain bowl.

- **The Single Road:** narrow, winding, and carefully maintained
    
- **Lodge Complex:** clustered stone halls and longhouses
    
- **Underground Fields:** terraced caverns used for agriculture
    
- **Upper Balconies:** open galleries overlooking the valley
    

The surrounding peaks block most weather and nearly all passing traffic. The sense of enclosure is total.

---

## **III. Economy & Work**

Zaros is largely self-sufficient.

Primary work includes:

- Subterranean farming
    
- Preservation and fermentation
    
- Hosting and provisioning travelers
    

The lodge’s most famous export—**Zarosi pickles**—is traded sparingly. Dill grown within Zaros’s underground fields is considered sacred and restricted.

Pickles made with outside dill are permitted for trade and are widely regarded as alarmingly potent.

---

## **IV. Governance & Authority**

Zaros is governed by a **Lodge Council**, composed of senior households and tradition-keepers.

- Custom holds the force of law
    
- Superstitions are codified formally
    
- Envoys are selected for patience and memory
    

No House claims Zaros directly, but its voice at the Housemeet is respected for its consistency and neutrality.

---

## **V. Labyrinth Pressure (Increasing)**

The Zaros Mountains have become a growing **Labyrinth hotspot**, though no single manifestation is publicly cataloged.

Delver crews increasingly pass through the lodge before and after expeditions. Entire caravans now make the journey, bringing coin, stories, and unfamiliar habits.

The community is divided:

- Elders fear dilution of tradition
    
- Most residents are delighted to share Zarosi customs
    

Discussion of Labyrinth specifics remains limited, in accordance with the Quiet Edict.

---

## **VI. People & Tone**

Zarosi are exuberant, superstitious, and intensely sincere.

Common traits include:

- Loud hospitality
    
- Rapidly shifting fashions
    
- Absolute confidence in their beliefs
    

Local fashion is a riot of clashing colors. Seasonal fads sweep the lodge entirely—current obsession centers on **tiny candles**, which burn in every window. Some Zarosi even affix them to the tips of their antlers.

Rules are taken very seriously:

- Visitors arriving in **odd numbers** may not stay on the first floor
    
- Non-Zarosi may not consume **local dill** under any circumstance
    

These rules are enforced politely, firmly, and without explanation.

---

## **VII. Rumors & Narrative Use**

Stories associated with Zaros include:

- Pickles strong enough to drop a grown elk
    
- Candles that burn without melting
    
- Visitors who stayed one night and returned every year after
    

**Zaros is best used as:**

- A welcoming but uncanny stopover
    
- A cultural outlier within Oria
    
- A pressure point where isolation meets outside curiosity
