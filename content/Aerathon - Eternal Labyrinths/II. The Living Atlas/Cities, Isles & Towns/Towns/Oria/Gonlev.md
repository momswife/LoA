### _Monastic Hold of the Southern Nattefrost Peaks_

**Region:** **Oria**  
**Nearest Authority:** **None**  
**Settlement Type:** Isolated monastery-settlement  
**Population:** ~300–400 (grandi feline monks and initiates)

---

## **I. Overview**

**Gonlev** is an isolated monastery concealed within a tight cluster of peaks just south of the **Nattefrost**. The settlement does not appear on most maps and offers no welcome to casual travelers.

It is home to a conclave of **grandi felines**, white-furred monks who maintain a martial tradition forged more than a thousand years ago. The discipline practiced here is severe, pragmatic, and intentionally stripped of ornament.

Gonlev exists to train, not to teach.

---

## **II. Geography & Layout**

The monastery is built directly into the rock.

- **Carved stone halls** embedded in cliff faces
    
- **Narrow ascent paths** known only to the monks
    
- **Training terraces** exposed to wind and snow
    
- **Silent courtyards** used for meditation and recovery
    

The surrounding peaks serve as both defense and filter. Few who attempt the climb are permitted to finish it.

---

## **III. Economy & Work**

Gonlev is largely self-sustaining.

Primary labor includes:

- Terrace farming and snow-fed cistern upkeep
    
- Weapon maintenance and training equipment crafting
    
- Instruction and physical conditioning
    

Trade is rare and tightly controlled. What little is needed arrives through prearranged contacts.

---

## **IV. Governance & Authority**

Gonlev is governed by a **Circle of Masters**, selected through merit and survival rather than age.

- Authority is collective and unquestioned
    
- Advancement is earned through endurance and discipline
    
- Failure is corrected immediately, without ceremony
    

No House claims Gonlev. The monastery claims itself.

---

## **V. Labyrinth Pressure (Unspoken)**

No Labyrinth presence is acknowledged near Gonlev.

The monks avoid deep tunneling and forbid excavation beyond established structures. Old traditions dictate where stone may be cut and where it must remain untouched.

These rules are followed without explanation.

---

## **VI. People & Tone**

Gonlev’s monks are severe, focused, and visibly intimidating.

Common traits include:

- Minimal speech
    
- Constant training
    
- Scowling demeanors even at rest
    

Outsiders often mistake hostility for indifference. The monks make no effort to correct this.

---

## **VII. Rumors & Narrative Use**

Stories circulating about Gonlev include:

- Initiates who leave stronger than steel or not at all
    
- Masters who fight without drawing blood
    
- A training style that does not permit mercy
    

**Gonlev is best used as:**

- A brutal training location
    
- A cultural extreme within Oria
    
- A test of discipline rather than ideology
