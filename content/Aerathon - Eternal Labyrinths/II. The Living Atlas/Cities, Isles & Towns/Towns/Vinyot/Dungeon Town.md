### _Expedition Settlement Above the Ever-Present Labyrinth_

**Region:** **Vinyot**  
**Nearest Authority:** **Dole** (contested jurisdiction)  
**Settlement Type:** Semi-permanent expedition town  
**Population:** Variable (1,500–4,000, fluctuating)

---

## **I. Overview**

**Dungeon Town** is a name of convenience, not a chartered settlement. It exists because **the Labyrinth beneath it never leaves**.

While Labyrinth manifestations elsewhere are transient and unpredictable, the site beneath Dungeon Town has remained **persistently present**, confounding centuries of study. As a result, an ad hoc settlement formed above it—initially tents and scaffolds, later reinforced into a dense sprawl of platforms, wagons, and walkways suspended over unstable ground.

Visitors are warned to watch their footing.  
A careless step can still mean a direct descent.

Dungeon Town attracts delvers, scholars, merchants, and spectators in equal measure. It is equal parts research site, marketplace, and proving ground—and it strains the Quiet Edict simply by continuing to exist.

---

## **II. Geography & Layout**

The town is built **around avoidance**, not permanence.

- **Scaffold Streets:** elevated walkways anchored where the ground is least likely to open
    
- **Entrance Zones:** dozens of marked, barricaded, or half-collapsed Labyrinth access points
    
- **Caravan Rings:** wagons arranged into temporary districts
    
- **Observation Platforms:** cranes, towers, and gantries overlooking active zones
    

Below it all, the Labyrinth twists unpredictably—architecture, environments, and threats overlapping without pattern or continuity.

---

## **III. Economy & Work**

Dungeon Town survives on **proximity to danger**.

Primary activity includes:

- Delver outfitting and resupply
    
- Salvage brokerage and artifact appraisal
    
- Guided descents and licensed observation
    
- Tourism framed as “controlled spectacle”
    

Coin flows quickly and disappears faster. Many arrive planning a short stay and remain far longer than intended.

---

## **IV. Governance & Authority**

Dungeon Town has **no single ruler**.

Authority is fractured between:

- **Expedition Coordinators** (temporary, rotating)
    
- **Merchant Collectives** enforcing trade norms
    
- **MDO Field Offices** maintaining partial oversight
    

Dole claims jurisdiction.  
The MDO enforces safety boundaries.  
Neither fully controls the site.

Order exists only so long as everyone agrees it is cheaper than chaos.

---

## **V. Labyrinth Pressure (Persistent)**

The Labyrinth beneath Dungeon Town is **always active**.

Observed characteristics include:

- Multiple simultaneous environments
    
- Rapid architectural transitions
    
- Threats crossing between zones
    

Navigation belowground is unreliable. A delver might pass from an animated hedge maze into a boiling oil flume without transition.

Public documentation is limited. Private study is constant.

The Quiet Edict is **selectively enforced** here—managed, not obeyed.

---

## **VI. People & Tone**

Dungeon Town is loud, restless, and tense.

Common features include:

- Crews constantly arriving and departing
    
- Scholars arguing over half-mapped corridors
    
- Performers and barkers selling controlled danger
    

The phrase _“see you in Dungeon Town”_ has become common among delvers who split up—often spoken half in jest, half in hope.

Not everyone treats the site lightly.

---

## **VII. Rumors & Narrative Use**

Stories circulating through Dungeon Town include:

- An entrance that always leads back to itself
    
- A section that reacts to applause
    
- Researchers who vanished while observing, not delving
    

**Dungeon Town is best used as:**

- A chaotic delver crossroads
    
- A pressure valve on the Quiet Edict
    
- A place where the Labyrinth is impossible to ignore
