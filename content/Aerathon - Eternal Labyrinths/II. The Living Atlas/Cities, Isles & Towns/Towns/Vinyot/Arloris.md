### _Fishing Town of Amicia Bay_

**Region:** **Vinyot**  
**Nearest Authority:** **Dole** (commercial oversight only)  
**Settlement Size:** Small coastal town with scattered hamlets

---

## **I. Overview**

**Arloris** is a ring of fishing villages and pearl farms wrapped around **Amicia Bay**, long sustained by quiet trade with the capital. Its people live modest, predictable lives—tide to dock, dock to ledger—comfortable so long as the bay remains generous.

Recently, it has not.

Unusual activity beneath the bay has drawn delvers and attention the town was never meant to handle. Nothing has been declared publicly. Nothing is officially wrong.  
But Arloris no longer sleeps as soundly as it once did.

---

## **II. Geography & Layout**

Arloris has no walls and no center.

- A **main dock** where fish and pearls are weighed and shipped
    
- **Pearl rings** in shallow water, marked by pale floats
    
- **Beach hamlets** owned by fishing companies and leased to workers
    
- A collapsed **old watch tower**, never repaired
    

Amicia Bay appears calm, warm, and shallow—except where it abruptly is not.

---

## **III. Economy & Work**

The town exists to supply others.

**Primary work includes:**

- Coastal fishing
    
- Pearl cultivation
    
- Shellcraft and bay-glass
    

Most pearl farms are **company-owned**, binding workers through debt and long contracts. Wages are stable but thin. Delvers pay better—and faster—than any buyer from Dole, which has quietly altered local priorities.

---

## **IV. Governance & Authority**

Arloris has no noble ruler.

Authority is divided between:

- **Dock foremen** (company-appointed)
    
- **Pearl stewards** (corporate interests)
    
- **The Tidekeeper**, a local mediator who handles disputes, deaths at sea, and Quiet Edict compliance
    

Real power sits in **Dole**, written into contracts no one here negotiated.

---

## **V. Labyrinth Pressure (Unspoken)**

No public Labyrinth designation exists for Amicia Bay.

Observed effects locals do not document:

- Shifting currents without weather
    
- Pearl beds failing overnight
    
- Diving bells returning dented inward
    

A private nickname for the bay circulates among fishers.  
It is never written down.

The MDO has not arrived openly. That worries people more than if they had.

---

## **VI. People & Tone**

Arloris is cautious, quiet, and watchful.

Locals:

- Count boats before sleeping
    
- Avoid night tides unless paid well
    
- Mark doors with shell-chalk on bad water nights
    

They are not superstitious—only experienced.

---

## **VII. Rumors & Narrative Use**

Whispers heard in Arloris include:

- “Pearls that crack wrong”
    
- “Knocking sounds below the farms”
    
- “A barge that never came back”
    

**Arloris is best used as:**

- A low-level southern delver starting town
    
- A Quiet Edict stress point
    
- A place where profit reached first, and safety did not
