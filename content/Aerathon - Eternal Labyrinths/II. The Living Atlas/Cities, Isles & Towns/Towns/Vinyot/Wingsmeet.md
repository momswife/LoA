### _Pass Village of the Bêtemère Foothills_

**Region:** **Vinyot**  
**Nearest Authority:** **Local Barony** (seasonal oversight)  
**Settlement Type:** Agricultural pass village  
**Population:** ~1,100

---

## **I. Overview**

**Wingsmeet** rests in a narrow mountain pass between two high cliffs of the **Bêtemère Mountains**, where upland winds funnel south each autumn. The village is defined not by trade routes or walls, but by a long-standing agreement with the land—and the sky above it.

For generations, Wingsmeet has maintained vast sunflower grasslands surrounding the village. These fields are not ornamental. They exist to receive the passage of migrating birds that pour through the pass by the thousands, fertilizing the soil as they move on toward warmer regions.

Twice a year, Wingsmeet stops working entirely.

---

## **II. Geography & Layout**

The village sits at the narrowest point of the pass.

- **Central Roadway:** a single broad path aligned with the migration corridor
    
- **Fence Fields:** miles of posts drilled with hollow spaces for roosting birds
    
- **Low Stone Homes:** built to withstand wind and avoid obstructing flight
    
- **Cliff Faces:** sheer walls that amplify sound and wind during migration
    

During peak season, the sky above Wingsmeet darkens with wings.

---

## **III. Economy & Work**

Wingsmeet survives through **avian-assisted agriculture**.

Primary work includes:

- Cultivation of sunflowers and hardy grasses
    
- Seed pressing and oil production
    
- Maintenance of roosting fences and fields
    

The fertilization provided by migrating flocks sustains crop yields across the surrounding barony. Exports are modest but reliable.

Labor is steady—except during migration weeks, when it ceases entirely.

---

## **IV. Governance & Authority**

Wingsmeet is governed by a **Village Steward**, appointed by the local barony but traditionally selected from long-standing Wingsmeet families.

- Day-to-day matters handled locally
    
- Agricultural obligations reported seasonally
    
- Migration observances protected by custom, not law
    

Outside authorities are expected to respect the village’s calendar. Those who do not are quietly refused cooperation afterward.

---

## **V. Labyrinth Pressure (Unspoken)**

No Labyrinth presence is publicly acknowledged near Wingsmeet.

However:

- The migration paths have shifted subtly over decades
    
- Birds avoid certain stretches of the pass without explanation
    
- Flocks arrive earlier some years, later in others
    

Villagers track these changes carefully. They do not speculate aloud.

---

## **VI. People & Tone**

Wingsmeet is calm, patient, and reverent toward cycles larger than itself.

Common traits include:

- Measuring time by seasons, not dates
    
- Speaking softly during migration weeks
    
- Treating interruption of the fields as a serious offense
    

Children are taught early not to frighten resting birds.

---

## **VII. Rumors & Narrative Use**

Whispers associated with Wingsmeet include:

- Fenceposts that remain warm after the birds depart
    
- Feathers that never rot in the fields
    
- A year when the flocks did not come
    

**Wingsmeet is best used as:**

- A peaceful interlude location
    
- A place where tradition outweighs urgency
    
- A subtle indicator of regional change
