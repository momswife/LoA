### _Hidden Village of the Bêtemère Plateau_

**Region:** **Vinyot**  
**Nearest Authority:** **None (geographically isolated)**  
**Settlement Type:** Remote mountain village  
**Population:** ~400–600 (snow cat beastfolk)

---

## **I. Overview**

**Verglass** is a secluded village hidden high within the **Bêtemère Mountains**, perched on a snowbound plateau encircled by sheer, impassable peaks. It is home to a tight-knit conclave of **snow cat beastfolk**, whose oral histories insist their people were uplifted separately from the felines of **Al’Ar**, following a different and older path.

Few outsiders ever reach Verglass. Fewer still are invited to remain.

The village does not participate meaningfully in Vinyotian trade, politics, or culture. It exists quietly, deliberately removed from the currents that shape the lowlands.

---

## **II. Geography & Layout**

Verglass sits on a broad, wind-scoured plateau under perpetual snow.

- **Stone-and-ice dwellings** dug partially into the plateau
    
- **Windbreak walls** carved from packed snow and granite
    
- **A central gathering hollow**, roofed with ice-latticed beams
    
- **Hidden mountain paths**, known only to local guides
    

The surrounding peaks form a natural barrier. Avalanches and whiteout conditions are common and treated as ordinary hazards.

---

## **III. Economy & Work**

Verglass is largely self-sufficient.

Primary work includes:

- Ice fishing from deep subglacial pools
    
- Mountain hunting and trapping
    
- Bone, fur, and icecraft
    

Trade is minimal and infrequent, conducted only with trusted intermediaries. Coin has little meaning here; obligation and memory carry more weight.

---

## **IV. Governance & Authority**

The village is governed by a **Council of Elders**, chosen for memory, restraint, and survival wisdom rather than lineage.

- Decisions are made by consensus
    
- Outsider access is decided collectively
    
- Leadership is quiet and rotational
    

No external authority is recognized. Vinyot’s capital holds no practical sway this high in the mountains.

---

## **V. Labyrinth Pressure (Unspoken)**

No Labyrinth site is publicly acknowledged near Verglass.

However, the snow cats’ oral histories speak of:

- Deep ice hollows that must never be entered
    
- “Listening places” beneath the plateau
    
- Old rules about where to hunt, dig, or settle
    

These traditions are not explained to outsiders. Questions are usually met with silence.

---

## **VI. People & Tone**

The people of Verglass are reserved, observant, and deeply communal.

Common traits include:

- Few spoken words, carefully chosen
    
- Strong emphasis on shared labor
    
- Long memory for slights and kindness alike
    

Snow cats who leave the plateau are rare and often regarded with concern rather than pride.

---

## **VII. Rumors & Narrative Use**

Stories whispered outside Verglass include:

- A hidden ascent only visible during certain snowfalls
    
- Snow cats who walk unmarked paths during blizzards
    
- A reason the plateau was never claimed or mined
    

**Verglass is best used as:**

- A hidden cultural enclave
    
- A place of ancient oral history
    
- A test of respect rather than strength
