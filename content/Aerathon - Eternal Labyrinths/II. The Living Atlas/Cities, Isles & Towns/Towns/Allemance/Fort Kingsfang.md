### _Crown Guard Training Hold_

**Region:** **Allemance**  
**Nearest Authority:** **Louvain** (administrative only)  
**Settlement Type:** Military fort & training town  
**Population:** ~2,200 (wolves, humans, and dwarves; permanent staff and rotating recruits)

---

## **I. Overview**

**Fort Kingsfang** stands behind the center of a vast stone wall that stretches **fifteen miles east and west**, once marking the historic **Allemance–Oria border**. The fort was the site of a decisive engagement during the **Invader War**, after which both the border and the authority it symbolized effectively dissolved.

Though the **Crown no longer holds meaningful political power**, the **Crown Guard** endures as a deeply rooted social institution—particularly outside **Louvain**, where tradition and shared identity still matter more than legal technicalities.

Today, Kingsfang trains young commoners—**wolves, humans, and dwarves alike**—sent by their baronies to learn martial discipline. Participation is less about loyalty to a crown and more about readiness, reputation, and communal expectation.

---

## **II. Geography & Layout**

The fort is built for order and repetition.

- **The Wall:** a massive stone rampart, now symbolic rather than defensive
    
- **Central Gate:** aligned with the main parade and drill grounds
    
- **Barracks Rows:** mixed-species housing adapted for varied builds
    
- **Training Yards:** open fields, obstacle courses, and sparring rings
    

The surrounding plains are deliberately exposed, reinforcing discipline and coordination.

---

## **III. Economy & Work**

Kingsfang exists to train and sustain itself.

Primary activity includes:

- Mixed-unit martial instruction
    
- Equipment forging, fitting, and repair
    
- Logistics, cooking, and stable work
    

Dwarven smiths, human quartermasters, and wolf instructors operate together, reinforcing cooperation as the fort’s core lesson.

---

## **IV. Governance & Authority**

Fort Kingsfang is administered by the **Crown Guard**, not by a reigning Crown.

- A **Commandant** oversees training doctrine and discipline
    
- Authority is internal and functional, not political
    
- Barony lords sponsor recruits but cannot issue commands
    

Louvain acknowledges Kingsfang as a legacy institution and permits its continued operation, recognizing its stabilizing influence in rural Allemance.

---

## **V. Labyrinth Pressure (None Recorded)**

No Labyrinth manifestation has ever been recorded at or near Fort Kingsfang.

Training remains focused on conventional warfare and defensive readiness. Any Labyrinth-related instruction is theoretical and limited, consistent with the Quiet Edict.

---

## **VI. People & Tone**

Kingsfang is disciplined, pragmatic, and transitional.

Common traits include:

- Wolves learning restraint and formation discipline
    
- Humans adapting to strict routine
    
- Dwarves emphasizing endurance and equipment mastery
    

Many recruits do not go on to serve formally. The training itself is the point.

---

## **VII. Rumors & Narrative Use**

Talk around Kingsfang often centers on:

- Whether the Guard still matters without a Crown
    
- Quiet updates to doctrine after recent conflicts
    
- Why baronies continue to send their youth
    

**Fort Kingsfang is best used as:**

- A mixed-species martial origin
    
- A symbol of tradition without authority
    
- A place where social expectation outlives political power
