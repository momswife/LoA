### _Farming City of the Glasrún Plains_

**Region:** **Allemance**  
**Sub-Region:** **Glasrún**  
**Nearest Authority:** **Louvain** (recognized, lightly exercised)  
**Settlement Type:** Agrarian city  
**Population:** ~8,000

---

## **I. Overview**

**Dermad** rests on the sunlit coastal plains of **Glasrún**, surrounded by some of the most fertile farmland in Aerathon. The city is often called the **“Forgotten City,”** a long-standing Glasrúnish joke—when **Aurelian Vossryn**, First Warden of Glasrún, relinquished control of the region, he did so unaware of the rich land on which Dermad now stands.

By the time Dermad’s value was understood, it had already become indispensable.

Today, Dermad quietly feeds much of forested Allemance, sustaining free communities through reliability rather than force.

---

## **II. Geography & Layout**

Dermad is designed to merge with its surroundings rather than dominate them.

- **Broad agricultural belts** encircle the city with no defensive walls
    
- **Low stone districts** arranged around communal greens and markets
    
- **Natural irrigation channels** following terrain and seasonal flow
    
- **Druid groves** integrated directly into working farmland
    

The transition from field to street is gradual—often unnoticed by visitors.

---

## **III. Economy & Work**

Dermad’s economy is agricultural, efficient, and unusually sustainable.

Primary work includes:

- Grain and staple crop farming
    
- Orchard cultivation and herbal agriculture
    
- Food processing and preservation
    

Through **innovative farming techniques and restrained magical aid**, Dermad feeds thousands without exhausting the soil. These methods underpin many long-distance caravan routes, making extended travel—including delver caravans—viable across the region.

---

## **IV. Governance & Authority**

Dermad is governed by a **Civic Stewardship Council**, composed of landwards, farming collective representatives, and trade coordinators.

- Decisions are reached by consensus
    
- Militarization is deliberately minimal
    
- Authority prioritizes continuity and land health
    

Louvain’s jurisdiction is acknowledged, but Dermad’s value as a food source affords it practical autonomy.

---

## **V. Labyrinth Pressure (Distant)**

No Labyrinth manifestation has occurred within Dermad or its surrounding fields.

Local belief credits **deliberate restraint**—limited excavation, cautious magic use, and controlled growth. Discussion of Labyrinths is rare and abstract, in keeping with the Quiet Edict.

---

## **VI. People & Tone**

Dermad is calm, principled, and openly pacifist.

Common traits include:

- Strong resistance to militarization
    
- A live-and-let-live approach to regional politics
    
- Deep cultural memory of recent war
    

Dermad’s stance often tempers more revolutionary voices elsewhere in Glasrún, particularly those emerging from **Daltiarna**.

---

## **VII. Rumors & Narrative Use**

Quiet talk in Dermad includes:

- Whether its farming model could survive open conflict
    
- Friction with more militant neighbors
    
- How long pacifism can endure if tested
    

**Dermad is best used as:**

- A logistical anchor within Allemance
    
- A moral counterweight to armed regions
    
- A reminder that restraint can be a form of strength
