### _Riverside Farming Town of the Esterhine_

**Region:** **Allemance**  
**Nearest Authority:** **Louvain** (distant administrative reach)  
**Settlement Type:** Rural farming village  
**Population:** ~450

---

## **I. Overview**

**Erin** is a tiny farming settlement nestled between the slow, looping waters of the **Esterhine River** and a series of low, rolling hills beyond. It is a place defined by continuity rather than change—families who have worked the same fields for generations, homes repaired rather than replaced, and lives measured in harvests instead of years.

Life in Erin is quiet and reliable. Nothing much happens here.  
That is precisely why some of its youth eventually leave.

---

## **II. Geography & Layout**

Erin is compact and open, with no defenses and few boundaries.

- **Riverside Fields:** fertile plots irrigated directly from the Esterhine
    
- **Hill Pastures:** grazing land and windbreak orchards
    
- **Central Green:** a shared space for markets, festivals, and meetings
    
- **Stone Farmhouses:** low, sturdy, and often expanded rather than rebuilt
    

Flooding is rare but anticipated; cellars are shallow, and barns are built on slight rises.

---

## **III. Economy & Work**

Erin survives through steady, unremarkable agriculture.

Primary work includes:

- Grain and vegetable farming
    
- Orchard tending
    
- Small-scale dairy and wool production
    

Most goods are sold upriver through Allemantic trade routes. Wealth is modest, but hunger is rare. Coin circulates slowly and stays close to home.

---

## **IV. Governance & Authority**

Erin is governed locally by a **Village Moot**, composed of long-established households.

- Decisions are made communally
    
- Disputes are resolved through mediation
    
- Outside authority is acknowledged but seldom felt
    

Louvain’s laws apply in theory, but in practice Erin governs itself through precedent and trust.

---

## **V. Labyrinth Pressure (Distant)**

No Labyrinth site is known near Erin.

Stories of delving reach the town only through travelers and returning youth. The Quiet Edict is observed by habit rather than enforcement—people here do not speak of such matters because they have no reason to.

When Labyrinth news arrives, it feels unreal, like weather from another country.

---

## **VI. People & Tone**

Erin is patient, familiar, and deeply rooted.

Common traits include:

- Recognizing strangers immediately
    
- Remembering family histories without records
    
- Expecting children to leave, and welcoming them back without question
    

Ambition is not discouraged—but neither is staying.

---

## **VII. Rumors & Narrative Use**

Quiet talk in Erin often centers on:

- Which young folk might leave next
    
- Whether the river has shifted its course slightly
    
- Letters from far-off cities read aloud in the green
    

**Erin is best used as:**

- A humble origin point for characters
    
- A place of emotional contrast to larger regions
    
- A reminder of what life looks like without spectacle
