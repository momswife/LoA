### _Mage-Silent Town of the Allemantic Lowlands_

**Region:** **Allemance**  
**Nearest Authority:** **Louvain** (acknowledged, rarely involved)  
**Settlement Type:** Small market town  
**Population:** ~1,800

---

## **I. Overview**

**Silette** is the only known settlement in Aerathon where **magic does not function**.

Within the town and the surrounding fields, arcane, divine, and pact-bound magic alike simply fail to manifest. Spells do not fizzle or backlash—they never begin. This phenomenon has remained stable for as long as records exist and is treated locally not as a mystery to be solved, but as a fact to be lived with.

As a result, Silette has grown into a town defined by practicality, self-reliance, and a quiet skepticism toward the arcane world beyond its borders.

---

## **II. Geography & Layout**

Silette sits on gently rolling Allemantic land, unremarkable at first glance.

- **Stone-built homes** designed without enchantment or warding
    
- **Broad workyards** for smithing, carpentry, and milling
    
- **A central market square** with no shrines or spellhouses
    
- **Outlying farms** arranged for efficient labor rather than defense
    

The boundary of the dead zone is subtle but consistent. Those who rely on magic learn to recognize it quickly.

---

## **III. Economy & Work**

Silette’s economy is resolutely mundane.

Primary work includes:

- Farming and animal husbandry
    
- Smithing, masonry, and carpentry
    
- Toolmaking and mechanical repair
    

Because magic cannot supplement labor, Silette’s crafts are prized for their reliability. Tools from the town are known to function anywhere—even where magic falters.

---

## **IV. Governance & Authority**

Silette is governed by a **Civic Council**, elected from established trade households.

- Laws emphasize fairness, safety, and accountability
    
- Outside magical authorities hold no special status
    
- Disputes are resolved through testimony and record
    

While Louvain’s authority is recognized, it intervenes rarely. Silette requires little oversight—and tolerates none lightly.

---

## **V. Labyrinth Pressure (Absent)**

No Labyrinth manifestation has ever been recorded within Silette.

This absence is well documented and widely accepted. Whether the arcane silence prevents such manifestations or simply coincides with their absence remains an open question—one the town has no interest in answering.

Delvers passing through are not questioned publicly. Official discussion of Labyrinth matters is discouraged, in keeping with the Quiet Edict.

---

## **VI. People & Tone**

The people of Silette are direct, capable, and quietly proud.

Common traits include:

- Valuing effort over aptitude
    
- Viewing magical curiosity as frivolous or risky
    
- Expecting visitors to carry their own weight
    

Spellcasters often find the town unsettling. Locals consider that reaction revealing.

---

## **VII. Rumors & Narrative Use**

Soft-spoken requests sometimes reach visiting delvers:

- Stories of places where magic still works
    
- Descriptions of Labyrinths seen elsewhere
    
- Confirmation that the wider world remains strange
    

**Silette is best used as:**

- A grounded contrast to high-magic regions
    
- A safe but limiting stop for spell-reliant parties
    
- A place where effort, not power, earns respect
