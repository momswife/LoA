## **I. Overview**

**Gocaire** is a baronial capital of **Allemance**, positioned several miles south of the **Queensriver**, the symbolic and legal boundary between Allemance and **Glasrún**. Long regarded as an unremarkable border city—best known for the first baron’s historic abandonment of the northern forestlands—Gocaire has recently redefined itself with bold, controversial ambition.

In the modern age, Gocaire proclaims itself the **City of First Delvers**, the hometown of the earliest formally recognized delving crew. In a bid to revive its fortunes and relevance, the barony has declared open favor toward delvers, offering privileges, protections, and public celebration.

The gates are open.  
Not everyone is pleased.

**Population:** ~24,000  
**City Type:** Border City / Baronial Capital  
**Primary Renown:** Delver hospitality, civic gamble  
**Civic Motto:** _“Opportunity favors the bold.”_

---

## **II. Geography & Architecture**

Gocaire lies on gently rolling terrain between cultivated Allemantic farmland and the darker treeline of Glasrún.

### **City Layout**

- A traditional Allemantic grid around a central keep
    
- Wide trade roads leading north toward the Queensriver
    
- Southern districts dominated by farms and granaries
    

### **The Old Line**

A raised earthen embankment marks the historic “limit” of Allemantic expansion—the point beyond which the first baron relinquished all claim. No permanent structures are built beyond it.

Architecture is practical and conservative:

- Stone civic buildings
    
- Timber residential blocks
    
- Newer inns and guildhouses clustered near the gate roads
    

---

## **III. History**

Gocaire’s early history is defined by **retreat rather than conquest**. When its founding baron deemed the forest beyond the Queensriver too costly and politically inconvenient to tame, he abandoned the claim outright—an act that earned ridicule across Allemance for generations.

For centuries afterward, Gocaire remained quiet and cautious, a city defined by what it chose _not_ to pursue. That identity shattered in the modern era, when a group of local youths formed what would later be recognized as the **first formal delving crew**.

Rather than distance itself from their dangerous notoriety, Gocaire embraced it—betting that proximity to danger might finally bring relevance, wealth, and legacy.

**Key Historical Events:**

- **533 A.D.** — Founding of **Gocaire** as a border barony capital, tasked with holding the northern line.
    
- **571 A.D.** — The **Forest Abandonment**, when the first baron relinquishes all claims north of the Queensriver, formalizing the Glasrún Pact’s spirit.
    
- **2871 A.D.** — Formation of the **First Delving Crew**, composed primarily of Gocaire natives.
    
- **3148 A.D.** — Proclamation of the **Delver Welcome Edict**, granting delvers privileges, discounts, and civic protections within city limits.
    

Gocaire’s history has pivoted from caution to **calculated risk**—and the city is still adjusting its footing.

---

## **IV. Governance**

Gocaire is ruled by a traditional **Baronial Court**, advised by:

- A civic council of merchants and elders
    
- A newly appointed **Delver Liaison**
    
- Representatives of the city guard
    

The current nobility strongly supports the delver initiative, viewing it as the barony’s best chance at prosperity—even if it costs internal harmony.

---

## **V. Economy**

Gocaire’s economy is in transition.

**Traditional Sectors**

- Agriculture
    
- River-road trade
    
- Craft and repair
    

**Emerging Sectors**

- Delver outfitting and supply
    
- Inns, taverns, and relic brokers
    
- Escort and recovery services
    

Local businesses that cater to delvers receive tax incentives, further inflaming resentment among traditional tradesfolk.

---

## **VI. Faith & Culture**

Faith in Gocaire is mixed and strained.

- Older families favor hearth rites and agrarian gods
    
- Younger residents idolize risk, glory, and survival
    
- Shrines near the gates increasingly include offerings for safe delves
    

Culturally, the city is divided between **pride and unease**.

> _“We fed ourselves just fine before they arrived.”_

---

## **VII. Law & Defense**

Gocaire maintains a reinforced city watch.

- Delvers are granted legal leniency for minor infractions
    
- Violence, property damage, or intimidation are still punished harshly
    
- Increased patrols separate delver districts from residential quarters
    

Security focuses on **containment rather than expulsion**.

---

## **VIII. Relationships to the Labyrinths**

Gocaire has no Labyrinth within its walls, but serves as a **launch point** for expeditions heading north and east.

- Delver traffic is constant
    
- Relics pass through under MDO scrutiny
    
- Rumors claim some delvers never leave the city—only descend elsewhere
    

The MDO maintains a **small liaison office**, watching closely.

## **X. Cultural Identity & Legacy**

Gocaire is attempting to rewrite its legacy.

Once known as the city that **turned away**, it now strains to be remembered as the city that **opened its gates**. Whether this gamble brings prosperity or fracture remains an open question—but Gocaire has committed to the risk.

> _“We were forgotten once,”_  
> _“we will not be again.”_

---

## **XI. Quirky Natures**

- Shops advertise “Delver Prices” openly
    
- Locals can identify delvers by how they walk—always scanning
    
- Tavern brawls spike after successful expeditions
    
- The Old Line embankment is never crossed at night
