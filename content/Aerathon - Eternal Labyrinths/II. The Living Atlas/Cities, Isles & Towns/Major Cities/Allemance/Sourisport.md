## **I. Overview**

**Sourisport** marks the **western terminus of the Causeway**, the great bridge-road that stretches across the **Strait of Glass** toward Arneria. It is the last city travelers encounter before leaving Allemance—and the first place where Arnerian restraint decisively unravels.

Though Sourisport hosts the **largest Arnerian population anywhere in Allemance**, its cultural identity is unmistakably and aggressively **Alley**. Loud, welcoming, indulgent, and unapologetic, the city prides itself on being the place where rules loosen, expectations slip, and life is lived at full volume.

If the Causeway is a promise,  
Sourisport is where it **breaks into celebration**.

**Population:** ~46,000  
**City Type:** Port City / Causeway Terminus  
**Primary Renown:** Trade, indulgence, cultural release  
**Civic Saying:** _“Come in. You look like you need it.”_

---

## **II. Geography & Architecture**

Sourisport sprawls along the coast at the edge of the **Strait of Glass**, its docks extending outward like open arms.

### **The Causeway Shore**

The massive bridge fades into the horizon eastward, its shadow falling across:

- Warehouses
    
- Taverns
    
- Gambling halls
    
- Pleasure dens
    

The closer one gets to the bridge, the louder the city becomes—as if to drown out second thoughts.

### **Cityscape**

Architecture blends:

- Arnerian stonework and arches
    
- Allemantic timber fronts and open balconies
    

Buildings lean close together, streets curve unpredictably, and everything seems designed to funnel newcomers **toward the docks first**.

---

## **III. History**

Sourisport began as a modest harbor settlement serving early Causeway construction crews. Its purpose was practical: supply, rest, and passage.

That purpose did not survive success.

As the Causeway opened and traffic surged between Arneria and Allemance, Sourisport became a **pressure valve**—the place where travelers, merchants, and pilgrims released months of discipline, faith, and expectation before or after the crossing.

Attempts to regulate or “clean up” the city repeatedly failed. Each wave of reform collapsed under the same truth: Sourisport thrived because it was allowed to be what other cities forbade.

**Key Historical Events:**

- **1732 A.D.** — Establishment of **Sourisport Harbor** as a logistical support town for early Causeway construction.
    
- **1819 A.D.** — Completion of the **Causeway Bridge**, triggering a population and trade explosion.
    
- **2094 A.D.** — The **Glass Night Riots**, following an attempted moral reform crackdown; all restrictive ordinances repealed within the year.
    
- **2988 A.D.** — Formal recognition as the **Causeway Terminus City**, cementing Sourisport’s cultural role.
    

Sourisport learned early that survival meant **embracing excess before it turned inward**.

---

## **IV. Governance**

Sourisport is governed by a **Harbor Syndicate**, an informal coalition of:

- Dockmasters
    
- Tavern guilds
    
- Trade brokers
    
- Shipping families
    

A nominal Allemantic magistrate exists, but real authority lies in consensus and commerce. Laws are written to keep the city functioning, not virtuous.

---

## **V. Economy**

Sourisport’s economy is unapologetically indulgent.

**Primary Industries**

- Dock trade and transit services
    
- Taverns, gambling halls, and entertainment
    
- Vice markets of every legal stripe
    
- Short-term lodging and repair
    

If something is bad for you,  
it is probably for sale—and taxed.

---

## **VI. Faith & Culture**

Faith in Sourisport is **private and optional**.

- Shrines exist, but rarely dominate
    
- Ritual abstinence ends at the docks
    
- Clerics are welcomed as guests, not authorities
    

Culturally, Sourisport operates on radical hospitality. Everyone is welcome. Everyone is offered something. Saying no is respected—once.

> _“You can always leave tomorrow.”_

---

## **VII. Law & Defense**

Law in Sourisport is pragmatic.

- Violence that disrupts trade is punished swiftly
    
- Vice is tolerated as long as consent is clear
    
- Dock Watch focuses on containment, not prevention
    

Defense is oriented outward: keeping piracy, smuggling wars, and Causeway incidents from spilling inward.

---

## **VIII. Relationships to the Labyrinths**

Sourisport does not court the Labyrinths—but does not avoid them either.

- Delvers pass through frequently
    
- Relics appear briefly, then vanish
    
- No Labyrinth entrances known nearby
    

The city prefers danger that spends coin.

## **X. Cultural Identity & Legacy**

Sourisport is not proud of excess.  
It is proud of **honesty**.

It does not pretend people are better than they are—only that they deserve warmth, noise, and a place to sit down before moving on.

> _“Everyone needs a door where the rules loosen.”_

---

## **XI. Quirky Natures**

- Taverns open before sunrise and close “eventually”
    
- Dock signs advertise sins euphemistically
    
- Causeway travelers are welcomed with unsolicited advice
    
- No one asks what you did before you arrived
