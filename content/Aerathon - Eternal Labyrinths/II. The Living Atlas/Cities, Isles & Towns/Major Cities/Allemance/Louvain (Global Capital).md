## **I. Overview**

**Louvain** is the undisputed **political, administrative, and cultural capital of Aerathon**. Where other cities command by trade, faith, or fear, Louvain governs through **memory**—law written into stone, ritual embedded in infrastructure, and history preserved as living civic function.

Built atop the consecrated ground of the **First Concord**, Louvain is home to the **Ministry of Delving Operations**, the largest archival complex in the known world, and countless guild, religious, and cultural institutions. To live in Louvain is to exist under record.

**Population:** ~2.6 million  
**Dominant Peoples:** Humans, Elves, Halflings; large Beastfolk and Dwarven minorities  
**Regional Symbol:** The Eternal Flame encircled by clasped hands  
**Civic Maxim:** _“To remember is to remain.”_

---

## **II. Geography & Architecture**

Louvain sits at the convergence of the **Silverway River** and the fertile **Heartplain**, surrounded by low hills that reflect lanternlight for miles at night.

### **Urban Layout**

The city is divided into **Seven Lantern Wards**, each radiating from the central **Flame Ward**, where the Eternal Flame burns.

|Ward|Primary Function|
|---|---|
|**Flame Ward**|Council Hall of Lanterns, Eternal Flame|
|**Scribe’s Ward**|Hall of Charters, Ministry Vaults|
|**Glass Ward**|Magitech, Emberlight Union|
|**Forge Ward**|Industry, Oric & Vinyoti trade|
|**Market Ward**|River docks, open bazaar|
|**Verdure Ward**|Gardens, sanctuaries, residences|
|**Ember Ward**|Delver academies, Free Company|

Architecture favors **brass, glass, and stone**, with enchantments woven into lampposts, bridges, and walls—every structure is designed to last, and to remember.

## **III. History**

Louvain was founded in the aftermath of the **Age of Collapse**, when survivors gathered around a patch of land still warm with divine fire. That fire became the **Eternal Hearth**, and around it rose a settlement dedicated not merely to survival, but to preventing history from being lost again.

As delvers ventured outward and guilds formed, Louvain evolved into a neutral ground where law, memory, and exploration intersected. The city did not conquer its neighbors—it **outlived them**, offering structure where chaos once ruled.

Louvain’s history is inseparable from Aerathon’s rebirth. To chronicle one is to chronicle the other.

**Key Historical Events:**

- **487 A.D.** — **The First Concord**, uniting surviving city-states under shared law, with Louvain established as neutral center.
    
- **853 A.D.** — Creation of the **Ministry Archives**, laying the foundation for modern delver regulation and relic law.
    
- **1479 A.D.** — Completion of the **Lantern Road**, enabling continental travel between Louvain, Vinyot, and Oria.
    
- **2688 A.D.** — Final sealing of the **Great Scribe Vault**, now the largest repository of labyrinth and relic records in existence.
    

Louvain endures because it remembers what the world was—and refuses to forget what it cost.

## **IV. Governance**

Louvain is governed by the **Council of Lanterns**, twelve delegates drawn from:

- Major guild syndicates
    
- Recognized faiths
    
- Civic institutions
    

The Council is presided over by a **High Curator**, traditionally selected with Ministry approval. Beneath them, each ward is administered by a **Lantern Syndic**.

The **Ministry of Delving Operations** holds **extrajudicial authority** within Louvain, overseeing delver licensing, relic containment, and breach response. This oversight is widely accepted as sacred duty rather than oppression.

> _“Paperwork is prayer; ink is sacrifice.”_

---

## **V. Economy**

Louvain is the wealthiest city in Aerathon, its economy built on **knowledge rather than extraction**.

**Primary Industries**

- Relic appraisal and registry
    
- Magitech fabrication
    
- Education and delver certification
    
- Cultural export (festivals, art, doctrine)
    

**Key Institutions**

- **The Luminspire** (Emberlight Union)
    
- **The Hearthhall** (Free Company)
    
- **Lantern Market**, operating under perpetual wardlight
    

Currency: **Crownflame**, a soft-gold coin that glows faintly in moonlight.

---

## **VI. Faith & Culture**

Faith in Louvain is **syncretic and civic**.

The city honors **Pelor, Oghma, Chauntea, and Gond** as the **Four Hands of the Hearth**, with shrines embedded into public buildings so worship and daily life are inseparable.

Major observances include:

- **Feast of First Light**
    
- **Charter’s Dawn**
    
- **Delver’s Rest**, marked by floating lanterns on the Silverway
    

Music, food, and storytelling are treated as civic obligations. Taverns and amphitheaters are often described as “temples of the common soul.”

---

## **VII. Law & Defense**

Louvain is beautiful—and deeply fortified.

- The **Lantern Guard** enforces civil and relic law
    
- Every lantern post functions as a warding nexus
    
- In crisis, the city can raise the **Halo of Concord**, a city-wide barrier of living fire and memory-runes
    

Punishment focuses on **record and restitution**. Crimes are entered into public civic ledgers; to be erased from record is considered the harshest sentence possible.

---

## **VIII. Relationships to the Labyrinths**

Beneath Louvain lies the **First Vault**, a sealed labyrinth where the Ministry recovered its founding relics.

- Officially closed
    
- Actively monitored for resonance
    
- Studied indirectly through controlled breaches
    

Many scholars believe the **Eternal Flame** itself is a relic heart—alive, listening, and perhaps dreaming.

---

## **X. Cultural Identity & Legacy**

Louvain is a paradox: progressive yet reverent, bureaucratic yet sacred. Its people believe that memory is the only true defense against collapse.

If Aerathon ever falls again, many believe Louvain will remain—glowing beneath the ash, a single lantern refusing to go out.

> _“To live in Louvain is to know your story will be kept.”_

---

## **XI. Quirky Natures**

- Citizens can identify wards by lantern tone alone
    
- Streets subtly realign during major civic reforms
    
- Locals leave written apologies for buildings they damage
    
- The Eternal Flame is said to dim briefly before great tragedy
