## **I. Overview**

**Riverbed City** is a long, terraced settlement built directly into the dry ravine once carved by the **Teplo River**. Though the river itself no longer flows through the basin, the city endures—repurposed from riverport to **premier freshwater shipwright hub** and northern transit point.

Situated along Allemance’s northern edge near the **Oric border**, Riverbed City occupies a critical logistical niche. Travelers heading north—particularly **Unknowns returning home from Allemance**—rely on its boats to make the final, perilous journey before winter seals the passes.

**Population:** ~18,000  
**City Type:** Border Craft City / Former Riverport  
**Primary Renown:** Freshwater shipbuilding, river transit  
**Civic Maxim:** _“If water remembers, so do we.”_

---

## **II. Geography & Architecture**

Riverbed City is defined by absence.

### **The Dry Ravine**

Once the Teplo’s course, the ravine now forms the city’s spine:

- Broad stone terraces carved by ancient flow
    
- Stepped districts descending toward the basin floor
    
- Dry channels repurposed as roads, markets, and slipways
    

The sound of running water is replaced by hammering wood and creaking hulls.

### **Northern Edge**

The city’s northernmost docks sit close to the **Oric border**, where boats are loaded for upriver journeys along the Teplo’s new course. Seasonal ice warnings are posted throughout the year.

Architecture favors:

- Stone foundations shaped by erosion
    
- Long boathouses recessed into cliff walls
    
- Open-air yards designed for hull assembly
    

---

## **III. History**

Riverbed City was once an unremarkable riverport—important locally, forgettable regionally. That changed **two centuries ago**, when a celestial impact struck the **Mantle Mountains**.

The resulting quake rerouted the mountain streams feeding the Teplo. Within weeks, the river reversed its course, abandoning the ravine entirely. Barges were left grounded. Docks stood dry. Trade collapsed overnight.

Many cities would have died then. Riverbed City adapted.

Rather than chase the river, its people learned to **build for it**—developing hulls capable of surviving shifting currents, cold runoff, and shallow channels. What began as necessity became mastery.

**Key Historical Events:**

- **2941 A.D.** — The **Mantle Impact**, a skyfall event that triggers regional seismic upheaval.
    
- **2942 A.D.** — The **Teplo Reversal**, permanently redirecting the river’s flow northward.
    
- **2968 A.D.** — Establishment of the **Riverwright Compact**, standardizing freshwater vessel design.
    
- **3087 A.D.** — Recognition by inland guilds as Aerathon’s foremost freshwater shipbuilding center.
    

Riverbed City remembers the disaster not as loss, but as instruction.

---

## **IV. Governance**

Riverbed City is governed by a **Craft Council**, dominated by:

- Master shipwrights
    
- Trade captains
    
- Border liaisons
    

A nominal Allemantic overseer handles external affairs, but internal governance is shaped by those who build and sail.

---

## **V. Economy**

The city’s economy is tightly focused.

**Primary Industries**

- Freshwater vessel construction
    
- Apprentice training and certification
    
- River transit outfitting
    
- Seasonal storage and winterization
    

Riverbed boats command premium prices across Aerathon. Many inland shipwrights apprentice here, though few stay—the winters are harsh, and the work unforgiving.

---

## **VI. Faith & Culture**

Faith in Riverbed City is quiet and geological.

- Offerings are made to hearth and threshold spirits
    
- Old river markers are treated as shrines
    
- Seasonal rites mark freeze, thaw, and departure
    

Culturally, the city values **adaptation over tradition**.

> _“The river taught us to move by leaving.”_

---

## **VII. Law & Defense**

Riverbed City maintains a compact but disciplined defense force.

- Patrols focus on docks and border approaches
    
- Theft of vessels is treated as high crime
    
- Winter embargoes are enforced strictly
    

Defense is practical; survival depends on timing and preparation.

---

## **VIII. Relationships to the Labyrinths**

Riverbed City maintains deliberate distance from the Labyrinths.

- No known entrances nearby
    
- Craft guilds prohibit Labyrinth-sourced materials in hulls
    
- Delvers are tolerated but not catered to
    

The city trusts stone and wood more than anomaly.

---

## **X. Cultural Identity & Legacy**

Riverbed City is proof that geography does not decide fate—response does.

It stands as a reminder that catastrophes do not erase cities. They **reshape them**, if the people choose to stay.

> _“The river moved on. We learned why.”_

---

## **XI. Quirky Natures**

- Children learn river charts before letters
    
- Old docks are preserved as meeting halls
    
- Boat names favor verbs over animals
    
- Locals still measure distance in “days of drift”
