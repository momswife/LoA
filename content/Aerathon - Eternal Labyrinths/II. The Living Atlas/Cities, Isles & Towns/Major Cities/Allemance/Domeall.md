## **I. Overview**

**Domeall** (DOM-ull) is the **northernmost city of Allemance** and the terminus of the great trade road that cuts through **Glasrún**. It serves simultaneously as a port city, naval stronghold, and the de facto center of **Glasrún’s foreign affairs**.

While formally within Allemance’s borders, Domeall operates with remarkable autonomy. Its people—often called the **Forest Wolves**—understand their independence not as a granted right, but as a **strategic reality**: conquering Domeall would be ruinously costly.

To ensure it stays that way, Domeall maintains the **largest naval force in Allemance**.

**Population:** ~42,000  
**City Type:** Port City / Border Power  
**Primary Renown:** Naval dominance, Oric trade, Glasrún diplomacy  
**Civic Maxim:** _“Neutral does not mean unarmed.”_

---

## **II. Geography & Architecture**

Domeall sits where the forest thins into rocky shoreline, its harbor protected by natural stone arms and reinforced breakwaters.

### **The Harbor**

- Deep-water docks capable of handling Oric heavy carriers
    
- Shipyards carved partly into stone
    
- Layered harbor chains and retractable barriers
    

### **The Lodge City**

Architecture blends Glasrún and Oric sensibilities:

- Massive timber-and-stone structures resembling lodge houses
    
- Sloped roofs and broad beams
    
- Communal hearth halls rather than narrow streets
    

The city is built outward and low, favoring **strength over height**.

---

## **III. History**

Domeall began as a pragmatic settlement: a place where forest routes ended and ships began. As Glasrún’s inland trade grew, Domeall became unavoidable—not just for goods, but for diplomacy.

During the **Mantle War** a century and a half ago, Allemance and Oria found themselves on opposing sides. Glasrún, and Domeall with it, remained neutral. That choice—dangerous at the time—proved foundational. Orians never forgot that the Forest Wolves did not turn their blades north.

In the generations since, Domeall has cultivated its identity as a city that survives by **standing between powers without bowing to either**.

**Key Historical Events:**

- **712 A.D.** — Founding of **Domeall** as a forest-edge port facilitating Glasrún trade.
    
- **3011 A.D.** — The **Neutral Harbor Accord**, formalizing Domeall’s open-port policy during the Mantle War.
    
- **3024 A.D.** — The **Wolffleet Expansion**, dramatically increasing Domeall’s naval capacity in response to regional instability.
    
- **3098 A.D.** — Signing of the **Elk-Wolf Compact**, reaffirming long-standing Oric–Glasrún trade and cultural exchange.

## **IV. Governance**

Domeall is governed by a **Harbor Council**, composed of:

- Ship-captains
    
- Trade stewards
    
- Naval commanders
    
- Forest elders
    

While a royal governor is technically appointed by Allemance, their role is largely symbolic. Real authority rests with those who command ships and supply lines.

---

## **V. Economy**

Domeall’s economy is built on **movement and mediation**.

**Primary Trade**

- Oric metalworks and heavy goods
    
- Glasrún timber, furs, and rare forest products
    
- Shipbuilding and repair
    

The city also profits from tariffs, docking rights, and escort contracts.

---

## **VI. Faith & Culture**

Faith in Domeall is pragmatic and blended.

- Glasrún ancestral rites remain strong
    
- Oric hunter traditions are widely respected
    
- Shrines often honor _journeys completed_, not gods appeased
    

Culturally, Domeall is a place of **adaptation**:

- Oric cuisine is common
    
- Glasrúnish wolves wear hair wraps echoing elk antler motifs
    
- Songs mix forest rhythms with ship-timing chants
    

> _“The sea listens. The forest watches.”_

---

## **VII. Law & Defense**

Domeall’s law is inseparable from its navy.

- The **Wolffleet** enforces maritime law
    
- Harbor violations carry severe penalties
    
- Foreign vessels are monitored continuously
    

On land, enforcement is light; at sea, it is absolute.

---

## **VIII. Relationships to the Labyrinths**

Domeall maintains a **deliberate distance** from the Labyrinths.

- No known entrances near the city
    
- Delvers are tolerated, but not centered
    
- Relics passing through are inspected swiftly and moved inland
    

The city prefers threats it can see coming over mysteries that cannot be blockaded.

Domeall influences the continent by deciding **which ships dock—and which turn away**.

---

## **X. Cultural Identity & Legacy**

Domeall is a city that knows its value.

It does not beg for freedom or boast of it. Instead, it maintains enough ships, allies, and resolve that no one finds it worth testing. To Glasrún, it is shield and voice. To Allemance, a necessary exception. To Oria, a friend that did not forget.

> _“We stayed neutral,”_  
> _“and remembered who noticed.”_

---

## **XI. Quirky Natures**

- Ships are named after forests as often as animals
    
- Locals can identify Oric ships by sound alone
    
- Antler motifs are considered respectful, not appropriative
    
- Harbor bells ring in different patterns for foreign nations
