## **I. Overview**

**Patrie** is a large and culturally singular city located in **Bluebell Valley**, notable not for political authority, but for origin. It is the largest intact remnant of a **human metropolis from the Broken World**, relocated into Aerathon during a pre-Edict spatial anomaly in the mid-1400s A.D.

Patrie has never been a capital, nor has it sought such a role. Instead, it exists as a **refuge-city**, a living archive of humanity’s origin, and a permanent reminder that the world once moved in ways it no longer does.

Today, Patrie is one of the most populous cities in Allemance by residents, yet remains administratively modest—its influence cultural and economic rather than legislative.

**Population:** ~120,000 (approx. one-third uninhabited)  
**City Type:** Refuge Metropolis / Baronial City  
**Primary Renown:** Human heritage, preservation, anomaly-origin  
**Civic Maxim:** _“We remember where we came from.”_

---

## **II. Geography & Architecture**

Patrie occupies the heart of **Bluebell Valley**, approached by newly laid stone roads that branch from older Allemantic trade routes. Terraced **coffee farms** climb the valley slopes, marking the transition from countryside to concrete.

### **The Wheel City**

Patrie’s layout is radial:

- A dense central core
    
- Twelve broad arterial roads
    
- Twelve wedge-shaped districts between them, known as **quartiers**
    

This geometry predates its arrival in Aerathon and is believed to have contributed to the city’s structural survival during transposition.

### **Material & Color**

Patrie’s buildings are composed primarily of **concrete, steel, and composite stone**, materials uncommon elsewhere in Aerathon. Surfaces are painted in **loud, defiant colors**—acid greens, pinks, yellows, and blues. Banners of unreadable script and abstract design hang across streets, many stitched from salvaged Broken World materials.

The city does not conceal its origin.  
It **declares it**.

---

## **III. History**

Patrie’s history begins not in Aerathon, but in the **Broken World**.

The city was already abandoned and partially ruined when displaced human communities gathered within it during the early stages of the **Pilgrimage**. It was chosen not for safety, but for familiarity—streets still existed, buildings still stood, and memory had shape.

In the **mid-1400s A.D.**, during a period now classified by the MDO as a **Pre-Edict Spatial Displacement Event**, the metropolis was removed wholesale from the Broken World and deposited into Bluebell Valley. No god manifested. No divine voice was recorded. No controlling relic or system has ever been identified. The event bore signs of deliberate stabilization, but its cause remains unresolved.

For centuries afterward, Patrie functioned as a **self-contained refugee city**, inward-focused and cautious. Its size vastly exceeded its population, and large sections were left untouched, treated as memorial space rather than opportunity.

Only later did Allemance formalize its status, reorganizing surrounding lands into the **Barony of Patrie** to provide legal protection and oversight—without elevating the city to regional authority. Governance was granted to **Diana**, a human officer whose legitimacy among Patrie’s people and loyalty to the crown made her an acceptable steward. Her marriage to **Indigo**, a canine noble, secured Patrie’s place within Allemantic law while preserving its autonomy.

Restoration, not expansion, became Patrie’s defining mission.

### **Key Historical Events**

- **c. 1430–1460 A.D.** — **The Transposition Event**, relocating a ruined Broken World metropolis into Bluebell Valley during a pre-Edict spatial anomaly.
    
- **c. 1500 A.D.** — Stabilization of Patrie as a refugee city; large districts intentionally left uninhabited.
    
- **1689 A.D.** — Formal incorporation into Allemance as a baronial city, explicitly denied capital status.
    
- **2214 A.D.** — Ratification of the **Old Third Preservation Mandate**, restricting development in abandoned districts.
    
- **3100s A.D.** — Cultural resurgence and economic integration without political ascent.
    

> _“The city arrived complete. The future had to be built.”_

## **IV. Governance**

Patrie is governed as a **baronial city**, not a seat of power.

- **Baroness Diana** oversees civic stability and external relations
    
- A **Quartier Council** represents inhabited districts
    
- **Shamans** hold independent authority over preservation zones
    

Authority here is custodial, not expansionist.

---

## **V. Economy**

Patrie’s economy blends survival, culture, and export.

**Primary Sectors**

- Coffee cultivation (**Bluebell Nip**)
    
- Salvage, restoration, and construction
    
- Fashion, streetwear, and design
    
- Cultural tourism and archival labor
    

Scavengers bringing usable materials from the Broken World are rewarded, feeding restoration efforts in the Old Third.

---

## **VI. Faith & Culture**

Patrie’s culture is shaped by memory rather than worship.

- Faith is personal, subdued, and often historical
    
- Rituals commemorate survival, not miracles
    
- No god holds civic authority
    

Street art, fashion, and music are protected expressions of identity. History is worn openly, not sealed away.

---

## **VII. Law & Defense**

Patrie maintains layered defense:

- Baronial guards
    
- Quartier watch groups
    
- Restricted zones under Shamanic authority
    

Crimes involving heritage destruction or exploitation of preserved districts are treated as severe offenses.

---

## **VIII. Relationships to the Labyrinths**

Patrie maintains a **zero-tolerance posture** toward Labyrinth activity.

- No known entrances permitted within city bounds
    
- Any anomaly is immediately quarantined
    
- Delving culture is discouraged and closely monitored
    

The city has already survived one impossible relocation.

---

## **X. Cultural Identity & Legacy**

Patrie is not a monument to divine action or political ambition. It is a city that **refused to disappear**, carrying its origin forward intact.

It does not rule.  
It **remembers**.

> _“We were moved without being asked. Staying was our choice.”_

---

## **XI. Quirky Natures**

- Coffee shops double as archival salons
    
- Street names still exist in untranslated Broken World scripts
    
- Restoration crews argue over which cracks to leave visible
    
- No one agrees on how old the city “feels”
