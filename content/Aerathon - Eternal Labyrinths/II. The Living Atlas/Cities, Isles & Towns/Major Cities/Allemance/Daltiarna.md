## **I. Overview**

**Daltiarna** (dal-TEER-na) is the largest settlement within **Glasrún** and its **unofficial capital**, a sprawling forest metropolis of more than **30,000 residents**. It is renowned across Aerathon as the boldest experiment in large-scale self-governance—a city with no ruler, no charter, and no fixed borders.

Daltiarna does not clear the forest to grow. It winds through it, negotiating space with ancient trees, roots, and rising boughs. Entire districts exist beneath continuous canopy, shaped by agreement rather than plan.

Daltiarna is not governed.  
It is **argued into existence every day**.

---

## **II. Geography & Architecture**

Daltiarna spreads outward in **organic rings**, guided by social gravity rather than geometry.

### **The Canopy Streets**

- Winding paths at ground level and in the boughs
    
- Elevated walkways adjusted as trees grow or fall
    
- Routes known by use, not signage
    

### **District Clusters**

Districts form wherever enough people agree to:

- Speak for one another
    
- Share labor and risk
    
- Accept collective responsibility
    

Structures are built to adapt—homes often move before trees do.

### **The Open Rings**

Large natural clearings left deliberately unclaimed, used for:

- Multi-district debate
    
- Festivals
    
- Emergency assemblies
    

These spaces are considered civic commons beyond ownership.

## **III. History**

Daltiarna began as a **meeting place rather than a settlement**, where Glasrún villages overlapped just long enough for trade to turn into argument—and argument into community. As paths converged, people stayed not because they were ruled, but because they were heard.

The city’s explosive growth followed the **Pilgrimage**, when waves of displaced peoples—especially humans from the **Broken World**—arrived seeking autonomy over safety. Rather than impose limits, Daltiarna demanded only one thing: that newcomers find others willing to stand beside them.

Over centuries, this simple requirement transformed Daltiarna into a city defined by negotiation, exhaustion, and resilience. It survives not through harmony, but through constant, structured disagreement.

**Key Historical Events:**

- **498 A.D.** — The **First Gathering**, when multiple Glasrún villages establish a shared clearing for dispute resolution and trade.
    
- **911 A.D.** — Recognition of the **District Principle**, formalizing the idea that neighborhoods speak collectively rather than individually.
    
- **1422 A.D.** — The **Broken World Arrival**, when the first large wave of displaced humans settles permanently, reshaping Daltiarna’s demographics.
    
- **1887 A.D.** — The **Speaker’s Challenge**, establishing the custom that any Speaker may be replaced by open nomination at any time.
    
- **3094 A.D.** — The **Seven-Day Debate**, a citywide argument over MDO regulation that ends without resolution—but prevents enforcement entirely.
    

Daltiarna remembers its history not through monuments, but through **precedent**.

## **IV. Governance**

Daltiarna has **no central government**.

### **District Autonomy**

Each district:

- Sets its own customs
    
- Maintains its own spaces
    
- Enforces its own expectations
    

Actions affecting others must be debated openly with neighboring districts.

### **Speakers**

Each district appoints a **Speaker**:

- Acts as final arbiter in disputes
    
- Represents the district externally
    
- Holds power only until challenged
    

Though no limits exist, cultural pressure enforces an average tenure of **less than two years**.

---

## **V. Economy**

Daltiarna’s economy is decentralized and interpersonal.

**Common Trades**

- Forest-compatible construction
    
- Repair and craft
    
- Trade mediation
    
- Food production and preservation
    

Coin circulates, but **labor exchange and mutual obligation** dominate. Daltiarna exports people—organizers, negotiators, Speakers—more than goods.

---

## **VI. Faith & Culture**

Faith in Daltiarna is **plural and unregulated**.

- Allemantic hearth rites
    
- Beastfolk ancestral practices
    
- Broken World philosophies and survival cults
    

Religion is treated as a private belief with public consequences. No faith holds authority without consent.

> _“Believe what you will,”_  
> _“but don’t pretend it doesn’t affect your neighbors.”_

---

## **VII. Law & Defense**

Daltiarna maintains no standing guard or army.

- Law is enforced socially
    
- Repeated endangerment leads to ostracism or expulsion
    
- Defense relies on numbers, terrain, and coordination
    

Order is maintained not by fear, but by **participation**.

---

## **VIII. Relationships to the Labyrinths**

Delvers are common in Daltiarna, drawn by its tolerance and autonomy.

- The MDO maintains liaison offices only
    
- No centralized delver regulation exists
    
- Districts may restrict or support delving individually
    

Labyrinth matters are debated endlessly—and resolved unevenly.

## **X. Cultural Identity & Legacy**

Daltiarna is exhausting by design.

It demands voices, time, and compromise. Yet for the displaced, the unknown, and the unwilling to kneel, it represents the rare place where survival does not require submission.

The MDO classifies Daltiarna as **politically volatile, socially resilient, and resistant to capture**.

> _“Daltiarna does not ask who you were,”_  
> _“only who is willing to argue beside you.”_

---

## **XI. Quirky Natures**

- Silence during debate is considered a vote
    
- District boundaries change faster than maps can update
    
- Children learn argument before arithmetic
    
- It is rude to end a discussion early, even if you agree
