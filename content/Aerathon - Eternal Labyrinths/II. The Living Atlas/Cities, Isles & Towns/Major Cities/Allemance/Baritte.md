## **I. Overview**

**Baritte** is the **trade and transport capital of Allemance** and the largest crossroads city in Aerathon. It sits at the convergence of four continental arteries—the **Lantern Road** (to Louvain), **Iron Trail** (to Oria), **Spice Road** (to Arneria), and **Glassway** (to Vinyot)—making it the logistical heart of the known world.

Baritte is not a city of rest. It is a city of **motion**: wagons, caravans, pilgrims, delvers, and couriers in perpetual circulation. To live here is to accept that stillness is temporary.

**Population:** ~1.1 million  
**Dominant Peoples:** Humans, Halflings, Dwarves, Leonine & Canine Beastfolk  
**Regional Symbol:** A wheel encircled by four crossed roads  
**Civic Motto:** _“All roads burn the same underfoot.”_

---

## **II. Geography & Architecture**

Baritte is a **circular city**, divided into four vast quarters aligned with the roads they serve, surrounding a central civic core.

|Quarter|Orientation|Description|
|---|---|---|
|**Brass Quarter**|South (Louvain)|Polished bronze facades; Ministry offices; Delver Registry|
|**Dust Quarter**|East (Arneria)|Desert architecture; spice markets; ritual goods|
|**Iron Quarter**|North (Oria)|Forges, workhouses, thundersteel trade|
|**Glass Quarter**|West (Vinyot)|Auctions, banking houses, guild contracts|

At the center lies **The Wayheart**, a circular plaza marking what cartographers insist is Aerathon’s geographic midpoint. At its heart burns the **Traveler’s Flame**, a relic brazier believed to guide the lost through storm and labyrinth alike.

---

## **III. History**

Baritte began as a **pilgrim rest stop** during the Reconstruction following the Age of Collapse. Nomadic merchants and Beastfolk traders raised the **First Waystone**, promising safety to travelers who offered bread, coin, and good faith.

Over centuries, the city expanded in concentric rings until its original heart became a rumor whispered by caravans. By the **Second Concord**, Baritte was recognized as the midpoint for Ministry convoys—**Aerathon’s circulatory system** for trade, goods, and information. Neutrality became law, and movement became sacred.

**Key Historical Events:**

- **387 A.D.** — Establishment of the **Lantern Road** between Louvain and Baritte, creating the first protected trade artery.
    
- **642 A.D.** — Construction of the original **Wayheart Monument**, consecrating Baritte as sacred ground for travelers.
    
- **1481 A.D.** — Formal declaration of Baritte as the **Neutral City**; no guild, kingdom, or noble house may claim it.
    
- **2319 A.D.** — Ratification of the Ministry’s **Transit Charter**, centralizing archives, convoy scheduling, and continental logistics in Baritte.
    

Baritte endures as a city built not for walls or war, but for **safe passage and remembered footsteps**.

---

## **IV. Governance**

Baritte is governed by the **Council of Roads**, an elected council of five:

- One representative for each major route
    
- One representing the city proper
    

Councilors are chosen from guildmasters, caravan leaders, and navigators. No individual may hold the same chair twice in a lifetime, ensuring power never stagnates.

The MDO maintains a permanent **Transit Bureau**, overseen by the **Marshal of Roads**—currently **Halra Venn**, a leonine veteran known for enforcing neutrality with equal parts charm and claw.

---

## **V. Economy**

Everything that moves passes through Baritte.

**Primary Exports:**

- Logistics contracts
    
- Relic documentation
    
- Trade licensing
    
- Caravan security
    

**Primary Imports:**

- Everything. Literally.
    

**Major Institutions:**

- **Wayheart Exchange** — Global trade floor for relics and transport goods
    
- **Guild of Tides and Wheels** — Oversees transportation guild charters
    
- **Concord Ledger** — Ministry-supervised vault of cross-regional economic data
    
- **The Thousand Tents** — A market that rebuilds itself differently every dawn
    

The **Hourbell**, a massive timekeeping relic, rings hourly to synchronize transactions citywide.

---

## **VI. Faith & Culture**

Faith in Baritte is practical and plural.

- Major temples to **Fharlanghn**, **Avandra**, and **Pelor**
    
- Countless roadside shrines maintained by guilds and caravans
    
- Prayers focus on safe passage, not salvation
    

Festivals are constant. The **Festival of First Steps** marks the cycle opening, when thousands carry lanterns from the Wayheart outward along the roads.

Common sayings include:

- _“Hospitality is the first trade.”_
    
- _“A liar can’t cross Baritte twice.”_
    

---

## **VII. Law & Defense**

Baritte’s greatest defense is **neutrality**.

- Enforced by the **Transit Charter**
    
- Policed by the **Roadguard**, a multi-racial militia
    
- Command shared between the Marshal and rotating guild captains
    

Criminals may be branded with **Traveler’s Ink**, a glowing mark preventing them from crossing city borders without sanction. In emergencies, embedded relic sigils allow streets to **reconfigure**, sealing quarters into defensive rings.

---

## **VIII. Relationships to the Labyrinths**

Many labyrinth entrances lie along the trade routes radiating from Baritte, making it a premier staging ground for delvers and supply convoys.

The **Traveler’s Flame** is believed to resonate faintly with labyrinthic disturbances and is used by the MDO as a **surface resonance monitor**.

Several guilds maintain chapterhouses here, including the **Cinderpaw Pact**, **Argent Banner**, and **Iron-Bough Syndicate**.

Baritte shapes the continent by deciding **how fast** it moves.

---

## **X. Cultural Identity & Legacy**

Baritte is the one city no one owns.

Its neutrality is sacred, its pace unending, its hospitality unmatched. Every delver passes through it, every merchant returns to it, and every pilgrim’s ashes eventually cross its roads.

> _“Baritte doesn’t remember names—only footsteps.”_

To the MDO, it is **Aerathon’s pulse**.

---

## **XI. Quirky Natures**

- Locals can give directions using only road names, never landmarks
    
- Some swear the Traveler’s Flame burns brighter before major upheaval
    
- Inns compete by guaranteeing silence, not comfort
    
- It is considered rude to ask where someone is going—only where they’ve been
