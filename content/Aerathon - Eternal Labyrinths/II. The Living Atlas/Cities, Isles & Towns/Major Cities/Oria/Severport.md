## **I. Overview**

**Severport** is a fortified Oric lodge city on the **Malenkean Sea**, renowned for maintaining the finest military sailors in Oria. Its massive icebreaking warships keep northern waters navigable long after lesser ports are sealed by winter, allowing surrounding lodge houses to fish, trade, and survive.

The city is ruled by **House Severinhald**, an elk house whose authority is built on endurance, discipline, and refusal of dependency. Severport accepts **no tributes from Jegervalt**, a stance reiterated publicly at every Housemeet as a declaration of self-sufficiency.

Severport carries two burdens without complaint:

1. Maintaining the Oric northern navy
    
2. Sustaining a city in one of Aerathon’s harshest climates
    

**Population:** ~21,000  
**City Type:** Fortified Lodge Port / Naval Stronghold  
**Primary Renown:** Icebreaking fleets, naval discipline  
**Naval Maxim:** _“The sea yields or we make it.”_

## **II. Geography & Architecture**

Severport is built along a jagged northern coast where pack ice presses relentlessly against stone.

### **The Harbor**

- Reinforced breakwaters of layered stone and iron
    
- Heated docklines fed by internal forges
    
- Deep-water slips designed for massive hulls
    

The harbor never fully freezes—by design.

### **The Lodge-Fort**

The central lodge house doubles as a naval bastion:

- Sloped stone walls to shed ice buildup
    
- Low, wide structures to resist wind shear
    
- Observation towers facing open water
    

Buildings prioritize strength over comfort. Ornamentation is rare.

## **III. History**

Severport was founded as a necessity of survival.

Early northern Oric settlements failed repeatedly, undone by winter seas that cut off fishing and resupply. House Severinhald emerged as the first to solve the problem—not by avoiding the ice, but by **breaking it**.

Their early ice-cutting vessels evolved into the colossal ships Severport now fields, capable of forcing passage through frozen seas weeks before and after the safe season.

Over time, Severport became the backbone of Oria’s northern maritime defense. Its refusal of Jegervalt’s tributes hardened into tradition after a bitter Housemeet debate, where Severinhald declared reliance a greater threat than hunger.

### **Key Historical Events**

- **1196 A.D.** — Launch of the first successful Oric icebreaker.
    
- **1632 A.D.** — Severport formally designated Oria’s northern naval seat.
    
- **2418 A.D.** — Refusal of Jegervalt tribute ratified as permanent House policy.
    
- **3149 A.D.** — Significant fleet losses linked to increased Labyrinth activity.
    

Severport has never asked for help.  
That may soon change.

## **IV. Governance**

Severport is governed by **House Severport**, whose stewardship is held by the **Severinhald family**, an Oric House whose authority is inseparable from the city’s role as the northern shield of Oria.

Governance in Severport mirrors **naval command structure**, reflecting the realities of ice, isolation, and maritime survival.

- The **High Admiral-Batko of House Severport** (traditionally of the Severinhald line) serves as both city ruler and supreme naval commander
    
- During the winter months, **fleet captains** are granted expanded civic authority, empowered to requisition labor, redirect resources, and enforce emergency measures as conditions demand
    
- **Civil councils** exist to manage daily life during fair seasons but defer immediately to naval priority during storms, icebreak operations, or external threats
    

Authority in Severport is **absolute when survival is at stake**. Debate is permitted in summer. In winter, orders are followed without delay.

Succession within House Severport favors proven commanders—those who have held the line when the Malenkean froze and others turned back.

## **V. Economy**

Severport’s economy is tightly constrained by climate.

**Primary Sectors**

- Naval construction and maintenance
    
- Cold-sea fishing and preservation
    
- Maritime training and service
    
- Icebreaker escort contracts
    

Luxury goods are nearly nonexistent. Everything imported must justify its weight.

---

## **VI. Faith & Culture**

Severport’s culture is austere and communal.

- Seasonal rites honor endurance, not victory
    
- Shrines are built into ship keels and harbor stones
    
- Faith emphasizes survival through discipline
    

Smallfolk—especially descendants of **Vinyotian laetines**—are deeply integrated into Severport’s identity. Ferret-run eateries, low-ceilinged workshops, and compact ship quarters are common throughout the city.

Several years ago, the **first otter admiral** was appointed, marking a cultural shift that Severport embraced without ceremony.

> _“The sea does not care what you are.”_

---

## **VII. Law & Defense**

Severport is perpetually on alert.

- Mandatory naval service rotations
    
- Harbor patrols year-round
    
- Swift justice for dereliction of duty
    

Defense is the city’s primary function. Civil law exists to support it.

---

## **VIII. Relationships to the Labyrinths**

The Labyrinths have become Severport’s greatest unseen enemy.

- Fleet losses attributed to Labyrinth-linked sea anomalies
    
- Increasing monster sightings beneath the ice
    
- Skilled sailors abandoning naval duty for delving
    

Piracy has risen in the north as patrol coverage thins, compounding the danger.

Severport faces a reckoning: adapt its traditions—or be outpaced by threats rising from below.

Severport defines honor as endurance without complaint.

Its people do not boast. They do not petition. They break ice so others may eat.
