## **I. Overview**

**Edelstann** is a fortified port city of **Oria**, positioned at the volatile border between the **Tiger Sea** and the **First Sea**. Once a desperate bulwark during the **Mantle War**, it has since transformed into a booming maritime hub and the eastern anchor of the **Diamondgale Shipping Lane**.

To Oric peoples, Edelstann is more than a city—it is **proof**. Proof that unity among the batkos could break a crown’s advance. Proof that Oria could hold when pressed to the edge of the sea.

To merchants, it is opportunity.  
To some veterans, it is a wound covered in gold.

**Population:** ~64,000 (rapid growth)  
**City Type:** Fortified Port City / Trade Hub  
**Primary Renown:** Mantle War legacy, Diamondgale shipping  
**Civic Motto:** _“Stone Holds. Wind Carries.”_

---

## **II. Geography & Architecture**

Edelstann’s harbor opens into a natural bowl protected by jagged coastal stone and reinforced sea walls.

### **The Lodge House**

At the city’s heart stands the **Great Lodge**, a massive elk-built structure of timber and stone:

- Serves as civic hall, ancestral record, and command seat
    
- Walls etched with victories of the Mantle War
    
- Upper balconies overlook both harbor and city
    

### **The Expanding Port**

Warehouses, dry docks, and counting houses press outward each year:

- Ships from **four homelands** dock regularly
    
- New stone quays extend into deeper water
    
- Older districts now sit landlocked, buried behind commerce
    

Architecture is layered—old war-stone at the base, new gold-trimmed trade halls above.

---

## **III. History**

Edelstann’s name entered history during fire.

At the height of the **Mantle War**, Allemagnian forces targeted the port as a decisive foothold between seas. Warned by mountain scouts, the **batkos of the Vanlig region** united under a single banner—something Oria had rarely achieved before.

The resulting battle was swift, brutal, and final. Edelstann became the site of the **first decisive Oric victory** against the crown’s advance, breaking momentum and reshaping the war’s outcome.

In the century since, victory hardened into legend, and legend into identity. Trade followed peace. Peace followed profit.

### **Key Historical Events**

- **3071 A.D.** — **Battle of Edelstann**, the Vanlig Union repels Allemagnian invasion.
    
- **3092 A.D.** — Formal recognition of Edelstann as a **free Oric port city**.
    
- **3128 A.D.** — Establishment of the **Diamondgale Shipping Lane** around Giant’s Foot Bay.
    
- **3156 A.D.** — Edelstann surpasses all other Oric ports in annual trade volume.
    

Edelstann won the war—then learned to survive peace.

## **IV. Governance**

Edelstann is governed by **House Edelstann**, an Oric lodge-house whose leadership is held by the **Vanlig family**, descendants of the batkos who united the region during the Mantle War.

While outsiders often conflate the House with the family that leads it, Orians draw a clear distinction: **the House endures; families are entrusted with stewardship**.

- The **High Lodge-Bearer of House Edelstann** (currently of the Vanlig line) presides from the Great Lodge
    
- A **council of batko representatives** drawn from the city’s districts and warrior lineages advises on civic and military matters
    
- **Port authorities and naval captains** answer directly to the Lodge, reflecting Edelstann’s strategic maritime role
    

House Edelstann governs through **recognized merit and historic trust**, not inherited mandate. Loyalty to the Vanlig leadership is strong—but, as with all Oric Houses, it is **earned continually** through protection, provision, and restraint.

## **V. Economy**

Edelstann’s economy surges year by year.

**Primary Sectors**

- Maritime trade and shipping fees
    
- Warehousing and bonded storage
    
- Ship repair and provisioning
    
- Precious metals and bulk goods transit
    

The city is the **eastern gate** of the Diamondgale Lane, connecting:

- Giant’s Foot Bay
    
- The Tresoli Islands
    
- The Vinyotian port of **Arloris**
    

Gold flows in faster than memory can keep pace.

---

## **VI. Faith & Culture**

Edelstann’s culture is split between **reverence and ambition**.

- War memorials stand beside counting houses
    
- Ancestral rites are observed—but quietly
    
- Younger generations value prosperity over remembrance
    

Some elder elk warn that monuments to Oric heroes are being **buried beneath warehouses**, both literally and symbolically.

> _“Gold does not remember who bled for it.”_

---

## **VII. Law & Defense**

Edelstann maintains strong but selective defenses.

- Harbor watch and sea patrols are well funded
    
- Lodge guards protect civic centers
    
- Civil law favors stability over retribution
    

Veterans still train the watch—but fewer each year.

---

## **VIII. Relationships to the Labyrinths**

Edelstann keeps the Labyrinths at arm’s length.

- No known entrances within city bounds
    
- Artifacts passing through port are logged and taxed
    
- Delvers frequent the docks but rarely linger
    

The city prefers dangers that arrive by sea.

## **X. Cultural Identity & Legacy**

Edelstann stands on a contradiction.

It is a city born of unity that now tests how much unity wealth can buy. Its people have not forgotten the war—but they argue about what remembrance should cost.

> _“We held once.”_  
> _“Holding forever is harder.”_
