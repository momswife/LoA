## **I. Overview**

**Jegervalt** is the largest Oric lodge city in Aerathon, both in population and physical scale. It stands at the **western mouth of the Grensa Tunnel**, the only reliable passage through the Grensa Mountains that does not demand weeks of lethal cliff travel.

To pass through Oria safely, one must pass **under Jegervalt**.

The city is not merely adjacent to the tunnel—it **is** the gatehouse. Two immense towers flank the tunnel entrance, connected by a massive skyway structure suspended directly over the arch carved into **Mount Roet**. Together, these three structures house nearly the entire population of the city and frame the mountain passage like a deliberately clenched jaw.

Jegervalt is known among the Oric as the **House of Houses**—a city built from hundreds of smaller lodges, layered, stacked, and interdependent.

**Population:** ~20,000  
**City Type:** Lodge Metropolis / Mountain Gatehouse  
**Primary Renown:** Grensa Tunnel control, layered urban engineering  
**Local Saying:** _“Stone holds because we do.”_

---

## **II. Geography & Architecture**

Jegervalt is both **carved and constructed**.

Rather than building outward, its founders hollowed Mount Roet itself, embedding the city into the stone while preserving massive veins of **Summerstone**, a luminous mineral that provides heat and light.

### **The Gatehouse Structure**

- **West Tower & East Tower:** 15 primary layers each
    
- **Skyway Lodge:** Suspended civic and administrative structure
    
- **Tunnel Arch:** Reinforced stone passage beneath the city
    

The towers’ outer gates rise twenty feet high, opening into cavernous ground levels that feel less like interiors and more like valleys roofed in stone.

---

## **III. History**

Jegervalt began as a necessity, not a vision.

The Grensa Mountains long isolated Oria’s western reaches, forcing travelers into deadly alpine routes. Early Oric attempts at tunneling failed—until the discovery of Summerstone veins that could stabilize internal climates.

House Roetvald emerged as the primary steward of the project, committing generations to carving the mountain safely. When the tunnel was finally breached, the lodge that guarded it grew upward, outward, and inward simultaneously.

As wars, migrations, and Labyrinth disruptions displaced populations, Jegervalt absorbed refugees without expanding its footprint—stacking lives vertically instead of spreading horizontally.

### **Key Historical Events**

- **971 A.D.** — First successful Summerstone stabilization within Mount Roet.
    
- **1134 A.D.** — Completion of the Grensa Tunnel; Jegervalt formalized as gatehouse lodge.
    
- **1842 A.D.** — Major refugee influx following regional conflicts; creation of the **Triple Layers**.
    
- **3099 A.D.** — Structural reinforcement of skyway after seismic anomalies linked to Labyrinth shifts.
    

Jegervalt has never fallen.  
The mountain does not allow it.

## **IV. Governance**

Jegervalt is governed by **House Jegervalt**, whose stewardship is held by the **Roetvald family**. The House’s authority is inseparable from its ancient duty as **Tunnel Wardens** of Mount Roet—the single most critical transit artery in Oria.

In Jegervalt, governance is not political in nature.  
It is **operational**.

- The **High Gatewarden of House Jegervalt** (traditionally of the Roetvald line) commands tunnel operations, oversees civic stability, and coordinates inter-House passage
    
- **Layer Councils**, drawn from senior residents and specialists within each vertical level, manage daily life, resource distribution, and internal disputes
    
- During tunnel instability, siege, or structural threat, **emergency authority centralizes instantly** under the High Gatewarden without appeal
    

The failure of House Jegervalt would not mean the fall of a city—it would mean **continental paralysis**. This reality grants the House extraordinary deference at the Housemeet and absolute authority within the gate.

Succession within House Jegervalt is conservative, deliberate, and closely scrutinized. No High Gatewarden is named unless the House is convinced they will hold when the mountain moves.

## **V. Economy**

Jegervalt’s economy is utilitarian and constant.

**Primary Sectors**

- Tunnel tolls and transit regulation
    
- Metalworking, stonecraft, and tanning
    
- Engineering apprenticeships
    
- Caravan provisioning
    

Trade pauses nowhere in Jegervalt—it simply flows through.

---

## **VI. Faith & Culture**

Faith in Jegervalt is pragmatic.

- Shrines are embedded into load-bearing walls
    
- Seasonal rites focus on endurance and repair
    
- The mountain itself is treated as a witness, not a god
    

Culturally, the Oric concept of **collective survival** is strongest here. Success is measured by how many people pass through safely—not by who stands tallest.

---

## **VII. Law & Defense**

Jegervalt is among the most defensible cities in Aerathon.

- Controlled access at every level
    
- Gate guards trained for siege and collapse scenarios
    
- Tunnel lockdown protocols rehearsed monthly
    

Crime is rare. Disruption is not tolerated.

---

## **VIII. Relationships to the Labyrinths**

Jegervalt maintains strict neutrality toward the Labyrinths.

- No entrances within city bounds
    
- Artifacts passing through the tunnel are inspected
    
- Delvers allowed passage but monitored
    

The city is already burdened with one existential risk: collapse.

## **X. Cultural Identity & Legacy**

Jegervalt’s people do not romanticize their city.

They live inside a mountain so others do not have to die crossing it.

> _“We carry the road.”_
