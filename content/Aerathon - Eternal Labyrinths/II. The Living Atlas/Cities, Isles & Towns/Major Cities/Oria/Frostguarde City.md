## **I. Overview**

**Frostguarde City** stands at the **southernmost survivable edge of the Nattefrost**, where permanent settlement gives way to frozen plains, auroral distortion, and temporal instability. It is neither fortress nor capital, but a **threshold city**—the last place in Aerathon where warmth, memory, and time still behave _mostly_ as expected.

The city is formally governed by **House Kaldryn**, an ancient Oric elk house whose lineage has long specialized in northern survival, ward maintenance, and cold-region stewardship. Their authority here is not absolute; it exists in constant negotiation with the **Ministry of Delving Operations**, whose presence is foundational to Frostguarde’s purpose.

Among travelers and scholars alike, Frostguarde is known as  
**“The Lantern at the End of the Road.”**

**Population:** ~18,000 permanent  
**City Type:** Research Threshold City  
**Primary Renown:** Cryo-temporal study, Nattefrost monitoring

---

## **II. Geography & Climate**

Frostguarde is built atop a **reinforced basalt shelf**, overlooking the southern approaches to the Nattefrost frostplain.

- **Region:** Extreme northern Oria
    
- **Surroundings:** Permafrost fields, frozen fjords, auroral skybands
    
- **Average Temperature:** −20°C (artificially stabilized)
    

Beyond the city’s wardline, conditions degrade rapidly.  
**Temporal Frost** and **Slow Snow** phenomena have been recorded just outside the perimeter, though historically never within—an assumption now under active review.

Without layered ward infrastructure maintained by House Kaldryn and the MDO, Frostguarde would become uninhabitable within hours.

---

## **III. History**

Frostguarde was established after early Oric expeditions proved that **traditional forge-cities could not survive** this far north without catastrophic loss. Rather than conquest, the Oric proposed endurance.

**House Kaldryn** was instrumental in this shift. Already known for maintaining remote watch-lodges along Oria’s northern edges, they offered stewardship rather than dominion—agreeing to rule Frostguarde only under strict non-expansionary doctrine.

The city’s founding charter outlined three immutable directives:

1. **Observe the Nattefrost without intrusion**
    
2. **Preserve knowledge, not territory**
    
3. **Ensure the continuity and warmth of its people**
    

### **Key Historical Events**

- **3021 A.D.** — Oric reconnaissance led by House Kaldryn confirms permanent settlement infeasible without ward networks.
    
- **3068 A.D.** — Frostguarde formally founded as a joint **House Kaldryn–MDO initiative**.
    
- **3114 A.D.** — First recorded Slow Snow event at the outer perimeter.
    
- **3152 A.D.** — Cryo-temporal anomaly spike prompts reclassification to Grade-II authenticity.
    

Frostguarde has never grown outward.  
It has only grown **denser, warmer, and more watchful**.

---

## **IV. Governance**

Frostguarde is governed by **House of Frostguarde**, a lodge-house formed not by lineage alone, but by mandate. Stewardship of the House rests with the **Stonewarden family**, whose members have served continuously on the northern watch since the city’s founding.

Unlike other Oric Houses, Frostguarde exists under **formal dual authority**, a structure born of necessity rather than compromise.

- **House of Frostguarde (Stonewarden Line)** — maintains civic order, allocates labor, oversees fortifications, and safeguards the Emberward systems that keep the city habitable
    
- **Ministry of Delving Operations** — embedded authority responsible for labyrinth research, anomaly surveillance, evacuation doctrine, and existential threat assessment
    

The head of the House appoints a **Warden-Regent**, drawn from the Stonewarden family or a proven successor line. This position is **strictly limited to a ten-year term**, after which the bearer is relieved of duty—regardless of success—to prevent psychological erosion from prolonged exposure to the Nattefrost and nearby labyrinth pressure.

Governance in Frostguarde is deliberately constrained:

- No noble expansion
    
- No dynastic estates
    
- No population growth initiatives
    
- No political accumulation beyond immediate survival needs

---

## **V. Economy**

Frostguarde produces almost nothing tangible.

Its economy survives through **knowledge mediation**:

- Cryo-temporal research
    
- Artifact stabilization and containment
    
- Ministry-contracted analysis
    
- Historical reconstruction from frozen ruins
    

> _“Frostguarde exports answers, not ore.”_

Secondary activity includes:

- Delver staging and resupply
    
- Pilgrimage and sanctioned observation
    
- Cold-forge services adapted from Oric craft traditions
    

---

## **VI. Faith & Culture**

Culture in Frostguarde is **ritualized stability**.

- Fixed meal schedules
    
- Bells marking time precisely
    
- Monthly public recitation of the names of the dead
    

House Kaldryn enforces grounding traditions rigorously, especially for children:

> _“Say where you are. Say who you are. Say it every day.”_

### **The Concordant Spire**

A circular cathedral dedicated to **all recognized gods**, deliberately constructed without a dominant altar.

- Rotating prayer hours
    
- Joint clerical maintenance
    
- Serves as a psychological stabilizer against Frostmadness
    

Faith here is not about miracles—it is about **continuity**.

---

## **VII. Law & Defense**

Frostguarde’s defenses are **intentionally constrained**.

- **North Barracks:** 200–300 guards trained for evacuation and civil defense
    
- No standing army, no siege capability
    
- Expansion petitions universally denied
    

Law prioritizes **order, routine, and calm**. Panic is classified as a civic hazard.

House Kaldryn guards are trained as **ward-maintainers first, soldiers second**.

---

## **VIII. Relationships to the Labyrinths**

Frostguarde maintains strict separation from the Labyrinths.

- No known entrances within city bounds
    
- Artifacts quarantined in de-icing vaults
    
- Delvers subject to authorization and quarantine
    

The city already watches one existential threat.  
It will not invite another.

## **X. Cultural Identity & Legacy**

Frostguarde exists so others do not have to.

It does not conquer the north.  
It does not sanctify it.  
It **records it**, patiently and without illusion.

House Kaldryn’s legacy here is not glory—but restraint.

> _“The cold does not hate us.”_  
> _“It does not notice us at all.”_
