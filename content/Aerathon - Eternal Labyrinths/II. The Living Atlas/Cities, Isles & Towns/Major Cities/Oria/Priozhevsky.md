## **I. Overview**

**Priozhevsky** is an Oric lodge city built at the **headwaters of the Shepka River**, where it springs fully formed from the vast alpine lake known as **Mountmirror**. The city is as much **library as settlement**, serving as Oria’s foremost center of alchemical, arcane, and medical scholarship.

Governed by **House Medvednaya**, an ursine house long associated with learning, healing, and record-keeping, Priozhevsky represents Oria’s belief that strength must be supported by understanding. The city’s influence flows outward like its river—quietly, steadily, and indispensably.

Among Oric scholars, Priozhevsky is simply called  
**“The Source.”**

**Population:** ~13,000 (seasonally variable)  
**City Type:** Lodge City / Academic Nexus  
**Primary Renown:** Alchemy, medicine, applied arcana  
**Scholastic Maxim:** _“Knowledge Is Load-Bearing.”_

---

## **II. Geography & Architecture**

Priozhevsky is built directly into the stone overlooking **Mountmirror**, a cold, reflective lake whose surface feeds the Shepka River.

### **The Lake & Riverhead**

- Crystal-clear waters, fed by mountain melt
    
- Carefully controlled spillways channel river flow
    
- Alchemical runoff strictly regulated
    

The lake itself is treated as a protected scholarly resource.

### **The Lodge-Library**

The central structure is both residence and archive:

- Tiered stacks carved into stone walls
    
- Study halls warmed by Summerstone veins
    
- Dormitory wings integrated into archive levels
    

The architecture favors permanence and silence. Footsteps are muffled by design.

## **III. History**

Priozhevsky began as a **record lodge**, founded to preserve Oric knowledge that was at risk of being lost during periods of migration and conflict. Its location—remote, defensible, and nourished by steady water—was chosen deliberately.

Over centuries, the lodge attracted alchemists, physicians, and mages who valued practicality over flourish. While Louvain’s **Library of the Gate** became known for philosophy, poetry, and speculative arcana, Priozhevsky’s stacks filled with **tested methods, failures recorded honestly, and results repeated until reliable**.

In recent decades, the rise of delving has brought a new class of scholar to Priozhevsky: those seeking applied solutions to Labyrinth-borne ailments, curses, and injuries.

### **Key Historical Events**

- **982 A.D.** — Founding of Priozhevsky as a record lodge at Mountmirror.
    
- **1417 A.D.** — Formal establishment of Oria’s first unified **alchemical curriculum**.
    
- **2093 A.D.** — Expansion of medical wings following major Causeway conflicts.
    
- **3141 A.D.** — Increase in delver-sponsored research into Labyrinth afflictions.
    

Priozhevsky has never sought prominence.  
It has never lacked relevance.

## **IV. Governance**

Priozhevsky is governed by **House Priozhevsky**, whose stewardship is held by the **Medvednaya family**, an Oric ursine House renowned not for conquest, but for preservation of knowledge and care of the body.

Authority within the city is rooted in **stewardship rather than command**. Governance exists to protect continuity—of learning, of health, and of historical truth.

- The **High Curator of House Priozhevsky** (traditionally of the Medvednaya line) oversees the city’s archives, scholastic charters, and long-term civic policy
    
- **Scholastic Councils**, composed of senior researchers, physicians, and historians, govern research ethics, accreditation, and instructional standards
    
- All **external funding, academic partnerships, and delver-sponsored research** require formal approval by the Curatorial Office
    

The city’s current batko, **Oleg the Bold Doctor**, serves as **High Curator**. A retired arena champion and veteran of the **Arnerian Storied Histories League**, Oleg’s body bears the cost of decades of combat, but his mind remains formidable. He now directs Priozhevsky’s medical research efforts, specializing in trauma recovery, chronic injury treatment, and the long-term physical toll of delving.

## **V. Economy**

Priozhevsky’s economy is cyclical and knowledge-driven.

**Primary Supports**

- Tuition and lodging for students
    
- Visiting scholar residencies
    
- Medical consultation and research contracts
    
- Modest riverland agriculture
    

When resident students depart for seasonal fieldwork, their homes are rented to visiting academics, delvers, and archivists—keeping the city solvent year-round.

---

## **VI. Faith & Culture**

Faith in Priozhevsky is quiet and methodical.

- Shrines double as meditation rooms
    
- Study is considered an act of devotion
    
- Failure is recorded, not hidden
    

Culturally, Oric pragmatism dominates. Poetry exists, but only if it teaches something useful.

> _“If it cannot be applied, it must at least be understood.”_

---

## **VII. Law & Defense**

Law in Priozhevsky prioritizes **preservation**.

- Archive damage is a severe crime
    
- Unauthorized experiments are harshly punished
    
- Defense focuses on containment, not confrontation
    

Scholars are expected to police themselves. When they fail, House Medvednaya intervenes decisively.

---

## **VIII. Relationships to the Labyrinths**

Priozhevsky has become increasingly tied to the Labyrinths.

- Hosts research on Labyrinth-induced conditions
    
- Maintains sealed archives of dangerous findings
    
- Delvers frequently lodge during treatment or study
    

The city does not delve—but it prepares others to survive it.

## **X. Cultural Identity & Legacy**

Priozhevsky believes knowledge is a responsibility.

It does not chase glory. It does not dramatize discovery. It records, tests, and preserves—so others may act without guessing.

> _“The river flows because the source is kept clean.”_
