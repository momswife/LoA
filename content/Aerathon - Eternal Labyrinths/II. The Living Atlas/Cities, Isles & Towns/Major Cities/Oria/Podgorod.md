## **I. Overview**

**Podgorod** is a fully subterranean Oric lodge city carved entirely within **frozen mountain rock**, renowned as Aerathon’s foremost center of underground engineering, mechanized labor, and cold-climate agriculture.

Unlike most lodge houses that rely on Summerstone-lit tunnels branching outward, Podgorod is **entirely enclosed**, descending through layers of worked stone, ice-veined caverns, and machine-lined corridors. Its people—predominantly ligonine moles—do not consider this a hardship, but an ideal.

The city is ruled by **House Bronzemolev**, a ligonine Oric house whose authority is rooted in mastery of systems: water, motion, pressure, and repair. In Podgorod, governance is not about command—it is about **keeping everything running**.

**Population:** ~17,000  
**City Type:** Subterranean Lodge City / Industrial Nexus  
**Primary Renown:** Underground agriculture, mechanized infrastructure, steam innovation  
**Local Maxim:** _“Stillness Is Death.”_

## **II. Geography & Architecture**

Podgorod exists entirely below the mountain’s surface.

### **The Deep Corridors**

- Excavated through frozen stone rather than soil
    
- Reinforced with brass ribs, iron struts, and stone arches
    
- Constant low vibration from moving systems
    

Brass gears, pistons, and rotating shafts are omnipresent. Their sound—whirring, clicking, pulsing—is the city’s heartbeat.

### **Vertical Design**

Podgorod descends rather than spreads:

- Upper layers: intake shafts, ventilation, and soot stacks
    
- Mid-layers: residences, workshops, and transit hubs
    
- Deep layers: agriculture caverns, waterworks, and engine halls
    

Light is provided by:

- Summerstone in critical corridors
    
- Antler charms glowing with refined mushroom oil
    
- Arcane orbs over communal spaces

## **III. History**

Podgorod was founded by necessity and stubbornness.

Early Oric settlers avoided frozen rock, favoring warmer stone or surface lodges. Ligonine engineers argued otherwise, claiming that frozen stone—once mastered—was more stable, predictable, and resistant to collapse.

House Bronzemolev led the experiment.

What began as a risky excavation became a revelation. Podgorod pioneered **subterranean agriculture**, cultivating mushrooms, tubers, and nutrient-dense root crops under controlled light and heat. These methods would later spread across Oria, sustaining populations far from arable land.

As mining expanded, so did invention. Waterwheels turned carts. Pistons lifted elevators. Clockwork golems replaced dangerous manual labor. Podgorod became a city that **did not sleep**, because stopping meant failure.

### **Key Historical Events**

- **1121 A.D.** — First successful excavation through frozen mountain strata without structural collapse.
    
- **1453 A.D.** — Development of large-scale subterranean crop cultivation, later adopted across Oria.
    
- **2017 A.D.** — Introduction of clockwork golems for hazardous labor roles.
    
- **3133 A.D.** — Refinement of Podgorod steam engines surpasses surface equivalents in reliability.
    

Podgorod proved that the mountain could be taught to work.

## **IV. Governance**

Podgorod is governed by **House Podgorod**, whose stewardship is held by the **Bronzemolev family**, an Oric house renowned for producing master engineers, mechanists, and subterranean planners. Unlike most Houses, leadership here is not inherited by age or acclaim alone—it is **proven through systems mastery**.

In Podgorod, authority is granted to those who understand how the city survives.

- The **Prime Mechanarch of House Podgorod** (traditionally of the Bronzemolev line) oversees all citywide infrastructure, including power distribution, water flow, ventilation, transit rails, and automated labor
    
- **Layer-Engineers** govern individual districts, each responsible for the mechanical integrity and productivity of their assigned strata
    
- In the event of catastrophic mechanical failure, **emergency authority becomes absolute**, centralizing immediately under the Prime Mechanarch until system stability is restored
    

Leadership transitions occur not through ceremony, challenge, or vote—but through **failure analysis**. A Prime Mechanarch who allows a system to collapse is relieved without debate. A Layer-Engineer whose district fails is replaced before the damage spreads.

## **V. Economy**

Podgorod’s economy is built on **functionality and exportable innovation**.

**Primary Sectors**

- Coal and mineral extraction
    
- Mechanical systems and components
    
- Clockwork golems
    
- Steam engine manufacture
    
- Agricultural techniques and seed stock
    

While many in Aerathon consider steam engines unreliable, **Podgorod engines** are viewed as marvels—well-oiled, precise, and obsessively maintained.

---

## **VI. Faith & Culture**

Podgorod’s culture reveres **process**.

- Shrines are integrated into engine halls
    
- Ritual maintenance precedes festivals
    
- Failure is analyzed publicly
    

Faith here is pragmatic. The gods are acknowledged, but effort is trusted more.

Ligonine traditions emphasize **tinkering as devotion**—to improve a system is to honor those who came before.

---

## **VII. Law & Defense**

Law in Podgorod is strict, technical, and unforgiving of negligence.

- Sabotage is treated as attempted murder
    
- Maintenance neglect carries severe penalties
    
- Defense focuses on internal security and collapse prevention
    

Clockwork golems serve as guards, laborers, and emergency responders.

---

## **VIII. Relationships to the Labyrinths**

Podgorod treats the Labyrinths with extreme caution.

- No known entrances within city bounds
    
- Artifact-powered machinery tightly regulated
    
- Delvers allowed only under escort
    

Uncontrolled variables are unacceptable.

## **X. Cultural Identity & Legacy**

Podgorod believes progress is not loud.

It is measured in smoother lifts, stronger beams, quieter failures. Its legacy is not heroism, but **reliability**—the kind that keeps cities alive through winter and war alike.

> _“The mountain does not forgive guesses.”_
