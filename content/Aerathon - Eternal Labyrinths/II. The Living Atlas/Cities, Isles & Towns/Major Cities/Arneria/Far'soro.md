## **I. Overview**

**Far’soro** is the principal **confluence city of Arneria**, where Bat’yan lowlands, Causeway traffic, inland caravans, and Beylik authority meet without collapsing into chaos. Built astride the **Attamek River**, which flows beneath the city and empties into the harbor below, Far’soro functions as both artery and valve—allowing movement, but enforcing control.

The city is widely regarded as the **purest realization of the Arnerian cultural experiment**: openness without permissiveness, diversity without disorder. Its people are known for their adaptability and forceful personalities, blending eastern discipline with western warmth.

Far’soro welcomes everyone.  
It does not let anyone forget where they are.

**Population:** ~110,000 (highly transient)  
**City Type:** State City / Trade Confluence  
**Primary Renown:** Caravan trade, medicines, regulated passage  
**Civic Maxim:** _“Pass through cleanly, or not at all.”_

---

## **II. Geography & Architecture**

Far’soro rises above the **Attamek River**, its stone foundations straddling channels that feed both irrigation networks and harbor flow.

### **The River City**

- The Attamek runs beneath vaulted stone corridors
    
- Markets and docks sit above controlled spillways
    
- Bridges are treated as checkpoints, not scenery
    

### **The Quilt Fields**

Surrounding Far’soro is a vast patchwork of farmland:

- Irrigated directly from the Attamek
    
- Cultivated with Bat’yan techniques
    
- Capable of feeding the city and its caravans
    

### **Urban Form**

Architecture reflects layered purpose:

- Caravanserai courtyards
    
- Broad roads for wagons and delver teams
    
- Administrative spines leading toward the palace
    

The city is designed to **absorb traffic**, not admire itself.

---

## **III. History**

Far’soro began as a river crossing and never forgot it.

Long before Arneria consolidated its modern borders, traders, pilgrims, and scouts used the Attamek ford as a meeting point between climates and cultures. When the Beylik rose, Far’soro was chosen not for beauty or legacy, but for **control**—a city placed precisely where movement could no longer be ignored.

As trade intensified and the Causeway matured, Far’soro expanded rapidly. Inns multiplied, roads hardened, and infrastructure adapted to increasing volumes of delvers and long-haul caravans. Where other cities chased novelty, Far’soro standardized it.

Under the rule of **Bey Vartan**, the city reached its modern form: open to all, owned by none, and watched carefully.

**Key Historical Events:**

- **c. 1120 A.D.** — Establishment of the **Attamek Crossing**, formalizing the site as a trade and travel hub.
    
- **1647 A.D.** — Construction of early **caravanserai courts**, enabling mass transient populations.
    
- **2411 A.D.** — Far’soro designated a **Beylik State City**, placing it under direct throne oversight.
    
- **2986 A.D.** — Major infrastructure overhaul to accommodate delver caravans and labyrinth logistics.
    

Far’soro did not grow by chance.  
It grew because stopping it would have broken too many roads.

---

## **IV. Governance**

Far’soro is ruled directly by the **Beylik of Arneria**.

- **Bey Vartan** governs from the **Sapphire Palace**
    
- Civic administration answers to palace ministries
    
- No elected mayor or independent council exists
    

Local leaders manage markets and districts, but ultimate authority flows downward from the Bey.

---

## **V. Economy**

Far’soro’s economy is defined by **movement**.

**Primary Sectors**

- Caravan trade and lodging
    
- Delver outfitting and coordination
    
- Rare Bat’yan medicines and alchemical herbs
    
- Jewelry and magic-infused finery from desert routes
    

Goods are rarely produced here—and constantly exchanged.

---

## **VI. Faith & Culture**

Far’soro’s culture is pragmatic and blended.

- Bat’yan adaptability shapes daily rhythms
    
- Beylik tradition enforces order and ritual
    
- Causeway influence brings foreign custom
    

Faith is present but subordinate to civic order. Shrines are permitted, sermons are tolerated, and disruption is not.

> _“Pray if you wish. Move when told.”_

---

## **VII. Law & Defense**

Law in Far’soro is **visible and enforced**.

- **Beylik infantry** patrol in pairs
    
- Soldiers carry **kilij swords** and state colors
    
- Questioning travelers is routine, not hostile
    

While ill-humored, Beylik forces are disciplined. Harassment without cause is punished, making encounters more predictable than in many freer cities.

---

## **VIII. Relationships to the Labyrinths**

Far’soro is deeply connected to delving logistics.

- Serves as a **meeting point** for scouts and teams
    
- Infrastructure designed for large delver caravans
    
- Labyrinth artifacts are regulated but not banned
    

The city does not glorify delvers—but profits from their inevitability.

---

Far’soro shapes Aerathon by deciding **how easily the world may pass through Arneria**.

---

## **X. Cultural Identity & Legacy**

Far’soro is not beloved—but it is respected.

It proves that openness does not require chaos, and that hospitality can exist alongside authority. To pass through Far’soro is to feel the weight of many worlds—and emerge intact.

> _“You will leave changed,”_  
> _“even if only tired.”_

---

## **XI. Quirky Natures**

- Inns advertise by travel time, not comfort
    
- Street food adapts weekly to caravan makeup
    
- Soldiers know more languages than most merchants
    
- No one stays forever—and that’s the point
