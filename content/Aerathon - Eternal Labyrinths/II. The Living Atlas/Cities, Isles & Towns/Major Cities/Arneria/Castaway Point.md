## **I. Overview**

**Castaway Point** is a small, isolated port city on the western fringe of **Arneria**, clinging to a rare calm inlet along the otherwise lethal waters of the **Azur Gulf**. Encircled by violent currents and a dense wall of living coral, the city exists in a place ships are not meant to survive.

Vessels thrown off course—often blamed on shifting winds associated with the **Throne of the Easterlies**—are frequently destroyed against the reefs. Yet the same currents that shatter hulls sometimes carry survivors intact to a narrow beach beneath the city’s cliffs.

Castaway Point is not advertised.  
It is discovered by those who were not meant to die.

**Associated Region:** Arneria (Bat’yan Coast)  
**City Type:** Isolated Port / Rescue Haven  
**Population:** ~6,500  
**Primary Renown:** Maritime rescue, coral navigation  
**Civic Maxim:** _“The sea chose you. Now choose wisely.”_

---

## **II. Geography & Natural Hazards**

Castaway Point is defined by **lethal beauty**.

### **The Coral Wall**

A living ring of vibrant reef surrounds the harbor approach.

- Razor-edged coral capable of breaching reinforced hulls
    
- Growth patterns that shift year to year
    
- Bioluminescence at night, creating false horizons
    

Charts are unreliable. Memory and instinct matter more.

### **The Currents**

Multiple converging flows pull vessels inward.

- Strongest during seasonal wind shifts
    
- Often carry wreckage—and survivors—directly to shore
    
- Sailors insist the currents “decide” who arrives
    

### **The Quiet Beach**

A calm strip of sand beneath the cliffs.

- Natural landing point for castaways
    
- Marked by memorial posts and signal fires
    
- Considered sacred ground
    

No permanent structures are built here.

---

## **III. History**

Castaway Point began as a place people **washed up**, not settled.

Early survivors built shelters to recover before attempting escape. Over time, rescue replaced departure as purpose. When one lived, one stayed—at least long enough to help the next.

Attempts by outside authorities to abandon, exploit, or control the Point failed repeatedly. Ships could not reliably reach it, and those who tried to rule from afar rarely remained.

The city formalized only when it became clear that Castaway Point would not disappear—and did not wish to.

**Key Historical Events:**

- **c. 980 A.D.** — Earliest recorded survivor encampments along the Quiet Beach.
    
- **1214 A.D.** — Emergence of organized rescue crews and signal protocols.
    
- **1872 A.D.** — First recorded Sea Rat vessels successfully navigating the reef intentionally.
    
- **3021 A.D.** — MDO classification as a permanent high-risk coastal settlement.
    

---

## **IV. The Murine of Castaway Point**

Castaway Point is home to a rare and continuous population of **murine ratfolk** who never left Arneria during earlier migrations.

They endured by:

- Building light
    
- Moving fast
    
- Living small
    

Murine citizens are deeply integrated into city life and are among the most skilled reef-readers and sailors. They are not a minority curiosity—they are a cornerstone of survival culture.

---

## **V. Society & Civic Character**

Castaway Point operates on **communal obligation**.

- Rescue is a civic duty
    
- Survivors are sheltered without negotiation
    
- Leaving without contributing is socially condemned
    

Leadership is informal, drawn from veteran sailors, reef-readers, and rescue captains. Coin matters less than reputation.

---

## **VI. The Sea Rats**

The city’s sailors—called **Sea Rats**—are its greatest export.

- Agile mariners piloting light craft inspired by **Al’Ar hullforms**
    
- Capable of navigating paths through coral invisible to outsiders
    
- Serve as rescuers, couriers, and sometimes pirates or delvers
    

Those who leave are still claimed proudly.

---

## **VII. Faith & Worldview**

Faith here is **quiet and transactional**.

- The sea is respected, not worshiped
    
- Survival is treated as responsibility, not blessing
    
- Shrines are small and weather-worn
    

Prayers are promises.

> _“If I live, I will help the next one.”_

---

## **VIII. Economy**

Trade is limited and precise.

**Exports**

- Salvaged goods
    
- Coral-safe navigation knowledge
    
- Skilled sailors
    

**Imports**

- Food staples
    
- Wood and cloth
    
- Replacement tools
    

Trade vessels arrive by invitation—or desperation.

---

## **IX. Delvers, Pirates & the MDO**

Castaway Point produces a disproportionate number of delvers and freebooters.

- No permanent MDO office
    
- Rotating maritime observers only
    
- Incident logs focused on wreck frequency
    

Attempts to regulate Sea Rat activity have consistently failed.

## **XI. Cultural Identity & Legacy**

Castaway Point is not a destination.  
It is an answer.

To the question of whether survival obligates kindness, the city answers without hesitation. The sea may be cruel. The people refuse to be.

> _“The sea takes most things. Castaway Point gives some of them back.”_
