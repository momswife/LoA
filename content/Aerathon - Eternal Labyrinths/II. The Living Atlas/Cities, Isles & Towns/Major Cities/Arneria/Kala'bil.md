## **I. Overview**

**Kala’bil** stands at the **northernmost point of the Causeway’s Shield Ring**, a hexagonal loop of fortification built to halt the advance of the **Blackwild**—an unnatural scar in Aerathon where corruption, undeath, and demonic influence bleed into the living world.

The city is both **fortress and sanctuary**, serving as the **headquarters of the Arnerian Dramphinians** and the primary operational base for the **Church of the Moon Wolf’s** efforts to contain and ultimately seal the Blackwild.

**Population:** ~38,000 (includes permanent military and clergy)  
**City Type:** Fortified Holy City / Frontline Bastion  
**Primary Renown:** Paladin training, Blackwild containment  
**Oath Inscription:** _“If we fail, the forest remembers.”_

---

## **II. Geography & Architecture**

Kala’bil is enclosed within the **Shield Ring**, a massive Causeway fortification whose walls form a defensive hexagon against the encroaching forest.

### **The Shield Ring**

- Elevated ramparts manned at all hours
    
- Integrated watch-towers and sanctified artillery
    
- Ritual anchors embedded in the stone
    

From the walls, the Blackwild can be felt more than seen—its presence a pressure behind the eyes.

### **City Layout**

- **Upper Levels:** Bethels, paladin outposts, ritual chambers
    
- **Inner Grounds:** Training yards, sanctified dueling fields
    
- **Lower City:** Archives, libraries, civilian quarters
    

Stone dominates all construction. Wood is used sparingly and warded heavily.

---

## **III. History**

Kala’bil was founded in response to fear—and refined through endurance.

When the Blackwild’s influence began to creep outward, early patrol forts proved insufficient. The Causeway was reshaped, reinforced, and closed into a defensive loop. Kala’bil was established at the most vulnerable point: where the forest pressed closest and resistance mattered most.

Over centuries, the city evolved from garrison to **theological and historical stronghold**. Knowledge became as vital as steel. The Dramphinians centralized their command here, and the Church of the Moon Wolf turned Kala’bil into both armory and archive.

### **Key Historical Events**

- **1322 A.D.** — Initial fortification of the Shield Ring in response to Blackwild expansion.
    
- **1479 A.D.** — Founding of **Kala’bil** as a permanent paladin garrison.
    
- **1894 A.D.** — Establishment of the **Dramphinian Grand Bethel**, formalizing centralized command.
    
- **2741 A.D.** — Opening of the **Public Sanctified Archives**, allowing civilian access under screening protocols.
    

Kala’bil has never known peace—only readiness.

---

## **IV. Governance**

Kala’bil operates under a **dual authority structure**:

- **Dramphinian Command Council** oversees military and containment operations
    
- **High Bethel of the Moon Wolf** governs spiritual doctrine and ritual research
    

Civil administration exists but remains subordinate to defense priorities.

---

## **V. Economy**

Kala’bil’s economy is functional and inward-facing.

**Primary Sectors**

- Armament forging and maintenance
    
- Arcane research and wardcraft
    
- Scholarly copying and archival preservation
    
- Training contracts and religious endowments
    

Luxury goods are rare. Purpose is currency.

---

## **VI. Faith & Culture**

Faith defines Kala’bil’s rhythm.

- Daily rites reinforce mental and spiritual resilience
    
- Oaths are renewed communally
    
- Failure is mourned, not hidden
    

The **Moon Wolf** is revered not as a distant god, but as an ideal of vigilance, sacrifice, and clarity under pressure.

> _“The hunt never ends. Neither does our watch.”_

---

## **VII. Law & Defense**

Kala’bil enforces the strictest law on the Causeway.

- Curfews near the outer walls
    
- Mandatory screening for Unnature’s influence
    
- Immediate containment of suspected corruption
    

Defense is layered, ritualized, and relentless.

---

## **VIII. Relationships to the Labyrinths**

Kala’bil maintains **active hostility** toward Labyrinth activity.

- No sanctioned entrances within the Shield Ring
    
- Artifacts are quarantined and studied only under heavy ward
    
- Delvers are restricted and monitored
    

The city tolerates curiosity only when it serves containment.

---

## **X. Cultural Identity & Legacy**

Kala’bil is not a city people dream of visiting.  
It is a city people are grateful exists.

Its legacy is measured not in conquest, but in how long the Blackwild has been held at bay.

> _“If the world sleeps, it is because we do not.”_

---

## **XI. Quirky Natures**

- Paladins learn meditation before swordplay
    
- Libraries smell faintly of incense and iron
    
- The forest is never referred to by name inside the walls
    
- Veterans can sense Blackwild shifts before instruments do
