## **I. Overview**

**Kazmak ve Küzmek** is the largest and most vital city along the **Kazmak Gem Road**, the desert caravan network linking the southern Beylik Causeway to the northern mines. It stands at the confluence of law and mercy, where travelers who survive the Sandstair find rest, water, and knowledge in improbable abundance.

The city exists because **water exists where it should not**—drawn endlessly from beneath the desert to form the **Küzmek River**, an artificial aquifer flow that sustains both city and trade routes stretching as far as the **eastern Matansil Sea**.

Kazmak ve Küzmek is not permissive.  
It is generous—on Beylik terms.

**Population:** ~10,000  
**City Type:** Caravanserai Metropolis / Academic City  
**Primary Renown:** Desert hospitality, water engineering, scholarship  
**Travel Maxim:** _“If you are turned back here, it was not by chance.”_

---

## **II. Geography & Architecture**

Kazmak ve Küzmek rises from open desert, its skyline defined not by towers but by **courtyards, wells, and aqueducts**.

### **The Sandstair**

At the city’s southern edge lies the **Sandstair**, the widest descent from the Causeway to ground level.

- Primary checkpoint between Causeway and Gem Road
    
- Heavily fortified and continuously patrolled
    
- Overseen by **Murad the Vast**, an Aubadian mouse general
    

Murad holds extraordinary authority, including the power to **forbid all passage** along the Gem Road except by direct order of **Bey Vartan**.

### **The Küzmek River**

An engineered miracle:

- Fed by deep aquifers
    
- Raised by gravity-powered pumps and well systems
    
- Runs southeast across the desert
    

The city’s layout follows the water, radiating outward in controlled rings.

---

## **III. History**

Kazmak ve Küzmek began as a calculated risk.

Early gem caravans crossing the desert faced unacceptable losses. Wells were unreliable. Routes shifted. The Beylik responded not with expansion, but **engineering**—authorizing a massive aquifer extraction project to anchor trade permanently.

The Küzmek River transformed a temporary caravan stop into a permanent city. Hospitality followed law, and law followed water.

The founding of **Broadgate University** cemented the city’s second purpose: to gather not just wealth, but understanding.

### **Key Historical Events**

- **1193 A.D.** — Establishment of the **Kazmak Gem Road** as a protected trade corridor.
    
- **1368 A.D.** — Completion of the **Küzmek Aquifer System**, creating a permanent desert river.
    
- **1721 A.D.** — Construction of the **Grand Caravanserai**, subsidized by Beylik treasury.
    
- **2465 A.D.** — Broadgate University relocates its **main campus** to Kazmak ve Küzmek.
    

Kazmak ve Küzmek did not conquer the desert.  
It negotiated with it.

---

## **IV. Governance**

The city is governed directly under **Beylik authority**.

- **Murad the Vast** controls access at the Sandstair
    
- A Bey-appointed civic administrator manages internal affairs
    
- Academic and trade institutions operate under charter
    

No elected council exists. Stability is enforced, not debated.

---

## **V. Economy**

Kazmak ve Küzmek thrives on circulation.

**Primary Sectors**

- Caravan lodging and logistics
    
- Gem trade and appraisal
    
- Water engineering and maintenance
    
- Academic services and research grants
    

The **Grand Caravanserai** famously operates at a loss, widely believed to be subsidized by **Bey Vartan’s personal fortune**—a strategic investment in loyalty and influence.

---

## **VI. Faith & Culture**

Culture here is shaped by **gratitude and discipline**.

- Water is treated with reverence, not worship
    
- Excess is discouraged, comfort is not
    
- Scholarship is a civic virtue
    

Religious practice is permitted but secondary to state order.

> _“The desert listens. We answer carefully.”_

---

## **VII. Law & Defense**

Law in Kazmak ve Küzmek is absolute at the edges and measured within.

- Strict checkpoint enforcement
    
- Caravan law governs disputes
    
- Theft of water infrastructure is a capital crime
    

Defense focuses on **control of access**, not walls.

---

## **VIII. Relationships to the Labyrinths**

Kazmak ve Küzmek is a **logistical hub for delvers**, not a delve site.

- Delvers frequent Broadgate and caravan routes
    
- Artifacts are regulated through university oversight
    
- No Labyrinth entrances within city bounds
    

Knowledge here is filtered before it spreads.

## **X. Cultural Identity & Legacy**

Kazmak ve Küzmek is proof that generosity can be calculated—and still sincere.

Travelers remember it not for power, but for relief: cool stone, endless water, and a bed that costs less than it should.

> _“The desert does not forgive mistakes.”_  
> _“The city forgives exhaustion.”_
