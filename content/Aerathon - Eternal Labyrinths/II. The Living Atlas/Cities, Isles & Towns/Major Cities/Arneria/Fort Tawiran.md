## **I. Overview**

**Fort Tawiran** is the **primary eastern landfall into Arneria**, standing at the far end of the six-mile crossing over the **Strait of Glass**. Any foreigner entering Arneria by Causeway must first pass through its gates.

Originally constructed as a **pure gate fort**, Tawiran was never meant to house a population. Over centuries, however, trade, waiting travelers, rejected petitioners, and opportunistic merchants transformed the perimeter into a dense, chaotic settlement clinging to the fort’s walls and spreading beneath the rainforest canopy.

Tawiran is not a destination.  
It is a **question**, and the answer determines everything that follows.

**Population:** ~22,000 (non-citizens fluctuate seasonally)  
**City Type:** Gate Fort / Border City  
**Primary Renown:** Controlled entry, Causeway administration  
**Official Notice:** _“Entry is a privilege, not a right.”_

---

## **II. Geography & Architecture**

Fort Tawiran sits at the **Arnerian end of the Causeway**, where stone road meets rainforest and sea spray gives way to humidity.

### **The Gate Fort**

- Twin stone doors over twenty feet tall
    
- Open during daylight hours only
    
- Reinforced watch towers embedded in ancient masonry
    

The fort’s architecture is deliberate, symmetrical, and unyielding—designed to project permanence and authority.

### **The Outer City**

Beyond the walls, a very different city exists:

- Buildings bolted directly to fort stone
    
- Structures stacked downward into the canopy
    
- Walkways improvised from rope, wood, and scavenged stone
    

This architectural dissonance is intentional only in retrospect. Tawiran looks exactly like what it is: **order imposed atop survival**.

---

## **III. History**

Fort Tawiran was raised during an early era of Causeway expansion, intended solely as a **customs and security checkpoint** between Arneria and the wider continent. It was never chartered as a city, and for generations, it functioned precisely as planned.

Then travelers began to wait.

Denied entry, delayed by paperwork, or stalled by seasonal restrictions, merchants and pilgrims lingered outside the walls. Traders followed. So did inns, brokers, guides, and smugglers. Over time, impermanence hardened into habit, and habit into settlement.

Arneria tolerated the growth reluctantly. Repeated attempts to dismantle the outer city failed—not from resistance, but from necessity. Too many functions had become dependent on what grew around the gate.

Tawiran became an accidental city—one Arneria now uses rather than admits.

**Key Historical Events:**

- **1612 A.D.** — Construction of **Fort Tawiran** as a dedicated Causeway gate fort.
    
- **1704 A.D.** — Establishment of permanent **entry protocols** requiring proof of business.
    
- **1889 A.D.** — Recognition of the **Outer Settlement**, following failed removal efforts.
    
- **2673 A.D.** — Codification of **Beylik Entry Law**, formalizing Tawiran’s authority over all Causeway arrivals.
    

---

## **IV. Governance**

Fort Tawiran is governed directly by the **Beylik of Arneria**.

- A **Gate Commander** oversees fort operations
    
- Bey infantry enforce entry law and patrol routes
    
- Civil matters in the outer city are tolerated but minimally regulated
    

No mayor exists. Authority flows inward, not outward.

---

## **V. Economy**

Tawiran’s economy exists entirely because people must wait.

**Primary Activities**

- Lodging and short-term housing
    
- Trade brokerage and document preparation
    
- Sale of Arnerian goods to first-time visitors
    
- Guiding, translation, and legal consultation
    

Everything sold here is designed to be **portable**.

---

## **VI. Faith & Culture**

Faith in Tawiran is fragmented and transitional.

- Shrines cluster near waiting quarters
    
- Prayers focus on acceptance, patience, and clarity
    
- Religious authority holds no influence over entry decisions
    

Culturally, Tawiran is defined by **first impressions**. Locals pride themselves on hospitality—not because it is policy, but because it is profitable and humane.

> _“You may not enter today. Sit. Eat. Try again tomorrow.”_

---

## **VII. Law & Defense**

Law in Tawiran is absolute at the gate and permissive beyond it.

- Entry violations are punished swiftly
    
- Forged documentation is a severe offense
    
- The outer city polices itself unless Arnerian order is threatened
    

Defense is inward-facing: the fort is designed to **prevent passage**, not repel invasion.

---

## **VIII. Relationships to the Labyrinths**

Fort Tawiran maintains strict prohibition against Labyrinth activity.

- No delving allowed within fort jurisdiction
    
- Artifacts are confiscated pending review
    
- MDO presence limited to liaison observation
    

Arneria does not allow unknown variables past its threshold.

## **X. Cultural Identity & Legacy**

Fort Tawiran is remembered as the place where journeys pause.

To Arnerians, it is a shield.  
To foreigners, it is a test.  
To the city itself, it is a balancing act between control and compassion.

> _“The gate stands. The road waits.”_

---

## **XI. Quirky Natures**

- Entry interviews are rumored to last longer than some delves
    
- Paperwork stalls spawn entire micro-markets
    
- Many travelers never leave—and stop trying
    
- The fort’s shadow marks unofficial time
