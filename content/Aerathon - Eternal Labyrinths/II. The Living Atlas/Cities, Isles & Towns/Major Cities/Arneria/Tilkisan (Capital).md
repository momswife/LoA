## **I. Overview**

**Tilksian** is the **capital city of Arneria**, seated where two tributaries of the **Mavimar River** converge beneath the Causeway’s arching spine. It is a city designed to be admired—from above, from afar, and from memory.

White stone Causeway walls reflect waterlight into the streets. Terraced gardens descend toward the riverbanks. Bridges curve like deliberate brushstrokes. From nearly any vantage, Tilksian looks flawless.

Life within it is not evenly so.

Tilksian is the administrative heart of the **Beylik**, home to ancient family lines whose ancestors once pleased a bey and never stopped being rewarded. Below them—quite literally—live the city’s commoners, pressed into narrow Causeway-side districts that overlook the wealth they sustain but will never touch.

Tilksian shines because it must.

**Population:** ~180,000  
**City Type:** Capital City / Causeway Metropolis  
**Primary Renown:** Architecture, governance, cultural prestige  
**Civic Motto:** _“Beauty Is Stability.”_

---

## **II. Geography & Architecture**

Tilksian is built around **controlled flow**—of water, people, and sightlines.

### **The Confluence**

Two tributaries of the Mavimar meet beneath the city:

- Regulated canals guide water through ornamental channels
    
- Reflective pools amplify natural light
    
- Floodgates double as ceremonial monuments
    

Water is never allowed to appear wild.

### **Vertical Stratification**

Tilksian rises and descends by class:

- **Upper Terraces:** Old mansions, private gardens, Beylik halls
    
- **Mid-City:** Administrative districts, cultural institutions
    
- **Causeway Edge:** Dense worker housing, inns, caravan services
    

Even the city’s elevation enforces hierarchy.

Architecture favors:

- Pale stone and polished marble
    
- Ivy-covered arches and glass-lined walkways
    
- Buildings meant to endure centuries without visible repair
    

Tilksian does not decay in public.

---

## **III. History**

Tilksian was not founded—it was **selected**.

When the Causeway reached the Mavimar Confluence, early beys recognized the site’s symbolic potential. Water could be controlled. Traffic could be monitored. Wealth would pass through whether welcomed or not.

Families who proved loyal were granted estates near the riverbanks. Their descendants still live there, bound by tradition and entitlement.

For centuries, Tilksian’s beauty was enough to keep dissent muted. But as Aerathon’s delving economy surged and wealth began appearing in the hands of common beasts, the city’s old equilibrium quietly shifted.

The ruling class has not yet adjusted.

### **Key Historical Events**

- **1084 A.D.** — Selection of the **Mavimar Confluence** as Arneria’s administrative seat.
    
- **1372 A.D.** — Completion of the **High Terraces**, cementing noble residence zones.
    
- **2129 A.D.** — Formal designation as **Beylik Capital**, centralizing state authority.
    
- **3148 A.D.** — Notable rise in delver caravan traffic and visible wealth disparity.
    

Tilksian has always adapted slowly—by design.

---

## **IV. Governance**

Tilksian is governed directly by the **Bey of Arneria**, supported by:

- Ministerial courts
    
- Dynastic advisory houses
    
- Causeway and trade oversight councils
    

Political power here is inherited, curated, and defended by precedent rather than popularity.

---

## **V. Economy**

Tilksian’s economy is fueled by **passage**.

**Primary Sectors**

- State administration and taxation
    
- Luxury trade and cultural patronage
    
- Caravan services and Causeway tolls
    
- Financial institutions tied to noble families
    

Recent increases in delver wealth have begun to strain traditional class expectations.

---

## **VI. Faith & Culture**

Faith in Tilksian is ceremonial.

- Grand temples serve civic function as much as spiritual
    
- Public rites emphasize continuity and order
    
- Personal belief is expected to remain discreet
    

Culturally, Tilksian prizes refinement, restraint, and aesthetic excellence. Art is commissioned to soothe, not provoke.

> _“Passion belongs in private.”_

---

## **VII. Law & Defense**

Law in Tilksian is immaculate and uneven.

- Causeway Guard patrols visible districts constantly
    
- Noble estates maintain private security
    
- Protests are redirected, not crushed
    

Defense focuses inward—on maintaining the image of calm.

---

## **VIII. Relationships to the Labyrinths**

Tilksian maintains **deliberate distance** from the Labyrinths.

- No entrances within city bounds
    
- Delvers tolerated but not celebrated
    
- Artifacts subject to immediate regulation
    

The city prefers controlled danger elsewhere.

---

## **X. Cultural Identity & Legacy**

Tilksian believes beauty is proof of right to rule.

Whether that belief can survive a changing Aerathon remains unanswered. Silent looks pass between commoners. Delver wealth glints in places it once did not.

The city has not noticed yet.

> _“The water still flows where we tell it to.”_

---

## **XI. Quirky Natures**

- Street cleaners outnumber street musicians
    
- Noble families measure age in renovations
    
- Commoners know every hidden stairway
    
- The city glows brightest just before dawn
