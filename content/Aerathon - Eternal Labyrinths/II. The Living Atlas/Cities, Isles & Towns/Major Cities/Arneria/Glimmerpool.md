## **I. Overview**

**Glimmerpool** is a subterranean city hidden deep beneath the northern reaches of the **Bey’s Head** mountains, accessible only through **The Loamlink**. Though often _assumed_ to be a seat of power due to its cultural weight, Glimmerpool holds **no capital authority** and claims none.

Instead, it is widely recognized as the **single greatest cultural gathering place for ligonines** in Aerathon—a pilgrimage city, meeting ground, and shared memory-space for a species otherwise scattered across regions and lifestyles.

Every ligonine tradition, regardless of origin, points to Glimmerpool as the place where the **three distant cousins first met**. How this occurred, given the city’s isolation, remains unresolved. That it occurred is not debated.

**Population:** ~28,000 (highly transient)  
**City Type:** Subterranean Pilgrimage City / Cultural Nexus  
**Primary Renown:** Ligonine gatherings, sacred waters  
**Common Saying:** _“If you are ligonine, you will come.”_

---

## **II. Geography & Architecture**

Glimmerpool surrounds a vast **underground lake** of the same name.

### **The Glimmerpool**

- Fed by a waterfall descending from fissures in the Bey’s Head
    
- Continues beneath the cavern before emerging far away as a primary source of the **Attamek River**
    
- Emits faint natural luminescence due to mineral content and biolife
    

The water is considered **sacred** by ligonines. Immersion rituals are common; contamination is unthinkable.

### **The Cavern City**

The city clings to the cavern walls and spreads across stone shelves, platforms, and suspended walkways.

Architecture is:

- A deliberate mishmash of styles from across Aerathon
    
- Built to accommodate wildly different body plans
    
- Non-linear, curved, and often counterintuitive
    

Hooks, hanging bars, and suspended rails allow **sloths** to traverse with ease. Low-clearance passages and reinforced floors accommodate **armadillos**. Clockwork lifts and lighting rigs reflect **mole** tinkering traditions.

No two districts look alike—and no one tries to fix that.

---

## **III. History**

Glimmerpool’s recorded history does not begin with construction, but with **arrival**.

Ligonine oral tradition holds that the city predates written record—that it was not built so much as **recognized**. According to every surviving lineage, the three disparate ligonine cousins reached Glimmerpool independently and met there for the first time, establishing a shared identity that persists to this day.

Archaeological surveys confirm that portions of the city are older than surrounding Loamlink infrastructure, though no clear founding phase has been identified. Expansion appears to have occurred in bursts, often coinciding with periods of broader ligonine migration.

What is clear is that Glimmerpool has never ruled—and never needed to.

### **Key Historical Events**

- **Pre-1100 A.D.** — Earliest ligonine oral accounts place the **First Meeting** at Glimmerpool.
    
- **1427 A.D.** — First non-ligonine record of Glimmerpool access via early Loamlink passages.
    
- **2013 A.D.** — MDO verification of the Glimmerpool–Attamek hydrological connection.
    
- **2890 A.D.** — Formal designation as a **protected cultural site**, restricting industrial expansion.

## **IV. Governance**

Glimmerpool has **no central ruler**.

- Civic matters are handled by rotating councils formed during major gatherings
    
- Long-term decisions are rare and consensus-driven
    
- Outside authority is politely acknowledged and quietly ignored
    

The city functions because its residents **expect difference**, not unity.

---

## **V. Economy**

Glimmerpool’s economy is minimal and specialized.

**Primary Activities**

- Craft exchange (clockwork, tools, adaptive wear)
    
- Pilgrimage services and guided Loamlink travel
    
- Cultural hosting and archival work
    

Coin circulates lightly. Trade is often reciprocal or symbolic.

---

## **VI. Faith & Culture**

Faith in Glimmerpool is inseparable from **water and memory**.

- The Glimmerpool itself is sacred
    
- Ritual bathing marks life transitions
    
- Storytelling is treated as a civic act
    

Ligonines encourage outsiders to visit—but only if they are prepared:

- To bring their own light
    
- To move slowly
    
- To accept that nothing will be explained twice
    

> _“Strange is only unfamiliar.”_

---

## **VII. Law & Defense**

Law in Glimmerpool is informal but absolute.

- Disrespect of the waters is grounds for expulsion
    
- Violence is extremely rare and swiftly halted
    
- Defense relies on terrain, community, and Loamlink control
    

No standing force exists. None has ever been needed.

---

## **VIII. Relationships to the Labyrinths**

Glimmerpool maintains **active avoidance** of the Labyrinths.

- No entrances within the city
    
- Loamlink passages are regularly monitored
    
- Any anomalous resonance is treated as contamination risk
    

The city values continuity over curiosity.

---

## **X. Cultural Identity & Legacy**

Glimmerpool is not important because it commands.

It is important because **everyone comes eventually**.

For ligonines, it is proof that separation does not erase kinship. For outsiders, it is a lesson in living without hierarchy.

> _“We met here once.”_  
> _“That was enough.”_

---

## **XI. Quirky Natures**

- No maps are considered accurate
    
- Lighting styles change weekly
    
- Outsiders always underestimate travel time
    
- The water reflects things no one mentions aloud
