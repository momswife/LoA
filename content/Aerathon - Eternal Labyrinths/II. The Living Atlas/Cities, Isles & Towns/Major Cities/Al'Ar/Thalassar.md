## **I. Overview**

**Thalassar** is a vast **submerged metropolis** resting beneath the waters of the **Tiger Sea**, positioned between the outer reaches of **Al’Ar** and the far western boundaries of the known world. It is the largest extant population center of **sea-bearing beastfolk** in Aerathon and functions as their de facto cultural capital.

Before its submergence, Thalassar was known as **Tresoli City**—a radiant canal-city of marble bridges, tidal plazas, and narrow stone walks, often likened in surviving texts to a floating crown upon the sea. That city no longer exists above the waves.

Thalassar is not a ruin.  
It is a **continuation**.

---

## **II. Geography & Architecture**

Thalassar occupies a massive depression in the Tiger Sea, where the seabed drops sharply and light fractures unevenly.

### **The Upper Ruins**

- Former rooftops, bridges, and towers
    
- Coral-entwined and partially collapsed
    
- Serve as trade interfaces with surface dwellers
    

### **The Living Depths**

- Sealed plazas and halls adapted for pressure habitation
    
- Reinforced with abyssal craft and grown stone
    
- Entirely airless districts housing permanent populations
    

### **The Labyrinth Scar**

At the city’s lowest depth lies the original breach site.

- Warded, sealed, and heavily monitored
    
- Considered both birthplace and wound
    
- No construction permitted within sanctioned distance
    

Architecture favors **endurance over beauty**, though remnants of Tresoli’s elegance still surface in curved arches and ornamented stonework.

---

## **III. History**

Tresoli City began as a marvel of surface engineering—a canal-bound metropolis designed to live with the sea rather than dominate it. Built upon pylons, artificial islands, and tidal foundations, Tresoli thrived as a trade nexus where merchant councils and sea-guild compacts ruled by coin, contract, and current.

That harmony ended when an **ancient Labyrinth** manifested beneath the city’s central canal nexus. The catastrophe did not come from storm or siege, but from below. The city folded inward as water was drawn downward at impossible angles, and entire districts vanished beneath the surface within days.

Yet Tresoli did not die all at once. Survivors adapted, fled downward, or transformed. Over generations, the drowned city was reclaimed—not by surface folk, but by those born to the depths. Tresoli became **Thalassar**, a city that chose to endure where others would have abandoned the ruins.

**Key Historical Events:**

- **732 A.D.** — Founding of **Tresoli City**, established as a canal-bound trade metropolis built on tidal engineering and sea-guild governance.
    
- **1189 A.D.** — The **Abyssal Breach**, when an ancient Labyrinth manifests beneath the central canal nexus; large portions of Tresoli are pulled beneath the sea.
    
- **1196 A.D.** — The **Last Surface Evacuation**, marking the final organized departure of air-dependent citizens from the upper ruins.
    
- **1364 A.D.** — Formation of the **Tide Concord**, uniting surviving depth-adapted populations under a new abyssal civic order.
    
- **3011 A.D.** — Formal sealing of the **Labyrinth Scar** under joint Concord and proto-MDO accords; Thalassar is recognized as a living city, not a ruin.

## **IV. Governance**

Thalassar is governed by the **Tide Concord**, a council composed of:

- Depth-district representatives
    
- Wardens of the Labyrinth Scar
    
- Elders whose memory predates the Drowning
    

Governance is slow, deliberate, and consensus-driven. Once a decision is made, it is absolute.

> _“What is decided here must survive pressure.”_

---

## **V. Economy**

Thalassar trades selectively and cautiously.

**Primary Exports**

- Abyssal materials
    
- Carefully vetted Tresolian relics
    
- Deep-sea navigation knowledge
    

**Primary Imports**

- Surface luxuries
    
- Air-dependent goods
    
- Arcane stabilizers and warding components
    

Trade is conducted exclusively at the **Upper Ruins**. No surface vessel is permitted deeper access.

---

## **VI. Faith & Culture**

Faith in Thalassar is shaped by catastrophe and memory.

- Gods are respected, never trusted
    
- The sea is home, not mercy
    
- The Labyrinth is feared, hated, and never revered
    

Shrines are oriented **away** from the Labyrinth Scar. Cultural rituals emphasize restraint, remembrance, and survival over triumph.

> _“We sank once. We will not be tempted again.”_

---

## **VII. Law & Defense**

Thalassar maintains no conventional military.

- Defense is handled by depth-wardens and environmental control
    
- Civic law prioritizes survival, secrecy, and containment
    
- Crimes involving the Labyrinth Scar carry absolute penalties
    

Security is preventative and final.

---

## **VIII. Relationships to the Labyrinths**

Thalassar’s relationship to the Labyrinths is **hostile and absolute**.

- The Tresoli Labyrinth is sealed, not explored
    
- Delving within city bounds is forbidden
    
- Any attempt to reopen the breach is treated as existential threat
    

The city exists as a warning of what happens when a Labyrinth is allowed to root unchecked.

---

## **X. Cultural Identity & Legacy**

Tresoli City was a masterpiece of surface ambition.

Thalassar is a monument to refusal—the refusal to vanish, to forget, or to repeat the same mistake. Where others remember tragedy, Thalassar remembers **choice**.

It stands as Aerathon’s clearest lesson: the sea forgives nothing, and the Labyrinth forgives less.

---

## **XI. Quirky Natures**

- Citizens avoid using the word _drowned_
    
- Children learn depth-counting before names
    
- Music is slow, low-frequency, and directional
    
- Many districts keep murals of streets that no longer exist
