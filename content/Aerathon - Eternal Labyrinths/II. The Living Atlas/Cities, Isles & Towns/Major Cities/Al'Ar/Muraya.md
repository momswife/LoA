## **I. Overview**

**Muraya** is a vast cliffbound port city on the island of **Sampura**, within **Al’Ar**. Known throughout the archipelago as the **City of Walls**, Muraya stretches for miles along the coastline while rising sharply upward into the porous stone face behind it.

Where other Al’Ari cities chase wind or water, Muraya chooses **height**. Its people are renowned climbers, scouts, and horizon-watchers, trained from youth to read the sea from impossible vantage points. Muraya is less a capital of culture and more a capital of **warning**.

It does not sing to the storm.  
It waits for it.

---

## **II. Geography & Architecture**

Muraya exists in a narrow vertical band between sea and stone.

### **Coastal Docks**

- Long, efficient docklines hugging the base of the cliff
    
- Warehouses carved directly into stone alcoves
    
- Minimal wasted shoreline
    

### **The Cliff City**

- Residential tiers cut into the rock face
    
- Switchback stairways, rope lifts, and anchor paths
    
- Buildings reinforced with stone ribs and iron braces
    

The porous cliff absorbs heat and moisture, casting long afternoon shade across the city and keeping upper districts cool even in peak season.

### **The High Walls**

At the uppermost reaches, the city opens into lookout terraces and fortified platforms used exclusively for long-range sighting and weather reading. Access is restricted to trained climbers.

---

## **III. History**

Muraya began as a modest trading outpost beneath Sampura’s great cliff, selected for its sheltered waters and commanding sightlines. Early settlers found inland expansion impossible; the land behind them rose sheer and unyielding. Instead of retreating, the city adapted upward, carving itself into the wall that confined it.

Over centuries, Muraya transformed from port to **watchtower-city**. Climbing became more than survival—it became doctrine. Those who ascended higher were entrusted with greater responsibility, and authority followed elevation rather than lineage. Muraya learned that walls were not meant to keep danger out, but to **see it coming**.

Today, Muraya’s past is measured not in years, but in terraces. The higher you stand, the more history you are expected to remember.

**Key Historical Events:**

- **214 A.D.** — Establishment of the **Lower Mooring**, a fortified dock settlement founded beneath the cliff to control coastal trade and storm shelter.
    
- **611 A.D.** — Completion of the **First Ascent Routes**, formalizing carved stairways and rope paths into the cliff; climbing becomes a civic expectation.
    
- **1043 A.D.** — The **Fall of the Seventh Terrace**, a catastrophic partial collapse that kills dozens and leads to modern Murayan anchoring standards.
    
- **1678 A.D.** — Formation of the **Wall Council**, granting senior climbers formal authority over civic planning and defense.
    
- **2894 A.D.** — The **Long Watch Storm**, when Murayan climbers sight a catastrophic offshore system days early, enabling full evacuation of the lower docks.

## **IV. Governance**

Muraya is governed by the **Wall Council**, composed of:

- Senior climbers
    
- Dockmasters
    
- Trade stewards
    

Authority is merit-based and situational. Those who understand the wall are trusted to decide for it. Leadership positions are earned through proven competence, not bloodline or wealth.

---

## **V. Economy**

Muraya is a logistical and observational hub.

**Primary Outputs**

- Trade handling and redistribution
    
- Maritime supplies
    
- Skilled climbers and long-range scouts
    

**Imports**

- Foodstuffs
    
- Timber
    
- Luxury goods from other Al’Ari ports
    

Efficiency, not spectacle, defines Muraya’s economy.

---

## **VI. Faith & Culture**

Faith in Muraya is subdued and structural.

- Shrines carved directly into the cliff
    
- Gods respected as forces to be navigated, not bargained with
    
- Offerings are small, frequent, and deliberate
    

Culturally, Murayans value restraint, preparedness, and responsibility. Excess is viewed as a distraction from vigilance.

> _“If you climbed above us, you owe us your eyes.”_

---

## **VII. Law & Defense**

Muraya maintains no standing army.

- Defense relies on early warning, evacuation, and terrain advantage
    
- Climbers serve as the first line of response
    
- Crime is rare and handled through restitution or exile
    

Security is preventative rather than punitive.

---

## **VIII. Relationships to the Labyrinths**

Muraya treats the Labyrinths with caution and distance.

- Sea caves and cliff-adjacent anomalies are heavily restricted
    
- Delving is permitted only with civic approval
    
- Reckless exploration is considered a civic endangerment
    

The city prefers **foreknowledge** over confrontation.

## **X. Cultural Identity & Legacy**

Muraya believes survival begins with **seeing**.

It does not chase storms, nor does it fear them. It climbs, watches, and prepares. In a region defined by motion, Muraya stands as proof that stillness—at the right height—can be power.

---

## **XI. Quirky Natures**

- Children are taught climbing knots before arithmetic
    
- Heights are measured by responsibility, not distance
    
- Murayans sleep best during storms
    
- Visitors are warned: _“If the wall feels warm, leave.”_
