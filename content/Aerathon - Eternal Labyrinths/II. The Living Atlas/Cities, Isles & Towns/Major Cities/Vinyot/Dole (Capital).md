## **I. Overview**

**Dole** is the capital of **Vinyot**, a radiant hill-city whose immaculate streets and cheerful pageantry mask one of the most tightly controlled baronies in Aerathon.

From a distance, Dole appears idyllic: stone streets polished to a river-smooth sheen, banners freshly dyed, and citizens dressed in bright colors who laugh easily in public squares. Every surface reflects light. Every corner is swept. Every first day of the month, the city halts so that its facades may be scrubbed spotless once more.

Up close, Dole is **exhausting**.

The city is governed by **Baroness Unknown**, a ruler infamous for impatience, cruelty, and swift punishment of behaviors she deems unsavory. Her rule is absolute within the barony, and her capital is both her pride and her shield.

**Associated Region:** Vinyot  
**City Type:** Capital City / Baronic Seat  
**Population:** ~180,000 (city proper)  
**Primary Renown:** Political authority, immaculate presentation  
**Civic Maxim:** _“Order is kindness, properly enforced.”_

---

## **II. Geography & Urban Form**

Dole rises from the surrounding lowlands as a **single, commanding hill**, terraced and paved with meticulous precision.

### **A. The Lower Rings**

- Dense residential quarters and markets
    
- Narrow streets kept obsessively clean
    
- Patrol routes embedded into urban design
    

Crowding is severe, but disorder is not tolerated.

---

### **B. The Gleamways**

Broad processional avenues leading upward.

- Used for festivals, inspections, and visiting dignitaries
    
- Lined with fountains that never run dry
    
- Citizens are expected to smile here
    

The Gleamways exist to be seen.

---

### **C. The Hilltop**

The summit of the city is reserved entirely for governance.

- Administrative halls
    
- Barracks and treasury vaults
    
- **Dole Manor**, visible from nearly anywhere below
    

From here, authority does not look down—it **watches**.

---

## **III. History & Rise to Capital**

Dole was selected as Vinyot’s capital not for centrality, but for **controllability**.

Its elevated position made it easy to defend and easier to isolate from the conditions of the wider barony. As Vinyot’s economic power grew through trade and industry, Dole remained deliberately **administrative rather than productive**—a place where decisions were made, not lived with.

Over time, this separation hardened into doctrine.

---

## **IV. Governance & Rule**

### **Baroness Unknown**

- Title: Baroness of Dole, High Administrator of Vinyot
    
- Reputation: Cruel, efficient, deeply insecure
    
- Governing Style: Punitive order, surface reinvestment
    

Taxes in the Dole barony are among the harshest in Vinyot. Reinvestment exists primarily as **optics**—repairs and improvements concentrated where visiting lords and foreign observers are most likely to look.

Punishments are swift, public, and memorable.

---

### **Lady Briere**

- Role: Castellan of Dole Manor
    
- Public Face of the Barony
    

Lady Briere oversees the daily operation of the capital and manages its appearances. She is widely regarded as more patient than the Baroness, but no less loyal.

A common saying in the city goes:

> _“The Lady waits an extra day to dust the Manor, lest Her Majesty become envious of its glimmer.”_

Whether this is humor or warning is unclear.

---

## **V. Peoples & Civic Life**

The people of Dole are **performatively content**.

- Public laughter is common
    
- Bright clothing is encouraged
    
- Complaints are private—or not at all
    

Entire industries exist to maintain appearances:

- Street polishers
    
- Facade scrubbers
    
- Banner dyers
    
- Festival coordinators
    

The city rewards those who understand the rules and punishes those who forget them.

---

## **VI. Economy & Extraction**

Dole itself produces little.

Its wealth flows inward from:

- Industrial cities like Malarthain
    
- Trade hubs such as Bella Madre
    
- Harsh taxation of surrounding baronies
    

Coin moves upward. Goods move outward. Accountability moves nowhere.

---

## **VII. Faith & Moral Order**

Faith in Dole is permitted, but regulated.

- Temples must maintain approved aesthetics
    
- Preachers are monitored
    
- Excessive zeal is considered disruptive
    

Moral authority is tolerated only when it reinforces civic obedience.

---

## **VIII. Delvers, Guilds & the MDO**

Delvers are unwelcome unless useful.

- Licenses are expensive
    
- Guild halls are restricted to lower districts
    
- Independent delvers are closely watched
    

The MDO maintains a **permanent liaison office**, primarily to:

- Monitor unrest
    
- Track tax pressure impacts
    
- Ensure Vinyot’s stability does not fracture
    

Relations are professional, not warm.

## **X. City Identity & Legacy**

Dole believes that **order is mercy**, and that mercy must sometimes be enforced with a closed fist.

It is a city that shines so brightly its shadows are forced to hide beneath it. Whether that brilliance is strength or fragility remains an open question—one the Baroness is determined to answer before anyone else can ask it aloud.

The Ministry classifies Dole as **administratively powerful, socially brittle**, and at high risk of unrest should appearances ever fail.

> _“Dole is clean,”_ one internal report concludes.  
> _“But cleanliness is not the same as health.”_
