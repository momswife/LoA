## **I. Overview**

**Southwinds** is the largest port city along the **hot, humid southern shoreline of Vinyot**, positioned between the **Honeymoon River** and the **Auric River**, where inland trade meets the open routes toward **Arneria and the Long Sea**.

To outsiders, Southwinds is a city of sails, markets, and opportunity.  
To its people, it is a city that **survived being erased**.

Southwinds bears deeper historical scars than any other Vinyotian port. It was the **first city occupied** during the Unknown-era invasion and the **last to be relinquished**. Though rebuilt into a thriving trade hub, memory lingers in its streets, laws, and wary glances.

**Population:** ~310,000  
**City Type:** Maritime Trade City / Post-Occupation Port  
**Primary Renown:** Long-sea trade, caravan staging, ship refit  
**Local Saying:** _“The wind carries more than coin.”_

---

## **II. Geography & Architecture**

Southwinds stretches along fertile coastal lowlands, its cityscape shaped as much by wind as by water.

### **The Shoreline**

- Warm, shallow waters suitable for extended anchorage
    
- Constant prevailing winds ideal for sail traffic
    
- Sandy soil reinforced by stone foundations
    

### **Urban Layout**

- **Lower Docks:** Warehouses, taverns, shipyards
    
- **Middle City:** Markets, guild halls, caravan courts
    
- **High Terraces:** Rebuilt civic buildings, wind towers, watch posts
    

Few structures predate the occupation. Most are **postwar constructions**, practical rather than ornate, as if permanence is still being tested.

---

## **III. History**

Southwinds was once a proud but unremarkable coastal city—until history arrived all at once.

### **The Occupation**

The **Unknown’s initial manifestation** occurred several miles east of the city. Within days, a human army swept through Southwinds and established it as their **primary coastal base**.

At the command of unseen authorities:

- All **beast religious symbols** were destroyed
    
- **Bethels were razed**
    
- Statues, murals, and civic markers were torn down
    
- Entire districts were stripped of cultural identity
    

Southwinds became an administrative and logistical hub for the occupation. Resistance was crushed quickly. Survival required silence.

### **After the War**

When the conflict ended, Southwinds made a defining choice:  
**it refused entry to humans entirely.**

While neighboring cities benefited from shared reconstruction, labor, and goodwill, Southwinds isolated itself. Trade caravans passed by. Prosperity bloomed elsewhere. The city healed materially—but not economically, nor emotionally.

Only in recent decades did curiosity about delvers and the wider world outweigh grief.

### **Key Historical Events**

- **1429 A.D.** — Founding of Southwinds as a coastal trade port.
    
- **1471 A.D.** — Full occupation and cultural erasure during the Unknown-era invasion.
    
- **1492 A.D.** — Final surrender and withdrawal of occupying forces.
    
- **1504 A.D.** — Citywide prohibition against human settlement enacted.
    
- **3091 A.D.** — Conditional reopening of the city to non-resident humans and delvers.
    

Southwinds did not forget.  
It simply chose when to look forward.

---

## **IV. Governance**

Southwinds is governed by a **Harbor Council**, composed of shipmasters, dock guild representatives, and civic stewards.

- Trade law is pragmatic and flexible
    
- Cultural protection statutes are unusually strict
    
- Entry permits are reviewed carefully
    

While humans may now walk its streets, **surveillance is constant and deliberate**.

---

## **V. Economy**

Southwinds thrives on movement.

**Primary Economic Drivers**

- Maritime trade and long-sea shipping
    
- Caravan resupply and staging
    
- Shipbuilding and sailcraft
    
- Warehousing and customs arbitration
    

The city profits enormously from traffic moving _through_ it—even if trust does not.

---

## **VI. Faith & Culture**

Faith in Southwinds is subdued.

- Shrines are small and unobtrusive
    
- Public religious iconography is limited by law
    
- Cultural festivals emphasize wind, sea, and survival rather than gods
    

Food, music, and storytelling carry memory where temples once stood.

> _“What was taken cannot be rebuilt the same.”_

---

## **VII. Law & Defense**

Southwinds maintains a strong but restrained watch.

- Harbor patrols prioritize prevention
    
- Cultural vandalism is punished severely
    
- Foreign altercations are resolved quickly to avoid escalation
    

The city’s defense doctrine focuses on **never being taken again**.

---

## **VIII. Relationships to the Labyrinths**

Southwinds is not defined by the Labyrinths, but they pass through it.

- Delving caravans resupply here
    
- Maritime labyrinth artifacts occasionally surface
    
- The city levies heavy fees on relic transport
    

Labyrinth wealth is welcomed.  
Labyrinth chaos is not.

---

## **X. Cultural Identity & Legacy**

Southwinds is a city learning how to exist after violation.

It no longer bars its gates—but it does not open its arms either. Memory lives in law, architecture, and the way strangers are watched until proven harmless.

> _“We survived the wind.”_  
> _“Now we decide where it blows.”_

---

## **XI. Quirky Natures**

- Locals still avoid rebuilding old bethel sites
    
- Wind direction is read like an omen
    
- Children learn occupation stories as history lessons
    
- The city never flies banners higher than its sails
