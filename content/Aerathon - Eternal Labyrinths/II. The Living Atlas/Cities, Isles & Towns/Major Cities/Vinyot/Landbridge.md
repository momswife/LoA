## **I. Overview**

**Landbridge** is a major **freshwater port city of Vinyot**, situated between the **Cristalmonte** and **Ficklefox** rivers at a critical elevation break on their journey toward the **Long Sea**. It exists because the water cannot go on without help—and because someone realized they could charge for that help.

Twin lock systems dominate the city, lifting and lowering vessels through steep gradients that would otherwise make passage impossible. Whoever controls these locks controls inland trade.

That authority rests with the **Landbridge Canal Concern**, a powerful commercial body that manages not only the waterways, but the farms, the city guard, and the majority of residential housing. Landbridge does not answer to a house or noble lineage. It answers to ledgers.

**Population:** ~68,000  
**City Type:** Inland Port / Lock City  
**Primary Renown:** Canal control, freshwater trade  
**Common Saying:** _“The water waits. So do you.”_

---

## **II. Geography & Architecture**

Landbridge is built vertically as much as horizontally.

### **The Twin Locks**

- Massive stone-and-brass chambers
    
- Counterweighted gates operated day and night
    
- Water levels change by dozens of feet within the city bounds
    

The locks are visible from nearly everywhere in Landbridge, a constant reminder of what the city exists to do.

### **Urban Layout**

- Upper City: lock control towers, Concern offices, overseer housing
    
- Mid-City: warehouses, counting houses, inns
    
- Low City: worker housing, docks, canal farms
    

Bridges cross water at multiple heights, giving the city its name—and its disorienting sense of scale.

---

## **III. History**

Landbridge began as a temporary solution.

Early Vinyotian traders struggled to move goods along the Cristalmonte–Ficklefox route due to the severe elevation change. A consortium of engineers and investors funded the first lock as a shared utility. It worked too well.

Control consolidated quickly. The original consortium reorganized into the **Landbridge Canal Concern**, purchasing surrounding land, expanding lock capacity, and absorbing rival operators. Over time, the city grew around the locks rather than beside them.

In recent decades, rising trade volume has sharpened inequality. To stabilize costs and retain profit, the Concern began transitioning worker wages from standard Vinyotian coinage into **canal scrip**—usable primarily within Landbridge-owned businesses and housing.

The city has not yet erupted.  
But it has begun to hum.

### **Key Historical Events**

- **1214 A.D.** — Completion of the first Cristalmonte lock.
    
- **1479 A.D.** — Formation of the Landbridge Canal Concern.
    
- **2336 A.D.** — Expansion into twin-lock system; city population triples.
    
- **3159 A.D.** — Introduction of partial wage payment in canal scrip.

## **IV. Governance**

Landbridge is governed entirely by the **Landbridge Canal Concern**.

- A **Board of Locks** sets policy
    
- Canal Wardens command the guard
    
- Civic councils exist but hold advisory status only
    

Most city services—housing, food distribution, security—are Concern-operated. Legal authority and employment are inseparable.

---

## **V. Economy**

Landbridge’s economy is absolute.

**Primary Sectors**

- Lock passage fees
    
- Freshwater shipping and warehousing
    
- Canal-adjacent agriculture
    
- Financial services tied to transit
    

The introduction of canal scrip has allowed the Concern to insulate itself from external currency shifts—at the cost of worker mobility and trust.

---

## **VI. Faith & Culture**

Landbridge’s culture is pragmatic and strained.

- Shrines cluster near locks and bridges
    
- Work schedules dictate religious observance
    
- Festivals are brief and tightly regulated
    

Faith here is less about hope than endurance.

> _“The river keeps moving. So must we.”_

---

## **VII. Law & Defense**

Law enforcement answers to the Concern.

- Canal Guard patrols docks and worker districts
    
- Upper City enjoys private security
    
- Strikes and unauthorized assemblies are quietly dispersed
    

Justice favors continuity of operation.

---

## **VIII. Relationships to the Labyrinths**

Landbridge has no known Labyrinth entrances.

However:

- Delver caravans pass through frequently
    
- Artifact transport is tightly monitored
    
- Concern levies special fees on delver cargo
    

The city profits from danger without touching it.

## **X. Cultural Identity & Legacy**

Landbridge sees itself as indispensable.

Its workers see themselves as replaceable.

The gap between those beliefs is widening, and the water keeps rising all the same.

> _“Nothing passes without our leave.”_  
> _“Not even fairness.”_

- Scrip designs change subtly every year
