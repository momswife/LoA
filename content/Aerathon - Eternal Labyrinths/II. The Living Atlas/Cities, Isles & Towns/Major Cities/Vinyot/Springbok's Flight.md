## **I. Overview**

**Springbok’s Flight** is a major city of **Vinyot**, revered not for wealth or force, but for **alignment**. Built amid steep coastal cliffs, the city is defined by two great ascending roads that rise east and west toward twin lighthouse-towers overlooking the sea.

These towers serve as the most enduring pilgrimage symbols in Vinyot:

- **The Western Light** honors the **Beast Mother**, embodying the gift of will and self-direction granted to all living creatures.
    
- **The Eastern Light** honors **Dramphine**, representing fairness, judgment, and balance extended equally to beast and brethren.
    

For centuries, Springbok’s Flight has been a required passage for religious pilgrims traveling across Vinyot, regardless of sect.

**Population:** ~64,000  
**City Type:** Pilgrimage City / Sacred Waypoint  
**Primary Renown:** Twin lighthouse shrines, religious processions  
**Common Saying:** _“Choose your climb.”_

---

## **II. Geography & Architecture**

Springbok’s Flight is carved into a natural saddle between cliffs that fall sharply to the sea.

### **The Twin Roads**

- One road ascends westward, one eastward
    
- Each winds deliberately, never straight
    
- Pilgrims traditionally walk both, in either order
    

The climb is considered part of the rite.

### **The Lighthouse Towers**

- Tall stone beacons visible far offshore
    
- Maintained continuously, regardless of weather
    
- No residential structures permitted within their immediate precincts
    

The city below is built low and orderly, careful not to overshadow the ascent.

---

## **III. History**

Springbok’s Flight began as a small cliffside settlement where early pilgrims stopped to pray before dangerous sea crossings. Over time, two separate shrines emerged—one honoring the Beast Mother’s will, the other Dramphine’s judgment.

Rather than allow rivalry, the city codified **duality** as doctrine.

The twin towers were raised not to compete, but to balance—an architectural declaration that faith in Vinyot does not demand uniformity, only sincerity.

As pilgrimage traffic grew, the city expanded cautiously, ensuring that devotion remained central rather than diluted by excess.

### **Key Historical Events**

- **811 A.D.** — First recorded pilgrim shrine on the western cliff.
    
- **947 A.D.** — Establishment of the eastern Dramphine beacon.
    
- **1216 A.D.** — Formal recognition of Springbok’s Flight as a dual-path pilgrimage city.
    
- **2684 A.D.** — Civic ordinances enacted enforcing public conduct standards for pilgrims.
    

Springbok’s Flight has never rushed expansion.  
Faith, here, is meant to arrive on foot.

---

## **IV. Governance**

The city is governed by a **Civic Conclave**, composed of:

- Municipal officials
    
- Recognized clerical representatives from major Vinyotian faiths
    
- Pilgrimage wardens
    

No single faith governs the city. Authority exists to preserve neutrality and order.

---

## **V. Economy**

Springbok’s Flight thrives quietly.

**Primary Economic Activity**

- Pilgrim lodging and food services
    
- Shrine maintenance and donations
    
- Religious scholarship and copying
    
- Maritime signaling support
    

Commerce is deliberately restrained. Excess is considered a distraction.

---

## **VI. Faith & Culture**

Faith defines daily life.

- Public behavior codes emphasize modesty and respect
    
- Celebrations are solemn rather than exuberant
    
- Music favors choral voices and wind instruments
    

Religious tourists are expected to **behave as though they are being witnessed**—by gods, by history, or by themselves.

> _“Devotion is not private here.”_

---

## **VII. Law & Defense**

Law enforcement is calm but uncompromising.

- Pilgrimage wardens patrol the roads
    
- Public intoxication and indecency are heavily fined
    
- Repeat offenders are expelled from the city
    

Springbok’s Flight is safe because it is predictable.

---

## **VIII. Relationships to the Labyrinths**

Springbok’s Flight has no Labyrinth entrances.

- Delvers may pass through
    
- Artifact display is restricted near shrines
    
- No delving rites are recognized here
    

The city’s purpose is reflection, not descent.

## **X. Cultural Identity & Legacy**

Springbok’s Flight believes restraint is itself sacred.

It stands as proof that devotion does not require excess—and that a city can choose calm, even when temptation lies only a day’s sail away.

> _“The climb matters more than the view.”_

---

## **XI. Quirky Natures**

- Pilgrims often walk barefoot on one ascent
    
- Bells mark sunrise and sunset, never noon
    
- Taverns close earlier here than anywhere else in Vinyot
    
- Locals can identify pilgrims by pace alone
