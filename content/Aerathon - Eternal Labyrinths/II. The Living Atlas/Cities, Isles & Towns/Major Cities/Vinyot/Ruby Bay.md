## **I. Overview**

**Ruby Bay** is a mainland port city of **Vinyot**, situated at the estuary of the **Ficklefox River** where it spills into the open sea. The city takes its name from the bay’s distinctive red-tinted waters, stained by naturally occurring iron deposits leached from upstream rock.

Once a quiet coastal inlet surrounded by fishing villages, Ruby Bay expanded rapidly during the great post-Pilgrimage influx, when thousands—especially humanity newly arrived in Aerathon—came seeking work, shelter, and permanence.

What began as emergency settlement has hardened into a city struggling to decide what it will be _after_ necessity.

**Population:** ~54,000 (including surrounding hamlets)  
**City Type:** Coastal Port / Rapid-Growth Settlement  
**Primary Renown:** Construction, settlement supply, estuary trade  
**Local Saying:** _“The water remembers the rush.”_

---

## **II. Geography & Architecture**

Ruby Bay curves gently inward, forming a naturally sheltered harbor.

### **The Red Waters**

- Iron-rich runoff stains the bay a deep crimson hue
    
- Color intensifies at low tide and after rainfall
    
- Harmless, but unsettling to newcomers
    

The bay is widely regarded as one of the strangest natural spectacles in southern Vinyot.

### **Settlement Ring**

- Inner city of stone, brick, and reclaimed lumber
    
- Outer ring of newer hamlets and prefabricated housing
    
- Fishing villages dot the shoreline
    

Much of the city was built quickly and cheaply—meant to house people _now_, not generations later.

---

## **III. History**

For most of its existence, Ruby Bay was little more than a river mouth and a scattering of fishing huts. That changed abruptly with the **arrival of humanity in Aerathon**, when the Unknown’s intervention displaced thousands into unfamiliar lands.

Ruby Bay’s geography made it ideal for rapid settlement: flat ground, fresh water, and easy harbor access. Charity-funded contracts flooded the region. Companies willing to set aside old grudges from the Invader occupation found themselves rewarded beyond expectation.

As the influx slowed and aid dried up, Ruby Bay was left with infrastructure built for crisis—and a population that expected continuity.

### **Key Historical Events**

- **1448 A.D.** — Establishment of permanent fishing hamlets around the bay.
    
- **3151 A.D.** — Massive settlement expansion following the Pilgrimage.
    
- **3154 A.D.** — Formation of the **Ruby Bay Trade Board**, consolidating corporate governance.
    
- **3162 A.D.** — First labor disputes as charity contracts end.
    

Ruby Bay was never meant to last this way.  
Now it must.

---

## **IV. Governance**

Ruby Bay is governed by the **Ruby Bay Trade Board**, a coalition of companies rather than a civic lineage.

- Seats held by major homebuilders, suppliers, and logistics firms
    
- Decisions made by weighted financial interest
    
- Civic councils exist but lack binding authority
    

Governance prioritizes **stability and profitability**, often at odds with community needs.

---

## **V. Economy**

Ruby Bay’s economy was forged in urgency.

**Primary Sectors**

- Construction and prefabricated housing
    
- Settlement logistics and supply
    
- Fishing and estuary trade
    
- Light manufacturing
    

With emergency funding gone, the city faces pressure to reinvent itself—or exploit its workforce further.

---

## **VI. Faith & Culture**

Ruby Bay’s culture is raw and unresolved.

- Faith varies wildly by neighborhood
    
- Shrines appear in construction yards and docks
    
- Charity is remembered fondly—and expected still
    

Resentment simmers beneath politeness, especially where old wounds from the Invader occupation were never fully healed.

> _“Kindness is easier when it pays.”_

---

## **VII. Law & Defense**

Law enforcement is inconsistent.

- Trade Board funds the city watch
    
- Enforcement varies by district
    
- Corporate property is heavily protected
    

Justice is functional, not equitable.

---

## **VIII. Relationships to the Labyrinths**

Ruby Bay has no known Labyrinth entrances.

However:

- Delvers pass through seeking work or transport
    
- Artifact salvage occasionally moves through the port
    
- The Trade Board levies special fees on delver cargo
    

Risk is tolerated when profitable.

## **X. Cultural Identity & Legacy**

Ruby Bay has not yet decided who it is.

Was it a moment of compassion?  
A profitable accident?  
Or a promise yet unpaid?

The water keeps its color regardless.

> _“Charity built the walls.”_  
> _“The test is who maintains them.”_

---

## **XI. Quirky Natures**

- Locals judge tides by color depth
    
- Newcomers assume the water is cursed
    
- Fish from the bay fetch higher prices despite no difference
    
- Red-stained boots are considered lucky
