# Ministry Authority and Continental Jurisdiction

**Issuing Authority:** Office of Continental Charters  
**Administering Body:** Ministerial Secretariat  
**Review Authority:** Charter Courts and Office of Ethics, Review, and Record Integrity  
**Document Classification:** Jurisdictional and Legal Standard  
**Application:** All Ministerial offices, regional branches, field personnel, licensed delvers, and partner authorities  
**Current Standard:** Seventh Jurisdictional Accord  
**Date of Revised Filing:** 7th Cycle, 3388 A.D.

---

## I. Purpose of This Record

The **Ministry of Delving Operations** exercises authority throughout Aerathon.

It does not possess sovereignty over all Aerathon.

This distinction is the foundation of Ministerial law.

The MDO may:

- Regulate access to recognized Labyrinths
    
- License delvers
    
- Restrict dangerous relics
    
- Coordinate cross-border emergencies
    
- Establish containment zones
    
- Investigate Ministerial misconduct
    
- Preserve public records
    
- Enforce the terms of continental charters
    
- Intervene where Labyrinth-related danger exceeds local boundaries
    

It does not ordinarily possess the right to:

- Appoint regional rulers
    
- Rewrite local civil law
    
- Collect universal civilian taxes
    
- Determine religious doctrine
    
- Govern ordinary marriage or inheritance
    
- Seize private property without lawful cause
    
- Replace local courts
    
- Occupy settlements indefinitely
    
- Suppress political opposition unrelated to its mandate
    

The Ministry’s power rests upon charters, treaties, reciprocal obligations, recognized expertise, public service, and emergency necessity.

Its authority is therefore substantial but conditional.

This record establishes:

- The legal sources of Ministerial authority
    
- The boundaries of continental jurisdiction
    
- The relationship between the MDO and sovereign regions
    
- The conditions under which emergency powers may be invoked
    
- The lawful use of search, seizure, quarantine, and access restriction
    
- The rights of individuals and communities subject to Ministerial action
    
- The mechanisms through which Ministerial decisions may be challenged
    

The Ministry’s legitimacy depends not merely upon its ability to act.

It depends upon its willingness to explain why it was entitled to act at all.

## II. Sources of Ministerial Authority

Ministerial authority arises from several overlapping legal foundations.

No single document grants the MDO unrestricted continental power.

### Continental Charters

Continental Charters are multiregional agreements recognizing common obligations concerning:

- Labyrinth safety
    
- Delver regulation
    
- Relic control
    
- Breach reporting
    
- Cross-border rescue
    
- Public warnings
    
- Shared maps
    
- Emergency movement
    
- Ministerial record integrity
    

These charters form the broadest foundation of MDO standing.

### Regional Accords

Regional Accords define how the Ministry operates within a particular nation, territory, House domain, city-state, league, or autonomous community.

They may establish:

- Office locations
    
- Local appointment powers
    
- Delver licensing reciprocity
    
- Site-management responsibilities
    
- Evidence-sharing requirements
    
- Emergency authority
    
- Appeals
    
- Tax or funding arrangements
    
- Cultural restrictions
    
- Limits on armed personnel
    

Regional Accords may differ substantially.

An action lawful in one region may require additional approval in another.

### Municipal and Local Compacts

Cities, towns, villages, ports, clans, temples, guilds, and other authorities may enter agreements with the Ministry.

These compacts may cover:

- Local field stations
    
- Rescue services
    
- Archive access
    
- Labyrinth perimeter support
    
- Public shelters
    
- Communication relays
    
- Cultural consultation
    
- Relic storage
    
- Shared infrastructure
    

### Guild and Professional Charters

The Ministry recognizes guilds, Free Companies, academic bodies, healers, relic handlers, and other professional organizations through formal charters.

These charters create reciprocal rights and obligations.

A licensed organization may receive access, legal recognition, and public standing in exchange for compliance with Ministerial standards.

### Emergency Standing

Emergency standing arises when immediate danger requires temporary authority beyond ordinary jurisdiction.

It is not a permanent source of sovereignty.

### Custodial Authority

The MDO may possess authority over a place, relic, archive, specimen, or route because it has been lawfully entrusted with custody.

Custody does not necessarily equal ownership.

### Contractual Authority

The Ministry may exercise rights created through:

- Employment
    
- Mission contracts
    
- Research agreements
    
- Sponsorship terms
    
- Storage agreements
    
- Rescue arrangements
    

A contract cannot grant authority that the contracting party did not lawfully possess.

## III. Jurisdictional Categories

Ministerial jurisdiction is divided into several practical categories.

### Direct Jurisdiction

The Ministry exercises direct jurisdiction over:

- Ministerial facilities
    
- Official archives
    
- Ministry-operated containment sites
    
- Registered central laboratories
    
- Internal personnel conduct
    
- Ministerial seals and records
    
- Ministry-issued licenses
    
- Property lawfully owned by the MDO
    

Within these areas, Ministerial administrative law ordinarily applies.

### Exclusive Operational Jurisdiction

The Ministry may receive exclusive control over a site or operation through charter, emergency declaration, or custodial agreement.

Examples include:

- A sealed Labyrinth entrance
    
- A relic-containment vault
    
- A temporary breach perimeter
    
- A quarantined structure
    
- A classified research station
    

Exclusive operational jurisdiction should be limited by:

- Purpose
    
- Place
    
- Duration
    
- Review
    
- Dissolution conditions
    

### Concurrent Jurisdiction

Most Ministerial activity occurs under concurrent jurisdiction.

This means the MDO and a regional or local authority both possess lawful standing.

Examples include:

- A Labyrinth within a sovereign city
    
- A relic-related criminal investigation
    
- A cross-border delver dispute
    
- A cultural site threatened by anomaly
    
- A public evacuation
    

Concurrent jurisdiction requires coordination rather than assumption of supremacy.

### Advisory Standing

The Ministry may possess recognized expertise without enforcement authority.

In such cases, it may:

- Investigate with permission
    
- Issue recommendations
    
- Publish advisories
    
- Offer mediation
    
- Provide technical support
    

A recommendation must not be presented as a binding order.

### Emergency Jurisdiction

Emergency jurisdiction is temporary authority activated by immediate and substantial danger.

Its scope must remain tied to the emergency that justified it.

### Disputed Jurisdiction

Where the Ministry and another recognized authority disagree over standing, personnel should seek:

- Written clarification
    
- Temporary preservation measures
    
- Charter Counsel review
    
- Neutral mediation
    
- Tribunal decision
    

Disputed jurisdiction does not automatically permit either side to destroy evidence, endanger civilians, or escalate force.

## V. Matters Outside Ordinary Ministerial Standing

The MDO does not ordinarily possess direct authority over:

- Regional succession
    
- Ordinary criminal law
    
- Civil disputes unrelated to its mandate
    
- Marriage
    
- Inheritance
    
- Religious office
    
- Local taxation
    
- Electoral procedure
    
- Domestic property disputes
    
- Guild business unrelated to Ministerial charter
    
- Cultural leadership
    
- Internal community membership
    
- Ordinary military command
    

Ministerial expertise may be requested in these matters.

Expertise does not create jurisdiction by itself.

### Incidental Connection

A distant or trivial connection to a Labyrinth does not automatically place an entire dispute under MDO control.

For example:

- A sword once recovered from a Labyrinth does not make every later theft of that sword a continental matter.
    
- A licensed delver’s marriage dispute is not ordinarily subject to Delver Affairs.
    
- A religious argument involving a relic symbol does not necessarily create relic jurisdiction.
    
- A trade dispute does not become Ministerial merely because one merchant supplies expedition rations.
    

The claimed connection must be material.

## VI. Sovereign Regions and Political Authorities

Aerathon’s greater regions and local polities retain distinct systems of government.

The Ministry operates through them, beside them, and sometimes against their objection.

It does not replace them.

### Recognition of Sovereignty

The MDO recognizes lawful regional authority over ordinary civic affairs.

Recognition does not require approval of every regional law or ruler.

### Regional Variation

Ministerial personnel must understand:

- Local law
    
- Local offices
    
- Forms of address
    
- Court procedure
    
- Property rules
    
- Cultural authority
    
- Religious protections
    
- Regional enforcement limits
    

A standard Ministerial practice may be unlawful or politically inflammatory in a particular region.

### Official Liaison

Major operations should appoint a liaison to the relevant local authority.

The liaison’s responsibilities include:

- Notification
    
- Permit coordination
    
- Legal interpretation
    
- Evidence sharing
    
- Public communication
    
- Dispute prevention
    

### Local Refusal

A region may lawfully refuse Ministerial access where:

- No applicable charter grants standing
    
- No emergency exists
    
- The request exceeds scope
    
- Cultural restrictions apply
    
- Required procedures were ignored
    
- Ministerial misconduct remains unresolved
    

The Ministry may challenge the refusal through legal or diplomatic channels.

It may not simply declare every refusal a threat to continental safety.

## VII. Regional Ministerial Offices

Regional Ministerial Offices act as the principal bridge between continental authority and local law.

### Regional Minister

A Regional Minister coordinates Ministerial policy within their assigned jurisdiction.

They may:

- Negotiate local implementation
    
- Issue regional guidance
    
- Authorize qualifying operations
    
- Coordinate offices
    
- Invoke limited emergency measures
    
- Represent the MDO before regional authorities
    

They do not rule the region.

### Local Appointment

Regional Accords may require that some Ministerial offices include:

- Local appointees
    
- Regional scholars
    
- Government representatives
    
- Community consultants
    
- Guild delegates
    

### Regional Independence

Regional offices may adapt procedures to local need.

They may not suspend:

- Public petition rights
    
- Archive integrity
    
- Research ethics
    
- Anti-retaliation standards
    
- Review of emergency powers

## VIII. Emergency Standing

Emergency standing is the MDO’s most powerful and controversial authority.

It may be invoked only where delay creates a credible risk of severe harm.

### Qualifying Emergencies

Emergency standing may be justified by:

- Active breach
    
- Uncontrolled Labyrinth expansion
    
- Planar incursion
    
- Relic detonation or instability
    
- Mass magical contamination
    
- Anomalous epidemic
    
- Collapse of a containment site
    
- Cross-border hostile entity
    
- Immediate danger to major infrastructure
    
- Large-scale evacuation need
    
- Credible continental chain reaction
    

### Non-Qualifying Grounds

Emergency standing may not be invoked merely because:

- A local authority is uncooperative
    
- Research access was denied
    
- A guild concealed embarrassing information
    
- A Ministerial deadline is approaching
    
- A regional law is inconvenient
    
- Public criticism has intensified
    
- A relic is valuable
    
- A political dispute is urgent but not dangerous
    

### Declaration

An emergency declaration should identify:

- Issuing authority
    
- Legal basis
    
- Location
    
- Threat
    
- Scope
    
- Powers invoked
    
- Duration
    
- Review time
    
- Conditions for termination
    

### Immediate Declaration

Where delay would cause imminent harm, a qualified field officer may declare limited emergency standing orally or through field seal.

The declaration must be documented at the earliest safe opportunity.

### Duration

Emergency powers expire unless renewed.

Renewal requires evidence that:

- The emergency continues
    
- The powers remain necessary
    
- Less restrictive measures are inadequate
    

### Review

Every major emergency declaration should receive independent review after the immediate crisis.

## IX. Emergency Powers

Depending upon the charter and threat, emergency standing may permit:

- Temporary access restriction
    
- Evacuation
    
- Quarantine
    
- Road or airspace closure
    
- Relic seizure
    
- Temporary detention
    
- Mandatory hazard screening
    
- Supply requisition
    
- Communication control
    
- Establishment of field command
    
- Entry into private property
    
- Destruction of dangerous material
    
- Suspension of delver licenses
    
- Cross-border pursuit of an active anomaly
    

These powers should be interpreted narrowly.

### Necessity and Proportionality

An emergency action must be:

- Connected to the threat
    
- Necessary
    
- Proportionate
    
- Time-limited
    
- Recorded
    
- Reviewable
    

### Least Harmful Means

Where several actions would address the danger, personnel should choose the one that creates the least unnecessary harm.

### Compensation

Lawful emergency action may still create a duty to compensate for:

- Requisitioned supplies
    
- Damaged property
    
- Destroyed structures
    
- Disrupted business
    
- Improper detention
    
- Ministry-caused injury

## X. Quarantine and Containment Zones

The Ministry may establish quarantine or containment zones where entry or exit creates a credible hazard.

### Required Elements

A lawful zone should identify:

- Boundary
    
- Reason
    
- Issuing authority
    
- Entry rules
    
- Exit rules
    
- Medical support
    
- Supply plan
    
- Communication rights
    
- Review schedule
    
- Expected duration
    

### Humane Conditions

Persons within quarantine retain rights to:

- Food
    
- Water
    
- Shelter
    
- Medical care
    
- Communication where safe
    
- Information
    
- Review
    
- Respectful treatment
    

### Cultural and Bodily Needs

Quarantine planning should account for:

- Diet
    
- Temperature
    
- Humidity
    
- mobility
    
- sensory needs
    
- religious practice
    
- family structure
    
- lineage-specific care
    
- metamorphosis
    
- wing or membrane maintenance
    
- aquatic or subterranean requirements
    

### Punitive Quarantine

Quarantine may not be used as punishment, coercion, or political isolation.

## XI. Search and Inspection

Ministerial officers may inspect persons, property, sites, records, or cargo only where lawful standing exists.

### Consent Inspection

A person or authority may voluntarily permit inspection.

Consent should be:

- Informed
    
- Documented where significant
    
- Limited to the stated scope
    
- Revocable before completion unless evidence has lawfully entered custody
    

### Administrative Inspection

Some charters permit routine inspection of:

- Licensed guild facilities
    
- Relic transport
    
- Ministerial contractors
    
- Registered containment sites
    
- Delver equipment
    
- Public safety systems
    

Administrative inspection must remain tied to the regulated activity.

### Warranted Search

A search warrant may authorize inspection where there is sufficient reason to believe that evidence, prohibited material, or an active hazard is present.

The warrant should identify:

- Place
    
- Subject
    
- Grounds
    
- Scope
    
- Issuing authority
    
- Duration
    
- Seizure limits
    

### Emergency Search

Personnel may enter without prior warrant where delay creates immediate danger to life, public safety, or containment.

The emergency basis must be recorded.

### Cultural Sites

Search of sacred, funerary, or culturally protected sites requires additional care.

Where time allows, the Ministry should involve appropriate custodians or cultural liaisons.

## XII. Seizure and Custody

The Ministry may seize objects, records, specimens, or materials where lawful authority exists.

### Grounds for Seizure

Possible grounds include:

- Immediate danger
    
- Illegal relic trafficking
    
- Evidence preservation
    
- Containment failure
    
- Breach-related contamination
    
- Violation of license conditions
    
- Court order
    
- Custodial agreement
    
- Abandoned hazardous material
    

### Seizure Record

The seizing officer should record:

- Item
    
- Location
    
- Owner or custodian
    
- Grounds
    
- Condition
    
- Hazards
    
- Witnesses
    
- Destination
    
- Review process
    

### Temporary Seizure

Temporary seizure should not be treated as permanent forfeiture.

### Cultural Ownership

An object may be dangerous and still belong lawfully to a community.

Containment does not automatically extinguish cultural, religious, or property rights.

### Return

Seized property should be returned when:

- Danger is resolved
    
- Evidence is no longer required
    
- Legal authority expires
    
- Containment can be maintained by the owner
    
- Review determines seizure was improper
    

### Restitution

Improper seizure may require:

- Return
    
- Compensation
    
- Repair
    
- Public correction
    
- Disciplinary action

## XIII. Relic Jurisdiction

Relics often create overlapping claims among:

- Discoverers
    
- Landowners
    
- Communities
    
- Temples
    
- Regional governments
    
- Sponsors
    
- Guilds
    
- The Ministry
    

### Discovery Does Not Equal Ownership

A delver who finds a relic does not automatically own it.

Ownership may depend upon:

- Site law
    
- Expedition contract
    
- Cultural claim
    
- Regional law
    
- Hazard class
    
- Ministerial restriction
    
- Prior custody
    

### Hazardous Relics

The MDO may restrict or seize relics that present:

- Mass-casualty risk
    
- Planar instability
    
- uncontrolled magical spread
    
- sentient coercion
    
- contamination
    
- reality distortion
    
- breach activation
    

### Cultural Relics

Relics central to living cultural, religious, or ancestral practice require consultation before permanent removal where safety permits.

### Commercial Relics

Commercial value does not reduce regulatory authority.

### Research Access

Ministerial custody does not mean unrestricted research use.

Sacred, personal, or hazardous limits may still apply.

## XIV. Detention

The MDO is not a general police authority.

It may detain persons only within lawful jurisdiction.

### Grounds

Possible grounds include:

- Immediate interference with containment
    
- Relic trafficking
    
- Breach sabotage
    
- Active delver-license violation
    
- Threat to evidence
    
- Escape from lawful quarantine
    
- Danger caused by anomalous influence
    
- Warrant or tribunal order
    

### Duration

Detention should last no longer than necessary to:

- Stabilize the threat
    
- Verify identity
    
- Transfer to lawful authority
    
- Complete urgent screening
    
- Preserve immediate evidence
    

### Rights

A detained person should receive:

- Reason for detention
    
- Identity of the detaining office
    
- Review process
    
- Medical care
    
- Safe conditions
    
- Communication where lawful
    
- Transfer information
    

### Anomalous Influence

A person affected by curse, possession, memory alteration, or relic coercion should not automatically be treated as criminally responsible.

Containment and investigation may still be necessary.

## XV. Ministerial Warrants and Orders

Formal orders may include:

- Search warrants
    
- Seizure orders
    
- Site-closure orders
    
- Quarantine orders
    
- License suspensions
    
- Relic transport restrictions
    
- Summons
    
- Preservation orders
    
- Emergency directives
    

### Validity

A valid order should identify:

- Issuing office
    
- Legal basis
    
- Subject
    
- Scope
    
- Duration
    
- Appeals route
    
- Seal
    

### Verbal Orders

Verbal orders may be valid during immediate danger.

They should be reduced to written form afterward.

### Invalid Orders

Personnel should not enforce orders that are clearly:

- Forged
    
- Outside jurisdiction
    
- Expired
    
- Unrelated to stated emergency
    
- Issued by an unauthorized office
    
- Designed to conceal misconduct

## XVI. Delver Licensing Jurisdiction

A Ministry-issued license carries continental recognition only where applicable charters provide reciprocity.

### Licensing Powers

The MDO may:

- Issue
    
- Renew
    
- Suspend
    
- Restrict
    
- Revoke
    
- Reclassify
    
- Recognize regional licenses
    

### Grounds for Discipline

Possible grounds include:

- Reckless conduct
    
- Falsified reporting
    
- Relic theft
    
- Unauthorized entry
    
- abandonment of duty
    
- harm to civilians
    
- cultural violation
    
- prohibited broadcast
    
- interference with rescue
    
- repeated safety failure
    

### Due Process

Except during immediate danger, a delver should receive:

- Notice
    
- Allegations
    
- Evidence summary
    
- Opportunity to respond
    
- Appeal route
    

### Emergency Suspension

A license may be suspended immediately where continued activity creates danger.

Emergency suspension requires prompt review.

## XVII. Cross-Border Movement

The MDO coordinates movement across regional boundaries during authorized operations.

### Emergency Passage

Applicable charters may permit:

- Rescue teams
    
- Containment personnel
    
- Hazard transport
    
- evacuation convoys
    
- medical units
    
- warning couriers
    

to cross borders under expedited procedure.

### Notification

Emergency passage should be communicated to relevant authorities as soon as practical.

### Pursuit

The Ministry may continue pursuit of an active anomaly, dangerous relic, or person directly causing a qualifying threat across borders where delay would create serious danger.

This power does not authorize general criminal pursuit unrelated to the Ministerial mandate.

### Refuge and Asylum

Ministerial facilities are not automatically embassies or asylum sites.

They may provide temporary protective shelter where immediate danger exists, subject to later legal review.

## XVIII. Relationship with Regional Military and Guards

The Ministry may coordinate with regional forces.

It does not automatically command them.

### Joint Operations

A joint-operation order should establish:

- Command relationship
    
- Mission objective
    
- Rules of engagement
    
- Evidence custody
    
- Arrest authority
    
- withdrawal conditions
    
- Public communication
    

### Ministerial Armed Personnel

MDO armed personnel are primarily assigned to:

- Site security
    
- rescue
    
- containment
    
- convoy protection
    
- emergency defense
    

They are not intended as a general occupation force.

### Regional Refusal of Support

A regional authority may refuse military participation unless a binding charter or emergency obligation applies.

### Conflict of Command

Where regional and Ministerial commanders issue conflicting orders, personnel should follow the written joint mandate where one exists.

## XIX. Cultural and Community Jurisdiction

Some communities possess authority not derived from ordinary regional government.

These may include:

- Clans
    
- Houses
    
- autonomous settlements
    
- temple custodians
    
- migratory peoples
    
- village councils
    
- ancestral guardians
    
- recognized cultural orders
    

### Cultural Standing

The Ministry should recognize relevant community authority concerning:

- Sacred sites
    
- burial grounds
    
- cultural materials
    
- restricted knowledge
    
- internal membership
    
- local routes
    
- ritual access
    

### Political Nonrecognition

A regional government’s refusal to recognize a community does not automatically erase that community’s cultural standing before the MDO.

### Limits

Cultural authority does not automatically permit:

- Concealment of immediate continental danger
    
- coercion
    
- trafficking
    
- harm to nonconsenting persons
    
- destruction of critical evidence
    

These conflicts require careful review rather than automatic dismissal of either side.

## XX. Archive and Information Jurisdiction

The Ministry possesses authority to protect the integrity of its own records.

### Archive Integrity

The MDO may investigate:

- Forgery
    
- suppression
    
- unauthorized alteration
    
- destruction
    
- seal misuse
    
- concealed revisions
    
- release of sealed material
    

### External Records

The Ministry does not own every document it authenticates.

Authentication does not transfer ownership.

### Dangerous Information

Access may be restricted where release would create:

- Relic replication
    
- breach activation
    
- mass panic
    
- vulnerable-site exposure
    
- active operational risk
    
- targeted persecution
    

### Political Embarrassment

Embarrassment is not a lawful basis for sealing a record.

Ministerial decisions may be challenged.

### Who May Appeal

Appeals may be filed by:

- Affected individuals
    
- communities
    
- guilds
    
- governments
    
- property owners
    
- licensed delvers
    
- research participants
    
- partner institutions
    
- Ministerial personnel
    

### Appealable Actions

These may include:

- License suspension
    
- property seizure
    
- access denial
    
- quarantine
    
- classification
    
- record restriction
    
- research misconduct
    
- emergency declaration
    
- compensation decision
    
- jurisdictional claim
    

### Filing Routes

Appeals may be submitted through:

- Regional Ministerial Offices
    
- Office of Continental Charters
    
- Community Petition Chamber
    
- Charter Courts
    
- Office of Public Account and Civic Relations
    
- Inspectorate channels
    

### Emergency Actions

An appeal does not always suspend an emergency action.

It should receive expedited review where continued enforcement creates serious hardship.

### Written Reasons

Major decisions should provide written reasons sufficient for meaningful challenge.

## XXII. Charter Courts and Tribunals

Charter Courts interpret Ministerial authority and resolve disputes involving:

- Jurisdiction
    
- regional accords
    
- licenses
    
- seizures
    
- emergency powers
    
- Ministerial contracts
    
- interoffice conflict
    
- public petitions
    

### Independence

Judicial review should remain institutionally separate from the office whose action is challenged.

### Regional Participation

Where local law is materially involved, regional judges, magistrates, or legal advisers may participate according to the applicable accord.

### Temporary Tribunals

Major cross-border incidents may establish temporary interregional tribunals.

### Remedies

A court or tribunal may:

- Uphold
    
- narrow
    
- suspend
    
- reverse
    
- remand
    
- order compensation
    
- require correction
    
- refer misconduct
    
- alter access conditions

## XXIII. Compensation and Restitution

Ministerial action may cause lawful but compensable harm.

### Compensable Loss

Possible claims include:

- Requisitioned property
    
- damaged structures
    
- destroyed goods
    
- business interruption
    
- relocation
    
- improper detention
    
- wrongful seizure
    
- research harm
    
- medical injury
    
- archive error
    

### Limits

Compensation may be reduced where:

- The claimant caused the danger
    
- The property was unlawfully possessed
    
- The loss was unavoidable and broadly shared
    
- Fraud occurred
    

### Nonmonetary Repair

Restitution may include:

- Return
    
- restoration
    
- correction
    
- public acknowledgment
    
- preservation funding
    
- access reform
    
- community-led review

## XXV. Abuse of Authority

Abuse may include:

- Inventing jurisdiction
    
- extending emergency powers without cause
    
- coercive research
    
- unauthorized seizure
    
- punitive quarantine
    
- concealment of legal basis
    
- retaliation
    
- warrant falsification
    
- improper detention
    
- occupation for political leverage
    
- using public safety as pretext for commercial gain
    
- suppressing lawful criticism
    
- withholding essential service to compel compliance
    

### Responsibility

Personnel are responsible not only for unlawful orders they issue but also for orders they knowingly enforce without defensible basis.

### Superior Orders

“Following orders” does not excuse conduct that is clearly outside Ministerial mandate or grossly abusive.

## XXVI. Withdrawal of Ministerial Authority

Ministerial standing may end when:

- The emergency concludes
    
- The charter expires
    
- The operation is dissolved
    
- Custody is transferred
    
- A court revokes authority
    
- Regional permission is withdrawn
    
- The mandate is fulfilled
    

### Closing Actions

Before withdrawal, the MDO should address:

- Evidence transfer
    
- property return
    
- compensation
    
- remaining hazards
    
- public notice
    
- record preservation
    
- local handoff
    

### Indefinite Presence

A temporary field office should not become permanent merely through bureaucratic inertia.

Permanent presence requires a lawful continuing basis.

## XXVII. Common Jurisdictional Misunderstandings

### “The MDO governs all of Aerathon.”

False.

The Ministry exercises continental authority within defined mandates.

### “Any Labyrinth connection gives the Ministry control.”

False.

The connection must be material and legally relevant.

### “Emergency standing overrides every law.”

False.

Emergency powers remain limited by necessity, proportionality, scope, and review.

### “A Ministry seal makes any order lawful.”

False.

The issuing office must possess authority.

### “Relics always belong to the MDO.”

False.

The Ministry may regulate, contain, or temporarily seize without possessing permanent ownership.

### “Regional governments may always exclude the Ministry.”

False.

Binding charters and qualifying emergencies may create lawful Ministerial standing despite objection.

### “Local law never applies inside a Ministry operation.”

False.

Concurrent jurisdiction is common.

### “An appeal automatically stops enforcement.”

False.

Emergency actions may continue during expedited review.

## XXIX. Jurisdictional Checklist

Before taking coercive action, personnel should ask:

### Authority

- What grants us standing?
    
- Does this office possess the power being used?
    
- Is another authority also responsible?
    

### Necessity

- What harm are we preventing?
    
- Is action required now?
    
- Is there a less restrictive option?
    

### Scope

- Who and what are affected?
    
- How long will the action last?
    
- What is outside the mandate?
    

### Rights

- Have affected persons been informed?
    
- Can they challenge the action?
    
- Are essential needs protected?
    

### Record

- Who issued the order?
    
- What evidence supports it?
    
- When will it be reviewed?
    

A coercive act that cannot answer these questions should not proceed except where immediate danger leaves no alternative.

## XXX. Summary Doctrine

Ministerial authority is governed by six obligations:

1. **Identify the lawful basis of action.**
    
2. **Distinguish expertise from jurisdiction.**
    
3. **Use emergency power only for genuine emergency.**
    
4. **Limit coercion to what necessity requires.**
    
5. **Preserve appeal, review, and accountability.**
    
6. **Withdraw when the authority to remain has ended.**
    

The MDO was created because Aerathon required coordination beyond borders.

It must not use that necessity to erase the borders, peoples, governments, and communities it was formed to protect.
