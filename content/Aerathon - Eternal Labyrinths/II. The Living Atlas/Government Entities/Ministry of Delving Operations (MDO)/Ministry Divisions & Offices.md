# Ministry Divisions and Offices

**Common Reference:** MDO Organizational Charter  
**Institutional Classification:** Administrative and Jurisdictional Registry  
**Issuing Authority:** Office of Continental Charters  
**Application:** All central, regional, field, research, and contracted Ministerial bodies  
**Current Structure:** Seventh Ministerial Reorganization  
**Date of Revised Filing:** 7th Cycle, 3388 A.D.

---

## I. Purpose of This Record

The **Ministry of Delving Operations** is not governed as a single descending military chain.

Its work is divided among directorates, offices, regional ministries, field commands, review councils, archives, laboratories, and temporary expedition authorities whose mandates frequently overlap.

This structure reflects the Ministry’s central difficulty:

It must study what it may also be required to contain.

It must regulate the delvers upon whom it depends.

It must preserve dangerous knowledge without permitting that knowledge to become another public hazard.

It must coordinate sovereign regions without claiming ordinary sovereignty over them.

No single division can fulfill these responsibilities alone.

This record establishes:

- The principal branches of the MDO
    
- Their general mandates
    
- Their relationship to one another
    
- The distinction between central, regional, and field authority
    
- The offices empowered to review Ministerial conduct
    
- The limits of internal hierarchy
    
- The procedure by which temporary joint commands are formed
    

Detailed procedures, rank requirements, and legal powers remain filed separately.

## II. Principles of Ministerial Organization

The Ministry’s structure follows four principles.

### Distributed Authority

No directorate possesses complete authority over every aspect of an operation.

A Labyrinth expedition may require simultaneous approval or consultation from operations, delver affairs, relic specialists, cartographers, regional officers, legal counsel, and research ethics staff.

This may slow action.

It also prevents one office from quietly defining danger, authorizing entry, controlling evidence, and judging its own conduct without outside review.

### Mandate Before Rank

Rank determines responsibility within an office.

Mandate determines authority across offices.

A senior archivist does not command a breach team merely by holding a higher civil grade. A field warden does not overrule a cultural-restrictions order merely because the matter occurs inside an operational perimeter.

Personnel are expected to know when their authority ends.

### Regional Adaptation

Ministerial organization varies by region.

An Oric office may emphasize winter rescue, House compacts, and remote route maintenance. An Al’Ari office may devote more personnel to maritime breaches, port law, and island evacuation. A Vinyoti office may maintain river, wetland, agricultural, and trade specialists unavailable elsewhere.

Regional variation is permitted so long as core charter duties remain fulfilled.

### Review Without Permission

Oversight bodies do not require permission from the office they are reviewing.

This principle applies especially to:

- Ethics investigations
    
- Archive challenges
    
- Emergency-power review
    
- Research misconduct
    
- Relic-chain audits
    
- Whistleblower petitions
    
- Correction and retraction proceedings
    

An institution cannot claim accountability while allowing every office to decide when it may be examined.

## III. Central Coordinating Authority

### The High Ministry

The **High Ministry** is the central coordinating authority of the MDO.

It does not administer every expedition, archive, or regional office directly. Its responsibilities are instead continental in scale:

- Maintaining the founding charters
    
- Coordinating interregional policy
    
- Resolving disputes between major directorates
    
- Approving extraordinary emergency standing
    
- Negotiating continental compacts
    
- Presenting the Ministry’s annual public account
    
- Appointing or confirming major directors
    
- Ensuring continuity during continental crises
    

The High Ministry is led by three principal ministers whose offices are distinct but interdependent.

|Office|Present Holder|Principal Responsibility|
|---|---|---|
|**High Minister of Operations**|**Director Halin Drauve**|Continental Labyrinth coordination, emergency readiness, funding allocation, and operational continuity|
|**High Minister of Record**|**Archivist Emeritus Venn Tarras**|Living Atlas integrity, Annals stewardship, public record, research continuity, and archival access|
|**High Minister of Concord**|**Magistrate Talar Shellmark**|Charter interpretation, regional agreements, jurisdictional disputes, appeals, and intergovernmental legitimacy|

Collectively, these offices are called the **Ministerial Concordance**.

The term replaced the older **Triumvirate of Descent**, whose title implied a narrower delver-centered institution and whose authority became excessively concentrated during the Fifth Ministerial Structure.

### Limits of the Concordance

The Concordance may coordinate, mediate, suspend, or refer.

It may not erase evidence, prohibit lawful review, or permanently settle a scholarly dispute by decree.

Its emergency decisions remain subject to later legal and archival examination.

No High Minister may simultaneously direct the office responsible for investigating their conduct.

## IV. The Ministerial Secretariat

The **Ministerial Secretariat** maintains the connective work that allows the Ministry’s divisions to function as one institution.

### Office of Continental Charters

**Counselor General:** Magistrate Talar Shellmark

The Office of Continental Charters interprets the treaties, compacts, emergency provisions, and jurisdictional agreements under which the MDO operates.

Its work includes:

- Charter interpretation
    
- Regional legal agreements
    
- Jurisdictional review
    
- Appeals
    
- Emergency-standing authorization
    
- Interregional arbitration
    
- Drafting Ministerial compacts
    
- Reviewing the legal basis of public orders
    

The office may determine whether the Ministry possesses authority to act.

It does not determine whether every lawful action is wise.

### Office of Internal Administration

This office manages:

- Personnel records
    
- Civil grades
    
- Payroll and pensions
    
- Procurement
    
- Ministerial property
    
- Internal transport
    
- Standard seals
    
- Appointment records
    
- Interoffice communication
    

Its work is rarely celebrated and nearly universal.

### Office of Public Account and Civic Relations

This office publishes public summaries of Ministerial activity and maintains routes through which civilians may:

- Request records
    
- Submit complaints
    
- Petition for correction
    
- Ask for the legal basis of an order
    
- Seek compensation review
    
- Appeal access restrictions
    
- Request local consultation
    

It also oversees official public statements, though operational warnings may be issued directly by authorized field offices.

### Chronometric Inquiry Office

**Chair:** Chronologist Eddric Vane

The Chronometric Inquiry Office maintains the unified Aerathonic calendar used in Ministerial filing and investigates:

- Conflicting chronologies
    
- Temporal anomalies
    
- Labyrinth time distortion
    
- Dating methods
    
- Era conversion
    
- Records whose filing sequence has been corrupted
    

Archivist Yelna Stonevoice serves as a senior consultant where oral chronology and Echo-Law intersect.

## V. Directorate of Labyrinth Operations

The **Directorate of Labyrinth Operations** is responsible for immediate action around known Labyrinths, breaches, anomalous incursions, and active containment sites.

It is the most visibly militarized branch of the Ministry, though most of its personnel are engineers, healers, logisticians, surveyors, handlers, wardens, and rescue specialists rather than soldiers.

### Principal Mandate

- Breach containment
    
- Site security
    
- Expedition clearance
    
- Emergency response
    
- Search and rescue
    
- Evacuation corridors
    
- Perimeter control
    
- Operational mapping
    
- Environmental monitoring
    
- Field logistics
    
- Closure and quarantine enforcement
    

### Arcane Breach Division

**Senior Liaison:** Lt. Salvia Korrin

The Arcane Breach Division responds to spatial distortions, containment failures, unstable magical zones, and Labyrinth effects that exceed ordinary field capacity.

Its specialists include:

- Breach liaisons
    
- Forensic thaumaturges
    
- Prism technicians
    
- Null-field engineers
    
- Quarantine wardens
    
- Environmental hazard officers
    
- Spatial cartographers
    

Notable officers include:

- **Lt. Salvia Korrin** — Arcane Breach liaison and field coordination officer
    
- **Inspector Jao “Greyglass” Vell** — Forensic thaumaturge specializing in illusion residue and false-space analysis
    
- **Warden Cyra Hesh** — Quarantine and environmental hazard authority
    

### Field Containment Service

The Field Containment Service establishes perimeters, stabilizes hazardous sites, and coordinates with regional guards, local authorities, and licensed delvers.

Its authority is strongest during active incidents and narrows after immediate danger has passed.

### Rescue and Recovery Office

This office coordinates:

- Delver extraction
    
- Civilian rescue
    
- Missing-person recovery
    
- Body retrieval
    
- Survivor stabilization
    
- Route reopening
    
- Family notification
    

Its personnel frequently work alongside the Directorate of Delver Affairs and regional medical offices.

### Operational Cartography Office

This office produces maps intended for immediate use during expeditions, evacuations, and containment.

Its work differs from the Living Atlas Cartographic Survey, which maintains long-term public and geographic records.

Operational maps may privilege speed over completeness.

They must be clearly marked as provisional.

- Contract disputes

- Anomaly screening
    
- Psychological care
    
- Reintegration support

## VII. Living Atlas Directorate

The **Living Atlas Directorate** records Aerathon as it presently exists.

Its work concerns the modern world: mapped, inhabited, governed, practiced, remembered, and contested.

It operates beneath the [[The Living Atlas - Research Charter|Living Atlas Research Charter]].

### Principal Mandate

- Geographic documentation
    
- Peoples and cultures
    
- Governments and institutions
    
- Faith and worship
    
- Economies and trade
    
- Civilian life
    
- Modern magic and technology
    
- Bestiary records
    
- Public reference standards
    
- Accessibility documentation
    

### Office of Folk Anthropology and Community Consultation

This office studies peoples, cultures, languages, kinship, identity, migration, ritual, and social practice.

It is also responsible for ensuring that cultural research includes meaningful consultation with living communities.

Its researchers may not treat Ministerial classification as the source of a people’s legitimacy.

### Cartographic Survey

The Cartographic Survey maintains long-term maps of:

- Regions
    
- Borders
    
- Settlements
    
- Roads
    
- Rivers
    
- Coastlines
    
- Air routes
    
- Maritime routes
    
- Hazard zones
    
- Public infrastructure
    

Political claims and geographic observations must be distinguished where borders are disputed.

### Office of Governments and Civic Institutions

This office documents:

- Governments
    
- Houses
    
- Councils
    
- Leagues
    
- Courts
    
- Civic authorities
    
- Recognized autonomous communities
    
- Public administrative systems
    

It does not determine whether an authority is morally legitimate merely by recording that it exists.

### Faith and Living Cosmology Bureau

**Lead Analyst:** Priest-Analyst Thera Daan

This bureau studies active religions, cults, divine phenomena, faith-field behavior, sacred law, and contemporary cosmological belief.

It works closely with Annals and Antiquities when modern practice depends upon ancient doctrine.

### Office of Civil Life, Trade, and Infrastructure

This office records the systems through which ordinary people live:

- Markets
    
- Labor
    
- Food
    
- Housing
    
- Transit
    
- Education
    
- Communication
    
- Public health
    
- Regional infrastructure
    

Clerk Senna Virel remains one of its senior statisticians and maintains several major population and market series.

### Bestiary and Ecological Records Office

This office documents known fauna, monsters, magical organisms, invasive species, and environmental threats.

It works with Arcane and Anomalous Research when a creature’s nature cannot be separated from Labyrinth or planar influence.

## VIII. Directorate of Annals and Antiquities

The **Directorate of Annals and Antiquities** reconstructs Aerathon’s past from incomplete, damaged, contradictory, and politically shaped evidence.

Its records include ancient peoples, vanished institutions, disputed eras, relic provenance, old magical systems, fallen states, and early Labyrinth theories.

### Principal Mandate

- Historical reconstruction
    
- Archaeology
    
- Ancient languages
    
- Relic provenance
    
- Archive preservation
    
- Oral history
    
- Disputed chronology
    
- Material conservation
    
- Extinct institutions
    

### Historical Reconstruction Office

**Senior Archivist:** Yelna Stonevoice

This office compares documentary, oral, material, and environmental evidence to reconstruct past events.

It is especially responsible for preserving competing interpretations when no final consensus exists.

### Antiquities Restoration Bureau

**Director:** Professor Arav Dhal-Senn

This bureau conserves and studies damaged artifacts, inscriptions, structures, and historical materials.

**Curator Rook-of-Rings** serves as a senior relic registrar and provenance specialist.

Restoration must not conceal the difference between original material and modern reconstruction.

### Office of Oral Traditions and Echo-Law

This office preserves spoken history, inherited testimony, ritual recitations, songs, genealogies, and traditions carried through memory rather than writing.

Its work rejects the older assumption that written records are inherently more truthful than oral ones.

### Dynastic and Institutional Records Office

**Senior Chronicler:** Ione Aurel

This office records the rise, fracture, succession, and collapse of governments, Houses, guilds, temple authorities, and other long-lived institutions.

### Legacy Archivists Registry

The registry preserves the seals and service histories of deceased researchers whose signatures remain attached to valid historical filings.

These marks are called **heritage seals**.

Recognized legacy archivists include:

- Scribe Ellisar Vale
    
- Archivist Prim Dorne
    
- Field Commander Maern Vosk
    
- High Curator Selene Kael
    
- Archivist Thelen Yarrow
    

A heritage seal verifies historical origin.

It does not exempt the associated record from modern review.

- Scrytech

### Relic Analysis and Containment Office

**Chief Handler:** Myra Keln

This office evaluates relic function, risk, provenance, transport, storage, and lawful release.

It maintains chain-of-custody standards and coordinates with Operations when an object presents immediate danger.

This office studies:

- Astral crossings

- Extradimensional organisms

- Reality displacement
    
- Observer-dependent environments

### Labyrinth Ecology Bureau

This bureau studies Labyrinth environments as active systems rather than collections of isolated rooms and monsters.

Its researchers examine:

- Food chains
    
- Reproduction
    
- Territory
    
- Environmental cycles
    
- Adaptive architecture
    
- Anomalous weather
    
- Ecological contamination
    

### Scrytech and Arcane Systems Office

This office studies arcane communication, observation networks, broadcast infrastructure, remote sensing, and magical systems used by the Ministry and its partners.

It works with the Arcane Broadcast Authority where public transmission and regulatory jurisdiction overlap.

### Faith-Field and Divine Anomaly Unit

This unit investigates measurable divine or faith-linked phenomena without declaring theological doctrine true or false.

Dr. Odrin Sallow serves as a senior arcane safety auditor.

## XIII. Regional Ministerial Offices

Each greater region maintains one or more **Regional Ministerial Offices**, commonly abbreviated as **RMOs**.

RMOs translate continental policy into local practice and maintain the relationships upon which Ministerial legitimacy depends.

They are not identical copies of a Louvain central office.

### Regional Responsibilities

- Local charter interpretation
    
- Regional research
    
- Delver licensing
    
- Breach response
    
- Public archives
    
- Cultural consultation
    
- Route advisories
    
- Government liaison
    
- Rescue coordination
    
- Regional hiring
    

### Regional Minister

A **Regional Minister** coordinates the major MDO functions within a region.

They do not rule that region.

Their authority depends upon applicable charters, local agreements, and the specific matter under review.

### Regional Councils

Most RMOs maintain advisory councils composed of some combination of:

- Local government representatives
    
- Community scholars
    
- Delver guilds
    
- Medical authorities
    
- Religious custodians
    
- Engineers
    
- Regional researchers
    
- Civilian petitioners
    

Council recommendations may be nonbinding, but dismissing them without written justification is considered poor Ministerial practice.

## XIV. Field Structure and Ranks

Field titles describe responsibility during active assignments.

They do not always correspond directly to civil grade.

|Field Title|General Function|
|---|---|
|**Field Minister**|Coordinates a major multi-office operation or temporary regional crisis authority|
|**Regional Warden**|Maintains a Labyrinth perimeter, quarantine zone, or high-risk operational district|
|**Field Commander**|Directs a defined expedition, rescue, containment, or security operation|
|**Inspector**|Conducts legal, relic, breach, ethics, or archive investigations within a defined mandate|
|**Keeper / Archivist**|Authenticates, interprets, preserves, or supervises official records and evidence|
|**Researcher / Specialist**|Provides subject expertise under an assigned office or expedition|
|**Field Scribe / Observer**|Maintains contemporaneous notes, witness records, and chain-of-custody documentation|
|**Handler**|Supervises dangerous relics, organisms, materials, or containment equipment|
|**Licensed Delver**|Operates under recognized license and may serve as contracted specialist or legal witness|

A field title does not grant unrestricted authority outside the assignment that created it.

## XV. Joint Commands and Temporary Authorities

Complex incidents may require a **Joint Ministerial Command**.

These commands combine personnel from several offices beneath a written operational mandate.

A joint-command order must identify:

- The incident or mission
    
- The lead office
    
- Participating offices
    
- Jurisdictional basis
    
- Duration
    
- Limits of authority
    
- Evidence custody
    
- Review requirements
    
- Conditions for dissolution
    

Joint commands are especially common during:

- Major breaches
    
- Cross-border evacuations
    
- High-tier relic recovery
    
- Culturally restricted excavations
    
- Missing-expedition searches
    
- Planar incursions
    
- Public broadcast incidents
    

The lead office coordinates action.

It does not absorb the mandates of every participating office.

## XVI. Interoffice Disputes

Disagreement within the MDO is expected.

Common disputes include:

- Preservation versus containment
    
- Public access versus dangerous knowledge
    
- Regional authority versus continental safety
    
- Delver independence versus professional regulation
    
- Cultural privacy versus historical record
    
- Rapid warning versus evidentiary caution
    
- Relic ownership versus public risk
    

### Resolution

Interoffice disputes may be resolved through:

1. Direct consultation between offices
    
2. Written mandate review
    
3. Regional mediation
    
4. Charter Counsel opinion
    
5. Ethics or archive referral
    
6. Concordance coordination
    
7. Formal tribunal
    

No executive ruling may compel a researcher to falsify evidence or prevent the preservation of a lawful dissent.
