# Document Classification and Authenticity Grades

**Issuing Authority:** Ministry of Delving Operations  
**Administering Body:** Office of Ethics, Review, and Record Integrity  
**Document Classification:** Archival Standards and Public Interpretation  
**Application:** All Ministerial records, certified copies, external submissions, and Lorevault filings  
**Current Standard:** Seventh Filing Standard  
**Date of Revised Filing:** 7th Cycle, 3388 A.D.

---

## I. Purpose of This Standard

The Ministry of Delving Operations produces and preserves records of many kinds.

Some are legal instruments.

Some are maps.

Some are cultural studies.

Some are battlefield observations made beneath collapsing skies.

Some are careful reconstructions of events whose witnesses have been dead for millennia.

Some contain conclusions supported by generations of evidence.

Others preserve rumors because the rumor itself has become historically important.

These records must not be interpreted as though they possess equal purpose, certainty, provenance, or authority.

The **Document Classification and Authenticity Standard** establishes the labels through which Ministry personnel and public readers may determine:

- What kind of record they are reading
    
- Who created or authenticated it
    
- How securely its origin has been established
    
- How strongly its conclusions are supported
    
- Whether it remains current
    
- Whether access is restricted
    
- Whether the record has been challenged, corrected, superseded, or withdrawn
    

Every formal Ministerial filing should identify four separate qualities:

|Filing Element|Question Answered|
|---|---|
|**Document Class**|What kind of record is this?|
|**Authenticity Grade**|Is the document and its provenance genuine?|
|**Research Status**|How strongly are its conclusions supported?|
|**Access Designation**|Who may read or reproduce it?|

These qualities must not be collapsed into one another.

An authentic document may contain incorrect conclusions.

A highly accurate account may survive only in an uncertain copy.

A restricted record may contain ordinary information whose release would create unusual danger.

A withdrawn theory may remain historically valuable.

The Ministry seal authenticates responsibility.

It does not manufacture truth.

## II. The Four Filing Elements

### Document Class

Document Class identifies the record’s purpose and expected use.

It tells the reader whether the filing is intended as:

- Public reference
    
- Legal authority
    
- Historical reconstruction
    
- Active warning
    
- Field evidence
    
- Internal analysis
    
- Operational instruction
    
- Cultural documentation
    
- Provisional intelligence
    

A field report should not be read as a settled cultural overview.

A legal opinion should not be mistaken for historical evidence.

A public advisory may simplify technical findings for speed and safety.

### Authenticity Grade

Authenticity Grade assesses the physical, magical, documentary, or institutional provenance of the record.

It asks:

- Is this the original filing?
    
- Is it a verified copy?
    
- Has it been reconstructed?
    
- Is its author known?
    
- Has its seal been authenticated?
    
- Has the text been altered?
    
- Is the surviving version complete?
    
- Does the record genuinely originate from the source it claims?
    

Authenticity concerns origin.

It does not determine whether the author was correct.

### Research Status

Research Status measures the present strength of the conclusions contained within the record.

It asks:

- Is the claim verified?
    
- Is it strongly supported?
    
- Is it provisional?
    
- Is it disputed?
    
- Is it speculative?
    
- Has it been superseded or withdrawn?
    

Research Status may differ across sections of one document.

A cultural overview may contain verified geography, disputed history, and speculative etymology.

Where such differences are significant, they should be marked within the relevant section rather than hidden beneath one general label.

### Access Designation

Access Designation identifies who may view, copy, publish, or act upon the record.

It may reflect:

- Public accessibility
    
- Personal privacy
    
- Sacred confidentiality
    
- Operational danger
    
- Active investigation
    
- Relic risk
    
- Vulnerable locations
    
- Diplomatic sensitivity
    
- Emergency containment
    

Restriction does not necessarily imply greater importance or truth.

Some records are restricted because they contain names, routes, or procedures that would be dangerous in the wrong hands.

## III. Document Classes

The following classes are recognized across the Lorevault and Ministerial record system.

### Institutional Overview

A broad public record explaining the purpose, structure, history, jurisdiction, or public role of an organization.

Examples include:

- Ministry overviews
    
- Guild summaries
    
- Government entries
    
- Institutional histories
    
- Directorate introductions
    

Institutional Overviews summarize.

They do not replace detailed legal, operational, or administrative records.

### Administrative Registry

A formal record of offices, personnel, rank, jurisdiction, appointment, property, or institutional structure.

Examples include:

- Ministry Divisions and Offices
    
- Rank registries
    
- Regional office lists
    
- Appointment rolls
    
- Authorized seal records
    

These filings establish current administrative recognition.

They do not necessarily describe the practical influence or competence of the offices listed.

### Institutional Charter

A foundational statement of authority, obligation, principle, or procedure binding upon a Ministerial body.

Examples include:

- The Living Atlas Research Charter
    
- Regional compacts
    
- Ethics charters
    
- Delver recognition agreements
    

Charters possess formal institutional force, though their application may still be interpreted or challenged.

### Legal Opinion

An authorized interpretation of:

- Charter law
    
- Jurisdiction
    
- Treaty language
    
- Emergency standing
    
- Delver liability
    
- Relic custody
    
- Ministerial procedure
    

A Legal Opinion explains how an authorized office understands the law.

It does not automatically settle the matter where appeal or regional conflict remains possible.

### Cultural Overview

A modern reference record concerning a living people, lineage, community, identity, tradition, or cultural institution.

Cultural Overviews should identify:

- Scope
    
- Regional variation
    
- Consultation
    
- Terminology
    
- Disputed claims
    
- Known limitations
    

No Cultural Overview should imply that one description applies universally to every person within the group.

### Geographic Survey

A record of land, water, settlements, routes, borders, climate, hazards, infrastructure, or environmental conditions.

Geographic Surveys must distinguish:

- Measured geography
    
- Political claims
    
- Local names
    
- Disputed borders
    
- Seasonal variation
    
- Provisional routes
    

### Historical Reconstruction

A record that interprets the past through surviving documentary, oral, archaeological, magical, environmental, or material evidence.

Historical Reconstructions are expected to identify:

- Missing sources
    
- Competing accounts
    
- Translation limits
    
- Political distortion
    
- Degree of confidence
    

### Antiquities Record

A filing concerning:

- Artifacts
    
- Relics
    
- Ruins
    
- Inscriptions
    
- Ancient remains
    
- Provenance
    
- Restoration
    
- Custody
    

An Antiquities Record may authenticate an object without resolving its original purpose.

### Bestiary Record

A biological, ecological, magical, or threat-based filing concerning a creature, organism, entity, or recognized lifeform.

Bestiary Records may include:

- Anatomy
    
- Habitat
    
- Behavior
    
- Threat level
    
- Reproduction
    
- Magical properties
    
- Delver guidance
    
- Cultural significance
    

Threat classification must not be confused with moral judgment.

### Labyrinth Record

A filing concerning a confirmed or suspected Labyrinth, breach, chamber network, anomalous zone, or associated phenomenon.

These records may contain:

- Entry conditions
    
- Known routes
    
- Environmental behavior
    
- Threats
    
- Expedition history
    
- Cartographic uncertainty
    
- Access restrictions
    

Labyrinth Records are especially vulnerable to rapid obsolescence.

### Field Report

A contemporaneous account created during or immediately after fieldwork.

Field Reports prioritize preservation of observation over polished interpretation.

They should distinguish:

- Direct observation
    
- Witness statement
    
- Preliminary inference
    
- Rumor
    
- Emotional response
    
- Unknown conditions
    

Field Reports may later support more authoritative records.

They are not automatically authoritative themselves.

### Incident Record

A formal account of a specific event involving:

- Breach
    
- Casualty
    
- Relic failure
    
- Misconduct
    
- Missing persons
    
- Containment loss
    
- Public disturbance
    
- Operational dispute
    

Incident Records should preserve sequence, evidence, decisions, responsibility, and unresolved questions.

### Operational Advisory

A public or internal instruction intended to reduce immediate risk.

Examples include:

- Travel warnings
    
- Quarantine notices
    
- Breach alarms
    
- Relic handling advisories
    
- Evacuation procedures
    
- Delver safety guidance
    

Operational Advisories may simplify uncertainty where delay would create danger.

They must be updated or withdrawn when conditions change.

### Cartographic Field Sheet

A map or route record produced for immediate operational use.

These sheets may be:

- Incomplete
    
- Provisional
    
- Time-sensitive
    
- Hazard-marked
    
- Based on limited access
    

A Cartographic Field Sheet must never be presented as a permanent survey without further verification.

### Research Memorandum

An internal analytical record exploring evidence, theory, methods, disagreement, or possible interpretation.

Research Memoranda encourage intellectual development.

They may contain unresolved or speculative material unsuitable for formal public reference.

### Technical Record

A detailed filing concerning:

- Magical systems
    
- Relic mechanics
    
- Infrastructure
    
- Scrytech
    
- Medical procedure
    
- Engineering
    
- Containment design
    
- Experimental method
    

Technical Records should identify required expertise and known hazards.

### Public Reference Entry

A condensed record intended for broad civilian use.

Public Reference Entries prioritize clarity and practical interpretation.

They should link to deeper records where simplification might conceal material uncertainty.

### Provisional Ledger Entry

A time-sensitive record within the Monthly Ledger.

It may contain:

- Active reports
    
- Rumors
    
- Market movement
    
- Political developments
    
- Missing persons
    
- Travel changes
    
- Unconfirmed threats
    

Provisional Ledger Entries should display their verification state prominently.

### Restricted Dossier

A compiled file concerning a subject whose complete disclosure would create a significant risk.

Restriction may arise from:

- Public safety
    
- Personal privacy
    
- Sacred confidentiality
    
- Active investigation
    
- Relic danger
    
- Military or diplomatic risk
    

A dossier’s restricted status does not exempt it from review.

### Testimony Record

A preserved witness account, interview, deposition, oral history, confession, or community statement.

A Testimony Record authenticates what a person said.

It does not automatically authenticate every claim contained within that statement.

### Petition and Dissent

A formal challenge, minority opinion, correction request, community objection, or internal disagreement attached to a Ministerial filing.

Petitions and Dissents remain part of the record even when the Ministry declines the requested change.

## IV. Authenticity Grades

Authenticity Grades assess the record’s provenance and integrity.

### Grade-I — Original or Fully Verified Ministerial Record

A Grade-I record is:

- An original Ministerial filing, or
    
- A complete copy whose content, seal, authorship, and chain of custody have been fully verified
    

Grade-I confirms that the record is genuinely what it claims to be.

It does not confirm that every conclusion is true.

A Grade-I record may still be:

- Disputed
    
- Outdated
    
- Politically biased
    
- Superseded
    
- Withdrawn
    
- Based upon incomplete evidence
    

### Grade-II — Authenticated Copy or Verified External Record

A Grade-II record is not the surviving original but has been authenticated through reliable means.

This may include:

- Certified copies
    
- Independently preserved duplicates
    
- Verified foreign records
    
- Faith or guild archives
    
- Regional government filings
    
- Accurate transcriptions
    
- Confirmed scry-records
    

Some formatting, marginalia, seals, or physical characteristics may be absent.

The central content is considered authentic.

### Grade-III — Partial, Reconstructed, or Indirectly Authenticated Record

A Grade-III record possesses credible provenance but remains incomplete or dependent upon reconstruction.

Examples include:

- Damaged manuscripts
    
- Partial inscriptions
    
- Fragmented memory crystals
    
- Translated records lacking the original
    
- Reassembled field notes
    
- Records known only through quoted passages
    
- Copies with missing sections
    

Grade-III filings must identify what has been reconstructed, inferred, or supplied by later editors.

### Grade-IV — Unverified Provenance

A Grade-IV record cannot presently be authenticated with confidence but is retained because it may possess historical, cultural, investigative, or research value.

Examples include:

- Anonymous manuscripts
    
- Unverified copies
    
- Unproven relic inscriptions
    
- Untraceable witness documents
    
- Records acquired without complete custody
    
- Alleged government or cult material
    

Grade-IV material may support an investigation.

It should not independently establish a high-consequence conclusion.

### Contested Authenticity

Applied when credible evidence both supports and challenges the record’s claimed origin.

The dispute may concern:

- Authorship
    
- Date
    
- Seal
    
- Alteration
    
- Translation
    
- Copying history
    
- Ownership
    
- Magical duplication
    
- Labyrinth fabrication
    

A Contested record remains available with clear warning.

### Fraudulent

Applied when the record has been proven intentionally false, forged, altered, or misrepresented.

Fraudulent documents may remain in the archive because they reveal:

- Political manipulation
    
- Criminal conduct
    
- Historical propaganda
    
- Public panic
    
- Institutional failure
    
- The development of later myths
    

Fraudulent does not mean worthless.

It means the document cannot be accepted as what it claimed to be.

### Fabricated by Anomalous Means

Used where a record was generated, copied, altered, or planted by a Labyrinth, relic, planar entity, magical effect, or unidentified anomalous process.

Such records require special analysis.

They may contain accurate information despite possessing false provenance.

They may also imitate trusted authors, seals, handwriting, or memories.

## V. Research Status

Research Status identifies the Ministry’s present confidence in the conclusions of a record.

### Verified

Supported by multiple reliable sources, repeatable observation, direct confirmation, or equivalent evidence.

Verified claims remain subject to revision if better evidence emerges.

### Strongly Supported

Backed by substantial evidence, though meaningful gaps or objections remain.

This status is common where full verification is impossible but competing explanations are weak.

### Provisionally Accepted

The best-supported current interpretation, pending additional evidence or review.

This status should be common rather than embarrassing.

It allows the Ministry to act without pretending the matter is permanently settled.

### Disputed

A claim supported by meaningful evidence but challenged by credible alternatives.

Dispute may arise among:

- Scholars
    
- Communities
    
- Governments
    
- Faiths
    
- Delvers
    
- Witnesses
    
- Ministerial divisions
    

The existence of disagreement must be preserved.

### Unverified

A claim reported or proposed without sufficient confirmation.

Unverified claims should not be described as false unless evidence supports that judgment.

### Speculative

A possible interpretation dependent upon assumption, analogy, limited evidence, or theoretical reasoning.

Speculative material may guide future research.

It must remain visibly labeled.

### Indeterminate

Used where evidence is:

- Insufficient
    
- Contradictory
    
- Corrupted
    
- Inaccessible
    
- Temporally unstable
    
- Altered by the Labyrinth
    
- Impossible to evaluate reliably
    

Indeterminate is a valid conclusion.

### Superseded

The record has been replaced by a newer filing that better represents current understanding, law, procedure, or institutional structure.

A superseded record may remain historically valid for its own period.

### Withdrawn

The Ministry no longer supports the record’s central conclusion.

Reasons may include:

- New evidence
    
- Invalid method
    
- Misclassification
    
- Ethical breach
    
- Political interference
    
- Fabrication
    
- Harmful error
    

A withdrawn record remains traceable unless continued access would create unacceptable harm.

### Retracted for Misconduct

A stronger designation used where the record’s findings were materially corrupted by:

- Fabricated evidence
    
- Deliberate mistranslation
    
- Suppression
    
- Undisclosed conflict
    
- Coercion
    
- Fraud
    
- Manipulation of review
    

### Operationally Expired

Used for advisories, field maps, emergency orders, and warnings whose period of usefulness has ended.

Operational expiration does not imply that the original filing was incorrect.

### Pending Review

The record remains active but has entered formal reassessment.

This may occur because of:

- Petition
    
- New evidence
    
- Community objection
    
- Internal audit
    
- Conflicting expedition results
    
- Suspected misconduct

## VI. Access Designations

### Public

Available through the Lorevault, regional archives, public reading rooms, or authorized relay systems.

Public records may be copied and cited subject to archive-integrity law.

### Public with Advisory

Accessible to the general public but accompanied by warnings concerning:

- Disturbing content
    
- Obsolete guidance
    
- Disputed terminology
    
- Cultural sensitivity
    
- Dangerous imitation
    
- Unverified claims
    

### Registered Access

Available to persons whose identity and purpose are recorded.

Commonly used for:

- Detailed site maps
    
- Vulnerable locations
    
- Professional technical records
    
- Sensitive ecological data
    
- Advanced relic studies
    

### Licensed Professional Access

Limited to authorized delvers, healers, researchers, handlers, engineers, legal officers, or equivalent certified professionals.

### Community-Restricted

Access governed partly or entirely through agreement with the living community whose sacred, private, funerary, or protective knowledge is preserved.

The Ministry may hold such records without possessing unrestricted publication rights.

### Operationally Restricted

Limited during an active incident, investigation, expedition, quarantine, negotiation, or emergency.

The restriction should be reviewed when the event concludes.

### Sealed

Access limited to specifically authorized offices or individuals.

A sealed record must identify:

- Sealing authority
    
- Legal basis
    
- Scope
    
- Review date
    
- Conditions for release
    

### Black Seal

The most restrictive ordinary Ministerial designation.

Used only where access could create severe continental, planar, relic, or mass-casualty danger.

Black Seal status requires repeated high-level review.

It may not be imposed solely to conceal embarrassment, misconduct, or political inconvenience.

### Personal Record

Restricted to the individual concerned, lawful representatives, authorized healers, investigators, or other parties with a defined legal basis.

### Redacted Public Copy

A public version from which names, routes, procedures, locations, or other dangerous information have been removed.

Redactions should be as narrow as possible.

## VII. Revision States

Every active record should carry a revision state.

### Current

The record represents active Ministerial understanding or policy.

### Current with Pending Addendum

The record remains valid but is awaiting additional material.

### Under Review

A formal challenge or reassessment is underway.

### Amended

The record has received a documented substantive change.

### Corrected

A defined error has been repaired.

### Superseded

A newer record now serves as the current reference.

### Withdrawn

The record no longer represents Ministerial understanding.

### Archived

The record is preserved primarily for historical, legal, or research purposes.

### Expired

The record’s operational period has ended.

### Partially Valid

Some sections remain current while others have been superseded, withdrawn, or rendered obsolete.

This state should identify the affected sections clearly.

## VIII. Composite Filing Examples

A record’s labels should be read together.

### Example One

> **Document Class:** Cultural Overview  
> **Authenticity Grade:** Grade-I  
> **Research Status:** Provisionally Accepted  
> **Access Designation:** Public  
> **Revision State:** Current

Meaning:

The record is an authentic Ministerial cultural overview available to the public. Its interpretation is currently accepted but remains provisional.

### Example Two

> **Document Class:** Historical Reconstruction  
> **Authenticity Grade:** Grade-III  
> **Research Status:** Disputed  
> **Access Designation:** Public with Advisory  
> **Revision State:** Under Review

Meaning:

The record has been reconstructed from incomplete authentic material. Its conclusions are disputed and presently under reassessment.

### Example Three

> **Document Class:** Operational Advisory  
> **Authenticity Grade:** Grade-I  
> **Research Status:** Verified at Time of Filing  
> **Access Designation:** Public  
> **Revision State:** Operationally Expired

Meaning:

The advisory was authentic and accurate during the relevant emergency, but it should no longer guide current action.

### Example Four

> **Document Class:** Testimony Record  
> **Authenticity Grade:** Grade-II  
> **Research Status:** Unverified  
> **Access Designation:** Registered Access  
> **Revision State:** Archived

Meaning:

The Ministry has verified that the preserved copy accurately records the testimony. It has not verified the truth of the witness’s claims.

### Example Five

> **Document Class:** Administrative Registry  
> **Authenticity Grade:** Grade-I  
> **Research Status:** Superseded  
> **Access Designation:** Public  
> **Revision State:** Archived

Meaning:

The record is genuinely Ministerial and accurately reflects a former institutional structure. It is no longer current.

## IX. The Meaning of “MDO-Verified”

The phrase **MDO-verified** is frequently misunderstood.

Depending upon context, it may mean only that:

- The Ministry authenticated the document
    
- The filing passed required review
    
- The copy matches the registered original
    
- The named office issued it
    
- The evidence meets a stated standard
    
- The record is current as of its filing date
    

It does not necessarily mean:

- Every claim is proven
    
- No dispute exists
    
- The record will never change
    
- The Ministry endorses every quoted statement
    
- The subject accepts the classification
    
- The document is safe for unrestricted use
    

Public-facing records should avoid using “MDO-verified” without specifying what was verified.

Preferred language includes:

- “Authenticated as an original Ministerial filing”
    
- “Verified against three independent surveys”
    
- “Ministerial provenance confirmed”
    
- “Current classification provisionally accepted”
    
- “Testimony preserved but not independently verified”
    

Precision prevents authority from becoming a substitute for explanation.

## X. Section-Level Labels

A single record may contain evidence of different strengths.

Where necessary, sections may carry their own status markers.

Examples include:

> **Research Status: Verified**

> **Community Dispute Recorded**

> **Translation Provisional**

> **Historical Reconstruction**

> **Operational Data Expired**

> **Speculative Interpretation**

> **Location Withheld for Protection**

These labels should appear close to the material they qualify.

A warning buried in a footer does not adequately correct certainty implied throughout the document.

---

## XI. Translation and Reconstruction Marks

Translated or reconstructed material must be marked.

### Direct Translation

The source survives and the translation has been completed from it directly.

### Comparative Translation

The translation relies upon related languages, parallel texts, or partial glossaries.

### Reconstructed Passage

Missing material has been supplied through scholarly reconstruction.

Reconstructed words or passages should be visually distinguished where practical.

### Contested Translation

Credible translators disagree materially.

### Untranslatable

A term, phrase, sign, or concept has no reliable equivalent.

Researchers should preserve the original term rather than forcing false clarity.

### Ministerial Standardization

Names or terms have been standardized for filing consistency.

Standardization must not be misrepresented as the sole correct local form.

---

## XII. Maps, Images, and Scry-Records

Visual evidence requires the same scrutiny as written evidence.

Maps, illustrations, portraits, diagrams, and scry-records should identify:

- Creator
    
- Date
    
- Location
    
- Method
    
- Alteration
    
- Reconstruction
    
- Scale
    
- Perspective
    
- Known distortion
    
- Missing context
    

### Scry-Record Authentication

A scry-record may be altered by:

- Illusion
    
- Signal interruption
    
- Perspective restriction
    
- Memory contamination
    
- Arcane interference
    
- Deliberate editing
    
- Labyrinth mimicry
    

The appearance of direct observation does not guarantee completeness.

### Illustrative Reconstruction

An image created to represent a lost site, person, creature, or event must be labeled as reconstruction.

It must not be presented as a surviving contemporary depiction.

---

## XIII. Historical Records and Heritage Seals

Older Ministerial records may contain seals belonging to deceased officers.

These **heritage seals** authenticate the record’s historical origin.

They do not indicate present endorsement.

A heritage-sealed record may be:

- Current
    
- Superseded
    
- Disputed
    
- Withdrawn
    
- Partially valid
    
- Preserved for legal history
    

Readers should distinguish between:

> “This officer genuinely certified the record.”

and:

> “The modern Ministry agrees with the officer’s conclusions.”

These are not the same claim.

---

## XIV. Fraud, Alteration, and Archive Misuse

Unauthorized alteration of a Ministerial record is a serious offense.

Prohibited acts include:

- Forging seals
    
- Removing revision notices
    
- Presenting superseded guidance as current
    
- Concealing dissent
    
- Editing testimony without disclosure
    
- Altering maps
    
- Removing uncertainty labels
    
- Publishing sealed content
    
- Falsifying authenticity grades
    
- Creating counterfeit public advisories
    

### Preservation of Fraudulent Records

Fraudulent records should not always be destroyed.

They may be preserved with restrictions because they provide evidence of:

- Criminal intent
    
- Propaganda
    
- Political interference
    
- Public manipulation
    
- Historical misunderstanding
    
- Labyrinth imitation
    

The archive must prevent reuse without erasing the evidence that deception occurred.

---

## XV. Reader Responsibilities

Ministerial records are intended to be interpreted, not merely obeyed.

Readers should ask:

1. What class of record is this?
    
2. Is its provenance authentic?
    
3. How strong are its conclusions?
    
4. Is it current?
    
5. Who may access or act upon it?
    
6. Are any sections disputed or speculative?
    
7. Has a later document superseded it?
    
8. Does the subject community contest its description?
    
9. Was the record created for public understanding, legal use, or emergency action?
    
10. What does the filing not claim?
    

A reader who ignores these distinctions may misuse an accurate record as easily as a false one.

## XVI. Standard Filing Header

Formal Living Atlas records should include, where applicable:

```text
Title:
Issuing Authority:
Filed Division:
Document Class:
Authenticity Grade:
Research Status:
Access Designation:
Revision State:
Primary Compiler:
Consulting Offices:
Community Consultation:
Date of Filing:
Date of Last Revision:
Supersedes:
Related Records:
```

Not every entry requires every field.

Fields should be omitted only when genuinely inapplicable, not because the information would be inconvenient.

---

## XVII. Standard Filing Footer

Formal records should conclude with sufficient information to identify:

- Responsible office
    
- Authenticating authority
    
- Review bodies
    
- Filing date
    
- Current status
    
- Superseded records
    
- Applicable warnings
    
- Correction or petition route
    

Decorative seals and ceremonial language may accompany these details.

They must not replace them.

---

## XVIII. Summary Standard

No official record should require a reader to infer whether it is genuine, current, disputed, restricted, or superseded.

The Ministry therefore requires every substantial filing to answer:

- **What is this record?**
    
- **Where did it come from?**
    
- **How certain is it?**
    
- **Who may use it?**
    
- **Does it still represent current understanding?**
    

Where those answers are unknown, the record should say so.

---

> [!note] Public Archive Notice  
> **Authenticity does not equal accuracy.**
> 
> An Authenticity Grade confirms the provenance and integrity of a record. Research Status describes the strength of its conclusions.
> 
> A Grade-I document may be disputed, superseded, or withdrawn.
> 
> A Grade-III fragment may preserve a correct account.
> 
> A testimony record verifies what a witness stated, not necessarily what occurred.
> 
> Readers should consult revision state, research status, and related records before relying upon a filing for legal, operational, historical, or cultural conclusions.
