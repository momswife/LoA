## **I. Overview**

Along the western slopes of Oria’s **Western Cradle** mountains sprawls a woodland older than any charted settlement.  
Known to locals as **Uriah’s Wood**, it is both sanctuary and snare — a living labyrinth of trees whose leaves glimmer like tears at dusk.

The Ministry classifies the region as a **Type-Ψ Enchanted Biosphere**, governed by persistent emotional resonance rather than ordinary magical ley.  
Travelers speak of hearing their own voices whisper forgiveness among the branches. Some return lighter, others… empty.

## **IV. The Sorcerer Uriah**

Identity indeterminate. Reports vary between an aged human, a beastfolk sage, and a disembodied voice speaking from the trees.  
Common attributes include: white garments, gold eyes, and the scent of rain on soil.

Scholarly consensus divides along three theories:

1. **Arch-Druid Hypothesis** — Uriah was once a Circle of Renewal master who bound emotion to flora.
    
2. **Penitent Deity Theory** — A minor god of healing stripped of name, tending mortal hearts as atonement.
    
3. **Collective Projection Model** — The Wood itself births Uriah from the grief of entrants, an embodied placebo.
    

> _“It doesn’t matter who he is; only that he listens.”_ — Stonevoice, Lecture on Empathic Thaumaturgy

## **VI. Cultural & Religious Context**

- **Pilgrimage of Healing:** Bereaved Oria clans journey to the Wood to bury tokens of the dead beneath silver-barked trees.
    
- **Vows of Renewal:** Couples renew bonds by sleeping one night within the Hollow Choir; voices at dawn are said to foretell longevity.
    
- **Delver Legends:** Some claim delvers cast out from guilds wander here to lose their names and be reborn as guides of the sorrowful.
    

Clerical rites forbid harvest of Wood flora without consent; transgressors report dreams of roots winding through their lungs.

## **VIII. Hazards**

|**Hazard**|**Description**|
|---|---|
|**Emotional Drain**|Extended exposure (≥48 hrs) causes apathy and loss of long-term grief memory.|
|**Echo-Loop**|Auditory phenomenon repeating personal regrets until acknowledged aloud.|
|**Over-Renewal**|Rare; subjects healed beyond equilibrium—lose fear, empathy, or moral inhibition.|
|**Soul Dissolution**|Total absorption into Wood; victims become part of the choir trees.|
Ministry recommends mental anchors and mnemonic wards before entry.

## **XI. Summary**

Uriah’s Wood remains one of Aerathon’s most benevolent yet perilous sanctuaries — a living sermon on the cost of healing.  
Within its boughs, pain does not vanish; it is replanted, growing into something quieter.  
Those who enter leave their grief behind… or are buried alongside it.
