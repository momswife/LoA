## **I. Overview**

South of **Mansque** and **Fort Kingsfang**, the **Alley Basin** splits open into a ravine where three mighty rivers converge.  
At their union, water does not roar — it **sings**.  
The resonance of their collision forms what delvers and bards alike call the **Universal Symphony**, a harmonic force so precise it shapes the air into visible ripples.

The Ministry classifies Symphony Canyon as a **Type-Θ Harmonic Confluence**, one of the few naturally occurring sites where **acoustic resonance produces stable magical fields**.  
To scholars it is an echo of creation itself; to musicians, proof that sound remembers divinity.

---

## **II. Geography & Structure**

|**Feature**|**Description**|
|---|---|
|**The Three Mouths**|Rivers of Veyra, Salend, and Dourin plunge into the canyon simultaneously; their flow ratio forms a perfect 3:2:1 cadence.|
|**The Mist Bridges**|Narrow vapor spans condensing from the falls’ resonance; bear weight while melody persists, collapse when silence falls.|
|**The Harmonic Caverns**|Network of limestone vaults vibrating at sub-audible frequency; entrance to Bardic Evermeet.|
|**Bardic Evermeet**|Cliff-side college devoted to music-magic, founded 2289 A.D.; still operational under Guild of Resonant Artisans.|
The Canyon walls are lined with crystalline sediment that acts as **natural amplifiers**, translating sound into radiant color.  
During equinox storms, the entire gorge glows as though filled with aurorae.

## **IV. Cultural & Religious Significance**

- **Faith of Milil and Corellon:** Regard the Canyon as a holy throat through which Aerathon praises its maker.
    
- **Beastfolk Traditions:** Wind-kin and songbirds perform annual duets against the canyon’s echo; missing a note is considered an omen of death.
    
- **Delver Guilds:** Revere it as training ground for “Resonant Casters” — spell-singers who harmonize magic with natural rhythm.
    

Every decade, the **Festival of the Ninth Chord** draws tens of thousands. Participants play a nine-note motif in unison; when successful, the falls briefly still, and the air sings back.
