## **I. Overview**

**Glasrún** (GLAZ-rune) is the vast, old forest that blankets the **northeastern reaches of Allemance**, stretching from the upper banks of the **Queensriver** to the shadowed western slopes of the **Mantle Mountains**.

Though formally claimed as part of Allemance, Glasrún exists **without noble governance, chartered oversight, or permanent Ministry authority**. This condition is not accidental, nor recent—it is the result of a centuries-old abdication agreement known as the **Glasrún Pact**.

To outsiders, Glasrún appears lawless.  
To its people, it is simply **unruled**.

**Associated Region:** Allemance  
**Nearest Political Borders:** Oria (across the Mantle Mountains)  
**Regional Symbol:** A split oak over running water  
**Cultural Maxim:** _“If you did not tend it, you do not command it.”_

---

## **II. Geography & Climate**

Glasrún is a **temperate old-growth forest**, dense enough in places that sunlight rarely reaches the loam.

Key geographic features include:

- **The Queensriver:** A wide, cold river marking Glasrún’s southern boundary. Despite its regal name, it is shallow, fast, and unpredictable.
    
- **The Mantle Slopes:** Rocky forested inclines leading toward Oria; riddled with narrow passes maintained by local wardens.
    
- **The Deepwood:** Central forest zones untouched by roads or logging, where trees older than written history still stand.
    
- **The Mossfall Clearings:** Naturally occurring glades used for gatherings, trade meets, and seasonal rites.
    

The climate is cool and damp, with long autumns and heavy winter snowfalls. The forest floor remains fertile year-round, fed by rot, rain, and patience.

---

## **III. History & Founding**

When the **Unknown** was first created in **367 A.D.**, Glasrún was deemed worthless.

The land north of the river was:

- Too dense to farm
    
- Too wild to tax
    
- Too politically inconvenient to abandon openly
    

**Unknown the First**, unwilling to bear the burden yet unwilling to lose face at court, executed a legal maneuver now infamous in Allemantic law. He relinquished all claim, tax right, and responsibility over the forest north of the river **to any who would settle it**, while theatrically naming the river and nearby features in honor of the crown.

This act formed the **Glasrún Pact**.

Though the **Gocaire family** continues to list Glasrún among its historical holdings in the Alley Court, the Pact has never been violated. No noble levy has crossed the river. No charter has been enforced. No lord has ruled.

---

## **IV. Peoples & Settlement**

Glasrún is sparsely populated by **small, self-reliant communities** rarely exceeding a few dozen families.

### **Settlement Patterns**

- Villages are built around clearings, never cutting more forest than necessary.
    
- Homes are timber-and-stone, designed to be dismantled rather than expanded.
    
- Roads exist, but are narrow, unmarked, and maintained collectively.
    

Most inhabitants are:

- Allemantic by culture
    
- Mixed-heritage by blood
    
- Fiercely local in identity
    

To say “I am Glasrún” is considered more meaningful than any national affiliation.

---

## **V. Society & Governance**

Glasrún has **no central government**.

Authority exists only at the **hearth level**:

- Family heads
    
- Elders
    
- Wardens chosen by reputation
    

Disputes are resolved through mediation, restitution, or exile. Written law is distrusted; remembered precedent carries greater weight.

The forest itself is treated as a political actor. Acts that harm it without communal consent are judged as civic violations.

---

## **VI. Faith & Worldview**

Religion in Glasrún is **quiet and ancestral**.

- Old Allemantic hearth-rites persist
    
- Beastfolk reverence for land and hunt is common
    
- Loamlink-adjacent beliefs are widespread, though unnamed
    

Formal clergy are rare. Rituals are seasonal, practical, and communal.

> _“We thank the forest by not asking too much of it.”_

---

## **VII. Economy & Trade**

Glasrún produces little surplus—but what it offers is valued:

**Exports**

- Timber cut only from fallen trees
    
- Medicinal mosses and fungi
    
- Furs and cured game
    
- Mountain guides and wardens
    

**Imports**

- Metal tools
    
- Salt
    
- Limited grain during harsh winters
    

Trade occurs at agreed-upon clearings and is governed by custom rather than contract.

---

## **VIII. Delving & Anomalies**

Glasrún officially has **no sanctioned labyrinth entrances**.

Unofficially:

- Old ruins exist deep within the forest
    
- Loamlink growth is unusually dense
    
- Travelers report paths that rearrange themselves
    

Delvers are tolerated if they:

- Do not excavate
    
- Do not claim land
    
- Do not linger without invitation
    

Those who ignore these rules often leave early—or not at all.

## **X. Regional Identity & Legacy**

Glasrún endures because it was **left alone**.

In an age of expansion, spectacle, and regulation, it remains one of the few places in Aerathon where governance failed to take root—and was replaced not by chaos, but by care.

The Ministry categorizes Glasrún as **low-risk, high-symbolic value**.

> _“Glasrún proves a dangerous idea,”_ one internal memo reads.  
> _“That sometimes, the best way to rule a land is not to.”_
