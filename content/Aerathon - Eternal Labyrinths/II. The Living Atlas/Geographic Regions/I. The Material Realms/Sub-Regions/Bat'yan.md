## **I. Overview**

**Bat’yan** is the vast rainforest region occupying **western Arneria**, stretching inland from the Causeway cities into dense, layered jungle where roads vanish as quickly as they are cut.

It is a land defined not by borders, but by **connectivity through growth**. Villages, paths, and clearings appear and disappear with the seasons, guided by rainfall, canopy spread, and ancestral memory rather than surveyed lines.

To outsiders, Bat’yan feels diffuse and ungoverned.  
To its inhabitants, it is **deeply ordered**, if never rigid.

**Associated Region:** Arneria  
**Nearest Political Influence:** Central Arneria (east), the Causeway (south)  
**Regional Symbol:** A braided root encircling a hearth-flame  
**Common Saying:** _“The forest decides the road.”_

---

## **II. Geography & Climate**

Bat’yan is a **humid tropical rainforest**, among the densest in Aerathon.

Key geographic features include:

- **The Upper Canopy:** Towering, interwoven treetops forming natural bridges and shade paths.
    
- **Rain Veins:** Seasonal streams and rivers that reshape travel routes annually.
    
- **Redsoil Clearings:** Fertile but fragile ground where villages are founded.
    
- **Deep Green Zones:** Old-growth regions where light barely reaches the forest floor.
    

The climate is **hot, wet, and cyclical**:

- Heavy rainfall dominates most of the year
    
- Short dry seasons allow travel and construction
    
- Heat sickness is a constant risk without acclimation
    

The forest grows faster than maps can track.

---

## **III. History & Formation**

Bat’yan was never conquered in full.

Early Arnerian records describe repeated failures to impose estates, roads, or permanent garrisons beyond the Causeway fringe. Structures rotted, paths vanished, and settlers either adapted—or left.

Over generations, Bat’yan became a place where **only flexible societies endured**.

Rather than resisting the forest, its inhabitants learned to **move with it**, allowing settlements to form where the land permitted and dissolve when it no longer did.

---

## **IV. Peoples & Settlement Patterns**

Bat’yan’s population is widely dispersed into **barangays**—small villages rarely numbering more than a few hundred.

### **Settlement Characteristics**

- Built on stilts or raised stone to avoid flood rot
    
- Designed for repair, not permanence
    
- Connected by narrow forest roads maintained communally
    

The population is predominantly beastfolk, though humans and other ancestries are not uncommon near trade routes.

Identity is:

- Village-first
    
- Family-centered
    
- Deeply local
    

Leaving Bat’yan does not sever belonging; return is always assumed.

---

## **V. Society & Daily Rhythm**

Life in Bat’yan follows the **cycle of heat and rain**.

- Mornings are communal and outward-facing
    
- Midday is reserved for rest and shelter
    
- Evenings return families and villages together
    

Work, art, and social life are inseparable. The pace is unhurried, but never idle.

> _“The forest does not rush. Neither should we.”_

---

## **VI. Faith & Cultural Character**

Faith in Bat’yan is **woven into daily practice** rather than centered on temples.

- Ancestral reverence is widespread
    
- Nature-spirits and old gods are honored situationally
    
- Formal clergy are rare outside major Causeway towns
    

Rituals often take the form of:

- Shared meals
    
- Seasonal songs
    
- Communal labor
    

Art and music are not professions first—they are **communal obligations**.

---

## **VII. Economy & Resources**

Bat’yan is largely **self-sustaining**, producing what it needs before what it can sell.

**Primary Resources**

- Fruits and root crops
    
- Medicinal plants
    
- Hardwood and resin
    
- Animal products (leather, horn, bone)
    

**Exports**

- Preserved foods
    
- Medicines and tonics
    
- Artisan crafts
    

**Imports**

- Metal tools
    
- Salt
    
- Select luxury goods from the Causeway
    

Trade is personal and reputation-based; contracts are remembered, not written.

---

## **VIII. Delving & Anomalies**

Bat’yan contains **numerous unstable and overgrown sites** believed to predate modern delving classification.

- Ruins reclaimed by jungle growth
    
- Labyrinth entrances that flood or seal seasonally
    
- Areas where sound behaves unpredictably
    

Delvers are viewed cautiously. Success without respect for the land often leads to rapid failure.

## **X. Regional Identity & Legacy**

Bat’yan is not unified by banners or walls.  
It is unified by **adaptation**.

The region endures because it never insists on permanence where the land will not allow it. Bat’yan does not resist change—it **absorbs it**, reshapes it, and continues.

The Ministry classifies Bat’yan as **ecologically dominant, culturally resilient**, and resistant to centralized control.

> _“Bat’yan will outlast the roads built through it.”_
