# **The Shattersea**

### _The First Realm — “Where Creation Broke Its Own Reflection.”_

**Filed Division:** Ministry of Delving Operations — Arcane Breach Division (Planar Inquiry & Origin Studies)**  
**Primary Compiler:** Lt. Salvia Korrin  
**Advisory Editors:** Professor Arav Dhal-Senn (Antiquities Restoration), Inspector Jao “Greyglass” Vell (Thaumaturgy)  
**Date of Filing:** 3224 A.D. (Age of Spectacle)**  
**Document Class:** _Planar Record / Grade-I Authenticity_

---

## **I. Overview**

The **Shattersea** is believed to be the **origin and grave of creation**, a vast, otherworldly ocean composed of liquid memory and fractured light.  
It exists beneath no horizon and above no heaven — a liminal expanse of glassy waves that reflect what _was_ rather than what _is._

The Ministry classifies the Shattersea as **a Class-VI Reality Hazard**, accessible only through relic resonance or deep dream-state traversal.

Sailors and delvers alike whisper that every drop of its water remembers something — and that those who drown in it do not die, but _replay_.

**Planar Designation:** Realm-01 — “The Shattersea”  
**Symbol:** A spiral of mirrored waves circling a single black void  
**Associated Element:** Memory  
**Primary Alignment Influence:** Lawful Neutral → Fragmented Neutral

---

## **II. Theological and Historical Context**

Arcanists of the early Ministry believed the Shattersea formed when the first gods **spoke creation into being and then shattered their own words.**  
The fragments — language turned to matter — became the foundation of all planes.

Over millennia, these broken syllables congealed into the sea: a vast echo-field where divine intent and mortal thought fuse.  
The result is a realm that behaves like both archive and mirror, storing the reflections of every world it has ever touched.

Scholars call this phenomenon the **Resonant Confluence**, the source of relic “memory bleed” and prophetic recursion.

---

## **III. Observed Properties**

### **Physical Traits**

- Water density shifts with observer memory; can harden into glass or mist upon recollection.
    
- Sound travels backward — conversations are heard before they are spoken.
    
- Objects and people cast multiple reflections, each depicting alternate choices.
    

### **Temporal Behavior**

Time moves unevenly; Ministry probes show paradoxical aging, with organic matter “reversing” to pre-embryonic states before dissolving.  
Lt. Korrin’s report:

> _“The longer you observe it, the more it begins observing you.”_

---

## **IV. Known Hazards**

- **Memory Displacement:** Visitors often awaken with rewritten histories.
    
- **Cognitive Fracture:** Minds exposed too long to the reflective medium begin speaking in recursive loops.
    
- **Echo Entities:** Sentient “Reflections” manifest — autonomous shadows composed of diverged choices.
    

The Ministry keeps all expeditions under **Echo-Silence Protocol** — no recording, no naming, no songs. Memory amplification triggers resonance cascades.

---

## **V. Inhabitants & Entities**

There are no confirmed living natives, yet field agents have documented recurring phenomena:

- **The Mirror Choir:** Repeating voices that mimic the listener’s hopes in unison.
    
- **The Saltborn:** Shell-armored revenants that drift between reflection and flesh, drawn to guilt.
    
- **The Architect Below:** A hypothetical entity — possibly remnant of pre-Collapse divine consciousness — credited with shaping labyrinth recursion patterns.
    

Worship of the Architect is forbidden under **Charter §9-I (Cognitive Hazard Clause).**

---

## **VI. Influence on the Material Plane**

The Shattersea’s influence manifests wherever **mirrors, glass, or memory-binding relics** are present.  
It is said every labyrinth contains a droplet of it in its heart — the core that makes every delve both physical and spiritual.

Humans are particularly attuned to its residue; scholars believe this explains their **Relic Affinity** and adaptability.  
Those “touched” by the Shattersea often display:

- Eidetic recall of events they never lived.
    
- Emotional resonance with ancient relics.
    
- Sensitivity to Echo-Law or temporal anomalies.
    

These individuals are known informally as **Glassborne**.

---

## **VII. Ministry Operations & Research**

Active exploration is suspended following **Incident 41-C: The Glasswake Breach**, wherein a research vessel returned crewless but broadcasting forty-two simultaneous copies of its log.

The Arcane Breach Division maintains observation posts at:

- **The Reflectory Gate (Louvain Subsector-3)**
    
- **Silt-9 Array (Arneria Border Faultline)**
    

All interaction with mirror-based relics now requires secondary containment.  
Lt. Korrin personally oversees dream traversal permissions under **Echo Code 7/12**.

---

## **VIII. Cultural & Religious Significance**

Among the faithful of Arneria, the Shattersea is revered as the place where gods “drowned themselves to become prayers.”  
In Al’ar, poets claim every artist sails its surface in dreams.  
The Beastfolk of Oria call it **“the First Forge”**, believing thunder is the sound of the world cooling from that event.

Despite the Ministry’s caution, countless delvers yearn for it — the ocean that remembers everything.

---

## **IX. Theoretical Impact on Delving**

Exposure to relics with **Shattersea resonance** grants both insight and instability.  
Effects include:

- Partial memory overlap between delvers.
    
- Hallucinations of past delves manifesting as physical afterimages.
    
- Enhanced perception of Echo-Law inscriptions.
    

Veteran delvers refer to this phenomenon as “sea-mind” — a euphoric clarity where every failure feels like a second chance.

---

## **X. Cultural Identity & Legacy**

The Shattersea remains the **archetypal myth** of Aerathon: proof that creation itself can fracture and still function.  
It is the oldest metaphor and the newest obsession — the mirror every civilization eventually mistakes for salvation.

The Ministry’s standing order endures:

> _“Observe. Do not touch. If it speaks your name, run.”_

---

### **Planar Feature: Echo-Touched Memory**

_Characters or relics influenced by the Shattersea gain the following trait:_

> **Echo-Touched Memory:** Once per long rest, when you fail an Intelligence, Wisdom, or Charisma saving throw, you may instead recall a “version” of yourself that succeeded. You pass the save but suffer minor psychic damage (1d6 per proficiency bonus) as your mind fractures between timelines.
