# **The Wanehollow**

### _The Third Realm — “Where the Light Forgets It Was Ever Warm.”_

**Filed Division:** Ministry of Delving Operations — Arcane Breach Division (Hazard Containment & Cognitive Silence Bureau)**  
**Primary Compiler:** Inspector Jao “Greyglass” Vell  
**Advisory Editors:** Lt. Salvia Korrin (Arc. Scriv.), Priest-Analyst Thera Daan (Faithfield Oversight)  
**Date of Filing:** 3224 A.D. (Age of Spectacle)**  
**Document Class:** _Planar Record / Grade-I Authenticity_

---

## **I. Overview**

The **Wanehollow** is the **Null Realm** — a metaphysical absence that exists wherever meaning erodes.  
It has no geography, no light, and no native sound. The Ministry defines it not as a place, but as **a condition of reality in retreat**.

Where the Shattersea preserves and the Dreaming creates, the Wanehollow **unwrites.**  
To encounter it is to be erased in increments — first your shadow, then your voice, then the idea that you ever cast either.

**Planar Designation:** Realm-03 — “The Wanehollow”  
**Symbol:** A hollow circle bisected by a single black thread  
**Associated Element:** Absence  
**Primary Alignment Influence:** Neutral Evil → True Void

---

## **II. Discovery & Early Accounts**

The first official reference to the Wanehollow appears in a recovered **Tharos Ring transcript** dated prior to the First Collapse. The report describes a “pocket of soundless inversion” encountered beneath a labyrinth’s lower floor, where light fell upward and all speech turned to breath.

Subsequent MDO explorers confirmed that localized **waning zones** form wherever divine or arcane memory decays—particularly in dead temples or relic vaults stripped of faith.

Its full presence, however, is accessible only through ritual nullification or death during relic resonance — both strictly forbidden.

---

## **III. Observed Phenomena**

- **Acoustic Erasure:** Sound decays exponentially; the deeper the intrusion, the less meaning any word holds.
    
- **Cognitive Drain:** Thoughts lose nouns first, then verbs, then self-reference.
    
- **Light Inversion:** Illumination curves inward, producing negative shadows that pulse like breathing.
    
- **Temporal Dissolution:** Clocks and hearts stop simultaneously at thresholds of 0.4 Null Saturation.
    

Ministry hazard tiers classify it as **Class-VII Cognitive Breach Environment.**

---

## **IV. Theoretical Function**

Arcane theologians posit the Wanehollow as **the grave of unmade ideas** — the entropy necessary to balance creation’s surplus.  
It is, in essence, **the recoil of existence**: what happens when the Dreaming imagines too much and the Shattersea remembers too long.

Faith scholars compare it to divine atrophy — where gods not worshipped simply dissolve into it, leaving behind _hollows of presence._

> _“The Wanehollow is mercy for memory; cruelty for those who still remember.”_  
> — Thera Daan

## **VI. Religious and Cultural Interpretations**

- **Arneria:** Described as _The Thirteenth Flame’s Ash_, the ultimate fate of unworthy prayers.
    
- **Al’ar:** Portrayed in theatre as a jealous god who steals applause.
    
- **Oria:** Feared as “the Empty Forge,” invoked to cleanse dishonor.
    
- **Vinyot:** Treated pragmatically — contracts end with “May the Wanehollow take all unpaid debt.”
    

Among delvers, it is simply called **“The Final Floor.”**

---

## **VII. Known Entrances & Breaches**

- **Site V-11:** Collapsed chapel beneath the Arnerian Drowned Wastes — residual silence measurable for ten leagues.
    
- **Site O-2:** Lightning-fused ravine in Oria producing negative echoes.
    
- **Site L-1 (“The Mute Gate”):** Contained beneath Louvain; sealed after the Echo Division reported language desynchronization in staff.
    

Entry to all three requires psychic dampeners and signed absolution.

---

## **VIII. Psychological Impact**

Prolonged exposure induces the **White Sleep**, a state where subjects appear awake but forget breathing, hunger, or self-preservation.  
Recovered survivors frequently repeat a single sentence, varying by individual but always ending in:

> _“…and then there was no more I.”_

No subject has retained memory beyond thirty days post-recovery.

---

## **IX. Ministry Response & Containment**

Following the **Null Breach of Cycle 1395**, all research teams are limited to seven minutes of exposure, monitored via mnemonic tethers.  
The Arcane Breach Division enforces **Protocol Stillglass**:

- No recording devices permitted.
    
- No personal names spoken within containment radius.
    
- Termination by silence incantation if Null Spread exceeds 0.6 saturation.
    

The Wanehollow remains officially **quarantined**, its study relegated to simulation models under Lt. Korrin’s supervision.

---

## **X. Symbolic & Philosophical Legacy**

The Wanehollow is the fear at the center of every faith — that nothing, once forgotten, remains.  
Where the Dreaming offers infinite possibility, the Wanehollow is **finality without consequence.**

And yet, paradoxically, civilization relies on its existence.  
For without forgetting, there would be no room to create anew.  
The Ministry calls it _“the price of progress.”_

> _“We build, we burn, we vanish.  
> The Hollow only keeps the quiet.”_  
> — Greyglass

---

### **Planar Feature: Hollow Resilience**

_Any being or relic influenced by the Wanehollow gains the following trait:_

> **Hollow Resilience:** Once per long rest, when reduced to 0 hit points, you may instead vanish into a brief state of null presence, becoming untargetable until the start of your next turn. You reappear with 1 hit point but cannot speak or cast spells for one round.
