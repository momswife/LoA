# **The Feywilds**

### _The Fourth Realm — “Where Memory Blossoms Faster Than It Can Die.”_

**Filed Division:** Ministry of Delving Operations — Arcane Breach Division (Ecological Anomalies & Natural Law Bureau)**  
**Primary Compiler:** Keeper Aeska Mourn-Wind  
**Advisory Editors:** Archivist Yelna Stonevoice (Echo-Law), Curator Rook-of-Rings (Relic Flora Registry)  
**Date of Filing:** 3224 A.D. (Age of Spectacle)**  
**Document Class:** _Planar Record / Grade-I Authenticity_

---

## **I. Overview**

The **Feywilds** are the **echo of nature uncontained** — a parallel bloom of the Material Plane where emotion grows roots, and thought becomes flora.  
It mirrors Aerathon imperfectly: every forest too green, every emotion too sharp, every sound too alive.

It is not a separate world, but the _fever-dream of the natural one_.  
Each tree, stream, and star here is aware — not sentient in the human sense, but remembering.

**Planar Designation:** Realm-04 — “The Feywilds”  
**Symbol:** A vine spiraling around a crescent moon  
**Associated Element:** Growth  
**Primary Alignment Influence:** Chaotic Good → Unstable Neutral

---

## **II. Origins & Nature**

Ministry scholars theorize the Feywilds were **born from the Dreaming’s overflow**, a “second harvest” of imagination that found physical form.  
Where the Dreaming thrives on concept, the Feywilds thrive on _continuity_ — nothing ever truly dies here.  
A fallen leaf will take root midair; emotions shed from mortal hearts become new creatures overnight.

The first delvers to enter described it as _“beauty with teeth.”_

---

## **III. Geography & Phenomena**

- **The Canopy of Echoes:** Forests whose leaves repeat conversations whispered beneath them for centuries.
    
- **The Moonroot Lakes:** Water that glows under emotional proximity — turning scarlet for anger, silver for love, green for curiosity.
    
- **The Spindle Paths:** Vines that grow into stairways leading to nowhere, yet often deliver travelers precisely where their hearts wish to go.
    

Time here is circular. Walk long enough, and you’ll meet yourself — older, wiser, or weeping.

---

## **IV. Ecology & Entities**

Life thrives here without restraint.  
Flora consume thoughts; fauna dream themselves into new species daily.

|**Entity / Species**|**Description**|
|---|---|
|**Thorn Shepherds**|Walking trees that protect ancient memories. Their sap induces reverie.|
|**The Lilt**|A phenomenon rather than a creature — music carried on pollen that alters emotions.|
|**The Court of Seasons**|Four shifting sovereigns — Spring (Curiosity), Summer (Arrogance), Autumn (Sorrow), Winter (Reprieve). They never rule simultaneously.|
|**Feyborn**|Mortals touched by this realm; their eyes mirror the color of the moon above their birthplace. They can hear plants _whisper their judgments._|
___
## **V. Emotional Resonance**

The Feywilds respond to mood with frightening precision.  
Fear causes thorns to emerge from soil; kindness makes water sweeter.  
Sorrow plants flowers that grow from tears.  
Every traveler leaves a garden of their state of mind — a trail visible for years.

Because of this, long expeditions are forbidden under **Charter §9-I (Emotive Hazard Clause)**.

---

## **VI. Cultural Interpretations**

- **In Allemance:** Believed to be the resting place of stories that became too happy to end.
    
- **In Arneria:** Viewed with suspicion — untempered creation without purpose.
    
- **Among Beastfolk:** Revered as ancestral paradise; many tribes consider themselves “Fey descendants.”
    
- **In Oria:** Studied for its indestructible plant species, though contact often drives miners to “green madness.”
    

---

## **VII. Ministry Research & Containment**

The Ministry maintains three sanctioned study sites:

- **The Verdant Aperture (Vinyot Borderline):** Observes trade contamination via pollen transfer.
    
- **Station Lilt-3 (Al’ar Outpost):** Monitors emotional flora interacting with musical resonance.
    
- **The Rootglass Vault (Louvain Subterrane):** Houses preserved specimens of _living vines_ under cryostatic stasis.
    

All researchers are required to wear emotional dampeners. Despite precautions, incidents of **“overgrowth hysteria”** remain common — defined as the compulsive need to nurture at personal expense.

---

## **VIII. Interaction with the Material Plane**

The Feywilds bleed through most clearly in **spring solstices, labyrinth oases,** and **Beastfolk settlements.**  
Beastfolk born during solstice or full bloom are said to carry a **Verdant Lineage**, marked by heightened empathy and instinctive magic.  
Clerics and druids draw their power from this resonance, unknowingly invoking fragments of its energy through divine focus.

---

## **IX. Delving Implications**

Labyrinths touched by the Feywilds — called **Verdant Convergences** — feature organic growths that react to emotional input, puzzle flora that demand kindness to open, or predators that mimic loved ones.  
Relics from these sites pulse faintly like heartbeats and sometimes sprout roots.

Feywild-touched delvers are nicknamed **Bloomwalkers**.  
They are valued for adaptability and feared for unpredictability.

---

## **X. Cultural & Philosophical Legacy**

If the Wanehollow erases, the Feywilds **remember too much.**  
Every kindness multiplies, every cruelty seeds vengeance.  
Civilizations crumble for lack of balance; the Feywilds stand eternal precisely because they lack moderation.

The Ministry regards them as a living contradiction: paradise, plague, and prophecy all blooming from the same root.

> _“The Feywilds remind us that mercy, left unchecked, devours as surely as malice.”_ — Aeska Mourn-Wind

---

### **Planar Feature: Verdant Echo**

_Any being or relic influenced by the Feywilds gains the following trait:_

> **Verdant Echo:** When you restore hit points or inspire an ally, faint green light spreads in a 10-foot radius. Once per long rest, when a creature within that light regains HP, it may also remove one minor condition (frightened, charmed, poisoned). However, each use attracts fey attention — roll a d20; on a 1, the user hears the Feywild whisper their name for one night.
