## **I. Racial Overview**

Kobolds were never meant to exist in the Beast World as people—not in the way other beastfolk do. Born originally as mindless thralls under the dominion of dragons in the Ancestral Homeland, the vast majority of kobolds remain barely sapient. They skitter, hoard, chatter, and swarm through forgotten tunnels and labyrinth margins with instinct alone. They do not question. They do not choose.

And then, rarely, something changes.

From within the mass of clawed feet and glittering eyes, a kobold awakens. It names itself. It recognizes itself. It becomes willful.

These rare individuals—known to the Ministry as **named kobolds**—are not merely more intelligent. They are _aware_. Their consciousness rises like a single flame above a sea of reflex and mimicry. In the Beast World, where such individuals number fewer than a thousand, each named kobold is an anomaly, a marvel, and an administrative complication.

Named kobolds live with a profound duality: surrounded by kin who cannot truly think, yet burdened with a mind that can. Some cherish their unthinking cousins and act as protectors or interpreters. Others flee their warrens entirely, desperate to prove that their thoughts are not borrowed echoes of draconic design.

Most people in Aerathon are unsure how to treat a talking kobold. Pet. Prisoner. Curiosity. Citizen.

But when one begins discussing philosophy, the Ministry recommends securing all loose valuables.

## **II. Core Kobold Traits**

**Creature Type.** You are a Humanoid.

**Ability Score Increase.** Your Dexterity score increases by 2, and one other ability score of your choice (excluding Intelligence) increases by 1.

**Size.** Small. Most kobolds stand between 3’2” and 3’10”.

**Speed.** Your walking speed is 30 feet.

---

### **Escaper’s Keepers**

You are proficient in the Sleight of Hand skill.

When a creature makes an opportunity attack against you, you can use your reaction to make a Dexterity (Sleight of Hand) check contested by the attacker’s Wisdom (Perception). On a success, the attack misses. You may also steal one worn or carried item (not held, no more than 5 lbs) without the creature noticing.

A creature that witnesses this feature becomes immune to it for 24 hours.

---

### **Clumsy Competence**

You gain a +1 bonus to any ability check that does not include a bonus from proficiency, class features, or magic—only your raw ability score.

If your Intelligence score is 9 or lower, this bonus instead becomes +2.

---

### **Stackable Intellect**

When other kobolds are nearby, your thoughts sharpen through shared instinct and emergent cognition. You gain a bonus to ability checks based on the number of kobolds within 60 feet of you:

|Nearby Kobolds|Ability Check Bonus|
|---|---|
|2|+1|
|4|+2|
|8|+3|
|16|+4|
|32+|+5|

This bonus represents emergent problem-solving rather than communication; the kobolds need not be conscious collaborators.

---

## **III. Kobold Expression**

Kobolds do not possess subraces in the conventional sense. Instead, variation manifests through **emergence**—the circumstances under which a kobold becomes named.

Some awaken through prolonged exposure to magic. Others through trauma, isolation, or accidental arcane resonance. A small number display signs of inherited cognition, suggesting that the phenomenon may be slowly propagating.

The Ministry has not yet reached consensus on whether this represents evolution, corruption, or escape.

---

## **IV. Environmental Adaptation (Homeland Traits)**

Kobolds do not hail from traditional homelands. Most dwell in labyrinth margins, abandoned tunnels, sewer networks, or improvised warrens beneath established cities. Named kobolds, however, often adopt regions—sometimes by invitation, sometimes by accident.

---

### **Hoarder’s Luck**

You have advantage on checks made to locate small, hidden, or valuable objects (such as gems, keys, trap triggers, or concealed mechanisms).

When identifying magical objects or deciphering runes, you may use Sleight of Hand in place of Arcana or Investigation, provided you have physically touched the item.

---

### **Squatters of the Forgotten**

If you spend at least 8 hours within a ruin, sewer, labyrinth margin, or abandoned structure, you gain the following benefits:

- You learn all natural paths of entry and exit
    
- You detect the nearest active source of magic within 30 feet (as if by _detect magic_)
    
- You learn whether the structure was once sacred, arcane, or mundane in origin
    

---

### **Adopted Origin**

A named kobold may select **one regional trait from any other Beastfolk species**, representing where they emerged, were raised, or were claimed. This choice reflects social adoption rather than ancestry and is subject to Ministry verification.

---

## **V. Ministry Assessment**

Named kobolds represent one of the clearest cases of **emergent sapience** currently documented in the Beast World. Whether they are escaping an imposed design or developing a new one remains unresolved.

For now, the Ministry recognizes named kobolds as people—conditionally.
