## **I. Racial Overview**

Bats are anomalies in the Ministry’s taxonomies—neither beastfolk nor planar outsiders, but something in between. They are the only known self-aware species to have evolved entirely within the **Astral Sea**, a boundless, mutable expanse once believed to be empty space between worlds. Instead, it is their homeland.

Astralborn bats construct floating sanctuaries and shifting towns through **astralcraft**: the manipulation of raw _astra_ via will, imagination, and communal intent. They eat it, mold it, and live within it. Concepts such as gravity, permanence, and scarcity are optional in their native environment, and this deeply shapes their psychology.

Culturally, bats value **creation over continuity**. Improvisation is preferred to tradition; novelty to legacy. History is often treated as trivia rather than inheritance, and grudges are viewed as inefficient uses of thought. Gods are rarely worshipped, though some bats resonate faintly with divine concepts tied to illusion, knowledge, or liminality.

Since the emergence of the Labyrinths and the thinning of planar boundaries, bats have begun appearing in Aerathon—some through sanctioned Astral Gates, others through accident or curiosity. Among delving parties, they are prized as scouts, dream-crafters, and planar sensitives, capable of perceiving disturbances others cannot.

## **II. Core Bat Traits**

**Creature Type.** You are a Humanoid.

**Ability Score Increase.** Your Charisma score increases by 2, and your Dexterity score increases by 1.

**Size.** Most bats stand between 4’7” and 5’7”. Your size is Medium.

**Speed.** Your walking speed is 30 feet.

**Cheiropocket.**  
You maintain a tethered pocket of the Astral Sea linked to your wing membrane. As a bonus action, you can open or close this cheiropocket, allowing you to stow or retrieve an item as an action. It can hold up to 12 × your Intelligence modifier (minimum 12) cubic feet of nonliving material. Living creatures or oversized items pass through harmlessly. The pocket collapses and spills its contents if you drop to 0 hit points or enter the Astral Sea.

**Wings.**  
While not flight-capable outside the Astral Sea, your wings allow you to glide. While unencumbered and not wearing heavy armor, you can fall 30 feet per round without taking damage and move 1 foot horizontally for every foot descended.  
While in the Astral Sea, you gain a flying speed of 40 feet and can hover.

**Astral Echoes.**  
Your voice resonates across planar veils. As a bonus action, you can emit an astral tone silent in this world but thunderous in the Astral. Any creature or object currently phasing into the Astral Plane within 120 feet becomes visible to you until the end of your next turn.  
While in the Astral Sea, this feature instead grants blindsight out to 120 feet.  
You can use this trait a number of times equal to your proficiency bonus per long rest.

## **III. Regional Variance (Homeland Traits)**

Bats do not possess regional homelands in the traditional sense. Instead, their cultural variance is tied to **planar proximity and astral exposure**.

---

### **Astral Sea**

Bat civilization exists primarily within the Astral Sea, where settlements are collaborative works of art rather than fixed cities. With no true weather, gravity, or external threats, bat society developed without war, famine, or territorial conflict. This absence shaped a people largely unfamiliar with fear-based hierarchy or inherited authority.

When bats enter Aerathon, many retain a metaphysical tether to their home plane, carrying trace astra within personal folds of space and memory.

**Practiced Astralcrafter.**  
You add your proficiency bonus to Intelligence checks made to manipulate astra or perform astralcrafting, especially when within the Astral Sea or interacting with astral-infused materials in Aerathon.
