# Corvid

**Ministry of Delving Operations — The Living Atlas**  
**Folk Classification:** [[Aerathon - Eternal Labyrinths/II. The Living Atlas/Races of Aerathon/Avianfolk/Avianfolk Overview|Avianfolk]]  
**Lineage Classification:** Corvid  
**Registry Scope:** Sapient Lineage and Homeland Variance  
**Recognition Status:** Full Sapient Recognition  
**Delver Eligibility:** Unrestricted  
**Common Names:** Corvids, Blackwings, Quillfolk  
**Date of Filing:** 4th Cycle, 3386 A.D.

---

## I. Lineage Overview

**Corvids** are an Avianfolk lineage distinguished by dexterous hands, expressive wings, sharp pattern recognition, strong social memory, and an uncommon ability to identify opportunity within confusion.

Their recognized sublineages include ravens, crows, rooks, jackdaws, magpies, jays, nutcrackers, choughs, and related feathered peoples.

Corvid cultures are frequently associated with secrets, cleverness, names, and dark humor. These descriptions capture only their most visible qualities.

At the center of many Corvid traditions lies a deeper principle:

**Nothing exists alone.**

A word gains meaning from the words surrounding it. An object reveals its purpose through wear. A person becomes known through the promises they keep, the stories told about them, and the company that answers when their name is called.

Corvids are therefore often attentive to connections other people overlook.

They notice who stops speaking when a door opens. They remember which tool was moved from its proper place. They recognize when three unrelated rumors share the same missing detail.

This does not make every Corvid a schemer, investigator, thief, or scholar.

It means many Corvid communities teach their children to look beyond the obvious shape of a thing and ask what else it touches.

## II. Origins and Collective Memory

Corvids possess no single accepted origin.

Their oldest communities appear in mountain forests, temperate plains, dense cities, coastal cliffs, agricultural settlements, and nearly every region where discarded objects, spoken news, and overlapping populations create complicated social landscapes.

Some Corvid traditions claim that their ancestors learned language by listening to the first people name the world.

Others tell that the earliest Corvid stole a word from death and carried it back to the living. Because of this theft, the dead could be remembered even after their voices faded.

Scholars have found no evidence that either account is literally true.

Corvid historians generally consider this beside the point.

### Living Memory

Corvid communities rarely separate history from the people who carry it.

Songs, jokes, tools, names, debts, scars, recipes, and minor household objects may all preserve pieces of the past. A chipped cup can mark the end of a feud. A bent key may be passed down because an ancestor once used it to open a prison gate. A rude children’s rhyme might contain the only surviving account of a disgraced magistrate.

This approach has created vast bodies of cultural memory that are difficult for outsiders to interpret.

A formal archive may contain only the official account of an event, while the fuller Corvid record survives through:

- Family stories
    
- Nicknames
    
- Memorial objects
    
- Repeated jokes
    
- Street songs
    
- Annotated maps
    
- Altered tools
    
- Witness chains
    
- Collections of seemingly ordinary possessions
    

Corvids do not necessarily believe every story they preserve.

Remembering a claim is not the same as trusting it.

## III. Body and Aerial Form

Corvids are generally light-framed and agile, with highly expressive wings, hands, neck feathers, and tails.

Most stand between **5 feet 3 inches and 6 feet 3 inches**. Their wings are usually broad enough for sustained flight while remaining compact enough to maneuver through forests, ruins, and crowded urban spaces.

Their beaks vary from slender and precise to heavy and powerful. Corvid hands commonly possess excellent fine-motor control, making them well suited to intricate tools, locks, writing implements, instruments, and mechanical components.

### Plumage

Common plumage includes:

- Glossy black
    
- Charcoal
    
- Deep brown
    
- White and black
    
- Blue-black or violet iridescence
    
- Grey with dark wings
    
- Brilliant blue, green, or barred markings among jay lineages
    
- White patches along the wings, throat, or shoulders
    

Iridescent plumage may appear plain under weak light and suddenly reveal intense color when viewed from another angle.

This has inspired the Corvid saying:

> A thing may be dark without being colorless.

Many Corvids decorate themselves with small objects woven into cords, garments, jewelry, or feather ornaments. Outsiders often assume these items were chosen because they glitter.

In reality, their value may be historical, sentimental, practical, humorous, or entirely private.

### Voice and Imitation

Many Corvids possess flexible voices capable of reproducing unfamiliar tones, environmental sounds, and the speech patterns of others.

This talent varies by individual and sublineage.

Imitating someone without permission may be playful among friends, disrespectful in formal settings, or legally serious when used to impersonate an official. Corvid communities frequently distinguish between imitation used as performance and imitation intended to deceive.

## IV. Society and Kinship

Corvid communities often organize themselves through overlapping networks rather than a single rigid structure.

A Corvid may belong simultaneously to a household, neighborhood watch, trade circle, witness chain, performance group, archive society, delving guild, and extended family.

These affiliations need not possess equal importance.

The person trusted to preserve one’s will may not be the person trusted with one’s fears. The relative who shares one’s blood may not be the person expected to answer during a crisis.

### Witness Chains

A **witness chain** is a relationship in which people agree to remember important events, promises, names, or possessions for one another.

The practice began in communities where records were vulnerable to fire, war, theft, or political revision.

A formal witness chain may involve several individuals, each preserving part of a statement. No single witness necessarily possesses the entire account. This prevents one death, bribe, or disappearance from destroying the record.

Modern witness chains may preserve:

- Marriage promises
    
- Business agreements
    
- Final words
    
- Birth names
    
- Confessions
    
- Ownership claims
    
- The location of hidden possessions
    
- Instructions to be followed after death
    

Serving as a witness is considered an honor and a burden.

Forgetting through carelessness can damage a Corvid’s reputation for years. Deliberately changing a witnessed account is regarded as a profound betrayal.

### Found Objects

Corvids are widely known for collecting objects.

The stereotype is true only in the broadest sense.

Many Corvid traditions teach that an abandoned object should be examined before being discarded. It may still have a use. It may reveal what happened to its owner. It may possess beauty unnoticed by those who valued only its original purpose.

Some Corvids become enthusiastic collectors. Others despise clutter and preserve only a few carefully chosen items.

The underlying value is not accumulation.

It is the refusal to assume that something has become meaningless merely because another person left it behind.

## V. Inner Life, Virtue, and Shadow

Corvid communities commonly admire:

- Curiosity guided by judgment
    
- Adaptability
    
- Social awareness
    
- Accurate memory
    
- Humor during hardship
    
- The preservation of overlooked people and events
    
- The ability to recognize opportunity without creating unnecessary harm
    

These values can produce insightful scholars, loyal friends, careful witnesses, and people capable of finding solutions in disorder.

They can also become invasive, manipulative, or exhausting.

|Admired Quality|Constructive Expression|Potential Shadow|
|---|---|---|
|Curiosity|Discovering what others missed|Violating privacy|
|Adaptability|Finding another path|Refusing lasting commitments|
|Social awareness|Understanding unspoken needs|Manipulating relationships|
|Memory|Preserving truth and identity|Nursing every grievance|
|Humor|Relieving fear and tension|Mocking genuine pain|
|Opportunism|Acting when circumstances change|Exploiting another’s failure|
|Resourcefulness|Giving discarded things new purpose|Hoarding objects or information|

A recurring Corvid anxiety is the fear of **being deliberately forgotten or falsely remembered**.

Some respond by recording everything. Others build loud reputations that cannot easily be erased. A few become deeply secretive, believing that controlling information is the only defense against having their story stolen.

### Humor in Hard Places

Corvid humor often emerges when circumstances are frightening, embarrassing, or grim.

This is not necessarily indifference.

A joke can restore a sense of control. It can test whether companions remain attentive. It can transform a terrible event into something that can be spoken aloud.

Problems arise when humor becomes a shield against sincerity.

Some Corvids become so skilled at turning pain into performance that even their closest companions struggle to recognize when they are genuinely afraid.

### Internal Divisions

Modern Corvid communities disagree over:

- Whether all knowledge deserves preservation
    
- When secrecy protects and when it corrupts
    
- Whether ancestral objects belong to families, communities, or public archives
    
- Whether mimicry used for political satire should receive legal protection
    
- Whether witness chains remain necessary in an age of Ministry records
    
- Whether opportunity should be judged by legality, benefit, or harm
    
- Whether the Sky-Reeve archives should be restored to descendant communities
    

These disputes can become intense because they concern who possesses the right to tell the past.

## VI. The Sky-Reeve Inheritance

Corvids played a vital role within the ancient [[The Sky-Reeve Clans|Sky-Reeve Clans]], though they were less celebrated in surviving Stormrider legends than the pilots who flew directly into the thunder.

Corvid scribes maintained sections of the **Ledger of Gales**, recorded the fulfillment of Wind-Tithes, preserved safe routes through changing currents, and adjudicated disputes concerning aerial law.

Many served as:

- Wind-Tithe witnesses
    
- Route recorders
    
- Sky-law interpreters
    
- Harness inspectors
    
- Signal keepers
    
- Stormrider chroniclers
    
- Custodians of Sky-Script Feathers
    

The pilots entered the storm.

The Corvids remembered what the storm took.

### Sky-Script Feathers

The Sky-Reeves developed methods of inscribing flight feathers with compact records, ritual marks, route instructions, and pressure-responsive runes.

These objects became known as **Sky-Script Feathers**.

Corvid artisans were particularly associated with their production. Their fine tool control and cultural traditions of layered meaning made them skilled at placing several forms of information upon a single feather.

A Sky-Script Feather might contain:

- A visible oath
    
- A concealed route
    
- A record readable only under rain
    
- A pressure rune that changed near dangerous currents
    
- A message intended to be spoken aloud
    
- A second meaning understood only by a particular witness chain
    

Many surviving feathers remain untranslated.

Others may have been translated incorrectly by archivists who assumed each mark possessed only one meaning.

### The Ledger of Gales

Corvid oral tradition claims that the **Ledger of Gales** was never a single book.

According to these accounts, the physical tome held by the Ministry was merely the central register. Its fuller record also existed through songs, flight patterns, Sky-Script Feathers, scars, and the memories of designated witnesses.

The Ministry has neither confirmed nor disproven this claim.

Corvid reclamation groups continue to petition for shared authority over the artifact and its interpretation.

### Stormriders

Corvid Stormriders certainly existed.

They were often known for unconventional routes, improvised equipment, and a willingness to exploit brief changes in weather that more disciplined pilots ignored.

Modern Corvid riders sometimes paint false seams, extra brands, or humorous warnings onto their harnesses. Traditionalists consider this disrespectful.

The riders consider irritating traditionalists one of the few pleasures that remains free.

## VII. Corvids in Modern Aerathon

Corvids can be found throughout all Five Greater Regions.

Their social awareness and adaptability often make them well suited to places where different populations, laws, and customs overlap. This has also produced the unfair assumption that Corvids are inherently deceptive or politically untrustworthy.

### Allemance

In [[Aerathon - Eternal Labyrinths/II. The Living Atlas/Five Greater Regions/Allemance|Allemance]], Corvids frequently serve as local historians, messengers, repairers, teachers, traders, and witnesses to family agreements.

Allemancian Corvid communities often preserve the histories of small places overlooked by formal archives. They remember who rebuilt a bridge, who opened their barn during a winter, and which traveler never returned to claim the belongings left beside the hearth.

Objects are commonly preserved through continued use rather than placed behind glass.

A repaired tool is considered a stronger memorial than a pristine one no one is permitted to touch.

### Oria

In [[Aerathon - Eternal Labyrinths/II. The Living Atlas/Five Greater Regions/Oria|Oria]], Corvids are valued for diagnostics, systems analysis, salvage, documentation, and the recognition of minor irregularities before they become catastrophic.

Oric Corvids often possess a direct, practical relationship with information.

A secret is not valuable merely because it is hidden. Knowledge must explain, repair, prevent, or improve something to justify the resources spent preserving it.

Many work as inspectors, investigators, engineers, archivists, and emergency coordinators. Their reports are notorious for extensive annotations explaining how earlier reports failed.

### Vinyot

In [[Aerathon - Eternal Labyrinths/II. The Living Atlas/Five Greater Regions/Vinyot|Vinyot]], information is a commodity.

Corvids work throughout finance, journalism, brokerage, entertainment, intelligence, advertising, appraisal, law, and negotiation. Some build fortunes by recognizing connections before the wider market notices them.

Vinyoti Corvids are often skilled at determining what information is worth, who needs it, and what its release will change.

This does not mean every Corvid sells secrets.

Many of Vinyot’s strongest protections for sources, witnesses, and private correspondence were established through Corvid-led legal campaigns.

Others were established after Corvid scandals made those protections necessary.

### Arneria

In [[Aerathon - Eternal Labyrinths/II. The Living Atlas/Five Greater Regions/Arneria|Arneria]], Corvids commonly serve as oath witnesses, ritual reciters, temple archivists, confessors, and keepers of names.

Certain Arnerian traditions hold that a vow spoken without a witness remains dangerously incomplete. Corvids are therefore invited to hear promises whose consequences may outlive the people making them.

This position carries considerable authority.

It also places Corvids in possession of knowledge that may threaten families, temples, or rulers. Arnerian Corvids often develop strict personal codes concerning which truths must be revealed and which must be carried silently into death.

### Al’Ar

In [[Aerathon - Eternal Labyrinths/II. The Living Atlas/Five Greater Regions/Al'Ar|Al’Ar]], Corvid culture thrives within crowded docks, storm shelters, traveling performances, salvage communities, and settlements rebuilt from the remains of earlier ones.

Al’Ari Corvids often treat improvisation as an art.

A broken object may become an instrument. A ruined building may become a stage. A failed plan may become the first draft of a better one.

Their humor tends to be fast, public, and merciless toward arrogance.

When storms destroy homes, Corvid communities are often among the first to catalogue what was lost and the first to decide which fragments are worth carrying forward.

## VIII. Corvids and Delving

Corvids are frequently recruited as investigators, scouts, interpreters, trap analysts, salvagers, negotiators, and chroniclers.

Their strength within a Labyrinth rarely comes from solving a problem through raw force.

They excel at recognizing:

- Repeated symbols
    
- Objects moved from their original positions
    
- Contradictions between rooms
    
- Changes in sound or airflow
    
- Patterns in creature behavior
    
- Connections between discarded items
    
- Failures that reveal how a mechanism functions
    

Corvid delvers often preserve objects other adventurers dismiss as useless.

A broken key, bent coin, scrap of fabric, or fragment of handwriting may later explain an entire floor.

This habit can also create danger.

The Labyrinth frequently produces objects designed to reward curiosity, exploit attachment, or punish possession. Experienced Corvids learn that not everything abandoned is waiting to be rescued.

### The Third Question

Several Corvid delving schools teach a method known as **the Third Question**.

When encountering something strange, the delver asks:

1. What does it appear to be?
    
2. What does it appear to do?
    
3. Why does the Labyrinth want me to believe the first two answers?
    

The method does not guarantee safety.

It does prevent some of the more embarrassing deaths.

## IX. Names, Titles, and Address

Corvid names vary considerably by homeland and community.

Many Corvids possess several names used in different social settings:

- A birth name
    
- A household name
    
- A public name
    
- A professional name
    
- A private name used only among trusted companions
    
- A by-name earned through a memorable event
    

Not all Corvid cultures follow this practice, and asking for someone’s “true name” is often considered intrusive.

Examples include:

- Brask of Seven Keys
    
- Cera Rain-Voice

## X. D&D Lineage Traits

Your Corvid character has the following traits.

### Creature Type

You are a Humanoid.

### Ability Score Increase

Your Dexterity score increases by 2, and your Intelligence score increases by 1.

### Size

Your size is Medium.

Corvids commonly stand between 5 feet 3 inches and 6 feet 3 inches tall.

### Speed

Your walking speed is 25 feet.

You have a flying speed of 50 feet.

### Flight

You can fly using your wings. To do so, you cannot be wearing medium or heavy armor.

### Talons

You have talons that you can use to make unarmed strikes.

When you hit with them, the strike deals slashing damage equal to `1d4 + your Strength modifier`.

### Skyward Pulse

As a bonus action, you can beat your wings with explosive force.

Each creature within 5 feet of you must succeed on a Strength saving throw or be pushed 5 feet away from you.

The saving throw DC equals:

`8 + your proficiency bonus + your Strength modifier`

You can use this trait a number of times equal to your proficiency bonus. You regain all expended uses when you finish a long rest.

### Keen Opportunist

When a creature you can see within 30 feet of you fails an ability check or saving throw, you can use your reaction to gain advantage on your next attack roll or ability check made before the end of your next turn.

You can use this trait a number of times equal to your proficiency bonus. You regain all expended uses when you finish a long rest.

## XI. Recognized Sublineages

Corvid sublineages describe broad physiological inheritances. They do not determine personality, culture, morality, profession, or homeland.

### Raven Lineage

Raven Corvids are often large-framed, long-winged, and capable of producing deep, highly varied vocalizations.

Many possess glossy black plumage with blue or violet iridescence. Their historic association with funerary rites, battlefields, and prophecy has produced numerous superstitions that vary from reverence to distrust.

### Crow and Rook Lineage

Crow and Rook Corvids are among the most widespread sublineage.

They are strongly represented in urban, agricultural, and communal settlements. Many possess exceptional social awareness and comfortably maintain large networks of relationships.

The assumption that they always act collectively is misleading. Their communities value cooperation but also contain intense personal rivalries.

### Magpie Lineage

Magpie Corvids commonly possess contrasting black-and-white plumage, long tails, and expressive body language.

They are strongly associated with collecting beautiful objects. While many Magpies do appreciate decoration and craft, the belief that they compulsively steal anything that shines is both inaccurate and insulting.

### Jay Lineage

Jay Corvids include numerous brightly marked populations, often displaying blue, green, grey, white, or barred plumage.

They are commonly vocal, adaptable, and capable of forceful calls. Outsiders sometimes misread their expressiveness as aggression.

Some Jay communities have developed elaborate public-speaking, warning-call, and performance traditions.

### Jackdaw and Chough Lineage

Jackdaw and Chough Corvids are generally compact, agile flyers with strong communal traditions.

Many are adapted to cliffs, towers, ruins, or mountain settlements. Historic populations served as messengers between Sky-Reeve outposts and as keepers of narrow aerial routes inaccessible to larger flyers.

### Nutcracker Lineage

Nutcracker Corvids tend to possess sturdy beaks, strong spatial memory, and adaptations suited to forests and highland environments.

Several communities maintain extensive seed stores and play important roles in forest cultivation. Their ability to recall dispersed locations has also made them respected navigators and cache-keepers.

## XII. Homeland Imprints

If the campaign uses Homeland Imprints, choose one trait representing the region that most strongly shaped your character.

A homeland represents upbringing and cultural experience. It does not need to match your birthplace or ancestry.

### Allemance — Roadside Recall

You gain proficiency in the Insight skill.

When you take the Dash or Disengage action on your turn, you can also make a Wisdom (Perception) check as a bonus action.

Allemancian Corvids learn that noticing who is missing can matter more than reaching one’s destination first.

### Oria — Contingency Flight

While flying, you have advantage on saving throws against being charmed or frightened.

When you are reduced to half your hit points or fewer, you can use your reaction to fly up to half your flying speed without provoking opportunity attacks.

Once you use this reaction, you cannot use it again until you finish a long rest.

Oric Corvids are taught that recognizing a failed approach is not cowardice. It is useful information.

### Vinyot — Calculated Descent

While falling, you can reduce the fall speed of yourself or one willing creature within 5 feet of you by 60 feet per round, potentially negating falling damage.

You also have advantage on Dexterity (Acrobatics) checks made while airborne.

Vinyoti Corvids know that a collapsing opportunity may still be turned into a survivable arrangement.

### Arneria — Witness of the Wind

You know the _gust_ cantrip.

When you reach 3rd level, you can cast _feather fall_ once with this trait. You regain the ability to do so when you finish a long rest.

When you reach 5th level, you can cast _augury_ as a ritual, using the movement of wind and cloud as your ritual focus.

Arnerian Corvids preserve questions in ritual form, asking the sky to bear witness when mortal testimony proves uncertain.

### Al’Ar — Storm Cackle

When you use **Skyward Pulse**, you may release a piercing laugh or cry carried by the force of your wings.

Creatures of your choice within 10 feet of you must succeed on a Constitution saving throw against your Skyward Pulse saving throw DC or be deafened until the start of your next turn.

Once you use this trait, you cannot use it again until you finish a long rest.

Among Al’Ari Corvids, laughter in disaster is often the first sign that someone has already begun imagining the escape.

## XIII. Playing a Corvid

When creating a Corvid character, consider:

- What kinds of details do they notice first?
    
- What do they collect, preserve, or refuse to discard?
    
- How do they distinguish curiosity from intrusion?
    
- What secret have they chosen to keep?
    
- What truth do they believe must eventually be revealed?
    
- How do they use humor when afraid?
    
- Do they trust written records, living witnesses, or neither?
    
- Which of their names do they share freely?
    
- Have they ever benefited from another person’s failure?
    
- Did they feel guilty about taking the opportunity?
    
- What event are they determined will not be forgotten?
    
- How do they regard the Sky-Reeve legacy: inheritance, stolen history, useful archive, or ancient obsession?
    

A Corvid may be solemn or playful, trusting or suspicious, meticulous or chaotic.

Their lineage teaches them to recognize connections.

It does not decide what they will do with them.

>
