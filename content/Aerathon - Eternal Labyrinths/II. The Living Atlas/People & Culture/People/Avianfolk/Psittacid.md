## I. Lineage Overview

**Psittacids** are an Avianfolk lineage distinguished by powerful curved beaks, dexterous feet and hands, vivid plumage, complex social communication, and an exceptional capacity for vocal imitation.

Their recognized sublineages include grey parrots, macaws, cockatoos, kea, amazons, lories, lorikeets, parakeets, budgerigars, and numerous related peoples.

Psittacids are commonly described as loud, colorful, clever, theatrical, and difficult to ignore.

These descriptions are not entirely false.

They are also frequently used to excuse treating Psittacids as decorations, entertainers, curiosities, or amusing copies of more “serious” speakers.

Psittacid cultures often place tremendous value upon memory, responsiveness, social presence, and the transformation of inherited material. A phrase is not always repeated because the speaker lacks words of their own. It may be repeated because it deserves preservation, because its meaning has changed, or because hearing it in another voice reveals something the original speaker concealed.

Imitation is therefore not merely entertainment.

It may be scholarship, testimony, satire, affection, mockery, warning, ritual, or accusation.

A Psittacid child is commonly taught two lessons at once:

> Remember what was said.

And:

> Decide whether it should be said again.

## II. Origins and Collective Memory

Psittacids possess no single accepted origin.

Their oldest communities have been identified in tropical forests, mountain valleys, coastal regions, island chains, river basins, dense cities, and warm highlands rich in fruit, seed, and flowering plants.

Some Psittacid traditions claim that their ancestors received color when they carried the first rainbow from one storm to another.

Others teach that they once possessed ordinary voices until a dying god entrusted them with the last words of every creature that had already vanished.

A Kea tradition from the southern highlands offers a less solemn explanation. According to it, the first Psittacid stole speech from a sleeping giant simply because the giant had declared the language forbidden.

The giant awoke to discover that every bird in the valley was repeating his private complaints.

Psittacid historians have found little evidence supporting any account.

Most agree that the Kea version is the most believable.

### The Kept Phrase

Many Psittacid cultures preserve history through **Kept Phrases**.

A Kept Phrase is a statement repeated as accurately as possible across generations. It may consist of:

- Final words
    
- Legal testimony
    
- A warning
    
- A blessing
    
- A confession
    
- The beginning of a speech
    
- A family joke
    
- A command given during disaster
    
- A phrase spoken by someone who was never seen again
    

The words are preserved alongside an account of who first spoke them, who heard them, and why they mattered.

Some Kept Phrases remain solemn.

Others become humorous as their original circumstances fade. A terrified ancestor’s warning about a collapsing bridge might eventually become a common phrase used whenever someone attempts an obviously poor plan.

Psittacid scholars argue over whether this diminishes history.

Many maintain that a phrase still spoken—even irreverently—has survived more successfully than one preserved in a sealed archive and forgotten.

### Memory Through Transformation

Psittacids do not always preserve history unchanged.

Stories may be retold in new rhythms, performed through several voices, accompanied by altered colors, or spoken from the perspective of someone who appeared only briefly in the original account.

This practice is known as **turning the voice**.

To turn the voice is to ask how an inherited story changes when spoken by someone it once ignored.

A heroic account may be repeated by the servant who cleaned the battlefield afterward. A royal proclamation may be performed using the voices of those who suffered beneath it. A famous speech may be interrupted by the phrases its speaker tried to exclude.

Traditionalists accuse such performances of corrupting the record.

Their defenders argue that a record protecting only the most powerful speaker was already corrupt.

## III. Body and Aerial Form

Psittacids are generally medium in build, with strong shoulders, powerful beaks, and highly dexterous hands and feet.

Most stand between **5 feet 1 inch and 6 feet 4 inches**. Macaw and cockatoo sublineages may possess particularly broad wings or tall crests, while parakeet and lorikeet populations are often more compact.

Psittacid beaks are adapted for gripping, breaking, climbing, manipulating objects, and processing hard foods. Their bite can be extremely powerful.

Their feet commonly possess two forward-facing and two rear-facing toes, allowing them to grip branches, ropes, tools, food, and architectural supports with considerable precision.

Many Psittacids use their feet as readily as their hands when eating, crafting, climbing, or handling small objects.

### Plumage

Psittacid plumage includes some of the most varied coloration found among Avianfolk.

Common colors include:

- Scarlet
    
- Cobalt
    
- Emerald
    
- Gold
    
- White
    
- Black
    
- Grey
    
- Violet
    
- Turquoise
    
- Rose
    
- Orange
    
- Combinations of several colors across the wings, face, chest, and tail
    

Color does not reliably indicate temperament, health, social standing, or magical ability.

Nevertheless, many Psittacid cultures incorporate plumage into clothing, heraldry, dance, mourning customs, and public presentation.

Some individuals accentuate their natural colors through jewelry and dyes. Others deliberately dress in muted tones to avoid being treated as a spectacle.

### Crests and Facial Expression

Cockatoo and certain other sublineages possess movable crests that can signal surprise, agitation, interest, excitement, or threat.

Psittacid expression may also involve:

- Eye movement
    
- Feather position
    
- Beak posture
    
- Wing placement
    
- Tail spread
    
- Rhythmic tapping
    
- Changes in vocal pitch
    

Outsiders sometimes mistake visible expressiveness for emotional simplicity.

A person may display excitement while concealing fear, perform outrage while remaining amused, or repeat another speaker’s tone without sharing their feeling.

### Vocal Mimicry

Many Psittacids can reproduce voices, tones, alarms, musical phrases, mechanical sounds, animal calls, and other noises with extraordinary accuracy.

The ability varies between individuals and sublineages.

Accurate imitation usually requires attentive listening. Complex speech patterns, unfamiliar languages, and magical voices may require repeated exposure.

A reproduced voice is still an imitation. It does not grant access to the original speaker’s knowledge, magic, authority, or intent.

## IV. Society and Kinship

Psittacid communities are often organized through broad, vocal, and highly interactive social networks.

A family or household may be called a **canopy**, especially when several generations or unrelated companions share responsibility for one another.

A canopy need not occupy a single home.

It may extend across:

- Several buildings
    
- A district
    
- A migratory route
    
- A trading company
    
- A performance troupe
    
- A religious community
    
- A delving guild
    
- Multiple regions connected through regular correspondence
    

Within many canopies, decisions are discussed openly and energetically. Several conversations may occur simultaneously, with participants moving between them as new information emerges.

To outsiders, the process may appear disorganized.

Psittacids often regard strict one-speaker-at-a-time procedure as needlessly fragile. A discussion that collapses because two people spoke together, they argue, was not much of a discussion.

### Voice-Keeping

Many Psittacid communities designate trusted individuals as **Voice-Keepers**.

A Voice-Keeper preserves important statements, pronunciations, songs, names, and vocal mannerisms. They may be asked to reproduce the words of someone who is absent, dead, ill, or unable to speak.

The role carries strict ethical expectations.

A Voice-Keeper must distinguish between:

- Words intended for public memory
    
- Words entrusted only to a family
    
- Words preserved as legal testimony
    
- Words repeated for comfort
    
- Words that should not be reproduced without permission
    
- Words whose original speaker withdrew them before death
    

Using a preserved voice for mockery, fraud, or political manipulation is considered a profound violation.

### The Right of One’s Own Voice

Psittacid societies have long debated who possesses authority over a spoken voice.

Most modern communities recognize some version of **the Right of One’s Own Voice**.

This principle holds that a person may imitate another’s words for commentary, education, warning, or art, but may not use their voice to falsely claim consent, authority, authorship, or presence.

Regional law varies considerably.

Vinyoti courts have produced hundreds of pages distinguishing satire from impersonation. Arnerian temples often treat sacred vocal reproduction as a matter of ritual permission. Oric Houses tend to focus upon whether imitation caused measurable harm or operational confusion.

Psittacids continue to argue about every version.

## V. Inner Life, Virtue, and Shadow

Psittacid communities commonly admire:

- Curiosity
    
- Strong memory
    
- Emotional presence
    
- Social courage
    
- Inventiveness
    
- Loyalty maintained across distance
    
- The ability to transform inherited knowledge
    
- The refusal to let powerful voices become the only voices remembered
    

These values can produce insightful scholars, devoted companions, fearless performers, skilled diplomats, and people capable of bringing energy into stagnant situations.

They can also become overwhelming.

|Admired Quality|Constructive Expression|Potential Shadow|
|---|---|---|
|Curiosity|Learning through direct engagement|Ignoring boundaries|
|Expressiveness|Communicating openly and vividly|Dominating every social space|
|Memory|Preserving important words|Refusing to release insults|
|Loyalty|Maintaining enduring bonds|Possessiveness|
|Playfulness|Encouraging creativity|Treating serious matters as games|
|Mimicry|Preserving and examining speech|Impersonation or ridicule|
|Confidence|Speaking when others hesitate|Mistaking volume for authority|
|Reinvention|Giving old traditions new life|Changing them beyond recognition|

A recurring Psittacid anxiety is the fear of **being valued only as an echo**.

Others may praise their ability to reproduce a song while ignoring what they themselves compose. They may be invited to repeat a famous speech but not to offer their own opinion. They may be remembered for color, charm, or performance rather than judgment.

Some respond by cultivating unmistakable personal styles.

Others refuse to imitate anyone, even when doing so would be harmless. A few repeat others so constantly that their own unguarded voice becomes difficult to recognize.

### Emotional Intensity

Many Psittacid communities permit emotions to be expressed visibly and immediately.

Joy may be loud. Anger may be public. Grief may involve repeated phrases, torn adornments, communal cries, or long conversations held through the night.

This does not mean Psittacids lack restraint.

Restraint may simply take a different form. A person might speak extensively while carefully avoiding one forbidden subject. Another may display anger openly to prevent it from becoming hidden resentment.

Outsiders sometimes perceive this intensity as instability.

Psittacids often perceive outsider restraint as dishonesty with better posture.

### Internal Divisions

Modern Psittacid communities disagree over:

- Whether voices can be owned
    
- When imitation becomes impersonation
    
- Whether sacred phrases may be artistically transformed
    
- Whether bright public culture pressures private individuals into performance
    
- Whether preserved voices should be destroyed at the speaker’s request
    
- Whether ancient Sky-Reeve commands may be legally reproduced
    
- Whether entertainers strengthen or trivialize Psittacid identity
    
- Whether memory should preserve offensive speech exactly
    
- Whether public confrontation is healthier than private resentment
    

These disputes frequently become loud.

They are not necessarily hostile.

## VI. The Sky-Reeve Inheritance

Psittacids served throughout the ancient [[The Sky-Reeve Clans|Sky-Reeve Clans]] as signal relays, diplomatic voices, ritual reciters, weather callers, harness engineers, and Stormriders.

Their ability to preserve exact phrasing made them especially valuable where a changed word could alter an oath, route command, or Wind-Tithe instruction.

### Gale-Tongues

Sky-Reeve **Gale-Tongues** were trained to reproduce the voices and commands of Wind-Mothers, route captains, and distant watch stations.

Their role allowed important instructions to pass across the Eyrie-Spire of Taellen without being altered by repeated interpretation.

A Gale-Tongue might memorize:

- Flight clearances
    
- Emergency descent commands
    
- Wind-Tithe verses
    
- Legal judgments
    
- Storm warnings
    
- Changes to aerial boundaries
    
- Final instructions from a departing rider
    

This authority was carefully restricted.

A Gale-Tongue repeating the voice of a Wind-Mother could redirect an entire formation. False repetition was therefore treated as both sacrilege and treason.

Several surviving Sky-Reeve disputes concern whether a Gale-Tongue reproduced an order incorrectly—or reproduced an order correctly that a leader later regretted giving.

### The Prism Tithes

Some Psittacid traditions preserve accounts of informal offerings called **Prism Tithes**.

These were not recognized among the formal Blue, Silver, and Crimson Tithes.

A Prism Tithe involved flights through rain and stormlight while wearing reflective metal, colored glass, polished shell, or vivid ceremonial plumage. The rider attempted to cast broken bands of color across the clouds.

Supporters claimed the rite reminded the storm that destruction was not the only thing light could create.

Critics called it vanity.

The surviving descriptions suggest it was both.

### Thunderplumes

Psittacid Stormriders were sometimes known as **Thunderplumes**.

They became famous for decorated harnesses, shouted challenges, coordinated cries, and the deliberate use of color against dark stormclouds. Many were skilled pilots.

Others survived primarily because the sky was apparently too surprised to kill them.

Thunderplumes contributed heavily to the transformation of Stormriding from solemn offering into public spectacle. Their races attracted enormous crowds and encouraged the elaborate introductions, sponsorship colors, and competitive call-signs still used in Vinyot and Al’Ar.

Traditional Sky-Reeve accounts often condemn them.

Those same accounts preserve their names with unusual care.

### The Last Commands

Following the destruction of the Eyrie-Spire, Psittacid survivors preserved hundreds of spoken fragments believed to have come from its final hours.

These **Last Commands** include warnings, prayers, route instructions, cries for missing family members, and statements whose meanings remain unknown.

Different Voice-Keeper traditions reproduce the phrases differently.

No complete record has been authenticated.

Some researchers believe that, when performed in the correct order and voices, the Last Commands may reveal what truly destroyed the Eyrie-Spire.

Others believe the attempt would activate whatever caused the disaster.

The Ministry has prohibited organized full-sequence recitation without atmospheric containment.

This prohibition has not eliminated organized full-sequence recitation.

## VII. Psittacids in Modern Aerathon

Psittacids live throughout the Five Greater Regions, with particularly large communities in warm forests, coastal settlements, major cities, and trade centers.

Their abilities often lead to work involving communication, craft, performance, diplomacy, teaching, and public life. These professions reflect social opportunity rather than biological destiny.

### Allemance

In [[Aerathon - Eternal Labyrinths/II. The Living Atlas/Five Greater Regions/Allemance|Allemance]], Psittacids commonly participate in household networks, local markets, craft circles, schools, courier routes, and seasonal gatherings.

Allemancian Psittacids often preserve family phrases and familiar voices.

A grandparent’s greeting may be repeated when opening the home. The warning of a long-dead bridge keeper may still be called before travelers cross the rebuilt span. Children may learn household history through the sayings of relatives they never met.

The value lies not in perfect imitation.

It lies in ensuring that the absent remain part of the conversation.

### Oria

In [[Aerathon - Eternal Labyrinths/II. The Living Atlas/Five Greater Regions/Oria|Oria]], Psittacids often serve in communications, emergency signaling, instruction, diagnostics, mechanical design, and operational coordination.

Oric institutions value exact repetition when precision matters.

A command, measurement, or warning that changes during relay can cost lives.

Psittacid memory is therefore respected, but rarely accepted without verification. Important instructions are commonly repeated back to the original speaker and confirmed before action begins.

Oric Psittacids often develop dry attitudes toward the assumption that mimicry replaces technical understanding.

Repeating an engineer does not make one an engineer.

Many become engineers anyway.

### Vinyot

In [[Aerathon - Eternal Labyrinths/II. The Living Atlas/Five Greater Regions/Vinyot|Vinyot]], voice, color, reputation, and attention all possess measurable value.

Psittacids are prominent in negotiation, advertising, theatre, music, broadcasting, political speech, legal advocacy, auctioneering, luxury craft, and public entertainment.

Vinyoti businesses frequently compete to secure recognizable Psittacid voices.

Contracts may control:

- Public appearances
    
- Recorded speech
    
- Magical reproductions
    
- Advertising phrases
    
- Posthumous performances
    
- Imitations of other public figures
    
- Ownership of a performer’s call-sign
    

Psittacid labor movements have fought extensively against contracts that claim permanent rights to a voice.

Other Psittacids have become extremely wealthy by selling those rights carefully.

### Arneria

In [[Aerathon - Eternal Labyrinths/II. The Living Atlas/Five Greater Regions/Arneria|Arneria]], Psittacids serve as ritual reciters, oath heralds, temple singers, doctrinal teachers, witnesses, and custodians of sacred pronunciation.

Certain rites require phrases to be spoken exactly as preserved.

A changed stress, breath, or tone may alter the ritual’s meaning.

Psittacid Voice-Keepers therefore hold considerable authority. Some can reproduce the voices of priests and rulers who died centuries earlier.

This practice is treated with reverence in some traditions and unease in others.

A dead authority repeating an old command may preserve sacred continuity.

It may also prevent the living from questioning it.

### Al’Ar

In [[Aerathon - Eternal Labyrinths/II. The Living Atlas/Five Greater Regions/Al'Ar|Al’Ar]], Psittacid communities flourish in dock-cities, tropical settlements, traveling performances, storm markets, cliff towns, and regions rebuilt repeatedly through improvisation.

Al’Ari Psittacids often transform whatever they inherit.

Foreign songs acquire new rhythms. Broken ornaments become instruments. Storm warnings become festival chants once the danger has passed.

Their culture prizes the ability to repeat something without leaving it unchanged.

This can frustrate historians.

It delights nearly everyone else.

## VIII. Psittacids and Delving

Psittacids commonly serve as communicators, negotiators, support specialists, scouts, interpreters, magical performers, and investigators.

Their vocal abilities can be valuable in environments where direct communication is dangerous or impossible.

A trained Psittacid delver may use mimicry to:

- Reproduce a discovered activation phrase
    
- Relay a warning in the original speaker’s tone
    
- Distract creatures with a familiar sound
    
- Test whether an echo returns accurately
    
- Preserve testimony before memory fades
    
- Recreate a mechanical noise for comparison
    
- Identify whether an apparent voice has changed
    
- Communicate using agreed calls
    
- Expose an impersonator through subtle vocal differences
    

The Labyrinth frequently weaponizes familiarity.

It may reproduce the voice of a loved one, repeat a command from a dead leader, or speak a private phrase that no stranger should know.

Psittacid delvers are not immune to these deceptions.

They may be especially vulnerable because they understand how much identity can live within a voice.

### The First Voice

Psittacid delving schools commonly teach a verification practice known as **the First Voice**.

Before entering a dangerous area, each companion speaks a phrase that has not been prepared in advance. The Psittacid listens for:

- Breathing
    
- Hesitation
    
- Emotional rhythm
    
- Pronunciation
    
- Changes caused by injury or fear
    

Later imitation may reproduce the words.

It may not reproduce the living circumstances in which they were spoken.

The method is useful against illusions and vocal mimics.

It is not infallible.

A convincing imitation remains convincing.

## X. D&D Lineage Traits

Your Psittacid character has the following traits.

### Creature Type

You are a Humanoid.

### Ability Score Increase

Your Dexterity score increases by 2, and your Charisma score increases by 1.

### Size

Your size is Medium.

Psittacids commonly stand between 5 feet 1 inch and 6 feet 4 inches tall.

### Speed

Your walking speed is 25 feet.

You have a flying speed of 50 feet.

### Flight

You can fly using your wings. To do so, you cannot be wearing medium or heavy armor.

### Talons

You have talons that you can use to make unarmed strikes.

When you hit with them, the strike deals slashing damage equal to `1d4 + your Strength modifier`.

### Skyward Pulse

As a bonus action, you can beat your wings with explosive force.

Each creature within 5 feet of you must succeed on a Strength saving throw or be pushed 5 feet away from you.

The saving throw DC equals:

`8 + your proficiency bonus + your Strength modifier`

You can use this trait a number of times equal to your proficiency bonus. You regain all expended uses when you finish a long rest.

### Echoed Voice

You can mimic sounds and voices you have heard, including speech.

A creature that hears your imitation can determine that it is false by succeeding on a Wisdom (Insight) check contested by your Charisma (Deception) check.

Additionally, as a bonus action, you can choose one creature within 30 feet of you that can hear you. The target must succeed on a Wisdom saving throw or become distracted by your call, imitation, or sudden vocal shift.

Until the start of your next turn, a distracted creature cannot take reactions.

The saving throw DC equals:

`8 + your proficiency bonus + your Charisma modifier`

You can use this bonus action a number of times equal to your proficiency bonus. You regain all expended uses when you finish a long rest.

## XI. Recognized Sublineages

Psittacid sublineages represent distinct hereditary and physiological branches within the broader lineage. They may influence appearance, anatomy, inherited adaptations, and cultural history, but do not determine personality, morality, profession, or homeland.

### Grey Parrot Sublineage

Grey Parrot Psittacids commonly possess ash-grey plumage, pale facial markings, dark beaks, and red or maroon tails.

They are widely associated with vocal precision, long memory, and careful observation.

This reputation frequently causes outsiders to assume that Grey Psittacids are solemn scholars. Many are.

Many others use their precision primarily to repeat embarrassing remarks at the worst possible moment.

### Macaw Sublineage

Macaw Psittacids are often tall, broad-winged, and vividly colored.

Common plumage includes scarlet, blue, gold, green, and combinations of several colors. Their long tails and strong beaks give them striking silhouettes.

Macaw communities possess strong traditions of public art, aerial display, long-distance communication, and ceremonial color.

Their visibility can make privacy difficult, particularly in settlements where few other Psittacids live.

### Cockatoo Sublineage

Cockatoo Psittacids commonly possess movable crests and plumage ranging through white, black, grey, rose, yellow, and red.

Their crests provide highly visible emotional signals, though individuals can learn to control or deliberately manipulate them.

Cockatoo communities are often associated with percussion, rhythmic movement, woodcraft, and intense household bonds.

The stereotype that they are inherently destructive usually comes from people who provided insufficient tools, stimulation, or structural reinforcement.

### Kea Sublineage

Kea Psittacids are adapted to mountains, cold highlands, and harsh environments uncommon among most of the lineage.

Their plumage is often olive, green, brown, or dark grey, with vivid color concealed beneath the wings.

Kea communities are renowned for curiosity, mechanical experimentation, and the dismantling of objects to understand how they function.

They are also renowned for not always asking whether the object’s owner wanted it understood.

### Amazon Sublineage

Amazon Psittacids are generally sturdy, green-plumed, and marked with yellow, blue, red, white, or purple around the face and wings.

Many possess powerful voices and are comfortable projecting across crowded spaces.

Amazon communities are prominent in political, religious, diplomatic, and mercantile traditions.

Individuals frequently encounter the assumption that confidence and volume indicate leadership ability.

Sometimes they do.

Sometimes they indicate only confidence and volume.

### Lory and Lorikeet Sublineages

Lory and Lorikeet Psittacids are commonly slender, agile, and brilliantly colored.

Their tongues and beaks are adapted to nectar, fruit, and flowering plants. Many communities maintain close relationships with orchard keepers, gardeners, forest settlements, and traveling pollination routes.

Their diets may require accommodations not commonly available in colder regions.

Lory and Lorikeet art often incorporates floral color, sweetness, fermentation, and seasonal abundance.

### Parakeet and Budgerigar Sublineages

Parakeet and Budgerigar Psittacids tend to be compact, quick-moving, and highly social.

Their plumage includes green, yellow, blue, white, grey, violet, and complex barred patterns.

They are common in cities, grasslands, forests, and large communal settlements.

Their smaller size frequently causes outsiders to mistake them for children or assume they are physically fragile.

Both errors are best corrected politely.

At first.

## XII. Homeland Imprints

If the campaign uses Homeland Imprints, choose one trait representing the region that most strongly shaped your character.

A homeland represents upbringing and cultural experience. It does not need to match your birthplace or ancestry.

### Allemance — Familiar Answer

You gain proficiency in the Insight skill.

When you take the Dash or Disengage action on your turn, you can also make a Wisdom (Perception) check as a bonus action.

Allemancian Psittacids learn the voices of their roads and households. They notice when a familiar greeting sounds wrong—or fails to come at all.

### Oria — Emergency Relay

While flying, you have advantage on saving throws against being charmed or frightened.

When you are reduced to half your hit points or fewer, you can use your reaction to fly up to half your flying speed without provoking opportunity attacks.

Once you use this reaction, you cannot use it again until you finish a long rest.

Oric Psittacids are taught that a message survives only if the messenger recognizes when the route has failed.

### Vinyot — Controlled Spectacle

While falling, you can reduce the fall speed of yourself or one willing creature within 5 feet of you by 60 feet per round, potentially negating falling damage.

You also have advantage on Dexterity (Acrobatics) checks made while airborne.

Vinyoti Psittacids know that spectacle is most valuable when the audience never realizes how close it came to disaster.

### Arneria — Voice of the Omen

You know the _gust_ cantrip.

When you reach 3rd level, you can cast _feather fall_ once with this trait. You regain the ability to do so when you finish a long rest.

When you reach 5th level, you can cast _augury_ as a ritual, using repeated sacred phrases, wind, and cloud movement as your ritual focus.

Arnerian Psittacids preserve the exact words of old questions.

The sky is permitted to change its answer.

### Al’Ar — Thunderplume Cry

When you use **Skyward Pulse**, you may release a brilliant, piercing cry carried by the force of your wings.

Creatures of your choice within 10 feet of you must succeed on a Constitution saving throw against your Skyward Pulse saving throw DC or be deafened until the start of your next turn.

Once you use this trait, you cannot use it again until you finish a long rest.

Among Al’Ari Psittacids, the storm is not merely survived.

It is answered loudly enough to know the difference.

## XIII. Playing a Psittacid

When creating a Psittacid character, consider:

- What does their natural voice sound like?
    
- How often do they imitate others?
    
- What kinds of sounds do they remember most easily?
    
- Whose voice would they never reproduce without permission?
    
- Have they ever been valued more for performance than judgment?
    
- What phrase have they preserved?
    
- What phrase do they wish they could forget?
    
- Are they comfortable attracting attention?
    
- How do they behave when ignored?
    
- Does their curiosity respect boundaries?
    
- What inherited tradition have they transformed?
    
- What part of themselves do others mistake for performance?
    
- Do they speak openly when angry, or hide their meaning within another person’s words?
    
- How do they regard the Sky-Reeves: ancestors, performers, sacred officials, reckless celebrities, or all four?
    
- Would they repeat one of the Last Commands?
    
- What would they want remembered in their own voice?
    

A Psittacid may be restrained or flamboyant, sincere or theatrical, private or intensely social.

Their lineage allows them to carry many voices.

It does not make their own voice any less real.
