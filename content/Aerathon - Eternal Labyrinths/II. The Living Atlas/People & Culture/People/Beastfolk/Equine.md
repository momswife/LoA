## **I. Racial Overview**

Equines are defined by motion. Whether through open plains, mountain passes, or crowded city roads, they are compelled toward the horizon—physically, emotionally, and culturally. Where other peoples build monuments, equines build **routes**. Roads, trade lines, migration paths, and message chains form the backbone of equine identity.

This restlessness is not impatience, but purpose. Equines endure long marches, carry heavy burdens, and hold lines that cannot afford to break. Horses chase momentum with grace; donkeys embody resolve through stubborn endurance; mules exist between, balancing strength and restraint.

Among delvers, equines are indispensable. They scout ahead, anchor caravans, and press onward when others falter. When the Labyrinths call, equines answer—not to conquer, but to **keep moving**.

## **II. Core Equine Traits**

**Creature Type.** You are a Humanoid.

**Ability Score Increase.** Your Wisdom score increases by 2.

**Speed.** Your walking speed is 35 feet.

**March on Hooves.**  
You have advantage on saving throws made to avoid exhaustion from forced marches.

**Powerful Build.**  
You count as one size larger when determining your carrying capacity and the weight you can push, drag, or lift.

**Eyes on the Horizon.**  
You add your Wisdom modifier to initiative rolls.

## **III. Equine Subraces**

The Equine lineage expresses three dominant forms, shaped by instinct and labor rather than hierarchy.

---

### **Horse**

**Ability Score Increase.** Your Strength score increases by 1.

**Size.** Medium  
**Typical Height.** 5’0” – 7’5”

**Out of the Gate.**  
Your speed is doubled during the first round of combat.

**Sure-Footed Stomp.**  
You can move through the space of a creature smaller than you or prone without it counting as difficult terrain. When you do, you may make an unarmed strike (1d4 + Strength bludgeoning). On a hit, that creature cannot make opportunity attacks against you until the end of your turn. You can’t use this feature on the same creature again until your next turn.

**Racial Quirks**

- Drawn to open spaces
    
- Competitive but sportsmanlike
    
- Restless when confined
    

---

### **Donkey**

**Ability Score Increase.** Your Constitution score increases by 1.

**Size.** Medium  
**Typical Height.** 5’3” – 5’10”

**Unbroken Focus.**  
You have advantage on Constitution saving throws made to maintain concentration on spells.

**Sleepwalker.**  
You require only 4 hours of sleep to gain the benefits of a long rest. While sleeping, you may move up to your speed in a half-conscious state. During this movement you have disadvantage on Perception checks, and your speed becomes 0 if you are carrying more than half your carrying capacity.

**Racial Quirks**

- Deeply stubborn once committed
    
- Strong sense of responsibility
    
- Endures hardship without complaint
    

---

### **Mule**

**Ability Score Increase.** Your Strength or Constitution score increases by 1 (your choice).

**Size.** Medium  
**Typical Height.** 5’5” – 6’8”

**Hybrid Endurance.**  
When you fail a saving throw against exhaustion, you can choose to succeed instead. Once you use this feature, you can’t do so again until you finish a long rest.

**Load-Bearer’s Balance.**  
You have advantage on Dexterity saving throws and Acrobatics checks made to keep your footing or avoid being knocked prone.

**Racial Quirks**

- Pragmatic and skeptical
    
- Values stability over speed
    
- Often acts as mediator between stronger personalities

## **IV. Regional Variance (Homeland Traits)**

Equine cultures adapt strongly to regional needs, leaving lasting marks on training and instinct.

---

### **Allemance**

Equines form the backbone of rural patrols and seasonal migration routes.

**Hunter Legacy.**  
You have advantage on saving throws against being charmed.

---

### **Oria**

Oric equines master frozen passes and long-distance supply routes.

**Horseshoes in the Drifts.**  
You have advantage on Survival checks in arctic terrain. Snow and ice do not count as difficult terrain for you. You gain proficiency with javelins, and you have advantage on attacks with them against creatures smaller than you.

---

### **Vinyot**

Urban equines act as couriers, religious messengers, and quiet information brokers.

**Carriers of the Good Word.**  
You gain proficiency in Religion. You may encode hidden messages within mundane text. Teaching an ally to decode takes 1 hour. Discovering encoded text requires 1 hour and a DC 15 Investigation check; decoding without instruction requires a DC 25 check.

---

### **Arneria**

Arnerian equines are legendary endurance runners and couriers.

**Mail Runner.**  
When you make a Dexterity saving throw or Acrobatics check to maintain balance or avoid being knocked prone, treat a d20 roll of 9 or lower as a 10. You also have advantage on Wisdom checks related to navigation.

---

### **Al’Ar**

With no open roads, Al’ari equines channel motion into martial discipline.

**Hammer Masters.**  
You gain proficiency with warhammers. When you attack objects with a warhammer, you deal double damage.
