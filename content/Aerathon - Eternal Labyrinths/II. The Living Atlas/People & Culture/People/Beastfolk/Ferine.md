## **I. Racial Overview**

Ferine beastfolk are shaped by **pressure rather than place**. Where other peoples organize around tradition, land, or inherited hierarchy, Ferine cultures emerge in environments defined by instability—scarcity, contested authority, violence, and collapse. Cooperation exists among Ferine, but it is **earned**, not assumed; rivalry is normalized, and dominance is an accepted social language.

Ferine are not inherently cruel, nor are they mindlessly violent. They are, however, **comfortable with conflict** in a way that unsettles more cooperative societies. Death, decay, and aftermath are understood as inevitable conditions of existence rather than moral failures. Humor among Ferine often skews sharp or abrasive—used to test boundaries, assert standing, or blunt fear.

Among delvers, Ferine are frequently underestimated. Their tolerance for chaos, resistance to intimidation, and refusal to panic under pressure make them exceptionally dangerous when cornered. They do not break easily. When they do, they tend to leave scars.

## **II. Core Ferine Traits**

**Creature Type.** You are a Humanoid.

**Ability Score Increase.** Your Constitution score increases by 2.

**Speed.** Your walking speed is 30 feet.

**Built for Aftermath.**  
You have advantage on saving throws against the poisoned condition, and you have resistance to poison damage.

**Competitive Instincts.**  
When a creature you can see within 30 feet drops to 0 hit points, you gain temporary hit points equal to your proficiency bonus. You can benefit from this feature once per turn.

**No Easy Prey.**  
When you are frightened, charmed, or poisoned, you can use your reaction to gain advantage on your next attack roll or ability check made before the end of your next turn. You can use this feature a number of times equal to your proficiency bonus per long rest.

## **III. Ferine Variants**

The Ferine lineage expresses several dominant archetypes. These variants reflect instinctive survival strategies rather than culture, class, or homeland.

---

### **Hyena**

**Ability Score Increase.** Your Charisma score increases by 1.

**Size.** Medium  
**Typical Height.** 5’4” – 6’4”  
**General Build.** Sloped posture, powerful jaws, highly expressive facial musculature.

**Racial Feat — Mocking Resilience.**  
When you take damage, you can use your reaction to impose disadvantage on the next attack made against you before the end of the attacker’s next turn. You can use this feature a number of times equal to your proficiency bonus per long rest.

**Racial Quirks**

- Laughs or jokes during high stress
    
- Highly attuned to social hierarchies
    
- Comfortable asserting dominance openly
    

---

### **Wolverine**

**Ability Score Increase.** Your Strength score increases by 1.

**Size.** Medium  
**Typical Height.** 5’0” – 6’0”  
**General Build.** Compact, heavily muscled, low center of gravity.

**Racial Feat — Territorial Fury.**  
When a creature enters your reach for the first time on its turn, you can use your reaction to make a melee weapon attack against it. You can use this feature once per turn, a number of times equal to your proficiency bonus per long rest.

**Racial Quirks**

- Fiercely territorial over chosen spaces
    
- Disproportionate responses to perceived threats
    
- Little interest in negotiation once provoked
    

---

### **Honey Badger**

**Ability Score Increase.** Your Dexterity score increases by 1.

**Size.** Medium  
**Typical Height.** 5’2” – 6’2”  
**General Build.** Thick hide, broad shoulders, scars worn openly.

**Racial Feat — Reckless Defiance.**  
When you fail a saving throw, you can choose to succeed instead. When you do, you take force damage equal to twice your proficiency bonus. You can use this feature once per long rest.

**Racial Quirks**

- Notably fearless, often to a fault
    
- Minimal concern for self-preservation
    
- Deep respect for audacity in others
    

---

### **Tasmanian Devil**

**Ability Score Increase.** Your Strength or Dexterity score increases by 1 (your choice).

**Size.** Medium  
**Typical Height.** 4’8” – 5’8”  
**General Build.** Compact, sharp-featured, constantly in motion.

**Racial Feat — Feeding Frenzy.**  
Once per turn, when you hit a creature that has already taken damage since the end of your last turn, you deal extra damage equal to your proficiency bonus.

**Racial Quirks**

- Loud vocalizations during conflict
    
- Thrives in chaotic group engagements
    
- Difficulty enduring prolonged stillness or restraint

## **IV. Regional Variance (Environmental Adaptation)**

Ferine do not develop identity around traditional homelands. Instead, their cultural expression adapts to **conditions**—the pressures of place rather than the place itself.

---

### **Allemance**

Ferine in Allemance exist at the margins of authority, often employed when order fails and discarded when it returns.

**Crowd-Breaker.**  
When you are adjacent to two or more hostile creatures, you have advantage on Strength (Athletics) checks and saving throws made to avoid being grappled, restrained, or knocked prone.

---

### **Oria**

Isolation and scarcity shape Oric Ferine into endurance specialists who prize grit over dominance displays.

**Thin-Air Grit.**  
You have advantage on Constitution saving throws made to resist exhaustion caused by environmental effects, including altitude, cold, or forced marches.

---

### **Vinyot**

Urban Ferine learn to survive sudden, public violence and volatile social hierarchies.

**No Easy Mark.**  
When you roll initiative, you gain temporary hit points equal to your proficiency bonus. These temporary hit points last for 1 minute.

---

### **Arneria**

Along war roads and scorched trade routes, Ferine are shaped by constant motion and attrition.

**Relentless Advance.**  
Once on your turn, when you reduce a creature to 0 hit points or score a critical hit, you can move up to half your speed without provoking opportunity attacks.

---

### **Al’Ar**

Ferine in Al’Ar survive amid shifting alliances and political chaos, where intimidation often speaks louder than force.

**Unsettling Presence.**  
As a bonus action, you can force one creature within 10 feet that can see you to make a Wisdom saving throw (DC 8 + your proficiency bonus + your Charisma modifier). On a failure, the creature has disadvantage on its next attack roll against you before the end of its next turn. You can use this feature once per long rest.

---

## **V. Settlement Patterns**

Ferine settlements are rarely permanent. They form in ruins, borderlands, war corridors, and disaster zones—often embedded within the remains of other civilizations. Ferine are frequently the first to occupy abandoned territory and the last to relinquish it.
