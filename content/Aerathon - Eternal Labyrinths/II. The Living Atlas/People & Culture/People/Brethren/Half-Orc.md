## **Overview**

Half-orcs in Aerathon are the embodiment of duality: strength and struggle, legacy and liberty. They walk a path forked between ancient orcish bloodlines and the cities and cultures they now call home. Their place in the world has often been defined _for_ them—as brutes, mercenaries, or tools of war. But in the modern age of labyrinth delving, many half-orcs have seized control of their own narrative.

Orcish ancestors hail from the shattered realms of the Material Plane, where they once served under brutal warlords and divine pacts. When the Convergence scattered peoples across Aerathon, many orcs found themselves enslaved, feared, or weaponized by early empires. In the centuries since, intermarriage, rebellion, and adaptation gave rise to the half-orc: individuals as varied as the lands they inhabit.

In delving crews, half-orcs are versatile powerhouses. Some lean into martial ferocity, fighting with ancestral wrath. Others become paladins of new causes, blending instinct with belief. Still others become unexpected leaders or unshakable sentinels. Though prejudice still lingers in some quarters, the Arena of Meritocracy rewards skill above blood—and half-orcs often _earn_ their place in the spotlight.

To be a half-orc in Aerathon is to inherit rage, and choose what to do with it.

## **Heritage Traits**

### **Allemance**

Urban half-orcs in Allemance often serve as enforcers, agents, or guards to noble houses. But more and more are breaking into arts, law, and academia. With effort, patience, and a stubborn refusal to be boxed in, Allemagnian half-orcs are rewriting their social script.

**In the Halls and Gutters.** You gain proficiency with one artisan’s tool or musical instrument. Additionally, when you roll a Charisma (Performance) or Charisma (Deception) check, you can choose to add your Strength modifier instead of Charisma.

---

### **Oria**

Half-orcs in Oria carry the deep scars of conquest. Many were born of warbands or slavery lines, yet those who survive earn their place as respected survivalists, blacksmiths, or mountain guides.

**Frostwrought Vitality.** You gain resistance to cold damage. Whenever you succeed on a saving throw against an effect that would reduce your hit point maximum, you regain 1d6 hit points.

---

### **Vinyot**

In the shadow of high towers and merchant palaces, Vinyotian half-orcs walk paths of defiance. Many are gladiators, dockmasters, or ex-militia turned adventurer. Their raw charisma and visible scars make them popular with labyrinth audiences.

**Blood in the Spotlight.** When you roll initiative, you can choose to gain advantage on your first attack roll that combat. Once you use this feature, you must finish a long rest to use it again.

---

### **Arneria**

Many half-orcs in Arneria are outlanders or raised by delver caravans. They become scouts, ruin-delvers, or lore-guardians. Among the sun-blasted stones, they're seen as fierce protectors of heritage—even if it isn't their own.

**Desert-Kin.** You gain proficiency in Survival. If you have not moved more than half your speed on your turn, you have advantage on Dexterity (Stealth) checks until the start of your next turn.

---

### **Al'Ar**

Among the islands of Al’Ar, half-orcs often lead fishing crews or serve as bouncers in seaside cities. Their strength is tempered by the rhythm of tide and music, and they embrace their fierceness with laughter.

**Tide-Blooded.** You can hold your breath for up to 10 minutes. In addition, when you take damage while submerged in water, you can use your reaction to move up to 15 feet in any direction underwater without provoking opportunity attacks.
