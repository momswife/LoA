## **Overview**

Elves in Aerathon are not merely long-lived—they are long-remembering. Born of echo and song, the elves claim ancestry that predates the Convergence. Their stories drift like pollen between worlds, carried on the wind of ancient pacts. Whether true or not, their very presence reshapes the rooms they enter: graceful, grave, and endlessly burdened by memory.

Unlike beastkin, who arose from divine uplift or transformation, elves arrived through more esoteric means. Some believe they are remnants of a vanished First World; others say they are dreamers who never woke. Whatever the truth, their souls shimmer with resonance, vibrating with forces older than language.

Elves are most often found in Oria's starlit forests, Arneria's stone-shrouded archives, and within the gilded courts of Allemance. Many have made homes in cities above Labyrinth mouths, where time unravels and the arcane mutates. They see the Labyrinths not just as threats or opportunities—but as broken songs to be studied and perhaps repaired.

Elven delvers are both envied and mistrusted. Their insight into arcane geometry, and their ability to interpret ancient glyphs, make them invaluable in the Labyrinth. But their quiet reserve and calculated detachment often alienate them from more passionate allies. Still, in the darkest corridors, when silence is strategy and magic is memory, no one is more comforting to follow than an elf.

## **Homeland Traits**

### **Allemance**

Elves in Allemance often serve as court diviners, illusionists, or ceremonial duelists. Their grace makes them stars of the stage and shadows behind the throne.

**Mask and Mirror.** You gain proficiency in Performance. Once per long rest, you can cast _disguise self_ without expending a spell slot.

---

### **Oria**

Oric elves dwell in moonlit forest manors, isolated monasteries, and mountaintop observatories. They are contemplative and cold-eyed, watching for signs in the stars and smoke.

**Glimmerwatcher.** You have darkvision out to 120 feet. In dim light or darkness, you have advantage on Wisdom (Perception) checks made to detect movement.

---

### **Vinyot**

Among the guilds of Vinyot, elves make potent legal minds, arcane brokers, and alchemical architects. Their long memories are perfectly suited for contract law—and vendettas.

**Ledgerborn.** You gain proficiency in Investigation. If you study a magical item or contract for 10 minutes, you can determine whether it is cursed, even if you do not identify it.

---

### **Arneria**

Elves in the Beylik are poets, prophets, and paladins of principle. They wander between cities like starlit ghosts, singing old warnings and watching for signs of the Labyrinth's hunger.

**Moon-Writ Oath.** You can cast _detect magic_ once per long rest. When you do, the spell also reveals the alignment (if any) of magic auras you perceive.

---

### **Al'Ar**

Al'ari elves found peace among the felines. They walk the coasts at dawn, listening to the tides and charting ley lines with shells and stones.

**Seabound Instincts.** You gain a swimming speed of 30 feet. While standing in saltwater, you can add your proficiency bonus to saving throws against spells.
