## **Overview**

Goliaths in Aerathon are born of stone, storm, and silent challenge. Towering and proud, they are descendants of elemental pacts made long before the Convergence, etched into the bones of mountains and bound to the thrum of ancient peaks. Most goliaths hail from the high passes of Oria and the sky-facing canyons of Arneria, where the wind speaks in riddles and the horizon promises nothing but struggle.

Unlike elves or gnomes, goliaths have no written record of their origin—only oral sagas chanted around pyres or shouted into thunder. Their culture is one of deed over declaration, where memory is muscle and honor is shaped by grit. Goliath children climb before they walk, race before they talk, and leap before they question the distance.

In the delving world, goliaths are shockingly adaptable. They bring physical dominance to the front line, yes, but they are also elite porters, mapless navigators, and storm-callers who feel the pull of magic in their marrow. They are rarely spellcasters, but those who are channel raw weather, echoing the elemental roots of their heritage.

The Labyrinths, in goliath eyes, are not just threats or treasure vaults—they are _trials_. And trials must be bested. For the ancestors, for the clan, and for the story.

## **Homeland Traits**

### **Allemance**

The few goliaths in Allemance serve as wardens and bodyguards, their presence a symbol of might carefully sculpted into civility. They learn not just to guard but to predict, reading crowds like weather patterns.

**Civic Sentinel.** You gain proficiency in Insight. Additionally, when you take the Ready action to guard an ally, they gain temporary hit points equal to your proficiency bonus.

---

### **Oria**

This is goliath heartland. Oric goliaths dwell in mountaintop keeps and cliff villages bound by wind-lashed bridges. Their society prizes personal feats, and many leave home to seek their Trial-Name—a second name earned through surviving a singularly defining ordeal.

**Trial-Name.** Once per long rest, when you would be reduced to 0 hit points, you can drop to 1 hit point instead. When you do, you gain temporary hit points equal to your level and cannot be frightened until the end of your next turn.

---

### **Vinyot**

In Vinyot, goliaths labor at dockworks or serve as elite debt collectors. Many choose gladiatorial arenas as their proving grounds, where their strength is as valued as the crowd's favor. These urban goliaths have learned to strike when the world is watching.

**Arena's Roar.** When you reduce a creature to 0 hit points or score a critical hit, you can force all hostile creatures within 10 feet to make a Wisdom saving throw (DC = 8 + your proficiency bonus + your Strength modifier) or be frightened until the end of their next turn.

---

### **Arneria**

Goliaths here are sand-walkers and ruin climbers, known for their endurance under blistering suns and blood-red moons. Many serve as memory-keepers for desert tribes, reciting histories carved into the bones of shattered gods.

**Sunforged Grit.** You gain proficiency in Survival. Additionally, you can go twice as long without water before suffering exhaustion.

---

### **Al'Ar**

Island goliaths are rare but revered. Found most often on Tempestat and the Broken Teeth, they are dock foremen, open-sea swimmers, and stormbreakers who fear no hurricane. Their bond with the ocean mirrors their ancestors' bond to sky and stone.

**Breaker of Storms.** You gain a swimming speed of 30 feet. When you are hit by lightning or thunder damage, you can use your reaction to grant yourself resistance to that damage and move up to 10 feet without provoking opportunity attacks.
