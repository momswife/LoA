# The Four Cardinal Winds

## I. Overview

The **Four Cardinal Winds** are among the oldest surviving cultural traditions of the Avianfolk.

They are known as:

- The North Gale
    
- The South Zephyr
    
- The East Tempest
    
- The West Cyclone
    

Although commonly associated with particular territories, the Winds are not regions, nations, faiths, or personality categories.

A Wind is a form of inherited household belonging.

It connects a family to one of the great cultural currents descended from the [[The Sky-Reeve Clans|Sky-Reeve Clans]], whose aeries, routes, weather stations, settlements, and ritual obligations once spanned much of Aerathon’s sky.

A household’s Wind may influence:

- Marriage
    
- Kinship
    
- Hospitality
    
- Funeral practice
    
- Household authority
    
- Seasonal rites
    
- Ideas of obligation
    
- Stories of departure and return
    

It does not determine temperament, morality, profession, political loyalty, or personal worth.

A Strigid born beneath the North Gale may be warm and impulsive. A Psittacid of the South Zephyr may be reserved. A Chiropterid of the East Tempest may avoid confrontation. A West Cyclone household may remain in the same village for generations.

The Winds are cultures carried through households, not destinies written into blood.

---

## II. Terminology

### Cardinal Wind

One of the four recognized cultural currents descended from the old Sky-Reeve confederations.

### House Wind

The Wind formally carried by a household.

Most Wind-bearing households recognize one House Wind, even when their wider ancestry includes several.

### Birth Wind

The House Wind into which a person was born.

### Turned Wind

A Wind entered later through formal household adoption or marriage.

The complete marriage custom is addressed in [[Turning the Wing]].

### Wind Territory

A region in which one Wind possesses deep historical settlement and cultural authority.

Wind territories may overlap and do not follow exact compass divisions.

### Wind-Bearer

A person formally recognized within a House Wind.

Depending upon local custom, this may include non-Avianfolk spouses, adoptees, and dependents.

---

## III. Origins of the Winds

The oldest Sky-Reeve records describe the Winds as both natural atmospheric systems and organized human jurisdictions.

Each Wind maintained a network of:

- Aeries
    
- Clan routes
    
- Landing towers
    
- Weather stations
    
- Refuge sites
    
- Agricultural settlements
    
- Ports
    
- Mountain passes
    
- Ritual grounds
    

The Sky-Reeves believed that stable passage through the sky required active stewardship.

Their settlements maintained the currents through offerings and public works known collectively as **Wind-Tithes**.

These included:

- Seed and harvest offerings
    
- Conductive metals
    
- Weather instruments
    
- Engine components
    
- Communal songs
    
- Ritual flight
    
- Repairs to towers and route markers
    

Some Tithes were ceremonial.

Others were dangerous acts of atmospheric engineering.

The most perilous were carried into active storms by oath-pilots later remembered as the [[Stormriders|Stormriders]].

Their surviving vow remains among the best-known fragments of Sky-Reeve culture:

> _“If I fall, let the sky remember my name in thunder.”_

Over generations, the currents became more than routes.

Families settled along them. Clans formed alliances. Marriage carried territorial consequence. Household obligations became ritual traditions.

The roads through the sky became civilizations.

---

## IV. The Fall of the Sky-Reeve Confederations

The Sky-Reeve system depended upon weather patterns that could be predicted, maintained, and crossed.

That certainty did not survive the spread of Labyrinth anomalies.

Storms appeared outside known seasons.

Wind corridors reversed.

Clear routes collapsed.

Lightning struck through stone.

Rain rose upward.

Migration paths that had remained reliable for centuries became deadly within a generation.

The Eyrie-Spire of Taellen, one of the greatest centers of Sky-Reeve authority, eventually shattered during a storm its own systems could no longer regulate.

The confederations fractured.

Some clans descended permanently into the lowlands.

Others entered the service of regional rulers, merchant leagues, temples, or emerging delver companies.

Many became couriers, scouts, builders, farmers, sailors, soldiers, and guides.

The old Wind-Mothers lost their shared continental authority.

The Four Winds nevertheless survived.

They ceased to describe the currents a people governed.

They came to describe the currents their descendants still carried.

## V. Modern Geographic Distribution

The Winds follow ancient settlement, migration, trade, and clan history rather than strict direction.

|Wind|Principal Cultural Territories|Historic Environments|
|---|---|---|
|**North Gale**|[[Oria]] and northern [[Allemance]]|Mountain, tundra, northern forest, cold coast, high migration routes|
|**South Zephyr**|Southern and southeastern [[Vinyot]], [[Bat'yan|Bat’yan]], and portions of [[Al'Ar|
|**East Tempest**|[[Arneria]] and [[Bat'yan|Bat’yan]]|
|**West Cyclone**|Portions of [[Allemance]], [[Vinyot]], and [[Al'Ar|Al’Ar]], including [[Glasrún]]|

These territories frequently overlap.

A single city may contain all four Winds.

A household may also remain loyal to its House Wind after living outside its traditional territory for generations.

Wind identity follows household inheritance, not nearest geography.

## VI. The North Gale

> _“Someone must keep the shelter standing.”_

The **North Gale** is strongest throughout Oria and northern Allemance.

Its oldest households descend from mountain aeries, tundra settlements, northern forests, cold lakes, fortified passes, and icebound coasts.

North Gale traditions developed in places where preparation was inseparable from survival.

A damaged roof, empty storehouse, forgotten route marker, or neglected warning could kill an entire household before spring.

North Gale culture therefore places strong emphasis upon preservation, preparation, shelter, duty, and memory.

### Primary Lineages

North Gale households are especially common among:

- Strigids
    
- Raptorids
    
- Corvids
    
- Anatids
    

Smaller populations exist among nearly every recognized Avianfolk lineage.

### Cultural Emphasis

North Gale traditions often value:

- Maintaining stores
    
- Preserving routes
    
- Recording disaster
    
- Repairing before replacing
    
- Preparing shelter for guests
    
- Passing down tools and practical knowledge
    
- Remembering who built, carried, guarded, or repaired what remains
    

Many North Gale households maintain some version of a final emergency reserve that may be used only during genuine need.

Using it is not shameful.

Failing to restore it afterward is.

### Common Misreading

Outsiders often mistake North Gale preparation for fear or emotional restraint.

North Gale households usually understand it as care made practical.

Their customs ask:

> What must be preserved so that others may survive what comes next?

---

## VII. The South Zephyr

> _“A guarded hearth is useless if no one is fed beside it.”_

The **South Zephyr** is strongest throughout southern and southeastern Vinyot, Bat’yan, and portions of Al’Ar.

Its oldest households descend from rainforests, wetlands, river deltas, fertile coasts, flowering uplands, warm islands, and agricultural ports.

South Zephyr traditions developed in regions where life was abundant, seasonal, and easily wasted.

Fruit spoiled.

Floods shifted.

Flowers opened and vanished.

Storms destroyed one harvest and enriched the next.

South Zephyr culture therefore places strong emphasis upon abundance, circulation, hospitality, beauty, cultivation, and renewal.

### Primary Lineages

South Zephyr households are especially common among:

- Pterygotids
    
- Chiropterids
    
- Limnids
    
- Psittacids
    
- Passerids
    
- Petaurids
    
- Anatids
    

Chiropterid communities are particularly prominent in Bat’yan, where communal roosting, fruit cultivation, pollination, nocturnal gathering, and seasonal movement align closely with South Zephyr practice.

### Cultural Emphasis

South Zephyr traditions often value:

- Shared meals
    
- First-harvest offerings
    
- Communal labor
    
- Living memorials
    
- Seasonal decoration
    
- Music
    
- Open gathering spaces
    
- Circulation of food, stories, and favors
    

Many households preserve some form of public bowl, shared table, or communal garden intended to prove that abundance remains connected to community.

### Common Misreading

Outsiders sometimes interpret South Zephyr hospitality as casual or without consequence.

In many households, generosity carries expectations of attention, reciprocity, and participation.

Their customs ask:

> What is the value of abundance if it cannot sustain anyone beyond its owner?

---

## VIII. The East Tempest

> _“A warning spoken after the lightning strikes is only an obituary.”_

The **East Tempest** is strongest throughout Arneria and Bat’yan.

Its oldest households descend from volcanic highlands, storm plains, monsoon forests, unstable wetlands, lightning-struck uplands, cavern settlements, and regions where the boundary with the Astral Sea is thin.

East Tempest traditions developed in places where danger arrived quickly and perfect certainty rarely came in time.

Storms crossed open land with little warning.

Volcanic routes shifted.

Seasonal water transformed entire regions.

Astral influence complicated distance, direction, and perception.

East Tempest culture therefore places strong emphasis upon readiness, courage, oath, transformation, warning, and responsibility for action.

### Primary Lineages

East Tempest households are especially common among:

- Raptorids
    
- Chiropterids
    
- Pterygotids
    
- Limnids
    
- Psittacids
    
- Passerids
    

Chiropterids are among the Wind’s principal modern lineages, especially within Bat’yan and other Astral-influenced regions.

Their traditions of nocturnal navigation, echolocation, communal roost defense, cavern settlement, storm sensing, and movement through uncertain spaces fit naturally within East Tempest culture.

### Cultural Emphasis

East Tempest traditions often value:

- Publicly witnessed promises
    
- Emergency signaling
    
- Preparation for rapid response
    
- Naming tools or places associated with survival
    
- Marking personal transformation
    
- Reviewing decisions after crisis
    
- Preserving failures alongside victories
    

Some households formally revisit major promises after those promises have been tested.

The purpose is not to repeat the oath perfectly.

It is to acknowledge what the oath actually required.

### Common Misreading

Outsiders frequently reduce the East Tempest to aggression or recklessness.

Its own traditions often distinguish sharply between courage and spectacle.

Their customs ask:

> What must be done before delay becomes another form of choice?

---

## IX. The West Cyclone

> _“The road home matters only because someone first dared to leave it.”_

The **West Cyclone** is the most geographically scattered of the Four Winds.

Its households are found throughout portions of Allemance, Vinyot, and Al’Ar, with especially deep cultural authority in Glasrún.

The West Cyclone follows maritime routes, island chains, rivers, trade roads, forests, caravan settlements, seasonal migrations, and the paths of Sky-Reeve dispersal.

Its oldest households descend from peoples who survived through movement.

Some traveled for trade.

Others fled political collapse, war, failed harvests, or the fall of the old aeries.

West Cyclone culture therefore places strong emphasis upon movement, adaptability, route knowledge, departure, return, and belonging across distance.

### Primary Lineages

West Cyclone households are especially common among:

- Pelagids
    
- Corvids
    
- Petaurids
    
- Passerids
    
- Anatids
    
- Chiropterids
    

Chiropterids are common within port cities, route settlements, cavern roads, and communities shaped by long-distance or planar movement.

### Cultural Emphasis

West Cyclone traditions often value:

- Route exchange
    
- Portable household records
    
- Accurate travel warnings
    
- Welcome-back meals
    
- Adaptable architecture
    
- Temporary household membership
    
- Multiple routes of return
    
- Communication during absence
    

Many households keep a cord, chain, board, or ledger marked with the names of those currently away.

The marker remains until the traveler returns or their fate is confirmed.

### Glasrún

Glasrún is culturally West Cyclone despite its northern location.

Its affiliation reflects historic migration routes, forest passage, adaptable construction, collective road maintenance, and suspicion of distant control.

A common Glasrúni saying holds:

> “A northern sky may still carry a western road.”

### Common Misreading

West Cyclone households are often stereotyped as rootless or unwilling to commit.

Their own traditions make a distinction between leaving and abandoning.

Their customs ask:

> How may a person travel without severing the road behind them?

---

## X. Bat’yan and the Meeting of South and East

Bat’yan is the principal shared territory of the South Zephyr and East Tempest.

Its rainforest settlements, seasonal waterways, violent storms, fruit-bearing canopy, cavern systems, and proximity to the Astral Sea shaped both traditions.

South Zephyr households often emphasize:

- Cultivation
    
- Shared food
    
- Ecological abundance
    
- Music
    
- Community labor
    
- Hospitality
    

East Tempest households often emphasize:

- Storm readiness
    
- Oath
    
- Rapid warning
    
- Transformation
    
- Night navigation
    
- Decisive response
    

These differences are often invisible to outsiders.

Two neighboring households may share language, food, architecture, and village obligations while carrying different Winds.

Chiropterids are especially important within this shared region.

They are not peripheral adoptees into either Wind.

They are among the principal peoples through whom both South Zephyr and East Tempest culture are lived in modern Bat’yan.

South-aligned Chiropterid households often emphasize:

- Pollination
    
- Fruit stewardship
    
- communal roosting
    
- seasonal abundance
    
- night festivals
    
- shared childrearing
    

East-aligned households often emphasize:

- Astral navigation
    
- storm listening
    
- cavern defense
    
- oath
    
- emergence through uncertainty
    
- communal warning
    

Bat’yan is not culturally divided into a South half and an East half.

Both Winds possess deep and legitimate roots throughout the region.

## XI. House Wind and Inheritance

Most Wind-bearing households formally carry one House Wind.

This principle preserves continuity in marriage, ritual, genealogy, burial, household leadership, and inheritance.

### Birth

Children ordinarily inherit the House Wind of the household into which they are born.

Under older Sky-Reeve custom, this was frequently the mother’s Wind because many clans were matrilineal.

Modern households may follow:

- Maternal inheritance
    
- Paternal inheritance
    
- Household agreement
    
- Local custom
    
- Clan obligation
    
- Adoptive affiliation
    

### Mixed-Wind Ancestry

A person may descend from all four Winds while carrying one formal House Wind.

Their broader ancestry may still shape:

- Food
    
- Speech
    
- blessings
    
- names
    
- family obligations
    
- ritual habits
    
- political relationships
    

House Wind is not a denial of mixed ancestry.

It identifies which inherited household tradition is formally carried forward.

### Marriage

Cross-Wind households ordinarily select one House Wind.

The formal rite through which one spouse joins the other’s Wind is known as [[Turning the Wing]].

The full emotional, legal, and ritual meaning of that custom belongs in its own record.

### Adoption

Adopted children ordinarily join the House Wind of the adopting household unless another arrangement is formally preserved.

### Households Without a Wind

Not every Avianfolk household carries a recognized Wind.

Some affiliations were lost through displacement.

Others were deliberately abandoned.

Several communities descend from peoples who were never fully integrated into the Sky-Reeve system.

Such households are not culturally incomplete.

---

## XII. Wind-Mothers and Household Authority

The old Sky-Reeve clans were commonly matrilineal.

Their senior ritual and infrastructural authorities were often known as **Wind-Mothers**.

A Wind-Mother could oversee:

- Household legitimacy
    
- Marriage recognition
    
- Route rights
    
- Tithe obligations
    
- Inheritance
    
- Refuge
    
- public memory
    
- clan obligation
    

The office was not identical across all four Winds.

Some Wind-Mothers acted primarily as elders or genealogical keepers.

Others possessed considerable political or engineering authority.

In modern Aerathon, the title survives in several forms:

- Household elder
    
- Clan representative
    
- Marriage witness
    
- Genealogical keeper
    
- Ritual custodian
    
- Cultural adviser
    

Some communities preserve the title formally.

Others use regional or gender-neutral equivalents.

The continental authority once associated with the office no longer exists.

The complete history of the role should be maintained separately in [[The Wind-Mothers]].

## XIII. Relationships Between the Winds

The Four Winds are not opposing factions.

Their traditions nevertheless create recurring tensions.

### North and South

North Gale households may regard South Zephyr generosity as poorly guarded.

South Zephyr households may regard North Gale preservation as hoarding.

At their best, North protects what South cultivates, while South reminds North why preservation matters.

### East and West

East Tempest households may interpret West Cyclone movement as avoidance.

West Cyclone households may interpret East Tempest oaths as unnecessary restraint.

At their best, East gives movement purpose, while West prevents purpose from becoming confinement.

### North and East

Both value preparation.

North often prepares to endure danger.

East often prepares to confront it.

### South and West

Both value exchange and movement.

South frequently builds networks through abundance.

West frequently builds them through routes.

### Traditional Balance

Later Sky-Reeve traditions often summarize the Winds as follows:

- North preserves
    
- South renews
    
- East acts
    
- West connects
    

This formulation remains culturally influential.

It should not be treated as a precise division of ancient labor or a rule governing modern people.

## XIV. The Winds in Modern Life

Wind identity remains important, but its meaning varies widely.

For some households, it governs:

- Marriage
    
- Burial
    
- Festivals
    
- household leadership
    
- hospitality
    
- names
    
- inheritance
    

For others, it functions primarily as ancestry or symbolic belonging.

A person may identify deeply with their Wind while observing few formal rites.

Another may maintain the rites largely for family continuity.

### Urban Communities

Large cities often contain associations representing all four Winds.

These may provide:

- Marriage witnesses
    
- Funeral support
    
- mutual aid
    
- household registries
    
- cultural festivals
    
- youth education
    
- emergency lodging
    

### Revival Movements

Modern Wind revival movements seek to restore:

- Old route names
    
- Weather songs
    
- Wind-Mother offices
    
- Tithe ceremonies
    
- Sky-Reeve architecture
    
- Stormrider memorial traditions
    

Some are scholarly.

Some are spiritual.

Some are political.

Several romanticize a unity that the ancient confederations may never have possessed.

### The Fifth Current

Some Chiropterid scholars and Veilriders use the term **Fifth Current** for passage between Aerathon and the Astral Sea.

The Ministry does not recognize it as a fifth House Wind.

It is better understood as a modern Chiropterid and planar philosophy concerned with:

- Liminal identity
    
- intention as navigation
    
- belonging across worlds
    
- movement between realities
    
- the limits of fixed direction
    

A Chiropterid household may carry the South Zephyr or East Tempest while also honoring the Fifth Current.

The two affiliations are not mutually exclusive.

## XV. Common Misconceptions

### “The Winds are personality types.”

False.

They are household and territorial cultures.

### “A person’s Wind is determined by where they live.”

False.

Migration does not automatically change House Wind.

### “The Four Winds divide Aerathon into equal compass regions.”

False.

Their territories follow ancient routes, settlement, and migration.

### “Chiropterids belong outside the Four Winds.”

False.

Chiropterids are major modern participants in both the South Zephyr and East Tempest, with additional West Cyclone communities.

### “Mixed-Wind families carry several House Winds.”

Usually false.

Most households formally carry one Wind while preserving ancestry from several.

### “Turning the Wing erases a person’s Birth Wind.”

False.

It changes formal household affiliation, not ancestry, memory, or family history.

### “Every Avianfolk household carries a Wind.”

False.

Some affiliations were lost, rejected, or never adopted.

### “The Fifth Current is the hidden fifth House Wind.”

False under present Ministerial classification.

### “The West Cyclone cannot include Glasrún because Glasrún lies in the north.”

False.

Wind geography is historical and cultural rather than strictly directional.

### “One Wind is naturally suited to a particular delver role.”

False.

Wind culture does not determine combat role, profession, or aptitude.

---

> [!note] Public Cultural Notice  
> Cardinal Wind identity is a protected household and cultural affiliation.
> 
> It must not be treated as evidence of personality, criminal intent, political loyalty, professional aptitude, military suitability, or delver role.
> 
> Most Wind-bearing households formally carry one House Wind, though individuals may possess ancestry and cultural influence from several.
> 
> The Ministry recognizes Chiropterids as principal participants within the South Zephyr and East Tempest, particularly throughout Bat’yan.
> 
> The Four Cardinal Winds are living cultures rather than behavioral categories or rigid territorial states.
