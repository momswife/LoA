### **Location: Halvask – The Sundering at Velgrove Square**

_"It happened in an instant. One moment, the market was alive with the sounds of trade. The next, the world cracked apart, and the city swallowed itself."_  
— Eyewitness Account, **Watch-Captain Darien Vael**

Halvask, the Jewel of Aerathon, the capital of progress and magic, had always believed itself safe from the mysteries of the Labyrinths. The Eternal Labyrinths belonged to the wilderness, the ruins, the lost places of the world—not the beating heart of civilization.

That belief was shattered at midday on the first day of the new year, when Velgrove Square—one of the city's busiest marketplaces—ceased to exist.

One moment, merchants haggled over spices and silks. Families gathered beneath banners celebrating the turn of the year. The great Astral Clocktower struck noon, its bell ringing across the city.

Then, with a single deafening crack, the earth split open.

Buildings folded inward like paper. The ground beneath the square collapsed into itself, swallowing market stalls, carriages, and people in a wave of dust and soundless force.

And when the dust cleared, where once stood Velgrove Square, now there was a Labyrinth.

A structure of impossible angles, seamless black stone, and doorways that led to nowhere. Its towers stretched too high, curving in ways that defied the laws of perspective, its entrance a jagged archway, humming with something just beyond the senses.

It had not been built.

It had not been uncovered.

It had simply appeared, replacing what had been there before as though the world itself had been rewritten.

---

### **Immediate Aftermath**

The moment it arrived, Halvask changed.

- The Astral Clocktower, standing only a block away, stopped working for the first time in recorded history. Its golden hands melted into a shape resembling a spiraled labyrinth, and now, it rings thirteen times instead of twelve.
- Hundreds vanished in the collapse—merchants, nobles, travelers—but those who should have been crushed in the wreckage left no remains. They were simply gone.
- Those who stood near the square when it happened report losing time—some for minutes, some for hours, and a few awoke days later with no memory of what transpired.
- Magic fluctuated across the city, with spells failing, warping, or producing unintended effects. The Magocratic Court of Halvask declared a state of emergency, fearing the anomaly might spread.

Within hours, the Halvaskan Guard sealed off the district, erecting wards and posting watch-captains and scholars to study the anomaly. But even the most powerful diviners, illusionists, and time-mages could not determine where the square had gone, nor why the Labyrinth had taken its place.

Only one thing is certain:

The Labyrinth was not waiting to be discovered.

It came to us.

---

### **Effects on the City**

The entire district surrounding Velgrove Square—once a hub of commerce and culture—has become a dead zone, now called The Sundering Hollow.

- No sound escapes the Labyrinth. Not the wind, not the voices of the brave (or foolish) who step near it. Even the city’s bells fall silent in its shadow.
- Shadows stretch the wrong way at night, bending toward the Labyrinth instead of the moon.
- Those who dream of the Labyrinth wake up closer to it. Some have been found sleepwalking at its threshold, whispering in languages they never spoke before.
- Paper and ink refuse to hold its description. Maps drawn of the Hollow fade, written reports erase themselves, and even spoken words about its architecture sound hollow and uncertain, as if the city itself cannot remember what it looks like.
- Every day, more people go missing—and their names vanish not just from records, but from memory.

The Magocratic Court has declared the site forbidden, and yet, each night, people slip past the barricades, drawn toward the Labyrinth as though it calls to them.

And some say, if you stand at the edge of the Hollow at midnight, you can see figures moving inside.

---

### **Final Observations: The First Urban Labyrinth**

This is the first recorded instance of a Labyrinth manifesting in a city, replacing an existing space as if it had always belonged there.

This changes everything.

- If it happened once, it can happen again.
- If it can appear in Halvask, it can appear anywhere.
- And if it replaced Velgrove Square, then where did the square go?

What was once a certainty of Delving—that the Labyrinths are ancient, lost places—has now been shattered.

The Nameless Labyrinth did not wait to be found.

It came to find us.
