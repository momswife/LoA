# **The Modern World of Aerathon**

## **A World Rewritten by Labyrinths**

Aerathon was once a continent of fractured kingdoms and old faiths, where war and worship dictated the rise and fall of nations. But all that changed when the first **Labyrinths** appeared—towering anomalies of stone and void, tearing open the skin of reality without warning.

What began as a cataclysm became an ecosystem. Labyrinths spawn monsters, relics, divine echoes, and cursed miracles. Entire civilizations were nearly wiped away before the world realized these dungeons weren’t merely threats. They were **resources**.

Now, Aerathon has entered a new age—one no longer ruled by monarchs or generals, but by **delvers**.

---

## **Delving and the Rise of Meritocracy**

Delvers are the new elite—adventurers reborn as state-sponsored, contract-bound explorers. Every Labyrinth is an opportunity: to gain power, gather wealth, or carve one’s name into the collective memory of the world.

Success is measurable. Failure is broadcast.

Guilds—independent or state-backed—train and fund delvers in exchange for shares of recovered magic, divine sponsorships, and viewer revenue. The strength of a delver is no longer just in arms or spells, but in **metrics**: kills logged, relics retrieved, audience engagement, and divine favor accumulated.

Fame means influence. Influence means power. And in Aerathon, **power is public.**

---

## **The Arcanet: Eyes That Never Close**

To manage and capitalize on the chaos of the Labyrinths, Louvain developed the **Arcanet**—an integrated magical network of surveillance, broadcasting, mapping, and divine registration.

Every registered delver is tracked. Every delve is streamed. Every artifact is cataloged.

Livecasting is the heartbeat of the modern delving world. Audiences across the continent tune into delver feeds through enchanted visors, temple mirrors, or projection arrays embedded in cityscapes. Viewers don't just watch—they participate. Emotional resonance can influence the Labyrinth itself. Voting systems determine route hazards, divine blessings, even trap intensity.

The Arcanet has transformed delving from survival into **entertainment, religion, and economy—merged into one**.

---

## **Louvain: The Heart of the New World**

Towering above all other cities stands **Louvain**, the central hub of Aerathon’s modern age.

Once a border city, it now functions as the capital of delving civilization. Here, gods walk as symbols, guilds rise as governments, and fame buys more than coin ever could. Floating sigil-lines carry goods and messages. Holy-lit towers serve as both temples and offices. Relic auction halls operate like stock exchanges, with bidding wars backed by nobility and divine patrons alike.

Louvain is clean, curated, and constantly watched.

But beneath the crystal surfaces and divine scaffolding lies something deeper: **erasure**. Forgotten districts. Delvers scrubbed from the record. Guilds dissolved without explanation. Beneath Louvain’s glow, its shadows grow long.

---

## **The New Gods and the Marketplace of Faith**

The old faiths remain, but they've been restructured to fit the Arcanet age.

Worship is **quantified**. The strength of a god is directly proportional to the attention they receive. Clerics and warlocks are often ranked alongside delvers, their miracles reviewed for authenticity and audience value.

Many gods have embraced this world:

- **Tyr** lends divine authority to the Ministry of Delving Oversight.
    
- **Lathander** sponsors rising stars through radiant boons.
    
- **Bane**, ever ambitious, funnels power to chaos-sowing delvers who increase ratings through risk.
    

Other gods have faded. Some were never named at all.  
And some—particularly those once worshipped by **beastfolk** and pre-Louvain cultures—have been erased deliberately, their names unspoken in modern doctrine.

---

## **Culture and Spectacle**

Modern Aerathon is a culture of consumption—of danger, identity, and legacy.

Delving is not a job. It is **narrative currency**. Delvers are public figures, shaped and edited by their guilds and fanbases. Propaganda posters, devotional merch, audience-led challenge runs—it’s all part of the business.

Children idolize S-rank delvers. Temples host viewings of divine interventions mid-delve. Common folk speak more about labyrinth rankings than noble lineages.

Delvers don’t just fight. They **perform**.

---

## **Beastfolk in the Modern Age**

Beastfolk walk in two worlds: one remembered and one rebranded.

Their species once held sacred traditions, gods, and territories long before the Labyrinths came. But in the rise of Louvain’s pantheon and Arcanet standardization, many of these legacies were left behind—or erased outright. Their names are underrepresented in top guilds. Their ancestral deities are missing from modern canon.

Still, beastfolk endure. Some integrate. Some resist. And some—quietly—remember truths no longer spoken aloud.

In the era of broadcast divinity and marketable valor, to be a beastfolk delver is not just to survive, but to **reclaim**.

---

## **The Shape of the World Today**

Aerathon no longer runs on conquest or coin.

It runs on **story**, **image**, and **resonance**.  
The Labyrinths remain ever-present—mysterious, shifting, and hungry.  
New ones appear with no warning. Old ones evolve. Some seem to remember those who walk them. And some refuse to be seen at all.

Aerathon is modern. Unified. Controlled.

But beneath the light… something ancient still stirs.

# **The Nameless Labyrinth**

In a world where every delver is monitored, every battle is livecast, and even miracles must trend to matter, the unexpected is supposed to be impossible.

You are new to this world—freshly licensed, barely noticed. One of hundreds of rookie delver teams hoping to rise through the ranks of Louvain’s guild circuit. You’ve studied the rituals, trained with the guild-issue spell mirrors, rehearsed your lines in front of projection runes. Maybe you believe in glory. Maybe you just need the coin. Either way, you’re ready for your first broadcast. Or you thought you were.

Then something broke the pattern.

A labyrinth has appeared on the outskirts of Louvain—unmarked, unregistered, **unnamed**. The Arcanet cannot detect it. The divine signals ignore it. Even the Ministry of Delving Oversight has no footage, no maps, no sanction. Most citizens can’t even see the entrance.

But **you** can.  
You, and others like you.  
**Beastfolk.**

It calls to you in dreams.  
In symbols older than any guild seal.  
In the beat of something ancient under stone—**a pulse that knows your name.**

This is not a delve with cameras.  
There are no sponsors. No ratings. No reset runes.  
This place doesn't want to be seen.  
**It wants to be remembered.**

And as you step across the threshold, you carry with you more than weapons or prayers. You carry bloodlines erased, gods forgotten, and a history stolen in silence. The world has moved on.

**But the Labyrinth remembers.**
