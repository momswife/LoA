## **Backstory**

### **The Rise & Fall: A Samurai Without a Purse**

Togi was once a man of means.

His sword was sharp, his reflexes unmatched, and for a time, so was his luck.

- Born to a minor noble family, he was trained in the ways of the blade, destined for a life of honorable service.
- But then he discovered gambling.
- And horse racing.
- And dice games.
- And high-stakes underground fights.

One loss became ten.  
Ten losses became a debt.  
And soon, Togi had nothing but the shirt on his back and a long list of people who wanted their money.

_"There was a time I could buy a warhorse outright. Now I bet on which one will trip first."_

## **Rock Bottom – And a Little Mercy**

Togi had finally **lost everything.**

- He had no sword (pawned it).
- He had no armor (bet it away).
- He had no money (all of it gone on a "sure thing").

He was lying in a Halvask gutter, lamenting his terrible, terrible luck when a Stormcall mercenary named Arthur "Cap" Penjamin saw him.

- Cap did not know him.
- Cap had no reason to help him.
- Cap gave him exactly 3.5 gold and a handful of sand.

_"Get up. You're embarrassing yourself."_

And, for whatever reason, Togi did.

## **The Last Gamble

Togi needed money.

Not for glory.  
Not for honor.  
Not even for survival.

He needed it so he could keep gambling.

And when the Nameless Labyrinth appeared, it was like the universe itself said:

_"Double or nothing."_

- What’s the biggest wager in the world?
- The deadliest, highest-stakes bet anyone could make?
- Risking your own life for a fortune inside a godforsaken dungeon.

Togi doesn’t care about secrets.  
He doesn’t care about lost knowledge.  
He doesn’t care about whatever horrifying truths lie in the depths.

He cares about winning.

_"If I walk out of this alive, I'm placing a bet so big, the gods themselves will be jealous."_
