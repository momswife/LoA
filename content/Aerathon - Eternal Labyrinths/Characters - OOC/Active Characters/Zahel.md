## **Backstory**

### **A Man of the Past, A Man of Many Sins**

Zahel does not talk about where he comes from.

- Those who knew him years ago remember a man of violence, a collector of debts, a shadow at the edge of alleys.
- He was a man you did not owe money to.
- Because if you did, he would not stop knocking until you paid—or until you bled.

His hands broke bones.  
His hands shut mouths.  
His hands stained the streets of Halvask in the name of men richer and crueler than himself.

And for a time, he did not question it.

Until he did.

And when doubt found him, it did not let go.

- The fear in a debtor’s eyes before Zahel’s fist connected.
- The silence of a man who no longer had the means to scream.
- The cold, empty stare of someone realizing they would never walk again.

It wore on him.

It rotted inside him.

And one day, Zahel simply walked away.

Not ran.  
Not vanished.  
He walked.

Right through the doors of his former masters, looked them in the eyes, and said he was done.

And then he waited for them to kill him.

They didn’t.

Instead, they laughed.

_"You think you can just leave? Walk away and be _someone else_?"_

They let him go.

Because they knew they would never really lose him.

Because sooner or later, all debts must be paid.

## **A Debt That Cannot Be Escaped**

For years, Zahel tried to make amends.

- He studied medicine, learned the ways of healing.
- He wandered, working for nothing, tending to the sick, helping the desperate.
- He tried to undo the harm he had caused—not in name, but in action.

It was not redemption.

It was atonement.

A slow, quiet attempt to put the past behind him, to be something else.

But the past never lets go so easily.

And when the Nameless Labyrinth appeared, his former masters came calling.

- They had lost something inside.
- They needed someone to retrieve it.
- And they decided it would be Zahel.

_"You owe us. And we always collect."_

He had spent years trying to walk away.

And now, they had dragged him right back in.

## **His Past With Togi**

Zahel’s past left trails of shattered men behind him.

Among them, Togi.

- A reckless gambler, always in debt, always on the edge of losing everything.
- And Zahel was the one who made sure he _paid up_.
- The bruises faded, but the memory remained.

Years later, Zahel does not expect forgiveness.

But he does wonder—

If Togi remembers.

And if so—

What will he do?
