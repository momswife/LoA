## **Backstory**

### **A Man of War, A Man of Conviction**

Batariel Aryabhor is not a man of subtlety.

His faith is ironclad, unshakable, and absolute—not a thing to be questioned, not a thing to be reasoned with.

To his allies, he is a beacon, a hammer, a war hymn made flesh.

To his enemies, he is the storm that does not cease until the battlefield is silent.

- He does not merely fight for his faith—he _revels_ in it.
- He does not see brutality as savagery—it is _cleansing._
- He does not entertain doubt—there is only the path laid before him.

For Batariel, the world is iron—and it must be reforged in the image of his god.

And for that, he has been blessed with divine firepower.

## **The Holy Shotgun of the Iron Ascendants**

Faith is his shield, but a righteous blast of consecrated steel is his sword.

Batariel is famous across battlefields for wielding "Reckoner," a sacred, double-barreled holy shotgun blessed by the High Clerics of the Order.

- The shells are inscribed with holy scripture—each one a sermon that delivers judgment in an instant.
- It fires not just metal, but divine wrath—burning, searing blasts that purge the wicked.
- It is both an executioner's tool and a prophet’s voice, speaking in thunder and fire.

He does not hesitate to use it.

- Heretics who mock the faith? Smitten.
- Cowards who refuse to fight? Smitten.
- Creatures of darkness? Smitten twice, just in case.

_"The first shot delivers the word of my god. The second ensures they heard it."_

Some see Reckoner as a weapon of terrifying destruction.

Batariel sees it as mercy.

For those who stand against righteousness must be given the swiftest path to redemption—through fire and steel.

## **Faith Through Fire**

Batariel is a proud and fanatical disciple of the Order of Iron Ascendants, a sect of Heironeous-worshiping zealots who see the Labyrinths as a blight upon the world.

- The weak must be purged.
- The faithless must be corrected.
- The Labyrinths themselves must be **burned from existence—no matter the cost.**

But for Batariel, the duty is not just sacred—it is profitable.

His faith promises victory, power, and wealth beyond imagining.

_"The righteous shall be rewarded. The heretics shall be crushed. And I shall walk upon golden roads in the afterlife for my service."_

Some within the Order serve with humility.  
Batariel serves with devotion and greed in equal measure.

Why _shouldn’t_ he enjoy the fruits of his labor?  
Why _shouldn’t_ he be rewarded for his loyalty?  
Why _shouldn’t_ he be exalted as the warrior he was meant to be?

To him, wealth and power are not indulgences—they are divine rewards for his righteousness.

And he will claim them.
## **A New Battlefield**

The Nameless Labyrinth appeared in Halvask without warning.

Some saw chaos.

Batariel saw purpose.

To the Order of Iron Ascendants, this was the next crusade, the next trial by fire.

To Batariel, it was an opportunity.

- To purge the Labyrinth’s corruption.
- To spread the will of his god.
- To claim glory, wealth, and divine favor.

He will walk into that place not as a mere warrior—but as a chosen champion.

"Let those who falter be cast aside. Let those who stand strong be exalted. The weak shall be crushed, and the worthy shall ascend!"
