## **Backstory**

### **The Street Urchin Who Found Balance**

Kurk was not born into balance.

His earliest memories are not of temples, but of alleys, hunger, and cold.

- No home. No parents. No direction.
- Surviving on scraps, stealing when necessary, learning quickly that the world was unfair.
- Watching people pass by—too busy, too important to notice a starving child.

Until one day, he stole from the wrong person.

A monk of Kelemvor, the god of death, caught him mid-theft, but rather than punishment, offered him a meal.

_"The living must be nourished. You were taking what you needed. But balance must be maintained—so come, and learn how to give as well as take."_

And so, Kurk followed.

### **The Temple of Kelemvor – A New Path**

Within the cloisters of Kelemvor’s sect, Kurk was taught the ways of balance.

- He learned to read and write, to think beyond survival.
- He learned to fight, but only to defend.
- He learned to cook and heal, to bring warmth where there was once cold.

The temple was not a place of comfort, but of discipline.

And yet, it was the first place that had ever truly been home.

- He watched over the dying, easing their suffering.
- He fed the hungry, remembering his own childhood pain.
- He sought fairness in all things—never favoring the strong over the weak, the wealthy over the poor.

When he came of age, the monks gave him a choice.

_"You have learned the ways of balance within these walls. Now go, and see if you can maintain it beyond them."_

And so, Kurk set out.

Not for glory.  
Not for riches.  
But for understanding.

For the simple belief that the world needed someone to bring balance where there was none.

## **A Quest for Harmony (and Food)**

As a wandering monk, Kurk sought to better the world in small ways.

- Settling disputes in towns, easing tensions between merchants and beggars.
- Breaking up fights without violence—disarming bandits, subduing troublemakers.
- Cooking for the hungry, brewing medicine for the sick.

He was respected, but also… taken advantage of.

For all his wisdom, Kurk was naïve.

- He trusted too easily.
- He gave without asking for anything in return.
- He saw the best in people, even when they deserved nothing.

But when he slept, he slept deeply.

- Too deeply.
- Slept through entire meals, missed town meetings, once woke up _two days later_ in a barn.
- Absolutely refuses to drink coffee to fix it.

_"If my body demands rest, who am I to argue? Balance, my friend."_

And yet, even in his travels, something was missing.

## **And Kurk Is Drawn In**

Kurk arrived in Halvask seeking nothing more than a place to rest and a warm meal.

And then, the Nameless Labyrinth erupted into existence.

He was there when it happened.

- He saw people disappear into nothingness, lost in the sundering.
- He saw the city react—not with mourning, but with _excitement_, as sponsors and warriors rushed to claim glory.
- He saw the scales tipping too far in one direction.

This was not balance.

And so, Kurk made his decision.

Not to conquer the Labyrinth.

Not to seek treasure.

But to restore equilibrium—to find those lost within and bring them home.

_"So much suffering, so much chaos—surely I can bring some harmony back to it."_

He does not yet understand the full weight of what he is stepping into.

But he will.
