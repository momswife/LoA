## **Backstory**: 

### **A Corsair of the Stormcall Isles**

Arthur Penjamin was born on the waves and raised in the tempest, a child of the Stormcall Isles, where the sea itself seems alive, and the people carve their legacies from wind and water. From a young age, he was trained in ship combat, blade-work, and the mercenary trade, earning his place among the Azure Corsairs, a renowned fleet of privateers, explorers, and sell-swords known for their loose code of honor and their willingness to fight for coin, but never for cruelty.

- His first raid was at fifteen, where he boarded an enemy ship to claim a prize—not treasure, but a prisoner being trafficked against their will. Even then, he could not turn a blind eye.
- By twenty-three, he captained his own small vessel, escorting merchant ships too poor to afford noble protection but too honest to turn to pirates.
- His peers saw him as soft-hearted, but unshakable—a man who never took a contract if it meant sacrificing those who could not defend themselves.

And yet, even amidst the sea’s infinite horizon, he felt there was something waiting beyond the waves. A path not yet walked. A purpose not yet fulfilled.

### **Witness to the Sundering**

Arthur and his fellow Corsairs had come to Halvask looking for work, coin, and perhaps a few rounds of drink before taking on another contract. Yet, only a day after arriving, the world itself shifted beneath his feet.

In an instant, Velgrove Square was torn apart—the air cracked, space itself folded inward, and people standing there simply… vanished.

Arthur was present. He saw it happen.

- A child, standing with their mother, screaming in confusion before they disappeared.
- A group of merchants huddled near their stalls, gone in the blink of an eye.
- No bodies. No blood. No remains. Just… emptiness.

He had seen men drown, ships sink, storms take entire fleets.

But this was different.

### **The Call to Delving: A New Purpose**

Arthur did not come to Halvask to become a Delver.

But now, he cannot walk away from it.

While mercenaries and adventurers flocked to the Labyrinth for fame, fortune, and spectacle, Arthur saw only those left behind.

- The families searching for their lost kin, begging for someone to bring them back.
- The scholars desperate for answers, ignored in favor of profit-seekers.
- The merchants more concerned with selling gear than asking what was truly inside the Labyrinth.

If people were trapped within, if souls had been stolen, then he would find them.

Arthur was a swordsman, a warrior, and a leader—but more than that, he was a man who could not turn his back on those in need.

And so, he made his choice.

- He would enter the Nameless Labyrinth—not for riches, but to find the lost.
- He would navigate its horrors—not for spectacle, but to learn its secrets.
- And he would carve a path—not for himself, but for those who would never make it out otherwise.

This was no accident.

This was his convergence.
